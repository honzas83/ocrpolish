> # AD 70 Follow-Up Action: Nuclear Aspects
> 
> This document is a draft report by the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group concerning the follow-up action on the nuclear aspects of the AD 70 report. Following the approval of the Study on Alliance Defence Problems for the 1970s (AD 70 Report) by the Defence Planning Committee Ministers, the Executive Working Group (EWG) was tasked with monitoring progress. However, the EWG declined responsibility for nuclear matters, asserting they fall under the purview of the Nuclear Planning Group. Consequently, the NPG must now assume both the substantive and managerial functions for follow-up on nuclear questions. The report identifies several nuclear problem areas, including the tactical use of nuclear weapons, reduction of risks to friendly troops and infrastructure, and the transition from conventional to nuclear operations. To avoid overburdening the NPG Ministers, it is recommended that the NPG Permanent Representatives handle the purely managerial aspects, such as monitoring progress and preparing status reports for the Defence Planning Committee. This approach ensures that NPG Ministers retain their prerogative to deal with substantive policy recommendations. The document concludes by recommending that the NPG Permanent Representatives confirm these managerial arrangements and task the NPG Staff Group to prepare a detailed report on current and planned actions regarding nuclear problems.

In December 1970, the Defence Planning Committee Ministers approved the Report
on the Study on Alliance Defence Problems for the 1970s(1) (short title: AD 70
Report) and tasked the Defence Planning Committee in Permanent Session with
drawing up and monitoring a suitable programme for following-up on the
recommendations in paragraph 118 and ANNEX I to ensure that progress is
systematically made on all of the matters concerned. The Defence Planning
Committee Permanent Representatives, in turn, delegated to the Executive Working
Group (EWG) the task of acting as their agent for these purposes.

2. The EWG sorted out the many problems mentioned in the AD 70 report and
assigned all of them, except the nuclear aspects, to specific committees, staff
sections and military commands for further action, requesting interim reports by

3. With respect to the nuclear problems, the EWG
- declined to assume responsibility on the grounds that such problems were

the Nuclear Planning Group is taking steps to ensure that appropriate follow-up
action is taken, either in the context of Nuclear Planning Group work already in
progress or as a result of new initiatives.

4. Despite their separation from the other problems, the nuclear aspects remain
part of the AD 70 complex on which the Defence Planning Committee Ministers
expect systematic progress to be ensured, the task of ensuring progress now
being a responsibility primarily of the Nuclear Planning Group.

Nuclear Problems Mentioned in the AD 70 Report

5. The AD 70 Report(1) outlines in general terms a number of nuclear problem
areas which appear to deserve attention. These areas may be summarized as
follows:

(a) Tactical use of nuclear weapons systems organic to ground forces or
providing them with nuclear support;

(b) Reduction of risks to friendly troops, population and infrastructure
(arising from NATO employment of nuclear weapons) through technological
developments in the correlated areas of accuracy, yields and fallout;

(c) Structure and tactical disposition of forces for operation in a nuclear
environment;

Managerial functions
7. The main managerial functions to be assumed by the Nuclear Planning Group
would seem to be:

(a) examination of the field of nuclear problems to identify actions that are
already underway or

(b) assignment, where appropriate, of follow-up responsibility with regard to

8. This managerial task, though legitimately assigned to the Nuclear Planning
Group, falls outside its normal scope of activity, especially insofar as the
Ministers themselves are concerned. Direct involvement of the Ministers in such
administrative matters would appear to be a totally unwarranted intrusion on the
already quite limited time and effort they are able to spend on more substantive
questions. There are also practical reasons why direct Ministerial involvement
is not feasible. For instance, because the Nuclear Planning Group meets only
twice a year and often only shortly before Defence Planning Committee
Ministerial sessions, it would be difficult, if not impossible, to process
status reports in a timely fashion, even if such reports were sent directly from

the Nuclear Planning Group Ministers to the Defence Planning Committee Ministers

For these reasons, it is appropriate for the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent
Representatives to carry out the purely managerial aspects of the AD 70 follow-
up effort pertaining to nuclear problems, without prejudicing in any way the
Ministerial prerogatives to deal with the substantive aspects of these problems.
Under this approach, it is envisaged that there would be no change in the
current procedures for the formulation of substantive policy recommendations by
the Nuclear Planning Group Ministers and their subsequent submission to the
Defence Planning Committee through the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee. It is
also envisaged that the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent Representatives, in
discharging their managerial functions, would be guided by instructions from
their respective Ministers.

Recommendations
9. It is recommended that the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent Representatives:
(a) confirm the managerial arrangements for handling the follow-up on nuclear
questions raised in the AD 70 Report, as outlined in paragraphs 7 and 8 above;
