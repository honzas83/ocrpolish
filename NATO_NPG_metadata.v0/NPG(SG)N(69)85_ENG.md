> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 17th November, 1969: Arrangements at the Airlie House Meeting
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on November 17, 1969, to discuss administrative arrangements and nuclear policy. The group expressed appreciation for the hospitality of the United States Authorities during the Ministerial meeting at Airlie House. They agreed that Norway and Portugal should receive copies of the Agreed Minute and background documentation for the upcoming Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) meeting on December 3rd. The group discussed the Provisional Political Guidelines, noting that they would become an NDAC action paper, and requested the Military Committee to provide their views in writing. They also addressed the presentation of the section covering demonstrative use of nuclear weapons, based on a Danish proposal. Furthermore, the group noted that the International Staff would circulate a revised draft paper on further studies and that countries wished to participate in these studies. Regarding strike forces, they noted that Germany and the United States would prepare a draft concept for the Spring Ministerial meeting. The group also invited Italy to suggest dates for the next NPG Ministerial meeting in the spring. Finally, they discussed the agenda and report for the upcoming NDAC meeting and scheduled their next Staff Group meeting for December 8, 1969.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 17th November, 1969
ARRANGEMENTS AT THE AIRLIE HOUSE MEETING
1. The Staff Group :
(a) expressed its sincere appreciation for the efficient arrangements and
generous hospitality provided by the United States Authorities during the course
of the Ministerial meeting on 11th and 12th November;

(b) requested the United States Representative to transmit the thanks of the
Staff Group to the Authorities in Washington.

DOCUMENTATION FOR THE NDAC MEETING ON 3RD DECEMBER
2. The Staff Group :
(a) agreed that Norway and Portugal should receive copies of the Agreed Minute
(1) as soon as possible;

(b) noted that Norway and Portugal automatically received documents with an NDAC
reference;

(c) noted that Norway and Portugal, as members of the Nuclear Defence Affairs
Committee, would require some background documentation for the meeting on 3rd
December, and agreed that they should ask the originators for such documents as
they thought necessary.

DISCUSSION OF THE AGREED MINUTE (1)
Provisional Guidelines
3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) with reference to paragraph 1 of the Agreed Minute, noted that the
Provisional Political Guidelines paper would now become an NDAC action paper;

(b) with reference to paragraph 4, requested the Chairman to invite the Chairman
of the Military Committee to make known the views of the Military Committee in
writing and if possible before December;

(c) noted with regard to paragraph 4, that the Danish proposal concerning the
presentation of the section covering demonstrative use should be addressed early
in the New Year.

Further Studies
4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted, with regard to paragraph 7 of the Agreed Minute, that the Permanent
Representatives had been given a task, and that the International Staff would
make the first step by circulating a revised draft paper (2) on the problem, at
the same time soliciting suggestions from all concerned;

(b) noted a general wish on the part of the countries to participate in the
various aspects of the studies;

(c) noted that participation in, and leadership of aspects of the studies was
connected with the problem of membership.

Strike Forces
5. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted, with regard to paragraph 9, that no Staff Group action was required
on this matter;

(b) noted that Germany and the United States would be preparing a draft concept
for the Spring Ministerial meeting, and expressed the hope that this paper might
be circulated in good time before the meeting;
