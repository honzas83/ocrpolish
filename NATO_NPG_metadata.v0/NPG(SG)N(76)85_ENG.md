> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 20th September, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on September 20, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key items. The meeting began with a welcome to Colonel Egon Winkler, who replaced Colonel Steindorf. The group discussed the circulation of a paper on Compilation and the status of agreement from member states, noting that the United States had not yet accepted the text. The group also reviewed the text of a Working Paper on the Classification of Nuclear Consultation Messages, awaiting confirmation from the United States. Additionally, the group discussed the agenda for forthcoming Permanent Representatives' meetings, including briefings by Mr. Cotter on NATO and Warsaw Pact nuclear systems, which led to a discussion on the possibility of postponing the Phase III Status Report. The group also received a briefing on newly developed procedures in the field of nuclear consultation from Commander Davies and Mr. Hentzel, agreeing to modifications such as adding an index of acronyms and providing briefing papers to Permanent Representatives and Ministers. Finally, the group noted a report on new technology produced by the Military Implications Team, which was to be circulated to Permanent Representatives via the Full Study Panel. The next meeting was scheduled for October 4, 1976.

WELCOME TO NEW MEMBER

1. The STAFF GROUP:
concurred with the words of welcome addressed by the Chairman to Colonel Egon
Winkler, replacing Colonel Steindorf.

COMPILATION

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who referred to the paper on
Compilation(1) and expressed the hope that it could be circulated as a two
language NPC Working Paper in time for the meeting of Permanent Representatives
on 29th September, 1976;

(b) noted that, with the exception of the United States, all countries had
received instructions and could accept the present text of the paper.

AGENDA FOR FORTHCOMING PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES' MEETINGS

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the United States representative who said that
Mr. Cotter would be coming to Europe shortly and would give two briefings to
Permanent Representatives, one on NATO nuclear systems and the other on Warsaw
Pact nuclear systems; it was suggested by his authorities that the briefings
should take place on 29th September and 5th October;

(b) noted the further remarks of the United States representative who said that
he realised that the above proposition would imply a postponement of the Phase
III Status Report;

(c) took note of the views expressed by a number of members who, in view of the
importance of the Phase III Study were disappointed by this postponement;

(d) discussed the possibility of spreading the various items for consideration
by Permanent Representatives over a series of meetings comprising 29th September
and 5th, 22nd and 28th October;

(e) agreed to continue discussion of this problem at an informal meeting to be
held in the Chairman's office next day.

PRESENTATION OF NEWLY DEVELOPED PROCEDURES IN THE FIELD OF NUCLEAR CONSULTATION

5. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a briefing given by Commander Davies, SNAEL, and Mr. Hentzel,
Nuclear Planning Directorate, presenting newly developed procedures in the field
of nuclear consultation;

(b) took note of the views expressed and the suggestions made during the ensuing
discussion and agreed upon certain modifications to the briefing;

(c) agreed in particular:

(i) that an index of acronyms should be attached to the briefing papers;

(ii) that Permanent Representatives and Ministers should receive copies of the
briefing papers before the meeting;

(iii) that the briefing should be repeated to the Staff Group and that it should
also be given to selected officers in SHAPE.

NEW TECHNOLOGY STUDY

6. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the United States representative who said that
the Military Implications Team had now produced a report in two volumes on new
Technology; a copy of each volume had been made available to the International
Staff who would arrange for printing and distribution;

(b) took note of the further remarks of the United States representative who
said that he was circulating a draft cover letter for forwarding the report from
the Full Study Panel to Permanent Representatives; he hoped that this draft
would be discussed at the Full Study Panel meeting on 29th September; the
reports would be circulated as soon as possible afterwards.
