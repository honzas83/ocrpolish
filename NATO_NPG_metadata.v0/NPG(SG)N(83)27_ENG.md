> [!abstract] 
> # Autumn 1983 NPG Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This note by the Secretary provides the detailed logistical arrangements for the upcoming Autumn 1983 Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministerial Meeting to be held in Canada. It lists the International Staff delegation, including the Secretary General, Assistant Secretary General for Defence Planning and Policy, and various directors and secretaries. The document outlines the transport of classified documents via the courier Mr. B. Tuccimei, specifying that documents must be submitted by October 24th. It also provides communication instructions for messages transmitted via the NATO-wide system to 'MONTEBELLO CAN' using the routing indicator 'RCCPGPA'. Additionally, it describes the transport arrangements between NATO Headquarters and Melsbroek for those traveling on a special Canadian forces flight on October 25th and 29th.

Transport of Classified Documents

2. Mr. B. Tuccimei, Head of the COSMIC Unit of the Central Registry, will carry
classified documents for the meeting. Delegations wishing to use the services of
the International Staff courier are requested to send him their documents by
midday Monday, 24th October at the latest.

3. Documents should be packaged and clearly marked with the name of the
delegation or person and a serial number. Packages should be as light as
possible and must not contain any unclassified material.

Communications

4. The Canadian authorities have asked me to inform delegations that messages to
be transmitted to the conference centre via the NATO-wide system should be
addressed to "MONTEBELLO CAN" and the message routing indicator "RCCPGPA" should
be used. The message centre will be operational from 22nd to 29th October.

Transport Between NATO Headquarters and Melsbroek

5. NATO buses will be available on 25th and 29th October to transport those
travelling on the special Canadian forces flight between the NATO Headquarters
and Melsbroek. Details will be circulated as soon as the Canadian authorities
have finalised arrangements with the base at Melsbroek.

(Signed) D.J. ROBERTS

NATO, 1110 Brussels.
