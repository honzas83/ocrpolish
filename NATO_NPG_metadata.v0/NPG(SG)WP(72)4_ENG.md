> # Draft Ministerial Agenda
> 
> This document is a draft agenda for the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministerial Meeting scheduled for May 18th and 19th, 1972, in Copenhagen. The agenda includes a briefing on the balance of strategic forces led by the United States, an illustrative study on battlefield use, and studies of follow-on use in Turkey and the Mediterranean Maritime area. It also covers the initiation of Phase II, the status of work in progress, and the finalization of a communique. The draft was submitted by the Chairman, H.B. Seim, and was proposed for discussion by the Staff Group on March 6th, 1972.
> 
> ## Categories/Topics
> 
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Extended-Deterrence-and-Tactical-Nuclear-Sharing — The document is a draft agenda for the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) focusing on the 'Battlefield Use' of nuclear weapons and 'Follow-on Tactical Use' in specific regions like Turkey and the Mediterranean Maritime Area, which directly relates to the deployment and sharing of nuclear responsibilities among allies.
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Nuclear-Strategy-and-Strike-Planning — The agenda includes specific items for 'Briefing on the Balance of Strategic Forces' and 'Illustrative Study - Battlefield Use', as well as 'Studies of Follow-on Use' for specific geographic regions, indicating internal mechanisms for war planning and execution consultation.
> #Internal-Alliance-Dynamics/Command-and-Control-Authority — The agenda explicitly references a briefing on 'ACEU's Nuclear Weapons Release Procedures', which concerns the mechanisms for ordering military action and the release authority of nuclear weapons.
