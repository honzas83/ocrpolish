> # Redesignation of Nike Hercules Study
> 
> This document serves as a notice from the Secretary of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) regarding the redesignation of the Nike Hercules Study as NPG/Study/48. The study, which is classified as ATOMAL, is intended for consideration at the upcoming Ottawa NPG Ministerial meeting in June 1977. The redesignation includes four primary documents: a cover note by the Secretary, Military Committee comments (CMCM-15-77), a letter from the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR), and the SACEUR Nike Hercules Study (SHAPE/ATOMAL/231/76). The study was originally commissioned by NPG Ministers in June 1976 and tasked to SACEUR to evaluate various levels of nuclear-capable Nike Hercules sites. The document specifies that recipients should return unneeded parts of the study to the International Staff ATOMAL Registry and warns against destroying documents until after the June ministerial meeting. The notice is signed by M.H.G. Wilson and includes transmittal notes signed by E.G. Luft.

NIKE HERCULES STUDY
Transmittal note by the Secretary

Early in March 1977, the SHAPE NIKE HERCULES Study
and SACEUR's comments were distributed to ATOMAL Registries
in NATO Headquarters.

2. Attached are two papers related to the above-mentioned
documents:

(a) a cover note, dated 29th April, 1977, by the
Secretary NPG designating the NIKE HERCULES Study
as NPG/Study/48;

(b) CMCM-15-77, dated 20th April, 1977 which provides
Military Committee comments on the Study.

3. These two papers should be attached permanently on top
of the SACEUR NIKE HERCULES Study with its accompanying letter
from SACEUR. These four documents(1) will, from this time forward,
be referred to as NPG/Study/48.

4. Recipients are requested to return to the International
Staff ATOMAL Registry all unneeded parts of this Study. Under no
circumstances should any of these documents be destroyed until
after the NPG Ministerial meeting in June.

5. This transmittal note should be separated from its
attachments when they are joined with the basic study.

(Signed) E.G. LUFT

1110 Brussels.

Attachment No. 2 to NPG(STAFF GROUP)N(77)38

NPG/Study/48

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP
NIKE HERCULES STUDY
Note by the Secretary

At their June 1976 meeting, NPG Ministers invited(1) the NATO Military
Authorities (NMAs) to prepare a study, in accordance with Terms of Reference
(TOR) to be established by the Permanent Representatives, of various levels of
nuclear-capable NIKE HERCULES sites. The TOR(2) were circulated on 15th July,
1976, and SACEUR was tasked to do the study.

2. The study was finished in response to the above-mentioned TOR and circulated
on 1st March, 1977, under reference SHAPE/ATOMAL/231/76 together with a covering
letter by SACEUP (6440.02.09/5220/S025/77).

3. On 20th April, 1977, the Chairman, Military Committee, circulated related
comments by Military Representatives of NPG nations under cover of CMCM-15-77
(NATO SECRET).

4. All three documents together with the cover note constitute NPG/Study/48 for
consideration at the Ottawa NPG Ministerial meeting in June 1977.

(Signed) E.G. LUFF
1110 Brussels.
