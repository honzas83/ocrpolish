> # Nuclear Aspects of the Nunn Amendment
> 
> This document, a notice from the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) of NATO, details the coordination between the North Atlantic Treaty Organization and the United States regarding a report to the US Congress on the nuclear aspects of the Nunn Amendment. US Public Law 93-365 directed the Secretary of Defense to study the overall concept for the use of tactical nuclear weapons in Europe, their relation to deterrence and conventional defense, and the development of a rational nuclear posture for the NATO Alliance. The document records that Secretary Schlesinger requested NATO's assessment of the political and military implications of the Nunn Amendment in November 1974. Following discussions among the Permanent Representatives (NPG) and SHAPE, SACEUR, and the Military Committee, a political evaluation of the military assessment was provided. The process involved briefings by Mr. Donald R. Cotter on behalf of the US Secretary of Defense and the distribution of an unclassified summary and a full unclassified version of the report titled 'The Theater Nuclear Force Posture in Europe'. The NATO Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) expressed appreciation for the receipt of the report prior to its public release and the opportunity to comment on the report before it was forwarded to Senator Stennis on May 2, 1975.

US Public Law 93-365 directed the Secretary of Defense to study the overall
concept for use of tactical nuclear weapons in Europe; how the use of such
weapons relates to deterrence and to a strong conventional defense; reduction in
the number and types of nuclear warheads which are not essential for the defense
structure for western Europe; and the steps that can be taken to develop a
rational and coordinated nuclear posture by the NATO Alliance that is consistent
with proper emphasis on conventional defense forces. The Secretary of Defense
was requested to submit a report on the results of this study to the United
States Congress on or before April 1, 1975.

2. On 5th November, 1974 Secretary Schlesinger, in response to US Public Law
93-365, requested NATO's assessment on the political and military implications
of the nuclear aspects of the NUNN Amendment(1). On 10th February, 1975 the
Permanent Representatives (NPG) met and discussed the SHAPE, SACEUR, Military
Committee military assessment called for by Secretary Schlesinger and agreed to
the preparation of a letter to provide the required political evaluation of the
military assessment.

3. PO/75/15(Revised) dated 25 February, 1975 advised the Permanent
Representatives (NPG) that the text of a letter to the United States Secretary
of Defense was approved by all participants and would be forwarded, as required,
by the Secretary General.

4. On 10 larch, 1975 Mr. Donald R. Cotter, representing the
United States Secretary of Defense briefed the Permanent
Representatives (NLC) on the Report on NATO's Theater Nuclear
Forces required by Public Law 95-365. On 25 March, 1975
Mr. Cotter returned to consult further with the Permanent
Representatives (NPG) and later that same day the US Ambassador
to NATO distributed copies of the report to the NPC Delegations
and to the Chairman, Military Committee.

5. On 13 April, 1975 the US Ambassador circulated an
unclassified summary of the report on "The Theater Nuclear Force
Posture in Europe" and requested a determination whether or not
the Summary would be acceptable to the Secretary General and the
NPG Allies. At the same time, the US Ambassador advised the NPG
Allies that his authorities were preparing an unclassified
version of the full report to the Congress at the request of
Senator Stennis. Both the unclassified Summary and the
unclassified version of the full report would form the basis for
statements by US officials in any Congressional hearings on this
subject.

6. On Tuesday, 22 April, 1975 the US ambassador ascertained
that the unclassified summary was acceptable to the Secretary
General and the NPG Allies and the United States Authorities
were so advised.

7. On 26 April, 1975 the US ambassador provided the
Permanent Representatives on the Nuclear Planning Group and the
Chairman of the Military Committee copies of the unclassified
version of the full report on "The Theater Nuclear Force Posture
in Europe." At the same time, he indicated that because of the
strong interest in Congress in receiving this report, it would
be forwarded to Senator Stennis on Friday, May 2.
