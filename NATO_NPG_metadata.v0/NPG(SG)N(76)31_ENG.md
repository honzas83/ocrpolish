> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 12th April, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on April 12, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key nuclear strategy and procedural issues. The group discussed the Tri-Service Study on Air Defence and noted the security clearances of all present. Regarding nuclear consultation, the group addressed delays in circulating Working Paper 1 due to amendments requested by Italy and the Netherlands, leading to the creation of an Ad Hoc Working Group consisting of the United Kingdom, United States, and the Netherlands to finalize the text. The meeting also covered the procedural handling of a United States paper on improving the effectiveness of NATO's Theatre Nuclear Force Posture, with plans for further discussion by Permanent Representatives and a visit from United States experts. Additionally, the group touched upon newly developed procedures in the field of nuclear consultation based on the WINTEX 77 scenario. Preparations for the NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting on June 14-15, 1976, were discussed, including a proposed agenda featuring briefings on Strategic Forces, NATO's Theatre Nuclear Force Posture, and the Single Integrated Operational Plan (SIOP). The group also addressed an advance press release and the possibility of the United Kingdom hosting the Autumn Meeting of NPG Ministers. Finally, the next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for April 23, 1976.

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared to the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

DOCUMENTATION
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the Tri-Service Study on
Air Defence was now circulated under reference AC/259-D/494 and AC/280-D/77
dated 15th March, 1976; he recommended that members should take note of the
conclusions and recommendations of this paper.

an NP Working Paper (white) in both languages; this had been delayed because of

PRESENTATION OF NEWLY DEVELOPED PROCEDURES IN THE FIELD OF NUCLEAR CONSULTATION

5. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the Staff Group

decision to circulate the CNI paper(1) had been taken;
