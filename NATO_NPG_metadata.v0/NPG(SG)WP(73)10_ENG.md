> # Early Combined Use of NATO Conventional and Nuclear Capabilities
> 
> This document is a draft report by the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Permanent Representatives discussing the military advantages and disadvantages of an early combined use of NATO conventional and nuclear capabilities. The report draws on comments from NATO Military Authorities and a report from the Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE). It traces the background of the proposal, noting that former Secretary General Brosio suggested examining the possibility of combined use before conventional forces suffer substantial losses, rather than waiting for impending conventional defeat. The report emphasizes that early combined use is an option within NATO's current strategy of flexibility in response, though the alliance should not be committed to such use in advance. The analysis provided by SHAPE focuses on military aspects and excludes political factors, concluding that a full assessment of the balance of advantages and disadvantages can only be made based on the specific situation at the time of a potential conflict. The document also references previous studies in the Follow-on Use Work Programme and the political guidelines for initial use of nuclear weapons.

I. PURPOSE
This report discusses advantages and disadvantages that might accrue from an
early combined use of NATO conventional and nuclear capabilities, drawing on

should resort to such use, the SHAPE Report observes(1) that early tactical use
is permissible within the approved strategy of flexibility in response and that

6. The SHAPE Report indicates(2) that its analysis is based on existing

tactical use of nuclear weapons(3). However, the analysis deliberately excludes
consideration of political factors that could have a direct impact on striking a
balance of military advantages and disadvantages. The Report concludes, in
essence, that a full and accurate assessment of this balance could only be made
in the light of the particular situation that prevails at the time that early
combined use might be under consideration. It does point out, however, that an
improved awareness on the part of political and military planners and decision-
makers of the variable elements discussed in the Report (as well as others that
might be identified in the future) would make an important contribution to the
goal of deterrence and toward a viable defence of NATO in the event of any
future military conflict. We agree with these general conclusions and are of the
opinion that they apply as well to the question of assessing the political
advantages and disadvantages of early combined use.

military course of action. Before we comment in detail on some of these factors,

Deterrent Role of Early Combined Use

11. While confirming the necessity of maintaining a convincing NATO capability
to use nuclear weapons as a deterrent, the SHAPE Report points out(1) that, in
accordance with its terms of reference, the development of the analysis is
confined to assessment factors concerning the actual use itself without any
consideration of the possible deterrent effect of the threat of an early
combined use. As noted earlier, early combined use is compatible with the
current NATO strategy of flexible response. So long as NATO maintains a viable
and visible capability to resort to early combined use and does not rule such
use out as a matter of policy, a potential aggressor must take the possibility
into account; the threat of early combined use should serve, in part, to deter
him from initiating an aggression(2).

12. Apart from the foregoing, we note that the actual tactical employment of
nuclear weapons in the early combined use context is likely to have a
significant deterrent as well as war-fighting aspect. With respect to the war-
fighting aspect, such use would be designed to inflict damage on the enemy
forces and cause him to reconsider his aggression and to cease his attack. But
such use could also

IV. RECOMMENDATIONS

14. The Ministers may wish to:

(a) take note of the SHAPE Report and the related MC Comments(1) as well as the
comments in this report;

(b) agree that these documents make a valuable contribu- buton toward providing
a basis on which political authorities might assess the question of early
combined use in an actual crisis;

(c) note that early combined use is one of the options open to NATO within the
current strategy;

(d) invite the NATO Military Authorities to continue to plan for the exercise of
this option if NATO should elect to do so in time of crisis, and to maintain the
necessary military capability to implement the option if directed; and

(e) note that the operational concepts and doctrine currently governing
employment of nuclear weapons for tactical purposes(2) are sufficiently broad
and flexible to accommodate the early combined use option.
