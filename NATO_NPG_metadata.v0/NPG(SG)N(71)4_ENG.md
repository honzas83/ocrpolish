> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 11th January, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on January 11, 1971, to address several administrative and strategic matters. The group noted personnel changes, including the departure of Mr. A.W.G. Le Hardy and the arrival of Mr. M. Stewart as the United Kingdom representative, and the appointment of Dr. van Ekeken of the Netherlands as the new doyen. The meeting covered the United States' paper on the targeting of Soviet Intermediate-Range/Medium-Range Ballistic Missiles (IR/MRBMs) on Europe. The group also discussed the International Military Staff's participation in Exercise WINTEX 71 and the need for briefings on Selective Release Procedures. There were discussions regarding the number of notetakers for military observers at NPG and NDAC ministerial meetings, and the future tasks of the Staff Group, including preparations for the May Ministerial Meeting. Additionally, the group reviewed the progress of follow-on studies on the tactical use of nuclear weapons, specifically mentioning studies for North Norway, Greek Thrace, Turkey, and NORTHAG, and the proposal for an ACLANT study. The meeting concluded with a mention of the nuclear aspects of the AD70 follow-up and a proposal for the Staff Group to visit SHAPE, AFCENT, and NORTHTAG headquarters.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 11th January, 1971

ARRIVALS AND DEPARTURES

1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted with regret that Mr. A.W.G. Le Hardy, the Doyen of the Staff Group,
was leaving shortly, and warmly approved the farewell remarks of the Chairman
and of the representative of Italy;

(b) noted with approval that a farewell lunch party in honour of Mr. Le Hardy
would be organised to take place in the near future;

(c) noted with regret that it had not been possible to give a farewell party to
Mr. N. Akiman, who had replaced Mr. Sungurtekin in the Staff Group as
representative of Turkey, because he had been appointed Assistant Secretary
General to CENTO, and had been obliged to go quickly to his new post;

(d) noted with approval the words of welcome addressed by the Chairman to Mr. M.
Stewart, replacing Mr. Le Hardy as United Kingdom representative;

(e) noted that the new doyen of the Staff Group was Dr. van Ekeken, Netherlands
representative.

TARGETING OF SOVIET IR/MRBMS ON EUROPE

2. The STAFF GROUP :

Authorities had recently sent copies of their paper regarding targeting of
Soviet IR/MRBs on Europe to the Secretary General for transmittal to the
Chairman of the military Committee and to SACEUR, SACILANT and CINCHAN;

STAFF GROUP PARTICIPATION IN EXERCISE WINTEX 71

3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the representative of the Military Committee who
described the extent of participation by the International Military Staff in
Exercise WINTEX 71, drew attention to the fact that the Defence Planning
Committee was not involved and that there would be no political response cell,
and said that from a nuclear point of view Monday 1st February and Wednesday 3rd
February would be the most interesting days; he suggested that informal
briefings on substantive aspects of nuclear release might usefully be planned
for the Staff Group for 27th or 28th January, possibly 1st February and
certainly on 2nd February;

(b) agreed that both the Staff Group and the Permanent Representatives required
briefing on Selective Release Procedures and that if it was not convenient to
give a briefing during the exercise then one should be planned to take place
during the week beginning 8th February;

(c) requested the Chairman to maintain contact on these matters with the
Military Committee and SHAPE representatives and to report progress to the Staff
Group at its next meeting.

NOTETAKERS FOR MILITARY OBSERVERS ATTENDING NPG AND NDAC
MINISTERIAL MEETINGS

4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the problem of
notetakers as set out in NPG(Staff Group)N(70)89, of 19th November, 1970;

(b) took note of the various opinions expressed by the representatives of member
countries of the NPG, and of the representatives of the Chairman of the Military
Committee, SACEUR and SACILANT;

(c) noted, as a separate matter, that some countries were in favour of
increasing the present limit of 5 persons per delegation, possibly to 6 but at
least to 6; in this regard, requested members to obtain the views of their
capitals;

FUTURE TASKS FOR THE STAFF GROUP

5. The STAFF GROUP :

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman in which he reviewed the future
tasks of the Staff Group, and referred particularly to the Spring and Autumn
Ministerial meetings, preparations for which constituted the peak periods of the
Groups activities;

(b) noted that, in the view of the Chairman, the agenda for the Ministerial
Meeting in May would probably include :

(i) discussion of one or more follow-on studies,

(ii) discussion of illustrative studies and scenarios,

(iii) examination of selective release requests,

(iv) taking note of a report from the NMA regarding the implementation of the
concept of theatre strike forces,

(v) other reports from countries and NMA's.

FOLLOW-ON STUDIES ON TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS

week beginning 16th January;

the Turkey study was delayed;

NUCLEAR ASPECTS OF THE AD70 FOLLOW-UP
7. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman that the nuclear aspects of the AD70 follow-up
might involve the Staff Group in some additional work.

BRIEFING ON SELECTIVE RELEASE PROCEDURES
8. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalling that there had been no opportunity in September 1970 when the DPC
Permanent Representatives were briefed on SACEUR's general strike plan for a
concurrent presentation on selective release procedures, agreed that it would be
useful to have such a briefing in the near future;

(b) requested the Chairman to explore possibilities with the IMS and SHAPE
representatives.

STAFF GROUP VISITS
9. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) endorsed a proposal by the Netherlands representative that the Staff Group
visit SHAPE (since the last visit was nearly three years ago) and that this be
followed by visits to other Headquarters such as AFCENT and NORTHTAG;

(b) welcomed a statement by the SHAPE representative that he was already working
on arrangements for Staff Group visits to SHAPE and other Headquarters.

NEXT MEETING
10. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 18th January, 1971, at 3 p.m.
