> # Future Work Programme
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines a proposed future work programme for 1974. It identifies several key topics for consideration, including the Follow-on Use Work Programme, implications of technological developments, consultation procedures and facilities, illustrative studies, the function of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation process, and politico-military strategy and military doctrine. The Chairman emphasizes that the timing and coordination of these projects are critical for their alignment with Ministerial meetings in Spring and Autumn 1974. The document provides a detailed list of predicted availability for each topic, noting that many depend on the input of ad hoc study groups or NATO Military Authorities. The proposed agenda for the Spring 1974 meeting includes illustrative studies, politico-military strategy, and a briefing on the strategic balance, while the Autumn 1974 meeting may cover the Phase II Report, communicating NATO's intentions, and the implementation of Special ADM guidelines. The Chairman concludes by observing that the timely receipt of inputs from external producers exerts a significant influence on the final make-up of the Ministerial agendas.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME
Note by the Chairman
During the past weeks the question of the future work programme of the NPG has
been discussed on several occasions by the Staff Group as well as by the
Permanent Representatives at their meeting on 4th October. These discussions
have concentrated to a large extent on identifying the individual subjects that
might be covered in the future NPG work. No particular attempt has been made to
integrate the separate projects into an overall pattern of activity and to
relate such a pattern to specific future Ministerial meetings, although some
aspects of timing have been mentioned in connection with individual topics.
Since any consideration of the future NPG work programme must necessarily take
into account its relationship to Ministerial meetings, it is suggested that it
might be useful for the Staff Group to discuss some aspects of timing and
coordination at its next meeting on Monday, 15th October. The comments in the
following paragraphs are intended to set the stage for such a discussion.

5. As a final observation, it might be noted that every topic in the above list,
except Communicating NATO's Intentions and Analysis of Deterrence (on which
procedural decisions are still pending), involves an input from an ad hoc study
group or from the NATO Military Authorities. The question of whether a topic
will appear on a specific Ministerial agenda is critically dependent on the
timely receipt of these inputs, for which deadlines are rarely established by
the NPG. As a result, the producers of these inputs exert a significant, if not
controlling, influence on the make-up of Ministerial agendas.

(signed) H.B. SEIM.
