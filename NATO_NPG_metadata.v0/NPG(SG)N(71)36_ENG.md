> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 15th March, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on March 15, 1971, to coordinate preparations for the upcoming May Ministerial meeting. The group discussed the issuance of official invitations to SACEUR and SACLANT, and reviewed national participation in NATO nuclear planning, concluding that current procedures were satisfactory for most members. The United States representative provided updates on the handling of follow-on studies, noting that US authorities had agreed to participate in preparing brief, issue-highlighting papers for Ministers. The meeting also covered the draft agenda for the May Ministerial meeting and a draft advance press release, with the deadline for objections being May 3rd. Additionally, the group discussed illustrative studies and sample release requests, inviting the International Staff to prepare a summary of options. The group scheduled future meetings of Permanent Representatives and the Staff Group, and briefly noted a false alarm on the NORAD circuit. The meeting was attended by representatives from Canada, Germany, Greece, Italy, Italy, Netherlands, Norway, United Kingdom, United States, Belgium, and Denmark.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 15th March, 1971

INVITATIONS TO THE MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS IN MAY

1. The STAFF GROUP :

took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that official invitations to
SACEUR and SAOLANT to the Ministerial meeting in May from the Chairman of the
Nuclear Planning Group would be sent during the coming week; he recalled that
the Chairman of the Military Committee had a standing invitation.

NATIONAL PARTICIPATION IN NATO NUCLEAR PLANNING

2. The STAFF GROUP :

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Staff Group
had discussed the question of national participation in NATO nuclear planning at
the meeting held on 15th February, and had agreed that representatives, using
the International Staff’s paper (1), should obtain instructions from their
authorities as to whether the paper, either in its present form or modified,
should be sent officially to capitals;

(b) noted further remarks by the Chairman who said that it was necessary to
distinguish between the procedures themselves and the manner in which they
functioned;

(c) noted that a large majority of the Staff Group, having received instructions

THE HANDLING OF STUDIES ON FOLLOW-ON USE

3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the United States representative who referred to
the discussion held at the previous meeting concerning what sort of paper, if
any, should be prepared for Ministers by the Staff Group or the NPG on the
subject of issues emerging from the follow-on studies;

(b) noted in this statement that the United States representative had informed
his authorities in Washington of the strong views of the Staff Group in this
matter and that the United States Authorities had now agreed to participate in
preparing a paper;

(c) further noted that the United States Authorities hoped that the paper would
be prepared along the following lines :

- it should be brief,
- it should high-light issues which emerged clearly from the Study or studies in
question,

- there should be no conclusions or recommendations unless already made in the
studies themselves,

- the form of the paper should be such as to facilitate and stimulate
ministerial discussion,

- it should be available for review by capitals before being approved by
Permanent Representatives.

(d) noted that the longer term question of the preparation of guidelines afte :
all the studies had been received, and the formation of a multinational group to

(e) took note of a statement by the Chairman who expressed, on behalf of the

AGENDA FOR THE MEETING OF MINISTERS IN MAY

4. The STAFF GROUP :

(a) recalled that on 9th February it had discussed a draft agenda for the NPG
Ministerial meeting (1) and had decided to re-examine the question in mid-March;

(b) discussed the agenda, agreed to a number of amendments, and decided to re-
open the questic. at a future meeting with a view to circulating a new version
for submission to capitals before the Easter Holidays.

(c) decided that if no objections to the text had been received by close of

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES AND SAMPLE RELEASE REQUESTS
6. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who described the material received from
SACEUR (1) which was already circulated and that received from SACLANT which
would be circulated (2) during the next few days; he drew attention to the
Canadian proposal(3) for a briefing for Ministers on release procedures, and
invited the Staff Group to discuss what could usefully be presented and in what
manner to Ministers in the short time available at their meeting;

(b) discussed in detail the various aspects of the problem, taking note of the
views expressed and in particular of the following points :

(1) the subject matter should be prepared and presented in such a way as to
stimulate Ministerial discussion;

(11) to permit full and fruitful consideration, it would probably be necessary
to defer discussion of some of the material to later meetings.

(c) invited the International Staff to prepare a paper before Friday 19th March
summarising the various options which had been discussed, and proposing possible
questions;

(d) agreed to continue the discussion at the next meeting of the Staff Group.
NEXT MEETINGS OF PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
7. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note that a meeting of Permanent Representatives should be
provisionally foreseen for 19th or 20th April with a view to hearing the
presentation of the NORTHAG Area Study and/or discussing Illustrative Studies
and Release Procedures;

(b) agreed to reserve Thursday 29th April for a meeting of Permanent
Representatives to attend the presentation of the Macedonia and Western Thrace
Area Study.
