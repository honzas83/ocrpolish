> [!abstract] 
> # Downgrading or Destruction of Selected NPG Documents and Related Working Papers
> 
> This document is a notice from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) regarding the review and authorized downgrading of documents issued between 1967 and 1970. Following a review of NPG documents, the NPG countries agreed to downgrade a number of documents, and the Head of the NATO Registry has notified holders of these actions. The notice further explains that a review of drafts and working papers associated with these documents has been conducted to identify additional papers for downgrading, which are listed in the attached Annex. Staff Group members are advised to verify that copies of documents listed in the Annexes to NPG/N(74)2 and this notice have been appropriately downgraded in their possession and are encouraged to destroy copies of documents they no longer need. The document is signed by H.B. Seim and originated from Brussels, Belgium. The Annex provides a detailed list of documents from 1967, 1968, and 1969, including their original classification levels (such as COSMIC TOP SECRET, NATO SECRET, and NATO CONFIDENTIAL) and their new, downgraded classifications (such as NATO UNCLASSIFIED or NATO RESTRICTED).

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
DOWNGRADING OR DESTRUCTION OF SELECTED NPG DOCUMENTS AND RELATED WORKING PAPERS
Note by the Chairman
As a result of a recent review of NPG documents issued in 1967, 1968, 1969 and
1970, it was proposed(1) that a number of documents be downgraded, to which the
NPG countries subsequently agreed. Holders of the documents were then
notified(2) of the authorised downgrading by the Head of the NATO Registry.

2. As a related matter, a review of the various drafts and working papers
circulated in connection with the preparation of the NPG documents for which a
downgrading action was authorised has been undertaken to identify additional
papers that may also be downgraded. These documents are listed at Annex,
together with the revised classifications that have been authorised by the
originators. It is expected that the Head of the NATO Registry will also notify
holders of these documents of this downgrading action.

3. Staff Group members may wish to verify that copies of the documents listed in
Annexes to NPG/N(74)2 and to this NOTICE which may be in their possession have
been appropriately downgraded. It is also suggested that Staff Group members may
wish to destroy copies of these documents for which they have no further need.

1110 Brussels.
(Signed) H.B. SEIM.
This document consists of one cover page and an Annex of 17 pages.
