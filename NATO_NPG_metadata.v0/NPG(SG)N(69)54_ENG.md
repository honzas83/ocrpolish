> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters on Monday, 16th June, 1969
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on June 16, 1969, at NATO Headquarters to address several key nuclear planning issues. The group discussed the distribution of papers to the Nuclear Planning Group Advisory Committee (NDAC), noting that the Netherlands and Italy would provide copies of their respective papers on consultation and AHI's, and that the United States would clarify its intentions regarding the distribution of its consultation paper. Additionally, the group addressed long-term studies related to the Anglo-German guidelines paper, focusing on the development of draft terms of reference for the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons and NATO responses to Warsaw Pact initial use. The group reached a consensus that the United States should prepare these draft terms of reference by July 15. Regarding the ADI problem, the group invited the International Staff to prepare a re-draft of the terms of reference for further analysis in selected areas of the Allied Command Europe (ACR). The meeting also touched upon the role of theatre nuclear strike forces, noting a request for comments from countries and the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) by August 1, to ensure the report be ready by mid-October. Finally, the group agreed to circulate articles of interest from journals and newspapers on nuclear matters and scheduled its next meeting for June 3, 1969.

DISTRIBUTION OF NFG PAPERS TO NDAC : EIBERS
1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman regarding the distribution to NDAC
members of NPG papers originating

(b) noted that the Netherlands authorities would arrange
for copies of their collation of answers on consultation(1)
to be made available to NDAC members; and that the Italian authorities would do
the same for their paper on AHI’s (2);

(c) noted that the United States representative would find out
the intentions of his authorities regarding further
distribution of their paper on consultation (3);
(d) noted that when the United States reply had been received,
the Chairman would then send to all members of the Staff
Group for information a list of documents issued to
NDAC members.

LONGER TERM STUDIES RELATED TO THE ANGLO-GERMAN GUIDELINES PAPER (1)
2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman regarding the development of draft terms
of reference for the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons as well as NATO
responses to Warsaw Pact initial use;

(b) noted that, because of the importance of the problem involved, these terms
of reference would have a major influence on the future long-term activities of
the Staff Group;

(c) noted a general consensus that the United States should prepare draft terms
of reference by 15th July for the consideration of the Staff Group; the United
States representative was unsure of the degree to which he could comply with
this request.

FURTHER ANALYSES OF THE ADI PROBLEM:
3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) recalled that the Permanent Representatives, at their meeting on 18th March,
1969, had instructed the Staff Group to prepare draft terms of reference for the
conduct by the NATO military Authorities in collaboration with host countries of
further analyses of the ADI problem in certain selected areas of ACR; and that
this instruction had been reaffirmed at the meeting of the Ministers in London
(2);

(b) took note of draft terms of reference prepared by the International Staff
(3);

(c) invited the International Staff, in the light of the comments made in the
discussion, to prepare a re-draft of the terms of reference, for discussion at
the next meeting.

THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES
4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) recalled that the Ministers in London had invited (1) MPG members to comment
on the report on the rôle of theatre nuclear strike forces (1) for which the
United States and Germany were the Discussion Leaders;

(b) noted a request by the discussion leaders that comments should be received
from Countries and from SACWUR by 1st August, not 5th September as originally
planned, so that the report could be made ready by mid-October.

CIRCULATION OF ARTICLES OF INTEREST FROM JOURNALS AND NEWSPAPERS
5. The STAFF GROUP :
noted a statement by the Chairman who offered to copy and circulate to the Staff
Group articles on nuclear matters which members might wish to bring to the
attention of the Group.

NEXT MEETING
6. The STAFF GROUP :
agreed to hold its next meeting on Sunday, 3rd June, 1969, at 2.45 p.m.
