> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Friday, 10th October, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on October 10, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key administrative and strategic items. The group discussed the timing and agenda for future Autumn ministerial meetings, with a majority agreeing to further discussion on October 15. Regarding the communication of NATO's intentions, the group reviewed and agreed to changes in the latest version of a paper on warning messages, instructing the International Staff to revise it for approval by Permanent Representatives on October 29. Additionally, the group considered communicating NATO's intentions (CNI) procedures, noting a consensus to treat these procedures separately from warning messages and postponing further preparation until after the Autumn Ministerial meeting. The meeting also covered logistical arrangements for a meeting in Hamburg, including hotel accommodation and transportation, as presented by the German representative. Finally, the group scheduled its next meeting for October 13, 1975.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTIONS - MESSAGES OF WARNING

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) discusses the latest version(1) in the light of amendments of paragraphs 11
to 13 and of suggestions made by members; agreed to a number of changes. ..

(b) invited the International Staff in the light of the discussion to revise the
paper and to issue it in the form of an NPG working paper for examination in
Capitals and approval by Permanent Representatives at their meeting on 29th
October.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTIONS - PROCEDURES

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman on CNI procedures and of a paper on
this matter handed out by the Canadian representative;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and of a growing
consensus in favour of considering CNI procedures separately from CNI warning
messages;

(c) agreed that it might be useful to postpone the preparation of a paper on CNI
procedures until after the Autumn Ministerial meeting when it would be possible
to take into account ministerial discussion on messages of warning;

(d) agreed to continue the discussion of this matter at a future meeting.
