> [!abstract] 
> # NPG Staff Group Visit to SHAPE
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group regarding a prospective visit to Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE) in Casteau. The proposed programme includes briefings from the Operations Division on the selective tactical use of nuclear weapons during conflict situations and briefings from the Plans and Policy Division on Special Air-Delivered Munitions (ADM) guidelines, including a refresher film on technical and military aspects of ADMs. The schedule suggests an arrival at 10:00 a.m., briefings until 12:15 p.m., and lunch with Major General Stanley C. Waters, the Assistant Chief of Staff, Plans and Policy. The Chairman, H. B. Seim, suggests that the visit take place in the second half of June 1971, specifically proposing dates such as the 16th, 18th, 23rd, 25th, or 30th of June to avoid conflicts with Ministerial meetings. The matter is proposed for examination at the next meeting on Monday, 26th April 1971, to finalize arrangements with SHAPE. Transportation from NATO Headquarters to SHAPE is anticipated to be provided via bus.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

NPG STAFF GROUP VISIT TO SHAPE

Note by the Chairman

During recent informal discussions with SHAPE concerning the prospective NPG
Staff Group visit to Casteau, SHAPE officers have suggested a programme designed
to contribute some background information relevant to two general areas of
current interest to the Staff Group:

(a) How questions of selective tactical use of nuclear weapons would be handled
at SHAPE during a conflict situation;

(b) Some steps SHAPE is taking in connection with the Special ADM Guidelines(1).

On the first topic, the Group would be briefed by officers of the Operations
Division, and would also visit the SHAPE Operations Center (SHOC). The Group
would be briefed on the second point by officers of the Plans and Policy
Division, after an initial refresher film on the technical and military aspects
of ADMs. Opportunity would be provided in both phases for questions to be posed
and for general discussion to take place.

2. With respect to the time schedule, it is suggested that the Group arrive at
SHAPE at 10.00 a.m., that the foregoing briefings and discussions continue to
12.15 a.m., that the Group then proceed to the Officers Club for lunch with
Major General Stanley C. Waters, the Assistant Chief of Staff, Plans and Policy,
presiding, and that the Group depart from SHAPE at 14.00 p.m. It is anticipated
the arrangements would be made for bus transportation from NATO Headquarters to
SHAPE and return.

1110 Brussels.

3. Regarding timing of a visit, it is recognized that, due to the various
forthcoming Ministerial meetings, Staff Group members are heavily committed up
through late May and early June. It is therefore suggested that the Group select
a suitable date in the second half of June for a visit. Selection of a Wednesday
or Friday morning would appear to interfere least with usual activities at NATO
Headquarters, so the Group may wish to look first at 16th, 18th, 23rd, 25th or
30th June.

4. I propose that we examine this matter at our next meeting on Monday, 26th
April, so that final arrangements with SHAPE can be made.

(Signed) H. B. SEIM
