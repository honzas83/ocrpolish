> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 23rd August, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on August 23, 1971, to address several key administrative and strategic issues. The group discussed invitations for the October meeting of NPG Ministers, agreeing that the Secretary General would invite SACEUR and SACLANT. They reviewed regional follow-on studies, specifically the Turkish Study and the CENTAG AREA Study, and agreed that military comments from the Major NATO Commander and the Chairman of the Military Committee should be included. Regarding the Central European Tactical Air Group (CENTAG) AREA Study, the German Representative noted that approval from Bonn was expected shortly. The group also deliberated on the draft agenda for the October Ministerial meeting, with conflicting views from the United States and the United Kingdom regarding specific items such as AD 70 follow-up action and progress reports on follow-on use studies. They further decided to maintain the yearly meeting of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) in December, with the main agenda item being a report by the NPG to the NDAC. They reviewed a draft report on the illustrative study on Maritime Use in the Mediterranean, but postponed further discussion due to the time of circulation. Finally, the group addressed unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence, agreeing to a number of changes to a revised paper and requesting the International Staff to prepare a list of the agreed changes. The next meeting was scheduled for August 30, 1971, in Brussels.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 23rd August, 1971
INVITATIONS TO THE MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS IN OCTOBER
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of, and concurred with, a statement by the Chairman who said that he
would prepare letters for signature by the Secretary General inviting SACEUR and
SACLANT to the meeting of NPG Ministers in October; he recalled that the
Chairman of the Military Committee had a standing invitation.

REGIONAL FOLLOW-ON STUDIES
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Turkish
Study(1) had recently been circulated and that the CENTAG AREA Study(2) would be
circulated very soon; he asked the Staff Group whether the military authorities
should be formally invited to comment on these studies; and suggested that it
would be useful for Ministers to have military comments in addition to those of
Permanent Representatives;

(b) took note of views expressed and in particular those of the United States
Representative who said that it would be useful to have military comments on
both studies and suggested that these should come from the most concerned Major
NATO Commander and, on a personal basis, from the Chairman of the Military
Committee;

MEETING OF NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE IN DECEMBER
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to the meeting of the
NDAC which took place every December, and asked the Staff Group whether this
meeting was necessary, and if so, what matters should be placed on the Agenda;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion; and in

through the NDAC to the DPC;

(c) agreed that:
(1) it was desirable to maintain the yearly meeting of the NDAC;
(ii) the main agenda item should be a report by the NPG to the NDAC on the
principal activities of the year;

(111) a report on AD 70 Follow-on action would not be sent through the NDAC to
the DPC.
