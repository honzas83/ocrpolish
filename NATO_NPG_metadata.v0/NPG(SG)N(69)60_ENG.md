> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters on 29th August, 1969
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters on 29th August 1969 to address several key agenda items. The group discussed Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs) and agreed that wording for a footnote in the terms of reference should be resolved between Greek and United States authorities before being sent to the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR). Regarding the upcoming NPG Ministerial meeting in Washington on 11th and 12th November, the group agreed on the general content and form of the draft agenda, specifically adjusting the title of Item 1.1 to match the Anglo-German paper and modifying Item 1.2 to begin with 'Follow-on tactical use'. The International Staff, with cooperation from the United States Delegation, were tasked with preparing a first draft of terms of reference for future work on follow-on tactical use. Under Item III (Consultation), the group identified specific documents from the United States, Belgium, and the Netherlands for listing. Additionally, the group noted suggestions for the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) to meet in late November around the DPC Ministerial meeting and agreed on the draft agenda for the next NDAC meeting. Administrative arrangements for the Washington meeting at Airlie House were noted, including a request for attendee names by 14th September. Finally, the group scheduled the next meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives for 16th September and the next Staff Group meeting for 15th September 1969.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at NATO Headquarters on 29th August, 1969
ATOMIC DEMOLITION NUNITIONS
1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman regarding the draft terms of
reference for further work on ADMs (1), with particular reference to a footnote
to paragraph 3(a) of that Working Paper proposed by the Greek Authorities, with
an alternative phrase suggested by the United States authorities;

(b) agreed that the wording of the footnote should be agreed between the Greek
and United States authorities and that it should then be included in the terms
of reference which will be sent to SACEUR.

AGENDA FOR THE NPG .INISTERIAL MEETING TO BE HELD NEAR WASHINGTON ON 11TH AND
12TH NOVEMBER.

2. The WORKING GROUP :
(a) discussed the draft agenda (2) for the Washington meeting and agreed to its
general content and form;

(b) agreed that the wording of the title of Item I.I should correspond with the
title of the Anglo-German paper;
