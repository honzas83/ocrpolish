> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 26th January, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on January 26, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with a security clearance confirmation and an expression of appreciation for the German authorities' hospitality during the recent Ministerial Meeting in Hamburg. The group discussed the introduction of the Phase III Study, with the United States representative noting that a first draft report might be available by October 1976. There was a discussion on the report regarding CNI Messages of Warning, with some members feeling the report was sufficient, though the question of developing CNI procedures remained open. The group also addressed the Future Work Programme, emphasizing the need for close collaboration with military authorities for the Technology Study progress report and the presentation of new procedures for testing in WINTEX 77. An informal meeting was scheduled for January 28 to further discuss these presentations. Additionally, the group noted that the date and location of the next NPG Ministerial Meeting should be decided by Permanent Representatives by the end of February. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for February 2, 1976.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 26th
January, 1976

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:

took note that all persons present were cleared to the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

APPRCIACTION

2. The STAFF GROUP:

warmly concurred with the remarks of the Chairman who, on behalf of the Staff
Group, expressed his appreciation of the efficient arrangements made by the
German Authorities for the Ministerial Meeting in Hamburg and for the generous
hospitality received; he invited the German representative to convey to his
Authorities his sincere appreciation for all they had done, at all levels, to
make the meeting such a success.

INTRODUCTORY PHASE III TALKS IN HAMBURG

3. The STAFF GROUP:

took note of the remarks of the Chairman who referred to the successful and
useful introduction to the Phase III Study which had taken place in Hamburg
after the Ministerial Meeting; he looked forward

PRELIMINARY DISCUSSION OF THE HAMBURG AGREED MINUTE(1)

4. The STAFF GROUP:

----(a) took note of a statement-by the Chairman who referred. to the Agreed
Minute for-the Hamburg meeting and proposed to hold a preliminary examination of
the varicus decisions which had been taken with a view to assessing future work
for the NPG Staff Group;

----(b) examined the Agreed Minute in detail and took note of the views
expressed; noted in particular:

(i) with regard to Phase III, the United States representative expected that a
first draft report might be available in October 1976;

(ii) with regard to the report(2) on CNI Messages of Warning, there appeared to
be a general feeling that the report was sufficient and that no new guidance had
been given to develop it; the question as to whether there really was a need to
develop CNI procedures remained open;

(iii) with regard to the Future Work Programme, it would be necessary to ensure
very close collaboration with the Military Authorities when preparing the
progress report on the Technology Study (for consideration at the Spring
Ministerial Meeting) and the comprehensive presentation of newly developed
procedures for testing in WINTEX 77 (for Ministerial consideration at their
Autumn 1976 NPG meeting);

----(c) took note of a suggestion by the Chairman that an informal meeting of
members interested in the above mentioned comprehensive presentation should be
held in his office on 28th January at 10.30 a.m.; military participation in this
group was essential;
