> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters on Tuesday, 5th September, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters on September 5, 1972, to address several key administrative and strategic items. The group discussed documentation including SHAPE comments on the Denmark Study and draft reports on the Denmark and SACLAN Illustrative studies. Regarding the updating of NATO nuclear planning directives, the group examined amendments proposed by the United States and SHAPE concerning the introduction of Poseidon, agreeing that these changes were technical in nature and could be handled at the Permanent Representatives level. The group also revised the draft Ministerial agenda, removing items related to nuclear directives and the implementation of Special ADM guidelines. Additionally, the group reviewed the status of work in progress, including the ACLANT Study and the Air Battle Study, and discussed the classification of the Denmark Study as ATOMAL. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next meeting on September 7, 1972, to further discuss the revised Denmark Study report and the ACLANT Illustrative Study report.

(c) confirmed the agreement reached at their meeting on 30th August, 1972, that

STATUS OF WORK IN PROGRESS
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who said that, since the ACLANT Study was
practically finished, the Status Report required no annex; the Air Battle Study
was referred to in paragraph 5 of the Report, and Phase II activity was covered

circulate the revised text in the form of an NPG Working Paper for despatch to
Capitals and for subsequent examination by Permanent Representatives at their
meeting to be held on 29th September.

NEXT MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
5. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that, since the Denmark
Study had been classified to ATOMAL, the arrangements for the presentation to
the Permanent Representatives on Thursday, 14th September, would have also to be
ATOMAL; however he suggested that if the Study Team in their presentation and
the Permanent Representatives in the ensuing discussion could avoid mentioning
specific ATOMAL data, it would not be necessary to give ATOMAL classification to
notes, reports and cables referring to the presentation; he invited members to
brief their Permanent Representatives on the foregoing.
