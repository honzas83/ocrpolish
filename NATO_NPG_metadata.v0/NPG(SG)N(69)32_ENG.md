> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters on Monday 31st March, 1969
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened at NATO Headquarters on March 31, 1969, to address several key strategic issues. The group noted that representatives from Canada and the United Kingdom had submitted a joint outline paper on the role of theatre nuclear strike forces, which requires further study and review by SHAPE, IMS, and the United Kingdom. The meeting also addressed the Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADM) Time Factors Study, noting that the final draft had been circulated and agreeing to delay the final issuance until April 8, 1969. Additionally, the group suggested that the Italian representative could use the current draft of the ADM study for the purposes of the Italian Study. The document concludes by scheduling future meetings for April 3, April 8, and April 10, 1969, to continue discussions on theatre nuclear strike forces and NATO's response to a potential initial tactical use of nuclear weapons by the Warsaw Pact.

Notes of a meeting held at NATO Headquarters on

THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES
1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted that the Canadian and United Kingdom representative,
in accordance with their proposal made at the last meeting
of the Staff Group, had prepared and circulated a fuller
version of their joint outline paper;
(b) agreed that more time was required to study this paper,
and decided to discuss it at the next meeting of the
Staff Group;
(c) noted that in the meantime the SHAPE, IMS and United
Kingdom representatives would examine the paper with
a view to checking facts, figures and the security
grading.
ATOMIC DEMOLITION NUNTITION TIME FACTORS
Document : NPG(Staff Group)WP(69)5-(NPG/D(69)1(Final Draft))
2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted that the final draft of the ADM Time Factors Study
had been circulated and that it had been proposed to
issue this document as the final version if no comments
were received by the evening of 2nd April;
(b) agreed to wait until 8th April before issuing the final
version;
(c) nevertheless suggested to the Italian representative
that the paper in its present form was unlikely to be
changed and therefore could be used immediately for
the purposes of the Italian Study.

FUTURE MEETINGS
3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) Thursday, 3rd April, 1969, at 10 a.m., with a view to discussing the rôle of
theatre nuclear strike forces, and in particular the paper prepared by the
Canadian and United Kingdom representatives;

(b) Tuesday, 8th April, 1969, at 2.45 p.m., to continue the discussion on the
rôle of theatre nuclear strike forces, and any other business;

(c) Thursday, 10th April, 1969, at 10 a.m. and 2.45 p.m., to discuss NATO's
response to Warsaw Pact's initial tactical use of nuclear weapons.
