> # Notes of a Meeting Held in NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 13th November, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on November 13, 1972, in Brussels to prepare for the upcoming Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) meeting scheduled for December 6, 1972. The meeting's agenda for the NDAC included a report on NPG activities during 1972 and the updating of Nuclear Planning Directives. The Staff Group discussed the participation of members at NPG Ministerial Meetings, noting a general feeling that the problem should not be approached with haste. They also reviewed a draft report on NPG activities for 1972, which was to be issued as an NPG Working Paper. Preparations for the Spring 1973 NPG Ministerial Meeting in Turkey were discussed, including the suggested principal tasks and a suggested timetable. The group also explored the possibility of a visit to the United States, with the United States Representative stating that Washington would welcome such a visit. Additionally, the the group noted the arrangements for a farewell luncheon for Colonel J.I. Dawson. Finally, the group agreed to hold its next meeting on November 20, 1972.

Notes of a meeting held in NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 13th
November, 1972

MEETING OF THE NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE – 6TH DECEMBER, 1972

Agenda

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that the NDAC would meet in Ministerial Session at 9.30 a.m. on
Wednesday, 6th December, 1972, just previous, as is customary, to the meeting of
the Defence Planning Committee, also in Ministerial Session;

(b) agreed that the Agenda should be as follows:
(i) Report on NPG Activities during 1972,
(ii) Up-dating of Nuclear Planning Directives,
(iii) Any other business.

Documentation

2. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that the following documentation was being prepared for the NDAC meeting:
(a) NDAC/D(72)1: This is the report on NPG activities during 1972 (see paragraph
6 below);

(b) NDAC/D(72)2: This document, to be circulated shortly, refers to the proposed
changes (contained in Cosmic Top Secret Document NPG/D(72)17) to the Concept for
the role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces (DPC/D(70)59);

Attendance

3. The STAFF GROUP:

were being sent to the Major NATO Commanders; the Chairman of the Military
Committee had a standing invitation.

AGREED MINUTE OF THE LONDON MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Agreed Minute
of the London Meeting of NPG Ministers had not yet been issued because the
wording of two paragraphs proposed for insertion at the last minute, had not
been finalised; he hoped that he would soon have agreement from the seven
countries concerned; this would permit immediate circulation of the Agreed
Minute whose reference would be NPG/D(72)18;

(b) took note of a statement by the United States Representative describing the
background to the statement by Secretary Laird which had given rise to the
proposal for an insertion in the Agreed Minute of a paragraph concerning the
need to maintain conventional strength within the Alliance;

(c) discussed possible action leading on from the paragraph on enlarged
participation at NPG Ministerial Meetings proposed by the Danish Defence
Minister (see paragraph 5 below).

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussion held in
the Staff Group at its last meeting and reviewed what had been said concerning
the advantages, disadvantages and possible repercussions of changing the present

SPRING 1973 NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced a paper(1) issued by
the International Staff setting out suggested principal tasks that the Staff
Group might wish to undertake, together with a suggested timetable at amex, in
preparing for the NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting to be held in Turkey in May,
1973; he invited comments upon the various suggestions made in the paper;

(b) took note of the views expressed and the suggestions made during the
discussion;

(c) agreed upon the provisional approach to the Agenda proposed in the paper;
(d) bearing in mind that the Military Authorities were preparing two studies
connected with Unresolved Problems(2), agreed that no illustrative study would
be requested for this meeting;

(e) invited the Chairman to initiate a request to the Chairman of the Military
Committee that the NATO Military Authorities should be asked to prepare comments
upon the ACLANT Follow-on Study;

(f) agreed to consider whether a presentation of the ACLANT Study by the Study
Team would be desirable, and if so, to confirm this requirement at a future
meeting;

(g) with regard to Consultation, agreed to give further thought to the matter

POSSIBLE STAFF GROUP VISIT TO THE UNITED STATES
8. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled that about a year ago the
Staff Group had begun to explore the possibility of a visit to the United
States; this had not been possible last Summer; the Staff Group might however
wish to re-open the question along the lines set out in the International
Staff's note(1) which proposed, inter alia, a possible period during which the
visit might take place, and raised the question of transport arrangements;

(b) noted with appreciation the remarks of the United States Representative who
said that his authorities would welcome a visit from the Staff Group;

(c) noted with appreciation the remarks of the Canadian and German
Representatives who said that they would check with their authorities the
possibility of providing transatlantic air transportation for the Staff Group;

(d) expressed the hope that other countries might also be able to provide
transportation and invited the International Staff at the same time to check on
possible arrangements for civilian transportation within the United States;

(e) agreed to examine the matter further at a future meeting.
