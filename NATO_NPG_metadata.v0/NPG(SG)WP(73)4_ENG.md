> # Draft Permanent Representatives' Report on Demonstrative Use Illustrative Study
> 
> This document is a draft report by the Permanent Representatives of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) regarding a SHAPE illustrative study on the demonstrative use of nuclear weapons in a hypothetical wartime scenario in North Norway. The report identifies areas for Ministerial discussion concerning the application of Provisional Political Guidelines for the Initial Defensive Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons by NATO. The hypothetical scenario describes a Warsaw Pact invasion of North Norway through the Norwegian-Soviet border and northern Finland, where SACEUR proposes the use of a small number of medium yield nuclear weapons against a Warsaw Pact amphibious force to prevent the loss of the region. The document emphasizes that illustrative studies are intended to provide a framework for dialogue between political and military authorities and are not intended to be forecasts of actual crises or pre-decisions on future actions. It further discusses the balance between psychological impact and military utility in the demonstrative use of nuclear weapons, noting that de-emphasizing military utility might be perceived as a sign of weakness by the enemy. The report was prepared for the NPG Staff Group to facilitate a preliminary discussion before a Ministerial meeting in Ankara.

3. The Study under consideration describes a hypothetical wartime situation in
which Warsaw Pact forces have invaded north Norway across the Norwegian-Soviet
border and through northern Finland. NATO forces on the scene are conducting a
stubborn defence pending arrival of external reinforcements. However, a Warsaw
Pact amphibious assault force threatens to make a landing in the rear of NATO
defence forces and to link up with the Warsaw Pact forces attacking overland.
This could result in the loss of north Norway before external reinforcements
become available to assist in the defence. In the circumstances, SACEUR proposes
the use of a small number of medium yield nuclear weapons against the Warsaw
Pact amphibious force. Neither NATO nor the enemy have used nuclear weapons.
This Study differs from other illustrative studies previously considered by the
Ministers by virtue of the particular emphasis it places on the demonstrative
character of the envisaged initial use.

4. In general, illustrative studies are intended to provide examples of the
analytical and procedural processes by which a Major NATO Commander might reach
a decision to request the selective use of nuclear weapons. The Ministerial
discussion of such studies helps political authorities to become familiar with
the broad implications of proposed courses of action, the kinds of decisions
that might have to be taken and the implications

9. The Political Guidelines note that the initial tactical use of nuclear
weapons, of which demonstrative use is one example, has both political and
military elements and that the emphasis accorded to the military content will
vary with the situation and the aim to be achieved(1). The use proposed in the
SHAPE Study illustrates one way in which the political and military
considerations might be combined. Within the same scenario, however, a range of
other alternatives are conceivable, in which the emphasis given to military
utility would be different. For example, it cannot be excluded that the
psychological aim of demonstrative use could be achieved by employing fewer
weapons of lower yield to attack the Warsaw Pact amphibious force, despite the
decrease in military effectiveness. In this regard, however, it is important to
recognise that decreased emphasis on the military aspects of tactical use will
not necessarily lead to a gain in political and psychological impact on the
enemy. On the contrary, de-emphasis of military utility might very well
accentuate the risk mentioned in the Political Guidelines that the enemy would
find such demonstrative use unimpressive or would take it as a sign of weakness,
especially if the military situation was deteriorating fast(2).

Implications of the Recommended Use
10. In his ROMEO message, SACEUR states that since the requested use of nuclear
weapons would be purely defensive, outside the Warsaw Pact territory and would
not involve civilian casualties, it would be less escalatory than any other
options available which are relevant to the situation(1). Similar observations
were made in all the illustrative and follow-on use studies dealing with use at
sea previously addressed by the Ministers. In assessing the most likely Warsaw
Pact responses, the SHAPE Study suggests that the enemy might cease the
aggression or continue to fight conventionally and that there would appear to be
little advantage to the Warsaw Pact in using nuclear weapons in the area(2).
While this might be true on military grounds, it cannot be ruled out that
political factors or a misinterpretation of NATO's intent might lead the Warsaw
Pact to a different decision.

SHAPE Study is worthy of attention when compared with maritime use discussed in
other NPG studies already considered by the Ministers(3). In the previous cases
examined, NATO use of nuclear weapons in defence of naval forces at sea

Timing Considerations
12. The release request (ROMEO) message in the SHAPE Study indicates that a
release authorisation should be received by SACEUR less than seven hours after
the message had been released at SHAPE. Under these circumstances, it would be
unrealistic to expect that political authorities would have more than about five
hours time for consultation after receipt of the ROMEO message. However, in the
given hypothetical situation SACEUR has originated a warning (WHISKEY) message
about six and a half hours before sending the release request itself, which
might permit political authorities to begin the consultation process earlier.
The potential value of the warning message will depend on the extent to which it
provides information needed for effective political consultation.

Information Requirements for Consultation
13. Though in the Study under consideration, prepared about two years ago, the
only information available to political authorities appears to be that included
in the WHISKEY and . ROMEO messages, it can be taken that in an actual crisis
more information would certainly be made available by other intelligence means
and by Major NATO Commanders' assessments, as the more recent illustrative
studies have clearly shown.

IV. RECOMMENDATIONS
14. It is recommended that the Ministers:
