> # NPg Future Work
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, addresses the question of future study activities for the NPG. It references several tasks identified in PO/76/116, the Agreed Minutes of recent Ministerial meetings, NPG/D(76)8, and the MIT report NPG/Study/47. The Chairman notes that current resources at SHAPE are fully committed to the Dual-Capable Aircraft and NICE HERCULES studies, while NATO delegations are fully committed to the Phase III effort and the New Technology Study. Due to the current high workload, the Chairman suggests that the group concentrate on completing current work in a timely manner for the next six months. However, to plan for the future, Staff Group members are requested to list tasks requiring NPG Permanent Representative or Ministerial attention on an attached form, including timing and priorities. These forms must be delivered to the Chairman by November 12, 1976, for discussion at the first meeting following the London Ministerial.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
NPg FUTURE WORK
Note by the Chairman

At the NPG Staff Group meeting on 25th October, 1976, the question of future
study activities of the NPG was raised. Paragraph 5 of PO/76/116 lists certain
tasks which could be under- taken in the future. Paragraphs 7 and 9 of the same
FO point to other areas where studies may be necessary. Most of these study
tasks have been identified in the Agreed Minutes of recent Ministerial meetings,
NPG/D(76)8 or in the MIT report(1). This being the case, the matter of defining
these tasks and agreeing to their priorities becomes a problem of special
interest to NPG members.

2. It is generally agreed that the two major studies now underway at SHAPE,
i.e., the Dual-Capable Aircraft and NICE HERCULES studies, commits fully their
resources in the near term.

3. It is also generally agreed that the Phase III effort and the New Technology
Study commits fully the resources capitals and NATO delegations can make
available now for outside study work.

4. This situation has caused some to observe that the NPG "plate" is full enough
for the moment and we should concentrate on ensuring that the work underway is
completed in a timely and satisfactory manner. This course of action does seem
most acceptable for the next six months or so. Nevertheless, some consideration
and discussion of the management of future work seems to be a reasonable task
for the Staff Group. As an aid to such deliberations, it is requested that Staff
Group members list on the attached form the tasks they consider require NPG
Permanent Representative or Ministerial attention and complete those portions of
the form dealing with timing and priorities. (This should be a useful exercise
for those countries participating in the Agreed Minute session in London.)

(1) NPG/Study/47, 1st October, 1976
