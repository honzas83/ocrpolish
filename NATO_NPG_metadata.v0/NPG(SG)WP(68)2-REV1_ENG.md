> # Draft Report by the Nuclear Planning Group
> 
> This draft report summarizes the general activities of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) during 1968, intended to provide background for the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) discussions on November 9, 1968. The report details that the NPG met twice at the ministerial level in The Hague and Bonn, and held 272 meetings of permanent representatives. It notes that decisions from these meetings were circulated to all committee members and that the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee ministers endorsed recommendations regarding Allied Command Europe (ACE) High deployment, ACE High deployment, national participation in operational nuclear planning, and NPG membership arrangements. The document further discusses consultations on the use of nuclear weapons, noting that the subject remains a permanent agenda item for the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee and is being studied by the NPG. Work on this subject is proceeding based on memoranda from the Netherlands and Germany, including host country arrangements. The report concludes by inviting the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee to take note of the report and invite the NPG to continue its studies and submit recommendations.

Draft Report by the Nuclear Planning Group

This report summarises the general activities of the Nuclear Planning Group
during 1968, in order to keep the Nuclear Defence A Fair's Committee up to date
and to provide a background to its discussions on 9th November 1968.

I. MEETINGS

2. The NEG has met twice at ministerial level: in The Hague on 18th and 19th
April, and in Bonn on 10th and 11th October. The next ministerial meeting is
scheduled for May 1969; on the invitation of the United Kingdom government, it
will take place in London. In addition, there have been 272 meetings at the
level of permanent representatives.

II. MATTERS UNDER DISCUSSION BY MINISTERS

3. The decisions taken by NEG Ministers at their meetings in The Hague and in
Bonn have already been circulated to all members of the Committee(1) – for ease
of reference copies of these minutes are attached at Annexes "A" and "B".

4. At their meeting on 10th May 1969, the ND C Ministers endorsed certain
recommendations(2) put forward by the NEG Ministers at their meeting in The
Hague relating to ABL deployment, national participation in operational nuclear
planning and NEG membership arrangements. On this occasion there was however no
substantive discussion on the other matters raised in The Hague minute.

5. To aid discussion at the forthcoming meeting, the NEG Ministers primarily
concerned will give short background presentations, briefly summarising the main
points made in discussion at Bonn and which led up to the decisions that were
taken.

III. CONSULTATIONS ON THE USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS

6. At their meeting in December 1967, NDAC Ministers agreed that this subject
should remain permanently on their agenda(1). During their meeting in Bonn, it
will be noted that NG Ministers considered that the subject should continue to
be studied by the NEG; and instructed the NG Permanent Representatives to
continue their examination of this subject.

7. Work will proceed on the basis of issues raised in a memorandum by the
Netherlands and a note by the Secretary General. The subject of host country
arrangements on which memoranda had earlier been submitted by Germany and the
Netherlands will be subsumed under this item.

IV. RECOMMENDATIONS

8. The NDAC is invited to:

(a) take note of this report;

(b) invite the NG to continue its studies and submit recommendations as soon as
it is able to do so.
