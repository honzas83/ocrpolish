> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 29th November, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on November 29, 1971, to review several key documents and coordinate future activities. The group took note of reports on NPG activities in 1971, nuclear aspects of the AD 70 Follow-up Action, and a revised paper on the introduction of the POSEIDON weapon system. Additionally, the group received briefings on Crisis Management aspects in NATO, the responsibilities and functions of the NATO Situation Centre, and the activities of the IMS Nuclear Policy Team (NPT). The meeting included a detailed tour of the Situation Centre and a briefing on wartime procedures for handling nuclear messages. The group also discussed future work, emphasizing the need to reach conclusions on the Illustrative Study exercise by February 1, 1972, and identified the Air Study and Phase II of the Follow-on Work Programme as priorities for early consideration. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next meeting on December 3, 1971.

Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 29th November, 1971
DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the following
four documents which had recently been issued:

(a) NDAC/D(71)1, dated 23rd November, 1971. This is the report on NPG activities
in 1971 by the NPG Permanent Representatives to the NDAC. It is quoted under
Item I of the NDAC Ministerial Agenda;

(b) NDAC/D(71)2, dated 26th November, 1971. This is the report by the NPG to the
NDAC on the nuclear aspects of the AD 70 Follow-up Action. The NPG recommends
that the NDAC take note of the report and forward it to the DPG. This document
is quoted under Item II of the NDAC Ministerial Agenda;

(c) DPO/D(71)31, dated 26th November, 1971. This is the same report on the
nuclear aspects of the AD 70 Follow-up Action (see sub-paragraph (b) above), but
with a DPO covering letter. It is quoted under Item III in the DPC Ministerial
Agenda;

CRISIS MANAGEMENT AND THE NATO SITUATION CENTRE
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a briefing by the Director of the Council Operations Directors
(Colonel G.L. Vincent) on Crisis Management aspects in NATO;

(b) took note of a briefing by the Chief of the Situation Centre (Captain J.D.
Scull, USN) on the responsibilities, rôle and functions of the NATO Situation
Centre;

(c) took note of a briefing by the Chief of the IMS Nuclear Policy Team (NPT),
(Colonel P.E. Montoya US Air Force), on the activities and rôle of the NPT;

(d) took note of a briefing by a member of the Nuclear Policy Team (Colonel H.
Lombard, US Army) on the procedures which would be used in wartime by the NATO
Military Authorities, the Military Committee and the NPT for the handling of
nuclear messages;

(e) took note of the matters raised during the questions and answers period;
(f) took part in a conducted tour of the old and new parts of the Situation
Centre and took note of the explanations given by spokesmen in each section;

(g) warmly endorsed a statement by the Chairman who thanked all those who had
given the briefings and organised the visit to the Situation Centre.
