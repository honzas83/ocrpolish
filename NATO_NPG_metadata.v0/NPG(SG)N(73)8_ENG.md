> # Visits and Briefings in Which the NPG Staff Group Has Participated
> 
> This document, authored by H.B. Seim, serves as a record of the educational activities undertaken by the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group from 1968 to 1972. The purpose of these activities was to strengthen the group's competence and knowledge in nuclear affairs. The record includes a detailed chronological list of briefings and field trips to various facilities and sites. Key activities include briefings on the SENTINEL ABM system, war-gaming techniques, and the General Strike Plan. Visits were conducted to nuclear strike units in the Netherlands, German Pershing units, and the US National Military Command Center. The group also participated in briefings by the International Military Staff (IMS) and the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) and the Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic (SACLANT). Field trips to Germany, Canada, and the United States were included in some of the activities. The document concludes with a brief mention of initial steps for a visit to the United States in late 1971 and a briefing on WINTEX 73 in December 1972.

THE NPG STAFF GROUP HAS PARTICIPATED
Note by the Chairman
From its inception, the Staff Group has arranged for, or has taken advantage of,
various activities that could strengthen its knowledge and competence in nuclear
affairs. For the information of Staff Group members as background for discussion
of possible future activities of this nature, I have attached a list of visits
and briefings in which all or some of the Staff Group members have participated,
that have formed part of this educational effort.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM.
NATO, 1110 Brussels.
