> # Draft Advance Press Release for the Autumn 1972 Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, H.B. Seim, regarding a proposed advance press release for the Autumn 1972 Ministerial meeting. The meeting is scheduled to take place in London on October 26th and 27th, 1972, under the Chairmanship of NATO Secretary General Joseph Luns. The note requests that delegations obtain approval or comments from their national authorities by October 10th, 1972, on the text and timing of the release. It is suggested that the press release be issued in Brussels at 3 p.m. local time on Friday, October 20th, 1972, with simultaneous releases in national capitals. The draft press release, provided in both English and French, lists the member states of the Nuclear Planning Group, including Belgium, Denmark, Germany, Italy, Turkey, the United Kingdom, and the United States.

DRAFT ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE FOR THE
AUTUMN 1972 MINISTERIAL MEETING
Note by the Chairman
Attached is the text in English and French of a proposed advance press release
concerning the forthcoming NPG Ministerial meeting in London on 26th and 27th
October, 1972. The text closely follows the language used in press announcements
issued during the past two years just prior to Ministerial meetings. It is
suggested that the release be made in Brussels at 3 p.m. local time on Friday,
20th October, 1972 with simultaneous additional release in the capitals of those
countries who desire to do so.

2. Delegations concerned are requested to obtain approval, or comment, by their
national authorities by close of business, Tuesday, 10th October, 1972 on the
text of the announcement and the timing of the release. Information would also
be appreciated on whether national authorities plan to make a simultaneous
release in the capital.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM
NATO, Brussels. 1110 Brussels.

DRAFT ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE
As arranged at its previous meeting in Copenhagen in May, 1972, the Nuclear
Planning Group of NATO, currently consisting of Belgium, Denmark, Germany,
Italy, Turkey, the United Kingdom and the United States, at the invitation of
the British Government, hold its twelfth half-yearly Ministerial meeting on 26th
and 27th October, 1972, in London, under the Chairmanship of Mr. Joseph Luns,
Secretary General of NATO.
