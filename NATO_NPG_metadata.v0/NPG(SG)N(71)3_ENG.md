> # SACEUR Message Concerning Participation in WINTEX 71
> 
> This document consists of a notice from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group and a message from the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR), General Goodpaster, addressed to the defense ministries of various NATO member states. The message concerns the WINTEX 71 exercise, scheduled for 27 January to 4 February 1971, which replaces the biennial FALLEX series of exercises. Unlike previous FALLEX exercises, WINTEX 71 is designed to highlight the substantial role that political decisions, both national and NATO-wide, play in the application of strategy as laid down in MC 14/3, particularly during periods of rising tension and escalatory stages. General Goodpaster emphasizes that the exercise offers an opportunity for political and military authorities to evaluate peacetime planning and for political leaders to become more aware of the implications of military recommendations. He urges active participation through the exchange of information and intelligence, including national capitals, over the NATO-wide communications system. Finally, the SACEUR requests that national authorities advise him of their current plans or intentions regarding their level of participation in WINTEX 71 by 11 January 1971.

1. (NC) WINTEX 71, which replaces the biennial FALLEX SERIES OF
EXERCISES, IS SCHEDULED FOR THE PERIOD 27 JANUARY TO 4 FEBRUARY
1971. UNLIKE PAST FALLEX EXERCISES WHICH WERE DESIGNED PRIMARILY
TO TEST MILITARY PROCEDURES, WINTEX 71, ALTHOUGH STILL PRIMARILY
PROCEDURAL, HAS BEEN PURPOSELY DESIGNED TO INCLUDE INCIDENTS
WHICH HIGHLIGHT THE SUBSTANTIAL ROLE THAT POLITICAL DECISIONS
(NATIONAL AND NATO) PLAY IN THE APPLICATION OF THE STRATEGY AS
LAID DOWN IN MC 14/3, PARTICULARLY DURING PERIODS OF RISING
TENSION AND THROUGHOUT THE ESCALATORY STAGES SHOULD ESCALATORY
MEASURES BECOME NECESSARY.
2. (NS) WINTEX 71 OFFERS EXCELLENT OPPORTUNITIES FOR POLITICAL
AS WELL AS MILITARY AUTHORITIES TO EVALUATE THEIR PACETIME
PLANNING, AND FOR POLITICAL AUTHORITIES TO BECOME MORE AWARE OF
THE IMPLICATIONS OF THE COURSES OF ACTION WHICH MAY BE RECOMMENDED
BY MILITARY AUTHORITIES; AS HAS BEEN RECOGNIZED BY THE DPC AS
