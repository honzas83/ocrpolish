> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 6th May, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on May 6, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to review several key documents and administrative matters. The group discussed the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process, noting that proposed changes to a draft report would need to be referred to national authorities. They also addressed a study on the Warsaw Pact's politico-military strategy and military doctrine regarding the tactical use of nuclear weapons, agreeing to discuss the paper further at their next meeting. Logistical arrangements for the meeting of NPG Ministers in Norway were detailed, including cruise schedules and delegation lists. Additionally, the United States representative provided an update on US nuclear policy, stating that Secretary Schlesinger would likely discuss strategic re-targeting at a subsequent Defence Planning Committee Ministers meeting. The group concluded by scheduling their next meeting for May 13, 1974, to finalize working papers before the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives on May 16.

(b) PO/74/52, 6th May, 1974: Comments of the Chairman, Military Committee on the
Draft NPC Permanent Representatives Report on the Military Committee Function in

MILITARY COMMITTEE FUNCTION IN THE NUCLEAR CONSULTATION PROCESS

3.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a)  took note of the remarks by the Chairman who referred to the draft
report(1) on the Military Committee Function in the Nuclear Consultation Process
and the comments on it(2) by the Chairman, Military Committee;

(b)  noted that while the draft report, as it stood, was acceptable to all
members, the changes now proposed by the Chairman, Military Committee, would
have to be referred to National Authorities;

(c)  held a preliminary discussion of the comments of the Chairman, Military
Committee and took note of the views expressed;

(d)  agreed to obtain instructions with all speed and to re-open the discussion
at the next meeting with a view to preparing a corrigenum to NPG/WP(74)4 in time
for the Permanent Representatives meeting on 16th May, if appropriate.

STUDY OF WARSAW PACT POLITICO-MILITARY STRATEGY AND MILITARY DOCTRINE FOR THE
TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS

4.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a)  took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to a proposed
change(3) to paragraph 3 of the Annex to NPG/WP(74)5 (Warsaw Pact Strategy and
Doctrine);

(b)  noting that NPG/WP(74)5 had only recently been circulated to Capitals,
agreed to discuss the paper as a whole and the proposed change in particular, at
the next meeting of the Staff Group.

(2)  advance copies of PO/74/52, 6th May, 1974, had previously been made

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS IN NORWAY

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Norwegian representative who made the
following points:

(i) the mini-cruise would start at 3 p.m. on Monday, 10th June (not 1 p.m. as
previously stated),

(ii) Delegations should inform him of the time of arrival of their aircraft,
(iii) Final lists of delegation members and their wives should be sent to him
not later than 15th May;

(b) noted the remarks of the Chairman who reminded members that the
International Staff would be interested in spare places in delegation aircraft
on the outward, and particularly on the return, trips.

UNITED STATES NUCLEAR POLICY

6. The STAFF GROUP:
noted with appreciation a statement by the United States representative who said
that his authorities believed that Mr. Schlesinger would discuss strategic re-
targeting at the Meeting of Defence Planning Committee Ministers to be held in
Brussels two days after the meeting of NPG Ministers; this information would be
of particular interest to those countries not attending the Norway NPG meeting.

NEXT MEETING

7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 13th May, 1974, at 3 p.m.
(b) noted that this meeting, to be held three days before the meeting of NPG
