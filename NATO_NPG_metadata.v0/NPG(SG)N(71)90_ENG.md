> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Tuesday, 17th August, 1971: Special Atomic Demolition Munition Guidelines
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on August 17, 1971, to address several key nuclear policy issues. The group discussed the implementation of special political guidelines for the possible use of Special Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADM), noting a progress report by the Military Committee and a draft report for Permanent Representatives. The Staff Group agreed to finalize a version of the ADM guidelines paper by the end of August and decide by August 30 whether it should be presented to Ministers. The Additionally, the group reviewed the status of work in progress on Follow-on Studies, with the group agreeing to reopen the discussion on the progress report at the end of August. The Canadian Representative mentioned that his authorities were considering a new Phase I study. The meeting also focused on the draft agenda for the NPG Ministerial meeting in October, with the United Kingdom Representative urging that the agenda be limited to matters of special interest to Ministers. The group agreed to re-open the discussion on the agenda at the end of August after members obtain views from their respective authorities. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next meeting on August 23, 1971, in Brussels.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Tuesday, 17th August, 1971
SPECIAL ATOMIC DEMOLITION MUNITION GUIDELINES

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that Ministers had
given certain instructions regarding ADM matters at their meeting at Ottawa in
October 1970, drew attention to the progress report(1) by the Military Committee
on the implementation of the special political guidelines for the possible use
of ADMs, and introduced the International Staff's draft(2) for a possible report
by Permanent Representatives;

(b) noted the Chairman's suggestion that the Staff Group should arrive at an
agreed version of this paper by the end of August, that it should then be sent
to capitals for comment, and that a final version should be agreed by Permanent
Representatives towards the end of September;

(c) further noted the Chairman's view that it was unnecessary for this report to
be placed on the Agenda for the Ministerial meeting in October;

(d) took note, in the ensuing discussion, of some divergent views on whether the
report should be discussed by Ministers at their meeting and agreed to reach a
decision on the matter not later than Monday, 30th August;

(e) invited the International Staff, in the light of the views expressed upon
the form and content of the report, to prepare a revised text, set out in such a
manner that it could, if necessary, be changed easily into a report for
Ministers.

STUDIES ON FOLLOW-ON USE – STATUS OF WORK IN PROGRESS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the International
Staff's draft(1) of a possible report by Permanent Representatives on the status
of work in progress on the Follow-on Studies; noted also that this draft report
followed the lines of previous progress reports, and that the status reports on
individual studies giving the situation as at 15th September, to be annexed to
the main report, were required to be submitted by Study Teams not later than
17th September;

(b) noted that the Chairman considered that this progress report should be
included as an Agenda item for the Ministerial meeting in October; this view was
not shared by the United Kingdom Representative;

(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and of the
remarks of the Canadian Representative who said that his Authorities were
considering proposing a new Phase I study;

(d) agreed to re-open the discussion on this paper at the end of August.

(iii) a final version of the Agenda, agreed by Permanent Representatives, should
be ready by the end of September;

(b) took note of a statement by the United Kingdom Representative who urged
strongly that items on the Agenda should be confined to questions of special

(d) noted various views on the question of how the nuclear aspects of the AD 70

(e) took note of further suggestions and opinions expressed in the general
discussion;

(f) agreed to re-open the discussion at the end of August, inviting members in
the meantime to obtain the views of their Authorities on the content of the
Agenda and on the naming of discussion leaders.
