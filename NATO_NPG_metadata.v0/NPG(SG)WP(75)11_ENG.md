> # Arrangements for Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme
> 
> This document is a draft report by the Permanent Representatives of the NATO Planning Group (NPG) regarding the arrangements for Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme. It describes the goal of producing a consolidated statement of NATO's concept for the use of theatre nuclear weapons, integrating conclusions and guidance from various basic policy documents. The document details the scope of the scope of the work, including deterrence objectives, the role of the NATO triad, and the role of thelyre nuclear forces in the context of the Warsaw Pact. It outlines a comprehensive list of reference documents, including Ministerial Guidance 1975 and the overall strategic concept for the defence of the NATO area. It further specifies the following subjects to be covered: deterrence as the overall objective, the participation of countries in nuclear planning, the credibility of the NATO triad, and the possible levels of aggression from the Warsaw Pact. It also addresses measures to support the objectives of theatre nuclear weapons, such as declaratory policy and communication arrangements. The proposed method for implementation involves a small Phase III Working Group and regular interaction with the NPG Staff Group, allowing for contributions from all member nations and the International Secretariat. The document was drafted on 22 October 1975 and signed by J-P. Mitzel, Acting Chairman.

I. PURPOSE

This document covers scope, coverage and method for drawing' together the
consolidated statement of NATO's concept for the use of theatre nuclear weapons
as part of the NATO triad.

II. BACKGROUND

2. In November, 1969 the Nuclear Planning Group approved the Provisional
Political Guidelines for the Initial Defensive Tactical Use of nuclear weapons
by NATO(1) as policy guidance for national authorities and as a directive to
NATO military authorities. At the same meeting, Ministers recognised the need
for a study of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons, to examine the phases
of tactical use subsequent to initial use by NATO and to explore NATO responses
to an initial tactical use of nuclear weapons by the Warsaw Pact. Rather than
attempt this task in a single comprehensive study, they agreed to proposals put
forward by the Permanent Representatives which defined(2) the objectives,
general assumptions and broad structure of an overall work programme to be
carried out in a series of interrelated steps.

V. METHOD

9. As we see it, the two most important considerations with regard to organising
the Phase III work are the widespread interest in this work among IFC countries
and among NATO staffs and the desirability for the timely completion of the
effort.

10. The work should be handled on two levels:
(a) a small Phase III Working Group with representation from a few countries,
and a Major NATO Commander;

(b) regular working group interaction with the nPG Staff Group.

11. It is envisaged that the Phase III Working Group would meet at an early
stage with the Staff Group for a wide ranging discussion of the detailed
suggestions which have been made for the scope and coverage of the paper.

12. All countries, as well as the International Secretariat and NATO Military
Authorities, would be invited to submit papers on subjects within their
competence, and of particular interest to them. It is then left to the working
group to develop its paper within an agreed framework.

13. The Phase III Working Group should be able to co-opt experts and
representatives from other countries or international staffs, especially if it
is dealing with a paper which any or a group of these had submitted. As they are
completed, draft sections of the paper will be circulated to the NPG staff
Group.
