> # Date of the Next NP Permanent Representatives' Meeting
> 
> The Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) issued a notice regarding the rescheduling of the Nuclear Planning Permanent Representatives' meeting. Due to overriding commitments, the Secretary General is unable to chair the meeting on May 9, 1974, or during the week of May 6. The Chairman proposes that the Staff Group discuss rescheduling at their meeting on March 25. The purpose of the Permanent Representatives' meeting is to take final action on documents for the Nuclear Planning Ministerial meeting scheduled for June 11 and 12, and to exchange views on matters for the Ministers. In evaluating alternative dates, the Chairman notes that the week of April 29 is too early for preparations and includes a NATO holiday on May 1. The week of May 13 is complicated by the Military Committee in Chiefs of Staff session on May 14 and SHAPEX on May 15-17. However, the Chairman suggests rescheduling the meeting for Monday, May 13, at 3:30 p.m., provided the Staff Group meets during the week of May 6 to review instructions from capitals and agree on corrigenda to Working Papers.

DATE OF THE NEXT NP PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES' MEETING
Note by the Chairman
Subsequent to the recent Staff Group meeting at which it was agreed to plan for
an NP Permanent Representatives' meeting on 9th May, 1974, I was informed that
other overriding commitments have now made it impractical for the Secretary
General to chair a meeting on 9th May, or on any other day during the week of
6th May. It is proposed therefore that the Staff Group at its meeting on 25th
March discuss the question of rescheduling the NP Permanent Representatives'
meeting which, as Staff Group members will recall, is for the purpose of taking
final action on various documents to be placed on the agenda for the NP
Ministerial meeting on 11th and 12th June, as well as providing an opportunity
for exchange of views on any matters that will come before the Ministers.

2. In choosing an alternative date, the following considerations should be taken
into account:

(a) It is questionable whether it would be possible to complete preparations in
time for a meeting during the week of 29th April, a week to ten days earlier
than the currently scheduled 9th May meeting. Furthermore, Wednesday, 1st May,
is a NATO holiday.

(b) During the week of 13th May, the Military Committee in Chiefs of Staff
session is scheduled to meet on 14th May, and SHAPEX (to which Ambassadors are
usually invited and at which the Secretary General will speak) is scheduled for
15th through 17th May.

(c) Scheduling an NPG Permanent Representatives' meeting during the week of 20th
May could delay approval and distribution of Ministerial agenda documents to an
extent that would seriously interfere with preparations in capitals.

3. The foregoing suggests that the Staff Group may wish to reschedule the NPG
Permanent Representatives' meeting for Monday, 13th May at 3:30 p.m. If this is
accepted, the Staff Group should plan to meet as necessary during the preceding
week of 6th May to review instructions received from capitals on the various
Working Papers to be considered by the Permanent Representatives, and to agree
on corrigenda to the Working Papers as appropriate.
