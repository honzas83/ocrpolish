> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 17th November, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on November 17, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The group discussed the postponement of the autumn Nuclear Planning Group Ministerial Meeting and proposed new dates of January 15-16, 1976, for the meeting in Hamburg, Germany. The Chairman suggested that the Staff Group utilize the intervening time to work on the organizational aspects of the paper on Arrangements for Phase III. Additionally, the Canadian representative raised the issue of Communicating NATO's Intentions (CNI), suggesting a revision of the paper on Messages of Warning to better distinguish between pre-decision and post-decision messages. The group agreed to continue discussions on CNI procedures and requested a detailed paper from Canada. Regarding Arrangements for Phase III, the Turkish representative indicated that Turkey would likely request participation as a Phase III Working Group member, while the United Kingdom representative noted that the Working Group should remain limited in size. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next session on November 24, 1975.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 17th
November, 1975

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared to the level of security
classification of the matters to be discussed.

NEW DATES FOR THE HAMBURG MEETING

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to a note by the
Secretary of the NPC(1) concerning the postponement of the autumn NPC:
Ministerial Meeting; he observed that, if no comments were received by close of
business on Thursday, 20th November, 1975, the Secretary General would inform
the Government of the Federal Republic of Germany that the dates of 15th and
16th January, 1976, were accepted by the participating countries;

(b) took note of the further remarks of the Chairman who said that, if these
dates were not suitable, it would be advisable to call a short meeting of the
NPG participating Permanent Representatives, preferably immediately after a
Council meeting, in order to discuss alternative dates.

STAFF GROUP ACTIVITIES DURING THE PERIOD BEFORE THE HAMBURG MEETING
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who suggested that the Staff Group
should consider 'how' to use the time available between now and the Hamburg
meeting and he suggested, as an example, that useful work could be done on the
organisational aspects of the paper on Arrangements for Phase III;

(b) agreed to consider this proposal and to reopen the discussion at the next
meeting.

COMMUNICATIIC NATO's INTENTIONS (CNI)
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Canadian representative who said that it
might be useful to work on the CNI problem during the intervening weeks
mentioned above; he handed out a paper representing the views of his authorities
who hoped for some revision of the paper on Messages of Warning(1); with regard
to messages of warning, his authorities wished that there should be a clearer
distinction between pre-decision and post decision messages;

(b) took note of the proposals made in the ensuing discussion and in particular
the views of several members concerning the question of whether or not CNI
procedures were required;

(c) noted the interest of the Canadian authorities with regard to the matter and
expressed the hope that they would prepare a new paper setting out their views
in detail;

(d) agreed to continue the discussion of this question at a future meeting.
(1) NPG/(75)9, 30th October, 1975
