> # NATO Senior Officers' Advanced Weapons Orientation Course
> 
> This document, issued by the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), provides notice of the 1972 schedule for the NATO Senior Officers' Advanced Weapons Orientation Course (IO-31). The course is conducted by the NATO Weapons Systems Department (NWSD) at the USAREUR Combat Support Training Center in Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarize senior NATO officers and key personnel in staff appointments with the major concepts of NATO weapons employment in Allied Command Europe. Eligibility is limited to officers of the rank of Colonel and above, and civilian-officials of equivalent grade, on a need-to-know basis, requiring a COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance. Instruction is provided in English or French. The course lasts one week from Monday through Friday, with a tuition fee of approximately $90 per student. The document lists ten scheduled courses for 1972, ranging from January 17 to December 1. Application processes differ based on the location of the official: those in Brussels are to apply through the Nuclear Planning Directorate of the International Staff, while those in capitals and national commands should apply through their respective Ministry of Defence. Due to limited space (approximately 30 per course) and high demand, early application is at least a month in advance is recommended. A 'Joining Report' must be sent to the NWSD at least two weeks prior to the course. Contact persons for further information are Mr. Serio and Miss Hyde of the Nuclear Planning Directorate.

2. The course provides a general orientation and background that is considered
useful for those involved in NPG work; it may also be of interest to other
senior officials in delegations and in capitals. Officials in national
delegations located at NATO Headquarters in Brussels who are interested in
attending one of the courses listed above should apply through the Nuclear
Planning Directorate of the International Staff which acts as the point of
contact with SHAPE and the NWSD at Oberammergau for obtaining spaces for
personnel in delegations and the International Staff. Officials in capitals and
national commands should apply through their own Ministry of Defence.

3. The number of spaces in each course is limited to about 30 and the demand is
usually quite heavy. Therefore, it is advisable to apply well in advance of the
desired course, i.e., at least a month before. This also allows time for
submission of the "Joining Report" which must be sent to NWSD at least two weeks
prior to commencement of the course.
