> # Further Studies of Follow-on Tactical Use
> 
> This document, a working paper from the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines proposed arrangements for the initial phases of a work programme concerning the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, requests views from national authorities on which proposed analyses should be conducted, which countries should participate, and who should lead the study teams. The work programme includes specific studies on battle situations in the Centre Region (Germany), Southern Region (Greek Thrace and Turkey), and Northern Region (Norway and Denmark), as well as tactical nuclear maritime warfare in the ACLANT and Mediterranean areas. The studies are to be conducted by ad hoc study teams composed of representatives from interested countries, with assistance from NATO Military Authorities. The Permanent Representatives of the Nuclear Planning Group are tasked with monitoring the work programme and providing periodic status reports. The document also mentions the need to pursue these studies expeditiously as requested by the Italian Minister of Defence. The proposed timeline for completion of most studies is January 15, 1971, with ministerial consideration in the Spring of 1971.

Note by the Chairman
Attached is a working paper setting out proposed arrangements for the fulfilment
of the initial phases of the Work programme accepted by the Ministers at their
meeting at Airille House (1).

2. The paper contains initial suggestions developed in the light of Staff Group
discussions of earlier drafts. Delegations are requested to seek instructions in
anticipation of a Staff Group meeting to discuss the Working Paper during the
week beginning 5th January, 1970.

3. In particular, views of the national authorities are requested on the
following points :

(a) which of the proposed analyses under serials 1 to 5 should be conducted (for
example, if the work load does not permit Germany to conduct both studies 1A and
1 B, which one should be undertaken) ?

(b) which countries should participate ? (2)
(c) which country or countries should be study team Leader(s) ?
4. In evaluating the suggested timing of the various studies, account should be
taken of the Italian Minister of Defence at Airille House concerning the need to
pursue the follow-on study task expeditiously (5).

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Further studies of follow-on tactical use

At their meeting at Airlie House on 11th and 12th November, 1969 (1), the
Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group accepted a work programme for the study
of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons (2).

2. The Ministers also invited the Permanent Representatives of the Nuclear
Planning Group to :

(a) make arrangements for the fulfilment of the initial work;
(b) monitor on a continuing basis the work programme and the specific studies
undertaken therein;

(c) propose, as appropriate, further work within the context of the work
programme (3).

3. Contained herein are further arrangements for the implementation of the
Ministerial decision. Both this paper and document NPG/D(69)9(Revised) should be
considered as forming the directives for the conduct of the various studies and
should be read together. The approved provisional political guidelines for the
initial defensive tactical use of nuclear weapons by NATO (4) as well as other
appropriate documents should also be taken into account as background material.

(1) NPG/D(69)10, paragraph 5, 11th November, 1969.
(2) NPG/D(69)9(Revised), 24th November, 1969.
(3) NPG/D(69)10, paragraph 7, 11th November, 1969.

4. The studies will be conducted by ad hoc study teams composed of
representatives of interested countries. The NATO Military Authorities will be
invited to provide assistance. Participation of the NATO military commands
directly concerned with the area to be studied is urged, although this is a
prerogative of the Major NATO Commanders.

5. The detailed working arrangements will be left to each study team. However,
the salient aspects of the initial arrangements such as the identity of the
selected corps area, distribution of study team responsibilities and tentative
time schedule, will be communicated to the Permanent Representatives by the team
leader to insure a reasonable conformity among the studies.

6. To permit the Permanent Representatives to monitor on a continuing basis the
work programme and the specific studies undertaken therein (1), team leaders
will submit periodic status reports as appropriate.

(1) NPG/D(69)10, paragraph 7(b), 14th November, 1969

APPENDIX A to NPG(Staff Group)WP(69)11

Study No 6 : POSSIBLE MEANS OF MAINTAINING POLITICAL CONTROL

DRAFT TERMS OF REFERENCE

The main question to which this study would be addressed is : How, in theory,
can political control of battlefield use of nuclear weapons under selective
release procedures be maintained in situations that lie between very limited use
and general nuclear war employment ? The study would proceed from the likely
requirement that in these situations political authorities will have to deal
with simultaneous multiple requests from military authorities involving a
relatively large number of weapons. The study should explore the possible ways
for political authorities to grant release authorization necessary to meet the
military need while retaining the greatest degree of political control. The
study should also examine how to present such multiple requests to political
authorities in order to facilitate decision-making and subsequent control of
employment.

APPENDIX B to NPG(Staff Group)WP(69)11

Study No 7 : POSSIBLE MEANS OF DEFINING POLITICAL CONSTRAINTS
DRAFT TERMS OF REFERENCE

The report should describe in general terms the physical effects of various
methods of employment of nuclear weapons of varying yield that can be used for
tactical purposes. It should then describe in general terms the military
advantages and the undesirable collateral effects of such employment, excluding
effects that are not subject to objective analysis, such as psychological impact
on populations. From the foregoing a comparison of the military advantages and
undesirable effects should be made so as to enable basic issues to be defined
and examined. Finally various possible measures for reducing or eliminating
undesirable collateral effects should be discussed.

In conducting the above analysis, the following factors should be considered :
- yield;
- type of burst;
- fallout;
- position of DGZ (Desired Ground Zero);
- accuracy of delivery means;
- selection of alternative targets;
- proximity of populated areas;
- warning of troops and populations.
