> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday, 23rd May, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on May 23, 1973, in Brussels to review the results of the NPG Ministers' meeting in Ankara. The meeting began with a farewell luncheon for representatives from the United Kingdom and SHAPE. The group discussed the need to inform capitals of future work proposals in advance to facilitate ministerial decisions. Regarding the balance of strategic forces, the group noted the Secretary General's role in assessing the impact of SALT II and the Mutualization of Forces in Europe (MBFR). The group also addressed the study of Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy and military doctrine for tactical nuclear weapons, with the Netherlands and Germany potentially contributing. The discussion covered four tasks related to consultation procedures and facilities, including the identification of nuclear consultation messages and the implications of communicating NATO's intentions. The group also reviewed the function of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation process and the results of WINTEX 73. An illustrative study on the nuclear aspects of deterrence was discussed, and the group requested additional studies from Major NATO Commanders. The group noted the future work programme and the implementation of Special ADM Guidelines. Finally, the group addressed the security of United States nuclear weapons deployed in Europe and the timing of the next NPG Ministerial meeting.

Examination of the Agreed Minute

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) with regard to Part I (Balanco of Strategic Forces), noted the Secretary
General had been invited(1) to consider means by which the Alliance might best
assess on a continuing basis the impact on NATO defence of SALT II and the
possible nuclear implications of MBFR; discussed points that might be relevant
and agreed to continue the discussion;

(b) with regard to Part II (Studies of Follow-on Use) observed that the
Permanent Representatives were invited(2) to arrange for a study of Warsaw Pact
politico-military strategy and military doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear
weapons, and noted:

(i) that the Netherlands, if help were forthcoming from another country, might
be prepared to lead the Study;

(ii) that Germany might be able to take part;
(iii) agreed that such a study could probably not be ready before the Spring
1974 meeting;

(c) with regard to Part III (Consultation procedures and facilities - WINTEX 73)
noted that four tasks had been specified:

(i) Paragraph 14: to examine the identification and handling of messages related
to nuclear consultation and the content of selective release messages, and to
report to Ministers in due course;
