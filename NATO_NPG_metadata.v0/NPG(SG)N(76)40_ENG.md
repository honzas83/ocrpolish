> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 10th May, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on May 10, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting covered several key administrative and strategic points. First, the Staff Group took note of the security classification of the participants and confirmed that the Advance Press Release for the NPG Ministerial Meeting would be scheduled for June 8, 1976. The United States, Canada, and Germany were noted as participating in the same-time release. The group also addressed security clearances for NPG Ministerial Meetings, emphasizing that all attendees must be authorized for access to ATOMAL and SIOF ATOMAL information. Regarding the records of ministerial discussions, the Chairman proposed to provide more detailed unofficial notes and personally brief Permanent Representatives. The The group also discussed a proposed PO on the Future Work Programme to be circulated by the Secretary General. Finally, the Staff Group continued its examination of procedures for nuclear consultation, including the possibility of a country requesting the use of nuclear weapons in another country, and established an Ad Hoc Working Group under Major MacDonald to refine the wording. The next meeting was scheduled for May 17, 1976.

PRESENTATION OF NEWLY DEVELOPED PROCEDURES IN THE FIELD OF
NUCLEAR CONSULTATION

6. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) continued its examination of the International Staff's paper on the
Presentation of Newly Developed Procedures and agreed to a number of changes in
the text;

(b) took note in particular of a problem raised in paragraph 13 which touched
upon the possibility of a country request for the use of nuclear weapons in
another country;

(c) invited an Ad Hoc Working Group, under the Chairmanship of Major MacDonald,
to try to agree to acceptable wording to this paragraph in time for discussion
at the next meeting.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP

7. The STAFF GROUP:
