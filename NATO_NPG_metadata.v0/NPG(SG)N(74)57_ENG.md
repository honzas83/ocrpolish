> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 20th May, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on May 20, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several administrative and strategic items. The group endorsed the welcome of Colonel T.A. Julian, who replaced Colonel Krone as the SHAPE PANDF representative. Regarding documentation, the group took note of a study on Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy and military doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear weapons, a revised future work programme, and a revised agenda for the NPG Ministerial meeting. They also discussed the report on the military function in the nuclear consultation process, specifically focusing on a revised paragraph 14 and extending the deadline for objections to noon on May 22, 1974. Additionally, the group coordinated arrangements for the NPG Ministerial meeting in Norway, including the agreement on an advance press release and the logistics of transporting classified documents to Bergen. Finally, the group scheduled its next meeting for May 27, 1974, and noted that they would not meet on June 3, 1974, due to Whitmonday.

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 20th

MEMBERSHIP
1. The STAFF GROUP:
warmly endorsed the words of welcome addressed by the Chairman to Colonel T.A.
Julian who replaces Colonel Krone as SHAPE PANDF representative.

DOCUMENTATION
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman regarding the following papers which
were about to be circulated:

(a) NGP/D(74)6, 16th May, 1974: Study on Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy
and Military Doctrine for the Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons. This paper had
been approved by Permanent Representatives and would be circulated on 21st May;

(b) PO/74/50(Revised), 22nd May, 1974: Future Work Programme; the Chairman
outlined the modifications and editorial changes made to this agreed paper,
which was due to be circulated on Wednesday, 22nd May;

(c) NGP/A(74)3(Revised): The revised Agenda for the NGP Ministerial meeting
would be sent out as soon as all documents mentioned in it had been circulated;

REPORT ON THE MILITARY FUNCTION IN THE NUCLEAR CONSULTATION PROCESS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled the Permanent
Representatives' discussion at their meeting on 16th May, 1974 on the draft
report(1) on the Military Committee Function with particular regard to the
changes to paragraph 14 proposed by the Chairman, Military Committee; a new
paragraph 14 had subsequently been prepared and the Secretary General had said
in his cover letter(2) that if no objections had been received by noon on 21st
May he would conclude that the new text was acceptable and that the final
document(3) could be circulated the following day; the Chairman of the Staff
Group said that it was his understanding of the wishes of the NPG Permanent
Representatives that if there was any objection to the revised paragraph 14, the
document would be circulated with the original text with two minor changes
proposed by the Netherlands' Ambassador; this publication would be without
prejudice to further attempts to arrive at an agreed amended text to paragraph
14;

(b) concurred with the Chairman's remarks and took note of the views expressed
in the ensuing discussion;

(c) agreed, in view of the time required to obtain instructions, that the
deadline should be extended to noon, 22nd May;

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN NORWAY
4. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the following points made by the Chairman:
(a) all the countries participating in the Norway Meeting had signified their
agreement to the content and timing of the Advance Press Release;

(b) a possible draft of the Final Communiqué would be circulated to members in
due course;

(c) Mr. Bittner of the NATO Headquarters cosmic control section would carry
classified documents to and from Bergen; the Secretary would circulate details
shortly.

NEXT MEETINGS OF THE STAFF GROUP
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 27th May, 1974, at 3 p.m.; this
meeting would be followed by an unofficial reunion in I-334 at about 5 p.m. to
bid farewell to Colonel Carl Smith who has been temporarily replacing Colonel
Krone as SHAPE representative;

(b) agreed not to meet on 3rd June (Whitmonday); however if circumstances made
it necessary a meeting might be called for 4th or 5th June.
