> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, on Monday, 2nd October, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters on October 2, 1972, to review several key documents and documentation. The group approved a United States amendment regarding forces transition, structure, and deployment. There was a detailed discussion on consultation procedures and facilities, including a modified United States revision and concerns expressed by the Chairman regarding the misuse of the ATOMAL classification. The group invited the United States to clarify the definition of the ATOMAL agreement. Regarding the future of the Illustrative Study programme, the group noted a suggestion from the SHAPE Representative to examine political consultation aspects, but the group recalled that political scenarios had been previously rejected. It was also noted that Ministers were now handling consultation procedures as a separate subject. Additionally, the Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE) Representative's suggestion was discussed. The group reviewed possible dates for the 1973 Spring Ministerial Meeting and deferred the discussion of a possible NDAC Ministerial Meeting in December 1972. The next meeting was scheduled for October 16, 1972, to review work in progress in light of the Permanent Representatives meeting on October 5.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES
Reference : NPG/WP(72)12 and Corrigendum dated 2nd October, 1972
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) discussed and modified a proposed United States revision to certain
paragraphs of the paper at reference above and invited the International Staff
to circulate the modified language(2) so that Representatives could seek
confirmation from their Authorities as to its acceptability; this confirmation
was received on Tuesday 3rd October, and a second Corrigendum to NPG/WP(72)12

(b) took note of a statement by the Chairman in which he expressed his concern
over both the specific United States amendments proposed to NPG/WP(72)12, and
more generally over the use of ATOMAL classification for purposes for which it
was not intended; he stressed in particular the operational and consultative
difficulties that might be generated through the misuse or overuse of the ATOMAL
classification;

(c) invited the United States to clarify the definition of what was in fact
covered by the ATOMAL agreement.
