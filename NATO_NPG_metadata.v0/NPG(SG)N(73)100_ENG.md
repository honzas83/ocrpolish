> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 1st October, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on October 1, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to review several working papers and coordinate upcoming meetings. The group examined working papers concerning operational concepts and doctrine, draft ministerial agendas, the implementation of special administrative guidelines, and consultation procedures for Permanent Representatives and Ministers. The meeting's focus shifted to the future work programme, specifically the Phase II status report on follow-on use, which caused some tension between the Canadian and United States representatives regarding delays in its release. The Staff Group agreed that the submission of the Phase II status report to Ministers would depend on its content and the importance of the interest it contains. They also scheduled the next meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives for October 4, 1973, and discussed administrative arrangements for an upcoming NPG Ministers' meeting in The Hague, Netherlands. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next Staff Group meeting on October 8, 1973.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the summary
of possible NPG future work attached to PO/73/130 dated 25th September, 1973; he
had received a paper on deterrence from the United States member of the Study
Group on Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme, but the Phase II status
report had not yet been released; its contents would have an important bearing
on the future work programme;

(b) took note of the views of the Canadian representative who said that his
authorities were disturbed by the lack of information received from the Phase II
Study Group and by the delay in circulating the status report; this was
retarding the activities of the Staff Group;

(c) noted the views of the Chairman who said that such an important and complex
subject could not be conducted

(d) took note of a statement by the United States representative who said that

(e) agreed, with regard to the possible submission of the Phase II status report

paper for examination in Capitals and by Permanent Representatives and
subsequent submission as a report to Ministers; in the meantime a decision on
this matter must await the arrival of the Phase II Study Team status report;

(f) agreed, with regard to the status report on Phase II, that this report need
not necessarily go to Ministers; this could be decided by Permanent
Representatives either at a meeting after 4th October, or by the silence
procedure.

MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that the next meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives would
take place on Thursday, 4th October, 1973 at 3.30 p.m.;

(b) agreed that, subject to approval by the Permanent Representatives on 4th
October, and unless the Phase II Report made it necessary, there was no need for
an NPG Ambassadors' meeting on 18th October, 1973.
