> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 9th November, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on November 9, 1970, in Brussels to coordinate administrative and strategic planning. The group expressed gratitude to Canadian authorities for their hospitality during a previous Ministerial meeting in Ottawa. The meeting addressed the invitation of the Portuguese Delegation, noting that they were unable to attend. A significant portion of the meeting was dedicated to the concept of the role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE), including discussions on modifications to the Theatre Strike Forces paper. The group also focused on preparations for the December ministerial meetings of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) and the Defence Planning Committee (DPC), reviewing and amending several annexes related to agendas and political guidelines. Additionally, the group discussed the proposal from the German representative to set the next meeting of the NPG Ministers for May 25-26, 1971, and scheduled their next Staff Group meeting for November 15, 1970.

OPTA. MEETING
1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of, and warmly approved statements by the Chairman and by the
United Kingdom representative who expressed, on behalf of the Staff Group, their
gratitude to the Canadian Authorities for the excellent arrangements and
generous hospitality during the Ministerial meeting in Ottawa and the post-
conference tour;

(b) requested the Canadian representative to convey to his authorities the
enthusiastic thanks of the Staff Group.

INVITATION TO PORTUGESE DELEGATION
2. The STAFF GROUP :
noted a statement by the Chairman who said that, in accordance with the wish
expressed by the Staff Group at its last meeting, he had invited the Portuguese
Delegation to send a representative to this present meeting, because Nuclear
Defence Affairs Committee business would be discussed; the Portuguese Delegation
had appreciated this invitation but had not been able to send a representative.
