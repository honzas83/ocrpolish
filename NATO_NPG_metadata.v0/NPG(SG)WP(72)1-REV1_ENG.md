> # Initiation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme
> 
> This document, a revised working paper from the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, outlines proposals for the initiation and implementation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme. The Work Programme is divided into three phases: Phase I involves the preparation of basic data inputs through studies; Phase II focuses on the comparative analysis of these inputs, synthesis of findings, and identification of policy questions; and Phase III involves the formulation of proposed political guidelines for follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. The report notes that while most Phase I studies are expected to be completed by the end of 1972, a new study on the air battle in the Central Region introduces some uncertainty. To address this, the document suggests a pragmatic approach by developing a provisional Phase II report for possible Ministerial consideration in Spring 1973, based on the studies available by the end of 1972. It recommends that the Ministers agree to initiate Phase II immediately after the Spring 1972 meeting and tasks the Permanent Representatives to develop the provisional report. The analytical work for Phase II would be carried out by a small ad hoc study team comprising participants from the United States, the United Kingdom, and Germany, who would report to the Permanent Representatives. The primary focus of the Phase II effort is intended to be on policy aspects rather than exhaustive technical analysis.

Purpose
This report submits for Ministerial consideration certain proposals for the
initiation and implementation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme,
in response to the request made by the Ministers at their meeting in Brussels on
26th and 27th October, 1971(1).

Discussion
2. One of the primary objectives of the Work Programme is to provide, through
its exploration of the implications and interactions of follow-on tactical use
of nuclear weapons, a basis for the Ministers to develop policy proposals, as
appropriate, for submission to the Defence Planning Committee. In this context,
the NPG has agreed that the Work Programme will consist of three major parts,
namely, Phase I (preparation of basic data inputs in the form of studies and
related comments from the NDAC countries and the NATO Military Authorities);
Phase II (comparative analysis of the basic data inputs; synthesis of findings
and identification of the policy questions); and Phase III (formulation, as
appropriate, of proposed political guidelines for follow-on tactical use of
nuclear weapons for NDAC and DPC approval)(2).

6. If a provisional Phase II report is to be available for possible Ministerial

the same token, no interim report on the Phase II effort should be expected for
the Autumn 1972 meeting. To facilitate the preparation of a provisional Phase II
report, all concerned should make every effort to insure the timely introduction
of Phase I basic data by, for example, submitting comments on Phase I studies as
soon as possible after they have been considered at Ministerial level.

7. Organisation for Phase II. The arrangements to be followed in carrying out
Phase II must take into account a number of considerations, some of which are
conflicting. Among these are the requirement to keep the analytical group from
becoming so large and unwieldy that it becomes inefficient; the desire to
involve participants from national capitals; the need to provide a full
opportunity to all interested NPG countries to make national inputs to the
analytical work, to contribute to the final report before it is placed on the
Ministerial agenda and, for those countries not participating at Ministerial
level, to make known any particular national views that they feel should be
taken into account during the Ministerial discussion; and the desire to
facilitate a meaningful input to the process from the NATO Military Authorities.

8. It is suggested that these various considerations can be most effectively
satisfied by using a twofold approach in which the Permanent Representatives
would be vested with the responsibility of preparing a provisional Phase II
report on the basis of a comparative analysis carried out by a small ad hoc
study team, composed of participants from the United States, the United Kingdom
and Germany.

9. The study team would determine its own internal organisation and work
schedule, and would be authorised and expected to call on other countries and
the NATO Military Authorities for expert assistance as appropriate. It would
also be expected to keep the Permanent Representatives informed of its progress
and the general trend of its analysis, preferably through informal briefings or
discussions rather than by means of formal written reports. This aspect of the
approach permits the detailed analytical work to be carried out effectively by a
small group involving participants from capitals, while at the same time
providing a means for non-participating countries to stay in contact.

10. At the conclusion of its work, the study team would submit a written report
to the Permanent Representatives containing the results of the analysis and the
policy questions that had been identified so as to permit multilateral
consideration and the preparation of a Permanent Representatives' report to the
Ministers, if deemed appropriate. It would also be possible for the NPG at the
same time to invite comments from the NATO Military Authorities on the study
team report for use by the Permanent Representatives and by the Ministers during
their subsequent discussions. Keeping in mind the intention that a provisional
Phase II report be available for possible Ministerial consideration at the
Spring 1973 meeting, it would not be unreasonable, for planning purposes, to aim
for the circulation of the study team report to the NPG countries by 1st
February, 1973.

11. Finally, the question might be raised whether it is necessary or desirable
to define the objective of the Phase II effort more precisely than has been
stated in already approved documents(1). While it might be argued that the
provision of more specific terms of reference for the study team might be
helpful, there is a danger that too much detail might unduly restrict the
flexibility that should be granted to those who will undertake the task.
Furthermore, the contacts that are envisaged between the Permanent
Representatives and the study team during the analytical effort will provide an
opportunity for clarification of any points of uncertainty that may arise. On
balance, it is concluded that no additional instructions are needed, although it
could perhaps be agreed that the primary focus of the Phase II effort should be
on the policy aspects and that an exhaustive technical analysis by the study
team is not intended.

Recommendations
12. In light of the foregoing, it is recommended that the Ministers:
(a) agree that Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme should be initiated
immediately after the Spring 1972 Ministerial meeting;

(b) invite the Permanent Representatives to develop a provisional Phase II
report for possible consideration by the Ministers at their Spring 1973 meeting,
taking into account all Phase I studies that will have been addressed by the
Ministers by the end of 1972 and any related comments;

(c) agree that the decision whether or not to place the provisional Phase II
report on the Spring 1973 Ministerial agenda would be made at a later date when
the implications of the Air Battle Study would be more clearly discernible;
