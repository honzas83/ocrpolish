> # Draft Advance Press Release for the Autumn 1973 Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This document is a note from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) of NATO, accompanying a draft advance press release in both English and French. The press release announces that the Nuclear Planning Group, consisting of Canada, the Federal Republic of Germany, the Germany, Greece, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, the United Kingdom, and the United States, will hold its fourteenth half-yearly Ministerial meeting in The Hague on 6th and 7th November 1973. The meeting will be chaired by Mr. Joseph Luns, Secretary General of NATO. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, requests that delegations obtain approval or comments from their national authorities by 19th October 1973, and to specify if they national authorities plan to make a simultaneous release in their respective capitals. It is suggested that the announcement be made in Brussels on Friday, 2nd November 1973, at 3 p.m. local time.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
DRAFT ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE FOR THE
AUTUMN 1973 MINISTERIAL MEETING

Note by the Chairman

Attached is the text in English and French of a proposed advance press release
concerning the forthcoming NPG Ministerial meeting in The Hague on 6th and 7th
November, 1973. The text closely follows the language used in press
announcements issued during the past three years just prior to Ministerial
meetings. As on past occasions, the release might be made a few days before the
meeting takes place; accordingly, it is suggested that an announcement might be
made in Brussels on Friday, 2nd November, 1973 at 3 p.m. local time, with
simultaneous additional release in the capitals of those participating countries
who so desire.

2. Delegations concerned are requested to obtain the approval, or comment, of
their national authorities on the text of the announcement and timing of the
release and to inform me by close of business on Friday, 19th October, 1973. We
would also like to know whether national authorities concerned plan to make a
simultaneous release in the capital.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM

NATO, 1110 Brussels.

DRAFT ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE

The Nuclear Planning Group of NATO, currently consisting of Canada, the Federal
Republic of Germany, Greece, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, the United Kingdom
and the United States, will, at the invitation of the Government of the
Netherlands, hold its fourteenth half-yearly Ministerial meeting in The Hague on
6th and 7th November, 1973, under the chairmanship of Mr. Joseph Luns, Secretary
General of NATO.
