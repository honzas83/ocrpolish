> # Studies on Follow-On Use - Status of Work in Progress
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) and a draft report by the Permanent Representatives regarding the status of work in progress for studies on the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, proposes a draft report to be developed into an agreed paper by September 6th, 1971, for consideration by the Permanent Representatives in late September. The report outlines a work programme accepted by the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group in November 1969, which includes monitoring the work programme and proposing further work. In February 1970, arrangements were made for five initial regional studies and three additional regional studies, as well as a study on the transition from conventional to nuclear posture. The work programme is divided into three phases: Phase I (Preparation of basic data inputs), Phase II (Comparative analysis and synthesis), and Phase III (Formulation of policy guidance). The document mentions specific studies relating to the Mediterranean maritime area, Denmark, the ACLANT area, and Transition. It concludes that no additional Phase I studies should be started at this time and that it is too soon to begin Phase II activities, but suggests submitting comments to the Ministers at the Spring 1972 meeting.

Note by the Chairman

In the light of earlier Staff Group discussion on this subject, I have prepared
the attached initial version of a Draft Report by Permanent Representatives
which the Staff Group may wish to address at its next meeting. It is intended
that the Group develop an agreed paper no later than September 6th, which could
then be circulated as a Working Paper to be considered by the Permanent
Representatives in the second half of September.

2. As in the past, individual study teams have been requested to provide the
information on the status of studies not yet circulated, for inclusion in the
Annexes of the final Ministerial agenda document. The Permanent Representatives
will consider only the basic report, and will not have to take action on the
Annexes.

Draft Report by the Permanent Representatives

At their meeting at Airlie House on 11th and 12th November, 1969(1), the
Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group accepted a work programme for the study
of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons(2). At the same time, they invited
their Permanent Representatives to:

(a) make arrangements for the fulfilment of the initial work;
(b) monitor on a continuing basis the work programme and the specific studies
undertaken therein;

(c) propose, as appropriate, further work within the context of the work
programme(3).

2. In February 1970, the Permanent Representatives approved arrangements for
fulfilment of the initial work(1), calling for the immediate initiation of five
regional studies and the later commencement of three additional regional studies
at times to be determined by the participants. Also, a study covering transition
from conventional to nuclear posture was to be undertaken. Pursuant to later
Ministerial instructions, the Permanent Representatives developed procedural
arrangements for handling the subsequent stages of the follow-on use work
programme and approved them in July 1971(2). These arrangements envisage three
kinds of work programme activity, i.e., Phase I – Preparation of basic data
inputs; Phase II – Comparative analysis and synthesis; Phase III – Formulation
of policy guidance.

3. In fulfilment of their monitoring responsibilities, the Permanent
Representatives submitted brief progress reports to the Ministers in June
1970(3) and May 1971(4).
