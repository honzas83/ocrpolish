> # Advance Press Release for NPG Meeting at Bonn
> 
> The Secretary of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) has distributed a proposed press release to the Staff Group following a meeting held on September 27, 1968. The press release is scheduled for release on October 7, 1968, at 3 p.m., provided no objections are received by October 2, 1968. The press release announces that the NATO Nuclear Planning Group, consisting of the Defence Ministers of seven NATO countries, chaired by Secretary General Brosio, will meet in Bonn on October 10 and 11, 1968. The group is currently composed of the Defence Ministers of Belgium, Denmark, Germany, Greece, Italy, the United Kingdom, and the United States. The meeting's agenda includes a review of nuclear defence planning problems and discussions on studies commissioned at a previous meeting in The Hague on April 18-19, 1968, specifically regarding the possible tactical use of nuclear weapons in the defence of NATO.

ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE FOR NEG MEETING AT BCNN
FOR RELEASE NOT BEFORE
2 PM, 7TH OCTOBER
1968
The NATC Nuclear Planning Group, consisting of the Defence Ministers of seven
N.A.T.C countries and chaired by Secretary General Brosio, will hold its
scheduled meeting in Bonn on 10th and 11th October, 1968. The last ministerial
meeting was held at The Hague on 18th and 19th April, 1968.

At present, the Nuclear Planning Group is composed of the Defence Ministers of
Belgium, Denmark, Germany, Greece, Italy, the United Kingdom and the United
States. In the forthcoming meeting at Bonn, the ministers will continue their
review of problems in the field of nuclear defence planning and will discuss
studies commissioned at The Hague meeting on several subjects pertaining to the
possible tactical use of nuclear weapons in the defence of NATC.
