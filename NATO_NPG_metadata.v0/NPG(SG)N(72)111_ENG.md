> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 11th December, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on December 11, 1972, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with a welcome to Captain H.K.J. Cock of the Royal Navy, who replaced Colonel J.I. Dawson. The group discussed a briefing on Exercise WINTEX 73, noting the exercise's purpose to test procedures for consultation on the use of nuclear weapons, the need for timely assessments from Major NATO Commanders and political-military assessments from capitals, and the activation of ATOMAL procedures. The group agreed that a report on the results of the exercise, focusing on the flow of information and the timeliness of WHISKY and ROMEO messages, would be included in the Autumn 1973 Ministerial Agenda. Regarding documentation, the group reviewed several documents including a draft decision sheet from the NDAC Ministers meeting, the ACLANT Follow-on Study, and studies prepared by Canadian Authorities on Phase II of the Follow-on Studies concerning collateral casualties, time factors, and scenarios and options. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next meeting on January 8, 1973, to discuss the ACLANT study and continue the examination of consultation procedures and facilities.

14th December, 1972

NOTICE
NPG(Staff Group)N(72)111

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 11th
December, 1972

WELCOME

1. The STAFF GROUP:
endorsed the words of welcome expressed by the Chairman on behalf of the Staff
Group to Captain H.K.J. Cock, Royal Navy, who is the replacement for Colonel
J.I. Dawson.

BRIEFING ON EXERCISE WINTEX 73

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a briefing given by the Director, Council Operations on
Exercise WINTEX 73; he described the background of this series of exercises, the
general form and timetable, and underlined that this exercise would, for the
first time, provide an opportunity for the exercising and testing of the
procedures for consultation on the use of nuclear weapons; in describing the
evolution of Exercise WINTEX 73 the Director of Council Operations drew
attention particularly to the need for timely assessments from the Major NATO
Commanders and concurrent political military assessments, both from capitals and
within this Headquarters, which would serve as advance advice; he also described
the scope of the Defence Planning Committee's participation with special
reference to the requirement for a single grouping of requests; he also referred
to the ATOMAL procedures which would be activated during the exercise;

(b) took note of the various points raised in the ensuing discussion and of the
views expressed;

(c) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that a report by
Permanent Representatives to Ministers on the results of the exercise would need
to be prepared; it should cover, in particular, the flow of information and the
timeliness and content of WHISKY and ROMEO messages; agreed that bearing in mind
the time required to prepare such a report it would be advisable to envisage the
inclusion of the question in the Autumn 1973 Ministerial Agenda rather than in
the Spring;

(d) warmly endorsed a statement by the Chairman who thanked the Director,
Council Operations, on behalf of the Staff Group, for the excellent briefing.

DOCUMENTATION

3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the following
documents which have been, or were about to be, circulated:

(a) NDAC/N(72)1, 6th December, 1972: the draft decision sheet of the meeting of
NDAC Ministers held on 6th December, 1972; if no objections are received by
close of business on 13th December, this will be re-published as NDAC/D(72)3;

(b) NPG/Study/19, 1st December, 1972: the ACLANT Follow-on Study; this will be
distributed this week;

(c) PO/72/427, 6th December, 1972: the Secretary General’s letter to the
Chairman, Military Committee, asking for military comments on the ACLANT Study;

(d) M2(72)14, 6th December, 1972: Ministerial final Communiqué; the NDAC
Communiqué is covered by paragraph 25;

(e) DPC/D(70)59(Revised), 21st December, 1972 (CTS): the revised paper, as
approved by NDAC and DPC Ministers; for circulation on 21st December;

(f) NPG/Study/35, 36 and 37: these three studies, to be circulated next week,
have been prepared by the Canadian Authorities and discuss Phase II of the
Follow-on Studies, with particular reference to Collateral Casualties, Time
Factors and Scenarios and Options.

NEXT MEETING
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 8th January, 1973 at 3 p.m.;
(b) agreed that the principal objects of this next meeting should be:
(i) to hold a preliminary exchange of views on the ACLANT study;
(ii) to continue the examination of consultation procedures and facilities.

GOOD WISHES
5. The STAFF GROUP:
received, and warmly reciprocated, the Chairman's good wishes to the Group for a
Happy Christmas and a successful New Year.
