> [!abstract] 
> # Visit to USEUCOM and USAREUR
> 
> This document is a notice from the Secretary of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) detailing a visit to Germany from January 12th to 14th, 1970. The visit includes visits to the United States European Command (USEUCOM) and the United States Army Europe (USAREUR). Annex A provides administrative details such as required identity documents, security clearance (Cosmic Top Secret Atcmail), and guidance on clothing and luggage. It also specifies accommodation at the International Hotel in Echterdingen and the Visiting Officers Quarters in Heidelberg. The itinerary in Annex B outlines a detailed schedule of activities, including briefings by General David A. Burchinal and the Deputy Chief of Staff for Operations, as well as a site tour of a US Missile Detachment and Artillery Battery in the Federal Republic of Germany. Transport is arranged via NATO bus and US Air Force aircraft, departing from Brussels and returning to Brussels via Wiesbaden Air Base.
>

>

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
VISIT TO USEUCOM AND USAREUR
12th - 14th JANUARY, 1970
Note by the Secretary

At the meeting of the Staff Group held on Monday, 15th December, 1969, the
United States Representative made a statement regarding a visit by the Staff
Group to Germany in early January 1970.

2. The dates and duration of this visit are now confirmed as being Monday 12th
January to Wednesday 14th January, 1970.

3. Some administrative details are set out in Annex A to this note. The
programme of the visit is set out at Annex B. This programme is subject to
alteration.

(Signed) N.H.G. WILSON

1110, Brussels.

ANNEX A to NPG(Staff Group)N(70)1

AD: INSTRUMENTAL DETAILS CONCERNING THE VISIT
BY THE NPG STAFF GROUP TO GERMANY
12th – 14th JANUARY, 1970

–

Identity documents
Passport and NATO pass. (Serving officers should bring their military identity
card.)

Security Clearance
Cosmic Top Secret Atcmai. Those persons wi) took part in the Airlie House
meeting need take no action. Those who did not should bring a certificate of CIS
Atcmail clearance, or, preferably, notify Us Delegation. (Lt.Col. Rhodeamel,
Ext. 5713) for more information.

Money
It is advisable to bring both US$ and DH. Payment for meals and accommodation by
individuals may be expected.

Luggage
If possible please restrict your baggage to one suitcase, clearly marked with
your name.

Clothing
Serving officers may wear uniform or civilian clothes. No formal wear will be
required. You are advised to bring warm clothing and strong footwear or
overshoes.

Accommodation
The first night will be spent at the International Hotel in Echterdingen near
Stuttgart where single rooms have been booked. Persons wishing to benefit from
the economy of sharing a room should inform Lt. Col. Rhodeamel (ext. 5713) not
later than Friday, 9th January. The second night will be in the Visiting
Officers Quarters near Heidelberg. (Patrick Henry village)

Flight Insurance
Members of the party requiring flight insurance should make their own
arrangements. Insurance can be obtained at the CIT Office (Mr. Aslanoglou) in
NATO (Ext. 5536).

Transport NATO – Nelsbrook – NATO
A bus, a van for luggage, and a luggage handling party will be provided for the
departure and for the return. The bus and the van will be at the NATO Press
Entrance at 8.15 a.m. on Monday 12th January, and will leave for the aerodrome
at 8.30 a.m. precisely.
