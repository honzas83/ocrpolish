> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 111C Brussels, on Monday, 3rd May, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on May 3, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to coordinate preparations for the upcoming Spring Ministerial Meeting. The group discussed the timing and content of an Advance Press Release, agreeing to a simultaneous release on June 8, 1976. They noted that invitations to SACEUR and SACLANT had been sent and that a Draft Ministerial Agenda would be circulated. The meeting also addressed a scheduled meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives on May 12, 1976, where a United States briefing on Improving the Effectiveness of NATO's Theatre Nuclear Forces Posture would be presented. Additionally, the group discussed the possibility of a meeting on June 2, 1976, and debated whether records of ministerial discussions should be circulated to non-participating countries; however, they decided to maintain the current system of no official records to ensure open discussion. The group also highlighted the urgency of progress reports on Phase III and the Technology Study for the Work Programme and discussed draft procedures for nuclear consultation. The meeting concluded by scheduling the next session for May 10, 1976.

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared to the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

PREPARATIONS FOR THE SPRING MINISTERIAL MEETING

Advance Press Release

2. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of the remarks of the United States representative who said that
his authorities would find it necessary to mention the forthcoming NPG
Ministerial Meeting earlier than proposed in the Staff Group paper;

(b) noted that the Advance Press Release was, in any case, in a very low key;

(c) agreed, subject to confirmation by some members, that the simultaneous
release should take place on Tuesday, 8th June, 1976, at 4 p.m., Brussels time.

Draft Ministerial Agenda

MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

Meeting on 'ednesday 12th May, 1976

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the United States representative who referred to
the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives to be held on 12th May, 1976 and
said that Mr. Cotter and a team of experts from Washington would attend this
meeting; it was their intention, after an introduction by the United States
Ambassador, to give a short briefing to be followed by a question period; the
subject of the briefing would be the United States paper on Improving the
Effectiveness of NATO's Theatre Nuclear Forces Posture;

(b) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who emphasised the need to obtain
full value from the question period and suggested that members should brief
their Permanent Representatives in some detail on possible questions they might
wish to ask;

(c) noted the remarks of the United Kingdom representative who referred to the
need for a letter of transmittal to which could be attached the views of NPG
countries and, in particular, those of the countries which would not be
participating in the meeting.

Possible Meeting on Wednesday, 2nd June, 1976

6. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the German representative who suggested that a
meeting on 2nd June might not be necessary; noted however, in this respect, that
it might be necessary to discuss the Permanent Representatives' transmittal
letter and possibly the Secretary General's PO on the Future Work Programme.

RECORDS OF MINISTERIAL DISCUSSIONS

7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a suggestion by the Danish representative supported by a number
of countries that a record of the discussions in NPG ministerial meetings should
be circulated to non-participating countries and to the other members if they so
wished;

(b) noted the remarks of the United States representative who said that the
advantage of the present system of having no official record was that Ministers
were able to discuss nuclear matters openly and freely in the knowledge that the
views expressed during these discussions would not be considered in any way
official or binding; he preferred therefore to continue the present system;

(c) noted strong support for the United States view from the Representatives of
Italy and the United Kingdom;

(d) noted the remarks of the Chairman who advised that there should be no change
in the present system and recalled that the place for definite decisions taken
by Ministers was the Agreed Minute.
