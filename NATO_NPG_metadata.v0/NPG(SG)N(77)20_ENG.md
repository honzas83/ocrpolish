> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 110 Brussels, on Wednesday, 2nd March, and Thursday, 3rd March, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held meetings on March 2nd and 3rd, 1977, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The primary focus of the meeting was the Maritime Nuclear Warfare Study, where the Chairman noted that the International Staff had been requested to prepare a new version of the report, including a cover letter and revised Terms of Reference. The group agreed that the present paper should be circulated as an NPG Working Paper under the silence procedure. However, on March 3rd, the United States representative interrupted the silence procedure to request additional time for their authorities to review the substance of the paper. Consequently, the Staff Group agreed to distribute a second revision of the draft Permanent Representatives' Report with Annex, allowing all capitals to evaluate the United States' alternative language proposal in context. The group scheduled its next meeting for March 11th, 1977.

MARITIME NUCLEAR WARFARE STUDY
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that, at the last
meeting, the International Staff had been requested to prepare a new version of
the report of the Maritime Nuclear Warfare Study including a cover letter and a
revised version of the Terms of Reference(1); he expressed the hope that this
paper could now be circulated-as a two language NPC Working Paper;

(b) took note of the suggestions made in the ensuing discussion, and in
particular:

(1) 'the probability that the Study required by Ministers'2) would lead on to a
second and broader review;

(ii) the manner in which this concept should be presented to Ministers;
(c) agreed, after making a number of changes to the text, that the present paper

MODIFICATION TO THE DECISION OF 2nd MARCH.
3. On 3rd March, 1977, the United States member of the Staff Group interrupted
the silence procedure to provide his Authorities additional time to deal with
the substance of the paper;

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed, as a consequence of this request from the United States
representative, that another Staff Group version(1) of the draft Permanent
Representatives' Report with Annex should be distributed, which would reflect
the 2nd March agreement, thus allowing all Capitals an opportunity to evaluate
the United States representative's alternative language proposal in a full and
proper context;

(b) Agreed that Staff Group members should be prepared to discuss the second
revision on 11th March at a time to be announced later.

NEXT MEETING
5. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Friday, 11th March, 1977, at a time to be
decided later.
