> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters on Monday, 10th March, 1969
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters on March 10, 1969, to address two primary agenda items. First, the group discussed the Chairman's draft report on NATO's response to the Warsaw Pact's initial tactical use of nuclear weapons, deciding not to include a table in the report and inviting the International Staff to circulate a clean version of the agreed text. This version will be sent to capitals for further review, with a subsequent meeting planned in three to four weeks to finalize the report for the Permanent Representatives. Second, the group received an alternative version of paragraph 7 of the International Staff's draft document regarding the role of theatre nuclear strike forces, deciding to postpone discussion of this paper to their next meeting on March 17, 1969, at 4:00 p.m.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at NATO Headquarters on Monday, 10th March, 1969
NATO'S RESPONSE TO WARSAW FACT'S INITIAL TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
Document: NPG(Staff Group)WF(69)2, dated 3rd February, 1969
1. The STAFF GROUP, after further discussion of the Chairman's draft report on
NATO's response to the Warsaw Pact's initial tactical use of nuclear weapons:

(a) decided not to include a table in the report;
(b) took note of the views expressed, and the amendments to the text proposed
during the meeting;

(c) invited the International Staff to circulate a clean version of the agreed
text;

(d) agreed to send this version to capitals and to hold a further meeting in the
light of national instructions in about three to four weeks in order to finalize
the report for consideration by the Permanent Representatives.

THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES
Document: NPG(Staff Group)WF(69)4, dated 5th March, 1969
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) received an alternative version of paragraph 7 of the International Staff's
draft document;

(b) decided to discuss the paper at its next meeting on Monday, 17th March,
1969, at 4.0 p.m.
