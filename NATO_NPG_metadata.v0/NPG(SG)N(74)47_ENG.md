> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 29th April, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on April 29, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group endorsed the Chairman's remarks regarding a successful visit to SHAPE, and expressed gratitude to the SHAPE representatives. The meeting covered the documentation review of several papers, including the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process and a Phase II Study Team status report. The group also discussed the Warsaw Pact's politico-military strategy and military doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear weapons, agreeing that a draft report could be circulated as an NPG Working Paper for final approval by Permanent Representatives on May 16, 1974. Additionally, the group noted the confirmation of the Autumn meeting of NPG Ministers in Rome on November 7th and 8th, and the group's own next meeting scheduled for May 6, 1974. The meeting also touched upon the United States Nuclear Deterrent Policy and the exchange of messages.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 29th
April, 1974

VISIT TO SHAPE BY THE STAFF GROUP
1. The STAFF GROUP:
endorse the remarks of the Chairman who said that the visit by the Staff Group
to SHAPE had been most interesting and useful, and requested the SHAPE
representatives to convey the thanks of the Staff Group to all those in SHAPE
who had contributed to the success of the visit.

DOCUMENTATION
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to two documents
which had recently been circulated:

(a) NPG/WP(74)4, 22nd April, 1974: Military Committee Function in the Nuclear
Consultation Process;

(b) NPG(Staff Group)N(74)45, 25th April, 1974: Phase II Study Team Status
Report.

3. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a further statement by the Chairman who said that he hoped that the
following papers would be available to members before the end of the week:

(a) a PO on the future work programme, addressed to Permanent Representatives;

WARSAW PACT POLITICO-MILITARY STRATEGY AND MILITARY DOCTRINE

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks by the Chairman concerning the latest revision(1)

(b) took note of the views expressed in the subsequent discussion and agreed to
a number of changes to the text of the annex to the report;

(c) agreed that the draft report could now be circulated as an NPG Working
Paper, for examination in Capitals and for final approval by Permanent
Representatives at their meeting to be held on 16th May, 1974.

AUTUMN MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS

5. The STAFF GROUP:

countries taking part in the Autumn meeting of NPG Ministers had now confirmed
their agreement to the place and dates of the meeting (Rome, 7th and 8th
November).
