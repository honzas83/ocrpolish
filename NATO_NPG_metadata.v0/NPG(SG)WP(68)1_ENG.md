> # Follow-On Action to the Ministerial Meeting in Bonn on 10th and 11th October, 1968
> 
> This document, a report by the Chairman of the Staff Group of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG), outlines proposed follow-on actions to implement decisions from the Ministerial Meeting held in Bonn on 10th and 11th October 1968. The report suggests the development of tentative political guidelines for the initial tactical use of nuclear weapons by NATO, with the United Kingdom and Germany tasked to lead this effort. It addresses the follow-up on Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADM), including the completion of a Time Factor study and subsequent coordination between Italy, Greece, Germany, and the United Kingdom. The document also proposes a meeting of experts to exchange views on Soviet thinking in the tactical nuclear field to inform NATO's response to initial nuclear use by the Warsaw Pact. Additionally, it examines the role of theatre nuclear strike forces and the relationship between SACEUR's Scheduled Programme and US strategic nuclear plans. The report further suggests continuing the examination of consultation on the use of nuclear weapons and notes that a formal NATO nuclear planning procedure will be part of political guidelines for the NATO Military Authorities. The Permanent Representatives are invited to agree to these proposed actions and indicate any priorities.

Report by the Chairman of the Staff Group
The following paragraphs are a summary of suggestions
by the Staff Group for actions that might be required to implement the decisions
taken by the Ministers at their meeting in Bonn on 10th and 11th October, 1968.

1. DEVELOPMENT OF TENTATIVE POLITICAL GUIDELINES
(paragraphs 3 and 4 of Agreed Minute)
2. Interested countries have been invited to submit by
21st December 1968 further papers commenting upon or supplementing
the four original guideline papers (1).
3. With regard to the study envisaged in paragraph 4 of
the Agreed Minute, it is desirable that the United Kingdom and
Germany jointly should start work designed to develop tentative
political guidelines for the NATO Military Authorities in
respect of the possible initial tactical use of nuclear weapons
by NATO, aiming at submitting their paper not later than
1st April, 1969.
4. With regard to the further work envisaged in
paragraph 5 of the Agreed Minute on the subject of Atomic
Demolition Munitions, the follow-up action required would seem
to be as follows :
(1) NPG/US(68)D4; NPG 10/ITALY; 21-08-3/3203/68(GERMANY);
NPG/UK(68)2.

(a) Completion as soon as possible of the Time Factor study. This is at present
being developed in the NPG Staff Group in conjunction with the NATO Military
Authorities. It is suggested that the Staff Group should aim to produce a report
to Permanent Representatives by 31st December, 1968;

(b) Once the Time Factor study has been endorsed by the Nuclear Planning Group
at Permanent Representative level it should be passed to the Italian Authorities
in order that they may complete their ADM Guidelines study;

(c) The Italian Authorities, in co-operation with Greece, will meanwhile
continue to develop the ADM guidelines study, taking into account when it is
made available, the study of Time Factors. When the whole study has been
completed it should be forwarded to the United Kingdom and Germany in order that
they may take it into account as necessary in their overall study of political
guidelines. This would be without prejudice to the possibility of submitting a
report on ADMs.

II. NATO'S RESPONSE TO INITIAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS BY WARSAW PACT (paragraph
7 of Agreed Minute)

5. The Permanent Representatives have been invited to consider questions
relating to NATO's response in the event of initial tactical use of nuclear
weapons by Warsaw Pact forces.

6. As a first step it is suggested that it might be advantageous to hold a
meeting of experts drawn from NPG nations to exchange views on Soviet thinking
in the tactical nuclear field; this would not of course exclude the submission
of papers on this matter by any member of the Group who wished to do so. Such an
initial exchange of views might also provide useful background material for the
study which the United Kingdom and Germany are to conduct in accordance with
paragraph 4 of the Agreed Minute.

7. It would also be necessary for Permanent Representatives to consider the
method of conducting the study, namely whether a discussion leader should be
nominated or whether the study would be conducted by the Group collectively.

III. THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES
(paragraph 6 of Agreed Minute)

8. The Permanent Representatives have been invited to examine the rôle of the
theatre nuclear strike forces with special reference to the relationship between
SACEIR’s Scheduled Programme and US strategic nuclear plans, taking into account
the issues raised by the Canadian paper on the rôle of fighter-bombers. It is
suggested that, as a first step, arrangements should be made for the Permanent
Representatives to receive a presentation on the above subject. Subsequently
they might wish to give instructions regarding further action.

IV. CONSULTATION ON THE USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
(paragraphs 8 and 9 of Agreed Minute)

9. The Permanent Representatives have been invited to continue their examination
of this subject. The list of questions (1) already circulated by the Staff Group
would appear to provide an appropriate starting point since in most cases
national guidance has been received by delegations. Permanent Representatives
may however wish to consider whether the Staff Group should endeavour to collate
the answers that have so far been received and submit a further report as a
basis for discussion; or whether they themselves would wish first to have an
initial general discussion.

V. NUCLEAR PLANNING PROCEDURE

10. A NATO nuclear planning procedure as envisaged in the various papers
submitted so far is only meaningful after the issue of political guidelines to
the NMA. No further action appears necessary at this stage.

VI. CONCLUSIONS

11. The Permanent Representatives are therefore invited :
(a) to agree to the action proposed in paragraphs 3 to 8 above;
(b) to indicate, if necessary, the priority of work.
