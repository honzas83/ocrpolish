> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Friday, 23rd April, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on April 23, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to discuss several key items. The group took note of the progress of the paper on Nuclear Consultation-CNI procedures and the request for military assessments of the United States document 'Improving Effectiveness of NATO's Theater Nuclear Forces'. The meeting also focused on the draft agenda for the Spring Ministerial meeting scheduled for June 14, 1976, and the draft advance press release associated with it. Additionally, the group scheduled two meetings for NPG Permanent Representatives on May 12 and June 2, 1976, with the May 12 meeting focusing on the procedural handling of the United States paper on theater nuclear forces. The group also conducted an initial examination of an International Staff paper regarding newly developed procedures in the field of nuclear consultation, discussing areas of potential problems, structural issues, and the interface with the VI.III.77 exercise. The meeting concluded by agreeing to continue discussions on these consultation procedures at the next meeting scheduled for May 3, 1976.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP(STAFF GROUP)
notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Friday, 23rd
April, 1976

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP: took note that all persons present were cleared to the
security classification of the matters to be discussed.

DOCUMENTATION
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that, no further comments
having been received, the paper on Nuclear Consultation-CNI procedures had been
passed to the Secretary General for approval and would be circulated during the
next few days under reference NPG/YP(76)1;

(b) took note of the further remarks of the Chairman who recalled the Secretary
General's request to the Chairman of the Military Committee(1) to provide
military assessments of the United States document "Improving Effectiveness of
NATO's Theater Nuclear Forces"; documents prepared by the Chairman of the
Military Committee, SACLEUR and SACLANT were expected to be circulated to
delegates and the International Staff during the next few days.

SPRING MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS

Ministerial Agenda:

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the draft Agenda for the Ministerial meeting(1) and of the
Chairman's remarks; this Agende was for discussion at the Staff Group meeting on
3rd May but he invited comments if members wished;

(b) noted general agreement that the Ministerial meeting could begin at 2.30
p.m. on Monday 14th June;

(c) noted general agreement with the content of the agenda and of the order in
which the items were placed;

(d) agreed that if no objections had been received by Thursday 29th April the
Agenda would be considered as ready for examination by Permanent Representatives
at their meeting on Wednesday 12th May, 1976.

Draft Advance Press Release

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted the general agreement with the text and timing of the draft advance
press release;

(b) noted the remarks of the United States Representative who said that his
Authorities might press for earlier release; he indicated that the United States
Secretary of Defense when leaving for the DPC might wish to mention the NPG as
well.

FUTURE MEETINGS OF NPG PARTICIPANT REPRESENTATIVES

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who said that two meetings of NPG
Permanent Representatives were foreseen:

(i) wednesday 12th May, 1976 at 3.30 p.m.
(ii) wednesday 2nd June, 1976 at 3.30 p.m.

(b) he recalled that the principal item for discussion at the meeting on the
12th May would be the Procedural Handling of the United States paper on
Improving the Effectiveness of NATO's Theatre Nuclear Forces Posture; a team of
experts was coming over from Washington to attend this meeting:

PRESENTATION OF NEWLY DEVELOPED PROCEDURES IN THE FIELD OF NUCLEAR CONSULTATION

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the statement by the Chairman who introduced the International
Staff paper(1) and proposed a general initial examination of this which was
being prepared for discussion by ministers in the Autumn 1976 meeting;

(b) took note of the views expressed and the suggestions made in the ensuing
discussion on basically three areas outlined by the Chairman:

- portions where countries foresee major problems,
- portions which seem to be either incomplete or overloaded in the overall
structure of the presentation,

- procedural handling between now and the Autumn Ministerial Meeting;
(c) noted the remarks of several delegates concerning the interface with various
portions of the VI.III.77 exercise itself, and concerning the emphasis given to
CMI and the certain lack of detail regarding Selective Employment Planning as
well as the omission of a SACEUR RORIO (request) message.

(d) agreed to continue discussion at the next meeting.
