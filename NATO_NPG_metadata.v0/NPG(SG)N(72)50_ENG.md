> # Preparations for the Autumn 1972 Ministerial Meeting
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on June 5, 1972, to discuss preparations for the Autumn 1972 Ministerial Meeting. The group agreed to keep the agenda simple and prioritize matters that cannot be decided by Permanent Representatives. Key agenda items discussed include the initiation of an ACLANT Illustrative Study on initial use at sea, the status of follow-on use studies regarding Denmark and Transition studies, and a request for a briefing from the United States Secretary of Defense on the balance of strategic forces. The group also addressed the updating of policy documents following the introduction of the POSEIDON system and the implementation of Special ADI Guidelines. Furthermore, Consultation Procedures and Facilities were identified as a priority for the Ministerial Agenda. The meeting also touched upon unresolved problems related to Tactical Nuclear Defence and the necessity of gathering data on possible Warsaw Pact responses to nuclear action. A provisional timetable for Permanent Representatives meetings was established for September and October 1972, and the next Staff Group meeting was scheduled for June 12, 1972.

PREPARATIONS FOR THE AUTUMN 1972 MINISTERIAL MEETING
General points
1. The ST'FF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the International
Staff's working paper(1) setting out a number of observations and suggestions
concerning the tasks which the Staff Group would have to undertake in preparing
for the next NPC Ministerial Meeting, and the timetable for this work ; he
invited comments ;

(b) noted that there was a considerable number of possible items for the
Ministerial Agenda and agreed that the aim should be to keep the agenda simple ;
further agreed that matters which could be decided upon by the Permanent
Representatives should not feature on the agenda ;

(c) agreed to consider the various items one by one, and also to consider the
timetable for Staff Group and Permanent Representatives.

Balance of Strategic Forces

Updating of Policy Documents

5. The STAFF GROUP :

(a) recalled that the United States Authorities had offered to make
recommendations on modifications which might have to be made to policy documents
consequent upon the introduction of the POSEIDON System ;

(b) agreed that this matter could perhaps be dealt with by Permanent
Representatives ; agreed to await the arrival of the United States report.

Special ADI Guidelines

6. The STAFF GROUP :

(a) recalled that a report on the implementation of Special ADI Guidelines was
expected from the Military Committee before 1st August ; recalled that some
information was expected from the United States Authorities ; agreed that a
decision on whether to include this matter in the Ministerial agenda should be
taken after the receipt of the report ;

(b) agreed that it would be helpful to the Staff Group, and especially to the
newer members, to have a briefing and review of ADI issues, possibly with a
film, as a background to future discussion.
