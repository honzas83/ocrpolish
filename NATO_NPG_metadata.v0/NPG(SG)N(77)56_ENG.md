> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 1st August, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels on August 1, 1977. The primary focus of the first agenda item was the status of new weapons technology follow-up work, where the United States Member noted that while they agreed with the substance of the draft report in NPF(Staf Group)P(77)7, they requested a more concise paper. The Chairman informed the group that the Nuclear Planning Directorate would prepare a shorter version of the draft report but would be unable to survey past and ongoing NATO studies due to manpower resources. The German Member emphasized the need for a conceptual framework to evaluate ongoing studies related to the Long Term Science Programme, and the Chairman agreed to consult with the German Delegation to ensure these concerns were addressed in the revised report. Additionally, the group discussed follow-up to the Ottawa Ministerial Meeting, noting that the International Staff would circulate a draft Permanent Representatives' Report to Ministers regarding the Maritime Nuclear Warfare Study. Finally, the meeting concluded with the setting of the next meeting date for August 8, 1977, in London.

I. STATUS O' NEW WEAPONS TECHNOLOGY FOLLO:-UP 'ORK

The STAF GROUP:

(1) noted a statement by the United States Member that while his Authorities
generally agreed with the substance of the draft report in NPF(Staf Group)
P(77)7, they too would welcome the preparation of a more concise paper at a
later stage;

(2) was informed by the Chairman that with its existing nampower resources, the
Nuclear Planning Directorate would not be able to undertake the survey and
review of past and on-going NATO studies and other Committee work(1); it would,
however, prepare, as requested, a shorter version of the draft report in
NPF(Staf Group)VP(77);

(3) noted a statement by the German Member that in the view of his Authorities,
a conceptual framework should be drawn up aimed at evaluating on-going studies
in connection with the Long Term Science Programme but that if it were not found
possible to do this, Ministers should be informed of the reasons;
