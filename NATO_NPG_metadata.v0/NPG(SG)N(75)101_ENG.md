> # Senior Officers' Orientation Course
> 
> The North Atlantic Council issued a notice on 26 November 1975 regarding the Senior Officers' Orientation Course (IO-31), conducted by the NATO Weapons Systems School (NWSS) in Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarize senior NATO officers of the rank of Colonel and above, and civilian officials of equivalent grade, with the major concepts of NATO weapons employment in Allied Command Europe. A COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance is required for attendance. The document provides a schedule of courses for 1976, listing dates for courses 210 through 221. Some courses are listed as having French translation available, while others are conducted in English only. The course lasts one week from Monday to Friday. Application processes differ based on the location of the official: those at NATO Headquarters in Brussels apply through the Nuclear Planning Directorate of the International Staff, while officials in capitals and national commands apply through their own Ministry of Defence. Each course is limited to approximately 30 spaces, with only one or two spaces typically allocated to NATO officials. Applicants are advised to apply at least a month in advance and submit a Joining Report three weeks prior to the course. The Nuclear Planning Directorate, specifically Mr. Serio and Miss Hyde, are the points of contact for further information.

The subject course (short title: IO-31) is conducted by the NATO Weapons Systems

2. The course provides a general orientation and background that is considered
useful for those involved in NPG work; it may also be of interest to other
senior officials in delegations and capitals. Officials in national delegations
located at NATO Headquarters in Brussels who are interested in attending one of
the courses listed above should apply through the Nuclear Planning Directorate
of the International Staff which acts as the point of contact with NPAE and the
NWS at Oberammergau for obtaining spaces for personnel in delegations and the
International Staff. Officials in capitals and national commands should apply
through their own Ministry of Defence.

3. The number of spaces in each course is limited to about 30 and the demand is
usually quite heavy. Only one or two spaces for each course are usually
allocated to NATO officials. Therefore, it is advisable to apply well in advance
of the desired course, i.e., at least a month before. This also allows time for
submission of the "Joining Report" which must be sent to NWS at least three
weeks prior to commencement of the course.

4. The Nuclear Planning Directorate would be pleased to provide further
information or to obtain quotas. Please contact Mr. Serio on extension 2048, or
Miss Hyde on extension 2027.
