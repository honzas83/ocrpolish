> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday, 4th September, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on September 4, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several administrative and strategic matters. The meeting began with the introduction of Mr. R. Shearer as the new Director of Nuclear Planning and Chairman of the NPG Staff Group. New members were welcomed, including representatives from Belgium, Norway, and the United States, as well as a temporary replacement from the United Kingdom. The group reviewed several documents, including a draft report on the Communicating of NATO's Intentions (CNI) study and a study on Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy and military doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear weapons. The group also discussed the planning for the upcoming NPG Ministers meeting in Rome on November 7-8, 1974, and proposed a meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives in October to finalize documents. The agenda for the Ministerial meeting was reviewed and agreed upon, with a request for SACEUR and SACLANT to provide introductory papers on Selective Employment Planning. The group confirmed that no further NPG report on WINTEX 73 was necessary. Finally, the group discussed a revised version of Working Paper WP(74)8 regarding the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Air Defence Use, awaiting further comments from the United Kingdom on CNI before finalization. The next meetings of the Staff Group were scheduled for September 9 and September 12, 1974.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday,
4th September, 1974

NEW CHAIRMAN
1. The STAFF GROUP: noted with appreciation the remarks of Mr. R. Shearer, who
introduced himself as the newly appointed Director of Nuclear Planning and
Chairman of the NPG Staff Group.

NEW MEMBERS
2. The STAFF GROUP: concurred with the welcome extended by the Chairman to
Colonel J.E.E. De Mild who is replacing Colonel M. Minit as Belgian
representative in the Staff Group; to Mr. H. Riisberg who has replaced Mr.
Skletnes (Norway); to Major W. Robbins who joins as assistant to Mr. J.
Woodworth (United States representative); and to Mr. M. Legge from UK MOD,
replacing Mr. N. Beaumont for the meeting.

DOCUMENTATION
3. The STAFF GROUP: took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention
to the following papers of interest to the Staff Group and which had recently
been issued: (a) NPG(Staff Group)WP(74)10(Revised), 13th August, 1974: Draft
Permanent Representatives Report on the CNI Study;

NEXT MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled the need for a meeting
in October of NPG Permanent Representatives to examine and give final approval
to the documents now being prepared for the Ministerial meeting in November and
to receive a briefing on the SHAPE Air Defence Illustrative Study;

(b) agreed that the afternoon of Thursday, 10th October, 1974 appeared to be the
most suitable date, with 17th and 31st October in reserve if other pending
matters should substantiate; invited the Chairman to submit these proposals to
the Secretary General.

LOCATION OF THE SPRING MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS
6. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled that a decision should be
reached before the Rome meeting regarding the location of the subsequent meeting
of NPG Ministers in the Spring of 1975; he invited the representatives of those
countries who might be concerned, to obtain some indication of the intentions of
their authorities.

DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS IN NOVEMBER

7. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the draft
Ministerial Agenda(1) and invited comments;

(b) noted with appreciation the remarks of the United States representative who
said that although he had not yet received formal confirmation, it could be
assumed that the strategic briefing tabled as Item I would be given;

(c) agreed, with regard to Item II (Status of Selective Employment Planning)
that the Ministerial discussion would be more fruitful if SACEUR and SACLANT
could prepare introductory papers on this matter, for submission to the
Permanent Representatives in October;

(d) noted with appreciation the remarks of the SHAPE representative who said
that SACEUR welcomed the opportunity of providing general information on this
planning;

(e) agreed, subject to a minor change, to the form and content of the draft
Ministerial Agenda.

REVIEW OF FINAL WINTEX 73 REPORTS

8. The STAFF GROUP:

confirmed its agreement reached at the meeting held on 29th July, 1974, that no
further NPG report on WINTEX 73 by Permanent Representatives appeared to be
necessary.
