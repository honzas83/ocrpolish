> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 8th February, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on February 8, 1971, to address several key agenda items. The group noted a scheduled briefing by the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) on Current Release Procedures on February 22, 1971. They also discussed staff support for military observers at the upcoming NPC Ministerial Meeting in Mittenwald, specifically noting the need for an extra seat for the German Representative. They reviewed proposals for Exercise HILEX 5 to include the General Guidelines for Consultation Procedure on the Use of Nuclear Weapons and noted that the International Military Staff (IMS) nuclear team was preparing a critique of Exercise WINTEX 71. They discussed illustrative studies and sample release requests provided by the Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic (SACIANT) and agreed to discuss these further in future meetings. The group also reviewed a draft agenda for the meeting of NPG Ministers in May, and addressed the question of national participation in military nuclear planning, requesting information on the implementation of the Defence Planning Committee (DPC) directive from May 1968. Finally, they discussed the North Norway Study, agreeing that such studies should be considered final documents and discussing specific issues emerging from the North Norway Study, before setting the date for their next meetings.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 8th February, 1971

BRIEFING BY SACEUR ON CURRENT RELEASE PROCEDURES(1)

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled that SHAPE would give a
Briefing on Current Release Procedures on Monday, 22nd February, 1971, at 3 p.m;
this Briefing will probably take place in the Presentation Room in the Situation
Centre;

(b) noted that SACEUR would be in attendance and that the classification of the
Briefing would be COSMIC TOP SECRET;

(c) noted that Permanent Representatives would probably wish to be accompanied
by their deputies, by their military and political advisers and by their Staff
Group member; seating would be organized on the basis of 1 + 5 or 6 per
delegation.

STAFF SUPPORT FOR MILITARY OBSERVERS(2)

2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the new arrangements for a
staff officer to attend NDIAC and NPG Ministerial Meetings with the military
observers, and requested the German Representative to note that an extra seat
would be necessary at the forthcoming NPC Ministerial Meeting to be held at
Mittenwald;

EXERCISE HILEX 5
3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to a COECWG paper
(A0/237-D/91, 4th February, 1971), in which it was proposed that Exercise HILEX
5 should include exercise of the General Guidelines for Consultation Procedure
on the Use of Nuclear Weapons(1).

EXERCISE WINTEX 71
4. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who said that the IMS nuclear team was
preparing a critique of WINTEX 71 which might be presented to the Staff Group
towards the end of the month.

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES AND SAMPLE RELEASE REQUESTS
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled that Annex B to NPG/N(70)5,
dated 14th October, 1970, contained background material provided by SACIANT for
illustrative studies, and some sample release requests;

(b) noted that Ministers had agreed to discuss material of this kind at their
meeting in May;

(c) agreed to discuss these questions during the next meetings of the Staff
Group.

DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS IN MAY
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of some comments by the Chairman and of a draft Agenda prepared by
the International Staff(2);

(b) discussed the draft Agenda and took note of the various views expressed;
(c) agreed to re-open the discussion on this matter towards the middle of March.

NATIONAL PARTICIPATION IN ALLIED NUCLEAR PLANNING
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman and of a Staff Group Notice on
National Participation in Allied Nuclear Planning(1);

(b) took note of various views expressed and suggestions made;
(c) agreed to focus attention for the present on the question of national
participation in military nuclear planning;

(d) invited IMS and SHAP E representatives to provide information on the
specific ways by which the DPC directive had been implemented since its
promulgation in May 1968;

(e) invited the International Staff to prepare a revised draft of a query to
countries for consideration at the next meeting.

NORTH NORWAY STUDY
8. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of various views and comments on the Follow-on Studies in general
and on the North Norway Study in particular;

(b) agreed that Studies should be considered as final documents and that no
changes to their texts should be envisaged;

(c) noted further views on how to extract specific issues for Ministers from the
various studies;

(d) took note of and discussed a number of specific issues emerging from the
North Norway Study;

(e) agreed to continue the discussion at the next meeting of the Staff Group.
NEXT MEETINGS OF THE STAFF GROUP
9. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 15th February, 1971, at 3 p.m.;
