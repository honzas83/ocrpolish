> # Communicating NATO's Intention to Use Nuclear Weapons
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on Monday, July 1, 1974. The primary focus of the meeting was the discussion of a draft study regarding the communication of NATO's intention to use nuclear weapons. The Staff Group took note of the Chairman's statement and continued the examination of the draft study, while expressing various views on the approach, form, and content of the draft. Consequently, the group agreed to several changes in method, presentation, and terminology. The group decided to continue discussions on the draft at another meeting within the same week and aimed to reach a decision on the procedure for preparing the Study and any necessary accompanying reports. Additionally, the Chairman was invited to prepare a revised version of the draft for circulation the following week. The meeting also established a schedule for upcoming meetings, with the next meeting set for Wednesday, July 3, 1974, and subsequent meetings tentatively scheduled for July 8 and July 18, 1974.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTION TO USE NUCLEAR WEAPONS
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that at the previous
meeting the Staff Group had held an initial discussion of the draft Study on
communicating NATO's intention to use nuclear weapons(1); he proposed to
continue the examination of the draft study;

(b) noted, during the ensuing discussion, the various views expressed concerning
the approach to the problem and the form and content of the draft study; agreed
to a number of changes of method, presentation and terminology;

(c) agreed to continue the discussion of the draft at another meeting in the
current week;

(d) agreed that a decision should be taken, if possible at the next meeting,
upon the procedure to be adopted regarding the preparation of the Study and any
accompanying report while might be thought necessary;
