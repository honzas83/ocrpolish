> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 140 Brussels, on Monday, 29th September, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on September 29, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting covered several key administrative and strategic items. The group noted the circulation of the 'Follow-on Use Work Programme – Initiation of Phase III' and discussed the status of current work, including proposed dates for the meetings of the NPC Permanent Representatives. üzerinden own a 'final' draft of the ministerial agenda, subject to confirmation by the Danish representative, and invited the International Staff to circulate it as an NPG Working Paper. Regarding consultation procedures for country requests, the group agreed to a modified text, subject to confirmation by the Netherlands representative, to be approved by the NPC Permanent Representatives on October 15, 1975. The group also discussed practical aids to decision-making, specifically focusing on the illustrative studies and the feasibility of using old studies. Finally, the group addressed arrangements for the Autumn Ministerial meeting and accommodation needs, as reminded by the German representative, and scheduled their next meeting for October 5, 1975.

DRAFT MINISTERIAL AGENDA

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) discussed in detail the form and content of the draft ministerial agenda(1)
and took note of the views expressed, in particular, those concerning the need
for reference documents for Item III (Force Posture Issues) and the subject of
future NPC Ministerial meetings;

(b) agreed, subject to confirmation by the Danish representative, upon a "final"
draft of the agenda and invited the International Staff to circulate it in the
form of an NPG Working Paper for examination in Capitals and approval by NPC
Permanent Representatives at their next meeting.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES – COUNTRY REQUESTS

5. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who invited final comments on the
second revision of the Staff Group's paper(2);

(b) took note of the suggestions made in the ensuing discussion and agreed to a
number of changes;

(c) agreed, subject to confirmation by the Netherlands representative, upon the
text as modified in the discussion and invited the International Staff to
prepare the draft report in the form of an NPG Working Paper for examination in
Capitals and approval by the NPC Permanent Representatives at their meeting on
15th October, 1975.
