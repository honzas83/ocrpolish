> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 5th April, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on April 5, 1971, to discuss several key strategic and operational matters. The group took note of three papers regarding work in progress, illustrative studies of release requests, and the draft agenda for the May Ministerial Meeting. They discussed the Macedonia and Western Thrace Area Study and the NORTHAG Area Study, which was awaiting Ministerial signature. The group also reviewed the Report by the Military Committee on the Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE), noting a potential contradiction in the Concept Paper. The meeting addressed a report by the Tri-Service Group on Air Defence, noting that certain paragraphs with nuclear implications were at variance with current Nuclear Planning Group thinking. The group also discussed follow-up action on the AD70s Report, noting that nuclear problems would be handled by the Nuclear Planning Group and its submissions to the Defence Planning Committee. Finally, the group scheduled the next meetings for the NPG Permanent Representatives on April 20 and 29, and the next Staff Group meeting for April 19, 1971.

WORK IN PROGRESS
1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman in which he recalled that three
papers had been circulated by the International Staff, all of which should be
sent to Capitals with a view to receiving comments in time for further
discussion to the Staff Group at its meeting on 19th April;

(i) Status of Work in progress on Studies of Follow-on Use (NPG/WP(71)1, 30th
March 1971)

(ii) Illustrative Studies and Examples of Release Requests (NPG/WP(71)2, 1st
April 1971)

(iii) Draft Agenda for the May Ministerial Meeting (NPG(Staff Group)N(71)18(2nd
Revise), 31st March, 1971).

(c) noted that the first two of the above-mentioned papers were on the agenda
for the Permanent Representatives' meeting on 20th April, and expressed the hope
that the discussion in the Staff Group on the previous day would resolve or
eliminate most of the problems which might arise;

REPORT BY THE TRI-SERVICE GROUP ON AIR DEFENCE-NUCLEAR IMPLICATION!
4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the Tri-Service Group

the Conference of National Armaments Directors (CNAD) would be requested to
approve the report at their meeting on 25th-28th May, 1971 and to pass it to the
NATO Military Authorities for endorsement and implementation. Several paragraphs
(4) of the report had nuclear implications which were of interest to the Nuclear
Planning Group and on which the NPG might wish to take a position;

FOLLOW-UP ACTION ON AD70s REPORT (1)

5. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that it had been
decided by the Defence Planning Committee at their meeting on 24th March, 1971
that the Nuclear problems referred to in Annex I of the AD70s Report would not
be dealt with by the AD70s Executive Working Group but would be taken into
account in the continuing programme of the Nuclear Planning Group and its
submissions made through the NDAC to the Defence Planning Committee (2):

(b) noted also that it was the intention of the Secretary General to call a
meeting of the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent Representatives in June to
discuss the action to be taken;

(c) agreed that the Staff Group should discuss in late April or early May what
preparatory action might be taken.
