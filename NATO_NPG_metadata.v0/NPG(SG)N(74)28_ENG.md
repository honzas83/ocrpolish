> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 11th March, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on March 11, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The group discussed the upcoming circulation of a report by the Ad Hoc Study Group on Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy and Military Doctrine for the Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons, noting that this would be a key agenda item for the NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting. Regarding the United States nuclear deterrent policy, the group reviewed a resume of points raised during a meeting of Permanent Representatives and the statement made by the United States Ambassador. The meeting also addressed the draft report on the Military Committee's function in the Nuclear Consultation Process, agreeing to modifications and scheduling a further revision for March 25. Additionally, the group discussed the timing of the next meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives, preferring Thursday, May 9, to prepare for the June Ministerial. Finally, the group considered a suggestion to include a brief statement by the Minister of Defence of the Host Country on nuclear issues affecting their country in the agenda of NPG Ministerial Meetings, though it was noted that this should not be considered obligatory.

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 11th

WARSAW PACT STRATEGY AND DOCTRINE
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the report by the Ad
Hoc Study Group on Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy and Military Doctrine
for the Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons(1) would be circulated within the next
24 hours; this matter would certainly become an agenda item for the NPG Spring
Ministerial Meeting, and it was therefore important to begin the process of
preparing a report for Permanent Representatives;

(b) concurred with the Chairman's remarks and agreed to hold a meeting during
the course of the week for the express purpose of making a preliminary
examination of the report.

UNITED STATES NUCLEAR DETERRENT POLICY
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to PO/74/24,
dated 11th March, 1974; at Annex A to this PO was a resume of the points raised
during the discussion at the meeting of Permanent Representatives held on 4th
February, and at Annex B the statement made at that meeting by the United States
Ambassador on US nuclear

MILITARY COMMITTEE FUNCTION IN THE NUCLEAR CONSULTATION PROCESS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced a new draft(1) of a
possible Permanent Representatives' report on the Military Committee Function in
the Nuclear Consultation Process, revised in the light of the discussion held at
the last meeting;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing detailed discussion which
covered the substance, text and form of the report; agreed to a number of
modifications;

(c) invited the Chairman, in the light of the discussion, to prepare, in time
for examination on 25th March, a further revision of the report;

(d) noted that the progress of work on this matter should be as such as to
ensure that the report, in the form of an NPG Working Paper for consideration in
capitals, should be ready for circulation before Easter.

NEXT MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
4. The STAFF GROUP:

BRIEFING ON NUCLEAR ISSUES AFFECTING THE HOST COUNTRY
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled the suggestion made at the previous meeting that the agenda of NPG
