> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters on Tuesday, 8th April, 1969
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met at NATO Headquarters on April 8, 1969, to address several key agenda items. First, the group proposed Wednesday, April 30, 1969, as the date for the next meeting of the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent Representatives, pending confirmation from Belgium and the United States. Regarding atomic demolition munitions, the group noted that the draft document NPG/D(69)1(Final) is ready for circulation, though publication is delayed until the German representative receives official approval. Additionally, the group discussed the role of theatre nuclear strike forces, reviewing redrafts of papers from the United Kingdom and the United States. They agreed to a two-stage study process: a short interim paper for ministers in May and a more comprehensive study for a subsequent ministerial meeting. The United Kingdom representative was tasked with preparing a further draft by April 11. Finally, the group scheduled upcoming meetings for April 10, 1969, to discuss Warsaw Pact Initial Use and the role of theatre nuclear strike forces.

THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES

3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of the re-draft of the first part of the United Kingdom paper,
prepared as requested at the previous meeting;

(b) took note of the new paragraph 8 prepared by the United States
representative, as requested at the last meeting;

(c) discussed the new paragraph 8 and invited the United Kingdom and United
States representatives to prepare a modified version in the light of comments
made;

(d) discussed in detail the form, content and background of the draft papers on
the subject and noted which parts of each should be incorporated in the next
draft;

(e) noted a growing consensus of opinion regarding the contents of the paper and
the issues to be raised;

(f) considered that the study should possibly be conducted in two stages, first,
a short interim paper presenting the major issues for consideration by ministers
in May, and second, a more ample study, if possible in time for the following
ministerial meeting;

(g) invited the United Kingdom representative, in the light of the views
expressed during the discussion to prepare a further draft for issue before
Friday evening, 11th April, for discussion at the following meeting of the Staff
Group;

(h) agreed to give further discussion to the questions of the title and status
of the report and the procedure leading up to its presentation to Ministers.
