> [!abstract] 
> # Provision of Distribution Lists
> 
> This document, issued by the Secretary of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) on 10 February 1971, addresses the provision of distribution lists for nuclear documents. The NATO Central Registry periodically requests delegations to revise their requirements for document copies to reduce unnecessary distribution. The Secretary notes that while the NATO policy is to keep distribution to a strict minimum based on a 'need-to-know' basis, insufficient copies can lead to excessive photocopying, which poses security and economic risks. The Secretary, M.H.G. Wilson, asks representatives to examine the requests for extra copies listed in the attached Annex and inform him during the next Staff Group meeting whether they wish to maintain or reduce these requests. The Annex provides a detailed list of current and requested distribution numbers for the NDAC, NPG, and NPG Staff Group series for delegations from Canada, Germany, West Germany, Portugal, the United Kingdom, Italy, the United States, Belgium, the Netherlands, and Norway.

NOTICE NPG(Staff Group)N(71)21

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
PROVISION OF DISTRIBUTION LISTS

Note by the Secretary

Every six months the NATO Central Registry sends a letter to delegations
requesting them to revise their requirements of documents and where possible to
reduce the number of copies distributed to them. I have received from the
Central Registry a list of requests for increases in the number of copies of
nuclear documents in the series NDAC, NPG and NPG Staff Group. Details of these
requests are attached as Annex.

2. In the case of the NPG Staff Group, and this applies to the NPG and NDAC, we
have always adopted the policy of keeping the distribution of our papers to a
strict minimum and to a "need-to-know" basis. When a delegation for a particular
reason requires extra copies of a particular document it has usually been
possible to supply them.

3. It is realised, however, that if delegations receive insufficient numbers of
copies this may be detrimental to efficiency and may result in an excessive
amount of photocopying, which is undesirable both from the point of view of
security and economy.

4. I believe that for these reasons requests for extra copies may have
originated from delegation registries without the knowledge of the members of
the Staff Group. In any case it will be much appreciated if representatives will
examine the requests concerning their delegation and inform me during the course
of the next meeting of the Staff Group whether they wish to maintain the
requests as they are or whether they are able to reduce them.

(Signed) M.H.G. WILSON

NATO, 1110 Brussels
