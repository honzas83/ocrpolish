> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 3rd March, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on March 3, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The group discussed arrangements for 'TINEX 75', including the timing of nuclear matters and post-exercise reporting. They also addressed the preparation of a Permanent Representatives' report on the Phase II Report, noting deadlines for final versions and the need for a long-term examination of nuclear guidelines and technological advances. The meeting covered the logistics of the NPG Ministers' Spring Meeting, specifically the potential conflict with SACLANT's Symposium in Annapolis and the possibility of moving the ministerial meeting to Annapolis. The proposed agenda for the Spring Ministerial meeting included briefings on the strategic forces balance, communicating NATO's intentions, and an illustrative study on air defence use. Additionally, the the group noted the possibility of a meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives on March 13 to discuss the Nunn amendment paper and planned a visit to SHAPE. Finally, the German representative offered to host the Autumn meeting of NPG Ministers in Germany, and the group scheduled their next meetings for March 14 and March 21, 1975.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 3rd
March, 1975

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that all persons present were cleared for the security classification of
the matters to be discussed.

EXERCISE V. 75
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) warmly endorsed the welcome extended to
Ir. L. M. Moran, Director of Council Operations;
(b) took note of a statement by the Director of Council Operations, concerning
the arrangements for "TINEX 75";

(c) noted, in particular, Mr. Moran's remarks with regard to the manner and
timing in which nuclear matters would be introduced into the exercise and the
probable sequence of events after the receipt of WHISKY messages;

(d) noted the further remarks by Mr. Moran concerning post-exercise reporting;
he foresaw a co-ordinated report by the MNCs, a possible report by the NPG and a
possible report by the CO3C on Crisis Management aspects;

(e) took note of the questions raised in the ensuing discussion and of the
replies given by Mr. Moran;

PREPARATION OF A PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES' REPORT ON THE
PHASE II REPORT
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who referred to the Phase II
Report(1) and expressed his thanks to the Phase II Study Team for its good work;

(b) took note of the remarks of the United Kingdom and United States
representatives who said that the deadline (20th March, 1975) for the submission
of the final version for the Phase II Report would be respected; it was hoped
that the texts of proposed changes could be communicated to the Chairman before
the deadline;

(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and, in
particular, those of the Canadian and United States representatives who
suggested that the report by the Permanent Representatives on the Phase II
Report might be the starting point for a long-term examination of the inter-
relationship of past nuclear guidelines papers and the future situation
resulting from the implications of technological advances, the repercussions of
the Non-proliferation Treaty and other recent developments in nuclear thinking;

(d) agreed to continue the discussion of this matter at the next meeting.

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the conflict
between the dates of the NPG Ministers' Spring Meeting (16th and 17th June,
1975) and SACLIANT's Symposium at Annapolis during the latter part of the same
week; he said that this situation created logistic problems particularly with

(b) noted the remarks of the United States representative who said that he had
unfortunately no fresh information to give to members; he requested

possibility of holding the NPG Ministerial Meeting in or near Annapolis.

Agenda
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the agenda for
the Ministerial meeting would have as principal items:

(i) briefing on the balance of strategic forces,
(ii) Communicating NATO's Intentions,
(iii) Illustrative Study - Air Defence Use;
he recalled that other possible agenda items were listed in NPG(Staff
Group)N(74)19, 13th December, 1974;

(b) requested the Chairman to contact SHAPE regarding the possibility of
arranging for a verbal statement by SACEUR on Selective Employment Planning.

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that it might be necessary to call a meeting of the NPG Permanent
Representatives on or about 13th March with a view to consulting on the Nunn

STAFF GROUP VISIT TO SHAPE
7. The STAFF GROUP:

8. The STAFF GROUP:

NEXT MEETINGS OF THE STAFF GROUP
9. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed, bearing in mind the dates of WINTEX, not to meet on Monday, 10th
March and instead to meet on Friday, 14th March, at 10 a.m.; this meeting would
have as principal objects, to discuss the results of WINTEX 75 and to give
further consideration to the Phase II Report;

(b) agreed that the subsequent meeting of the Staff Group should take place one
week later on Friday, 21st March, 1975 at 10 a.m.
