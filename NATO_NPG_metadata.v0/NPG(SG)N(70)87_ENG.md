> # Draft Paragraphs Pertaining to Nuclear Matters for Inclusion in DPC Communique
> 
> This document, issued by the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), contains draft paragraphs intended for inclusion in the Defence Planning Committee (DPC) communique of the December Ministerial meeting. The Chairman, H. B. Sein, notes that the proposed text is intended for an initial exchange of views before being finalized by the drafting Committee. The annex provides the draft text, which mentions that the Defence Ministers of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee—comprising Belgium, Canada, Denmark, Germany, Greece, Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, Turkey, the United Kingdom, and the United States—reviewed the work of the Nuclear Planning Group. It further details that the Defence Planning Committee adopted two policy documents regarding the role of the Alliance's nuclear forces and guidelines for the use of atomic demolition munitions, while refining political guidelines for the initial defensive tactical use of nuclear weapons. All these strategic documents are based on NATO's concept of flexibility in response, which remains the basis of allied strategy.

ANNEX to NPG(Staff Group)N(70)87

DRAFT PARAGRAPHS PERTAINING TO NUCLEAR MATTERS
FOR INCLUSION IN DPC COMMUNIQUE

The Ministerial meeting also provided the Defence Ministers comprising the
Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (Belgium, Canada, Denmark, Germany, Greece,
Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, Turkey, the United Kingdom and the United
States) with the occasion to review work in progress in the Nuclear Planning
Group during the past year and planned for the future.

2. Acting upon the recommendation of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee, the
Defence Planning Committee adopted two policy documents originated by the
Nuclear Planning Group at their meeting in Ottawa last October concerning the
rôle of the Alliance's nuclear forces and guidelines for the possible use of
atomic demolition munitions. The Defence Planning Committee refined the
political guidelines for the initial defensive tactical use of nuclear weapons
which it had adopted a year ago. All these documents are based upon NATO's
concept of flexibility in response which remains the basis of allied strategy.
