> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 2nd February, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on February 2, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several administrative and strategic items. The meeting covered security clearances for all attendees and the preparation of a draft paper on Communicating NATO's Intentions (CNI) procedures. The group discussed an omnibus presentation regarding newly developed procedures for testing in WINTEX 77, intended for ministerial consideration in Autumn 1976. Additionally, the group reviewed follow-up actions from the Hamburg ministerial meeting, including the need for a Permanent Representatives' report on the Theatre Nuclear Force Posture and a letter from the Secretary General to the Chairman of the Military Committee. The Staff Group expressed a desire for early confirmation of the dates for the 1976 Spring and Autumn NPG ministerial meetings to facilitate planning. The representative from the United Kingdom raised concerns regarding the survivability of NATO Headquarters and its implications for nuclear consultation. Finally, the Chairman mentioned plans to arrange visits to SHAPE and operational units in Germany, and the group agreed to meet again on February 9, 1976.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 2nd
February, 1976

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared for the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTIONS (CNI)
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that his directorate was
preparing a draft paper on CNI procedures, incorporating matter from
DPP/NPD/75/59; this paper would be circulated in time for the next meeting.

OMNIBUS PRESENTATION
3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman who said that an informal meeting had
taken place in his office on 28th January to discuss a comprehensive
presentation of newly developed procedures for testing in WINTEX 77 (for
ministerial consideration at their Autumn 1976 NPG meeting); he hoped that the
ad hoc working group would be able to provide a draft of the presentation at the
end of the month for Staff Group discussion.

FOLLOW UP ACTION TO THE HAMBURG MINISTERIAL MEETING

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced NPG(Staff
Group)N(76)9, dated 30th January, 1976 which summarised and commented upon
actions required to implement decisions taken and recommendations made by NPG
Ministers at their recent Hamburg meeting; he invited comments;

(b) took note of the views expressed particularly... with regard to the
following points:

(i) the possible contents of the 1976 Spring and Autumn NPG ministerial
meetings;

(ii) the need for a Permanent Representatives' report taking into account the
preliminary assessment by the NATO Military Authorities of the Theatre Nuclear
Force Posture, which should be available for the Spring ministerial meeting;

(iii) the dispatch of a letter from the Secretary General to the Chairman,
Military Committee, recalling the wishes of the NPG Ministers and requesting
appropriate reports and assessments from the Major NATO Commanders;

(c) noted general agreement with the content of the International Staff's paper.

DATES OF 1976 SPRING AND AUTUMN MINISTERIAL MEETINGS

5. The STAFF GROUP:

expressed the hope that early information would become available concerning the
dates of NPG ministerial meetings in 1976 in order to facilitate the planning of
work schedules.

OTHER ACTIVITIES OF INTEREST TO THE STAFF GROUP

6. The STAFF GROUP:

took note of the remarks of the United Kingdom
