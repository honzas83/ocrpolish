> # Suggested Modifications to the Draft Report on the WP Strategy Study
> 
> This document, issued by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) on 17 April 1974, outlines suggested modifications to a draft report concerning the Warsaw Pact (WP) strategy study. The modifications are necessitated by the new intelligence assessment of Warsaw Pact strength and capabilities recently completed by the Ad Hoc Intelligence Working Group (AHIWG). The document provides a detailed list of ten specific textual changes, including deletions, insertions, and additions to footnotes, to ensure the report reflects the current 1974 NATO agreed intelligence estimate. These changes specifically address the risk of abrupt escalation if NATO were to employ nuclear weapons and the likelihood that the Warsaw Pact would seek to limit conflict through political means while preparing for escalation. The proposed modifications are intended for consideration by the Staff Group at its meeting on 22 April 1974.

THE DRAFT REPORT(1) ON THE WP STRATEGY STUDY(2)
Note by the Chairman
Noting that the Ad Hoc Intelligence Working Group (AHIWG) had just completed
revising the NATO agreed intelligence assessment of Warsaw Pact Strength and
Capabilities, the Staff Group agreed, during consideration of the draft
Permanent Representatives report(1) at its meeting on 8th April, that any
relevant aspects of the new intelligence assessment should be taken into
account. A draft of the new AHIWG estimate has now been circulated for Military
Committee approval(3).

2. Attached for Staff Group consideration at its next meeting on Monday, 22nd
April, are some suggested modifications to the draft Permanent Representatives
report, based on a review of the new AHIWG estimate. Also included are a few
proposed changes of a minor nature to reflect views expressed at the last Staff
Group meeting.
