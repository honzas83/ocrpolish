> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday 4th March, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on March 4, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting's primary focus was the discussion of a draft report on the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process, including guidelines for consultation and the resolution of national differences. The Staff Group also reviewed documentation regarding the technological developments of tactical nuclear weapons and the downgrading of classified documents. Other agenda items included the status of the Warsaw Pact Doctrine Study and the timing of the Phase II Study Team's report. Logistical arrangements were planned for a meeting in Bergen, including air transport for couriers and accommodation for participants. Additionally, the Staff Group agreed to a visit to the Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE) on April 23, 1974, to review strike planning and release procedures. The group also scheduled its next meetings, noting a cancellation for March 18 due to Exercise HILEX-6.

Administration Arrangements

6. The STAFF GROUP:

STAFF GROUP VISIT TO SHAPE

7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted with appreciation the invitation of the SHAPE representative who said
that his authorities would be happy to organise a visit by the NPG Staff Group
to SHAPE on the afternoon of Tuesday, 23rd April, if this date were agreeable;
he outlined the proposed programme which would include strike planning, release
procedures and a visit to SHOC;

(b) agreed that 23rd April was acceptable and invited the International Staff to
examine the possibility of transport by a NATO bus.

FUTURE MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

8. The STAFF GROUP:
