> # SHAPE Report Use of the Term "Escalation"
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), provides a detailed content analysis of the term "escalation" as used in a SHAPE report on early combined use. The analysis breaks down the term into several categories: definition, speculation about origin and impact, possible consequences, and its relationship with escalation risk. Escalation is defined as a significant increase in combat intensity or the enlargement of the battlefield area, and is noted as a significant uncertainty when facing a nuclear-capable enemy. The document discusses how escalation could result from the use of nuclear weapons or a combination of conventional and nuclear forces, and notes that fear of escalation has historically acted as a deterrent against Communist aggression. Factors influencing escalation risk include the likelihood of an enemy response of equal or greater magnitude, the type of target selected, and the cause of collateral damage. The analysis further explores how escalation risk might be reduced through the use of small yield, purely defensive weapons or by targeting non-populated areas. It also addresses the concept of deliberate escalation, which is decided at a broader command level and governed by the requirement for force survival and effective defense. Finally, the document notes that military control of escalation can be achieved through the judicious use of combined conventional and nuclear resources.
> 
> ## Categories/Topics
> 
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Flexible-Response — The document is a content analysis of the term 'escalation' and 'escalation risk' (ER), specifically discussing 'deliberate escalation' (DE) and the 'judicious and timely use of the most effective combination of the conventional and nuclear resources available'. This aligns with the core tenets of Flexible Response (MC 14/3), which emphasizes tailored, escalating force over immediate total war.
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Nuclear-Deterrence — The document explicitly mentions that the 'Fear of E [escalation] has prevented the Communists from aggression (deterrence)' in section 7(a), directly linking the concept of escalation management to the overarching goal of nuclear deterrence.

CONTENT ANALYSIS OF THE SHAPE REPORT(1)
1. In this analysis, use of the term "ESCALATION" (escalatory etc.) in the SHAPE
Report is discussed in the following aspects:

(a) definition
(b) speculation about origin and impact
(c) possible consequences
(d) in connection with other terms, for example, escalation risk, fear of
escalation, military control of escalation.

2. Definition of Escalation(E)
(a) E is a significant increase in the intensity or magnitude of the combat
action or enlargement of the battlefield-area(9c)

(b) E is a most significant uncertainty in any use against a fully nuclear-
capable enemy (39) (46).

3. Speculation about Origin and Impact
(a) E could result from the use of nuclear weapons (9c)
(b) E might result from the conduct of effective military operations involving a
combination of conventional and nuclear weapons (41)

(c) E has been feared by Communist leaders (38)
