> # Allied Command Europe Senior Officers' Orientation Course
> 
> The Allied Command Europe Senior Officers' Orientation Course (I-31) is conducted by the NATO School (SHAPE) in Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarize senior NATO officers and key personnel in staff appointments with the major concepts of NATO weapons' employment in Allied Command Europe. Eligibility is limited to officers of the rank of Colonel and above, and civilian officials of equivalent grade, on a need-to-know basis, requiring a COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance. The course lasts one week from Monday through Friday, with a tuition cost of 150 United States dollars per student. The document lists eight course dates in 1983, ranging from February to December, with some courses offering French translation. Tuition for personnel of NATO Delegations must be paid by their national authorities. Officials in capitals and national commands should apply through their Ministry of Defence, while those at NATO Headquarters in Brussels should apply through the Nuclear Planning Directorate of the International Staff. Bids for vacancies must be submitted to SHAPE by 25th October 1982, and members of Delegations should send applications to Mr. R. Serio or Miss K. Winters by 20th October 1982.

Note by the Chairman

The Allied Command Europe Senior Officers' Orientation Course (short title:
I-31) is conducted by the NATO School (SHAPE) in Oberammergau, Germany. The
purpose of the course is to familiarize senior NATO officers and key personnel
in staff appointments with the major concepts of NATO weapons' employment in
Allied Command Europe. Officers of the rank of Colonel and above and civilian
officials of equivalent grade are eligible to attend on a need-to-know basis. A
COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance is required. The course lasts one week – Monday
through Friday. Tuition costs are 150 United States dollars per student, which
for personnel of NATO Delegations must be paid by their national authorities.
The course is offered on the following dates in 1983. The annotation "F" in the
last column below shows courses for which French translation will be available.
