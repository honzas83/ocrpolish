> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters on Monday, 27th January, 1969
> 
> The NATO Nuclear Planning Group Staff Group held a meeting on January 27, 1969, at NATO Headquarters to address several strategic nuclear issues. The group discussed the role of theatre nuclear strike forces and requested SHAPE representatives to provide further replies to specific questions. Regarding Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADM) time factors, the group noted that a report issued on December 20, 1968, was required by Italian authorities for developing political guidelines for the use of ADMs, while German and United Kingdom authorities were awaiting the results of this study. The group agreed that this report should be brought to the attention of Permanent Representatives and that an ambassadorial-level meeting should be place in February. Additionally, the group noted that the International Staff would distribute a paper on Soviet initial use of nuclear weapons and agreed to address this subject in the future, preferably on February 24. Finally, the group established an agenda for its next meeting on February 3, 1969, which will be classified as abnormal, and included items such as the study on consultation and the agenda for the NPQ Ministerial meeting in London.

THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES
1. The STAFF GROUP addressed the Working Paper of 21st January (1) and requested
the SHAPE representatives to reply to the questions set out therein.

2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted and discussed the replies given by the SHAPE representatives;
(b) noted that there was insufficient time to discuss all the replies and agreed
that the discussion should continue at the next meeting of the Staff Group;

(c) invited Rear Admiral Harty and his colleagues from SHAPE to attend the next
meeting.

3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) recalled that the Permanent Representatives had invited the Staff Group to
submit a report on ADM Time Factors by the end of 1968, and that this report had
been issued on 20th December, 1968(2);
