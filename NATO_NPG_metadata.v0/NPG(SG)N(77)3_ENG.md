> # NPG Future Work Priorities
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines a proposed strategy for addressing future work priorities. Following a meeting on 10th January 1977, and subsequent discussions with representatives from the United Kingdom and the United States, the Chairman suggests that it is premature to set final recommendations for future work due to the status of several ongoing activities. These activities include the New Technology Study, Phase III, studies in the TNF field, NIKE HERCULES, Dual-capable Aircraft, the Maritime Nuclear Warfare Study, and the TIES status report, as well as discussions on the nuclear balance and cruise missiles. Consequently, the Chairman proposes a two-step process: first, a status of work report to advise the Permanent Representatives and the IPG community of current activities, and second, a Permanent Representatives' report on future work activities to be developed in the Spring of 1977 for the next Ministerial meeting. Staff Group members are invited to discuss this approach at their next meeting on 17th January 1977.

Note by the Chairman
At their meeting on 10th January, 1977, the Staff Group discussed. the working
paper on Future Work Priorities(1). National representatives offered views on
the purpose and structure of the paper, and the United Kingdom and United States
Representatives were ready to put forward additional and alternative language in
writing. It was then agreed to meet in my office on 11th January with the UK and
US Representatives to consider these two drafts prior to further Staff Group
discussion. As a result of the 11th January discussion, the following is
forwarded for consideration by the Staff Group.

2. The original draft on Future Work Priorities dealt with a broad spectrum of
thoughts on this issue and quite deliberately went beyond a stocking exercise to
set out alternatives for decisions with regard to priorities. Now it has been
suggested that it might be too early to reflect fully on these priorities since
it is expected that within the next two months the status of a number of the on-
going activities will be changed considerably, for example, the New Technology
Study, Phase III, the on-going studies in the TNF field, NIKE HERCULES and Dual-
capable Aircraft, the initiation of the Maritime Nuclear Warfare Study,
preparation of the TIES status report, and the continuation by Permanent
Representatives of the discussion on the nuclear balance, including the
assessment of the role and value of cruise missiles. Since a full appreciation
of the progress made in these activities over the next few months will be of
critical importance in preparing recommendations for future work to Ministers,
it appears that it would be premature to go
