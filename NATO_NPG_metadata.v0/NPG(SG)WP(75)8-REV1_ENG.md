> # Consultation Procedures – Country Request
> 
> This document outlines the procedures for member governments within the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) to formulate and process requests for the possible use of nuclear weapons. The report, drafted by the Permanent Representatives, was prompted by discussions during WINTEX 75 in June 1975, where NPG Ministers invited the Permanent Representatives to establish more detailed procedures for nuclear release requests. It emphasizes that the ultimate decision on the use of nuclear weapons always lies with the nuclear power, distinguishing between proposals from nuclear powers and requests from member governments or Major NATO Commanders. The procedures cover both national assessments and formal requests for use, specifying that such communications should be sent via AIG 6014 with FLASH precedence. Upon receipt of a country request, the concerned Major NATO Commanders (MNCs) should provide a military assessment and recommended courses of action to the Council/DFC and capitals. The document concludes with a recommendation that the Ministers take note of the report and approve the suggested procedures for processing country requests for the possible use of nuclear weapons.

Draft Report by the Permanent Representatives
I. PURPOSE
1. This document outlines procedures for formulating and processing requests for
the possible use of nuclear weapons by member governments within the frame of
current procedures for nuclear consultation.

II. BACKGROUND
2. During their discussion of WINTEX 75 at Monterey in June 1975, NPG Ministers
invited(1) the Permanent Representatives to examine the need for establishing
more detailed procedures for formulating and processing nuclear release requests
from NATO member governments. The possibility that member governments may
discern a need to forward requests for the use of nuclear weapons is recognised
in the General Guidelines for Consultation Procedures(2), and in an earlier
Permanent Representatives' report(3), and it is implied in the Provisional
Political Guidelines(4). But the procedural aspects concerning such requests are
treated only in very general terms in these documents.

3. Experience gained during WINTEX 75 pointed to the need for amplified
procedures for country requests as an aid to expediting the consultation
process. Therefore the Permanent

4. In dealing with these procedural matters, some basic principles should be
kept in mind:

(a) The nuclear consultation process, whether activated by an MNC or a country
request, does not culminate in a use decision in the NAC/DPC. The General
Consultation Guidelines state(1) that the ultimate decision on the use of a
nuclear weapon will always lie with the nuclear power.

(b) Consequently, the Guidelines distinguish(2) between a proposal for the use
of nuclear weapons in defence of NATO originating from a nuclear power, and
requests from a member government or from a Major NATO Commander. It is
understood, in further elaboration of these basic principles, that a nuclear
power also can-forward a request-for-the use of nuclear weapons by another
nuclear power.

III. PROCEDURES
5. Depending upon circumstances, a NATO member government may raise the issue of
the possible use of nuclear weapons by a warning in general terms or by a more
definite request. There is a degree of procedural similarities between these two
actions and those available in the same context to the Major NATO Commanders.
Therefore, it appears desirable to strive for similarity in the procedures
concerning such actions so as to reduce their volume and facilitate common
understanding and rapid handling of the various related messages.

Assessment
6. Under certain conditions, politico-military assessments prepared by member
governments in time of hostilities could properly contain national views on a
possible need for the use of nuclear weapons in defence of the Alliance. These
assessments should not be bound to any rigid format, but they should contain
sufficient information to allow the initiation of specific nuclear consultation
and help member governments and the Major NATO Commanders concerned in preparing
their views of the national assessment. The address on a national assessment of
this kind should be the same as for nuclear consultation messages originated by
MNCs, that is AIG 6014, which includes all political and military authorities
concerned with the consultation process. The precedence should be FLASH, while

Request for Use.

7. If a NATO country decides that a request for use of nuclear weapons should be
put forward to the attention of political authorities in the Alliance, it
should, in addition to any other action the country may take to inform NATO
capitals of its views on such issue, send a message to the Council/DFC, capitals
and MNCs expressing its request in as much detail as possible, including all
information which may help in carrying out the consultation process. This
information should state, in particular, the politico-military objectives of the
requested use, including the possible consequences of non-use.

8. According to the NATO organisation and current policy, only MNC(s) have
authority to implement the weapon release decisions and to direct nuclear weapon
use. For this reason, the country submitting the request should address it
simultaneously to the political authorities and to the MNC(s). The use of AIG
6014 would satisfy such necessity.

9. Since, as stated above, the implementation of the use decision is the
responsibility of the MNCs, country requests should fit into the machinery of
the MNCs Nuclear Weapons Release Procedures (IWRP) and into the nuclear
consultation process without disrupting the in-being proven arrangements. In our
opinion the following suggestions meet this requirement.

10. Upon receipt of a country request the HNC(s) concerned should provide, as
soon as possible, a military assessment of the request to the Council/DFC and
capitals including, where appropriate, recommended courses of action to satisfy
the purposes of the requested use. To facilitate consultation, the MNC should
also provide those elements of information which are necessary to the process of
consultation(1) which may be lacking in the country request. Under certain
circumstances, the MNC concerned may generate a request in the appropriate
format (ROMEO or SIERRA), making reference to the original national request. We
do not think that a message prepared by the requesting country need be bound to
any particular format provided the requirements for rapid distribution (AG 6014
and FLASH precedence) and clear identification (NUCLEAR CONSULTATION MESSAGE
label) are fulfilled. The internal distribution and handling of these messages
will be in accordance with present procedures for nuclear consultation messages.
The currently established procedures for nuclear consultation(2) would then be
followed in processing the country request to a decision by the nuclear
power(s).
