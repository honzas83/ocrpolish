> # WINTEX 75
> 
> The Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) issued a note suggesting that NPG Permanent Representatives prepare a report on the WINTEX 75 exercise. This report is intended for presentation to the NPG Ministers at their Spring meeting, provided it contains items worthy of discussion. Because other official reports from the Council Operations Exercise Committee (COEC) and Military Authorities will not be available in time, the Staff Group is encouraged to gather their own material by observing exercise activity and seeking assistance from delegation colleagues. The note recalls lessons learned from WINTEX 73, emphasizing the need to refine procedures for consultation on nuclear weapon use, including the timeliness of information and the classification of selective release messages. It references several previous reports and measures to improve the nuclear consultation process, such as NPG/D(72)2 and NPG/D(73)3. Additionally, the document mentions the participation of the Defence Planning Committee (DPC) in WINTEX 75 and reminds members that special passes are required for entry into the Situation Centre and the Presentation and Consultation Rooms during DPC meetings.

Note by the Chairman

During recent Staff Group discussions, it was concluded that it might be
desirable for the NPG Permanent Representatives to prepare a report on WINTEX
'75. It was agreed that such a report would be presented to NPG Ministers at
their Spring meeting only if it contained items worthy of discussion. It was
agreed that the Staff Group would meet on March 14 to consider preparing a draft
of the report.

2. In view of the fact that other reports (c.g., from COEC, Military
Authorities) will not be available in time for consideration by the Staff Group
when preparing their draft report, the Staff Group will have to depend on its
own resources to gather material that might be of interest in a Permanent
Representatives' Report. Consequently, it is suggested that Staff Group members
should plan to observe as much of the exercise activity as possible, whether or
not they have an active role in their respective delegations. Members may also
wish to seek advice and assistance from their delegation colleagues who may be
in a position to contribute. Finally, it is expected that the Staff Group will
be able to draw informally on WINTEX 75 participants from the International
Staff and International Military Staff for information and preliminary comments.

3. In preparing to observe the exercise, it may be useful to recall lessons
learned through WINTEX 73, which led to a number of actions aimed at improving
procedures for consultation on nuclear weapon use and showed that further
experience would help in refining these procedures or in highlighting new areas
requiring corrective actions(1). Such experience might also be useful to confirm
the effectiveness of the actions taken. Topics of interest are among others :
