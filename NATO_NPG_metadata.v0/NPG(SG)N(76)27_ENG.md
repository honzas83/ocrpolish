> # Procedural Aspects of US Paper on Improving the Effectiveness of NATO's Theater Nuclear Force Posture
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines the proposed procedural aspects for handling a United States paper regarding the improvement of the effectiveness of NATO's Theater Nuclear Force (TNF) posture. The process involves Permanent Representatives preparing a report based on military assessments from SACFUR and SACLANT, which will then be advised to Ministers that the initial military assessment phase of the Alliance review has been completed. The proposed modus operandi suggests that Permanent Representatives could meet in the second week of May to discuss the draft report and national views, providing an opportunity for the United States to provide experts and additional information. Following this, the Staff Group could refine the draft report before the final Ministerial-meeting. National views may be attached as annexes to the report or distributed separately to ensure that views of non-attending members are represented. The ultimate goal is for Ministers to provide direction through an Agreed Minute for future actions based on the the United States paper, military assessments, and national views.

Procedural Aspects of US Paper on Improving the Effectiveness of NATO's Theater
Nuclear Force Posture

Note by the Chairman

1. As a result of the Staff Group Meeting on 5th April, 1976, the following is
proposed as the Modus Operandi for forwarding the US paper on Improving the
Effectiveness of NATO's Theater Nuclear Force (TNF) Posture(1) to NPG Ministers
for discussion at their Spring 1976 meeting.

(a) If agreeable to Capitals, Permanent Representatives, upon receipt of the
report from the Military Committee, covering the military assessments by SACFUR
and SACLANT, will prepare a report which will advise Ministers that the initial
phase of the required Alliance review and assessment of the US TNF paper has
been completed, i.e., the Military assessment. This Permanent Representative
report could, if Ambassadors so desire, note for Ministers the highlights of the
US TNF paper and the significant points made by the NATO Military Authorities.
Ambassadors then could call attention to the emphasis placed by Ministers at the
Hamburg NPG meeting on the discussion by the NATO Military Authorities'
assessments and of the national views and contributions at the next Ministerial
meeting.

(b) NPG Permanent Representatives could meet during the second week in May to
discuss the proposed draft report to Ministers and such other related papers
available at that
