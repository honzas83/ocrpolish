> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 27th January, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on January 27, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group discussed the implications of the Nunn Amendment, noting that the United States Secretary of Defense was due to report to Congress on the effects of the re-evaluation of the U.S. nuclear posture. The group expressed concern that the military assessment of the implications of the Nunn Amendment by the Standing Group for Nuclear Planning (SAGNR) and the Military Committee's comments had not yet been available. They decided to await these military assessments before preparing a draft political assessment. They also agreed on the need for an early meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives to discuss the Nunn Amendment and confirmed a briefing on the Phase II Study by the Study Team on February 20. Regarding preparations for NPG Ministerial meetings, the group considered reporting lessons learned from Exercise WINTEX 75 in March. They also noted the Chairman's account of his visit to French nuclear sites and requested a paper on the visit. Finally, the group agreed to hold its next meeting on February 5, 1975.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 27th

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that all persons present were cleared for the security classification of
the matters to be discussed.

IMPLICATIONS OF THE NUNN AMENDMENT
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that the United States Secretary of Defense was due to report to
Congress on 1st April on the effects on NATO of the re-evaluation of the U.S.
nuclear posture; the Secretary General had wished(1) to send the NATO assessment
to Mr. Schlesinger not later than 1st February;

(b) noted with concern that the report by SAGNR on the military assessment of
the implications of the Nunn Amendment had not yet been sent to Permanent
representatives, nor had the Military Committee's comments on the report yet
been finalised;

(c) noted the remarks of the International military Staff representative who
said that he confidently expected the report and the Military Committee's
comments to be available to Permanent Representatives in early February;

(c) discussed the desirability, in order to save time, of inviting the Chairman

(f) agreed to re-open the discussion as early as possible.

FORTHCOMING MEETINGS OF THE NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

3. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) agreed to the need for an early meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives on
the Nunn Amendment question (see paragraph 2(e), above) but noted that it was
not possible at the moment to fix a date;

(b) confirmed that a meeting should take place on Thursday, 20th February, at
which Permanent Representatives would be briefed on the Phase II Study by the
Study Team;

(c) noted with appreciation that this briefing would start with a presentation,
and would take into account any comments received by 7th February.

PREPARATIONS FOR NPG MINISTERIAL MEETINGS

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) agreed, in view of the time factor, to leave open the possibility of
reporting to Ministers lessons which might be learnt during Exercise WINTEX 75
in March;

(b) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who suggested that 'it was time to
begin thinking'.
