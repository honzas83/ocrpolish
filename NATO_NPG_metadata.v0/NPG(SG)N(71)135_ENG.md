> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Friday, 3rd December, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on December 3, 1971, to coordinate several administrative and strategic matters. The group reviewed arrangements for the North Atlantic Council (NDAC) Ministerial Meeting scheduled for December 8, 1971, including seating, access passes, and interpretation services for Italian and Turkish representatives. The meeting also addressed the development of illustrative studies, with the United Kingdom and Belgium recommending that the SHAPE battlefield study be supplemented with informative papers while cautioning against political wargaming. Discussions were held regarding the Air Study and a preliminary assessment of methodology and resource requirements offered by the United States. The group discussed potential dates for the NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting in 1972, noting the United States' preference for coordinating these meetings with other ministerial gatherings to reduce travel. Additionally, the Staff Group considered a possible visit to the United States, with the United States representative agreeing to transmit the group's interest to his authorities. Finally, the group noted a scheduled briefing on the Turkish Thrace and North-Western Turkey Study for NPG Permanent Representatives on January 27, 1972, and set the dates for their next meetings in December 1971 and January 1972.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held on Friday, 3rd December, 1971

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE NDAC MINISTERIAL MEETING ON 8TH DECEMBER

1. THE STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who reviewed the arrangements made for
the NDAC Ministerial Meeting to be held on Wednesday, 8th December, 1971, at
9.30 a.m., and recalled that:

(a) the meeting would take place in Room 14;
(b) each Delegation would have 5 seats, two at the table and three behind;
(c) only those holding "privilege passes" (Ministers and Permanent
Representatives) or violet passes would be admitted(1);

(d) The Chairman of the Military Committee would have a seat at the Conference
Table;

(e) reserved, marked seats would be available for SACEUR, SACIANT, CINCHAN and
their notetakers behind and to the left of the Chairman;

(f) normal English/French simultaneous interpretation would be supplemented by
interpretation in Italian for the Italian Minister and a system of "chuchotage"
for the Turkish Minister.

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled its discussion on illustrative studies held at its meeting on 15th
November, 1971;

(b) took note of a statement by the Chairman, who introduced a paper(1) prepared
by the International Staff suggesting a possible method of developing and
presenting illustrative studies;

(c) took note of the views expressed in the subsequent discussion, and in
particular those of the United Kingdom and Belgian representatives who
recommended that the SHAPE battlefield study should be supplemented by an
informative paper describing the categories of available information and
intelligence, the resources in communications and other facilities, and the
procedures for consultation; they advised strongly against the preparation of a
parallel political study or scenario which might lead to political wargaming
which was undesirable at this stage, and in any case could hardly be ready in
time for the NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting;

(d) invited the United Kingdom representative to circulate a paper setting out
the views of his Authorities;

(e) agreed to continue the discussion early in January.

THE AIR STUDY – FOLLOW-ON USE STUDIES

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that, at its meeting on 22nd November, the Staff Group had
discussed the Air Study and the offer by the United States Authorities to
conduct a preliminary assessment of the methodology and resource requirements;

(b) noted the remarks of the United States Representative who said, in
amplification of his statement on 22nd November, that comments from the NMAs
would also be most welcome;

(c) agreed, since some representatives were without instructions, to decide at
the next meeting upon sending a formal invitation to the United States
Authorities to proceed with the assessment.

MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS IN SPRING 1972

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that at its meeting held on 22nd November, the Danish
Representative had proposed 16th and 17th May 1972, as being suitable dates for
the NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting, and that representatives had agreed to
obtain the views of their authorities;

(b) took note of the views of the United States Representative who said that in
order to avoid making several transatlantic flights his Authorities would prefer
that the meetings of the NPG Ministers, Defence Ministers and Ministers of
Foreign Affairs, should be held as close together as possible;

(c) noted sympathy, but little support, for the United States position;
(d) agreed that the dates of the three meetings were unavoidably linked and
expressed the hope that an early decision, possibly in another forum, might be
reached on this matter;

(e) noted with appreciation a statement by the Danish Representative who said
that he thought his Authorities would offer to receive the NPG not only during
the weeks beginning 8th or 15th May, as previously stated, but also during the
week beginning 23rd May, if this would be helpful.

POSSIBLE STAFF GROUP VISIT TO THE UNITED STATES

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the military
authorities in Washington had indicated to him that they would give serious
consideration to a visit by the Staff Group to the United States; he drew
attention to a short paper(1) by the International Staff setting out the
proposed purpose and scope of the visit together with possible duration and
timing, and administrative and security considerations; he invited comments;
