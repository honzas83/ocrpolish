> [!abstract] 
> # Transportation of Classified Documents to and from Ankara
> 
> This notice from the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) outlines the arrangements for the transportation of classified documents from Brussels to Ankara and back. Mr. K. Bittner, Head of the NATO Cosmic Registry, will be transporting these documents using an official aircraft provided by the Netherlands Authorities. Delegations and the International Staff are requested to deliver their sealed and marked packages to Mr. Bittner by specific deadlines: Friday, 11th May for Delegations and Monday morning, 14th May for the International Staff. Upon arrival in Ankara, documents will be held by the Security organisation for collection on Monday evening or Tuesday morning. For the return journey, documents are to be returned to Mr. Bittner immediately following the meeting to ensure they can depart on the Netherlands aircraft. The notice emphasizes that the weight and bulk of packages should be kept to a minimum and that no unclassified material should be included.

NATIONALLY CLASSIFIED

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

TRANSPORTATION OF CLASSIFIED DOCUMENTS TO AND FROM ANKARA

Note by the Secretary

Mr. K. Bittner, Head of the NATO Cosmic Registry, will transport classified
documents for Delegations and for the International Staff, from Brussels to
Ankara and return. The Netherlands Authorities have very kindly reserved a seat
for Mr. Bittner in their official aircraft which arrives in Ankara probably
about 5 p.m. on Monday, 14th May.

2. Delegations wishing to take advantage of this arrangement are requested to
inform Mr. Bittner (Tel. No. 2481) and to deliver their documents to him not
later than 4 p.m., Friday 11th May, in sealed packages clearly marked with an
identification number and the name of the Delegation and the name of the
person(s) authorised to withdraw the package from safekeeping in Ankara.

3. The same arrangements apply to the International Staff, whose deadline
however will be 9.30 a.m. on Monday morning.

4. On arrival in Ankara the documents will be placed in the safe-keeping of the
Security organisation and can be collected either on the evening of Monday 14th
or on the morning of Tuesday 15th May.

5. With regard to the return journey, Delegations are requested to return their
classified documents, sealed and appropriately marked, immediately after the end
of the meeting (or even before if possible), so that Mr. Bittner can leave with
the Netherlands aircraft.

6. It is requested that the weight and bulk of packages of documents should be
kept to a minimum; please do not include any unclassified material.

1110 Brussels.
(Signed) M.H.G. WILSON
