> # ADM Cratering Effects
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), circulates technical material regarding Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADM) cratering effects. The data is extracted from the United States unclassified field manual FM 5-26, titled 'Employment of Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADM)'. It includes a detailed table showing crater dimensions and depths for various yields ranging from 0.01 to 10 kilotons, comparing surface bursts, bursts at a depth of 5 meters, and bursts at an optimum depth of burst (DOB). The document explains that the optimum depth of burst is designed to maximize crater dimensions while significantly reducing the radioactive fallout area to 1/100 of that of a surface burst of the same yield. Furthermore, it notes that using the optimum depth of burst in conjunction with reducing the yield can decrease fallout by a factor of 1/1,500. The material is intended for the Staff Group's discussion of ADM issues.

Notes
1. Optimum DOB is that depth of burst which optimizes crater dimensions.
2. At optimum depth of burst, the theoretical area over which fallout extends is
reduced to 1/100 of that occurring in a surface burst of the same yield.

3. At optimum DOB, some crater dimensions are obtained with about 4% of the
yield employed on surface.

Example: a 1Kf ADM surface burst produces a crater diameter of 40m; the same
crater diameter is obtained with a 0,04KT ADM exploded at 18m depth. For a given
yield, the ratio

crater diameter optimum DOB / crater diameter surface burst = 2,65
4. The combined effect of using optimum depth of burst and reducing the yield
(while obtaining the same crater dimensions) results in a decrease of fallout by
a factor of 1/1,500.
