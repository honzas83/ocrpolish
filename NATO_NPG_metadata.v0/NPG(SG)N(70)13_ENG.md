> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 9th March, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on March 9, 1970, to review several key documents and documents concerning topics for exploration. The group noted a paper from the United Kingdom Delegation regarding the development of United Kingdom thinking on tactical nuclear weapons and lists of nuclear weapons employment manuals provided by the International Military Staff. Additionally, the group discussed further studies of follow-on tactical use, noting that the Secretary General had requested assistance for study teams from NATO Military authorities, a request that would be forwarded to SHAPE and SACLANT. The meeting also included an informal talk on Selective Release Procedures, which the group agreed would need updating to align with the Guidelines for Tactical Use and Consultation approved by the Ministers. The group decided to continue treating the Selective Release Procedures on an informal basis and planned a further discussion with representatives from SHAPE. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next session on March 16, 1970.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

DOCUMENTS CONCERNING TOPICS FOR STAFF GROUP EXPLORATION(1)
1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who said that two documents had recently been
issued in response to requests by the Staff Group:

(a) a paper(2) from the United Kingdom Delegation on the subject of the
development of United Kingdom thinking regarding tactical nuclear weapons;

(b) lists and tables of contents(3) provided by the International Military
Staff, of nuclear weapons employment manuals.

FURTHER STUDIES OF FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who informed the Staff Group that
the Secretary General had written to the Chairman of the Military Committee
regarding the work envisaged in the NPG document on further studies of follow-on
tactical use(4) and the need for assistance to study teams from the appropriate
NATO Military authorities;

(b) noted a statement by the representative of the International Military Staff
who said that the Chairman of the Military Committee would shortly be forwarding
this request to S.OUR and S.OLANT.

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of an informal talk on the subject of Selective Release Procedures
given by a member of the International Military Staff;

(b) noted that the procedures described in the talk were based upon a S' IF
document prepared before the meeting of Defence Planning Committee Ministers
held in December, 1969;

(c) noted that these procedures would require some up-dating in order to
correspond more closely to the guidance contained in the documents on Guidelines
for Tactica Use(1) and Consultation(2) as approved by the Ministers;

(d) agreed that this matter should continue to be treated on an informal basis,
and that a further discussion in the Staff Group, attended by representatives
from S' IFE, should be held shortly;

(e) discussed various points of detail covered in the talk, and took note of the
views expressed;

(f) expressed its appreciation to the International Military Staff
representative for his valuable and interesting talk.

N'T JULTING
4. The STAFF GROUP: agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 16th March, 1970.
