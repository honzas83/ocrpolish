> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 25th April, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on April 25, 1977, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels, Belgium. The group discussed and established the agenda for the meetings of NPG Permanent Representatives scheduled for May 5th and 23rd, including observations on INTEX 77(1), a New Technology Study, and the NPG Status Report. The group also addressed the draft agenda for the NPG Ministerial Meeting in June in Canada, emphasizing the need to circulate the draft to capitals by the first week of May and considering the inclusion of cruise missiles in the agenda. Additionally, the group noted that Canada and Germany were unable to agree to simultaneous release of an advanced press release, and discussed the process for finalizing the draft report on the New Technology Study. The meeting concluded with the the agreement to hold the next meeting on May 3, 1977.

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared to the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR OTTAWA
2. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that no fresh information was available on arrangements for the Ottawa
Meeting, nor for transatlantic air transport.

AGENDA FOR MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES ON 5TH AND 25TH MAY
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the introductory remarks by the Chairman concerning the agenda
for the meetings of NPG Permanent Representatives on 5th and 23rd May; he
underlined the relationship between these agenda and the NPG Ministerial Agenda
for the June Meeting in Canada;

(b) noted the suggestions made during the ensuing discussion, particularly those
regarding the

DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN JUNE

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who drew attention to the short
time remaining before the NPG Ministerial meeting in Canada, and said that it
was essential to circulate a draft agenda to Capitals not later than the first
week in May; he asked the Staff to consider the desirability of including cruise
missiles in Item I, and how the future work Programme (Item V) should be
prepared;

(b) agreed that members should obtain instructions from their authorities and
that the Ministerial agenda should be discussed at the next meeting.

ADVANCED PRESS RELEASE
5. The Staff Group:
(a) noted tht Canada and Germany were no' able to agree to simultaneous release

(b) invited the members of countries who had not yet replied, to obtain

NEXT TECHNOLOGY STUDY
6. The Staff Group:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the
International Staff's Working Paper(1) on the New Technology Study; he asked for
views on how this draft report when finalised, should be processed, and whether
it should go through the MD/C to the DIC;

(b) agreed to raise the matter, and to consider the content of the draft at the
next meeting.
