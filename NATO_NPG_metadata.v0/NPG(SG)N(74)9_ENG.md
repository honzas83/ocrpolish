> [!abstract] 
> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 21st January, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on 21 January 1974 at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key nuclear agenda items. The group discussed the downgrading of NDAC classified documents and noted that the downgrading of NPG documents from 1967-1970 would soon be undertaken. Regarding deterrence, the group agreed to develop a Working Paper for the approval of NPG Permanent Representatives. They also emphasized the need for a more active role for NPG Permanent Representatives in current nuclear activities. A significant portion of the meeting was dedicated to the United States' nuclear position, specifically regarding the partial retargeting of weapons and the Secretary of Defense's statements on strategic forces flexibility and essential equivalence with the Soviet Union. The group also touched upon technological developments, the communication of NATO's intentions, and an illustrative study on nuclear air defence. Finally, the group noted that a Military Committee report on nuclear consultation would be available in early February and scheduled their next meeting for 28 January 1974.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels,
on Monday, 21st January, 1974

DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled
that a letter concerning the downgrading of NDAC
classified documents(1), circulated on 12 December,
1973, stated that if no objections had been received
by 18th January, 1974, it would be assumed that all
delegations concurred with the downgrading;
(b) noted that, no objections having been received,
the International Staff would issue a downgrading notice
in due course;
(c) noted that the downgrading of NPG documents for
the period 1967-1970 would also shortly be undertaken.

CONCEPTUAL ANALYSIS OF NUCLEAR ASPECTS OF DETERRENCE
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled
the decision not to initiate a study on deterrence
at this time; he drew attention to the paper(2) whose
purpose was to develop an NPG Working Paper for NPG
Permanent Representatives approval, either by the
silence procedure or at an NPG Permanent
Representatives' meeting that might be scheduled
for that purpose in late February or early March;

DEVELOPMENTS IN THE UNITED STATES NUCLEAR POSITION
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of several members who referred to recent press
reports on the partial retargeting of United States weapons mentioned in a
recent statement by Mr. Schlesinger;

(b) took note of a statement by the United States representative who said that

matter; he said that the text of Mr. Schlesinger's statement and his answers to
questions from the press, copied from the Wireless File, had been circulated to
Delegations; the United States Ambassador had also distributed selected extracts
by letter to his colleagues;

(c) took note of clarifying remarks(2) by the United States representative who
said Secretary Schlesinger's comments reflect policy of the United States
administration on strategic forces and its desire to explain policy more fully
to the public; the two major themes are not new: (a) The necessity to achieve

negotiations, but if necessary by unilateral measures, regarding flexibility,
Secretary Schlesinger's remarks elaborate on points made by the United States on

IMPLICATIONS OF TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENTS
5. The STAFF GROUP:

technological study in time for the meeting next week.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTIONS
6. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he hoped to be able to
issue a paper on Communicating NATO's Intentions in the very near future.

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDY FOR SPRING 1974 MEETING
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted with appreciation a statement by the IMS representative who said that
the SHAPE Illustrative Study on nuclear air defence, together with the comments
of the Military Committee, would be available before the end of the week;

(b) noted that this study would be circulated under a NATO cover letter on the
"Study" series (English only).

THE ROLE OF THE MILITARY COMMITTEE ON NUCLEAR CONSULTATION
8. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the IMS representative that the Military Committee
report would be available, hopefully, in early February.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
9. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 28th January, 1974 at 3 p.m.
1110 Brussels.
