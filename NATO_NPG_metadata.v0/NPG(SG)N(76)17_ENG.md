> [!abstract] 
> # Inclusion of Country Request Procedures in the Compilation of Nuclear Consultation Procedures
> 
> This document, issued by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines the inclusion of updated procedures for NATO member governments to formulate and process nuclear release requests. Following a decision by NPG Ministers in Hamburg in January 1976, the document proposes that these procedures be added to the Compilation of Nuclear Consultation Procedures immediately after the 'Information Sources' section. The text details the process for politico-military assessments and requests for the use of nuclear weapons, specifying that such initiatives should remain simple and flexible. Politico-military assessments should contain national views on the need for nuclear weapons in defense of the Alliance, using the FLASH precedence and AIG 6014 address. If a member country decides to make a formal request for the use of nuclear weapons, the message must be addressed to the North Atlantic Council/Defense Policy Committee (NAC/DPC) and Military Committees (MNCs), specifying politico-military objectives and the possible consequences of non-use. The document also includes an annex providing a specific unformatted message format for country requests, including the required precedence, address, and content requirements.

Note by the Chairman

At their meeting in Hamburg in January 1976, NPG Ministers approved(1) amplified
procedures for the formulation and processing of nuclear release requests from
NATO member governments and directed that such procedures should be included in
the Compilation of Nuclear Consultation Procedures(2).

2. While it is understood that the substance of paragraphs 5 through 9 of the
Report by the Permanent Representatives on Country Requests must remain
unchanged, a slight alteration of the language is necessary to tailor the text
to the other parts of the Compilation. In the same vein a general format to be
used by member governments was developed for inclusion in Part III.

3. It is intended that Staff Group members approve the attached draft for
inclusion in the Compilation immediately after "Information Sources".

(Signed) R.E. SHEARER

ACTION INITIATED BY A MEMBER GOVERNMENT
*1. In line with the General Guidelines for Consultation Procedures on the Use
of Nuclear Weapons(1) a NATO member government may, in a politico-military
assessment of 'the' conflict situation, raise the issue of the possible need for
use of nuclear weapons or may make a definite request. To allow modification in
the light of actual circumstances, procedures related to such initiatives have
been kept as simple and flexible as possible.

Assessments
2. Politico-military assessments prepared by member governments in time of
hostilities could properly contain national views on a possible need for the use
of nuclear weapons in defence of the Alliance. These assessments need not
conform to any rigid format, but they would contain sufficient information to
assist member governments and the Major NATO Commanders concerned in preparing
their views. The address on a national assessment of this kind would be AIG
6014, which includes all political and military authorities concerned with the
consultation process. The precedence would be FLASH, while the classification
would be determined by the originator. The consultation process would start as
soon as a country assessment dealing with the possible need for the use of
nuclear weapons is received at

Request for Use
3. If a NATO country decides to make a request for the use of nuclear weapons it
would send a message to the NAC/DPC, capitals and MNCs making the request in as
much detail as possible, and including all information which may help in
carrying out the consultation process. This information would state, in
particular, the politico-military objectives of the requested use and also the
possible consequences of non-use. The consultation process would begin as soon
as a country request is received at NATO Headquarters and in capitals.

4. It is essential for country request messages to be addressed to the MNCs at
the same time as to the political authorities, since the implementation of a
nuclear release decision is the responsibility of the MNCs alone. The use of AIG
6014 would satisfy such necessity. The message prepared by the requesting
country would carry FLASH precedence and a clear identification (NUCLEAR
CONSULTATION MESSAGE label). The internal distribution and handling of these
messages would be in accordance with present procedures for nuclear consultation
messages.

This unformatted message is used by member governments to make a request for the
use of nuclear weapons in defence of the Alliance:

Format of the Message

Precedence
- FLASH

Address
- AIG 6014 – NATO Brussels
Defence Ministries
(with exceptions as appropriate)
- NCS

- As determined by the originator

Designation
- "NUCLEAR CONSULTATION MESSAGE" and at the beginning of the text: "COUNTRY
REQUEST"

Body of the Message
- Unformatted

Content
- Information related to the politico-military situation and objectives of the
