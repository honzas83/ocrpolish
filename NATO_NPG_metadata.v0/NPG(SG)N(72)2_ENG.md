> # Preparations for the Spring 1972 NPG Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines the principal tasks and timetable for preparing for the Ministerial meeting scheduled for May 18th and 19th, 1972. The proposed agenda for the Ministerial meeting includes the balance of strategic forces, the Illustrative Study, and the Follow-on Use Work Programme. Regarding the balance of strategic forces, the United States will provide a briefing. The Staff Group is tasked with preparing reports on the facilities for consulting on requests for the use of nuclear weapons and the report on the Battlefield Use Study.  Additionally, the Permanent Representatives are to prepare reports on the points for Ministerial discussion, the initiation of Phase II work, and a status report on the Work Programme. The document also details a schedule of meetings for the Permanent Representatives, including presentations on the Turkish Follow-on Use Study and the Mediterranean Maritime Follow-on Use Study. Administrative routine actions such as press releases and invitations to Major NATO Commanders are also mentioned. The document concludes with an Annex A containing a detailed timetable of activities from January to March 1972.

NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING
Note by the Chairman
The following paragraphs outline briefly the principal tasks that, in my view,
the Staff Group will have to accomplish in preparing for the Ministerial meeting
to be held on 18th and 19th May, 1972. Mention is also made of the meetings of
the NP Permanent Representatives that are likely to be required. A tentative
timetable reflecting these activities and an Outline Ministerial Agenda are at
Annex. It is intended that the Staff Group discuss those matters at its next
meeting.

Staff Group tasks
2. In the light of earlier Staff Group discussions, it appears likely that the
Spring NPG Ministerial agenda will include the following substantive subjects:

(a) Balance of Strategic Forces
(b) Illustrative Study
(c) Follow-on Use Work Programme
With respect to the first item, balance of strategic forces, it is expected that
this will consist of a briefing by the US and that no particular papers will
have to be prepared in advance by the Staff Group, the Permanent Representatives
or other countries.

3. Illustrative Study. In addition to the SHAPE Battlefield Use Illustrative
Study that is expected to be available for distribution to the NPG by 1st
February, 1972, it is suggested that two reports be prepared: one would be a
factual description of the facilities that are available to the Alliance for
consulting on requests for use of nuclear weapons and the process that would be
followed for such consultation. The other would be a report on the Battlefield
Use Study itself identifying points for possible Ministerial discussion.

4. An initial draft of the descriptive paper is now being prepared for Staff
Group consideration in the near future. It is envisaged that the Staff Group
would produce a Working Paper that could be sent to capitals during the first or
second week of February, with a view to having the Permanent Representatives
take final action on the paper in late February. The final version would be
circulated as an NFC/D document that would be included as a background paper on
the Ministerial agenda. It would also be the source for material on the
political consultation process that might be included in the presentation on the
Battlefield Use Study to be given at the Ministerial meeting.

5. Work on the second paper, i.e., areas for possible Ministerial discussion,
would begin as soon as the SHAPE Battlefield Use Study becomes available. The
Staff Group would seek to complete drafting of a Working Paper to be sent to
capitals during the week of 13th March. The Permanent Representatives would take
final action on the paper, in light of instructions from capitals, during the
week of 10th or 17th April after which it would be issued as a Ministerial
agenda document in the NPG/D series.

6. Permanent Representatives would be given a preview of the Battlefield Use
presentation during the week of 1st May.

7. Follow-on Use Work Programme. It is suggested that three reports should be
prepared by the Permanent Representatives under this general heading, i.e., a
paper identifying points in the studies under consideration that the Ministers
may wish to discuss; a report on the initiation of Phase II work; and a brief
status report on the Work Programme.

8. SHAPE comments on the Turkish Follow-on Use Study are expected to be received
by 1st February and drafting of the discussion points paper should begin shortly
thereafter. When additional Follow-on Use Studies become available, e.g., the
Mediterranean Maritime Study, the Staff Group will have to decide whether to
expand the discussion points paper to cover both studies or whether a separate
report should be prepared for each study.

9. It is intended to make an initial draft of the Phase II report available for
Staff Group consideration in early February. A draft of a status report could be
addressed in late February. The objective would be to agree at Staff Group level
on versions of each of these papers that could be forwarded to capitals as
Working Papers during the week of 15th March. After receipt of reactions from
capitals, the Permanent Representatives would take final action during the week
of 10th or 17th April and the papers would be circulated as Ministerial agenda
documents.

10. Ministerial Agenda. It is envisaged that the Staff Group would develop a
proposed agenda in early March that could, after referral to capitals, be
approved by the silence

11. Other Actions. There are a number of additional routine actions that will be
required, e.g., preparation of an advance press release, invitations to Major

several months in other activities not directly related to the Spring NPG
