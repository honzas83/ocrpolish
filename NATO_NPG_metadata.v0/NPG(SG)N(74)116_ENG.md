> # Draft Report to the NDAC on NPG Activities
> 
> This document is a draft report detailing the 1974 activities of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) for the information of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC). It covers the period since the last NDAC meeting on December 7, 1973, and notes that the NPG convened twice at the Ministerial level in Bergen and Brussels. The report outlines several key areas of activity, including the Phase II Study Team's work on the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons and briefings by the United States Secretary of Defense on the balance of strategic forces and modern technology in the nuclear field. It discusses the importance of the U.S. nuclear commitment to NATO and the importance of deterrence. The report also mentions a study on communicating NATO's intentions (CNI) and a study on the Warsaw Pact's politico-military strategy and military doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear weapons. Furthermore, it addresses the function of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation process and a statement on Norwegian nuclear and base policy. The report concludes with recommendations that the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee take note of the report and invite the NPG to continue its studies and submit proposals on general policy on nuclear defence affairs.

NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP ACTIVITIES DURING 1974
Draft Report by the Nuclear Planning Group
PURPOSE
This report summarises the 1974 activities of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG)
for the information of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee. The report covers
the period since the last NDAC meeting on 7th December, 1973.

ACTIVITIES
2. The NPG convened twice at Ministerial level, in Bergen on 11th and 12th June,
1974 and at NATO Headquarters, Brussels, on 10th December, 1974. The next NPG
Ministerial meeting has been scheduled to take place in the United States in the
Spring of 1975.

3. Countries represented at the 1974 meetings were Belgium(1), Denmark(1), the
Federal Republic of Germany, Greece, Italy, the Netherlands(2), Norway(2), the
United Kingdom and the United States.

4. Actions taken by NPG Ministers at their meetings in Bergen and at NATO
Headquarters, Brussels, are reflected in the Agreed Minutes(3)which have been
circulated to all countries participating in NFG work. All NDAC members hold the
basic documentation discussed at these Ministerial meetings.

(1) Present only in Brussels
(2) Present only in Bergen

Work Programme on Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons
5. The Phase II Study Team, composed of participants from the United States
(discussion leader), the Federal Republic of Germany and the United Kingdom;
during 1974 completed its report covering the comparative analysis of the Phase
I studies, a synthesis of findings, and the identification of the policy
questions which will be needed for the formulation of political guidance (Phase
III). This preliminary report has been circulated recently for written comments
by countries and the NATO Military Authorities and a discussion with the NPG
Permanent Representatives. It is expected that the final Phase II report will be
on one of the NPG Ministerial agendas in 1975.

Balance of Strategic Forces
6. As was the practice in previous years, the NPG Ministers were briefed at the
two meetings by the United States Secretary of Defense on the balance of
strategic forces. Ministers, particularly in the light of détente and recent
Soviet armament improvements, welcomed the detailed explanation of the evolution
of the U.S. targeting doctrine. They agreed that these measures to increase the
selectivity and flexibility of nuclear forces were consistent with the present
strategy of flexible response and made the U.S. nuclear commitment to NATO more
credible thus enhancing deterrence. To be updated after the Autumn meeting.7

7. In this context, Ministers noted with appreciation that the Supreme Allied
Commander Europe had already begun to develop selective employment planning
options. The Major NATO Commanders at the Autumn meeting gave statements on the
status of their work in this field.

Explanation of NATO's Nuclear Doctrine

8. At their Bergen meeting, the NPG Ministers also agreed that an explanation to
the public of the nature and advantage of recent developments in the nuclear
targeting doctrine would assist in dispelling any possible misunderstandings of
that doctrine.

9. The NPG Study on Communicating NATO's Intentions (CNI)(1), already completed,
was undertaken to examine the problems and implications of providing the enemy,
as well as the public, with information concerning NATO's intentions to use
nuclear weapons. The CNI Study will likely be discussed by the NPG Ministers at
their meeting in 1975.

Research and Development with Respect to Nuclear Weapons for Tactical Use

10. In addition to highlighting that a basic feature of reducing the risk of
nuclear war is the continued maintenance and improvement of the conventional
forces of the Alliance, the NPG Ministers at Bergen were also briefed by the
U.S. Secretary of Defense on the possibilities of modern technology in the
nuclear field to include an examination of those technological improvements
which might affect NATO's deterrent posture and defence capabilities.

11. In connection with this review of improved capabilities, the first steps
have been taken to study, in more detail, the political and military
implications of the technological developments affecting the tactical use of
nuclear weapons. It is understood that this study work will function as a
bridgebuilder between what has been learned about the implications of weaponry
presently available during the Follow-on Use Work Programme and the broader
question of stockpile modernisation in Europe as implied in U.S. law provisions
(Nunn Amendment). /To be updated after the Autumn meeting.7

Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy and Military Doctrine for the Tactical
Use of Nuclear Weapons

12. NPG Ministers, at their Bergen meeting, also discussed a number of issues
raised in the Study on the Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy and Military
Doctrine for the Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons(1), prepared by six NPG
countries under the chairman-ship of the Netherlands. It was agreed that this
Study is a valuable contribution toward the objective of reaching a deeper
understanding of the issues examined and of deterrence in particular. Ministers
of the NPG also agreed that the Warsaw Pact Study conclusions do not call into
question the validity of current NATO strategy and other basic policy guidance.
The Study, in view of its potential relevance to political decision-making was
endorsed by the NDAC and forwarded to the DPC.

(1) NPG/Study/45, 8th March, 1974.

13. The DPC agreed that the Warsaw Pact Study and the comments in the Nuclear
Planning Group Report should be taken into account in NATO peacetime planning
and in decision-making in time of tension or conflict. The DPC invited national
authorities and appropriate NATO activities to contribute on a continuing basis
toward improving NATO understanding of Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy
and military doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear weapons.

Function of the Military Committee in the Nuclear Consultation Process

14. The NPG Ministers, during their Bergen meeting, approved a Permanent
Representatives' Report on the Function of the Military Committee in the Nuclear
Consultation Process(1). They agreed that the conceptual approach outlined in
this report is consistent with the General Guidelines for Consultation
Procedures on the Use of Nuclear Weapons(2) and they concurred that the Military
Committee, in carrying out its function in support of the nuclear consultation
process, should conform with the conceptual approach as outlined in the report.
This report was then forwarded to the NDAC for endorsement and to the DPC. The
DPC approved the report and the above mentioned NPG recommendations in August
1974.

Other Activities

15. The Minister of Defence of Norway, in conjunction with the Bergen meeting,
gave a statement on Norwegian nuclear and base policy.
