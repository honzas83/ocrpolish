> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 3rd December, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on December 3, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to coordinate activities and prepare for the upcoming Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) meeting. The group discussed several documents, including the report on NPG activities for 1973 and measures to improve the Nuclear Consultation Process. The meeting's agenda focused on future NPG activities, specifically the study of Communicating NATO's Intentions, the analysis of nuclear aspects of deterrence, and the political aspects of early combined use. The group agreed to re-examine the study on NATO's intentions and decided that further analysis of deterrence was not necessary at this time. Regarding technological developments, the group agreed to form an ad hoc Study Group, with the United States suggested as the study leader. Other administrative matters were discussed, including senior officers' courses at Oberammergau and the scheduling of the NPG Spring Meeting for June 4-5, 1974, where the United States would provide a briefing on the strategic balance. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next Staff Group meeting on December 12, 1973.

FINAL EXCHANGE OF VIEWS PRIOR TO THE NDAC MEETING
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled that the meeting of the
NDAC would begin at 9 a.m. on Friday, 7th December, 1973; each Delegation would
have two seats at the table and three behind, and would receive five violet
passes giving admission to the meeting.

FUTURE NPG ACTIVITIES
3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who invited discussion of five future
NPG activities.

Communicating NATO's Intentions
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that Italy, Norway and the United States were prepared to undertake
a study on Communicating NATO's Intentions but that none wished to be Study
Leader;

(b) noted that while no other representatives had received further instructions,
there appeared to be a growing agreement that the study should be undertaken by
the Staff Group under the auspices of the NPG Permanent Representatives;

(c) agreed to re-examine the matter at the next meeting and invited
representatives to obtain, in the meantime, the further views of their
authorities.

Analysis of the Nuclear Aspects of Deterrence
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Phase II
Study Team had made a short report on Aspects of Deterrence(1), which, in the
view of some representatives, sufficiently covered the matter;

(b) noted, in the ensuing discussion, that whilst deterrence was a fundamental

Political Aspects of Early Combined Use
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that at the last meeting it had appeared that most members
considered, with respect to the Political Aspects of Early Combined Use, that it
was undesirable to proceed beyond the existing report(1) by Permanent
Representatives submitted to NPG Ministers at The Hague meeting;

(b) noted however that the Italian representative hoped shortly to receive
advice on the matter from his authorities and agreed therefore to re-examine the
question as soon as this information was available.

be more appropriate for the Spring meeting, 1975; he invited views;

IMPLICATIONS OF TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENT

8. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced a paper(1) by the
International Staff setting out the background to the need for a study of the
political and military implications of current developments in the technological
field; such a study would be outside and would go beyond the terms of reference
of the Phase II Study Team; he also noted the Study Team suggestion that the
group might usefully consist of representatives of more than three countries;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion, and agreed that
the matter was of special interest and importance and would require much hard
work;

(c) agreed that an ad hoc Study Group should be formed to undertake the task;
noted that the size of the group could have a bearing on the study approach;

(d) noted a general feeling that the study leadership should be taken on by the
United States, in view of their special position as a major nuclear power;

(e) noted that most of those countries whose representatives had instructions
wanted to take part in the ad hoc Group; agreed to re-examine the question of
participation at the next meeting;

SENIOR OFFICERS' COURSES AT OBERAMMERGAU
9. The STAFF GROUP:

THE NPG SPRING MEETING
10. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Norwegian representative who invited those
countries who had not already done so to inform him whether their authorities
could accept the 4th and 5th June, 1974, as dates for the NPG Spring Meeting;

(b) noted with appreciation a statement by the United States representative who
confirmed that a briefing on the strategic balance would be given at the Spring
Meeting, 1974.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
11. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed to hold its next meeting on Wednesday, 12th December, 1973, at 3
p.m.;

(b) noted that the meeting would be followed by a farewell drink to mark Mr.
Moray Stewart's departure, and to meet his successor.
