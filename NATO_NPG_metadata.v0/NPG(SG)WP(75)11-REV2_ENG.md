> # Arrangements for Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme
> 
> This document outlines the scope and organization for preparing a consolidated statement of NATO's concept for the use of theatre nuclear weapons as part of the NATO triad. It details the progression of the Follow-on Use Work Programme, which was divided into three phases: Phase I focused on individual studies of follow-on use options, Phase II involved comparative analysis and synthesis of findings, and Phase III aims to produce a consolidated statement of NATO's theatre nuclear capability and the political and military concepts for its use. The document specifies that the consolidated statement should draw upon a wide range of source materials, including basic policy documents such as the Overall Strategic Concept for the Defence of the North Atlantic Treaty Organisation Area and the Provisional Political Guidelines for the Initial Defensive Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons by NATO. It also lists numerous completed and ongoing studies, including reports on Warsaw Pact strength and capabilities, the transition from conventional to nuclear postures, and the US nuclear weapons employment policy. The proposed content for the Phase III document is intended to include a general statement of NATO's deterrent strategy, the position of theatre nuclear weapons within the NATO triad, and a review of the Warsaw Pact's conventional and nuclear forces. The document was forwarded by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) for discussion at a meeting during the week of 20th October 1975.

I. PURPOSE
This document discusses the scope and organisation for preparing; the
consolidated statement of NATO's concept for the use of theatre nuclear weapons
as part of the NATO triad.

II. BACKGROUND
2. In November, 1969 the Nuclear Planning Group approved the Provisional
Political Guidelines for the Initial Defensive Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons
by NATO(1) which was subsequently promulgated by the Defence Planning Committee
as policy guidance for national authorities and as a directive to NATO Military
Authorities. At the same NPG meeting, Ministers recognised the need for a study
of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons, to examine the phases of tactical
use subsequent to initial use by NATO and to explore NATO responses to an
initial tactical use of nuclear weapons by the Warsaw Pact. Rather than attempt
this task in a single comprehensive study, they agreed to propose proposals put
forward by the Permanent Representatives which defined(2) the objectives,
general assumptions and broad structure of an overall work programme to be
carried out in a series of interrelated steps.

3. The Follow-on Use Work Programme was divided into three major components(1):

(a) Phase I: a series of individual studies to examine in detail the effect of
various follow-on use options on the tactical evolution of specific situations;

(b) Phase II: comparative analysis of the Phase I Studies, synthesis of their
findings and identification of the policy questions;

(c) Phase III: formulation of proposed political guidelines for follow-on
tactical use of nuclear weapons, for endorsement by the Nuclear Defence Affairs
Committee and approval and promulgation by the Defence Planning Committee.

4. Phase I of the Work Programme, in which all NPG member nations had
participated, together with the NATO Military Authorities, came to a conclusion
in May, 1973. Following a decision(2) by the Ministers in May 1972 to initiate
Phase II as suggested by Permanent Representatives(3), a Report on Phase II of
the Work Programme on Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons(4) was developed
in the NFG and forwarded, together with an accompanying report by the Permanent
Representatives(5) to NFG Ministers for their Spring 1975 Monterey Ministerial
meeting. Ministers endorsed the recommendations and conclusions in these reports
and agreed the Permanent Representatives'

Possible Content

8. The full spectrum of considerations to be taken into account for drawing
together all threads of NATO thinking about the use of theatre nuclear weapons
(as well as for the organisation of this material) may be further developed
during the preparation of the consolidated statement. Nevertheless, at this
point of the overall work programme, it is necessary to establish the general
approach and it is possible to identify many of the basic features of a final
synthesis. The following, general summary, which draws on recent country
contributions(1), is intended to provide the basis for further amplification at
the working level.

9. The Phase III document might commence with a general statement of NATO's
deterrent strategy, and of the position of Theatre Nuclear Weapons within the
NATO triad. This interdependence of the three elements of the Triad should also
be stated, and the principle of flexibility in response. There should be
reference to basic political elements of the Alliance strategy, related to
participation in both the nuclear planning and nuclear forces of the Alliance,
the need for solidarity under pressure or attack and readiness to share risks
and, if necessary, to escalate. This broad statement of the principles of
Alliance strategy might be linked to a general description of the threat it is
designed to counter, with a review of Warsaw Pact conventional and nuclear
forces and the

10. Regarding the concept for nuclear weapon use within NATO strategy, the
following aspects appear to deserve consideration in the Phase III effort:

a. Political Control of Nuclear Weapons (general characteristics),
- need for broad direction and control
- consistency in control and execution
- continuity of control.
b. Objectives of Nuclear Weapon Use
- to preserve or restore the integrity and security of the North Atlantic Treaty
area by restoring deterrence at the lowest possible level of escalation.

c. Principles for the Employment of Nuclear Weapons
- flexibility and selectivity in response
- response to the general situation.
- escalation control
- constraints (NATO objectives and collateral damage).
d. Theatre Nuclear Options.
- general objective as described in NC 14/5 (direct defence, deliberate
escalation, general response)

- general options (e.g., battlefield use, use in an extended geographical area)
- structure and characteristics of options.
11. Finally, Phase III work should include consideration of measures to support
the objectives of theatre nuclear weapons, e.g., the declaratory policy,
communicating NATO's Intention (CMI), and should also cover NATO's decision-
making process in the context of the agreed consultation guidelines.

IV. ORGANISATION AND CONDUCT
12. We believe the two most important considerations with regard to organising
the Phase III work are the widespread interest in this work among NFG countries
and among NATO staffs and the desirability for the timely completion of the
effort.

13. The work should be handled on two levels:
(a) a small Phase III Working Group with representation from a few countries,
and from the NATO Military Authorities;

(b) regular working group interaction with other NPC countries through the NPG
Staff Group, reinforced as desired by representatives from capitals and
headquarters.

14. It is envisaged that the Phase III Working Group would meet at an early
stage with the Staff Group for a wide ranging discussion of the detailed
suggestions which have been made for the scope and coverage of the paper.

15. All countries, as well as the International Secretariat and NATO Military
Authorities, would be invited to submit papers on subjects within their
cognizance, or of particular interest to them. It is then left to the working
group to develop its report.

16. The Phase III Working Group should be able to co-opt experts and
representatives from other countries or international
