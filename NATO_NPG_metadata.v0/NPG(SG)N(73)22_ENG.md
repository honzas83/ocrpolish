> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 19th March, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on March 19, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels, Belgium, to address several key strategic and operational items. The group discussed the nuclear aspects of Exercise WINTEX 73, agreeing that nuclear consultation procedures and facilities should be included in the agenda for the NPG Ministerial meeting in Ankara, Turkey, and that a preliminary report be prepared for Ministers. The group also reviewed the draft agenda for the Ankara meeting, considering whether to include items regarding the Illustrative Study on Demonstrative Use and Operational Concepts and Doctrine. They decided to circulate a draft agenda containing both items in square brackets to Capitals for final approval by Permanent Representatives on April 16. Additionally, the group examined the draft report on the North Norway Demonstrative Use Illustrative Study, agreeing to modifications and requesting the International Staff to prepare new wording in cooperation with the United Kingdom representative. The meeting also noted the circulation of NPG Working Papers regarding the ACLANT Study and the on-going Follow-on Work Programme. Finally, the group scheduled its next meetings for March 26 and April 9, 1973.

EXERCISE WINTEX 73

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussion on a
possible report to Ministers on the Nuclear aspects(1) of WINTEX 73 which had
taken place at the meeting of the Staff Group held on 26th February, 1973; he
said that the Staff Group should now give its opinion as to whether a report to
Ministers should be made, and if so, what should be its form, scope and content;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and noted a
statement by the Director, Council Operations;

(c) agreed that the question of nuclear consultation procedures and facilities
in WINTEX 73 should be included in the agenda for the NPG Ministerial meeting in
Ankara, and that a report on these matters should be prepared for Ministers;

(d) agreed that the proposed report should be considered as preliminary and that
it should not attempt to reach definitive conclusions;

(e) noted that the Military Authorities and the Council Operations and Exercise
Committee would be preparing in due course a very detailed report on all aspects
of WINTEX 73;

DRAFT MINISTERIAL AGENDA FOR ANKARA
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that a preliminary
discussion on the draft agenda(1) for Ankara had taken place at the last
meeting, and that the Staff Group should now decide upon a draft which could be
circulated to Capitals with a view to final approval by Permanent
Representatives on 16th April; he said that the first three proposed items(2)
appeared to be generally agreed and that it was now necessary to decide, with
regard to the next two items(3), whether one, or the other, or both should
appear in the agenda; he recalled that some progress in the preparation of Item
IV(3) had been made by the Staff Group; with regard to Item V(3), he understood
that the military paper might become available in the last week of March; in the
case of both possible items for the agenda, the time-factor was critical;

(b) noted a statement by the SHAPE representative who said that if a
presentation by SACEUR on Illustrative Use (possible Item IV) was required, a
firm decision should be taken now since time was short;

(c) noted a statement by the International Military Staff representative who
said that the Military Committee would most probably take the paper on
Operational Concepts and Doctrine (possible Item V) on Thursday, 29th March;
