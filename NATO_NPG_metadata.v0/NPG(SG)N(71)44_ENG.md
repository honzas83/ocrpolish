> [!abstract] 
> # A Philosophy For NATO Air Defence
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated 2nd April 1971. It addresses a report produced by the Tri-Service Group on Air Defence, which is intended for approval by the Conference of National Armaments Directors (CNAD) between 25th and 28th May 1971, and subsequent endorsement by NATO Military Authorities. The Chairman notes that several paragraphs of the report have nuclear implications that are of interest to the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG), and suggests that the Staff Group meet on Monday, 5th April, to determine if action by the NPG is desirable and to explore available alternatives. The document is signed by H. B. Seim in Brussels, Belgium.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
A PHILOSOPHY FOR NATO AIR DEFENCE
Note by the Chairman
The Tri-Service Group on Air Defence has produced a report(1) the covernote of
which indicates that the Conference of National Armaments Directors (CNAD) will
be requested to approve the report at their meeting on 25th-28th May, 1971 and
to pass it to the NATO Military Authorities for endorsement and implementation.
Several paragraphs(2) of the report have nuclear implications which are of
interest to the Nuclear Planning Group and on which the NPG may wish to take a
position.

2. It is suggested that the Staff Group may wish to explore this matter at our
meeting on Monday, 5th April, for the purpose of discussing whether action by
the NPG is desirable and, if so, what alternatives are open.

(Signed) H. B. SEIM
NATO, 1110 Brussels.
This document consists of one page.
