> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Thursday, 27th June, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels on June 27, 1974. The meeting focused on several key agenda items, including the review of documentation concerning the Military Committee Function in the Nuclear Consultation Process and a study on Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy and Military Doctrine for the Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons. Additionally, the group discussed an initial draft for a study on communicating the North Atlantic Treaty Organization's intention to use nuclear weapons, noting the need for an early decision on the procedure to be adopted. The group also took note of the Chairman's intention to circulate a first draft of a Permanent Representatives' report on the Supreme Allied Commander Europe's (SACEUR) Illustrative Air Defence Study. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold a subsequent meeting on July 1, 1974, and the possibility of a further meeting later that week.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Thursday,
27th June, 1974

DOCUMENTATION

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to, and commented
upon two documents about to be circulated:

(a) NDAC/D(74)1, 27th June, 1974: concerning the NPG report on the Military
Committee Function in the Nuclear Consultation Process(1);

(b) NDAC/D(74)2, 27th June, 1974: concerning the NPG report(2) on the Study on
Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy and Military Doctrine for the Tactical
Use of Nuclear Weapons.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTION TO USE NUCLEAR WEAPONS

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced an initial draft(3)
of a possible study on communicating NATO's intention to use nuclear weapons; he
recalled the approach to the

(2) Annex to NPG/D(74)6, 16th May, 1974

4. The STAFF GROUP:
