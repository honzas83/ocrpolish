> # Arrangements for the Study of Implications of Technological Developments
> 
> This document, a working paper from the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, proposes arrangements for a study on the political and military implications of technological developments, specifically regarding the tactical use of nuclear weapons. The proposal is based on a status report from the Phase II Study Team which highlighted that new guidance systems and other technological advancements could significantly impact tactical nuclear weapon use. The Technology Implications Study Group is tasked with assessing factors such as military effectiveness, targeting options, target acquisition, constraints policy, time factors, safety of friendly forces and civil populations, command and control, deterrence value, escalation risk, release procedures, psychological effects, and logistics. The group will be led by the United States and will include participants from Belgium, Canada, Denmark, the Federal Republic of Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Portugal, Turkey, the United Kingdom, and the United States. The group is authorized to invite representatives from SHAPE, the SACLAN staff, the International Military Staff, and the International Staff to participate. The group is also tasked with providing periodic written reports to the NPG on the status of its work and substantive issues, ensuring that reports are submitted at least a month before Ministerial meetings.

I. PURPOSE
This report outlines arrangements for initiating the study of the political and
military implications of technological developments insofar as questions
surrounding the tactical use of nuclear weapons are concerned.

II. BACKGROUND
2. A recent status report by the Phase II Study Team stated(1) that " ....... it
has become apparent to the Study Team that current developments, especially in
the field of new guidance systems, could have a potentially important impact on
the whole complex of questions surrounding the tactical use of nuclear weapons.
Since a full examination of this subject would be beyond its terms of reference
and in any event should be considered in a wider forum, the Study Team suggests
that the NPG should consider the advisability of a separate study of the
political and military implications of these developments." With respect to this
suggestion, the NPG Permanent Representatives commented(2) that

4. For purposes of this study effort, the meaning of the term "technological
developments" should be broadly construed to cover devices and techniques that
are within the state of the art even though they may not be reflected in a
deployed capability or have been demonstrated in developmental models.
Developments in nuclear weapon technology are included in this context, although
it is recognised that they will have to be discussed in terms of their resultant
weapons effects rather than in terms of warhead technology itself.

5. The factors to be considered in assessing the political and military impact
of technological developments are left for the Technology Implications Study
Group to determine in the course of its own explorations but they might include,
for example (not listed in any particular order of priority):

(a) potential changes in military effectiveness of weapons systems in terms of
accuracy, effects, reliability, cost, etc.;

(b) targeting options;
(c) target acquisition;
(d) constraints policy;
(e) time factors;

Reporting
8. The form, content, frequency and timing of the submission of the results of
the study effort are left to the discretion of the Technology Implications Study
Group. Bearing in mind the complexity of the overall subject and the strong
interest of the Ministers, the Group should give serious consideration to
providing reports to the NPG on selected substantive issues from time to time
during the course of its work, particularly if it appears that the study effort
will extend over a considerable period of time. Similarly, the Group should feel
free to make proposals to the NPG at any time for follow-up action falling
outside the Group's immediate area of responsibility.

9. In addition to the foregoing, the Technology Implications Study Group will be
expected to make periodic written reports to the NPG on the status of its work,
covering in general terms its organisational arrangements, the subject matter
being addressed and information, as practicable, on its future plans. These
periodic status reports should normally be submitted at least a month before
Ministerial meetings in order to be available for the general review of future
NPG activities.
