> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday, Thursday and Friday, 11th, 12th and 13th May, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting in Brussels from May 11th to 13th, 1977, to address several administrative and strategic matters. The group confirmed that all present members were cleared for the security classification of the discussed topics. They expressed gratitude for a recent field trip to installations in Germany. Logistics for an upcoming trip to Ottawa, Canada, including flight plans for a German Boeing 707 and accommodation in Whitehorse, were discussed. The group agreed to an agenda for the upcoming meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives on May 23rd, 1977. Additionally, they reviewed a draft report on Nuclear Technology Study, agreeing to changes in form and text and deciding to circulate it as a two-language working paper. Discussions on Theatre Nuclear Force Improvements were also held, with the group noting that consensus was nearly achieved, pending final instructions from one member's capital. The group decided that the future circulation of the draft report on Theatre Nuclear Force Improvements would depend on the response from this member state. The next meeting of the Staff Group will be scheduled based on the situation regarding Theatre Nuclear Force Improvements.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday,
Thursday and Friday, 11th, 12th and 13th May, 1977

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared to the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

STAFF GROUP FIELD TRIP

2. The STAFF GROUP:
warmly concurred with the thanks expressed by the Chairman to those members who
had organised a most interesting field trip to see installations in Germany.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR OTTAWA

3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the German represent- ative who requested the names
of those persons wishing to travel in the (German Boeing 707); he recalled that
the flight plan of this machine, in Canada, depended upon the accommodation
facilities at Whitehorse.

AGENDA FOR THE MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES ON
23RD MAY 1977

4. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to the agenda proposed by the Chairman and invited him to circulate it by
the end of the week.

NUCLEAR TECHNOLOGY STUDY

5. The STA GROUP:

(a) discussed the draft report by Permanent Representative(1) and agreed to a
number of changes to its form and text;

(b) agreed that this paper should now be circulated as an NPG two-language
working Paper and invited the International Staff to circulate the new paper
with all speed.

THEATRE NUCLEAR FORCE IMPROVEMENTS

6. The STAF GROUP:

(a) took note of the Chairman's remarks concerning NPG(Staff Group)P(77)5 and
proceeded to discuss the form and content of the draft report on Theatre Nuclear
Force Improvements;

(b) noted that a consensus was nearly-achieved but that one member required
final instructions from his capital;

(c) agreed that, if an affirmative response was received from this country, the
draft report should be circulated as an IPG two-language paper for consideration
by Permanent Representatives;

(d) noted that, if a negative reply was received, it would be necessary to call
a meeting of the Staff Group with a view to recommending the detailed discussion
of this paper.

NEXT MEETING

7. The STAF GROUP:

agreed to hold its next meeting in the light of the situation regarding Theatre
Nuclear Force Improvements (see paragraph 6(b) above).

NATO, 1110 Brussels.
