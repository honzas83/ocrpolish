> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters on Monday, 28th August, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters on August 28, 1972, to review several key items. The group took note of several papers, including the agenda for the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives and the SACLANT Illustrative Study on Initial Use at Sea. A significant portion of the meeting focused on the security classification of the Denmark Study, which had been re-classified as ATOMAL, necessitating verification of access authorizations for attendees of the upcoming presentation. The Danish representative requested information on the reasons for this re-classification. The group also discussed consultation procedures and a second revision of a working paper on Consultation, though action was deferred due to a reserve maintained by the United States. The meeting addressed the updating of NATO nuclear planning directives in light of the POSEIDON programme, specifically considering whether amendments to the 'Concept for the rôle of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces' required Ministerial approval or could be approved by the Defence Planning Committee in Permanent Session. The group invited the SHAPE representative to provide suggestions on updating the Concept paper and removing transitory information. Additionally, the group approved invitations for SACEUR and SACLANT to attend the 12th meeting of NPG Ministers. Finally, the group established a timeline for preparing Working Papers for the NPG Ministerial Meeting and scheduled their next meetings for August 30 and September 4, 1972.

SITUATION REGARDING THE SECURITY CLASSIFICATION OF THE DENMARK STUDY

2.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that a presentation of
the Denmark Study by the Study team was to be made on 14th September to the NPG
Permanent Representatives. Bearing in mind that the Study had been re-classified
as ATOMAL he recommended that members should verify in good time whether all the
persons who wished to attend this meeting were in fact on the NATO list of
persons authorised to have access to ATOMAL information; he further suggested
that it might be possible, if guidance was received in time from Washington, for
the Study team to prepare a presentation containing no ATOMAL information;

(b) noted the remarks of the Danish representative who said that his Authorities
would be glad to be informed as soon as possible of the reasons why the Denmark
Study had been declared ATOMAL;

(c) took note of a statement by the United States representative who said that
he hoped to receive the required information by the end of the week and would
immediately pass it to the Chairman for distribution to all concerned by the
International Staff.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES

discussion, and which was now before the Staff Group; he invited comments on the
latest version;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion, and agreed to a
number of changes to the text of the paper and to its annex.
