> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 26th April, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on April 26, 1971, to review several key documents and current papers. The group discussed the draft report on Follow-On Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons and a statement by the Turkish Ambassador regarding Illustrative Studies. They reached a consensus on the need for clarification of the Concept Paper on the role of theatre nuclear strike forces, agreeing to adopt specific wording from the Military Committee's report. The meeting also provisionally agreed on arrangements for a visit to SHAPE (Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe) on June 16, 1971, including attendance limits and a transport plan. Additionally, the group decided that the presence of Dr. Shaw from the United Kingdom was not necessary for the upcoming meeting of Permanent Representatives. The German Representative provided information on the arrangements for the Ministerial Meeting in Mittenwald, including a conference booklet and social events. The group confirmed the procedure for the Advance Press Release for the Mittenwald meeting, and discussed the follow-on action to the AD 70s Study regarding nuclear problems. Finally, the group agreed to hold its next meeting on May 3, 1971.

CURRENT PAPERS AND DOCUMENTS

REPORT BY THE MILITARY COMMITTEE ON THE CONCEPT FOR THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR
STRIKE FORCES

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who referred to the report by the Military
Committee(1), to the note by the International Staff(2) on the matter, and to
the Staff Group's view that a clarification of certain paragraphs in the Concept
paper could be achieved by an entry in the Agreed Minute of the Mittenwald
Meeting, and invited views;

(b) discussed in detail the problem set out in the Military Committee's report,
and took note of the various views expressed;

(c) noted a general consensus that the Concept Paper(3) was not ambiguous but
that a clarification on the lines of the language proposed by the Military
Committee was desirable;

(d) agreed that this clarification could adopt the wording in the third sentence
of paragraph 4 of the Report by the Military Committee, subject to the phrase
"of ACE Strike forces" (line 11, paragraph 4) being dropped;

(e) requested the Chairman to inform the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group
of the content and results of the detailed discussion held in the Staff Group;

(f) recalled that the Nuclear Planning Group would be discussing this matter at
their meeting to be held on 29th April, 1971(4).
