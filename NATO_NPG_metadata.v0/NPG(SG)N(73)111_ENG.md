> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 29th October, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on October 29, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key items. The group discussed the transportation of classified documents to The Hague for an upcoming meeting, noting that the Head of the Cosmic Registry, Mr. Bitter, would handle the transport under escort. They also noted the Advance Press Release scheduled for November 2, 1973. Regarding the upcoming NPG Ministers meeting, the group discussed the possibility of preparing draft framework texts for the Agreed Minute and Final Communiqué. Additionally, the group addressed senior officer courses in special weapons at Oberämmergau, requesting delegations to provide the number of places required for 1974. There was a discussion on the implementation of atomic information agreements, specifically a revision of Annex III to C-M(68)4 submitted by the United States. The group also noted Portugal's decision to become active in Nuclear Planning Group affairs, which would place them in Group II and delay their participation in a ministerial meeting until Autumn 1974. Finally, the group discussed a study on the communication of NATO's intentions, with Italy and Canada providing their positions on the study's leadership and execution. The next meeting was scheduled for November 12, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 29th

MATTERS CONCERNING THE FORTHCOMING MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS
Transportation of Classified Documents
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the Head of the Cosmic
Registry, Mr. Bitter, would take classified documents, under escort, from NATO
Headquarters to The Hague on Monday, 5th November, and would bring them back on
Wednesday, 7th November; Delegations could take advantage of this
transportation; packages, duly marked, should be taken to the Cosmic registry
not later than 1030 a.m. on Monday, 5th November.

Advance Press Release
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled that the Advance Press
Release(1) would be made in Brussels on Friday, 2nd November, at 3 p.m.

Draft Framework Texts for the Acreed Minute and Final Communiqué
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the reply by the Chairman to a request by the Canadian
representative who asked whether a draft framework text for the Acreed Minute
and for the Final Communiqué could be prepared;

PARTICIPATION IN NG ACTIVITIES
6. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who announced that he had been informed
by Mr. Queiroz de Barros of the Portuguese Delegation that Portugal had decided
to become active in NPG affairs, and that the Portuguese Permanent
Representative had sent a letter to the Secretary General during the past week
informing him of this intention; current membership arrangements(1) provided
that, if Portugal elected to participate actively in the NPG, she would be added
to Group II; this implied that Portugal would not take part in an NPG
Ministerial Meeting until the Autumn of 1974; a Private Office memorandum would
be circulated shortly; to it would be attached a copy of the Portuguese
Ambassador's letter and a copy of the Secretary General's reply.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTIONS
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he had been informed
by the Italian representative that Italy would accept to participate in a study
on the communication of NATO's intentions, but would not wish to be Study
Leader;

(b) took note of the remarks of the Canadian representative who said that his
authorities would not object to the study being undertaken by the Staff Group;

(c) agreed to re-examine the matter in November.
NEXT MEETING
8. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 12th November, 1973, at 3 p.m.
NATO, 1110 Brussels.
