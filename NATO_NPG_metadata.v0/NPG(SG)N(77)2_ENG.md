> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 10th January, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on January 10, 1977, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with a welcome to new representatives from Belgium and the United Kingdom. The group discussed Theatre Information and Engagement Systems (TIES), noting a requirement for expertise from the Chairman of the Military Committee, the Assistant Secretary General for Defence Support, and the Director of Council Operations and Communications. They also addressed the preparation of terms of reference for the Maritime Nuclear Warfare Study, with the United States representative providing instructions from his authorities. The group further discussed the preparation of a second draft report by Permanent Representatives on future work priorities, involving input from Germany, Denmark, the United States, the United Kingdom, and Canada, and Turkey. The Chairman invited the United Kingdom and the United Kingdom and United States members to meet for further language refinement. Additionally, the group noted the dates for the NPG Spring Ministerial meeting in Canada on June 8th and 9th, 1977. Finally, the group addressed a press report concerning Theatre Nuclear Forces and requested the United States representative to circulate the relevant US report. The next meeting was scheduled for January 17, 1977.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 10th

WELCOME

1. The STAFF GROUP:
warmly concurred with the welcome extended by the Chairman to Colonel Alphonso
Schmict, the new Belgian representative on the Staff Group and Mr. William
Heidley the new number two United Kingdom representative.

SECURITY

2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared to the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

THEATRE INFORMATION AND ENGAGEMENT SYSTEMS (TIES)

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the question
of a TIS status report and said that the Staff Group would require expertise
from the Chairman of the Military Committee, the Assistant Secretary General for
Defence Support and from the Director of Council Operations and Communications;

(b) he drew attention to the letter by the Secretary General to the above-
mentioned authorities(1);

* SPRING MEETING OF NPG IN CANADA

6. The STAFF GROUP:

took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he believed that the
dates of the NPG Spring Ministerial meeting were now firmly fixed for 8th and
9th June, 1977; he would be glad however to receive confirmation of the
suitability of these dates from all concerned.

PRESS REPORT

7. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Turkish representative who referred to an
article in the Press concerning Theatre Nuclear Forces; he asked whether the US
representative could provide copies of the US report upon which the Press
article had been based;

(b) invited the US member, if possible, to circulate the report in question to
members of the Staff Group.

NEXT MEETING

8. The STAFF GROUP:

agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 17th January, 1977 at 3 p.m.
