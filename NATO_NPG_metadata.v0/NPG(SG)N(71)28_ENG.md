> # NPG(Staff Group)N(71)28
> 
> The NATO Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group meeting addressed several administrative and strategic concerns. The group agreed on the timing for briefings on the NORTTHAG Area Study and the Northern Army Group (NORTTHAG) and Greek Study, scheduling them for April 19th to 23rd, 1971. A meeting of Permanent Representatives was scheduled for the week of April 26th, 1971, to prepare for a Ministerial Meeting, focusing on issues emerging from follow-on studies and progress reports. The group also discussed a proposal by the United States Representative regarding the approach to issues raised in follow-on studies, suggesting that individual countries prepare their own issues papers for Ministers. The discussion on illustrative studies and sample release requests, including the release procedures of SACLANT and CINCHAN, and the problem of pre-conditioned release for naval forces, specifically regarding weapons choice and use, comentário on the same. Additionally, comentário on the same. The group noted a suggestion by the Canadian Representative that SACLANT might be invited to give a briefing to Ministers on illustrative studies. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for Monday, March 8th, 1971, at 3 p.m. in Brussels, Belgium.

APPROACH TO ISSUES RAISED IN FOLLOW-ON STUDIES
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the United States Representative in which he
proposed that:

(1) instead of the Staff Group and the Permanent Representatives discussing and
presenting to Ministers issues arising from Follow-on Studies, the individual
country concerned should prepare for Ministers its own issues paper;

(ii) countries and military authorities would comment on the particular study
and the issues proposed by the country concerned after the Ministerial Meeting;

(iii) after all studies had been received and addressed in the above manner, a
group composed of some but not all of the NPG countries should be formed to
extract guidelines from the material available;

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES AND SAMPLE RELEASE REQUESTS

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) continued its discussion of Annex B to NPG/B(70)5, dated 14th October, 1970;
(b) noted that the release procedures employed by SACLANT AND CINCHAN were
almost indentical with those of SACEUR;

(c) discussed the problem of pre-conditioned release in the context of the
special needs of naval forces, particularly with regard to the number and choice
of weapons, and of the different circumstances pertaining to initial and follow-
on use;

(d) noted a suggestion by the Canadian Representative that SACLANT might be
invited to give a briefing to Ministers on illustrative studies; agreed to
discuss this suggestion at the next meeting.

NEXT MEETING

4. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 8th March, 1971, at 3 p.m.
