> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday, 12th December, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on December 12, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with the welcome of Mr. N.J. Beaumont as the new United Kingdom representative. The group discussed the draft decision sheet for the NDAC Ministerial meeting (NDAC/N(73)1) and noted that no specific tasks had resulted from recent Ministerial meetings, though issues like deterrence, consultation, and technological improvements were raised. There was a a detailed discussion on the need for a study on the political and military implications of current technological developments, with the United States representative suggesting a resolution on participation before deciding on leadership of the Study Team. The group agreed that an illustrative study was not required for the Autumn 1974 meeting, but may be reconsidered for the Spring 1975 meeting. Regarding the analysis of nuclear aspects of deterrence, the group decided that the Phase II Study Team's paper should be brought to the attention of Permanent Representatives. The Italian representative mentioned an upcoming paper on the political aspects of early combined use. The group also agreed that a study on Communicating NATO's Intentions should be undertaken by the Staff Group under the auspices of the Permanent Representatives. The representative of the International Military Staff noted that a report on the nuclear consultation process (IMSW-235-73) was expected in January. the Norwegian representative confirmed the dates for the NPG Spring Meeting on June 4-5, 1974. The meeting concluded with holiday wishes and the agreement to meet again on January 7, 1974.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday,

CHANGE OF MEMBERSHIP
1. The STAFF GROUP:
endorsed the remarks of the Chairman who welcomed Mr. N.J. Beaumont who replaces
Mr. J.M. Stewart as United Kingdom representative on the Staff Group.

DOCUMENTATION
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to NDAC/N(73)1,
dated 7th December, 1973; this paper was the draft decision sheet for the NDAC
Ministerial meeting held on 7th December, and is circulated for approval by the
silence procedure; if no objections or comments are received by close of
business on 14th December, the draft will be considered approved and will be
issued as a final NDAC document.

MATTERS ARISING FROM THE NDAC, DPC AND COUNCIL MINISTERIAL SESSIONS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who observed that although no
specific tasks for the NPG had resulted from the Ministerial meetings on 7th,
10th and 11th December, nevertheless certain questions had been raised which
might sooner or later require attention, or parallel action, by the NPG;

IMPLICATIONS OF TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENT
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted the remarks of the Chairman who referred to the discussion at the
previous meeting of the Staff Group on the need for a study of the political and
military implications of current developments in the technological field; he
recalled the considerable interest displayed by all members in this study and
asked if members had received further instructions regarding participation in an
ad hoc Study Group;

(b) noted a statement by the United States representative who suggested that the
first step should be to resolve the question of participation; his authorities
would then be able to say whether the United States could accept the leadership
of the Study Team as proposed by the other members of the Staff Group;

(c) agreed that instructions should be sought as a matter of urgency by all
those members who had none, with a view to deciding upon participation at the
first meeting of the Staff Group to be held in early January.

ANALYSIS OF THE NUCLEAR ASPECTS OF DETERRENCE
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled that the Staff Group
had agreed at the previous meeting that it did not appear necessary, on the
matter of the analysis of nuclear aspects of deterrence, to go further than the
paper(1) prepared by the Phase II Study Team; there remained however the
question of how to formalise this position with regard to Permanent
Representatives and Ministers;

(b) noted a general feeling among members that it would be premature to attempt
to make the Phase II Study Team's paper into a formal document representing
official views, doctrine or policy;

(c) agreeing, however that the Study Team's paper should be brought to the
attention of Permanent Representatives, invited the International Staff to
examine the best way of doing this, and to prepare, if appropriate, a short
draft to which the paper might be attached;

(d) agreed to continue the discussion of this matter at the next meeting.
