> # Follow-Up Action to the Ministerial Meeting in Monterey on 16th and 17th June, 1975
> 
> This document outlines the proposed follow-up actions required to implement the decisions made by the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministers during their meeting in Monterey in June 1975. It details the initiation of Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme, which aims to develop a consolidated statement of NATO's concept for the use of nuclear weapons. The text addresses the necessary military and political evaluations of theatre nuclear force posture, specifically in relation to the Nunn Amendment to U.S. Public Law 93-365. It discusses requirements for upgrading communications, command and control, and the development of concepts for controlled use of nuclear weapons. Furthermore, it proposes the use of Illustrative Studies to present problems in the area between political decision-making and military action. The document also suggests the setting up of a working group to address the issue of warning an enemy of NATO's intention to use nuclear weapons (CNI). Finally, it touches upon the preparation for future NPG Ministerial meetings, including the Spring 1976 meeting and the timing of Technology Studies.

FOLLOW-UP ACTION TO THE MINISTERIAL MEETING IN MONTEREY ON 16th AND 17th JUNE,
1975

Note by the Chairman

The following paragraphs comment on actions that might be required to implement
what the International Staff – on the basis of its own unofficial record –
considers were the decisions made by the NPG Ministers in Monterey on 16th and
17th June, 1975. These decisions, as agreed between members of the participating
countries at Staff level, will be contained in the text of a draft Agreed Minute
which will be circulated soon. The thoughts below are intended as background
information on follow-up actions to the Monterey Ministerial meeting.

1. Follow-on Use Work Programme - Initiation of Phase III

2. As one major result of the Monterey meeting, Permanent Representatives are
required to develop – along the lines of their recommendations to Ministers(1) –
detailed proposals for the initiation and implementation of a consolidated
statement of NATO's concept for the use of nuclear weapons, drawing together in
one paper the conclusions and guidance contained in the basic policy documents
as well as lessons learned from on-going NPG work in related areas. A number of
additional suggestions made during the 17th Ministerial NPG meeting might also
impact this process: questions of doctrinal linkage with conventional defence on
one side and strategic defence measures on the other, differences in overall
defence concepts with regard to various NATO sections and consequently the kinds
of interrelationships of various uses of nuclear weapons which had been studied.
The discussion in Part II below might add further insight with regard to the
overall task of initiating Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme.

3. When considering terms of reference for the Phase III work, -Staff Group
members may wish to look, in general terms, into the problems of scope, coverage
and degree of detail to be contemplated. Other areas of interest could be basic
definitions, organisation, time required and timing interphase with on-going

military follow-up studies on the theatre nuclear force posture initiated by the
U.S. Secretary of Defense's report to Congress in connection with the Nunn
Amendment to U.S. Public Law 93-365.

II. NPG Follow-up Work to U.S. Study on Public Law 93-365 (Nunn Amendment)
4. Theatre Nuclear Force Posture. The SHAPE NIKE HERCULES Study(1) was a first
of a series of expected studies in follow-up to the report originated in
connection with the Nunn Amendment. Studies to follow are on the replacement of
HONEST JOHN and SERGEANT by LANCE, modifications of the current SAS site
dispositions in ACE and the maintenance of peacetime QRA posture,
notwithstanding necessary adjustments. All these military studies are, at the
same time, related to SACEUR’s report(2) on the revised U.S. MBRF option III(3)
and all will require a political evaluation.

5. With regard to NATO policy for the employment of theatre nuclear weapons, the
U.S. Secretary of Defense Report to Congress pointed also to a study requirement
with regard to upgrading of communications, improvement in command and control,
planning efforts for limited use options and the development of concepts for
controlled use. It is understood that elaboration on these aspects, each of
which to some extent have already been subject to NPG examination(4) or
requests(5), would be discussed or might become part of the Phase III work.

6. Communications, Command and Control. At least four tasks come to mind in
connection with communications, command and control in the politico-military
field:

(a) the implications flowing from the requirement to use the ATOMAL
classification for certain nuclear consultation traffic in line with
guidelines(1) still under revision, a matter for which the Chairman, Military
Committee at Monterey has requested an urgent decision;

(b) procedures for country requests for the use of nuclear weapons in addition
to those foreseen by the Major NATO Commanders;

(c) more detailed procedures for the specific handling of the particular CNI
question(2) during the nuclear consultation process;

(d) a compilation of all documents so far forwarded by the NPG concerning
nuclear consultation; this can be developed in due course within the next
months.

It should not be ruled out from the outset that a single paper covering matters
under (a), (b) and (c) could be forwarded to Ministers for their Autumn meeting,
with the compilation mentioned under (d) at Annex or as a separate document.

7. Theatre Nuclear Options and Concepts for Controlled Use. From the Spring of

desirability for NATO to work towards a selective employment planning. This
notion was mostly combined with remarks on further SLBIs to be or being
transferred to SACEUR and also combined with the thought of regionally
differentiated employment planning. – In a certain sense all previous policy
documents issued by NPG(3) initiation have been contributions to framing the
concept of controlled use of theatre nuclear weapons. Control with regard to
target selection and weapon application (constraints policy) have always been
part of military operational planning in NATO in line with, and according to,
the above-mentioned political guidance. Actual and future selective employment

III. Illustrative Studies

8. Since the possibility that the Air Defence Illustrative Study(2) together
with a related Permanent Representatives Report(3) be discussed by ministers at
a future meeting has not been foreclosed(4), and since Ministers in Monterey did
express considerable interest in reviving the practice of being regularly
confronted with Illustrative study presentations, the Autumn 1975 NPG meeting
could be the right time to discuss the Air Defence Illustrative Study, together
with some results of the Munn follow-up work (NIKE HERCULES). The Chairman of
the Military Committee could be requested by the Permanent Representatives,
through the Secretary General, to arrange for SACEUR to give an updated
presentation on these issues. Other studies on topics such as ADM in the light
of modern technology might be initiated now for presentation in 1976.

9. Furthermore, some other ideas have recently been associated with the term
Illustrative Studies. While the original idea had been to illustrate problems in
conjunction with the procedural steps as seen by the Major NATO Commanders for
the release request of nuclear weapons, later suggestions were to add problems
in the area between political decision-making and military action. Such studies
could cover, after completion of Phase III, the relationship between various
kinds of initial and follow-on use (including the various forms of nuclear use
under the aspect of regional differentiation) or country requests once
procedures are established.

IV. Warning of NATO's Intentions.
10. Since there was no basic objection from any Minister to further study the
issue of warning an enemy on NATO's intention to use nuclear weapons, the NPG
will likely follow the suggestion made in the Permanent Representatives'
Report(1) on the Study of Communicating NATO's Intention (CNI)(2) and set up a
working group to address the questions whether a warning message should be sent
prior to the nuclear use decision and/or whether such a message should be sent
after a decision but prior to actual use. Staff Group members, in the light of
the comments by the Chairman of the Military Committee with regard to
priorities, may wish to discuss whether a separate study on this issue should be
undertaken or whether debate on the already completed document would have to be
re-opened.

V. Preparation of Future Ministerial Meetings.
11. In connection with the preparation of future NPG Ministerial meetings, three
basic topics might be addressed:

(a) arrangements for the Spring 1976 Ministerial meeting.
(b) discussion of future meetings schedules for the NPG;
(c) the timing of the Technology Study.
While finding a place for the NPG Spring 1976 Ministerial meeting will be
handled according to procedures followed on previous occasions, the discussion
of the future meetings schedule might require early involvement of the Permanent
Representatives. Whether the discussion of these future schedules can be
terminated prior to the autumn 1975 Ministerial meeting is an open question, but
such action would certainly be desirable. With regard to the Technology Study
effort, the placing of this item on the Spring 1976 Agenda would seem to be
advantageous because a relatively early finalisation of this matter could
facilitate the elaboration of the consolidated statement on the theatre nuclear
concept as outlined in paragraphs 2 and 5 above.

VI. Agenda Items for the Autumn Ministerial Meeting.

12. In the light of the above comments, the following items might be feasible
objectives in the Autumn 1975 Ministerial Agenda:

(a) briefing of strategic balance;
(b) discussion of selective employment planning;
(c) political evaluation of military studies on the theatre nuclear force
posture in Europe as a follow-up to Secretary Scalesinger's 1st April, 1975
Report to the U.S. Congress;

(d) initiation of Phase III of the follow-on use work programme (terms of
reference);

(e) nuclear consultation procedures (to include country requests, CNI procedures
and ATOMAL classification);

(f) warning of NATO's intentions to use nuclear weapons;
(g) schedule for future NPG meetings.
