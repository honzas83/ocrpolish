> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 7th October, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on October 7, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to prepare for the meeting of Permanent Representatives scheduled for October 10. The group noted that all attendees were cleared for the security classification of the discussed matters. The meeting focused on the examination and approval of the Ministerial Agenda and two working papers regarding the CNI Study and the Illustrative Study, which were to be further examined in November. Additionally, the group discussed the Secretary General's PO on Future Work. The representative of SHAPE (Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe) reported that a background paper on Selective Employment Planning would be delayed until after the October 10 meeting, though a representative of SACEUR (Supreme Allied Commander Europe) would be available to answer questions. The group also addressed scheduling changes for the Permanent Representatives' meeting, moving it to 10:15 AM on October 10, and considered the possibility of reconvening the NPG after the SALT meeting on October 17. Finally, the group scheduled its next meeting for October 14, 1974.

WORKING PAPERS FOR SUBMISSION TO THE PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
2. The STAFF GROUP:

object of the meeting of the Permanent Representatives to be held on 10th

November; the Permanent Representatives would also wish to discuss the content
of the Secretary General’s PO on Future Work; the Chairman invited members to
say if they had any last-minute amendments to propose regarding any of the
papers;

(b) whilst agreeing that no further amendments to the working papers were

SELECTIVE EMPLOYMENT PLANNING
3. The STAFF GROUP: took note of a statement by the representative of SHAPE who
said that, regretfully, the background paper on Selective Employment Planning
which the Permanent Representatives had hoped to receive in time for their
meeting on 10th October would not be available until after that date; however
the General/Flag officer who would represent SACEUR on 10th October would be
prepared to answer questions on background matters.

NEXT MEETING(S) OF THE PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that one country had requested that the meeting of Permanent
Representatives on 10th October be changed from the afternoon to the morning,
and that,although this appeared feasible to all concerned, there were two views
as to when the meeting should start; it was hoped that agreement would be
reached next morning(1);

(b) agreed that if time did not permit the Permanent Representatives to finish
their business on 10th October, it might be possible to re-convene the NPG after
the SALT meeting on 17th October.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
5. The STAFF GROUP: took note that its next meeting would take place on Monday,
14th October, 1974, at 3 p.m.

(1) It was agreed to meet at 10.15 on Thursday, 10th October.
