> # Addition to Fusion Representative's Report of Observations on MITEX 75
> 
> This document is a letter from the Chairman of the Nuclear Fusion Group, dated 15th May 1975, proposing amendments to the Nuclear Fusion Group Permanent Representatives' report on MITEX 75. The Chairman notes that at a meeting of Permanent Representatives on 12th May 1975, it was generally agreed to insert additional language into the report. The proposed amendments target Paragraph 8, adding text regarding the confusion that could develop concerning the sequential ordering of requests for the use of nuclear weapons, specifically the relationship between initial use and follow-on use. It also proposes a change to Paragraph 13(c) to acknowledge that the initial use/follow-on use relationship will be considered in the ongoing follow-on Use Work Programme. The document originates from NATO headquarters in Brussels, Belgium, and is signed by R. R. Elk.

ADDITION TO FUSION REPRESENTATIVE'S REPORT OF OBSERVATIONS ON MITE: 75

Lete by the Chairman

At the Permanent Representatives' meeting on 12th May 1975 it was generally
agreed to insert additional language into the NFG Permanent Representatives'
report on MITEX 75(1).

2. Staff Group members may wish to consider the following amendments:

- Paragraph 8 to continue after last sentence with the following:

"In discussing these requests for the use of nuclear weapons, the DPC noted that
confusion could develop with regard to their sequential ordering in connection
with the unique characteristics of initial use and its relationship to follow-on
use. This is also discussed in the follow-on Use Work Programme now underway."

- Paragraph 13(c), change full stop to a comma and continue as follows:

"... 'while realising that the initial use/follow-on use relationship will be
considered in the follow-on Use work Programme now underway.'"

NATO, 1110 Brussels.
(Signed) R. R. ELK.
