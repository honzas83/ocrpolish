> # Addendum to Working Paper NPG(Staff Group)WP(74)7 (4th Revise)
> 
> This document is an addendum to Working Paper NPG(Staff Group)WP(74)7 (4th Revise), dated 18th September 1974, which adds paragraphs 35-38 to the original study. The text emphasizes that while NATO's declaratory policy and military planning demonstrate a willingness to use nuclear weapons for the defense of NATO territory, the Alliance must decide whether to communicate its intentions to enemies, other countries, and the public if aggression occurs. It notes that communicating intentions, known as Communication of Nuclear Intentions (CNI), could reinforce NATO's resolve and reduce the risk of enemy over-reaction by clarifying the limited character of the nuclear effort. However, the document warns that such a decision would be based on a complex mix of conflicting political and military considerations. It further states that political authorities must become familiar with the substantive and procedural factors involved in the communication process and begin their considerations early to ensure effective coordination. The document refers to Parts IV and V of the study and an Annex regarding the procedural aspects of the consultation process within NATO.

VI. SUMMARY
35. NATO's declaratory policy, force structure and political consultation
activity and military planning in peacetime testify to the willingness and
determination of the Alliance to use nuclear weapons, if necessary, for the
defence of NATO territory. Therefore, it is clear that NATO has already given
strong indications to a potential aggressor of this intention.

36. However, if aggression occurs and NATO decides to use nuclear weapons, NATO
would also have to decide whether to communicate to the enemy, to other
countries and to the public its intention with respect to the specific use in
mind. It might be desirable for NATO to do so in order to reinforce the evidence
of NATO's resolve and at the same time to reduce the risk of enemy over-reaction
as a result of failure to appreciate the controlled, limited character of NATO's
nuclear effort. Nevertheless, there are risks and possible disadvantages which
must be carefully considered. A decision on whether or not to implement a CNI
action in connection with a particular selective use of nuclear weapons would be
based on the overall assessment of a mix of political and military
considerations that are likely to be conflicting and difficult to weigh.
Although the Provisional Political Guidelines clearly indicate that a CNI action
is likely to be to NATO's advantage, the actual decision can only be reached in
the context of the situation that exists at the time that a use decision is
being made.

37. Farts IV and V of this Study discuss some of the considerations that
political authorities would have to take into account in making a decision on
whether to send specific messages of explanation and warning in connection with
the use of nuclear weapons. These sections also examine some of the factors that
would need to be considered by political authorities in implementing a decision
to communicate NATO's intentions. The Annex discusses some procedural aspects
that bear on the consultation process within NATO and that relate to carrying
out an affirmative decision.

38. It is essential that political authorities become aware now of the probable
requirement for considering the question of communicating NATO's intention at
the time of a contemplated selective use of nuclear weapons, and that they
familiarise themselves in general terms with the substantive and procedural
factors that would have to be taken into account. Political authorities should
recognise the importance of beginning their consideration of the communication
question early, in parallel with their consultation on the nuclear weapon use
itself. A timely decision on the communication question would be essential so
that necessary co-ordination arrangements can be worked out in time to permit
the communication effort to be implemented effectively and without delay, in the
event that the nuclear power concerned approves the release request.
