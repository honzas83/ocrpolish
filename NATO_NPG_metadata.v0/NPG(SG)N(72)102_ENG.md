> # Preparations for the Spring 1973 Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group detailing the preparatory work required for the Spring 1973 Ministerial meeting to be held in Turkey. The proposed agenda topics include the ACLANT Follow-on Use Study, a briefing on the balance of strategic forces, follow-up on PO 226, and consultation procedures and facilities. The Chairman suggests that the Staff Group coordinate with NATO Military Authorities (NMA) and Permanent Representatives to draft reports and provide briefings. Specific attention is given to the the balance of strategic forces, which is typically an initiative of the United States. The document also addresses the need for a briefing from Colonel Vincent regarding Council Operations Directorate work and preparations for WINTEX 73. A detailed planning timetable is provided, accounting for interruptions such as the Christmas leave period, the WINTEX 73 exercise, and Easter. The goal is to ensure all agenda documents are delivered to capitals by approximately May 1, 1973, with final reports from Permanent Representatives due by mid-April.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

PREPARATIONS FOR THE SPRING 1973 MINISTERIAL MEETING

Note by the Chairman

The following paragraphs discuss briefly the principal tasks that, in my view,
the Staff Group may have to undertake in preparing for the Ministerial meeting
to be held in Turkey in May, 1973. Some timing considerations are also
mentioned. It is suggested that the Staff Group discuss these matters at its
next meeting.

Ministerial Agenda Topics

2. Although it would be premature to attempt to make any decisions at this time
on the content of the agenda for the next NPG Ministerial meeting, it would be
useful, in terms of organising the Staff Group’s work, to review the
possibilities. On the basis of ministerial actions at past meetings and in the
light of recent Staff Group discussions, the following topics should be
considered:

(a) the ACLANT Follow-on Use Study;

(b) briefing on the balance of strategic forces;

(c) PO 226 follow-up;

(d) consultation procedures and facilities.

These topics are discussed in greater detail in later paragraphs. Timing
considerations are also addressed.

ACLANT Follow-on Use Study
3. At Copenhagen the Ministers agreed to consider this study at the Spring 1973
meeting. We have been informed that the study will be circulated in the near
future. If past practice is followed, it can be expected that the NATO Military
Authorities would be invited to comment on the study, and that a Permanent
Representatives' report identifying possible areas for Ministerial discussion
would be prepared. Since it is highly desirable that the NMA comments be
available when the Permanent Representatives' report is drafted, it is suggested
that the Staff Group agree now that an invitation to comment should be extended
to the NMA as soon as the study is distributed. For planning purposes, it is
anticipated that the Staff Group would begin its efforts to draft a Permanent
Representatives' report in January, although preliminary discussions on the
study could take place later this month or in December as opportunity permits.
In addition to the foregoing arrangements, it is suggested that an opportunity
be provided for the ACLANT study team to give a presentation to the Permanent
Representatives, following previous practice.

Briefing on the Balance of Strategic Forces
4. This topic has been placed on past agendas at the initiative of the United
States and the MFG Ministers at London expressed the hope that the practice
would continue. It is expected that the United States will, in good time, advise
the MFG of its intentions with respect to the next meeting. In any event, this
topic does not normally require any preparatory work on the part of the Staff
Group.

PO 226 Follow-up
5. About a year ago, the NPG invited the NMA to prepare reports on three
subjects related to PO 226. One of these (structure and deployment) has already
been received and was considered by the Ministers in London who agreed that the
subject should be kept under review as the investigation of other PO 226
problems proceeds. We have been informed that the reports on the two remaining
topics (current operational concepts and doctrine; military advantages and
disadvantages of early combined use) are expected to be received early in 1973,
in which case they might be placed on the agenda for the May 1973 MFG
Ministerial meeting. If this occurs, it is

Consultation Procedures and Facilities

6. There are a number of specific matters under this general heading that are
under active consideration in the NPG, some aspects of which may be suitable for
inclusion on the agenda for the next Ministerial meeting. As a first step toward
examination of this general subject and identification of what aspects, if any,
might be covered in a report for the May meeting, an invitation has been
extended to Colonel Vincent, the Director, Council Operations Directorate, to
inform the Staff Group about the relevant COE work, including preparations for
WINTEX 73. His briefing is tentatively scheduled for the Staff Group meeting on
27th November.

7. In a similar vein, it is planned that the Staff Group should continue its
informal discussion of procedural aspects of release procedures so as to be
better prepared to address the question of what work, if any, needs to be done
to set the stage for the further consideration of the problem that the Ministers
have agreed to schedule for their next meeting.

Timing Considerations

8. As a general comment, it might be observed that any time schedule for meeting
preparations during the next several months should take into account certain
periods of unavoidable interruption. These include the Christmas leave period
which could be assumed to extend from about mid-December to mid-January, the
WINTEX 73 exercise period (about 7th to 16th March) and the Easter period (weeks
of 16th and 23rd April; Easter is 22nd April). There will, of course, be other
demands

which will compete for the time and efforts of Staff Group members and Permanent
Representatives, and for conference and administrative services during the
months of preparation. These factors should be taken into account in considering
the time schedule outlined below.

9. Although the precise dates of the Spring meeting have not yet been agreed, it
appears reasonably certain that it will take place around the 15th of May. Using
this date as a reference point, the timing of some critical steps in getting
ready for the meeting can be established. For example, in order to give capitals
time enough to prepare for the Ministerial meeting effectively, all documents
shown on the agenda should be in their hands at least ten days to two weeks
before the date of the meeting, that is, about 1st May, 1973. Taking into
account the processing and delivery time, the Permanent Representatives should
take final action on their reports no later than mid-April. Similarly, Staff
Group action on the Working Papers to be addressed by the Permanent
Representatives at that time should be completed during the third week in March,
that is, immediately after WINTEX 73 is concluded. It follows from the foregoing
that all input materials needed for the Staff Group drafting activity, such as
studies, reports or comments, should be available no later than mid-February if
possible. These considerations are reflected in the attached timetable.

10. Several meetings of the Permanent Representatives should be foreseen as part
of the preparation for the May NPG Ministerial meeting; for such purposes as
receiving a presentation on the ACLANT Follow-on Use Study, approving reports to
the Ministers and having a final exchange of views on Ministerial agenda items.
The attached timetable shows some possible dates for these sessions.
