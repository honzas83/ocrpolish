> [!abstract] 
> # Storage of Classified Documents During the Meeting of NPG Ministers to be Held in Brussels on 26th and 27th October, 1971
> 
> This document outlines the security arrangements provided by the Belgian Authorities for the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministers' meeting held at the Palais d'Egmont in Brussels on October 26th and 27th, 1971. It details the entry requirements for conference car parks, specifying that Red Cards are for VIPs entering the Cour d'honneur and Yellow Cards are for other vehicles entering the Prince Albert Barracks car park. It further explains that security passes will be available for collection at the Security Office in Block A starting from 9:30 a.m. on Monday, October 25th. Additionally, the document specifies that each delegation is allotted a safe in the security zone for the storage of classified documents, with combinations obtainable from the Security Office. Delegations wishing to store documents on Monday evening are advised to send a member of their delegation, preferably escorted, to the Palais d'Egmont to be shown how to operate their allotted safe.

26TH AND 27TH OCTOBER, 1971
Note by the Secretary
1. The following information has been received from
the Belgian Authorities concerning security arrangements
and the method of placing classified documents in a
secure place prior to and during the Meeting of NPG
Ministers to be held at the Palais d'Egmont on 26th and
27th October.
2. Entry to Conference Car Parks
The Belgian Authorities have sent car park cards to
all delegations. These cards are of two sorts:
(a) Red Card, for the cars of VIPs permitting
entrance into the Cour d'honneur;
(b) Yellow Card, for other vehicles, permitting
entrance into the Prince Albert Barracks
car park.
3. Collection of Passes
These will be available for collection at any time
after 9.30 a.m. on Monday, 25th October, in the Security
Office, Room No. 8 on the ground floor of the office block
(Block A) near the entrance leading from the Prince
Albert car park. Delegations may find it convenient to collect
all their laisser-passer on Monday and to distribute them that evening.

4. Delegation Offices
The Delegation Offices are within the security zone. Each Delegation has been
allotted a safe, the combination of which can be obtained from the Security
Office, Room 8, in Block A.

5. Classified Documents
Delegations who wish to place their documents in security on Monday evening,
25th October, are advised to send a member of their Delegation to the Palais
d'Egmont with the documents preferably escorted by a second person. The security
service will show him how to open and close the safe in the offices allotted to
his Delegation.

(Signed) M.H.G. WILSON
