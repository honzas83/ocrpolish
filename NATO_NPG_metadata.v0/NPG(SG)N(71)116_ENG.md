> # SHAPE Preliminary Review of CENTAG Area Follow-On Use Study
> 
> This document consists of a note by the Chairman and a draft report and note intended for the Permanent Representatives and Ministers. The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, following instructions from the Permanent Representatives on October 14, 1971, sought to make suggestions on how to handle the SHAPE preliminary review of the CENTAG area follow-on use study. The Staff Group noted that while the SHAPE review should have been included in the Permanent Representatives' Report on the CENTAG area study, it was not available in time. Due to stringent time limitations, the Group suggests that the SHAPE review be brought to the attention of the Ministers via a Note by the Chairman rather than by modifying the existing report. The draft Note by the Chairman, signed by Joseph M.A.H. Luns, emphasizes that the SHAPE review raises matters that may not be fully reflected in the Permanent Representatives' report and suggests that Ministers take theAPE review into account during their discussions. Furthermore, the SHAPE review will be integrated into future examinations of the CENTAG area study by the Permanent Representatives and the Staff Group, as well as during Phases II and III of the follow-on use work programme.

Draft Report by the Staff Group
At their meeting on 14th October, 1971, the NPG Permanent Representatives
instructed the Staff Group to make suggestions for how the SHAPE preliminary
review(1) of the CENTAG area follow-on use study(2) might be handled in
preparing for the Ministerial meeting.

2. The Staff Group noted that the SHAPE review would have been reflected in the
Permanent Representatives' Report on the CENTAG area study(3) had it been
available in time. However, the Group also recognises that stringent time
limitations rule out any reasonable prospect for being able to obtain agreement
by all delegations on a new Permanent Representatives' Report or on
modifications to the previous document. Nonetheless, the Staff Group believes
that the SHAPE review should be brought to the attention of the Ministers and
suggests that this might be done by means of a Note by the Chairman along the
lines of the attached draft.

H.B. SEIM.

STUDIES OF FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE OF

Draft Note by the Chairman
Since the circulation of the Report by the Permanent Representatives(1) on the
CENTAG area follow-on use study(2), the SHAPE preliminary review(5) of that
study has been received. It raises matters which may not be fully reflected in
the Permanent Representatives' report, but lack of time has precluded the
preparation of an additional paper identifying these matters as possible areas
for Ministerial discussion. Nonetheless, the Ministers may wish to take the
SHAPE review into account in their discussion of the CENTAG area study.

2. The SHAPE review will also be taken into account during any further
examinations of the CENTAG area study by the Permanent Representatives and their
Staff Group as well as during Phases II and III of the follow-on use work
programme.

Joseph M.A.H. LUNS
