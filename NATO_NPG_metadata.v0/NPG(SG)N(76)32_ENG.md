> # Nuclear Consultation – CNI Procedures
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, outlines proposed changes to the Annex of NPG(Staff Group)WP(76)1(Second Revise). Following a meeting on 12th April 1976, delegates from the Netherlands, United Kingdom, and United States worked to resolve an amendment proposed by the Netherlands. The proposal suggests modifying the language in paragraph I.1(b) to clarify that messages may be sent after consultation on a specific use of nuclear weapons has started but before a decision is taken, or after a decision is taken but before the use itself occurs. Additionally, for consistency, paragraphs 6(b) and (c) of the basic paper are to be updated accordingly. The Chairman, R.E. Shearer, requests that Staff Group members seek approval from their respective authorities. If no comments are received by 22nd April, the white working paper will be circulated for Permanent Representatives' consideration via the silence procedure.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
NUCLEAR CONSULTATION – CNI PROCEDURES

Note by the Chairman

At their meeting on 12th April, the NPG Staff Group tasked the delegates from
the Netherlands, United Kingdom and United States to deal with the Netherlands'
amendment(1) to paragraph I.1(b) of the Annex to NPG(Staff Group)WP(76)1(Second
Revise), 24th February, 1976. An ad hoc meeting of these representatives led to
the following proposal:

(a) change second sentence in paragraph I.1(b) of the Annex to read as follows:
"This message may be sent after consultation on a specific use of nuclear
weapons has started but before a decision has been taken or after a decision has
been taken but before the use itself has occurred."

(b) For the sake of consistency, paragraphs 6(b) and (c) of the basic paper
should be changed accordingly:

"(b) after consultation on a specific use of nuclear weapons has started but
before a decision to use them has been taken (raised);

(c) after a decision to use nuclear weapons has been taken but before the use
itself has occurred (decided); and".

2. It is suggested that Staff Group members seek their authorities' approval of
the above language. If no further comments are received by close of business on
22nd April, the white working paper will be circulated for Permanent
Representatives' consideration through the silence procedure.

(Signed) R.E. SHEARER

1110 Brussels.

(1) Netherlands letter No. 2109, 6th April, 1976
