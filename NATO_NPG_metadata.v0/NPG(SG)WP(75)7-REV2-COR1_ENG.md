> # Nuclear Consultation Procedures Compilation
> 
> This document is a corrigendum to Working Paper NPG(Staff Group)WP(75)7 (2nd revise), issued by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) on August 4, 1976. It mandates the following amendments to the previous working paper: the renumbering of pages for Blue Dot Romeo and Sierra messages, and the replacement of pages 15 through 20. The replaced pages detail the specific formats and purposes of four types of 'Blue Dot' messages: the Blue Dot Whiskey message, used as a warning to political authorities that a military situation is deteriorating and nuclear weapons might be required; the Blue Dot Romeo message, used to request approval for the selective release of nuclear weapons; the the Blue Dot Sierra message, used to request preconditioned authority for selective release against fleeting targets or in rapidly developing situations; and the Blue Dot Echo message, used to report the actual expenditure of selectively released nuclear weapons and the results thereof. Each message type specifies the required precedence (FLASH), address lists for NATO Foreign and Defence Ministries, and detailed body parts including assessments, justifications, and target data.

BLUE DOT WHISKEY MESSAGE

4. This message is used by MNCs to warn NATO political authorities and NATO
member governments that the military situation in one or more NATO areas is
deteriorating in such a way that the selective.use of nuclear weapons might be
required, and, therefore, a formal request might soon be forthcoming. It is not
in itself a request for nuclear weapon release: it is intended to reduce the
time needed for consultation and decision-making on any subsequent request. For
this reason, it would contain as much of the information required by the BLUE
DOT ROMEO/SIERRA (which may follow) as is available and applicable.

The WHISKEY message may be originated by an MNC on his own initiative or as a
follow-up action to a warning or request message received from a subordinate
commander.

BLUE DOT SIERRA MESSAGE
6. This message is used by MNCs to request preconditioned authority for
selective release of nuclear weapons for use against the enemy when, and if, a
depicted set of conditions, or a described situation occurs. The SIERRA message
may be originated by an MNC on his own initiative or as a follow-up action to a
similar request received from a subordinate commander. It is intended to avoid
severe loss of forces that might occur during the period required to obtain
authorisation to employ nuclear weapons using the ROMEO message procedure and to
enable the timely and effective use of such weapons against fleeting targets,
when attacks on friendly forces develop very quickly. In some of these cases,
the release authorisation must be received by the executing commander before
specific tactical contact with the attacking enemy unit(s) has been made. Time
permitting, the SIERRA message will normally be preceded by a BLUE DOT WHISKEY
message concerning the same request for preconditioned authority for selective
release and sent to the same addressees. Consultation based on a SIERRA message
will culminate with a decision of the nuclear power concerning that request.

The format of the message is the same as for BLUE DOT ROMEO messages, with the
following addition:

PART THREE of the message will include after ECHO:

"FOXTROT – Conditions" (which must develop to require the actual employment of
the weapon(s))(1)
