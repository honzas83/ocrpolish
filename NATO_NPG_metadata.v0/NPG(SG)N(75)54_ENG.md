> # Follow-Up Action to the Ministerial Meeting in Monterey
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group Staff Group (ST FF Group) held a meeting at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels on July 9, 1975. The primary focus of the meeting was to address follow-up actions resulting from the Ministerial Meeting in Monterey. Key points of discussion included a preliminary review of a note circulated by the Chairman regarding the Follow-On Use Programme - Initiation of Phase III, and a request for delegations to provide comments on this programme. Additionally, the group agreed that the International Secretariat would compile a draft of all documents forwarded by the IFC concerning nuclear consultation. There was also a discussion regarding the potential upgrade of the document's classification to NATO SECRET based on a suggestion by the SHAPE Representative. The Chairman planned to contact delegations informally to form a working group to study warnings of NATO's intentions. Finally, the group agreed to add the Deincro Illustrative Study to their agenda and scheduled their next meeting for July 14, 1975.

FOLLOW-UP ACTION TO THE MINISTERIAL MEETING IN MONTEREY
1. The ST FF GROUP:
(a) had a preliminary discussion of this subject on the basis of a note
circulated by the Chairman in IPG(ST ff Group)N(75)52;

(b) noted that the International Secretariat would examine a suggestion by the
SHAPE Representative that in view of the content of paragraph 4 in particular,
the classification of this paper should be upgraded to N TO SECRET;

(c) requested delegations to report at the next meeting as to when their
authorities would be able to provide comments on the Follow-On Use Programme -
Initiation of Phase III;

(d) agreed that the International Secretariat should make a draft compilation,
for consideration by the Staff Group in the autumn, of all documents forwarded
by the IFC concerning nuclear consultation;

(e) noted that the Chairman would contact delegations informally regarding
possible participation in a working group to study the questions referred to in
paragraph 10 regarding warning of NATO's intentions;
