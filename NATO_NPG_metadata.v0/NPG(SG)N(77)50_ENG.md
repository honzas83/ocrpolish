> # Follow-Up Action to the Ministerial Meeting in Ottawa on 8th and 9th June, 1977
> 
> This document outlines the required actions and ongoing activities for the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) following a ministerial meeting in Ottawa to implement decisions regarding NATO's theatre nuclear forces and strategic planning. It details specific tasks assigned to the United States and the NPG Permanent Representatives, including a review of the quality and quantity of NATO's theatre nuclear forces (TNF) and the study of NIKE HERCULES site configurations. The note also addresses the conduct of follow-on work in new technology, review of CNI procedures, and the update of Selective Employment Planning (SEP) by SACEUR. Additionally, it discusses ongoing activities such as Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme and the Long-Term Programme for NATO Defence Planning. The document highlights the need for a continuing review of cruise missile developments and the study of Maritime Nuclear Warfare. It establishes a timeline for the TNF improvement priority programme, with progress reports due to the Defence Planning Committee (DPC) in December 1977 and final approval in Spring 1978. The note also mentions the US Nuclear Weapons employment policy and the SIOCP, as well as the role of the NPG as the basic task force for TNF improvements.

II. POSSIBLE COURSES OF ACTION

5. Cruise Missile. This issue could continue to be covered within the context of
future briefings on the Balance of Nuclear Forces. However, it should be noted
that in the past views expressed on this subject seemed to be rather stratified
and complex and that no attempt had so far been made to consolidate the NPG
Ministerial discussions into any written document. While it is understood that
the NPG would not look into any further specific or detailed matter in this area
prior to the discussions which can be foreseen in the NATO Council, it should
not be excluded that subsequent to Council review more definite efforts might be
undertaken in the NPG (for example, in continuation of the deliberations on
political implications of modern technology). The interdependence of any such
effort with future considerations in Phase III and on the TNF modernization
cannot be ignored. On the other hand, it seems to be evident that work in these
fields must not await any further efforts on the cruise missile topic.

6. TNF Improvements. This item will now also be given consideration outside the
NPG, the body which deals with the matter since 1974(1), although the NPG will
remain the basic task force. The timeframe foreseen for the establishment of the
TNF Improvement Priority Programme in the DPC is tight. Handling in the broader
forum might lead to administrative and procedural distinctions, both with
respect to the material being considered and its classification. However, the
possibility of providing sensitive material, if necessary, through the NPG is
maintained. Ministers have also agreed(2) in Ottawa that the usual NPG
procedures should be followed in staffing this effort. It can be anticipated
that either an alternative version of the results will be offered to the DPC or
that the final results will be kept to adequate classification, if so deemed
necessary. While the DPC requirements have been mentioned (see paragraph 4
above), it should also be recalled that NPG Permanent Representatives must be
enabled to take necessary steps to forward the progress and final reports to NPG
Ministers and to the DPC by the usual NPG procedure. The views of the NATO
Military Authorities should also be available in time as a basis for Permanent
Representatives' considerations.

9. Employment Policy. In June 1976 the United States Secretary of Defense,
Chief-of-Staff and SACEUR briefed the NPG on the US Nuclear Weapons employment
policy, the new SIOCP effective as of 1st January, 1976 and its interrelation
with SACEUR’s SSP. No changes have been indicated since. With regard to NATO’s
theatre employment policy it is recalled that the urgency of work on Phase III
was reiteratet in Ottawa and a first draft should be expected soon, probably
timely related to efforts in connection with the TNF modernization programme.

10. Work undertaken in SHAPE on Selective Employment Planning (SEP) has been
reported as advancing since the last SACEUR briefing in Hamburg in January 1976.
A related SACEUR contribution to Ministerial discussions at the Autumn NPG
meeting would be beneficial in conjunction with further progress in Phase III
and might perhaps impact the discussion on the US outline for a TNF Improvement
Programme.

11. Maritime Nuclear Warfare Study. The TOR for this study(3) foresee its
presentation to NPG Permanent Representatives in July 1977. As on past
occasions, Permanent Representatives, while studying the MNCE input, may wish to
await any comments by Military Representatives of NPG nations, before requesting
the Staff Group to prepare their formal report for Ministerial consideration. At
the moment, however, it is expected that MILREP comments can be provided to
Permanent Representatives along-with-the-study during July.

12. New Technology. Future efforts in the NF have basically been constrained by
Ministers to work which falls specifically in the nuclear field(1). Out of the
four tasks reflected in the Permanent Representatives' report(2) and endorsed by
Ministers(3) at least the first (survey and review of past and ongoing NATO
studies and other committee work) somewhat cuts across the efforts which are now
being undertaken by the Executive Working Group (EWG) established by the DPC to
make arrangements in connection with long-term NATO Defence Planning(4).
However, IS and IMS could immediately start to consider this issue in any more
detail, if nations so desire.

13. Some aspects contained in the other three tasks (conceptual framework,
assessment of technological comparisons between NATO and Warsaw Pact, technology
implications out to 1990 or 1995) could be returned to an ad hoc study group,
such as the Technology Study Group (TSG), with the objective of providing
Permanent Representatives with some considerations for detailed future work
arrangements on which they could draw when reporting to Ministers, probably for
the Spring 1978 Ministerial Meeting. In this case, meetings by this Group still
in 1977 might be desirable to provide Permanent Representatives with adequate
recommendations early in 1978. Alternatively, the work could be undertaken by
Permanent Representatives themselves and the NMAs could be asked for
contributions.

14. CNI Procedures. As Permanent Representatives have been requested to consider
a proposal that contain aspects (i.e. Message of Explicit Warning, general
warning) of the CNI procedures should be reviewed, further discussion of these
matters in the Staff Group needs guidance from capitals as to when, in what
depth and possibly with what other NATO bodies (in the case of general warning)
these issues should be pursued. In view of the complexity, the genesis and the
particular sensitivity of the CNI issue any written contributions or draft
material from capitals would be highly appreciated.

15. Review of ADM Guidelines. Since the process of implementation of these
guidelines is still ongoing and since the matter is already under review in
Phase III and during the ongoing and forthcoming examinations within the
framework of TNF Improvements, the Ministerial invitation to Permanent
Representatives to consider the need for and timing of an ADM guidelines review
only reinforces the need for advancements in the areas referred to above
(compare paragraphs 7 and 9).
