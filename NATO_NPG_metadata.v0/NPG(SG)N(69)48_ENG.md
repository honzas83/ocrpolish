> # Follow-On Action After NPG Ministerial Meeting in London
> 
> This document, a note by the Secretary of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG), outlines the proposed follow-on actions resulting from decisions made at a ministerial meeting in London on May 29th and 30th, 1969. The document details a consultation process on the use of nuclear weapons, with Belgium leading the discussion. It addresses the tactical use of nuclear weapons, establishing a timeline for comments from SACEUR and SACLANT on an Anglo-German guideline document, and sets deadlines for national comments from NDIC countries. The document also covers the use of atomic demolition munitions, with the Staff Group tasked to prepare terms of reference for analyses of their potential utility in the Allied Command Europe (ACE) Command. The Regarding strategic use of nuclear weapons, the Secretary General will request recommendations from SACEUR on the aircraft/missile mix in ACE and a program for achieving dual-capability. The The rest of the document establishes a detailed schedule for the collation of national replies and the final submission of documents for review by ministers in November 1969.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP
Follow-on action to the Ministerial meeting in London on 29th and 30th May, 1969
I. CONSULTATION ON THE USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
(paragraph 3 of Agreed Minute)
1. Belgium has accepted to continue as discussion leader and to prepare a new
document for submission to the next Ministerial meeting. Belgium has requested
that prior to the preparation of this document an exchange of views should take
place among the Permanent Representatives. The Secretary General, after
consultation with the Permanent Representative of Belgium will call a meeting at
an appropriate time.

II. TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
(paragraphs 6-10, 14 of Agreed Minute)
A. Tentative political guidelines for the possible initial tactical use of
nuclear weapons by NATO.

2. The Secretary General, in consultation with the Chairman of the Military
Committee, has arranged for comments by SACEUR and SACLANT on the Anglo-German
guideline document (1) to be made available to the discussion leaders by 18th
July, 1969.

3. The International Staff has distributed copies of NPG/D(69)2 to Norway and
Portugal; Canada, Greece and the Netherlands have received copies when the
document was first circulated in April last. Any national comments on the
document by the NDIC (including NPG) countries should be made available to the
discussion leaders by 18th July, 1969.

4. After that date, an exchange of views should take place in the Staff Group in
order to clarify and, to the extent possible, resolve any issues which might
emerge from the various comments submitted by the NATO Military and National
Authorities. However, it is hoped that the NPG delegations and the
representatives of the NMCs will be in a position to start the discussions
before that date.

5. The United Kingdom and Germany will then refine their document and circulate
it by 19th September, 1969 for review by the Permanent Representatives around
1st October.

6. If this timetable can be adhered to, it should be possible for the discussion
leaders to submit their final document before the end of Ootober and to hold the
next Ministerial meeting on 13th and 14th November.

7. Work on the development of terms of reference for exploring in more detail
the implications and interactions of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons,
as well as NATO responses to Warsaw Pact initial use can start in the Staff
Group immediately.

B. Tentative guidelines covering the use of atomic demolition munitions.
(paragraphs 12 and 13 of Agreed minute)
8. The Staff Group has already been instructed by the Permanent Representatives
to prepare terms of reference for analyses of the potential utility of ADs for
the defence of certain selected sectors of representative areas of ACE.

9. Draft terms of reference have recently been distributed by the International
Staff and discussions can start in the Staff Group immediately. There is no time
limit for the completion of the analyses. Working out a time schedule will be
among the tasks of the Staff Group.

III. STRATEGIC USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
(paragraphs 17-19 of Agreed minute)
10. The Secretary General will convey to SACEUR, through the Chairman of the
Military Committee, the Minister's invitation to submit specific recommendations
on the aircraft/missile mix in ACE and on a programme for achieving dual-
capability by 25th July/12th September.

11. The comments by the NPG members on the preliminary report by the Staff Group
on the role of theatre nuclear strike forces (1) and their replies to the
questions contained in that report should also be submitted by 25th July.

12. The United States/German collation of the national replies and SACEUR's
recommendations should be submitted by mid-October for review by the ministers
in November.
