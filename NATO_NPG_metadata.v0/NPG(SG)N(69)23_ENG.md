> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters on Monday, 3rd March, 1969
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met at NATO Headquarters on March 3, 1969, to discuss NATO's response to the Warsaw Pact's initial tactical use of nuclear weapons. The group continued its detailed discussion of a draft report, noting a comparative table of nuclear weapons available to NATO and the Warsaw Pact. The Staff Group invited the International Staff, with assistance from representatives of Germany, the United Kingdom, and the United States, to redraft Part IV and other sections of the document. It was agreed that the document should be formatted as a report by Permanent Representatives to Ministers. The group also addressed the role of theatre nuclear strike forces, noting that the International Staff would circulate a draft paper on the subject. Additionally, the meeting covered the schedule for the next meeting of the Permanent Representatives on March 18 and the draft agenda for the NPG Ministerial in May, specifically regarding discussion leaders for Soviet initial use, strategic use, and consultation. The Staff Group scheduled its next meeting for March 10, 1969, to continue discussions on Soviet initial use and the role of theatre nuclear forces.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at NATO Headquarters on Monday, 3rd March, 1969
NATO's RESPONSE TO WARSAW PACT'S INITIAL TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
Document : NPG(Staff Group)WP(69)2, dated 3rd February, 1969.
1. The STAFF GROUP continued its detailed discusson of the Chairman's draft
report on NATO's response to the Warsaw Pact's initial tactical use of nuclear
weapons and :

(a) noted, but did not discuss at this stage, a comparative table prepared by
the International Staff showing nuclear weapons available to NATO and the Warsaw
Pact, and their ranges;

(b) took note of the views expressed, and the amendments to the text proposed
during the discussion;

(c) invited the International Staff, with the assistance of various
representatives, in particular those of Germany, United Kingdom and United
States, to redraft Part IV of the document, and other parts where possible;

(d) agreed that the document should be in the form of a report by Permanent
Representatives to Ministers, and that the recommendations in Part VI should be
worded with this in mind;

(e) agreed to continue its discussion on this subject at its next meeting.

THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES
2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) recalled that, at its meeting held on 10th February, 1969, it had invited
the International Staff to prepare a draft paper on the rôle of theatre nuclear
strike forces;

(b) noted that the International Staff intended to circulate its draft paper
during the course of the week;

(c) agreed to discuss the draft paper at its next meeting, if time permitted.
NEXT MEETING OF THE NGP
3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted that the meeting of the Permanent Representatives on 18th March would
start at 4 p.m.;

(b) agreed that there would not be time on 18th March to include a presentation
on ADM Time Factors.

DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE NGP MINISTERIAL IN MAY
4. The STAFF GROUP :
noted that it was not yet known which countries would not act as discussion
leaders for items I.2 (Soviet Initial Use), II (Strategic use) and III
(Consultation).

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF-GROUP
5. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 10th March, 1969, at 2.45 p.m.;
(b) agreed to continue its discussion on Soviet Initial Use, and to discuss the
draft paper on the rôle of theatre nuclear forces (to be issued), if time
permitted.
