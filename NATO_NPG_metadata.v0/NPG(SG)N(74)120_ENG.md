> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 16th December, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on December 16, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group took note of a film on the air-launching of a Minute-Man missile and confirmed the security clearances of all attendees. Personnel changes were noted, with Colonel W. Merkl replacing Colonel R. Thyes as the German representative. The group discussed a draft report to the Nuclear Delegations Advisory Committee (NDAC) regarding Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) activities in 1974, agreeing to re-open the discussion at the next meeting. Regarding the Nunn Agreement, the group discussed nuclear aspects and agreed that a political assessment, informed by a military assessment from the Military Committee, should be prepared according to normal NPG procedure. The group also reviewed the Secretary General's Future Work Programme, specifically focusing on the Phase II Study on follow-on tactical use and the Technological Study. It was noted that the Phase II Study Team would meet on January 20, 1975, and a meeting of the Technological Study Team was proposed for the same week. Finally, the group briefly discussed potential topics for the Spring 1975 Ministerial Meeting and scheduled their next meeting for January 6, 1975.

FILM

took note of a short film showing the air-launching of a Minute-Man.
SECURITY

the matters to be discussed.

DEPARTURE
3. The STAFF GROUP:
noted with appreciation the farewell remarks made by Colonel R. Thyes, the
departing Dean of the Staff Group, and welcomed his successor, Colonel W. Merkl,
as German representative.

(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who drew

MATTERS ARISING FROM THE NEG MINISTERIAL MEETING ON 10TH DECEMBER
Nuclear Aspects of the Nunn Agreement
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who drew attention to paragraphs
4-7 of the Agreed Minute(2) concerning the nuclear aspects of the Nunn Agreement
and also to the Secretary General’s PO letter(3) on the same subject;

(b) took note of the remarks of the Canadian representative and agreed that the
programme dates given in the PO might require setting back 15 days bearing in
mind the time needed for the preparation of a full military assessment by the
Military Committee;

(c) agreed, after discussion, that the task of preparing a political assessment
taking into account the military assessment should be undertaken according to
normal NPG procedure with the Staff Group providing the working level;

Future Work Programme
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Secretary
General's Future Work Programme(1) had been noted by Ministers, and he drew
attention to the International Staff paper(2) on preparations for the Spring
1975 Ministerial Meeting; he invited comments, with particular reference to the
Phase II Study (Follow-on tactical use) and to the Technological Study;

(b) took note of a statement by the United States representative who said that
the members of the Phase II Study Team would meet in NATO on 20th January, 1975;
this would give all interested countries the possibility of contributing to the
report;

(c) concurred with the Canadian representative who proposed that a meeting of
the Technological Study Team should take place in NATO Headquarters during the
same week;

(d) noted from the remarks of the United States representative that the paper on
the Technological Study should be available for distribution during the next few
days.

SPRING MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS
