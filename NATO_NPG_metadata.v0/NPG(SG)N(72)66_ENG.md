> [!abstract] 
> # Destruction of Unwanted Documents
> 
> This document is a notice from the Secretary of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated 24th August 1972. It addresses the issue of the destruction of unwanted documents, specifically NPG Studies. The Secretary notes that while destroying unwanted documents is standard procedure for most documents, NPG Studies are produced in very limited numbers and are highly sensitive, which makes maintaining a spare stock of limited. Because of these constraints, the Secretary requests that any unwanted copies of these studies be returned to stock to maximize the use of available copies. Additionally, any addressee who receives more copies than required is requested to inform the Secretary to allow for the distribution list to be amended. The notice is signed by M.H.G. Wilson.

24th August, 1972
NOTICE
NPG(Staff Group)N(72)66
NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
DESTRUCTION OF UNWANTED DOCUMENTS
Note by the Secretary
Recently two copies of an NPG Study were destroyed because the recipients had no
further use for them. This is standard procedure with regard to most documents.
However, it should be remembered that the follow-on studies in the NPG Study
series are produced in very limited numbers and distributed on a very limited
basis because of their sensitivity and, in many cases, because of reproduction
problems. This also implies that only a small stock of spare copies can be
maintained.

2. In order, therefore, to permit the maximum use of the limited number of
copies available, it is requested that any unwanted copies of NPG Studies should
be returned to stock, not destroyed. Any addressee who receives more copies of
these studies than he requires is requested to inform me, and arrangements will
be made to amend the present distribution list.

(Signed) M.H.G. WILSON
NATO, 1110 Bruxelles.
