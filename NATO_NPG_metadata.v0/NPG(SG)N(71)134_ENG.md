> # Possible Staff Group Visit to the United States
> 
> The Chairman of the NATO Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, H.D. Seim, proposes a visit to the United States to familiarize members with the U.S. strategic nuclear posture relating to NATO security. The proposal outlines a detailed itinerary including briefings on command and control arrangements in Washington, visits to the Strategic Air Command (SAC) Headquarters in Omaha, an ICBM site, the North American Air Defence Command (NORAD) Headquarters, and a missile submarine port. The visit is planned for approximately 10 days during the period of June 15th to 30th, 1972, with a participation limit of about 30 people, including representatives from Delegations, SHAPE, the International Military Staff (IMS), and the International Staff (IS). The document also addresses logistical considerations such as transportation via official airlift (German, Canadian, or U.S.) and accommodations arranged by the U.S. hosts, while noting that participants must be cleared for CTS ATOMAL security clearance. The purpose of the visit is to provide an opportunity for exchange of views between U.S. planners and the U.S. strategic nuclear posture relating to NATO security.

POSSIBLE STAFF GROUP VISIT TO THE UNITED STATES
Note by the Chairman
In the past, the Staff Group has made a number of visits to various
headquarters, such as SHAPE and CENTAG, and to certain field installations.
Members have commented on the value of such visits and expressed the hope that
additional ones could be arranged. In this context, the Staff Group has on
several occasions discussed the possibility of visiting the United States.
During the recent NPG Ministerial Meeting, I raised the question informally with
Pentagon staff officers and was informed that they would be willing to give
serious consideration to such a visit but would like to have some specific
indication of what we had in mind. So that I may pursue this matter further with
the U.S. Authorities, I propose that the Staff Group discuss at an early
opportunity the particulars of a projected visit, and offer the following as a
basis for our discussion:

(a) Purpose of the Visit. To familiarise Staff Group members with elements of
the U.S. strategic nuclear posture relating to NATO security; to provide an
opportunity for exchange of views between U.S. planners and Staff Group members.

(b) Scope of Visit. To Washington for briefing on command and control
arrangements and discussions with U.S. planners; to Omaha for briefing on U.S.
strategic forces and visit to SAC Headquarters; to an ICBM site; to North-
American Air Defence Command Headquarters (NORAD) for briefing; to a port to
visit a missile submarine.

(c) Duration of Visit. About 10 days.

(d) Timing of Visit. During the period 15th-30th June, in order to minimise
conflict with other scheduled activity and to provide time to complete
arrangements.

(e) Participation. About 30, which would allow about two persons per Delegation,
SHAPE, IMS and IS. No wives.

(f) Transportation. If possible, from Europe to North America by scheduled
official airlift (German, Canadian or U.S.); if possible, within the U.S. by
U.S. official airlift. Group members should be prepared to say to what extent
they may be willing or able to pay for their own air transportation.

(g) Accommodations. As arranged by the U.S. hosts. Group members should be
prepared to indicate to what extent they would be willing or able to pay for
their own accommodations.

(h) Security Clearance. All participants cleared for CTS ATOMAL.

(Signed) H.D. SEIM.

NATO, BRUSSELS, 1110.
