> # Notes of a Meeting: Held in the NATO Headquarters, 110 Brussels, on Monday, 6th January, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on January 6, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with a security clearance check and a welcome to Mr. A.T. Tuyan of the International Staff Political Division. Regarding the draft report to the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) activities in 1974, the group agreed, subject to confirmation from capitals, to waive the report as an exception to normal practice. Discussions on the tactical use of nuclear weapons were addressed, specifically focusing on two secret papers from the United States Delegation regarding the organization and conduct of the NPG Study on new weapons technology and weapon characteristics. The group proposed a meeting for January 20-22, 1975, to discuss both the technology study and Phase II of the work programme on follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. Additionally, the group discussed preparations for the NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting and the implications of the Nunn Amendment, noting that further progress on the Nunn Amendment would await a paper from the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR). The meeting concluded by scheduling the next meeting for January 13, 1975.

NUCLEAR PLANNING COUNCIL (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting: held in the NATO Headquarters, 110 Brussels, on Monday, 6th
January, 1975

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP: noted that all persons present were cleared for the level of
classification of the matters to be discussed.

WELCOME
2. The STAFF GROUP: endorsed the welcome extended by the Chairman to Mr. A.T.
Tuyan, International Staff Political Division, whose interest was to follow the
progress of the Staff Group's discussions on the amendment, and cordially
invited him to attend all future meetings dealing with this matter.

DRAFT REPORT TO THE ND.C ON NPG ACTIVITIES IN 1974
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who drew attention to the draft
report(1) to the ND.C on NPG activities and invited views on what action should
be taken;

(b) noted a statement by the United States representative who said that his
authorities preferred, under present circumstances, to dispense with the report

(b) took note of the remarks of the United States representative who proposed
