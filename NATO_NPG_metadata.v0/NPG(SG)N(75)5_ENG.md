> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 13th January, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on January 13, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The group appointed Mr. Bent Rosenthall of Denmark as the new Dean of the Staff Group. It was decided that the annual report to the Nuclear Delegation and Advisory Committee (NDAC) on NPG activities for 1974 would be exceptionally left in abeyance. The group confirmed a three-day meeting scheduled for January 20-22, 1975, to receive briefings on the Phase II Study and the Technology Study, noting that COSMIC clearance would the required for certain discussions. The meeting also addressed the following: the exchange of views on the 'Nuclear aspects of the Nunn Amendment' based on a paper by the International Military Staff (IMS), International Military Staff representative agreed to yaitu transmit observations to his authorities. The group expressed a desire for the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACMIN) military assessment of the Nunn Amendment's implications to be available to Permanent Representatives. The group also discussed preparations for the 1975 Spring NPG Ministerial Meeting, including requests for the United States to provide probable dates and a suggestion from Germany to include Selective Employment Planning (SEP) on the agenda. Finally, the group scheduled future meetings of NPG Permanent Representatives for February 6 and 20, 1975, and their next ordinary Staff Group meeting for January 27, 1975.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 13th
January, 1975

NEW DEAN OF THE STAFF GROUP
1. The STAFF GROUP:
warmly concurred with the invitation of the Chairman to Mr. Bent Rosenthall,
Danish representative, to become the Dean of the Staff Group, and noted with
appreciation his words of acceptance.

DRAFT REPORT TO THE NDAC ON NPG ACTIVITIES IN 1974
2. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed that the customary report to the NDAC on NPG activities during the past
year should, exceptionally, be left in abeyance, it being understood that this
in no way created a precedent.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE MEETINGS ON 20, 21 AND 22 JANUARY
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) confirmed its agreement to a three-day meeting to be held on 20, 21 and 22
January, 1975, with a view to hearing briefings by the Study teams on the Phase
II Study and the Technology Study with subsequent discussion;

(b) agreed that the outline programme should be as follows. Phase II – Monday
20th January, 2.30 p.m. Technology Study – Tuesday 21st and Wednesday 22nd
January, 9.30 a.m. and 2.30 p.m.;

NUNN AMENDMENT – EXCHANGE OF VIEWS
4. The STAFF GROUP:

(c) noting that there appeared to be some uncertainty regarding the immediate
availability of the assessment, invited the Chairman to pursue the matter.

PREPARATIONS FOR THE 1975 SPRING NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who requested the United States
representative to enquire of his authorities the probable dates for the Spring
NPG Meeting; early knowledge of these dates was important so that the Secretary
General's programme could be co-ordinated;

(b) noted a suggestion by the German representative that Selective Employment
Planning (SEP) should be placed as an independent item on the Spring Agenda;
