> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 30th September, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on September 30, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting covered several key administrative and strategic points. First, the same group noted that all present were cleared for the security classification of the matters discussed. They also took note of a draft report by Permanent Representatives on the CNI Study. The group discussed the need to determine the place and date of the Spring 1975 Ministerial Meeting before the Rome meeting. Regarding Selective Employment Planning, the group noted delays in providing introductory material to Permanent Representatives and expected it would be available before the Ministerial meeting in Rome. The Future Work Programme was discussed, incorporating the impact of Section 302(d) of the United States Military Procurement Authorisation Act of 1975. The group agreed to changes in the draft and invited the Chairman to re-draft the proposed PO. The Implementation of Special ADM Guidelines was designated for examination by Permanent Representatives on October 10, 1974. The group also agreed with the text of a Draft Advance Press Release and set a deadline for Rome Meeting participants to inform the Chairman of their agreement. Finally, administrative arrangements for the Rome meeting, including air transportation for the NATO Courier, and the next meeting date of October 7, 1974, were established.

STATUS OF SELECTIVE EMPLOYMENT PLANNING

4.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that, at its meeting held on 9th September, 1974, it had invited
the SHAPE representative to emphasise to his authorities the need for
introductory material on Selective Employment Planning to be made available to
Permanent Representatives before their meeting on 10th October, 1974;

(b) noted the remarks of the SHAPE representative who said that unavoidable
delays had occurred and that it was doubtful whether the required material could
be available on 10th October; he was however confident that it would be ready
and circulated to all concerned before the Ministerial meeting in Rome; he
reminded members that Selective Employment Planning was not, at present, very
far advanced; SACEUR's representative at the Permanent Representatives' meeting
on 10th October would be a general/flag officer and would hopefully be able to
answer questions on this matter;

(c) noted the desire of members, and especially of these not taking part in the
Rome meeting, to be informed as soon as possible;

(d) invited the SHAPE representative to convey the views of the Staff Group to
his authorities.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME

5.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussion of the
Future Work Programme(1) held at the last meeting, and introduced new paragraphs
3 to 6, which were distributed at the table; these new paragraphs took into
account the impact which Section 302(d) of the United States Military
Procurement Authorisation Act of 1975(2) could have upon present and future NPG
activities;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and agreed to a

(c) invited the Chairman, in the light of the discussion and in consultation

DRAFT ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE
6. The STAFF GROUP:

should inform the Chairman of their agreement and intentions not later than 18th
October, 1974.

ADMINISTRATIVE ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE ROME MEETING
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a request by the Chairman who recalled that the International
Staff would be grateful for any offers of air transportation in national
official aircraft particularly in respect of the NATO Courier for classified
documents;

(b) took note of a request by the Italian representative who asked that details
concerning persons taking part in the meeting should be sent to him within the
next few days.
