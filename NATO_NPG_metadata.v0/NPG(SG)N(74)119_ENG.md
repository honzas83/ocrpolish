> # Preparation for the Spring 1975 NPG Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, discusses the principal tasks required to prepare for the NPG Ministerial meeting to be held in the United States in Spring 1975. The proposed agenda includes briefings on the balance of strategic forces, communicating NATO's intentions to use nuclear weapons, and an illustrative study on air defence use. Additionally, the document addresses the progress of the Phase II final report, the Technology Study, and the United States' Secretary of Defense report on the Nunn Amendment. It also mentions a preliminary report on the WINTEX 75 exercise regarding nuclear consultation aspects. The Chairman, Richard E. Shearer, provides a tentative planning timetable for 1975, which includes deadlines for comments on the Phase II Preliminary Report, meetings of the Permanent Representatives and the Study Team, and the final action on reports to Ministers. The document concludes with an annex containing a detailed planning timetable for the first half of 1975.

PREPARATION FOR THE SPRING 1975 NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING
Note by the Chairman
This paper discusses briefly the principal tasks the
Staff Group may have to undertake in preparing for the NPG Ministerial
meeting to be held in the United States in the Spring of 1975.
Ministerial Agenda Topics
2. Although it would be premature to attempt to make firm
decisions at this time on the subjects to be included on the agenda
for the next meeting, it would be useful, for the purpose of
organising the Staff Group work, to review the likely agenda
candidates. On the basis of current information, the following
subjects' appear to be reasonable possibilities:
(a) briefing on the balance of strategic forces;
(b) Communicating NATO's Intentions to Use Nuclear Weapons;
(c) Illustrative Study : Air Defence Use.
3. Among the issues mentioned in the latest PO on Future
NPG Work(1), those which might be treated at the next NPG Ministerial
meeting include the Phase II final report, the status of the
Technology Study, the United States' Secretary of Defense report
on the Nunn Amendment, and a preliminary report by the Permanent
Representatives on WINTEX 75. It can not be determined at this
time exactly how these various reports will relate one to the other,
nor their state of readiness when the Ministers next meet. That
there is a relationship among the Phase II report, the Technology
Study and the report required by the Nunn Amendment is beyond
question. The exact manner in which this relationship will be
portrayed must await completion of actions now underway, e.g., the
military assessment of the Nunn issues, the related political assessment
and the development of the Technology Study.

Possible Ministerial Agenda.

4. Briefing on the Balance of Strategic Forces. This briefing, which has become
a regular feature of Ministerial meetings, not only provides information for the
Ministers on developments and future prospects in the strategic nuclear field
but also serves as a background for the discussion of other basic nuclear
defence issues of common concern. For these reasons, it is assumed that the NPG
would welcome a U.S. offer to brief the Ministers on this topic at the next
meeting. This topic does not normally require any preparatory work on the part
of the NPG Permanent Representatives or Staff Group.

5. Communicating NATO's Intentions. The Study(1) on this subject and the related
Report(2) by the Permanent Representatives are ready; further work should not be
required.

6. Illustrative Study : Air Defence Use. The Study(3) and the related Permanent
Representatives' Report(4) are ready and no further work is required.

7. Follow-on Use Work Programme -- Phase-II Study Team Report. The inclusion of
this item in the next NPG Ministerial meeting agenda will depend upon meeting
the schedule to implement the necessary preparatory steps. It is reasonable to
suggest that should the next Ministerial meeting take place around the middle of
May 1975, the Final Phase II Report and the accompanying comments by the
Permanent Representatives should be ready no later than 25th April. This means
that approximately four months are available to satisfy the following:

(a) preparation of comments by capitals and NATO Military Authorities (the Phase
II Preliminary Report was circulated 4th December, 1974);

(b) revision of the Preliminary Report by the Study Team as deemed necessary in
the light of the exchange of views between the Team and the Permanent
Representatives;

(c) preparation of a related report by the Permanent Representatives.

The Secretary General, in his Note accompanying the Preliminary Report(5),
requested that written comments by countries and NATO Military Authorities
should be circulated no later than 20th January, 1975 and the revised final
version of the Study Team no later than

21st March, 1975. However, since the publication of that Note, circumstances
have changed sufficiently to suggest that a more realistic date for the
submission of written comments would be 7th February, and an NPG Permanent
Representatives' meeting on 20th February. Thus about one month would be
available to the Study Team for such revision of the preliminary report it deems
necessary. The 21st March deadline for submission of the final report is not
changed.

8. Technology Study. The first outline(1) concerning the organisation and
conduct of the Study suggests that a report might be submitted in time for
consideration by the NPG Ministers at their meeting in Autumn 1975. It may be
possible to provide a Status Report to the Ministers at their next meeting.

9. WINTEX 75. As mentioned in the Future Work Programme(2), since WINTEX 75 is
planned to take place in early March, it may be possible for the Permanent
Representatives, if they so desire, to prepare a report covering some
preliminary observations on the nuclear consultation aspects of this exercise
for discussion by the NPG Ministers at their meeting in Spring 1975. In the
affirmative, the Staff Group should be ready to work out a draft report for the
Permanent Representatives immediately after WINTEX 75.

Timing Considerations

10. At Annex is a planning timetable for preparing for the next Ministerial
meeting. The timetable is tentative and might require adjustments as work
develops. It does not reflect the impact of other activities and meetings (e.g.,
work related to Nunn Amendment, the Technology Study and WINTEX 75) which can
affect the Staff Group preparatory work.
