> # Atomic Demolition Instructions
> 
> This document, issued by the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines a proposed additional modification to the draft report on Atomic Demolition Instructions (ADIn(1)). Following discussions on May 11, 1970, the German delegation, acting on instructions from authorities in Bonn, was instructed to propose a modification to Page 20, paragraph 40(s). The proposed change suggests that NATO Military Authorities, subject to agreement by host countries, should make arrangements for minimizing the risk of fallout from nuclear demolition munitions (NDMs) used in demolition roles. This minimization should be achieved through methods such as pre-chambering in peacetime, site preparation including drilling emplacement holes during times of tension and initial hostilities, and the use of lowest yield weapons required for the demolition tasks. The proposal is intended to be addressed by the IPG Permanent Representatives at their meeting on May 12, 1970, when the basic report and its corrigendum are considered.

the German delegation was instructed by authorities in IOM to propose the
following additional modification :

Page 20, paragraph 40(s), change to read :
"(s) request the NATO Military Authorities, subject to agreement by host
countries, to make arrangements for minimizing the risk of fallout from /DMs
used in the demolition r6lo through, for example, pre-chambering in peacetime;
site preparation, such as drilling emplacement holes in times of tension and
initial hostilities; and use of lowest yield weapons required for the demolition
tasks".

2. This additional German suggested change should be addressed by the IPG
Permanent Representatives at their meeting on 12th May when the basic report and
its corrigendum are considered.
