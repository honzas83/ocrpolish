> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels on Monday, June 17, 1974. The meeting focused on several key items, including a review of documentation such as the Agreed Minute of the Bergen meeting and assessments regarding Warsaw Pact military forces and strike aircraft delivery tactics. The group noted that Ministers in Bergen had approved reports on the Military Committee Function and the Warsaw Pact Study, with the Secretary General seeking North Atlantic Council (NDAC) endorsement by July 12, 1974. Additionally, the group discussed the results of the Bergen meeting and the draft work programme for future activities. The meeting also addressed SACEUR's Illustrative Air Defence Study, proposing a pattern for presentation including a Permanent Representatives' report and a briefing by the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR). The group agreed to discuss agenda possibilities for the Autumn meeting of NPG Ministers, focusing on the Illustrative Study and the Study on Communicating NATO's Intentions. Finally, the group expressed gratitude to the Norwegian representative for the hospitality provided during the Bergen meeting and thanked the Norwegian authorities. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for June 24, 1974.

graduated response;

Commanders' Final Reports on Exercise WINTEX 73
(to note Part IV – Nuclear Warfare and Part VIII –
Communications).
