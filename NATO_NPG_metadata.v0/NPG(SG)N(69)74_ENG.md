> # Notes of a Meeting of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group)
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held meetings on Monday, October 6, and Tuesday, October 7, 1969, to address several key strategic and administrative matters. The group noted the distribution of the Anglo-German report on Tentative Political Guidelines for the Possible Initial Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons by NATO and agreed to send copies to the Permanent Representatives of Norway and Portugal. Regarding the upcoming November NPG Ministerial Meeting, the group acknowledged arrangements for guest privileges at the Army and Navy Club in Washington for members and senior participants. The group also scheduled future meetings for NPG Permanent Representatives, including a session on October 13 to discuss Consultation and a session on October 27 to discuss the Anglo-German Guidelines, Theatre Strike Forces, and the Work Programme for Follow-on use. Additionally, the group discussed a Draft Working Paper on the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons and requested the International Staff to prepare a revised version for circulation on October 9. The meeting concluded by agreeing to the next Staff Group meeting date of October 20, 1969.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 6th October, 1969, at 2.45 p.m., and on
Tuesday, 7th October, 1969, at 10 a.m., and 2.45 p.m.

ANGLO-GERMAN GUIDELINES PAPER
1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted that the Anglo-German report on Tentative Political Guidelines for the
Possible Initial Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons by NATO (1) had been
distributed to members of the Nuclear Planning Group on 2nd October;

(b) agreed that copies of this report should be sent to the Permanent
Representatives of Norway and Portugal, in anticipation of possible
recommendations that might be put to the Ministers of the Nuclear Defence
Affairs Committee at their December meeting.

NPG Ministerial Meeting in November
2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted with appreciation a statement by the Chairman who said that he was
making arrangements for guest privileges to be available at the Army and Navy
Club in Washington for members of the Staff Group;

(b) noted with appreciation that these privileges would also be extended to
senior participants in the November meeting provided that members informed the
Chairman in good time of the names, titles and ranks of their Defence ministers,
Chiefs of Staff etc.

DRAFT WORK PROGRAMME FOR FURTHER STUDIES ON THE FOLLOW-ON
TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) discussed in detail the Draft Working Paper and its
annexes (1);
(b) invited the International Staff, in the light of
the suggestions made in the discussion, to prepare
a revised working paper for circulation on 9th October;
(c) agreed to send the revised working paper to
Capitals, with a view to discussing it again,
in the light of instructions, at the next meeting
of the Staff Group.
