> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 12th July, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on July 12, 1971, to discuss several administrative and strategic matters. The group agreed that the next meeting of Permanent Representatives would take place on July 22, 1971, to discuss unresolved nuclear problems and obtain approval for Working Papers on Procedural Arrangements and Illustrative Studies. It was noted that the Permanent Representatives of the Netherlands, Norway, and Turkey would be absent from Brussels on that date. Additionally, the Norwegian Representative requested that the Norway Study not be used for discussion while Norway remained an associate member of the NPG. The group also addressed the scheduling of the Autumn 1971 Ministerial Meeting, noting that the United States had not yet confirmed the dates of October 26 and 27, and that the Portuguese Government had been approached regarding the opening of the new COMIBERLIANT Headquarters. The Staff Group scheduled their next meeting for July 19, 1971, to finalize the working papers before presenting them to the Permanent Representatives.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 12th July, 1971
NEXT MEETING OF THE NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
1. The Staff Group:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the paper circulated
by the Secretary General on the subject of unresolved nuclear problems(1), and
informed the Staff Group that the Secretary General had again repeated his
strong interest in this important field, and had expressed the wish to hold a
meeting of Permanent Representatives to discuss the matter before the holiday
season, and specifically on Thursday, 22nd July, 1971, at 10.15 a.m.;

(b) further noted the Secretary General's hope that Permanent Representatives
would consider and make suggestions for an overall approach to the problem,
where possible giving their countries' points of view, or if these were not yet
available, their own personal opinions;

(c) noted that, whilst there was unanimous agreement that this matter should be
discussed by the Permanent Representatives, some countries felt that so
important a subject should, at this early stage, be approached with care, and
with the awareness that the results of important current studies were not yet
available;

(d) noted that the Permanent Representatives of the Netherlands, Norway and
Turkey (all of which countries are at present only associate members of the NPG)
would be away from Brussels on 22nd July;

(e) agreed that Thursday, 22nd July, 1971, at 10.15 a.m., would be an

Permanent Representatives;

(f) agreed that the meeting of Permanent Representatives would be a suitable

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES AND EXAMPLES OF RELEASE REQUESTS – PRESENTATION TO
MINISTERS

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Norwegian Representative who said that his
Authorities would very much prefer that the Norway Study should not be chosen
for discussion at a time when Norway was not a full member of the Nuclear
Planning Group;

(b) in response to the Norwegian request, invited the SHAPE Representative to
inform his Authorities of the Norwegian position;

DATES OF THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN AUTUMN 1971
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the United States Representative who said that he had
not yet received confirmation from his Authorities that 26th and 27th October,
1971 would be suitable to them;

(b) noted a statement by the Chairman who said that he had been informed that
the Portuguese Government had been approached with a proposal to hold the
official opening of the new COMIBERLIANT Headquarters on 29th October, 1971, or
28th October, 1971, not on 26th October, 1971, as previously planned;

(c) expressed the hope that the United States' reserve on the dates of 26th and
27th October would be lifted before the next meeting of the Staff Group.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed to discuss the working papers on Procedural Arrangements(1) and on
Illustrative Studies(2) at their next meeting with a view to presenting agreed
