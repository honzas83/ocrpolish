> # Military Input for a Study of Maritime Nuclear Warfare in the SACLANT Area and in the Mediterranean
> 
> This document consists of a cover letter from the Secretary of the North Atlantic Council and a memorandum from the Chairman of the North Atlantic Military Committee to the Secretary General of the North Atlantic Treaty Organization. The memorandum outlines the military input for a study on maritime nuclear warfare in the SACLANT area and the Mediterranean, as requested by NPG Ministers in November 1976. The study is intended to provide guidance to NATO Military Authorities for the preparation of a paper on maritime nuclear warfare, focusing on how Theatre Nuclear Forces contribute to deterrence and defence capabilities within the NATO strategy. The goals of the study are to ensure an unmentioned ability to execute NATO's general nuclear response and carry out limited nuclear operations with discrimination and limited collateral damage. The paper should cover the Warsaw Pact threat to NATO maritime forces and NATO's own maritime capabilities, including the role of maritime nuclear forces in NATO's deterrent posture. The enclosure provides detailed military parameters for the study, including the analysis of maritime nuclear capabilities of both NATO and the United States, and the difference between SACLANT and the Mediterranean areas. It also examines the role of maritime nuclear forces in relation to area ASW, fleet protection, and strike warfare, and planned improvements in survivability and employment flexibility. The Military Authorities are expected to complete the study by the end of June 1977.

References:
a. NPG/D(76)12, 18 Nov 76

1. (NS) At their November 1976 meeting NPG Ministers invited Permanent
Representatives to arrange for the preparation of a study on Maritime Nuclear
Warfare, including new technology, in the SACLANT area and in the Mediterranean
(reference a., para 11). The Military Representatives of the NPG countries are
of the view that the terms of reference to be developed should provide guidance
to the NATO Military Authorities (NMA s) for the preparation of a paper on
Maritime Nuclear Warfare. This guidance should not attempt to determine all the
details of the study work. The study team or group should have wide discretion
within the guidelines and time limits below to arrange its own detailed working
procedures.

2. (NC) The Military Representatives of NPG countries are of the view that the
political framework of the paper has been determined by the guidance given by
Ministers with regard to Theatre Nuclear Forces at their June 1976 meeting
(reference b.). Of particular interest, however, is how Theatre Nuclear Forces
contribute to, or improve, deterrence and defence capabilities within the broad
framework of the NATO strategy contained in MC 14/3. In particular, NATO
maritime nuclear forces should

cover, inter alia, the appropriate aspects of the following:

ENCLOSURE
MILITARY PARAMETERS FOR A STUDY OF
MARITIME NUCLEAR WARFARE IN
THE SACLANT AREA AND IN THE MEDITERRANEAN
1. (NS) The military framework of the paper should be based on consideration of;
- NATO and Warsaw Pact maritime nuclear capabilities; and
- the difference between SACLANT and the Mediterranean areas owing to
geographical and other factors. In particular,

a. NATO and Warsaw Pact maritime strategy, objectives and missions, including:
(1) basic mission asymmetries;
(2) force and mission situations relating to Southern and Northern flank
protection and reinforcement; and

(3) control and protection of vital shipping.
b. Nature of NATO's maritime nuclear forces:
(1) the role of maritime nuclear forces in NATO's deterrent posture. Each
component of maritime nuclear forces should be examined with respect to:

(a) area ASW;
(b) fleet protection (air defence, ASW, and amphibious operations);
(c) strike warfare;
(d) complementary nature of nuclear and conventional maritime forces;
(e) maritime nuclear force relationship to NATO's Flexible Response Strategy;
