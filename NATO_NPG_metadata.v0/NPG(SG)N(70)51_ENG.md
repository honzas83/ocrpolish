> # Draft Final Communiqué for the NG Ministerial Meeting in Venice
> 
> This document is a draft communiqué regarding a two-day conference in Venice, Italy, held by the NATO Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) composed of Ministers of Defence from eight member nations. The meeting was chaired by NATO Secretary General Manlio Brosio and attended by Ministers of Defence from Canada, Germany, Italy, Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Turkey, the United Kingdom, and the United States. The Ministers discussed substantive policy issues concerning the possible employment of nuclear weapons for both strategic and tactical purposes within the context of NATO's strategy of flexibility in response. Based on comprehensive studies and reports, the Ministers provided further directions for continuing work on these issues to formulate policy proposals for the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee and the Defence Planning Committee. The document also notes that the next meeting of the Nuclear Planning Group is scheduled to take place in Canada in the Autumn of 1970.

DRAFT FINAL COMMUNIQUÉ
The NATO Nuclear Flanning Group, composed of Ministers of Defence of eight NATO
countries, adjourned today after a two-day conference in Venice, Italy.

Ministers of Defence attending the meeting were
Mr. Leo Cadieux, Canada; Mr. Helmut Schmidt, Germany;
Mr. Mario Tanassi, Italy; Mr. Willem den Toom, Netherlands;
Mr. Gunnar Hellesen, Norway; Mr. Ahmet Topaloglu, Turkey;
Mr. Denis Healey, United Kingdom; and Mr. Melvin R. Laird,
United States. Mr. Grieg Tidemand, former Minister of Defence
of Norway, also participated. The meeting was chaired by
Mr. Manlio Brosio, Secretary General of NATO.
On the basis of a number of comprehensive studies
and reports prepared by member countries and by the
NATO Military Authorities, the Ministers continued their
discussion of substantive policy issues pertaining to the
possible employment of nuclear weapons for both strategic
and tactical purposes in the context of NATO's strategy
of flexibility in response.
In the light of their deliberations, the Ministers
gave further directions for continuing work on the
exploration of these issues with the intent of formulating
policy proposals for eventual consideration by the Nuclear
Defence Affairs Committee and the Defence Planning
Committee.
The Ministers agreed to hold the next meeting
of the Nuclear Planning Group in Canada in the Autumn of 1970.
