> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 8th May, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on May 8, 1972, to coordinate preparations for a Ministerial Meeting in Copenhagen. The meeting included welcoming new representatives from Denmark and acknowledging the departure of former members. The group discussed the timing and content of briefings by Secretary Laird and SHAPE, as well as the International Staff's consultation procedures. They agreed on the timing of agenda items to ensure briefings were not interrupted by lunch and requested the circulation of initial drafts of the Agreed Minute and Final Communiqué. Logistics for the safe-keeping of documents in Copenhagen were addressed, specifically the role of the NATO Cosmic Control Office in transporting classified documents. Additionally, the group discussed the possibility of a future visit to United States installations and addressed concerns regarding low-yield tactical nuclear weapons. The meeting concluded with the noted availability of a United States Department of Defense field manual on nuclear weapons employment effects data for NPG countries. The next meeting was scheduled for May 30, 1972.

NUCLE R PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 8th
May, 1972

WELCOME:
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) endorsed a statement by the Chairman who welcomed Mr. B.V.G. Rosenthal, the
new Danish representative on the Staff Group;

(b) agreed to hold, at some convenient date, an entertainment to mark the
departure from the Staff Group of Mr. E.V. Quaade, who had become Deputy
Permanent Representative of Denmark;

(c) regretted that it had not been possible to mark appropriately the departure
of Mr. S.B.J. Jacobsen, but noted that the Chairman had presented him with the
traditional certificate.

NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN COPENHAGEN
Advance Press Release
2. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that simultaneous announcements would be made on 12th May by Denmark,
Germany, Turkey and the United States; that Belgium and the United Kingdom would
not make the release, and that Italy was still considering the matter.

Agenda Item I (Briefing by Secretary Laird)
3. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he had been informed
that part of Mr. Laird's briefing would deal with the threat to ACE of Soviet
Medium Range and Intermediate Range weapons; as a result of a suggestion by Mr.
Laird the Secretary General had invited General Goodpaster to comment on ACE

(b) noted the wish of those countries not taking part in the meeting to be
informed subsequently of the general content of Mr. Laird's briefing and
recalled the suggestions made to this effect at the recent meetings of the NPC
Permanent Representatives.

Agenda Item II – (Briefings on Illustrative Study and Consultation procedures)

4. The ST.FF GROUP:
(a) recalled that it had been suggested at the meeting of NPC Permanent
Representatives held on 4th May, that the SHAPE briefing (Illustrative Study)
and the International Staff briefing (Consultation procedures and facilities)
should be shortened;

(b) took note of a statement by the SHAPE representative who emphasised that
SACEUR considered no further modifications to the SHAPE briefing were desirable;

(c) invited the International Staff to reproduce copies of WHISKEY and ROMEO
messages for distribution to Delegations before the Copenhagen Meeting; these
copies should be made available to Ministers and their assistants during the
meeting;

(d) took note of suggestions regarding the International Staff briefing, and
invited the Chairman to shorten it in the light of the views expressed.

Timing of Agenda Items

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a letter from the Danish Delegation(1)

Safe-keeping of Documents at Copenhagen

7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalling paragraph 3 of the letter from the Danish Delegation(1) mentioned

in safe-custody;

(b) took note that the Head of the NATO Cosmic Control Office (Mr. K. Bittner,
Tel. No 2481), would be travelling to Copenhagen by military aircraft and would
be available to carry ATOMAL and other classified documents for the
International Staff and Delegations; such documents should be given to him not
later than 1100 hours on Wednesday, 17th May for the outward journey, and
immediately after the end of the Meeting for the return journey.

POSSIBLE STAFF GROUP VISIT TO THE UNITED STATES
8. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled the discussions held at previous meetings on the possibility of
organising a visit by the Staff Group to certain installations in the United
States, and agreed that it was no longer practicable to plan for a visit in June
this year;

(b) agreed however that a visit at a later date (Autumn 1972 or Winter 1972/73)
should be envisaged, and agreed to continue the discussion of such a visit at a
future meeting.

LOW-YIELD TACTICAL NUCLEAR WEAPONS
9. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled the remarks made by the Canadian representative at the last meeting
concerning a newspaper article on smaller, cleaner tactical nuclear weapons and
his request for more information from the United States authorities;

(b) took note of a statement by the United States representative who drew
attention to a number of inaccurate statements in the newspaper article in
question, and briefly explained the present United States policy with regard to
tactical nuclear weapons.

Field Manual, Nuclear Weapons Employment Effects Data Modified"
(FM-101-51-2(Modified)); it was available in the NATO Registry (Atomal Section);
