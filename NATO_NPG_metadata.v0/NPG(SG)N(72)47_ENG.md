> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 110 Brussels, on Tuesday, 30th May, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on May 30, 1972, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to review actions following the 11th NPG Ministerial Meeting in Copenhagen. The group endorsed a statement of gratitude to the Danish authorities for the success of the Copenhagen meeting. They discussed the distribution of the Agreed Minute and follow-up action notices, and agreed to provide Portugal with basic documentation of NPG Ministerial Meetings. The meeting covered several tactical nuclear weapon studies, including the Turkish Study, the Mediterranean Maritime Study, and the the Air Battle Study. The group noted the initiation of Phase II of the Follow-on Work Programme and discussed how views from Ministers and Permanent Representatives should be communicated to the Working Group. The United Kingdom representative mentioned the intention to circulate the ACLANT Follow-on Use Study in Autumn 1972. The group also discussed the initiation of an illustrative study of Initial Use at sea in the ACLANT area, to be completed by August 25, 1972. Consultation procedures and facilities were identified as a priority for Permanent Representatives to examine. Potential future work items were listed, including the implementation of Special ADM Guidelines and the implications of the POSEIDON upgrading. Finally, the Italian representative reported that the Transition Study, involving Italy, Belgium, and Turkey, was about to be circulated.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES

8. The STAFF GROUP :

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to the Ministers' wish
(Agreed Minute, paragraph 15) that the Permanent Representatives should, as a
matter of priority, examine further the consultation procedures and facilities ;
he observed that the remit was open to various interpretations and wondered
whether the priority was in the timing or the importance of the work ; he said
that although this matter could be discussed at the next meeting, he would
welcome initial views ;

(b) noted, during the subsequent discussion, a general feeling that consultation
procedures were a direct responsibility of the NPG whereas consultation
facilities were linked with communications, crisis management and headquarters'
survivability – matters which concerned other committees ; agreed therefore that
it might be necessary to call upon these other committees for advice and
assistance ;

(c) agreed to examine further the desirability of an early report on this matter
to Permanent Representatives ; agreed to re-open the discussion at the next
meeting.
