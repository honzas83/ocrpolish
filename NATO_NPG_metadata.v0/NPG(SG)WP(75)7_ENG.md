> # Nuclear Consultation Procedures (Compilation)
> 
> This document, a working paper from the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, provides guidance on political consultation procedures within the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) in time of hostilities concerning the use of nuclear weapons. It serves as a compilation of procedures contained in various NPG and MNC documents. The guidance is designed to be consistent with approved policy guidance and complementary to general consultation procedures set forth in C-M(72)46. It outlines the process for providing the views of alliance members to nuclear powers for consideration in deciding whether to release weapons. The document includes a detailed table of contents covering general information, actions initiated by Major NATO Commanders or member governments, and descriptions of various 'Blue Dot' messages. The Annexes provide a list of NATO policy documents dealing with nuclear consultation, such as the Athens Guidelines, and define key terms such as 'Assessment', 'ATOMAL Classification', 'Initial Use', and 'Selective Release'. The document emphasizes that consultation processes are designed to be as simple and flexible as possible to ensure timely decision-making during a crisis.

INTRODUCTION
PURPOSE
1. This document provides guidance on procedures for political consultation
within the Alliance in time of hostilities on the question of using nuclear
weapons.

SCOPE
2. The guidance herein is concerned with political consultation on the question
of using nuclear weapons which is part of a broader consultation or crisis
management process. It is based on and consistent with approved policy guidance
on nuclear consultation procedures(1). It is also compatible with and
complementary to the general consultation procedures set forth in C-M(72)46, and
will be followed if time and circumstances permit. Major NATO Commanders Nuclear
Weapons Release Procedures (MWRP)(2) also apply for that part which concerns the
submission of requests to political authorities. This guidance will be up-dated
as required.

3.   BACKGROUND

3.   General consultation among Alliance members is an important objective and a
prominent feature in [AFC]. Because each of the Alliance members retains its
full independence in foreign affairs and defence matters, the consultation
process is an essential element of collective planning and co-ordinated action.
This consultation activity takes place in the Council/DPC as the normal forum.
Carriec out continuously in peacetime and designed to be intensified in times of
tension and war, it covers a wide range of political and defence subjects
including questions of possible NATO use of nuclear weapons.

4.   One of the first major steps in this specific field was the adoption of the
Athens Guidelines in 1962, which outlined in general terms the agreed Alliance
assessments of the possibilitie: for consultation in the Council under three
different assumptions concerning the scale of Warsaw Pact attack(1). These
Guidelines reflected the necessity for consultation on the use of nuclear
weapons in relation to the development of a conflict as envisaged by the
strategy of massive retaliation effective at that time. As nuclear weapon assets
in NATO Europe expanded, and as NATO strategy shifted toward flexibility in
response, there was a growing recognit: of a requirement for procedures and
facilities to permit consultation on selective and controlled tactical use of
nuclear weapons to be carried out in an effective and timely fashion. The
conclusions and recommendations of the Special Committee of Defence Ministers to

Definitions: The following terms are frequently used in the document. In
accordance with agreed NATO understanding; they are defined as follows:

1) **Assessment**. An unformatted message or document containing the evaluation
of the military and/or political situation by the originator, based on
intelligence and other available information. Might contain views on possible
use of nuclear weapons.

2) **ATOMAL Classification**. The classification covering Atomic Information
made available by the Government of the United States of America to the North
Atlantic Treaty Organisation and its member states, pursuant to the Agreement
between the Parties to the North Atlantic Treaty for-cooperation regarding
Atomic Information dated 18th June, 1964. Guidance for identification and
handling of ATOMAL information is contained in CM(68)41(Revised), 24th November,
1970.

3) **Early Notification Message**. An unformatted message, originated by SACEUR
and directed to proper political and military authorities, dealing with the
possible impending use of nuclear weapons.

4) **Executing Commander**. The Commander designated by the Releasing Commander
as responsible for ordering the delivery of the weapon(s) to the target(s)
authorised in the release message.

5) **Follow-on Use**. The selective use of nuclear weapons after initial
selective use by NATO.

6) **Initial Use**. The selective first use of nuclear weapons by NATO.

7) **Nuclear Weapons Categories**.(1)

a) **Ground Organic**: Nuclear weapons organic to Army Groups and Marine Corps
Units.

b) **Air, Ground Non-Organic and Ship-borne**: All nuclear weapons to be
delivered by aircraft, non-organic ground systems and surface ships against land
or sea targets.

NUCLEAR CONSULTATION PROCEDURES
CHARTER I

GENERAL
1. Consultation on the use of nuclear weapons is one particular aspect of a
broader process of consultation among member governments of the Alliance that
takes place continuously in peacetime and would increase in intensity should the
international situation worsen to tension, crisis and hostilities.

2. The nuclear consultation in its proper sense begins as early as the possible
use of nuclear weapons becomes an issue. Depending on the circumstances, this
matter can be raised in general terms in assessments of the evolving situation
prepared by member governments, the Council/DFC, or the Major NATO Commanders
before any specific requests or proposals for nuclear weapon use are made. In
more specific terms, it can be raised by a Major NATO Commander or a member
government through a request or proposal for nuclear weapons use or a warning
that such a request or proposal might soon be forthcoming.

3. The nuclear consultation process takes place on the basis of information
concerning the politico-military situation as available to the Council/DFC and
to capitals. It would unfold between capitals and in the Council/DFC. It
culminates with the decision by the nuclear power(s) concerned on the proposed
use. Time permitting such a decision, in conformity with the Consultation
Guidelines(1), would take into account the results of the related consultation
among member countries of the Alliance.

CHAPTER 2
INFORMATION SOURCES
5. The effectiveness of the nuclear consultation process is critically dependent
on the amount of available information and on the ability of participants in
that process to exchange information and views in a rapid, secure and reliable
way. Nuclear consultation takes place against the background of the flow of
information envisag in the process of general consultation during peacetime and
expected to increase in time of tension, crisis and hostilities. Further-more,
part of the context for nuclear consultation would be the basic information
available to capitals and the Council/DFC in peacetime, such as the summary of
political guidance, concepts and doctrine of NATO military forces, and weapons
characteristics and effects. The Situation Centre of NATO Headquarters has the
continuing task of supporting the Council/DFC in its consultation role and
acting as a clearing house for the exchange of data and intelligence that forms
the essential common data base for consultation. In fulfilling the latter task,
the NATO Situation Centre is assisted by the Current Intelligence Group (CIG),
which sifts and collates the political, politico-military, economic and
technological information that is made available to NATO Headquarters from many
sources such as national capitals, the NATO Military Authorities and public
information media, and produces regular summaries of this information for
circulation both within the Headquarters and throughout the Alliance.

6. In time of war, political authorities will need to be kept fully informed of
developments that may foreshadow the use of nuclear weapons. This requirement
will extend from the early stages of a conflict when only very general
indications are likely to be available through later stages when specific
release requests may be made.

7. In the early stages of a conflict, the main sources of information are
"Intelligence Summaries" on enemy activities, "Situation Summaries" concerning
the operation of our own forces and "Assessments." Assessments can be
evaluations of the military situation (prepared either by MNCS or by the
Military Committee) or politico-military appraisals developed by individual
governments or by the Council/DFC. Under certain circumstances, one or more of
these assessments might contain general indications of impending necessity of
nuclear weapons use by NATO or indications of impending Warsaw Pact use, as
reflected in the evolving situation.

8. At later stages of a conflict, the information required by political
authorities to consult on specific proposals for NATO use of nuclear weapons are
provided, in large measure, by the nuclear consultation messages originated by
the MNCS, such as SACEUR's "Early Notification Messages" and MNCS, WHISKEY,
ROMEO and SIERRA messages(1).

9. Detailed procedures for handling of information within the NATO Headquarters
have been developed by the Council Operations Directorate.

CHAPTER 3

ACTION INITIATED BY A MAJOR NATO COMMANDER

10. NATO Major Commanders have established common or similar Nuclear Weapons
Release Procedures (NWRP)(1). In implementation of such procedures, the first
concrete action undertaken by a Major NATO Commander would be the transmission
of one of the following messages:

(a) Early Notification Message (SACEUR only – not formally part of his NWRP
referenced in footnote (1));

(b) WHISKEY Message (warning message);
(c) ROMEO Message (actual selective release request message)
(d) SIERRA Message (pre-conditioned selective release message).

These messages may be originated by the Major NATO Commander on his own
initiative or as a follow-up action to similar messages (as those under (b), (c)
and (d)) received by him from a subordinate commander.

11. With regard to the Early Notification Message, these are part of SACEUR's
procedures by which he provides early notification to the Council/DFC and
National Capitals when consideration is being given to the possible use of
nuclear weapons in defense of ACE.

12. All of the above-mentioned Messages will normally be sent on the NATO-wide
network. If, for any reason, it appears that delivery to any of the addressees
might be delayed or impossible on the NATO-wide network, it is expected that one
or the other of the NATO-wide switching centres will refile the messages on
another circuit, such as the HIGOM network, for transmission to the relevant
addressees.

COUNCIL/DPC COMMUNICATION TO THE NUCLEAR POWER(S)

BLUE DOT WHISKEY MESSAGE
This message is used by M/ICs to warn NATO political authorities and NATO member
governments that the military situation in one or more NATO areas is
deteriorating in such a way that the selective use of nuclear weapons might be

in itself a request for nuclear weapon release: it is intended to reduce the
time needed for consultation and decision-making. For this reason, it would
contain as much of the information required by the BLUE DOT ROTE/O/SIERRA (which
may follow) as is available and applicable.

The WHISKEY message may be originated by an MNC on his own initiative or as a

BLUE DOT SIERRA MESSAGE
This message is used by NfCs to request pre-conditioned authority for selective
release of nuclear weapons for use against the enemy when, and if, a depicted
set of conditions, or a described situation occurs. The SIERRA message may be
originated by an MNC on his own initiative or as a follow-up action to a similar
request received from a subordinate commander. It is intended to avoid severe
loss of forces that might occur during the period required to obtain
authorisation to employ nuclear weapons using the ROMEO message procedure and to
enable the timely and effective use of such weapons against fleeting targets,
when attacks on friendly forces develop very quickly. In some of these cases,
the release authorisation must be received by the executing commander before
specific tactical contact with the attacking enemy unit(s) has been made. Time
permitting, the SIERRA message will normally be preceded by a BLUE DOT WHILIEY
message concerning the same request for pre-conditioned authority for selective
release and sent to the same addressees. The consultation based on a SIERRA
message will culminate with a decision of the nuclear power concerning that
request.
