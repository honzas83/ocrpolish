> [!abstract] 
> # Transport of Classified Documents to and from Copenhagen
> 
> This notice from the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) outlines the procedures for transporting classified documents, specifically ATOMAL and COSMIC TOP SECRET documents, to and from Copenhagen. Mr. Bittner, the Head of the NATO Cosmic Control Section, is tasked with carrying these documents in a Belgian military aircraft. Delegations are instructed to pack, seal, and identify their packages with the names of authorized personnel for withdrawal. Packages must be delivered to the NATO Cosmic Registry by 11 a.m. on Wednesday, 17th May, for arrival in Copenhagen at approximately 19.00 hours at the Frederiksberg Slot. For the return journey, delegations are requested to hand in packages to Room 226 in the Frederiksberg Slot before the end of the meeting. Any packages handed in after Mr. Bittner's departure will be returned to Brussels under arrangements made by the United States Delegation and will be available for collection on Tuesday, 23rd May.

NOTICE
NPG(Staff Group)N(72)44
NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
TRANSPORT OF CLASSIFIED DOCUMENTS TO AND FROM COPENHAGEN
Note by the Secretary
As stated at our meeting on Monday, 8th May, Mr. Bittner, the Head of the NATO
Cosmic Control Section, has been invited to travel from Brussels to Copenhagen,
and return, in a Belgian military aircraft. Delegations who have no secure means
of carrying their classified documents, and in particular their ATOMAL and
COSMIC TOP SECRET documents, are invited to give such documents to Mr. Bittner
for transportation.

2. Documents should be packed, sealed and given an identification number by the
Delegation. The name or names of the persons authorised to withdraw the package
from safe-keeping should be clearly stated on the cover.

3. Packages should be given to Mr. Bittner in the NATO Cosmic Registry not later
than 11 a.m. on Wednesday, 17th May. Mr. Bittner is due to arrive at the
Copenhagen military airport at 18.10 hours, and will therefore probably arrive
at the Frederiksberg Slot about 19.00 hrs, where he will put all packages into
safe-keeping. Delegations or individuals may collect their packages when they
wish between 19.00 and 22.00 hours that evening, or the next morning.

4. For the return journey, since the Belgian military aircraft will be departing
almost immediately after the end of the Meeting, Delegations are requested to
hand in their packages to Room 226 in the Frederiksberg Slot if possible before
the end of the meeting, so that Mr. Bittner can leave quickly. Packages which
are handed in after the departure of Mr. Bittner will be taken back to Brussels
under arrangements which have kindly been made by the United States Delegation,
and will be available for collection by their owners from the United States
Delegation on Tuesday, 23rd May.

(Signed) M.H.G. WILSON
