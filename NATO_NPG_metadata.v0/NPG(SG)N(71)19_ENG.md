> # National Participation in Allied Nuclear Planning
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, provides a chronological survey of principal documents and decisions regarding national participation in NATO's allied nuclear planning process. Prepared by the International Staff in response to a request from the Staff Group to facilitate discussions on a recent Italian initiative, the survey covers a period from 1966 to 1968. It details the recommendations of the Special Committee of Defence Ministers and the Defence Policy Committee (DPC), { "//": "OCR error correction: 'DPC/D(66)39' is a reference code, not a part of the title" } the reorganization of the Military Committee (MC) to enhance national military views, and the outcomes of NPG Ministerial meetings in Washington and Ankara. The survey highlights various proposals from the United States and Italy regarding the establishment of a NATO nuclear planning system, including the roles of the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) and the host countries. It also mentions the US suggestion to establish a a NATO nuclear planning system for Allied Command Europe. The document outlines the process for formulating and review of NATO nuclear policy and the process for the development of policy guidelines. It concludes with the reports of the Permanent Representatives and the the Military Committee's recommendations for increased national participation at the MC, MNC, and MSC levels.

At annex is a chronological survey of principal documents pertaining to national

issues that have been advanced for consideration as well as the decisions taken
by the NPG/NDAC/DPC.

2. There were a number of other documents, dealing specifically with items such
as consultations on the use of nuclear weapons, host country arrangements and
stockpile information, that also touched indirectly upon the subject of national
participation. These documents are not included in the survey since their impact
on the national participation question was not major significance.

Group’s request for some background information to facilitate the Group’s

I. SPECIAL COMMITTEE OF DEFENCE MINISTERS
The terms of reference of WG III stated inter alia: "To submit recommendations
... on possible ways of improving and extending allied participation in the
planning for the use of nuclear weapons in defence of NATO".

2. Several documents originated by Germany, Italy, Turkey and Greece were
addressed by WG III during 1966 in the course of developing recommendations for
improving the planning process.

3. Special Committee recommendations(1) were approved by the DPC in December
1966.

II. DPC/D(66)39, 7th December, 1966
4. National participation in allied nuclear planning was seen in two aspects:
(a) National participation in the development of general guidance concerning
policies, plans, programmes and procedures for the use of nuclear weapons. The
NPG/NDAC arrangements would provide the means for doing this;

(b) National participation in military planning. The Ministers believed that the
proposed reorganisation of the Military Committee would constitute a major step
toward enabling national governments to bring their views importantly to bear on
the work of the NATO Military Authorities. Subject to policy guidance of the
political authorities, the Military Committee would be charged with preparing
military guidance for the Major NATO Commands on the preparation of plans. The
Military Committee would also review

IV. MILITARY COMMITTEE DOCUMENT, MCM-25-67, 25th August, 1967
6. The Military Committee considered that existing arrangements at MNC and MSC
level were effective and should continue. It suggested that improvements could
be made at the level of the MC which could enhance the introduction of national
military views into nuclear planning and policy formulation. It was stated that
NATO nuclear plans were not reviewed above MNC level to check effectiveness and
compliance with policy.

7. The document envisaged the following functions of the MC in discharging its
nuclear role:

(a) Resolving national military nuclear views into military policy and guidance;
(b) Issuing Council-approved policy and guidance to MNCs;
(c) Reviewing and approving nuclear plans of MNCs;

VI. INTERNATIONAL STAFF NOTE FMM/67/104, 17th November, 1967
9. The note lists documents relevant to NATO liaison arrangements with US
national nuclear planning bodies.

VII. US DOCUMENT NPG/US(67)-D4, 22nd December, 1967
10. This is an elaboration by the US of their suggestion in the footnote to the
Ankara Minute. While it was stated that there was no need to settle on any fixed
procedure at that time, the following approach to the formulation and review of
NATO nuclear policy and to the development of policy guidelines was outlined:

(a) Following ministerial consensus, the MNC concerned – if appropriate with the
countries most concerned – should develop a specific study or plan;

(b) These studies or plans should be reviewed by host countries, countries
providing the weapons or delivery systems and the MC.

(c) The studies or plans, together with national comments and a summary report
by the MC should be examined by the NPG Ministers;

(d) The NPG should develop political guidance and transmit it to the NDAC and
DPC for approval.

VIII. ITALIAN WORKING PAPER NPG/IT(68)D.1, 25th February, 1968
11. Italy felt that the US suggestions dealt with a procedure for the
establishment of an overall concept and political guidance. There was, however,
a need for a NATO nuclear planning system of which the US proposals would be
only a part. This system should include the following steps:

(a) (Preliminary phase): Development by NPG/NDAC and approval by DPC of a
"general concept for the use of theatre nuclear weapons" on the basis of
detailed studies;

(b) On the basis of this concept, development by the NATO military commands of
"detailed plans for the employment of nuclear weapons for deterrence and defence
in various contingencies". At this stage opportunity should be given to the
national military authorities concerned, i.e., – of the country on or from whose
territory the nuclear weapons would be used;

IX. MC DOCUMENT MCM-16-68, 27th March, 1968
12. The MC stated that there were various ways in which national authorities can
participate effectively in NATO military nuclear planning by contact with the
NMAs at several levels. It made specific recommendations for increased national
participation at MC, MNC and MSC levels.

X. PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES PROGRESS REPORT - NPG/D(68)3, 4th April, 1968
13. The Permanent Representatives drew attention to the distinction between NATO
military operational planning, and the development and review of an Alliance
nuclear policy.

14. With respect to military planning, the Permanent Representatives supported
the MC suggestions in MCM-16-68. They added that the Italian proposals in
NPG/IT(68)D.1 were complementary to the MC suggestions.

15. With regard to the development and review of policy, they referred to the
policy-making function of the NPG/NDAC; the US proposals in NPG/US(67)-D4 were
designed to assist this function.

XI. NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN THE HAGUE, 18th – 19th April, 1968

16. The Ministers(1):

"(a) in respect of national participation in military operational nuclear
planning, agreed to forward the recommendations of the Military Committee to the
Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee for approval and onward submission to the
Defence Planning Committee;

(b) in respect of the development and review of NATO nuclear policy, invited the
Permanent Representatives to undertake further work aimed at developing more
specific proposals for a nuclear planning procedure, taking into account the
papers already produced and the discussion among Ministers."

XII. DPC MINISTERIAL MEETING, 10th May, 1968

17. The Ministers approved the recommendations of the Military Committee,
endorsed by the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee and the Nuclear Planning Group
as follows(2):

"(a) that, following the provisions of MC 57/2 and the conclusions of MCM-25-67,
the Military Committee should continue to 'develop' its capabilities to perform
its role in NATO military nuclear planning by, amongst other things,
accommodating national views;

(b) that increased national participation in NATO military nuclear planning
should be achieved by some or all of the following:

at Major NATO Command level by

(1) Ministries of Defence taking greater advantage of the existing channels
between Major NATO Commands and nations by direct co-operation;

(ii) increasing the use of National Military Representatives, keeping National
Military Representatives periodically informed on the subject of nuclear plans;

(iii) making available within NATO Headquarters the relevant portions of nuclear
plans to affected Ministries of Defence for analysis;

XVI. ITALIAN NOTE, 25th January, 1971
21. Italy suggested to re-examine the question. As a first step, countries may
usefully be invited to express their views as to the appropriateness of the
existing procedures, indicating in particular whether such procedures appear
adequate to ensure a full presentation of national military views about current
nuclear planning; and whether the policy followed in ACE responds to the MC
recommendations as approved by the DPC. Such views should be supplemented, if
necessary, by recommendations or suggestions for improvements.
