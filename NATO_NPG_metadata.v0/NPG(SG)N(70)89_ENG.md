> [!abstract] 
> # Provision of Note-Takers for Military Observer Attending NPG and NDIC Ministerial Meetings
> 
> Following a request by Admiral Henderson and General Goodpaster at an Ottawa meeting, the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group is addressing the formalization of the provision of note-takers for military observers attending NPG and NDIC Ministerial meetings. The document outlines the established practices of limiting attendance to ensure frank and uninhibited discussions and maintaining the privileged nature of the proceedings. It details the attendance of the Chairman of the Military Committee and Major NATO Commanders, such as SACEUR and SACLANT, as observers. The Chairman notes that while individual delegations may take notes for their own use, military observers have historically been expected to refrain from making full reports of the proceedings. To establish a formal basis for modifying these arrangements, the Chairman poses several questions to the Staff Group members regarding the authorization of note-takers, the number of note-takers allowed, the the rotation of personnel, rank restrictions, and the dissemination of notes. Staff Group members are requested to seek views from their respective capitals on these points in anticipation of a future discussion.

PROVISION OF NOTE-TAKERS FOR MILITARY OBSERVER ATTENDING NPG AND NDIC
MINISTERIAL MEETINGS

Note by the Chairman

Just prior to the beginning of the Ottawa meeting I was asked by Admiral
Henderson and General Goodpaster whether they could bring a note-taker into the
meeting. I replied that the present strict rules about attendance at Ministerial
meetings did not permit this and that, since these rules had been established by
the NPG Ministers, any change in the rules would have to be agreed by them. I
told them that, in my view, it would be impossible to get such agreement in the
few minutes remaining before the meeting began. I also said that they and their
staff officers directly concerned with NPG matters were welcome to look at the
notes taken by the International Staff for the Secretary General.

2. After the meeting Admiral Henderson informed me that he had talked to each
Minister and that none had objected to allowing a note-taker to accompany the
military observers at NPG meetings. Admiral Henderson later indicated that he
had in mind that each military observer at an NPG meeting would be authorized to
bring along his own note-taker.

3. Since Admiral Henderson's contacts with Ministers at Ottawa were of an
informal nature, it is appropriate for the Staff Group to address the question
in order to establish a more formal basis for modifying current arrangements and
to make any changes a matter of record.

NATO, 1110 Brussels.

4. The question of authorizing note-takers for the military observers should be
viewed in the light of two well-established practices which the NPG has adopted
in the interest of facilitating frank and uninhibited Ministerial meetings:

(-) severely limiting the number of persons present in the conference room, and
(b) carefully maintaining the privileged nature of the discussions.

For background purposes, the evolution of these practics is outlined briefly in
the following paragraphs.

5. Attendance
(a) The NPG Charter(1) states that membership of the IPG should consist of
Defence Ministers and that, for the sake of efficiency, the membership should be
as small as possible. The document also notes that discussion in small groups
with very limited numbers of persons in attendance is essential to substantive
and frank exchanges of views.

(b) In consonance with this approach, the number of persons actually present in
the conference room from a participating delegation is set at a maximum of five.
The same ceiling applies to the contingent from the International Staff(2).

(c) It was also agreed that the attendance of representatives of the NTO
Military authorities should not be institutionalised; military participation
should be invited on a case-by-case basis whenever necessary (Gentlemen's
agreement). Subsequently,

(i) the Chairman of the Military Committee was invited to attend all Ministerial
meetings personally as an observer (decision taken at NPG Permanent
Representatives meeting on 5th June 1967);

6. Record of meetings

(a) Continuing the practice developed in the Special Committee, the remarks of
Ministers at an NPG meeting are considered to be of a privileged nature and no
official verbatim or summary record of the deliberations of the NPG is
distributed; the actions of the NPG are recorded officially only in a brief
Agreed Minute. Individual delegations are permitted to take notes during the
meeting for their own use but, in view of the privileged nature of the
proceedings, the individual Ministers exercise close personal supervision over
any dissemination of information from these notes;

(b) with respect to military observers, it was recognized that their presence at
NPG meetings would be beneficial not only to give the Ministers direct personal
access to the views of the senior NATO Military authorities during the NPG
deliberations but also to provide these senior officers some insights into NPG
positions, interests and concerns that would assist them in carrying out their
own responsibilities. In this regard, however, the military observers were
expected to respect the privileged nature of the NPG deliberations and, in
particular, to refrain from making full or summary reports, oral or written, of
NPG Ministerial or Permanent Representative meetings which would contravene the
NPG practice.

7. In light of the foregoing, it would be helpful for the Staff Group to have
the views of NPG members, including those countries not present at Ottawa, on
the following questions:

(a) Should the military observers (Chairman, Military Committee and those Major
NATO Commanders who are invited to attend) be authorized to have a note-taker at
NPG Ministerial meetings?

(b) If so, should one note-taker serve for all military observers or should each
observer be authorized to have his own?

(c) Should rotation of note-takers be permitted, similar to the practice now
followed in many delegations?

(d) Should any restrictions be established concerning the rank or position of
the note-taker, e.g., would there be objections to a deputy commander or other
senior officer acting as note-taker?

(e) Should the military observers be requested to identify prior to the meeting
all officers whom they plan to use as note-takers, similar to the practice now
followed by country delegations?

(f) What restrictions, if any, should be placed on the dissemination of notes
(or information derived from notes) taken for military observers?

(g) Should any arrangements that may be agreed with respect to note-takers for
military observers at NPG Ministerial meetings also apply to NDAC Ministerial
meetings? If not, what arrangements, if any, should apply to NDAC sessions?

8. Staff Group members are requested to seek views of their capitals on the
foregoing points in anticipation of an initial Staff Group discussion of the
matter at an early date.

(Signed) H. B. SEIM
