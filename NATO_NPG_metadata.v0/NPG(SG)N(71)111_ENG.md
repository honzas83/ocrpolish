> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 4th October, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on October 4, 1971, to review several key items. The group took note of the issuance of documents regarding maritime use in the Mediterranean, the CENTAG Area Follow-on use Study, and tactical nuclear defence. They discussed a battlefield illustrative study to be provided by the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) for the next Ministerial Meeting. The meeting also addressed a draft advance press release for the October NPG Ministerial Meeting, including a request from Belgium to ensure alphabetical order in the French translation. Arrangements for the October Ministerial Meeting in Belgium were outlined, including hospitality and transport for delegations. The group discussed the participation of SACEUR in meetings of NPG Permanent Representatives to encourage dialogue between military and political sides, though the Italian Representative advised caution. The group also noted that ten Senior Officers Orientation Courses on nuclear weapons would be held at the Oberammergau Training Centre in 1972. Finally, the group discussed the status of Follow-on Studies and scheduled their next meeting for October 18, 1971, noting a meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives on October 14, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 4th October, 1971
DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that as a result of decisions made at the meetings of NPG Permanent
Representatives held on 27th September and 1st October, the following documents
had been or were about to be issued:

(a) NPG/D(71)10, 4th October, 1971 – Illustrative Study – Maritime use in the
Mediterranean

(b) NPG/D(71)11, 4th October, 1971 – CENTAG Area Follow-on use Study
(c) NPG/D(71)12, 4th October, 1971 – Status of Work in progress – Follow-on
Studies

(d) NPG/D(71)13, 4th October, 1971 – Unresolved problems related to tactical
nuclear defence

(e) NPG/D(71)14, 30th September, 1971 – Implementation of Special ADM
Guidelines.

BATTLEFIELD ILLUSTRATIVE STUDY
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted with appreciation a statement by the Chairman who said that the
Secretary General had received a letter from the Chairman of the Military
Committee saying SACEUR could provide a battlefield illustrative study for the
Spring or Autumn Ministerial Meeting next year;

ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE FOR THE OCTOBER NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the proposed
draft advance press release circulated in the International Staff paper(1), and
recalled that if no comments or objections were received by 15th October, the
text and release timing would be considered as approved;

(b) took note of a request by the Belgian Representative who asked that, in the
French text, countries should be placed in alphabetical order.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE OCTOBER MINISTERIAL MEETING
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Belgian Representative who briefly described
the programme for the NPG Ministerial on 26th and 27th October, gave details of
the hospitality and entertainments proposed, and outlined the probable programme
for the Ladies; he said that printed programmes would be made available in due
course;

(b) noted that the Belgian Authorities intended to make arrangements to meet
delegations at Zaventem on the evening of Monday, 25th October, and would be
glad to receive information as soon as possible on times of arrival.

PARTICIPATION BY SACEUR IN MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the fact that
SACEUR had been represented at the table during the last two meetings of the NPG
Permanent Representatives; he hoped that this practice would continue because it
encouraged the dialogue between the military and political sides; he gave some
historical background to the matter and drew attention to the useful
contribution of the military authorities to the work of the Staff Group;

COURSES ON NUCLEAR WEAPONS AT OBERAMMERGAU

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that ten Senior Officers¹
Orientation Courses on nuclear weapons would be held at Oberammergau Training
Centre during 1972; reservations for these courses could be made through his
office; he requested Delegations to inform him of their requirements.

STUDIES OF FOLLOW-ON USE – PHASE

7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) discussed briefly the status of work on Follow-on Studies, the desirability
of initiating new work such as an Air Study, the comparative analysis of the
regional studies, and the advantages and disadvantages of beginning the second
phase before the first phase was completed;

(b) recognised the magnitude of the task and agreed that the Staff Group should
continue its discussions at an early date.

NEXT MEETING OF THE NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

8. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that the next meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives would
take place on Thursday, 14th October, 1971, at 3.30 p.m. in the Situation
Centre, Presentation Room;

(b) took note of a statement by the SHAPE Representative who said that
preparations for the briefing on 14th October on the Illustrative Study were
nearly completed, and that it was the intention of SACEUR, on 26th October,
personally to introduce the briefing and to comment upon it at the end; the
briefing was expected to last about 30 minutes;
