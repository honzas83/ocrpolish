> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 9th September, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on September 9, 1974, in Brussels to coordinate several administrative and strategic matters. The group confirmed that the next meeting of the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent Representatives would take place on October 10, 1974, with October 17 as a reserve date. Discussions regarding the Status of Selective Employment Planning were held, with the group agreeing that introductory papers should be prepared jointly by the Major NATO Commanders (MNCS) and provided to Permanent Representatives before the October meeting. The group also noted a briefing on the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Air Defence Use will be given on October 10, and that invitations have been sent to SACEUR and SACLANT for the NPG Ministerial Meeting in Rome. The Italian representative provided updates on the logistical arrangements for the Rome meeting, including hospitality and accommodation. Finally, the group addressed the communication of NATO's intention to use nuclear weapons, noting that the United Kingdom's proposed modifications to the CNI Study and related report required a review. A small working group was established to prepare a third revision of the Study, with a plan to examine it on September 12, 1974. The Staff Group provisionally agreed to meet again on September 12, 1974, at 3 p.m.

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 9th
September, 1974

NEXT MEETING OF NIG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he was now able to
confirm that the next meeting of the NPC Permanent Representatives was fixed for
Thursday, 10th October, 1974, in the afternoon; in reserve was 17th October,
1974, in the morning.

STATUS OF SELECTIVE EMPLOYMENT PLANNING

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted the remarks of the Chairman who recalled that the Status of Selective
Employment Planning was Item II on the agenda for the NPG Ministerial Meeting in
November, and that it would be helpful if some introduction to this matter were
provided by the Major NATO Commanders to the NPG Permanent Representatives prior
to the Ministerial Meeting;

(b) agreed to the suggestion of the SACLANT representatives that the
introductory paper could be prepared jointly by the two MNCS;

(c) invited the SHAPE representatives to emphasize to his authorities the need
for the introductory material to be available to the Permanent Representatives
before 10th October, 1974.

SHAPE ILLUSTRATIVE STUDY ON AIR DEFENCE USE
3. The STAFF GROUP:
noted with appreciation the remarks of the SHAPE representative who confirmed
that a briefing on the SHAPE Illustrative Study would be given at the meeting of
NPG Permanent Representatives on 10th October, 1974; this briefing would be a
rehearsal for Item IV on the agenda for the NPG Ministerial Meeting.

INVITATIONS TO MAJOR NATO COMMANDERS
4. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who said invitations had been sent to SACEUR
and SACLANT to attend the meeting of NPG Ministers to be held in Rome in
November; no letter was sent to the Chairman of the Military Committee because
it was customary that he had a standing invitation to such meetings.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE ROME MEETING
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted with appreciation the remarks of the Italian representative who said
that he would shortly circulate information concerning the timetable for the NPG
Ministerial Meeting in Rome, with details regarding hospitality, the Ladies
Programme, hotel accommodation and other matters;

(b) noted the remarks of the Chairman who said that the International Staff
would very much appreciate any offers of air transportation to and from Rome and
in particular for the NATO courier carrying classified papers who should if
possible travel in military transport.

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Deputy Chairman

who recalled that the CNI Study(1) and the related draft Permanent
Representatives report(2) had achieved a measure of agreement within the Staff
Group, but that comments and suggestions just received from the United Kingdom
made it necessary to review the form, procedure and content of these two papers;

limitations and exclusions; he recalled the need to circulate agreed versions of
the Study and the Report not later than 20th September so that they could be
examined in Capitals before submission to Permanent Representatives on 10th
October;

(b) took note of a statement by the United Kingdom representative who explained
the reasons for his authorities' change of position, and outlined the various
modifications to the form and content which they proposed; he appreciated the
constraints which time imposed upon the production of these papers;

(c) noted that two representatives had received instructions to agree to the
latest revision of the Study, but that the other representatives were without
instructions;

(d) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion which addressed
principally the extent to which the warning/ultimatum issue should be included
in the Study;

(e) agreed that a small, open-ended working group should meet next day with the
object of preparing a 3rd Revision of the Study in the light of the United
Kingdom proposals and of the views expressed in the discussion;

(f) agreed, subject to the progress achieved by the Working Group and to the
need to give representatives time to read the new version, to examine the 3rd
Revision without delay, and, if possible on Thursday afternoon, 12th September.
