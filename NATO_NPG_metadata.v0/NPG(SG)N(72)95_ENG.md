> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 23rd October, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels on October 23, 1972. The meeting's primary focus was the follow-on use programme, the updating of Nuclear Planning Directives, and the transport of classified documents to Lancaster House. The Staff Group took note of several documents, including those relating to the follow-on use programme and a draft agenda for a possible Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministerial Meeting in December 1972. Additionally, the United States Representative discussed the possibility of modifying the text of the Denmark Study to remove its ATOMAL grading. The SACLANT Representative mentioned that his authorities intended to submit a paper concerning unresolved problems by the end of the year. Finally, the group agreed to schedule their next meeting for November 6, 1972.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 23rd
October, 1972

DOCUMENTATION

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the following
documents which had recently been issued or were about to be issued:

(a) NPG(Staff Group)N(72)88, dated 10th October, 1972: List of principal
documents relating to the follow-on use programme;

(b) NPG(Staff Group)N(72)91, dated 17th October, 1972: Draft agenda for a
possible NDAC Meeting in December 1972;

(c) NPG/D(72)17, dated 17th October, 1972 (CTS): Updating of Nuclear Planning
Directives;

(d) NPG(Staff Group)N(72)94, dated 20th October, 1972: Transport of classified
documents to Lancaster House;

(e) NPG/Study/34, to be circulated: Netherlands observations on Phase II of the
Follow-on study programme.

CLASSIFICATION OF THE DENMARK STUDY

2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the United States Representative who said that he
had received instructions from his authorities concerning the possibility of
