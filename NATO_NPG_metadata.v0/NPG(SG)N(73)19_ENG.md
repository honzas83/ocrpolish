> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 5th March, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on March 5, 1973, in Brussels to coordinate preparations for the upcoming Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministerial Meeting in Ankara. The group discussed the scheduling of a meeting for NPG Permanent Representatives on April 16, and noted that the Norwegian Ambassador would be unavailable, but the date remained unchanged. The meeting covered the agenda for the Ankara meeting, including potential items such as preliminary observations on WINTEX 73 and a choice between the Illustrative Study on Demonstrative Use and the military paper on Operational Concepts and Doctrine. Additionally, the group reviewed the draft status report on the Follow-on Use Work Programme and requested an annex from the Phase II Study Team. The meeting also addressed a draft report on the North Norway Demonstrative Use Illustrative Study, deferring detailed examination until the agenda for the Ministerial meeting was finalized. Finally, the group agreed to hold its next meeting on March 19, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 5th
March, 1973

NEXT MEETING OF NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that it had been decided to hold a meeting of NPG Permanent
Representatives on 16th April with the object of examining and approving the
working papers which would become the documents for the NPG Ministerial Meeting
in Ankara in mid-May;

(b) noted that the Norwegian Ambassador would not be in Brussels that week, but
after discussion, agreed nevertheless to make no change to the date and time of
the meeting which therefore remained as Monday 16th April at 3.30 p.m.

THE ACLANT AREA FOLLOW-ON STUDY

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the fourth
version(1) of the report on the ACLANT Area Follow-on Study should, if approved
by the Staff Group, now be prepared as an NPG Working Paper for circulation to
Capitals prior to its examination by the Permanent Representatives at their
meeting on 16th April;

(b) noted that all members were now able to accept the report and invited the
International Staff to circulate it as a working paper.

DRAFT MINISTERIAL AGENDA FOR ANKARA

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the draft(1)
prepared by the International Staff and said that every eligible item had been
included with a view to giving the Staff Group as wide a choice as possible; he
suggested that this choice should be made at the next meeting of the Staff
Group, so that a draft agenda could be circulated to Capitals not later than the
end of March;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion;
(c) noted a general view that preliminary observations on WINTEX 73 should if
possible feature on the agenda;

(d) noted a general view that a choice should be made between the Illustrative
Study on Demonstrative Use and the military paper on Operational Concepts and
Doctrine; noted in this respect that the situation regarding the availability of
the latter paper would be determined in a few days time;

(e) agreed to continue the discussion at the next meeting.
DRAFT STATUS REPORT ON THE FOLLOW-ON USE WORK PROGRAMME
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recommended that, in accordance
with past practice, a brief status report on the Follow-on Use Work Programme
should be prepared for Ministers; a draft(2) had been made by the International
Staff and he suggested that an annex prepared by the Phase II Study Team should
be attached setting out progress in this phase; this status report was non-
controversial;

(b) invited the three representatives of the Phase II Study Team to prepare the
proposed Annex;

(c) agreed to the text of the draft status report and invited the International
Staff to circulate it to Capitals in the form of an NPG Working Paper for
comments and for subsequent approval by Permanent Representatives at their
meeting on 16th April.

NORTH NORWAY DEMONSTRATIVE USE ILLUSTRATIVE STUDY

5. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced a draft report(1) on
a Demonstrative Use Illustrative Study(2) and invited general comments on it; he
suggested however that a detailed examination ought to be deferred until it was
known whether the matter would be included in the Ministerial agenda;

(b) noted the views expressed in the discussion and agreed to address the matter
again before the end of the month and before finally deciding upon a Ministerial
agenda;

(c) invited the International Staff to prepare a note on the question of
communicating NATO's intentions to the enemy(3).

NEXT MEETING

6. The STAFF GROUP:

agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 19th March, 1973, at 3 p.m.
