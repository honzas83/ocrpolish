> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday, 30th April, 1975
> 
> The NATO Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on April 30, 1975, in Brussels to examine an unclassified report regarding the nuclear aspects of the Nunn Amendment. The meeting's purpose was to prepare a short paper recording that an exchange of views had taken place in the spirit of consultation. Several member states, including the United Kingdom, Canada, Canada, Denmark, Germany, and Italy, requested more time to consult their authorities. The Netherlands representative noted that while their authorities had seen the report and found it consistent with the classified version, formal approval would be difficult. The Belgian representative and the International Military Staff representative expressed concerns that publishing the report on the Congressional record could disturb public opinion regarding the tactical nuclear weapons balance between NATO and the Warsaw Pact, as well as impact Mutual and Balanced Force Reductions (MBFR) considerations. The United States representative emphasized the pressure on the Secretary of Defense and the difficulty of obtaining further delays. The meeting concluded with the invitation to the Chairman to prepare a short paper for circulation. The group also noted that their next meeting was scheduled for May 2, 1975, to examine and potentially approve a report on Phase II.

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that all persons present were cleared for the security classification of
the matters to be discussed.

UNCLASSIFIED REPORT TO THE US CONGRESS ON THE NUCLEAR ASPECTS OF THE NUNN
AMENDMENT

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the NGP Permanent
Representatives at an informal meeting held on the previous day, had expressed
the wish that the Staff Group should meet without delay with a view to examining
the unclassified report(1) on the nuclear aspects of the Nunn Amendment; this
report would be sent by the US Permanent Representative to Senator Stennis on
2nd May, 1975; the object of the examination by the Staff Group was to prepare a
short paper recording that an exchange of views had taken place, in the spirit
of the continuing process of consultation, and to record the appreciation of the
NGP Staff Group for the opportunity of seeing the unclassified report before its
despatch; he invited views;

(1) circulated by the US representative in 3 copies to each --- Delegation on

(b) took note. of. the. observations. made in. the ensuing discussion and in
particular of the following views:

(i) the United Kingdom representative asked whether the Staff Group should
approve the unclassified text, rather than prepare a short paper; he asked if
the United States could give more time;

(ii) the Canadian, Danish, German and Italian representatives asked for more
time in which to consult their authorities; the Netherlands representative said
that his authorities had seen the unclassified report and felt that it conformed
with the classified version; formal approval however would be difficult to
obtain;

(iii) the Belgian representative suggested that the date for the appearance of
the paper on the Congressional record should be delayed; he recalled that it was
undesirable to disturb public opinion by discussing the balance of tactical
nuclear weapons between NATO and the Warsaw Pact, and that there were also MBFR
considerations;

(iv) the International Military Staff representative agreed with the Belgian
representative in hoping that the publication on the Congressional record could
be delayed;

(c) took note of a statement by the United States representative who emphasised
the pressure on the Secretary of Defense, and expressed the opinion that further
delays would be difficult to obtain; he would however report the views of the
Secretary General and individual comments by representatives to his authorities
in Washington;

(d) invited the Chairman to prepare a short paper, in the light of the
discussion, for circulation to NPG Staff Group members(1).
