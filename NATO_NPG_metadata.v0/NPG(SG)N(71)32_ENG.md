> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 8th March, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on 8th March 1971 to address several administrative and strategic concerns. The group discussed new procedures for controlling access to classified conferences, limiting attendance to those with appropriate security clearance and a need-to-know basis, as detailed in PO/71/109 and ON/71/22. They noted reports on release procedures from SACEUR and SACLANT/CINCHAN, and discussed the approach to handling follow-on use studies. The group agreed that principal issues in these studies should be identified and circulated as an issues paper to capitals to prepare Ministers for their meeting in May. The United States expressed a different preference regarding the drafting of the issues paper, while other members agreed that it should be drafted in the Staff Group. The group also noted the upcoming distribution of Illustrative Studies and Examples of Release Requests from SACEUR and SACLAN. Additionally, the Staff Group planned a visit to SHAPE Headquarters and scheduled their next meeting for 15th March 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 8th March, 1971
CONTROL OF ACCESS TO CLASSIFIED CONFERENCES
1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the new
procedures concerning the control of access to classified conferences and
limitation of attendance to those persons who held the appropriate level of
security clearance, and who had a need to know;

(b) noted that these procedures were set out in PO/71/109 of 5th March, 1971,
addressed to Permanent Representatives, and in ON/71/22, also 5th March, 1971,
to the International Staff;

(c) noted that the new procedures would come into effect on Monday, 15th March,
1971.

BRIEFING GIVEN BY SACEUR TO THE DPC ON RELEASE PROCEDURES ON 22nd FEBRUARY
2. The STAFF GROUP :
noted a report by the SHAPE representative that the text of the briefing on
release procedures given to the DPC on 22nd February should be forwarded
shortly.

SACILANT AND CINCHAN RELEASE PROCEDURES
3. The STAFF GROUP :
took note of a statement by the Chairman in which he said that the Secretary
General had considered the desirability of arranging a briefing for Permanent
Representatives on the subject of SACILANT and CINCHAN Release Procedures, and
bearing in mind that SACEUR’s procedures were almost identical, had decided that
this would serve no useful purpose at present.

HANDLING OF STUDIES ON FOLLOW-ON USE - APPROA.H TO ISSUES
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) discussed in detail the various aspects of the problem in the light of the
US position(1) on the approach to be used in handling the follow-on use studies
and the suggestions of the Secretary General(2) on the same subject as reflected
in his introductory remarks at the NPG Permanent Representatives meeting on 4th
March;

(b) noted statements by most delegations, reconfirming the views of their
authorities that a meaningful role for the NPG Permanent Representatives and the
NPG Staff Group was an essential element for effective functioning of the NPG,
particularly in preparing for Ministerial meetings;

(c) took note of a statement by the Chairman in which he summarized the
discussion as follows:

(i) all members agreed that it was highly desirable that the principal issues in
the follow-on use studies be clearly identified and circulated in one or more
papers to capitals to assist them in preparing their Ministers for the meeting
and to serve as a common point of departure for the Ministerial discussion;

(ii) all members except the United States preferred that an issues paper should
be drafted in the Staff Group for submission to the Permanent Representatives
for approval;

(iii) it was hoped and expected that those countries that had participated in
specific studies would take the initiative in providing inputs for the issues
paper, drawing on their respective capitals for advice;

(iv) there was no reason why representatives from capitals could not participate
in meetings of the Staff Group or Permanent Representatives dealing with the
issues paper, if individual delegations or capitals so desired;

(v) the submission of an issues paper by the Permanent Representatives would in
no way preclude a country from circulating an

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES AND EXAMPLES OF RELEASE REQUESTS
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman in which he said that Illustrative Studies
and Examples of Release Requests from SACEUR and SACLAN would arrive very
shortly and that he hoped to distribute these during the course of the week;

(b) noted that the material in the SACEUR and SACLAN contributions might be too

problem after the papers had been received;

Permanent Representatives might be needed to facilitate discussion of the
illustrative studies at the May Ministerial meeting.

VISIT BY THE STAFF GROUP TO SHAPE HEADQUARTERS

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted with appreciation a statement by the Chairman who said that an
invitation had been received from SHAPE for the Staff Group to visit the
Headquarters on a date to be mutually agreed; noted that this would be a morning
visit followed by lunch and return to NATO immediately afterwards;

(b) noted that no suitable date for this visit was available in March, and
agreed to postpone it until April or May.

FUTURE MEETINGS OF NPC PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that a briefing by the NORTHAG team might take place during the week
beginning 19th April, and a briefing on the Greek Study on 29th April;

(b) noted that Permanent Representatives would probably wish to discuss the
Illustrative Studies, the documents for the Ministerial Meeting, the status
report on the Follow-on use Studies, and the Issues question at meetings to be
scheduled during the period of the second half of March to the end of April;

(c) noted a suggestion by the Canadian Representative who said that it might be
useful for Ministers if briefings on SACUR's or SACLAN's release procedures
could be given to them at the Spring meeting. (1)

NEXT MEETING

8. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 15th March, 1971, at 3 p.m.
