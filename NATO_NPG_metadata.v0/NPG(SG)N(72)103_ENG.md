> # Possible Staff Group Visit to the United States
> 
> The Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) proposes reopening consideration of a visit to the United States for members of the Staff Group members to familiarize themselves with elements of the U.S. strategic nuclear posture relating to NATO security and provide an opportunity for exchange of views between U.S. planners and Staff Group members. Previous attempts to organize the visit in the summer of 1972 were ruled out due to transportation difficulties and scheduling conflicts. The proposed visit, planned for a two-week period between January 21st and February 16th, 1973, would include briefings in Washington, Omaha, and Norfolk, as well as visits to a missile site and the North American Air Defence Command Headquarters. Participation would involve one or two persons per delegation from the International Military Staff (IMS), Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE), Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic (SACLAINT), and the International Staff (IS). The Chairman suggests examining the possibility of obtaining official air transportation for transatlantic and internal U.S. movements, potentially requesting assistance from the United States, Germany, and Canada. If official transportation cannot be secured, the use of commercial means is suggested as an alternative.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

POSSIBLE STAFF GROUP VISIT TO THE UNITED STATES

Note by the Chairman

About a year ago the NPG Staff Group began exploring the possibility of a visit
to the United States(1) for the purpose of familiarising Staff Group members
with elements of the U.S. strategic nuclear posture relating to NATO security
and providing an opportunity for exchange of views between U.S. planners and
Staff Group members. At that time, contacts were made with United States
authorities concerning details of a possible trip(2) and the matter of
transportation, official and commercial(3), was also raised. However,
transportation difficulties and scheduling conflicts ruled out the possibility
of a visit during the summer of 1972, and the Staff Group agreed that prospects
in the early months of 1973 might be more promising. It is suggested, therefore,
that the Staff Group might wish to reopen its consideration of this subject.

2. For discussion purposes, it is envisaged that the scope of the visit might be
as originally foreseen: to Washington for briefing on command and control
arrangements and for discussion with U.S. planners; to Omaha for briefings on
U.S. strategic forces at SAC Headquarters and on the SACEUR liaison
arrangements; to a missile site; to the North American Air Defence Command
Headquarters; and to Norfolk for briefing at SACLAINT Headquarters and visit to
a missile submarine, if feasible. The duration would be about two weeks, perhaps
sometime in the four-week period from 21st January to 16th February.
Participation would be as originally planned: one or two persons per delegation,
IMS, SHAPE, SACLAINTREPEUR and IS.

3. With regard to transportation, it is suggested that the possibilities of
obtaining official air transportation for the transatlantic legs and for
internal movements within the U.S. be examined. The Staff Group may wish to ask
the members of those countries that may be in a position to provide such
assistance (for example, the United States, Germany and Canada) to make
enquiries with their authorities concerning the availability of official air
transportation. In this connection, it may perhaps be worth mentioning that the
prospects of obtaining assistance may be enhanced if several countries share in
one way or another in providing support for the visit. In the event that
official air transportation cannot be made available, the Staff Group may wish
to explore the option of using commercial means.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM.
