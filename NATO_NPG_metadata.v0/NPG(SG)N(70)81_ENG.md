> # Transportation of Classified Documents for the Ottawa Ministerial Meeting
> 
> Due to recent aircraft hijackings, the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) has issued a notice to increase precautions for transporting classified documents between NATO countries. The document outlines specific arrangements for the transport of documents from Brussels to Ottawa for the Ministerial meeting, requiring documents to be delivered to the NATO Central Registry by October 22, 1970. These documents will be transported via Canadian military aircraft via Laahr, accompanied by a courier, and stored in the Security Office of the Government Conference Centre in Ottawa. Similar arrangements are provided for the return trip from Ottawa to Brussels on October 30, 1970. Delegations are encouraged to use these official channels and are asked to limit the weight of packages to essential documents only. The Office of Security emphasizes that hand-carrying documents should be reduced to an absolute minimum, suggesting that delegates leave documents in Brussels and pick up copies in Ottawa.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
TRANSPORTATION OF CLASSIFIED DOCUMENTS FOR THE OTTAWA
MINISTERIAL MEETING
Note by the Secretary
Recent cases of hijacking of civilian aircraft have made it necessary to
increase the precautions concerning

the transport of classified documents between NATO countries. Detailed official
instructions on this subject are expected

shortly; in the meantime restrictions must be placed on the transport of
documents by civilian aircraft and wherever

possible only land or sea transport or military aircraft should be used.
2. With regard to the NPG Ministerial meeting in Ottawa it is proposed to make
the following arrangements for the

transport of classified documents:
(a) Brussels to Ottawa Classified documents, properly
packaged and sealed in accordance with normal
procedure, and addressed to the person who will
collect them in Ottawa, should be taken to the
NATO Central Registry not later than Thursday evening
22nd October, 1970. Arrangements will be made for
these documents to be transported to Ottawa by a
Canadian military aircraft via Laahr, accompanied by a
courier. The Canadian Authorities in Ottawa will
place these documents in safe keeping and make
them available to their owners in the Security Office,
Room 211 in the Government Conference Centre.
(b) Ottawa to Brussels The same arrangements will apply
in reverse for the return trip. Documents duly
packaged and addressed should be handed in to the
Conference Security Office on Friday afternoon,
30th October, 1970; arrangements will be made for
return of the documents to Brussels by the same
courier.
Delegations and other representatives taking part
in the conference at Ottawa are encouraged to take advantage
of these arrangements. Please send only essential documents
in order to limit the weight of the package to be carried
by the courier.

3. Although it is realised that some documents may have to be hand-carried to
Ottawa, the Office of Security have asked me to emphasise that the number of
such documents should be reduced to an absolute minimum. If a delegate is
obliged to work on a particular paper until the day before departure; he should,
if possible, leave it behind in Brussels and pick up a copy in Ottawa, which he
should arrange to be sent in advance by courier as described above.
