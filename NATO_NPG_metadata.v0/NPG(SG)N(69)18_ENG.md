> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters on Monday, 10th February, 1969
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting at NATO Headquarters on February 10, 1969, to address several key strategic and technical issues. The group discussed the role of theatre nuclear strike forces, noting statements from the International Military Staff (IMS) and the United Kingdom, specifically regarding the relationship between the Single Integrated Operational Plan (SIOP) and the Strike Support Plan (SSP). The group invited the United Kingdom to provide written notes on these views and requested the International Staff to prepare a draft paper. Regarding consultation, the group agreed to defer further work until the new United States administration in Washington could provide its views, with a potential date of March 17. Additionally, the group noted that Belgium would likely act as the discussion leader for the item on consultation at the upcoming NPG Ministerial Meeting in May. The meeting also covered the modification of the Atomic Demolition Munition (ADM) Time Factors document based on comments from Germany, Greece, Italy, and the United Kingdom. Finally, the the group scheduled a briefing on Atomic Demolition Munitions for February 25, 1969, and set the next meeting for February 24, 1969, to discuss Soviet Initial Use and the draft agenda for May.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held at NATO Headquarters on monday, 10th February, 1969

THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES

1. Continuing from the meetings held on 27th January and 3rd February, the STAFF
GROUP pursued its study of the rôle of theatre nuclear strike forces.

2. During the course of the discussion the STAFF GROUP :

(a) noted a statement by the IMS representative who said that the various papers
(1) sent by the IMS Nuclear Team to the Staff Group should be considered as
educational background material and not as an official transmission from the
IIs;

(b) noted a statement by the Chairman who described the form in which the
International Staff proposed to produce the first draft of the paper on the rôle
of theatre nuclear forces;

(c) took note of a statement by the United Kingdom representative who commented
upon the background to the problem, recalled the views expressed on various
occasions by Mr. Macnamara and Mr. Healey, and said that the first issue to be
defined was that of the rôle of the forces assigned to the SSP and its
relationship with the SIOP;

(d) invited the United Kingdom representative to provide the International Staff

(e) noted the various other views expressed by the other members, and agreed

(f) invited the International Staff, in the light of the views expressed, to

CONSULTATION
3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) agreed that further work on the question of Consultation should be deferred
until the United States authorities in Washington had had the time to obtain the
views of the new administration;

(b) accepted a suggestion by the United States representative that he would
enquire from his authorities whether they might be able to produce their views
by 17th March;

(c) agreed to discuss the matter on 17th March, when it was hoped that more
definite information would be available.

DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN MAY
4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted with appreciation that Belgium would probably accept to be discussion
leader for Item III (Consultation); this would be confirmed shortly;

(b) agreed to bring the matter up at the next meeting.
ATOMIC DEMOLITION MUNITION TIME/FACTORS
5. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) agreed that the ADM Time Factors document (1) should be modified in the
light of comments from the German, Greek, Italian and United Kingdom
representatives;

(b) examined the document and agreed to a number of modifications to the text;
