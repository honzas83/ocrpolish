> # Notice of a Meeting Held on Tuesday 24th March, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on March 24, 1970, to address several key security and operational matters. The group noted reports on security matters and confirmed that Belgian authorities were taking feasible action. Regarding visits to Germany, the United Kingdom Delegation abandoned efforts to visit the British Army of the Rhine (BAOR) due to national command restrictions, but suggested a visit to NORTHAG and AFCCENT Headquarters instead. The group also discussed nuclear briefings for the Military Committee, noting that a more classified third briefing would be held in April. A significant portion of the meeting focused on Atomic Demolition Munition (ADM) studies, where the group agreed that a general statement of ADM issues should be prepared by the NPG Permanent Representatives for Ministerial discussion in Venice. Additionally, the group discussed the agenda for the upcoming NPG Ministerial meeting in Venice and the logistics of air transport to the city. Finally, the group rescheduled its next meeting to April 6, 1970, in Brussels.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notc of a meeting held on Tuesday 24th March, 1970

NUCLEAR PLANNING – SECURITY ASPECTS

1. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who reported upon the present situation
regarding a security matter discussed at previous meetings;

(b) noted a statement by the Belgian Representative who confirmed that his
authorities had been fully informed since the start of the matter and were
taking all feasible action.

STAFF GROUP VISIT TO GERMANY

2. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) noted a statement by the Chairman that, due to restrictions on discussing
NATO matters at national command level, the United Kingdom Delegation had
abandoned its efforts to arrange a Staff Group visit to BAOR. He had been
informed, however, that SHAPE would be happy to make suitable arrangements for
the Staff Group to visit NORTHAG;

(b) noted a suggestion that this visit should include AFCCENT Headquarters;

(c) invited the Chairman to pursue this matter with SHAPE representatives, with
a view to arranging a visit to take place about the third week in June.

NATO NUCLEAR BRIEFINGS FOR THE MILITARY COMMITTEE
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) discussed certain questions and other matters arising from the nuclear
Briefing held for the Military Committee on Monday, 23rd March, and took note of
the answers given and views expressed;

(b) noted that the third briefing, to be held in April, would be of a more
classified nature, and would be presented by staff from the major NATO commands;

(c) recorded its appreciation for having been invited to the second briefing,
and expressed the hope that the Military Committee would see fit to invite the
Staff Group to the third.

ATOMIC DEMOLITION MUNITION STUDIES
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted with appreciation that the intervention of the Secretary General and
prompt action by SACEUR had resulted in the timely receipt of three ADM studies;

(b) noted that a fourth study would arrive in due course from NORTHAG;
(c) noted with interest SHAPE'S initial comments on the ADM studies (SHAPE 45/70
of 18th March, 1970);

(d) took note of the remarks of the United States Representative who made a
short synthesis of the three papers, on a personal basis, as a starting point to
the discussion;

(e) took note of the various views expressed during the subsequent discussion,
in particular those concerning pre-conditioned release and custodial
arrangements;

(f) agreed that the ADM studies were too voluminous to be of direct value to
Ministers in Venice, and concurred that a general statement of ADM issues should
be prepared by the NPG Permanent Representatives to provide a basis for
Ministerial discussion;

(g) invited the International Staff to prepare an initial draft of the general
statement in time for the next meeting of the Staff Group.

AGENDA FOR NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN JUNE

5.  The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to discuss at the next meeting the revised draft agenda(1) for the
Meeting of NPG Ministers to be held in Venice.

NEXT MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
6.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that at the last meeting, when the timetable of work(2) had been
discussed, it had been agreed that there was a need for a meeting of the NPG
Permanent Representatives on or about 15th April;

(b) noted that it was questionable whether the paper on ADMs would be ready by
that date, and agreed to discuss at the next meeting whether the Permanent
Representatives meeting should take place later in April.

AIR TRANSPORT TO VENICE
7.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Secretary who referred to the notice(3) on the
subject of air transport to Venice, and said that if sufficient people were
interested, it might be possible to charter an aircraft for the journey; he
requested early answers from Delegations;

(b) noted that if this charter was not possible, any offers of seats in
delegations’ official aircraft going to, or returning from, Venice, would be
much appreciated by members of less fortunate delegations.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
8.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) decided not to meet on 3rd April, as originally envisaged;
(b) agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 6th April at 3 p.m.

1110 Brussels.
