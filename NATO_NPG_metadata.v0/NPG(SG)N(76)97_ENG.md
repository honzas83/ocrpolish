> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 25th October, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on October 25, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The meeting began with a welcome to representatives from the NATO School at Oberammergau. The group discussed current work problems, including the views of the Chairman and the Turkish representative regarding the submission of matters to Ministers and the usefulness of the PO Work Programme and follow-up action documents. There was a discussion on the possible revision of the concept for the role of theatre nuclear strike forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE), with the group agreeing to continue the discussion after the results of the Phase III Study are circulated. The group also reviewed briefings by Mr. Cotter on NATO and Warsaw Pact nuclear force capabilities, with the Danish representative suggesting more consideration of French tactical nuclear capabilities. The Chairman announced his intention to circulate a proforma for members to list priority tasks for the coming months. Additionally, the group addressed arrangements for the upcoming NPG Ministerial Meeting in London, with the United Kingdom representative requesting delegation lists and security certificates. Finally, the the Belgian representative provided a statement on the classification of nuclear consultation messages, and the group decided to the date of its next meeting would be decided later.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 25th
October, 1976

WELCOME

1. The STAFF GROUP:
concurred with the warm welcome extended by the Chairman to Lt.Col. Williamson
and Major Mansart from the NATO School at Oberammergau.

CURRENT NPG WORK PROBLEMS

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the personal views of the Chairman concerning the examination
and discussion by Permanent Representatives of matters for submission to
Ministers;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the subsequent discussion and in
particular those of the Turkish representative, who considered that insufficient
time had been allowed for Permanent Representatives to have a full and open
discussion;

(c) discussed the usefulness of the PO 'Work Programme, bearing in mind that the
Agreed Minute was the basic tasking instrument resulting from an NPG Ministerial
Meeting;

(d) noted also the usefulness of a follow-up action document such as NPG/D(76)8.

POSSIBLE REVISION OF THE CONCEPT FOR THE ROLE OF THEATRE
NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES IN ACE

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that a revision of the
Strike Plan would become effective on 1st November, 1976; for this reason, he
had circulated a Staff Group Notice(1); this Notice invited Staff Group members
to discuss a possible revision of the concept(2) with a view to determining
procedural and timing arrangements;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and noted a
general feeling that this problem should await the circulation of the first
results of the Phase III Study;

(c) agreed to continue the discussion at the next meeting.

FOLLOW-ON COMMENTS ON THE BRIEFINGS GIVEN BY MR. COTTER

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(e) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who drew attention to the Staff
Group paper(3) on Soviet Nuclear Doctrine and invited comments on the briefings
by Mr. Cotter on NATO and Warsaw Pact Nuclear Force Capabilities;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the resulting discussion and in
particular of the remarks of the Danish representative who suggested that more
account should be taken of French Tactical Nuclear Capabilities.
