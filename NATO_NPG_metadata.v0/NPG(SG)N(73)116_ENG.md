> # Draft Report to the NDAC on NPG Activities
> 
> This document is a draft report summarizing the activities of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) during 1973 for the information of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC). It details the NPG's ministerial meetings in Ankara and The Hague, and the participation of various member states including Canada, Germany, Greece, Italy, The Netherlands, Norway, Turkey, the United Kingdom, and the United States. The report covers the progress of the Follow-on Use Work Programme, specifically the completion of Phase I and the transition into Phase II, which involves comparative analysis and the identification of policy questions. It also discusses the balance of strategic forces, noting the United States' commitment to avoid strategic inferiority and the mutually reinforcing nature of strategic nuclear, tactical nuclear, and conventional forces. Additionally, the report addresses illustrative studies on the initial demonstrative use of nuclear weapons and the ongoing efforts to improve political consultation procedures and facilities during times of tension or hostilities, including a review of the function of the Military Committee.

PURPOSE
This report summarises the general activities of the Nuclear Planning Group
(NPG) during 1973, for the information of the Nuclear Defence Affairs
Committee(NDAC).

BACKGROUND
2. The NPG convened twice at Ministerial level during 1973: in Ankara on 15th
and 16th May, and in The Hague on 6th and 7th November. The next NPG Ministerial
meeting is scheduled to take place in Norway in June, 1974.

3. Countries represented at the NPG Ministerial meetings during 1973 were
Canada, Germany, Greece(1), Italy, The Netherlands, Norway, Turkey(1), the
United Kingdom and United States. All countries mentioned above, as well as
Belgium and Denmark, participated in Nuclear Planning Group activities at
Permanent Representative and staff levels.

4. The actions taken by the NPG Ministers at their meetings in Ankara and The
Hague are reflected in the Agreed Minutes(2)(3), which have been circulated to
all NDAC countries.

ACTIVITIES
Work Programme on Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons
5. With the consideration, at the Ankara meeting, of the study concerning
follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons in the ACLANT area, Phase I of the
Follow-on Use Work Programme(preparation of basic data inputs in the form of
studies and related comments from NDAC countries and the NATO Military
Authorities) was completed. In the context of their discussion of the ACLANT
Study, the NPG Ministers continued their examination of the procedural aspects
of pre-conditioned authority and noted with satisfaction the view of the Supreme
Allied Commander, Atlantic (SACLANT) that the current procedures would allow a
Major NATO Commander to make a timely release as long as the conditions
specified for that release are properly stated and excessively stringent
constraints are not levied on the commanders involved. The NPG Ministers
concluded, without prejudice to possible actions that political authorities
might take on specific release requests, that retention of the pre-conditioned
authority option in the Major NATO Commanders' Nuclear Weapons Release
Procedures provides a useful element of flexibility. In the course of the
discussion, they also recognised that, in order to be able to use nuclear
weapons effectively in defensive engagements at sea where attacks on friendly
forces develop very quickly, some cases may arise

Balance of Strategic Forces
7. As was the case at earlier NPG Ministerial sessions, the briefings given by
the United States in Ankara and in The Hague created a greatly appreciated
opportunity for the NPG Ministers to exchange views on the East-West strategic
nuclear posture. In the light of their discussions in Ankara, they agreed that
the strategic nuclear forces provided by the Alliance would be adequate for the
foreseeable future. In The Hague, they noted various measures that the United
States had adopted to enhance the credibility and flexibility of its strategic
nuclear forces and welcomed the assurances by the United States Secretary of
Defense that the United States will not accept a position of strategic
inferiority. In the course of their free-ranging exchange of views, the NPG
Ministers stressed the fact that strategic nuclear, tactical nuclear and
conventional forces each make a unique and significant contribution to the
overall deterrent posture of the Alliance and that these contributions are
mutually reinforcing. They also emphasised the important bearing which the
strength and readiness of NATO conventional forces, particularly those located
in Europe, have on NATO's overall deterrent posture and noted that this subject
might deserve special attention at the next Ministerial session of the Defence
Planning Committee.

Illustrative Studies
8. At their meeting in Ankara, the NPG Ministers considered an illustrative
study dealing with initial demonstrative use of nuclear weapons, prepared by the
Supreme Allied Commander, Europe (SACEUR). In the light of their discussion,
they concluded that, insofar as demonstrative use is concerned, decreased
emphasis on military utility will not necessarily lead to a gain in political
and psychological impact on the enemy but could, on the contrary, be interpreted
by him as a sign of weakness, particularly if "no target" demonstrative use is
selected.

9. The consideration of illustrative studies, providing as it does an
opportunity for a useful dialogue between political authorities and the NATO
Military Authorities, is a valuable means for the Ministers to familiarise
themselves with the possible political and military implications of the
recommended use as well as of the consequences of non-use in the various
hypothetical situations described in the studies. It also permits them to
evaluate, to some extent, the suitability of the procedures and facilities that
would be available to the Alliance for political

Consultation Procedures and Facilities
10. With respect to procedures and facilities for political consultation in time
of tension or hostilities on the possible use of nuclear weapons, the NPG
Ministers, at their meeting in Ankara, examined a report by the Permanent
Representatives containing some preliminary observations on the nuclear
consultation aspects of Exercise WINTEX 73, on the basis of which they
commissioned further work aimed at improving existing procedures. Among other
things, they asked for a study on the implications of communicating NATO's
intentions to an enemy, to other countries and to the public and for a review of
the function of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation process.
After considering a second Permanent Representatives' Report at The Hague
meeting, the NPG Ministers requested the NPG Permanent Representatives to
prepare a compilation of nuclear consultation procedures as a contribution to
the crisis management work being carried out under the aegis of the Council
Operations and Exercise Committee. Meanwhile, the NPG will continue to work on a
number of other aspects of consultation procedures and facilities where problems
still remain.

Unresolved Problems related to Tactical Nuclear Defence
11. Continuing their examination of various matters raised under this heading by
former Secretary General Brosio in 1971(1), the NPG Ministers discussed reports
on two important aspects at their meeting in The Hague: NATO military
operational concepts and doctrine currently governing the employment of nuclear
weapons for tactical purposes, and the possible advantages and disadvantages
that might accrue to NATO as a result of the early combined tactical use of
conventional and nuclear weapons. As a result of their discussions, the NPG
Ministers agreed that the current concepts and doctrine are compatible with DPC
guidance on nuclear planning and that they offer a framework within which
Alliance political as well as military objectives can be effectively supported
in time of war, as regards both the defence and deterrence aspects. With respect
to early combined use, they noted that the NATO Military Authorities would
continue to plan for the exercise of this option, as one of the options open to
NATO within the current strategy, in the event that NATO should elect to employ
it in case of aggression. In this context, the NPG Ministers noted that the
current concepts and doctrine are sufficiently broad and flexible to accommodate
the early combined use option. They also agreed that increased NATO conventional
strength would provide NATO with additional conventional options which could in
turn reduce the need for early combined use by NATO. Finally, the NPG Ministers
suggested that a number of general political aspects of early

Implementation of Special ADM Guidelines
12. At their meeting in The Hague, the NPG Ministers reviewed the status of
implementation of the Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of
Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs) which had been approved by the DPC in
December 1970. In their discussions, they noted that good progress had been made
in implementing the Special ADM Guidelines in those fields where SACEUR has a
primary responsibility but that perhaps less progress has been made elsewhere.
They also noted in this context that the United States has under active
consideration several measures that would enhance the readiness and utility of
ADMs. The NPG Ministers concluded their review by urging countries concerned and
the NATO Military Authorities to take that action necessary for further
implementation of the Special ADM Guidelines.
