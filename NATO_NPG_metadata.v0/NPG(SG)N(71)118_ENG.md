> # Draft Agenda for the NDAC Ministerial Meeting on 5th December, 1971
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, H.B. Seim, regarding the draft agenda for the upcoming Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) Ministerial meeting on December 5, 1971. The note discusses the inclusion of Item II, a report on the follow-up action for AD 70 nuclear aspects, and presents two alternatives for reporting this information to the Defence and Foreign Ministers (DFC Ministers). The first alternative suggests submitting a report from the Inter-Allied Group (IFG) to the DFC Ministers via the NDAC, while the second suggests providing input for the DFC Permanent Representatives to use in their own report. The Chairman requests that Staff Group members be prepared to discuss these options and the draft agenda at a meeting on November 5, 1971, to reach an agreement on the final agenda and the reporting method. The attached draft agenda for the NDAC Ministerial meeting includes a report by the Nuclear Planning Group, follow-up action on the nuclear aspects of AD 70, and a other business and a communiqué to be addressed as part of the DPC Communiqué.

DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE IDAC MINISTERIAL MEETING
ON 5th DECEMBER, 1971
Note by the Chairman
Attached is an initial draft of an agenda for the next NDAC Ministerial meeting.
It will be noted that the draft includes, as Item II, a report by the NPG to the
DFC Ministers on AD 70 follow-up action. The Staff Group will recall that, when
a Working Paper(1) on this subject was discussed in mid-September, it was agreed
that the matter should not be placed on the agenda for the NPG Ministerial
meeting to be held on 26th and 27th October. However, no decision was reached on
how the follow-up action on the nuclear aspects of AD 70 might be reported to
the DFC Ministers in December. Two alternatives are open :

(a) submit a report from the IFG to the DFC Ministers via the NDAC, the report
to be approved by the NPG Permanent Representatives on the basis of national
instructions;

(b) provide an input for the DFC Permanent Representatives to use in their
report to the DFC Ministers on AD 70 follow-up action.

If alternative (a) is adopted, Item II would remain on the NDAC Ministerial
agenda.

2. Staff Group members are requested to be prepared to address the draft NDAC
agenda and the related question of handling the AD 70 follow-up action reporting
at our meeting scheduled for Monday, 5th November, with the objective of
reaching an agreement on the agenda to be circulated by the Chairman of the
NDAC, and on the way of dealing with the AD 70 reporting. It is also intended at
that time to confirm the substance of the AD 70 follow-up action report itself,
using the Working Paper(1) as a basis for discussion.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM.
