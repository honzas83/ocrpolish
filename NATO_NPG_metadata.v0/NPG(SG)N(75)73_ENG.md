> # Notes of a Meeting of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group)
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels on September 19, 1975. The meeting covered several key agenda items, including a review of security clearances and the review of papers on Soviet Air Forces and transportation improvements in Eastern Europe. The group discussed revised procedures for political consultation in the event a member government proposes the use of nuclear weapons, incorporating changes proposed by the United Kingdom. The group also addressed NATO's intentions regarding warning messages, with the Italian representative proposing a split between warning messages and procedures into two separate papers. The meeting included a review of the draft ministerial agenda, where the United States representative noted that the US Secretary of Defense would provide an updating briefing on the strategic balance. Additionally, the Belgian representative requested that introductory papers for all agenda items be available to Ministers, and the United Kingdom representative suggested that future NPC meetings be a separate agenda item. The group also noted the progress of the Follow-on US Work Programme - Phase III. Finally, the meeting concluded by scheduling its next meetings for September 25 and September 29, 1975.
