> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 5th April, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on April 5, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The group discussed several key items, including amendments to the Staff Group working paper on CNI procedures and a request from the Netherlands for a delay in publication. The meeting included a briefing by Colonel Steindorf of the International Military Staff (IMS) on Blue Dot Sierra pre-conditioned authority request messages. Additionally, the group addressed the inclusion of country request procedures in the compilation of nuclear consultation procedures, with the United Kingdom recommending that these be circulated before the summer recess for WINTEX 77. The group also discussed the procedural handling of a United States paper on improving the effectiveness of NATO's theatre nuclear force posture, noting that this would be be a primary focus of the next NPG Ministerial meeting. The meeting concluded with a note on the upcoming Spring NPG Ministerial Meeting on June 14-15, 1976, and an invitation from Denmark to the Military Implications Team (MIT) to meet in their country from April 26-28, 1976. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for April 12, 1976.

INCLUSION OF COUNTRY REQUEST PROCEDURES IN THE COMPILATION OF
NUCLEAR CONSULTATION PROCEDURES

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted the remarks of the Chairman who recalled that the Staff Group had
agreed at its last meeting to continue its discussion concerning the inclusion
of country request procedures in the compilation of nuclear consultation
procedures; he invited further comments;

(b) discussed the text of the Staff Group notice(1) and agreed to a number of
changes to the text particularly with regard to the use of the conditional
tense;

(c) agreed that the text should be included in the Staff Group’s Working Paper
(75)7 on Compilation; the Chairman said that the Staff Group would have the
opportunity of having another look at this combined paper which would also
contain the CNI Procedures which are still being discussed;

(d) noted the remarks of the United Kingdom representative who recommended that
procedures should be circulated in good time for WINTEX 77 and suggested
therefore that the Compilation of procedures should be circulated before the
summer recess; he further suggested that the complete document should be
submitted to Permanent Representatives and to Ministers if necessary.
