> # Draft Report by the Nuclear Planning Group
> 
> This draft report summarizes the general activities of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) during 1968, intended to provide background for the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) discussions on November 14, 1968. The report notes that the NPG met twice at the Ministerial level in The Hague and Bonn, and held 127 meetings of Permanent Representatives. It details that the NDAC Ministers endorsed recommendations regarding Atomic Energy for Peace (AEM) deployment, national participation in operational nuclear planning, and NPG membership arrangements. The report further discusses the development of political guidelines for the tactical use of nuclear weapons, the Southern Region Study, and new study areas concerning the response to initial tactical nuclear use by Warsaw Pact forces and the role of theatre nuclear strike forces. Additionally, it mentions a statement by the United States Representative regarding strategic nuclear forces. The NPG is instructed to continue examining the problem of consultation on the use of nuclear weapons based on issues raised by the Netherlands and the Secretary General. The document concludes by inviting the NDAC to take note of the report and invite the NPG to continue its studies.

NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
Draft Report by the Nuclear Planning Group
This report summarises the general activities of the Nuclear Planning Group
during 1968, in order to keep the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee up to date
and to provide a background to its discussions on 14th November, 1968.

MEETINGS
2. The NG has met twice at Ministerial level: in The Hague on 18th and 19th
April, and in Bonn on 10th and 11th October. The next Ministerial meeting is
scheduled for May 1969; on the invitation of the United Kingdom Government, it
will take place in London. In addition, there have been 127 meetings at the
level of Permanent Representatives.

MATTERS FOR DISCUSSION BY MINISTERS
3. The decisions taken by NG Ministers at their meetings in The Hague and in
Bonn have already been circulated to all members of the Committee(1) – for base
of reference copies of these minutes are attached at Annexes "A" and "B".

4. At their meeting on 10th May, 1968, the NDAC Ministers endorsed certain
recommendations(2) put forward by the NG Ministers at their meeting in The Hague
relating to AEM deployment, national participation in operational nuclear
planning and NG membership arrangements. Other matters raised in The Hague
minute were not addressed on this occasion.

concerned will give short background presentations, briefly summarising the main
points made in discussion at Bonn which led up to the decisions that were taken
