> # Draft Ministerial Agenda
> 
> This document is a working paper from the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated July 23, 1974, which outlines a draft agenda for a Ministerial meeting to be held in Rome on November 7th and 8th, 1974. The agenda includes a briefing on the balance of strategic forces led by the United States, statements by the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) and the Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic (SACLANT) on the status of selective employment planning options, and discussions on communicating NATO's intentions to use nuclear weapons. Additionally, the agenda covers an illustrative study on initial air defence use, a future work programme, and the finalization of an agreed minute and communique. The document was signed by H.B. Seim, the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), and references a previous notice on the preparation for the autumn 1974 NPG Ministerial meeting.

DRAFT MINISTERIAL AGENDA
Note by the Chairman
To provide a basis for Staff Group discussion at an early meeting, I have
attached hereto a draft of a possible agenda for the NPG Ministerial meeting to
be held in Rome on 7th and 8th November, 1974.

2. On the basis of information obtained after the circulation of my Notice on
the preparation for the autumn 1974 NPG Ministerial meeting(1), it is understood
that the Major NATO Commanders would be prepared to make a statement at that
meeting on the status of selective employment planning options. This item has
therefore been included on the attached draft agenda.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM.
NATO, 1110 Brussels.
