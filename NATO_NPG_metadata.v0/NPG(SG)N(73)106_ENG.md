> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels on Monday, 15th October, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on October 15, 1973, in Brussels to address several key administrative and strategic items. The meeting began with a welcome to Commander L. Giraud, who replaced Colonel P. Caligo as the Italian representative. The group discussed the final agenda for the NPG Ministers meeting in The Hague and the future work programme, noting that Turkey required more time to obtain instructions regarding the status report on the Follow-on Use work programme. The Staff Group also considered the prospective topics for 1974 NPC Ministerial Meetings and the necessity of a Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) meeting in December 1973. Additionally, the group reviewed a conceptual analysis of the nuclear aspects of deterrence provided by a United States study team, agreeing that it would serve as a useful basis for a report to Ministers. Discussions also touched upon the communication of NATO's intentions and the status of special weapons training courses at Oberammergau. Finally, the group agreed to meet again on October 22, 1973, to receive a briefing on Air Defences (ADs).

NEED FOR A MEETING OF THE NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE (NDAC) IN DECEMBER
1973

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that in the past, on
the occasion of the meetings in Ministerial session of the Defence Planning
Committee and of the Council held in December each year, it had been the
practice to hold a short meeting of the NDAC; the agenda usually consisted of
the consideration of a report on the activities of the NPG during the current
year, and, when necessary, the examination and forwarding to the DPC of
documents requiring final approval by that Committee; the Chairman invited the
Staff Group to say whether it felt that a meeting of the NDAC was required this
year, and if so, what matters should be placed on its agenda;

(b) noted in the discussion which followed, that there was general agreement
that a meeting of the NDAC in December would be appropriate, and that the
question should be re-examined after the meeting of NPG Ministers in November.

ASPECTS OF DETERRENCE
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the NPG
Ministers, at their meeting in Ankara, had invited the Permanent Representatives
to consider the value of a conceptual analysis of the nuclear aspects of
deterrence for Ministerial consideration at a future meeting; he referred to the
notice(1) circulated by the International Staff to which was attached a copy of
a letter to him from the United States representative who was also a member of
the Phase II Study Team, and a copy of the working paper prepared by the Team on
the subject of "Aspects of Deterrence"; the Chairman asked for views on this
paper and on the question whether the value of an analysis had been
demonstrated;
