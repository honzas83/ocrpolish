> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 18th February, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on 18 February 1974 at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key strategic and technical issues. The group discussed the arrangements for studying the political and military implications of technological developments concerning the tactical use of nuclear weapons, noting the potential participation of Greece. They also reviewed the report by the Military Committee on its function in the nuclear consultation process, stemming from Exercise WINTEX 73 and the Ankara remit. The meeting covered the progress of the Study Group on Warsaw Pact Strategy and Doctrine, with a final report expected to be distributed. The Turkish representative raised questions regarding United States nuclear position developments, specifically concerning re-targeting announcements by Mr. Schlesinger. the group agreed that these matters would be addressed during the strategic briefing at the NPG Ministerial meeting in June. Finally, the Chairman proposed a preliminary agenda for the NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting, including briefings on the strategic balance and research and development of nuclear weapons, as well as discussions on the Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE STUDY OF POLITICAL AND MILITARY IMPLICATIONS OF
TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENTS CONCERNING THE TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS

that his country would take part in the study; noted that if this was confirmed,
it would be appropriate to modify the remarks on participation made in paragraph
5 of the draft report;

(c) concurred, subject to some minor changes, with the content of the revised
draft, and agreed that it should

MILITARY COMMITTEE FUNCTION IN THE NUCLEAR CONSULTATION PROCESS

3.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the report(1)
by the Military Committee on its function in the nuclear consultation process;
he recalled that this matter stemmed from preliminary observations on Exercise
WINTEX 73, and the Ministerial remit at Ankara; he observed that the input from
the Military Committee cleared the way for preparation of the Permanent
Representatives' report requested by the Ministers, for consideration at the
Spring meeting;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing initial discussion and in
particular of the need to keep constantly in mind the guidance laid down in the
general guidelines for consultation in NATO on the use of nuclear weapons(2);

(c) invited the International Staff to prepare an initial draft of a report by
Permanent Representatives as a basis for further discussion of this matter at
the next meeting of the Staff Group.

STUDY OF WARSAW PACT STRATEGY AND DOCTRINE

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) noted with appreciation a statement by the Netherlands representative who
said that the final report of the Study Group on Warsaw Pact Strategy and
Doctrine was now practically terminated; copies would be distributed informally
to representatives and if no further comments had been received by 1st March the
Chairman of the Study Group would send his report officially to the Secretary
General;

(b) noted the remarks of the Chairman who said that as soon as the report became
official it would be circulated as an NPG Study; he presumed that the Staff
Group would be in favour of placing this report on the agenda for the Spring
Ministerial Meeting since the Netherlands would not be participating in the
Autumn Ministerial.

DEVELOPMENTS IN THE UNITED STATES NUCLEAR POSITION

5. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a question by the Turkish representative who recalled the
restricted session of Permanent Representatives on 4th February, 1974 at which
the United States Ambassador had given some background information relative to
Mr. Schlesinger's announcements regarding re-targeting; he asked whether any
further information might be available;

(b) engaged in a brief review of the state of affairs, noting that, at a
minimum, the subject would be addressed during the strategic briefing at the NPG
Ministerial meeting in June.
