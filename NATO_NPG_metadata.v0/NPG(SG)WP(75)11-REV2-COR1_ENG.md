> # Arrangements for Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme
> 
> This document serves as a corrigendum to the second revision of a draft Permanent representatives' report regarding the arrangements for Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme. Issued by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), R.E. Shearer, on 22nd October 1975, the document outlines specific textual amendments to paragraphs 5, 6, 7, 9, 10, 11, 14, 15, and 18. These changes reflect discussions held by the NPG Staff Group on the same date. Key updates include the refinement of guidance on nuclear weapon use, the inclusion of the Athens Guidelines, and the redefined objectives of nuclear weapon use, such as restoring deterrence at the lowest possible level of escalation and preserving the integrity of the North Atlantic Treaty area. Additionally, it specifies that the Working Group would be chaired by the United States and include a representative from the NATO Military Authorities.
> 
> ## Categories/Topics
> 
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Nuclear-Strategy-and-Strike-Planning — The document discusses the 'Follow-on Use Work Programme' and specifically mentions the need for a 'description of NATO's own nuclear capability and its relation to Alliance political and military objectives,' as well as the development of 'broad classes of targets, matrix of options' and 'coordination of US and NATO's employment options.'
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Nuclear-Deterrence — The document explicitly lists the objectives of nuclear weapon use as wanting to 'restore deterrence at the lowest possible level of escalation' and to 'induce the enemy to make the political decision to cease his attack and withdraw.'
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Flexible-Response — The document's focus on restoring deterrence at the 'lowest possible level of escalation' and the need to refine guidance on nuclear employment options indicates a strategy of tailored, escalating force rather than immediate total war.

1. Paragraph 5: insert new second sentence after first sentence, as follows:
"This consolidated statement would not supersede the present guidance, although,
when completed, might show where that guidance should be refined."

2. Paragraph 6: insert under new (a) the following:
"Athens Guidelines(1)
(1) C-M(62)66, 30th May, 1962."
Change the following sequence and footnotes accordingly. i.e. (a) now becomes
(b), etc.

3. Paragraph 7: Delete (h) through (k) and label following letters accordingly.

4. Paragraph 9: Second sentence, first word: Replace "this" by "the". Change
penultimate sentence to read:

"This should be followed by a description of NATO's own nuclear capability and
its relation to Alliance political and military objectives."

Delete last sentence entirely.

5. Paragraph 10: Delete (b) and replace by:
"b. Objectives of Nuclear Weapon Use
- restore deterrence at the lowest possible level of escalation
- induce the enemy to make the political decision to cease his attack and
withdraw

- preserve or restore the integrity and security of the North Atlantic Treaty
area."

"b. Third insert, add the following in brackets:
"(broad classes of targets, matrix of options)"
Then add a fourth insert, as follows:
"coordination of US and NATO's employment options."
