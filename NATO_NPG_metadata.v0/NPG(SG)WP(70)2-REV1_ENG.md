> # Work Programme for Further Studies on the Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons
> 
> This document is a draft report by the Permanent Representatives of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) regarding the work programme for further studies on the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. It refers to a meeting of the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group on 11th and 12th November 1969, where a work programme was accepted. The Permanent Representatives were tasked with making arrangements for the initial work, monitoring the work programme, and proposing further work. In response, five regional studies were immediately initiated: NORTHAG, Greek Thrace, Turkey, Norway, and the Mediterranean area. Additionally, three further studies were planned for later commencement: CENTAG,CENTAG, Denmark, and the ACLANT area. The document also notes that the Staff Group has begun exploring ancillary problems through informal discussions and briefings. The Permanent Representatives have received brief reports from study team leaders as of 1st May 1970, which are annexed for the information of the ministers. Finally, the document suggests that proposals for further work be made at a later date based on experience gained from the initial studies.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Work Programme for further studies on the follow-on
tactical use of nuclear weapons
Draft Report by the Permanent Representatives
1. At their meeting at airlie House on 11th and 12th November, 1969(1) the
Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group accepted a work programme for the Study
of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons(2).

2. In this context, the Ministers also gave three tasks to their Permanent
Representatives(3):

(a) make arrangements for the fulfilment of the initial work;
(b) monitor on a continuing basis the work programme and the specific studies
undertaken therein;

(c) propose, as appropriate, further work within the context of the work
programme.

3. In response to the first task, the Permanent Representatives have made
further arrangements for the fulfilment of the initial work(4), calling for the
immediate initiation of five regional studies:

(a) NORTHAG,
(b) Greek Thrace,
(c) Turkey,
(d) Norway,
(e) The Mediterranean area;
and the later commencement of three additional studies at times to be determined
by the participants:

(f) CEN TAG,
(g) Denmark,
(h) The ACLANT area.

The Permanent Representatives also took note of the particular interest
expressed by several delegations in a concurrent preliminary examination of some
important ancillary problems(1). The Staff Group has begun to explore these
subjects by means of informal discussions and briefings.

4. With respect to the second task, the Permanent Repre- statives have received
brief reports from the various study team leaders, outlining, as of 1st May,
1970, the essential features and the present status of the various studies which
have been initiated. These reports are annexed for the information of the
ministers. The wording in this paragraph may have to be revised somewhat by the
International Staff when the study team submissions are received.7

5. With respect to the third task, the Permanent Representatives consider that
proposals for further work can best be made at a later date in the light of
experience gained during the conduct of the initial work.

6. The ministers may wish to take note of this status report.
