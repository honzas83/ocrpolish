> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 22nd January, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on January 22, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several administrative and strategic matters. The group discussed the distribution of documents in the NDAC, NPG, and NPG(Staff Group) series, requesting members to review and reduce their copy requirements. Additionally, the group reviewed the status of the WINTEX 73 exercise, noting that NATO Headquarters' participation would end on March 13, and discussed the availability of the NATO-Wide Communications System for Phase II. The United Kingdom representative suggested that the group meet after the exercise to examine the possibility of preparing a paper for Ministers in Ankara. Regarding the ACLANT follow-on study, the group reviewed an initial draft of a Permanent Representatives' report and discussed pre-conditioned release. The group agreed to a refined draft of the report to be distributed for the next meeting and scheduled a presentation by the ACLANT Study Team to the NPG Permanent Representatives for February 22, 1973. Finally, the group agreed to hold its next meeting on January 29, 1973.

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 22nd
January, 1973

DISTRIBUTION OF DOCUMENTS ON NUCLEAR MATTERS

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to a Staff Group
Notice(1) which informed members of the number of copies of documents in the
NDAC, NPG and NPG(Staff Group) series distributed to addressees, and requested
them to review, and where possible to reduce, their requirements; he recalled
that replies to this notice should be sent by 29th January;

(b) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to his notice(2)
regarding the availability of spare copies of papers in the NPG(Study) series
and said that requests should be notified not later than 2nd February, after
which stocks would be destroyed.

EXERCISE WINTEX 73

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that NATO Headquarters
participation in WINTEX 73 would end as soon as the decision(s) of the nuclear
power(s) had been communicated to Governments; this would be sometime on 13th
March; Phase II (14th-16th March) would be limited to the testing of military
procedures by military headquarters(5);

(2) NPG(Staff Group)N(72)112, 15th December, 1972

THE ACLANT FOLLOW-ON STUDY
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to the initial
exchange of views on the ACLANT Study(1) which had taken place at the last
meeting, and introduced the International Staff partial initial draft(2) of a

draft;

particular attention was given to the question of pre-conditioned release;

refine, expand and modify the first draft in the light of the discussion, and
that he would add the remaining paragraphs (Page 13 - Section entitled "Warsaw
