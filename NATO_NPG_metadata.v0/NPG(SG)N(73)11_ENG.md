> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 5th February, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on February 5, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group first reviewed documentation, noting statements regarding the SHAPE review of the ACLANT Area Follow-on Study and comments from SACLANT. The group also addressed the distribution of spare copies of NPG Studies. Regarding the ACLANT Area Follow-on Use Study, the group discussed a draft report and the sensitive nature of pre-conditioned authority, noting that while the SHAPE Representative recommended deleting certain paragraphs, the group agreed that the procedural aspects of release procedures should be addressed in the Permanent Representatives' report. The group invited the International Staff to prepare a further revision of the draft report, incorporating comments from the Chairman of the Military Committee. The group planned to discuss the revised text on February 26, 1973, and subsequently circulate it as an NPG Working Paper for consideration by Capitals and Permanent Representatives in April. Additionally, the group discussed arrangements for the NPG Ministerial meeting in Ankara, including hotel accommodation, security certificates, and transportation for the NATO Classified Documents courier and International Staff Delegation, with a request for the International Staff to investigate charter flight options. The meeting concluded with a tentative agreement to hold the next meeting on February 12, 1973.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN ANKARA
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Turkish Representative who said that a note
from his authorities on arrangements for the Ankara meeting had been sent to the
concerned Embassies in Ankara, and that he would send copies of this note to the
members of the Staff Group; this note covered such matters as hôtel
accommodation, security certificates, outline programme, etc;

(b) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled his hope that air
transportation by military aircraft might be offered by a participating country
to the NATO Classified Documents courier, and even perhaps to the International
Staff Delegation;

(c) invited the International Staff to investigate the possibility of organising
a charter flight by Turkish Airways or another company.

NEXT MEETING
4. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed tentatively to hold its next meeting on Monday, 12th February, 1973,
subject to confirmation.
