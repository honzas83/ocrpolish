> # Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) Note of a Meeting Held on 3rd and 4th September, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on September 3rd and 4th, 1970, to address several key administrative and strategic items. The group approved a welcome for new members and Admiral Lebourgeois from SHAPE. Regarding the upcoming NPG Ministerial Meeting in Ottawa, the group noted that invitations were being sent to SACEUR and SACLANT, and the Canadian Representative detailed logistical requirements including security certificates, smallpox vaccinations, and hotel arrangements at the Chateau Laurier Hotel. The group also noted that SACEUR would provide a briefing to the Defence Planning Committee (DPC) and Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) on September 14th, 1970. Additionally, a briefing by CONSTRIMPORTANT would be arranged for the Staff Group on October 2nd, 1970. The group discussed and modified work and meeting schedules leading up to the Ottawa meeting. Finally, the group addressed Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs), reviewing the Special Political Guidelines and a United States redraft, and requested the International Staff to prepare a further revised version of the draft guidelines for the possible use of ADMs.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Not. : of a meeting held on 3rd and 4th September, 1970
WELCOME TO NEW MEMBERS AND TO A DISTINGUISHED VISITOR
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) approved the warm welcome extended by the Chairman to new members and
assistant members of the Staff Group, and to Admiral Lebourgeois who had come
from SHAPE to attend the meeting;

(b) invited the International Staff to circulate a list of members of the Staff
Group, updated in the light of recent changes.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN OTTAWA
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that letters of invitation
to SACEUR and SACLAN for the Ottawa meeting had been prepared and would shortly
be sent out by the Secretary General;

(b) took note of a statement by the Canadian Representative who described the
arrangements which his Authorities were making and emphasised the following
points:

i) countries should provide the Canadian Delegation with a list of persons and
their wives attending the meeting, by 1st October, 1970 at the latest;

ii) a certificate of Atomal security should be provided for all concerned;
iii) all persons should have proof of smallpox vaccination;

briefing and to answer questions.

ATOMIC DEMOLITION MUNITIONS
6. THE STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the statement by the Chairman concerning the Special Political
Guidelines prepared by the Staff Group and circulated as NPG(Staff
Group)WP(70)3(3rd revise), on 24th July, 1970;

(b) took note of a statement by the United States Representative concerning the
United States Redraft circulated under cover of NPG(Staff Group)N(70)63, of 31st
August, 1970;

(c) noted the views expressed concerning the Staff Group working paper, the
United States Redraft, and the desirability of treating the final version as a
separate document or as an annex to the Political Guidelines paper(1);

(d) invited the International Staff, in the light of the detailed discussion
which then took place, to prepare and circulate a further revised version of the
draft Special Political Guidelines for the possible use of ADMS.

NEXT MEETING
7. THE STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold _its next meeting_ on Tuesday 15th or Wednesday 16th September,
1970, at 10.15 a.m. and 3 p.m. (2).
