> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 6th April, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on April 6, 1970, to address several administrative and strategic items. The group noted that plans to charter an aircraft for transport to Venice were abandoned due to insufficient demand. They determined that a planned meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives around April 15 would be premature and agreed to discuss a later date at their next meeting. The group also deferred discussion of the revised draft agenda for the July Ministerial meeting in Venice. Regarding Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADM), the Staff Group reviewed a paper outlining major issues arising from S.C.EUR's analyses. While most members approved the method and form of the paper, the United States representative expressed disagreement with its composition and thrust, proposing changes to content and layout. The group agreed that the final goal of the work on ADM was to refine the tentative guidelines set out in the Italian Report. The International Staff was invited to revise Part I of the working paper and prepare a summary of S.C.EUR's analyses. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for April 9, 1970, in Brussels.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 6th April, 1970
AIR TRANSPORT TO VENICE
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the plan to charter an
aircraft for the transport of members of the Delegations and the International
Staff to and from Venice had had to be abandoned because there were not enough
requests for seats for the charter to be economical.

NEXT MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that the meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives planned to take
place on or about 15th April would almost certainly be premature;

(b) agreed to discuss the possibility of holding a meeting at a later date at
the next meeting of the Staff Group.

AGENDA FOR MINISTERIAL MEETING IN JULY
3. The STAFF GROUP:
not having had time to discuss the revised draft agenda(1) for the meeting of
NPG Ministers to be held in Venice, agreed to discuss it at the next meeting.

REPORT ON ATOMIC DEMOLITION MUNITIONS
The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the International
Staff’s paper(1) outlining major ADM issues arising from S.C.EUR’s analyses;

(b) noted that the method and form of the paper, and the sections recommended
therein, were strongly approved by most members of the Staff Group;

(c) took note however of a statement by the United States representative who was
not in full agreement with the composition and thrust of the paper and proposed
certain changes in contents, layout and emphasis;

(d) agreed that a final aim of present work on ADM was the refinement of the
tentative guidelines set out in the Italian Report(2);

(e) invited the International Staff to revise Part I of the working paper, and
to prepare a summary of S.C.EUR’s analyses, either in the form of an annex, or
as a revised Part II;

(f) agreed to continue its discussion of the working paper at the next meeting.
NEXT MEETING
5. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Thursday, 9th April, 1970, at 3 p.m.
N'TO, Brussels.
