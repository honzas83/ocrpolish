> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 21st April, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on April 21, 1975. The agenda items included the nuclear aspects of the Nunn Amendment, where the group took note of correspondence between the Secretary General and Secretary Schlesinger, as well as the U.S. Ambassador's letter regarding a non-classified version of the Nunn Report to Congress. Additionally, the group discussed the provision of nuclear weaponry for NIKE HERCULES and noted that the United States had initiated a priority study on the matter. The meeting also covered a report on Exercise VINTEX 75, with the Chairman providing a summary of post-exercise discussions at SHAPE and inviting comments on a new draft report. The International Staff was tasked with preparing a revised text of the draft report. Finally, the the group noted a statement from the United States representative regarding the upcoming Spring Meeting of NPG Ministers and agreed to hold its next meeting on April 25, 1975, to discuss the Phase II report.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 21st
April, 1975

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that all persons present were cleared to the security classification of
the matters to be discussed.

NUCLEAR ASPECTS OF THE NUNN AMENDMENT

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Secretary
General, as foresen in his PO/75/38 of 8th April, 1975, was sending a letter to
Secretary Schlesinger reiterating NATO's appreciation for the opportunity of
consultation with the U.S. authorities within the framework of the NPG;

(b) took note of further remarks by the Chairman who referred to the U.S.
Ambassador's letter to the Secretary General concerning the preparation of a
non-classified version of the Nunn Report to Congress;

(c) noted, with regard to the question of nuclear weaponry provided to NIKE
HERCULES, that the U.S. authorities had initiated a priority study of this
matter;

REPORT ON EXERCISE VINTEX 75
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he had attended the
post-exercise discussion of "VINTEX 75" at SHAPE on 14th April, and gave a
summary of his impressions of the meeting and the views expressed;

(b) noted the further remarks of the Chairman who introduced the new version of
the draft report on "VINTEX 75(1)" and invited general and detailed comments;

(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and invited the
International Staff, in the light of the suggestions made, to prepare and
circulate with all speed a further revised text of the draft report, for
discussion at a future meeting.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE SPRING MEETING OF NPG (MINISTERS)
4. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the United States representative who said that he
hoped to be able to circulate information concerning the Spring Meeting during
the next few days.
