> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels on 13th August, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on August 13, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with membership updates, welcoming the new Greek representative, Lieutenant Colonel Dimitroulopoulos, and noting the Turkish representative, Mr. S. Calislar, had been transferred. The group discussed upcoming documentation, including NATO Operational Concepts and Doctrine and Consultation Procedures and Facilities. A significant portion of the meeting focused on the early combined use of conventional and nuclear capabilities, where the group resumed discussions on the SHAPE report and invited the International Staff to prepare a draft report for the Ministers. Additionally, the group addressed the implementation of Special Political Guidelines for the possible use of Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs), noting a report from the Military Committee and agreeing that Permanent Representatives should submit a parallel report to Ministers. The meeting concluded by scheduling the next session for August 20, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels on 13th August,
1973

MEMBERSHIP
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) welcomed the new Greek Representative, Lt.Col. Dimitroulopoulos;
(b) noted a statement by the Chairman that the Turkish Representative, Mr. S.
Calislar, had been transferred to a new post at short notice and had therefore
been unable to bid farewell to his colleagues in person;

(c) expressed its appreciation for Mr. Calislar's contribution to the Staff
Group and wished him success in his new post.

DOCUMENTATION
2. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that the following documents would be circulated before the end of the
week:

NPG/WP(73)6: NATO Operational Concepts and Doctrine;

Consultation Procedures and Facilities.

IMPLEMENTATION OF SPECIAL ADM GUIDELINES
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman recalling that as NPG Ministers had
delegated to Permanent Representatives the task of monitoring the implementation
of the Special Political Guidelines for the possible use of ADMS, previous
implementation reports by the NATO Military Authorities had not been submitted
to Ministers; however at their previous meeting, Minister: had requested a
status report and Permanent Representatives had accordingly requested the
Military Committee to provide information on implementation; this report had now
been circulated under reference NPG/D(73)9, dated 2nd August, 1973;

(b) agreed that the best course of action would be for Permanent Representatives
to submit a report to Ministers in parallel to the Military Committee report;

(c) took note of the points made in the ensuing discussion with particular
regard to those parts of the Military Committee report which Permanent
Representatives might wish to clarify or amplify;

(d) invited the International Staff to prepare the first draft of a report to
the Ministers to be submitted to Permanent Representatives for approval at their
meeting of 4th October, 1973.
