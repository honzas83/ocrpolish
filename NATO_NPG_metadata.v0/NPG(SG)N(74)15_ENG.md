> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 4th February, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on February 4, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key strategic and administrative items. The group discussed a draft report on the arrangements for studying the political and military implications of technological developments, agreeing to await feedback from the United States Authorities before revising the draft. The meeting also noted that the NPG Permanent Representatives had agreed to hold the Spring Ministerial Meeting on June 11th and 12th in Bergen, subject to confirmation by the United States. The Staff Group was informed that the Military Committee report on the role of the Military Committee in nuclear consultation had been approved. Additionally, the group addressed the United States' strategic nuclear posture, specifically the targeting approach for its strategic nuclear forces, and requested members to report any Soviet reactions or intelligence on the targeting approach. The meeting concluded with a report from the United States Representative on a Phase II Study Team meeting held in London in January 1974, and the date for the next meeting was set for February 11, 1974.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE STUDY OF IMPLICATIONS OF TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENTS

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) had a preliminary exchange of views on the initial draft, NPG(Staff
Group)WP(74)2, of a possible Permanent Representatives' report setting forth the
arrangements for the study of the political and military implications of
technological developments;

(b) agreed to await the reactions of the United States Authorities to this draft
before requesting the International Secretariat to issue a revised draft;

(c) was informed by the Norwegian Representative of the possibility of his
Authorities reconsidering their decision not to participate in the study, and
noted that the Greek Representative awaited instructions on the question of
Greek participation;

(d) agreed at its next meeting to resume discussion of NPG(Staff Group)WP(74)2
and also to consider whether it should eventually be submitted to the NPG
Permanent Representatives for approval by the silence procedure or at an NPG
Permanent Representatives' meeting.

DEVELOPMENTS IN THE UNITED STATES' STRATEGIC NUCLEAR POSTURE
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that at its meeting that morning, NPG Permanent Representatives had
requested the Staff Group to look at possible requirements for follow-up action
in the light of the United States Permanent Representative's presentation, and
the subsequent exchange of views, on the targeting approach being adopted by the
United States for its strategic nuclear forces;

(b) had a preliminary exchange of views on possible follow-up actions which
might be proposed to Permanent Representatives and agreed to resume discussion
at its next meeting;

(c) requested Staff Group Members to report to the Group any Soviet official or
press reactions to the new United States targeting approach and requested the
Representatives of the NATO Military Authorities to report any information on
this subject which might become available from intelligence sources.
