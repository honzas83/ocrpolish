> # Corrigendum to NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)10: Early Combined Use
> 
> This document is a corrigendum to the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) working paper NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)10, dated 29th August 1973, concerning the early combined use of nuclear weapons. It specifies eight distinct changes to be made to the draft report, including modifications to footnotes and paragraphs on pages 3, 5, 6, 7, 8, and 10. The corrections refine the language regarding the tactical use of nuclear weapons and the balance of military advantages and disadvantages. It emphasizes that early combined use remains an option open to the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) within its current strategy and that a visible capability to resort to such use serves as a deterrent against potential aggressors. The document also references the Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE) Report and Provisional Political Guidelines, referencing the need to avoid unnecessary linkages between the recognition of major aggression and the decision to use nuclear weapons to maintain flexible response options.
>

> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Extended-Deterrence-and-Tactical-Nuclear-Sharing — The document focuses on the 'tactical use of nuclear weapons' and 'early combined use' within the NATO alliance, involving consultation between Ministers and the SHAPE report's analysis of military advantages and disadvantages of such tactical options.

EARLY COMBINED USE
Holders of the draft report(1) are requested to make the changes indicated
below.

1. Page 3, footnote (5), first line, delete "In its first four years" and
substitute "Right from its inception".

2. Page 5, paragraph 4, line 9, place full stop after "fighting" and delete the
remainder of the sentence.

3. Page 6, paragraph 5, delete the last two sentences and substitute the
following:

"Bearing in mind the statement in the Provisional Political Guidelines that a
variety of situations can be envisaged in which tactical use might have to be
initiated at an early stage(1), the Ministers may wish to discuss the SHAPE
observation and confirm that early combined use is one of the options open to
NATO within the current strategy."

4. Page 6, paragraph 6, delete and substitute the following:

"6. The SHAPE Report indicates that its analysis is based on existing political
guidance and reflects current ACE concepts and doctrine governing the tactical
use of nuclear weapons(1). The analysis deliberately excludes consideration of
political factors that would not be known before an actual situation arises but
that could nevertheless have a direct impact on striking a balance of military
advantages and disadvantages(2). The Report concludes, in essence, that a full
and accurate assessment of this balance could only be made in the light of the
particular situation that prevails at the time that early combined use might be
under consideration(3). It points out, however, that an improved awareness on
the part of political and military planners and decision-makers of the variable
elements discussed in the Report (as well as others that might be identified in
the future) would make an important contribution to the goal of deterrence and
toward a viable defence of NATO in the event of any future military conflict(4).
We agree with these general conclusions, and are of the opinion that they apply
as well to the question of assessing the political advantages and disadvantages
of early combined use. Moreover, because the SHAPE Report is based on existing
political guidance, many of the observations in its analysis are applicable also
from the political point of view.

(1) These concepts and doctrine are covered in a separate SHAPE Report
circulated under cover of NPG/D(73)5, 7th June, 1973.

(2) SHAPE Report, paragraph 7
(3) SHAPE Report, paragraph 14
(4) SHAPE Report, paragraph 47."

5. Page 7, paragraph 7, delete last sentence and substitute the following:
"We believe, however, that the SHAPE Report usefully develops in considerable
detail the ways in which these separate factors might operate to the military
advantage to NATO as well as the ways in which they might work to NATO's
disadvantage. Subject to clarifying comments later in this report, we concur in
general terms with the thrust of the SHAPE analysis. Without detracting from the
value of the SHAPE Report, we note in passing that a number of the
considerations mentioned in its analysis in connection with assessing relative
advantages and disadvantages would be applicable to other nuclear options open
to NATO as well as early combined use."

6. Page 8, paragraph 8, add at end of paragraph:
"In evaluating this approach, we note that the objective of the study of early
combined use was to look at a part of the spectrum of conflict that had not yet
been given full attention. Accordingly, we interpret the mention of identifying
an attack as a major aggression to be a way of defining the part of the spectrum
to be examined, and not as a suggestion to introduce a further condition in the
Provisional Political Guidelines(1) to require that major aggression must always
occur before NATO could resort to the use of nuclear weapons. If this further
condition were introduced, undesirable limitations would be imposed on the range
of flexible response options and an unnecessary linkage would be created between
recognition of major aggression and decisions to use nuclear weapons."
