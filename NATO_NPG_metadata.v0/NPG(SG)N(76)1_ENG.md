> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 5th January, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on January 5, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with the Chairman's welcome and an expression of appreciation for the 'Conference and Registry Services' for their support during 1975. The group discussed documentation for the upcoming NPG Ministerial Meeting, including a draft Agreed Minute and a possible Communiqué. There was a focus on the Communication of NATO's Intentions (CNI) and its inclusion in the the Exercise Specification for WINTEX 77. a further paper on the origins of CNI was deemed unnecessary at this time. The group also addressed the arrangements for the Hamburg meeting, including the request for members to inform the group of any particular points their Ministers intended to raise under Agenda Item III (Nuclear Force Posture Issues). The German representative provided updates on the arrangements and programme for the meeting in Hamburg and Bonn. The group expressed a desire for information regarding the date and location of the Spring Ministerial Meeting and suggested early consideration of its agenda. Additionally, the United States representative reported on the Working Group on Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme, and the Turkish representative stated that Turkey would not join the Working Group at this time. The Danish representative requested updates on the forthcoming meeting of the Tri-Service group and the Air Defence Study. Finally, the group agreed to hold its next meeting on January 12, 1976.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 5th
January, 1976

GOOD WISHES
1. The Chairman welcomed the members of the Staff Group to their first meeting
in 1976 and wished them a happy and successful New Year.

APPRCIAITON
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) warmly endorsed a statement by the Chairman who recalled the efficient and
conscientious support received during 1975 by the Nuclear Planning Group from
all sections of the 'Conference and Registry Services';

(b) invited the Chairman, on behalf of the Staff Group, to express in writing
his appreciation of the good services received to the Head of Conference and
Registry Services with a request that the thanks of the Staff Group be conveyed
to all concerned.

DOCUMENTATION
3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman who drew attention to the following
papers which had been or were about to be circulated:

(a) NPG/A(75)10(2nd revise), 31st December, 1975: Agenda for the meeting of NPG
Ministers on 21/22 January, 1976;

COMMUNICATION OF NATO'S INTENTIONS

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled
that CNI would be discussed by Ministers at Hamburg
(Item V(b)) when they examined the Permanent
Representatives' report on CNI – Messages of Warning;

for WINTEX 77 whose specific exercise objectives
included consideration of CNI(1);

(b) took note of the further remarks of the Chairman who
said that the Nuclear Planning Directorate,
International Staff, had the material for a further
paper on the origins of CNI and examples of messages,
but he did not consider, in the circumstances, that
any further paper from the International Staff was
necessary at this time unless the Staff Group
particularly wished for one.

HAMBURG MEETING – MINISTERIAL INTERVENTIONS

5. The STAFF GROUP:

took note of a request by the Chairman who expressed the
hope that members would try to find out and inform their
colleagues in advance of the meeting whether their
Ministers intended to raise any particular points under
Agenda Item III (Nuclear Force Posture Issues) or under
any other agenda item.

SPRING MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS

7. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) expressed the hope that information might soon be forthcoming regarding the
place, country and date of the NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting;

(b) concurred with the view of the United Kingdom representative who suggested
that early consideration shall be given to the content of the Spring agenda.

FOLLOW-ON USE WORK PROGRAMME – PHASE III

8. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the United States representative who said that
the first meeting of the Working Group on Phase III would take place in Hamburg
after the NPG Ministerial meeting; he had sent a letter on this matter to the
countries concerned(1).

(b) noted a statement by the Turkish representative who said that his
authorities had reconsidered their position and did not now intend, at any rate
for the present, to join the Working Group.

MEETING OF TRI-SERVICE GROUP ON AIR DEFENCE

9. The STAFF GROUP:

took note of a request by the Danish rrepresentative who referred to the
forthcoming meeting of the Tri-Service group and the Air Defence Study and asked
that the Chairman should inform the Staff Group in due course, of the matters
discussed.

(1) US Delegation letter dated 30th December, 1975, addressed to Canada,
