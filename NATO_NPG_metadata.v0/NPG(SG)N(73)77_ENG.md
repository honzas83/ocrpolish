> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 20th August, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on August 20, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to prepare for an upcoming meeting of NPG Ministers in The Hague. The meeting covered several key agenda items, including the review of documentation such as the working paper on NATO Operational Concepts and Doctrine for tactical nuclear weapons employment. The Staff Group discussed the processing of working papers, establishing a timeline for review and circulation to capitals and Permanent Representatives. They also addressed consultation procedures and facilities, agreeing to separate the basic working paper and its annex into two distinct NPG Working Papers. Furthermore, the group examined the implementation of Special ADM Guidelines, noting national constraints and the need for information from United States authorities regarding cooperation programs. The meeting also touched upon the early combined use of conventional and nuclear capabilities, with a first draft paper expected to be circulation. Additionally, the Staff Group endorsed the invitation of SACEUR and SACLAN to attend the Hague meeting, and agreed to discuss the draft ministerial agenda at their next meeting. Future meetings were scheduled for August 27 and September 4, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 20th
August, 1973

DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the recent
circulation of NPG/WP(73)6, 16th August, 1973, entitled "NATO Operational
Concepts and Doctrine Currently Governing Employment of Nuclear Weapons for
Tactical Purposes"; he recalled that this Working Paper was the first of the
series of working papers to be produced by the Staff Group in preparation for
the meeting of NPG Ministers in The Hague.

PROCESSING OF NPG WORKING PAPERS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman, who said that as soon as a working
paper had been agreed by the Staff Group it was circulated in both official
languages as an NPG Working Paper, for comments from capitals and subsequent
examination by the Permanent Representatives at their meeting to be held on 4th
October; a final review would be made by the Staff Group on 1st October of all
working papers, and, if necessary, corrigenda would be issued, in time for the
Ambassadors' meeting on 4th October; however, as much work as possible should be
done before 1st October and he urged members to obtain the views of their
capitals as early as possible in the hope that a good part of the work could be
done at the Staff Group's meeting on 24th September, or even earlier.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman, who introduced the most recent
revision(1) of the possible report by the Permanent Representatives on some
selected aspects of consultation procedures and facilities; he explained that
the paper was in two parts, the first being a basic working paper on which final
NPG action would be taken at Permanent Representatives' level (this part would
not be placed on the Ministerial Agenda), and an Annex which was a short summary
report for submission to Ministers at The Hague; he suggested that the aim of
the Staff Group should be to agree upon this or an amended version so that it
could be issued as an NPG Working Paper or Papers as soon as possible, for
examination in Capitals and subsequent submission to Permanent Representatives
on 4th October;

(b) took note of the views expressed and the suggestions made during the
subsequent discussion; noted that although there was general agreement with the
new form and content of the papers, a number of members had no instructions;

(c) agreed to certain changes to the text, and invited the International Staff
to prepare, in the light of the discussion, some explanatory sentences for
addition to paragraph 4 of the main paper;

(d) agreed to resume the discussion at the next meeting and expressed the hope
that guidance would soon be forthcoming from Capitals and Military Headquarters
so that the paper could be circulated without delay at NPG level;

(e) agreed that the basic Working Paper and the Annex should be circulated as
two separate NPG Working Papers.

IMPLEMENTATION OF SPECIAL ADM GUIDELINES
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks by the Chairman, who introduced the first draft(1)
of a possible report by the NPG Permanent Representatives on the status of
implementation of the Special ADM Guidelines; this draft had been prepared in
the light of a Staff Group discussion of the Military Committee report(2) on the
same subject; he invited general and detailed comments;

(b) noted the views expressed in the ensuing discussion, which covered the
following main points:

(i) the imposition of national constraints;
(ii) advance preparations, and the need to indicate progress, if any;
(iii) the need for information from the United States Authorities on their
intentions regarding ADM programmes of co-operation;

(iv) the need to give Ministers a clear idea of what progress had been achieved;
the tone of paragraph 23 (Conclusion) was examined;

(c) invited the International Staff to formulate some additional language for
paragraphs 13-15 (advance preparations), and to prepare, in the light of the
discussion, revised wording for other parts of the text in time for the next
meeting;

(d) agreed to continue the discussion at the next meeting and to aim at agreeing
to a version for circulation as an NPG Working Paper, for examination by
Capitals, and for submission to Permanent Representatives at their meeting on
4th October.

EARLY COMBINED USE OF CONVENTIONAL AND NUCLEAR CAPABILITIES

5. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman, who said that he hoped to circulate during
the week a first draft of a paper on Early Combined Use; this draft would be the
basis of a report by Permanent Representatives on the SHAPE paper(1) on this
matter; he foresaw that it would be necessary for the Staff Group to examine
this during two or three meetings before an NPG Working Paper could be
circulated.

INVITATIONS TO SACEUR AND SACLAN TO ATTEND THE HAGUE MEETING

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that in accordance with
customary practice, he would suggest to the Secretary General that invitations
to the meeting of NPG Ministers in the Hague should be sent to SACEUR and
SACLAN; the Chairman of the Military Committee had a standing invitation; he
requested the approval of the Staff Group;

(b) endorsed the proposed action.

MINISTERIAL AGENDA FOR THE HAGUE MEETING

7. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that the Chairman hoped to circulate a draft agenda for The Hague meeting,
and agreed to discuss it at the next meeting, if available.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP

8. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meetings as follows:
(a) Monday, 27th August, 1973, at 3.0 p.m.
(b) Tuesday, 4th September, 1973, at 3.0 p.m.
