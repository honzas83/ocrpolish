> [!abstract] 
> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 7th June, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on June 7, 1971, to address several items resulting from the Mittenwald Meeting. The group expressed thanks to the German representative for the meeting's arrangements. They discussed the need for further study on ensuring political control of nuclear engagement in follow-on use phases while maintaining military effectiveness, noting that the United States representative would check for updated views from his authorities. The group agreed that current and future follow-on studies should be presented as concise main documents with supporting details in annexes. They noted that Nuclear Defence Assistance Committee (NDAC) countries could provide written comments on studies regarding Norway, Greece, and NORTHAG. The group also noted that the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) and Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic (SACLANT) the review of regional studies, and discussed unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence. Additionally, they addressed procedural arrangements for follow-on work, the analysis of Illustrative Studies and Release Procedures, and the planning for the next NPG Ministerial Meeting in Belgium. Finally, the group agreed to distribute basic documentation to Portugal and scheduled their next meeting for June 14, 1971, and a visit to SHAPE on June 16, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 7th June, 1971
EXPRESSION OF THANKS FOR THE MITTENWALD MEETING
1. The STAFF GROUP :
unanimously approved a statement by the Chairman who expressed his sincere
appreciation and thanks to the German representative for the excellent
arrangements made for the Mittenwald Meeting, and requested the German
representative to convey these sentiments to his Delegation and to his
Authorities in Germany.

FOLLOW-UP ACTION TO THE MITTENWALD MEETING
2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to the Agreed Minute
(1) of the Mittenwald Meeting, and to a subsequent Staff Group Notice (2)
summarising the actions which appeared to be required as a result of the
decisions taken by ministers on 25th and 26th May;

(b) agreed to discuss the various points in the order set out in the Staff Group
Notice.

POLITICAL CONTROL (paragraph 8 of the Agreed Minute)
3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) discussed the need to give further study to the problem of ensuring
indispensable political control of the nuclear engagement in follow-on use
phases while also ensuring military effectiveness;

FORMAT OF FOLLOW-ON STUDIES (paragraph 4 of the Agreed Minute)
4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted the view of Ministers that the best format for the Follow-on Studies
was a concise main document presenting only the essential features and findings,
with all supporting details set forth in annexes;

(b) agreed that it did not seem that the Ministers intended that studies already
completed should be re-written but rather that current and future studies should
be prepared in the form desired;

(c) agreed that the teams currently working, or about to work, on studies should
be informed of the Ministers’ wishes.

WRITTEN COMMENTS BY NDAC COUNTRIES (paragraph 9 of the Agreed Minute)

5.  The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted Ministers had decided that it would be useful if the NDAC countries,
if they so wished, wêrë to circulate written comments on the Norway, Greece and
NORTHAG studies, for use in later phases of the follow-on use programme;

(b) agreed that such comments should be welcomed as contributions to the general
store of information and views on follow-on use, but, in order to avoid
overloading, should not be included in the agenda for the next NPG Ministerial
meeting.

SACEUR and SACLANT REVIEWS (paragraph 10 of the Agreed Minute)

6.  The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted that Ministers had invited SACEUR to review the three regional studies
already completed, and SACLANT to review the North Norway Study, preliminary
views being required by 15th September, 1971, and final reports by 1st February,
1972;

(b) noted that this decision gave the MNGs the possibility of examining in
greater depth and detail the studies in question, and of contributing to the
general store of information.

UNRESOLVED PROBLEMS (paragraph 11 of the Agreed Minute)

7.  The STAFF GROUP :
(a) recalled that Ministers had invited the Permanent Representatives to make
suggestions, to be considered by Ministers at their next meeting, for
investigating unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence, with
special reference to the views of the Secretary General as set out in his
PO/71/226 dated 10th May, 1971;

(b) agreed that the questions raised in PO/71/226 opened up a wide field for
action and study, and had obvious connections with the nuclear aspects of the
AD70s Study;

(c) agreed that the matter should be discussed by the Staff Group at its next
meeting.

PROCEDURAL ARRANGEMENTS FOR FOLLOW-ON WORK (paragraph 12 of
the Agreed Minute)

8.   The STAFF GROUP :
(a)   noted that Ministers had invited Permanent
Representatives to develop procedural
arrangements for handling the subsequent
stages of the follow-on use work programme;
(b)   noted that some members thought that it would
be premature at this stage to prepare a firm
programme and method of work, bearing in mind
that it was not yet clear whether further
complementary studies might be required;
(c)   agreed however that it would be valuable to
have a more precise idea of the direction the
work should take, and invited the International
Staff to prepare a paper setting out suggested
phases, timings and participation, for
discussion towards the end of the month.

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES (paragraph 15 of the Agreed Minute)

9.   The STAFF GROUP :
(a)   recalled that Permanent Representatives, in
collaboration with the NMA, were invited by
Ministers to conduct an analysis of the
procedural and substantive questions involved
in Illustrative Studies and Release Procedures,
and to arrange for a suitable presentation to
be given at the next ministerial Meeting;
(c)   agreed to discuss this matter at a future
meeting and invited the International Staff
to prepare a paper on it.

NEXT MINISTERIAL MEETING (paragraph 22 of the Agreed Minute)

10.  The STAFF GROUP :
(a)   noted that Ministers had accepted the
invitation by the Belgian Authorities
to hold the next NPG Ministerial Meeting
(b)   agreed that there should be a suitable time
gap between this meeting and that of the
NDAC/DPc;
(c)   agreed with a suggestion by the Belgian
representative that the meeting should take
place sometime towards the end of October or
the beginning of November, preferably in the
middle of a week.

DISTRIBUTION OF BASIC DOCUMENTATION TO PORTUGAL
11. The STAFF GROUP :
agreed that, as in the past, a distribution of the recent Ministerial Meeting’s
basic documentation should be made to Portugal which was the only NDAC country
not yet in possession of this documentation.

STAFF GROUP VISIT TO SHAPE
12. The STAFF GROUP :
took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Staff Group was
invited to visit SHAPE on Wednesday, 16th June; departure would probably be at 8
a.m. from the Press Entrance, NATO Headquarters; final details would be arranged
at the next meeting.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
13. The STAFF GROUP :
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 14th June, 1971, at 3 p.m.
