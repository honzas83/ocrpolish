> # Arrangements for Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme
> 
> This document outlines the scope and organization for preparing a consolidated statement of NATO's concept for the use of theatre nuclear weapons as part of the NATO triad. It details the progression of the Follow-on Use Work Programme, which was divided into three phases: Phase I focused on individual studies of follow-on use options, Phase II involved comparative analysis and synthesis of findings, and Phase III aims to formulate proposed political guidelines for follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. The consolidated statement is intended to provide broad policy guidance for NATO political and military authorities, drawing upon previous studies, ministerial guidance, and reports from the Military Committee and the Defence Planning Committee. The document specifies the basic policy guidance and past studies to be considered, including the Warsaw Pact's politico-military strategy and the US nuclear weapons employment policy. It further outlines the possible content of the final synthesis, emphasizing the necessity for a credible nuclear Alliance policy and a credible NATO triad to meet various threats. Key considerations for Phase III include political control of nuclear weapons, the objectives of nuclear weapon use, principles for employment, and forward defence options. The process is intended to to ensure a common understanding within the Alliance on the relationship between declaratory policy and NATO's readiness to respond to military aggression.

I. PURPOSE
This document discusses scope and organisation for preparing the consolidated
statement of NATO's concept for the use of theatre nuclear weapons as part of
the NATO triad.

II. BACKGROUND
2. In November, 1969 the Nuclear Planning Group approved the Provisional
Political Guidelines for the Initial Defensive Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons
by NATO(1) as policy guidance for national authorities and as a directive to
NATO military authorities. At the same meeting, ministers recognised the need
for a study of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons, to examine the phases
of tactical use subsequent to initial use by NATO and to explore NATO responses
to an initial tactical use of nuclear weapons by the Warsaw Pact. Rather than
attempt this task in a single comprehensive study, they agreed to proposals put
forward by the Permanent Representatives which defined(2) the objectives,
general assumptions and broad structure of an overall work programme to be
carried out in a series of interrelated steps.

3. The Follow-on Use Work Programme was divided into three major components(1).
(a) Phase I: a series of individual studies to examine in detail the effect of

(c) Phase III: formulation of proposed political guidelines for follow-on
tactical use of nuclear weapons, for endorsement by the nuclear Defence Affairs
Committee and approval and promulgation by the Defence Planning Committee.

**Possible Content**

9.  On the basis of the principle of deterrence as NATO's overall security
objective, the consolidated statement should postulate the necessity for a
credible nuclear Alliance policy and for a credible NATO triad to meet all kinds
of threats.  This statement should reflect a common view on such basic political
elements as solidarity under pressure or attack and through shared risk,
readiness to escalate, as well as perceptible linkages between the elements of
the triad.  Essential to the preparation of the report will also be a review of
present NATO and Warsaw Pact nuclear capabilities as well as Warsaw Pact
objectives and strategy including possible levels of Warsaw Pact military
aggression.  Furthermore, to support NATO's objective with regard to theatre
nuclear weapons, the common understanding in the Alliance on the relationship
between declaratory policy and NATO's readiness to respond adequately to any
kind of possible military aggression should be explicitly stated.

10.  Regarding the concept for nuclear weapon use within NATO strategy, the
following aspects appear to deserve consideration in the Phase III effort:

a.  political control of nuclear weapons (general characteristics),
– broad directives
– consistency in control and execution
– continuity.
