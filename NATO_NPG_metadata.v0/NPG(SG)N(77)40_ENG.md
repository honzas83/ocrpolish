> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Tuesday, 3rd-May-1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on May 3, 1977, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels, Belgium. The meeting covered several administrative and strategic items. First, the group discussed arrangements for an upcoming meeting in Ottawa, including the draft programme, security orders, and logistics for spouses. The German representative provided information regarding transatlantic air transport for delegations. The group also noted arrangements for a visit to military installations in Germany from May 8th to 10th, and a potential meeting with Dr. Edward Teller on May 27, 1977. Additionally, the group addressed the redesignation of the NIKE HERCULES Study and the Dual-Capable Aircraft Study. Observations on the WINTEX 77 exercise were reviewed, with the US representative proposing specific changes. Finally, the group discussed the New Technology Study, noting a lack of consensus on the layout and text, leading to the appointment of an appointment of an ad hoc group to rework the paper.

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared to the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR OTTAWA
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Canadian representative who gave some
details regarding administrative arrangements for the Ottawa meeting and in
particular said that:

(i) the draft programme and the security orders were expected to be ready for
circulation at the end of the week;

(ii) his Authorities would-be-grateful for information regarding the number of
wives taking part in the ladies' programme;

(b) took note of a statement by the German representative who said that his
Authorities would be glad to provide transatlantic air transport to those
delegations which had requested it, but regretted that seats could not be
provided for

NEW TECHNOLOGY STUDY
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted the remarks of the Chairman who expressed the hope that the draft
report by Permanent Representatives on the New Technology Study(1) could be
agreed at this meeting and subsequently circulated as an NPG Working Paper in
both languages;

(b) invited comments on the form and content of the International Staff's paper;
(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and of a lack of
consensus on the layout and text, noting also that one representative had
received no instructions;

(d) agreed to continue the discussion as soon as all members had instructions.
(e) continued the discussion in the afternoon of the same day, at which time an
ad hoc group was appointed to re-work the paper as soon as possible and submit
it to the Staff Group.
