> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 26th July, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on July 26, 1976. The meeting began with a welcome to new members Mr. J. Lunde from Norway and Col. S.C. Cooszen from the Netherlands. The group discussed the terms of reference for a study on various levels of nuclear capable NIKE HERCULES sites, noting remarks from the Chairman and views from the United States Authorities. The group also addressed the Phase III Study, noting that a meeting of the Phase III Study Group had been postponed by the United States Authorities until September, which may impact the first draft target date of October 1. The discussion included the update of a Compilation paper and the potential for its circulation as an NPG Working Paper for approval by the Permanent Representatives. The group also touched upon the request for NATO Military Authorities to clarify procedures for changes to nuclear consultation messages. The United Kingdom representative proposed holding the next NPG Ministerial Meeting in Great Britain, with the members invited to check the suitability of the week beginning November 22. Finally, the group agreed to continue meeting every Monday and potentially hold an additional meeting on July 30, 1976.

WELCOME TO NEW MEMBER

1. The STAFF'GROUP:
warmly concurred with the welcome extended by
the Chairman on behalf of the Staff Group to
Mr. J. Lunde, the new Norwegian member and to
Car. S.C. Cooszen, the new Netherlands member.

TERMS OF REFERENCE FOR THE NIKE HERCULES:STUDY

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who
recalled that Ministers at their meeting on
14th and 15th June, 1976 had invited the NATO
Military Authorities to prepare a study, in
accordance with the terms of reference to be
established by the Permanent Representatives,
of various levels of Nuclear Capable NIKE
HERCULES Sites;
(b) took note of two letters(1) concerning the
United States Authorities' views on the
approach to the study of nuclear capabilities
of NIKE HERCULES;

PHASE III STUDY

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Secretary
General, at the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives held on 14th July,
1976, had underlined the importance of the Phase III Study and that the United
States Representative had said that a meeting of the Phase III Study Group would
be held on 22nd and 23rd July, 1976; unfortunately, the United States
Authorities had been obliged to postpone this meeting until September;

(b) took note of the further remarks of the Chairman who said that he very much
regretted this postponement in view of the importance of the matter and of the
Permanent Representatives' hope for a meeting in July; furthermore this would
make it difficult to meet the first draft target date of 1st October.

Group's members were received by close of business on Friday 6th August, the

AUTUMN MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS

5. The STAFF GROUP:

took note of a statement by the United Kingdom representative who referred to
His Authorities' proposal to hold the next NPG Ministerial Meeting in Great

members to check with their Authorities whether the original dates proposed
could be acceptable.
