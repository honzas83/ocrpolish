> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on 26th, 28th and 29th March, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on March 26, 28, and 29, 1973, to address several key nuclear planning and operational issues. The group discussed and revised a preliminary report on the nuclear consultation aspects of Exercise WINTEX 73, agreeing to circulate it as an NPG Working Paper to Capitals for further consideration by Permanent Representatives on April 16. Additionally, the group reviewed the draft report on the Illustrative Study regarding demonstrative use and agreed to further changes to the text, noting that the Secretary General had requested SACEUR to prepare a presentation for Ministers in Ankara. If included in the Ankara agenda, a preliminary presentation of the Illustrative Study would be given to Permanent Representatives on May 3. The group also noted that the examination of a military paper on Operational Concepts and Doctrine by the Military Committee had been postponed to April 5, making it unlikely to appear on the Ankara agenda. Finally, the group agreed to the text of a draft Advance Press Release for the Ankara meeting and scheduled their next meetings for April 2, 9, and 26, 1973.

EXERCISE WINTEX 73
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the first draft(1)
of a possible report on the nuclear consultation aspects of WINTEX 73, prepared
by the International Staff in accordance with the request made by the Staff
Group at its meeting on 19th March; the Chairman emphasised that this report was
of a preliminary nature and was limited to a specific aspect of WINTEX 73; it
was without prejudice to the formal reports being prepared by the NATO Military
Authorities and by the Council Operations and Exercise Committee who had
responsibility for scheduling, conducting and reporting on such exercises; he
said that the aim of the Staff Group should be to reach agreement upon a text by
the end of the current week so that the report might be circulated as an NPG
Working Paper to Capitals for comments and subsequently for consideration by
Permanent Representatives at their meeting on 16th April; he invited general and
detailed comments;

(b) took note of the views expressed and the suggestions made in the ensuing
discussion and agreed to a number of changes to the text;

(c) agreed to continue its discussion on 28th March and invited the
International Staff to prepare a new draft, revised in the light of the views
