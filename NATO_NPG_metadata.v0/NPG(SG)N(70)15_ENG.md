> [!abstract] 
> # Work Table for the Period Leading Up to the IFC Administrative Meeting of 8th and 9th June
> 
> The Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group outlines a work timetable to prepare for the IFC ministerial meeting scheduled for June 6th and 9th, 1970, in Venice. The document notes that despite the three-month lead time, available time is limited by parallel activities, public holidays, and military exercises such as SILPLX. It identifies key tasks including the preparation and approval of the ministerial agenda, drafting status reports on follow-on studies, and the printing and distribution of reports. The Chairman emphasizes the intense competition for NATO resources like conference rooms and translation services. The proposed timetable suggests weekly Staff Group meetings on Mondays and two specific meetings for Permanent Representatives on April 15th and May 16th. These Permanent Representative meetings are intended to approve the agenda and finalize reports, including a report to Turkey on follow-on regional Study 23 and a German-US paper on the strike role. The document concludes with a detailed schedule of Staff Group and Permanent Representative meetings from March through June 1970.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
WORK TABLE FOR THE PERIOD LEADING UP TO THE IFC ADMINISTRATIVE MEETING OF 8TH
AND 9TH JUNE

Note by the Chairman

The meeting of IFC ministers to be held in Venice on 6th and 9th June, 1970, is
not less than three months ahead. It first sight this appears to give plenty of
time for preparing the meeting, but this is deceptive because the time really
available to the members of the IFC Staff Group and to the IFC Permanent
Representatives is limited by a number of factors :

(a) par-ll-el activities (some members of the NPC Staff Group are also members
of the Defence Review Committee which has a very heavy programme during the next
few weeks);

(b) public holidays (E-star, 1st day, i-oonson Day and 't'itmonday all fall in
this period);

(c) exercises (SILPLX takes place on 13th-15th May);
(d) Councill meeting in home (this takes place on 26th and 27th May, but will
effectively block the whole week 25th-29th day).

2. The effect of the above factors will be particularly noticeable in May which
has 5 weekends, 3 public holidays, a 3-day exercise and a ministerial meeting of
the Council. The number of days suitable for a Permanent Representatives'
meeting will, for example, be limited.

3. Among the tasks to be pursued during the next 12 weeks are the following :
(c) preparation and approval of an agenda (1) for the ministerial meeting (IFC
Permanent/Staff Group);

1970.

4. This considerable volume of work has to be done at a time when many other

for the time of Permanent Representatives, and for facilities such as conference
rooms, interpretation, translation, typing and reproduction.

5. For the above reasons it is suggested that the Staff Group may wish:
(a) to take note of the limited time available in which to prepare for the IFC
ministerial meeting, and in particular of the practical difficulties to be
foreseen in May;

(b) to prepare a work timetable aiming at completing the preparations for the
IFC ministerial meeting in early May, and fixing the dates on which it is hoped
to be able to hold meetings of the IFC Permanent Representatives.

(c) where appropriate, to urge countries or IFCs to submit reports
expeditiously.

6. Attached is a draft timetable for your consideration. I suggest that this
matter be discussed at our next meeting.

DRAFT TIMETABLE FOR NUCLEAR PLANNING ACTIVITIES DURING
THE PERIOD JANUARY - JULY, 1970
It is suggested that the timetable for nuclear planning
activities during the period leading up to the IFC ministerial meeting
on 6th and 9th June should be based upon :
(a) a weekly meeting of the Staff Group, to be held on
Mondays at 3 p.m., or if the Monday is a holiday, as
soon after as possible, if the pressure of work demands,
additional meetings would be arranged;
(b) two meetings of the Permanent Representatives;
(i) on or about 15th April, with the object of :
- approving the ministerial agenda
- considering the status report on follow-on studies
- considering a draft report on A.D.I.
(ii) on or about 16th May, with the object of :
- finalising the status report
- finalising the A.D. report
- notifying the report to Turkey on follow-on
regional Study 23, if available
- providing an opportunity for expression of
preliminary views on the German-US paper on
the strike rôle.
