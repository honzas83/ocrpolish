> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 8th January, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on 8th January 1973 at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with the Chairman's welcome and expressions of appreciation for the support provided by the Conference Service and the NATO Registry. The group discussed several documents, including decisions from the Nuclear Delegation and Advisory Committee (NDAC) and revised concept papers. The Staff Group decided to defer a projected visit to the United States due to lack of official air transportation, and requested Canada, Germany, and the United States to investigate further transport options. It was confirmed that the meeting of NPG Ministers would be held in Ankara, Turkey, on 15th and 16th May 1973. The group also addressed the ACE Orientation Courses for Senior Officers and the progress of Phase II of the Follow-on Studies Work Programme, which focuses on military effectiveness, timing, scale, and collateral damage. Additionally, the group established a tentative timeline for the ACLANT Area Follow-on Study, and planned for future meetings of the NPG Permanent Representatives and the Staff Group.

VISIT BY THE STAFF GROUP TO THE UNITED STATES
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to his note of
19th December(1) in which it was said that it appeared unlikely that official
air transportation across the Atlantic could be made available to the Staff
Group during the January–February timeframe; he asked whether the Staff Group
wished, in these circumstances, to plan for a visit during this period, using
commercial air transport, or to try to make arrangements for a visit later in
1973 hopefully in official air transport;

(b) agreed to defer the projected visit in the hope that free air transportation
might become available later in the year;

(c) invited the representatives of Canada, Germany and the United States to
investigate the possibility of official air transport becoming available during
the Summer.
