> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 27th August, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels on August 27, 1973. The primary focus of the meeting was the review of consultation procedures and facilities, specifically discussing a report by Permanent Representatives and amending the basic Working Paper and its Annex. The group noted a statement from SHAPE regarding the Special Operating Signal, stating that its adoption throughout NATO was not yet recommended as its value had not been proven. The International Staff was requested to revise the section on the handling and identification of nuclear consultation messages to better link paragraphs on precedence and identification. The group also resumed discussions on the implementation of special administrative guidelines based on a revised Working Paper, adopting certain changes to the text. The meeting concluded by scheduling future sessions for August 31 and September 4, 1973.

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 27th

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) resumed discussion of the latest version of the possible report by Permanent
Representatives on consultation procedures and facilities(1);

(b) accepted the amendment to paragraph 4 of the basic Working Paper and
paragraph 5 of the Annex circulated separately(2) and agree. to other changes to
the text;

(c) with respect to paragraph 9 of the basic Working Paper (Special Operating
Signal), noted the statement by SHAPE that as the value of the distinguishing
signal was not yet proven, SHAPE was not in a position to recommend its adoption
throughout NATO at the present time;

(d) invited the International Staff to revise the section on handling and
identification of nuclear consultation messages, linking together the paragraphs
dealing with the Special Operating Signal, precedence and the clear
identification of nuclear consultation traffic;

IMPLEMENTATION OF SPECIAL ADM GUIDELINES
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) resumed its discussion of a possible report by Permanent Representatives on
the implementation of special ADM guidelines, on the basis of a revised version

to this subject at its next meeting in the hope that the additional information
required to complete the report would then be available.
