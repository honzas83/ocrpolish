> # Arrangements for the Study of Implications of Technological Developments
> 
> This document outlines the establishment of an ad hoc Technology Implications Study Group to examine the political and military implications of technological developments on the technological developments on the tactical use of nuclear weapons. The initiative follows a suggestion from the Phase II Study Team and subsequent recommendations from the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Permanent Representatives and NPG Ministers. The study group is tasked with contributing to NPG knowledge of technological developments, identifying and assessing their political and military implications, and making recommendations for follow-up action. The term 'technological developments' is broadly construed to include devices and techniques within the state of the art, including nuclear weapon technology discussed in terms of weapons effects. The group is composed of participants from Belgium, Canada, Denmark, the Federal Republic of Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, Turkey, the United Kingdom, and the United States, under the leadership of the United States. The group is also authorized to invite representatives from SHAPE, the SACALNT staff, the International Military Staff, and the International Staff to participate. The reporting requirements for the group are left to its discretion, though it should provide periodic written status reports to the NPG at least one month before Ministerial meetings.

I. PURPOSE
This report outlines general arrangements for initiating the study of the
political and military implications of technological developments concerning the
tactical use of nuclear weapons.

II. BACKGROUND
2. A recent status report by the Phase II Study Team stated(1) that "....... it
has become apparent to the Study Team that current developments, especially in
the field of new guidance systems, could have a potentially important impact on
the whole complex of questions surrounding the tactical use of nuclear weapons.
Since a full examination of this subject would be beyond its terms of reference
and in any event should be considered in a wider forum, the Study Team suggests
that the NPG should consider the advisability of a separate study of the
political and military implications of these developments." With respect to this
suggestion, the NPG Permanent Representatives commented(2) that

of the Technology Implications Study Group includes contributing to NPG
knowledge of technological developments that may have an impact on the whole
complex of questions surrounding the tactical use of nuclear weapons,

The specific factors to be considered in assessing the political and military

4. For purposes of this study effort, the meaning of the term "technological

be discussed in terms of their resultant weapons effects rather than in terms of
warhead technology itself.

part in the technology study effort: Belgium, Canada, Denmark, the Federal
Republic of Germany, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, Portugal, Turkey, the
United Kingdom and the United States. The Group will be under the leadership of

Reporting
6. The form, content, frequency and timing of the
submission of the results of the study effort are left to the
discretion of the Technology Implications Study Group. The Group
should consider providing reports to the NPG on selected issues
during the course of its work. Similarly, the Group may make pro-
posals to the NPG at any time for follow-up action falling outside
the Group's immediate area of responsibility. The Group should
also make periodic written reports to the NPG on the status of its
work, covering the subject matter being addressed and information,
as practicable, on its future plans. These periodic status
reports should be submitted at least a month before Ministerial
meetings.
