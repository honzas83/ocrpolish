> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 10th January, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on 10th January 1972 to address several administrative and planning items. The group endorsed the Chairman's welcome to Mr. F. Spinelli, who replaced Mr. Da Rin as the Italian Representative. A farewell luncheon for Mr. Da Rin was scheduled for 19th January 1972. The group expressed gratitude to the NATO Conference Services for their support during 1971. The Chairman noted that the final version of a decision sheet from the Meeting of the NDAC in Ministerial Session on 8th December 1971 had been distributed. Preparations for the Spring 1972 NPG Ministerial Meeting in Denmark, scheduled for 18th and 19th May 1972, were discussed, including a tentative timetable and a draft Ministerial Agenda. The group noted that weekly meetings from 17th January to 6th March would focus on developing drafts of Permanent Representatives reports. Discussions were held regarding the Mediterranean Maritime Study and the Turkish Follow-On Study, with the Turkish Representative invited to propose a date in February for the presentation of the latter. A meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives was proposed for 13th April 1972 to approve working papers and the final agenda. The group agreed that the next meeting would focus on Phase II of the follow-on use programme and the consultation process, with the next meeting scheduled for 17th January 1972.

PREPARATIONS FOR THE SPRING 1972 NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced a notice(3) prepared
by the International Staff outlining briefly the various tasks which the Staff
Group would have to accomplish in preparing for the NPG Ministerial Meeting to
be held in Denmark on 18th and 19th May, 1972; he said that the notice also
contained:

- a list of meeting: of the NPG Permanent Representatives that are likely to be
required (page 5),

- a tentative timetable reflecting the activities of the Staff Group and the NPG
Permanent Representatives: (Annex A),

- a draft outline Ministerial Agenda (Annex B), showing, inter alia, the reports
by Permanent Representatives mentioned in the notice and in the timetable;

(b) noted that the weekly Staff Group meetings from 17th January to 6th March
would be mainly devoted to the development of drafts of Permanent
Representatives reports;

(c) took note of the remarks of the Chairman concerning the Mediterranean
Maritime Study, which was expected shortly; discussed whether consideration
might be given to presenting this Study at the NPG Autumn meeting at the same
time as the ACLANT Study;

(d) took note of the remarks of the Chairman concerning the possible
implications for specifically mentioning CINCHAN in requests for comments on
maritime follow-on studies; he asked if members felt that this would necessarily
involve inviting CINCHAN to NPG Ministerial Meetings;

(e) noted that it now appeared that the proposed presentation on 27th January of
the Turkish Follow-On Study(1) would not now be convenient to the Secretary
General and the Permanent Representatives; invited the Turkish Representative,
in collaboration with the International Staff, to propose a date in February;

(f) expressed the hope that it would be possible for the presentation to
Permanent Representatives of the Mediterranean Maritime Study to take place on
or about 24th February, 1972;

(g) noted the desirability, in view of the incidence of other NATO activities,
of preparations for other Ministerial Meetings in May, and of public holidays,
of holding a meeting of NPC Permanent Representatives on Thursday, 13th April,
1972, in order to approve in good time the various NPG Working Papers and the
final NPG Ministerial Agenda;

(h) discussed all the above matters, and took note of the views expressed;
agreed that work should proceed in accordance with the general outline set out
in the International Staff Notice, subject to any modifications that might
become necessary.

(j) agreed that the principal items for discussion at the next meeting of the
Staff Group should be Phase II of the follow-on use programme, and the
preparation of a factual description of the consultation process.

NEXT MEETING
6. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 17th January, 1972 at 3 p.m.
