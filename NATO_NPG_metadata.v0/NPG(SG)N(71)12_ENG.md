> # Staff Group Briefings During WINTEX 71
> 
> The Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) issued a notice regarding a series of briefings to be provided by the IMS Nuclear Policy Team during the WINTEX 71 exercise. The first briefing, scheduled for January 28th, will cover the initial exercise setting and review selective release procedures. The second briefing, scheduled for February 1st, will provide a brief resume of the development of the exercise situation towards a requirement for selective release. The third and most detailed briefing, scheduled for February 2nd, will will cover the tactical situation, examples of release requests, information for decision-makers, factors relevant to release decisions, and follow-on use. The Chairman emphasizes that these sessions will allow for informal Staff Group discussions on procedural and substantive questions, provided ATOMAL clearance is obtained. Furthermore, it is clarified that these briefings are not directly linked to the exercise play and are not intended to provide exercise input, but are instead part of the continuing Staff Group program of study of nuclear policy questions.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
STAFF GROUP BRIEFINGS DURING WINTEX 71
Note by the Chairman
As a result of discussions at recent Staff Group meetings, the IMS Nuclear
Policy Team have agreed to give the following briefings to the Staff Group while
WINTEX 71 is in progress:

(a) 28th January, 3 p.m., Presentation Room in the Situation Center, to cover
the initial exercise setting and to review selective release procedures;

(b) 1st February, during the regular weekly Staff Group meeting, to provide a
brief resume of the development of the exercise situation towards a requirement
for selective release;

(c) 2nd February, 3 p.m., in a conference room to be announced, a detailed
briefing to cover, among other things, the development of the tactical
situation, examples of release requests received, details of additional
information that would be provided to decision-makers, factors relevant to
release decisions, and the situation with respect to follow-on use.

2. During each of the above sessions, there will be an opportunity for informal
Staff Group discussions of both procedural and substantive questions. ATOMAL
clearance is required.
