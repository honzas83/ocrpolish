> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday 23rd February, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on February 23, 1970, to establish a background for regional studies and foster cross-fertilization of ideas. The group agreed to create a common data base using NATO information on nuclear matters and requested a list of appropriate documents from the International Military Staff. The United Kingdom representative offered to provide a British document on nuclear wargaming, and the group accepted a briefing on Selective Release procedures from Colonel W.G. Thomas. Additionally, the group discussed nuclear briefings for the Military Committee, agreeing to meet immediately after each briefing to discuss points with experts from the International Military Staff and SHAPE. The United Kingdom representative reported on the inability to arrange a visit to the NORTHAG region, suggesting a different approach via SHAPE and AFCELT. Finally, the group noted statements regarding Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs), including a new study from Germany and existing studies from Germany and Italy being examined by SHAPE. The meeting concluded with the agreement to meet again on March 9 and March 21, 1970.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held on Monday 23rd February, 1970

TOPICS FOR STAFF EXPLORATION

1. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the tentative list
of topics for Staff Group exploration(1), and suggested that the aim of the
Staff Group's studies should be to obtain a background which would:

(i) enhance the Staff Group's ability to review regional studies when they
become available,

(ii) achieve a cross-fertilisation of ideas between the Staff Group and the
Study Teams,

(iii) ensure, by an analytical approach, a clearer understanding of the
problems.

(b) took note of the views expressed in the subsequent discussion;

(c) agreed that a common data base should be drawn from available NATO
information on nuclear matters, and requested the representative of the
International Military Staff to make available to the Staff Group a list of
appropriate documents with details regarding their classification, titles,
reference numbers and present distribution;

(d) noted with appreciation a statement by the United Kingdom representative who
said that he would try to obtain copies of a British document on recent nuclear
wargaming;

NATO NUCLEAR BRIEFINGS FOR THE MILITARY COMMITTEE

2. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) noted with appreciation a statement by the
International Military Staff representative who
said that the Staff Group was invited to the
second and third nuclear briefings for the
Military Committee, the next of which would
take place on Monday, 23rd March;
(b) agreed that the Staff Group should meet
immediately after each briefings with a view
to discussing in depth the points which had
arisen, preferably with experts from the IMS
and SHAPE present.

STAFF GROUP VISIT TO GERMANY

3. The STAFF GROUP:

noted a statement by the United Kingdom
representative who recalled that he had
promised to try to arrange a visit by the
Staff Group to the NORTHAG region, and said
that he regretted to have announced that he
had been unable to make any progress on this
matter. He suggested that it might now be
necessary to make a different approach,
possibly through SHAPE and AFCELT.

were about to produce a study on ADMs which
would replace their original "Preliminary
