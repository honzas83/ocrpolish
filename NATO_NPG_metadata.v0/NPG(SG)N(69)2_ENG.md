> # NATO's Response to Initial Use of Nuclear Weapons by Warsaw Pact
> 
> On January 7, 1969, the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels, Belgium, to address the potential initial use of nuclear weapons weapons by the Warsaw Pact. The meeting included experts on Soviet thinking from various member states, including Belgium, Germany, West Germany, the Netherlands, Turkey, the United Kingdom, and the United States. The Staff Group listened to expert opinions and views on questions set out in the Chairman's paper titled 'Initial Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons by Warsaw Pact Forces'. Following the discussion, the Staff Group invited the Chairman to prepare a report based on these views, which would eventually be form the basis of a report to Ministers. Additionally, the group agreed to hold a next meeting on January 21, 1969, to discuss further questions to be put to the Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE) regarding the role of theatre nuclear strike forces.

3. During the meeting the Staff Group and the Experts addressed the questions
set out in the Annex to the Chairman's paper entitled "Initial Tactical Use of
Nuclear Weapons by Warsaw Pact Forces" (1).

4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) listened with interest to the information provided by the experts, and to
their views and opinions on the questions under consideration;

(b) took note of the various points raised in discussion;
(c) invited the Chairman, in the light of the views expressed, to prepare for
the Staff Group a Chairman's report which would form the basis, after further
discussion, of a report to Ministers.

NEXT MEETING
5. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) agreed that its next meeting would be for the purpose of discussing further
questions to be put to SHAPE on the subject of the rôle of theatre nuclear
strike forces;

(b) decided that this meeting should take place on Tuesday, 21st January, 1969.
