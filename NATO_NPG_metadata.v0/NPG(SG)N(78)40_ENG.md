> # Theatre Nuclear Force Improvements
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines a suggested approach and timing for dealing with the Short-Term Proposals of the NATO Military Authorities in response to the Frederikshavn Agreed Minute. The proposed work plan spans from early June to mid-September 1978, divided into three stages: the analysis and examination of short-term measures based on instructions from capitals (Parts IV, V, and VI of NPG/Study/52), the analysis of Parts II, III, and VII of NPG/Study/52 while preparing a draft report to NPG Ministers, and the final drafting of a report by the NPG to the Defence Planning Committee. The Chairman suggests that short-term measures be categorized into bilateral interests, measures requiring force planning community attention, and measures of particular interest to the NPG regarding policy aspects of NATO's nuclear affairs. Additionally, the process includes the provision of an initial draft Permanent Representatives' report on Study 52 to the Staff Group in early July, which would serve as a springboard for a 'guidance/advice and recommended actions' document for the Defence Planning Committee. The final step involves deciding in September whether to forward both reports to Ministers or handle the draft DPC report separately. The note is signed by R.E. Shearer.
>

>

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
THEATRE NUCLEAR FORCE IMPROVEMENTS

Note by the Chairman

The following procedure and timing are offered to the Staff Group as a suggested
approach for dealing with the Short-Term Proposals of the NATO Military
Authorities in response to paragraphs 6, 7 and 17 of the Frederikshavn Agreed
Minute.

2. During the period early June to mid-September three stages of work are
anticipated:

(a) June and July, analysis and examination of the short-term measures on the
basis of instructions from capitals (Parts IV, V and VI of NPG/Study/52);

(b) July and August, analysis and examination on the basis of instructions from
capitals of Parts II, III and VII of NPG/Study/52 (bearing in mind the High
Level Group Report, the Phase III effort and the Provisional Political
Guidelines) while simultaneously preparing a draft report to NPG Ministers;

(c) August and September, drafting of a report by the NPG to the Defence
Planning Committee.

3. Task (a) should start immediately. During June, and into July as necessary,
Staff Group members should be in a position to present national views on the
short-term measures. For ease of handling, it is suggested that these measures
be set out in three categories as follows:

(a) measures primarily of bilateral interest to the nuclear power concerned and
an Alliance member, e.g., FcCs;

(b) measures requiring the attention of the force planning community; these
measures would probably be the centre-piece of the NPG report to the DPC;

(c) measures of particular interest to the NPG in view of their impact on the
policy aspects of NATO's nuclear affairs.

3. At the beginning of July, possibly immediately after the meeting of NPG
Permanent Representatives(1), an initial draft Permanent Representatives' report
on Study 52 should be provided to the Staff Group. The major objectives of this
draft would be:

(a) to provide Permanent Representatives with a political examination and review
of NFG/Study/52, as broad as possible and as detailed as necessary, with
particular emphasis on the short-term measures. This report could, inter alia,
advise NPG Ministers that detailed consideration of Parts II, III and VII of the
NATO Military Authorities' report have been remitted to the Phase III study and
the High Level Group as the most appropriate fora.

(b) to provide the springboard for a 'guidance/advice and recommended actions'
document to be prepared for submission to the Defence Planning Committee if so
desired. This paper should be worked out by the Staff Group in August and early
September.

4. Early in September, and in preparation for the meeting of Permanent
Representatives prior to the NFG Ministerial meeting, it should be decided
whether both reports should be forwarded to Ministers (with the draft NFG report
to the DPC attached to the Permanent Representatives' report to NPG Ministers),
or whether the draft DPC report should be forwarded separately with a short
cover note and the Permanent Representatives' report serve as a reference
document.

5. If these procedures are generally acceptable we should delay no longer.
(Signed) R.E. SHEARER
