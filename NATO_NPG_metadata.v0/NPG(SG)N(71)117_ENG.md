> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 18th October, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on October 18, 1971, to coordinate administrative and hospitality arrangements for the 10th meeting of NPG Ministers in Brussels, including delegation compositions and parking requirements. The group discussed the action to be taken on the SHAPE Preliminary Review of the CENTAG area study, considering three possible courses of action: preparing a new report to Ministers, creating an addendum to the original report, or having the Chairman provide an oral introduction. The Staff Group generally opposed a new report and noted reservations from Belgium, Canada, and the United States regarding specific paragraphs of the draft report. It was agreed that the Chairman and these three countries would attempt to reach an agreement outside the committee. The group also confirmed that the agenda for the Ministerial Meeting did not require amendment to reflect the availability of the SHAPE Preliminary Review. Additionally, the Staff Group reviewed and approved the advance press release for the Ministerial Meeting, noting that no release would be made in London. The next meeting was scheduled for November 8, 1971.

Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 18th October, 1971
ADMINISTRATIVE ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) endorsed a statement by the Chairman who warmly welcomed Commandant Ferrier
of the Office of Interallied Relations, Belgian Ministry of Defence;

(b) took note of Commandant Ferrier's remarks regarding the administrative and
hospitality arrangements for the 10th meeting of NPG Ministers to be held at the
Palais d'Egmont in Brussels, on 26th and 27th October, 1971; noted Commandant
Ferrier's request that delegations, if they had not already done so, should send
him, through the Belgian Delegation, the composition of their delegation to this
meeting, in order of seniority, and their needs for car park stickers for
official and private cars;

(c) took note of a draft Conference Handbook handed out by Commandant Ferrier
and noted that a final version would be circulated shortly.

ACTION TO BE TAKEN ON THE SHAPE PRELIMINARY REVIEW OF THE CENTAG AREA STUDY
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the principal object
of the meeting was to continue the discussion held by NPG Permanent
Representatives at their meeting on 14th October, and to consider further the
question of drafting a Permanent Representatives' report to be forwarded to
Ministers in time for their meeting on 26th and 27th October;

agreement on the matter outside Committee;

(g) agreed that if an accord was reached the revised text should be sent out as
a NPG working paper for consideration by Permanent Representatives at their
meeting on 21st October; if no accord could be reached, invited the Chairman to
inform the Secretary General of the situation so that he might make an
appropriate introduction to the discussion to be held on 21st October(2).
