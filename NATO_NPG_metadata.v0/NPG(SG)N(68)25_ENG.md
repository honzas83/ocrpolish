> # Initial Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons by Warsaw Pact Forces
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, dated December 5, 1968, regarding the initial tactical use of nuclear weapons by Warsaw Pact forces. Following a meeting on December 2, 1968, the Staff Group invited the International Staff to prepare a paper outlining the possible contingencies in which the Soviet Union might initiate such use. The document outlines several key questions for discussion, including whether the Soviet Union follows an all-or-nothing approach or a graduated attitude similar to NATO's flexible response concept. It further explores potential scenarios such as unrestricted use of nuclear weapons, restricted use, or use following unsuccessful conventional operations. The examination is intended to be placed within the framework of Soviet nuclear doctrine and the structure of Warsaw Pact forces, while also considering potential differences in attitude among the Soviet Union and other Warsaw Pact countries. The Staff Group is scheduled to meet again on January 7, 1969, to discuss these matters, as requested by the NPG Permanent Representatives following a meeting in Bonn on October 10-11, 1968.

NPG(Staff Group)N(68)25
INITIAL TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
BY WARSAW PACT FORCES
1. At their meeting in Bonn on 10th and 11th October, 1968 the Ministers invited
the NPG Permanent Representatives to consider questions relating to NATO's
response in the event of initial tactical use of nuclear weapons by Warsaw Pact
forces (1).

2. On 24th October 1968 the Permanent Representatives decided that this study
could be approached by an examination of possible contingencies in which the
Soviet Union might initiate tactical use of nuclear weapons. This might be done
with the help of experts on Soviet thinking.

3. This examination should be placed in the framework of Soviet nuclear doctrine
in general, and should deal with what is known about a possible Soviet concept
on the tactical use of nuclear weapons. The following are the main, but not the
only, questions for discussion :

(a) Does the Soviet Union have an all-or-nothing approach towards nuclear
warfare, or are there signs of a more graduated attitude analogous to NATO's
flexible response concept ?

(b) What is known about Soviet thinking relative to the use of nuclear weapons
in the following contingencies :

(i) attack by the Warsaw Pact with unrestricted use of nuclear weapons;
(ii) attack by the Warsaw Pact with restricted use of nuclear weapons (what
might be the self-imposed constraints ?);

(iii) use of nuclear weapons by Warsaw Pact after unsuccessful conventional
operations (use at what intensity : demonstrative use; restricted use on
battlefield; unrestricted tactical use; general use) ?

4. These questions should be viewed in light of the present and anticipated
structure of Warsaw Pact forces.

5. Differences of attitude, if any, among the Soviet Union and other Warsaw Pact
countries should be indicated.

(1) NPG/D(68)10, paragraph 7
