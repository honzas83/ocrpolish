> # Follow-On Use Work Programme Status Report
> 
> This document is a status report from the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) regarding the Follow-On Use Work Programme as of October 1, 1973. The report, authored by Chairman H.B. Seim, is intended for the Permanent Representatives to provide background information for a Ministerial meeting on the Future Work Programme. The Work Programme is designed to explore the implications and interactions of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons to provide a basis for policy proposals for the Defence Planning Committee. The programme is divided into three phases: Phase I, which involved the preparation of basic data inputs, concluded in May 1973. Phase II, which involves the comparative analysis of basic data inputs and synthesis of findings, is currently being conducted by an ad hoc study team comprising representatives from the United States, the Federal Republic of Germany, and the United Kingdom. Phase III will focus on the formulation of proposed political guidelines for follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. The report notes that there are currently no proposals for additional work within the context of the programme.

STATUS OF WORK IN PROGRESS AS OF 1ST OCTOBER, 1973

I. PURPOSE
This report summarises the status of work in progress in the programme for the
study of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons(1) as of 1st October, 1973.

II. BACKGROUND
2. At their meeting in November, 1969, the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning
Group approved the Follow-on Use Work Programme and invited the Permanent
Representatives to make arrangements for the fulfilment of the initial work, to
monitor the work programme on a continuing basis and to propose further work as
appropriate(2). In discharging their monitoring function, the Permanent
Representatives have several brief progress reports in the past(3).

3. As one of its primary objectives, the Work Programme seeks to provide,
through its exploration of the implications and interactions of follow-on
tactical use of nuclear weapons, a basis for the Ministers to develop policy
proposals, as appropriate, for submission to the Defence Planning Committee. In
this context, the NPG has agreed that the Work Programme would consist of three

III. STATUS

4. Phase I of the Work Programme came to a conclusion in May 1973 with
Ministerial consideration of the last of the nine studies envisaged for that
part of the programme. Following a decision(2) by the Ministers in May 1972 to
initiate Phase II(3), an ad hoc study team, composed of participants from the
United States, the Federal Republic of Germany and the United Kingdom, has been
engaged for the past year in developing a Phase II report. A brief summary of
the status of this work as of 1st October, 1973, prepared by the study team, is
attached at Annex.

5. There are no proposals for additional work within the context of the Follow-
