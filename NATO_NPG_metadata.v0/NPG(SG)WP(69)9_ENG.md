> # Draft Report by the Nuclear Planning Group to the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee
> 
> This document is a draft report summarizing the general activities of the Nuclear Planning Group during 1969 to provide background for the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee's discussions on December 3, 1969. The report notes that the Nuclear Planning Group met twice at the Ministerial level in London and at Airlie House in Warrenton, Virginia, during 1969. It mentions that decisions from these meetings have been circulated to all committee members, including minutes from the London and Airlie House meetings. The report further specifies that recommendations concerning the consultation on the use of nuclear weapons and provisional guidelines for the possible initial tactical use of nuclear weapons by NATO have been forwarded to the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee. The Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee is invited to take note of the report and invite the Nuclear Planning Group to continue its studies and submit further recommendations as appropriate.

DRAFT
NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
Report by the Nuclear Planning Group

This report summarizes the general activities of the Nuclear Planning Group
during 1969, in order to keep the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee up to date
and to provide a background to its discussions on 3rd December, 1969.

2. The Nuclear Planning Group has met twice at Ministerial level : in London on
29th and 30th May, and at Airlie House in Warrenton, Virginia, on 11th and 12th
November, 1969. The next Ministerial meeting is scheduled for ………… 1970; on the
invitation of the ………… Government, it will take place in ………… .

3. The decisions taken by the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group at their
meetings in London and at Airlie House have already been circulated to all
members of the Committee (1) – for ease of reference copies of these minutes are
attached at Annexes "A" and "B".

4. The basic documentation discussed by the Ministers in London was distributed
to the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee delegations after the meeting (2).

5. At their meeting at Airlie House, the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group
agreed to forward certain recommendations concerning "consultation on the use of
nuclear weapons" and "provisional guidelines for the possible initial tactical
use of nuclear weapons by NATO" to the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee. These
have been forwarded under separate cover (3).

6. The other basic documentation of the Airlie House meeting has also been made
available to the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee delegations (4).

RECOMMENDATIONS

7. The Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee is invited to :
(a) take note of this report;
(b) invite the Nuclear Planning Group to continue its studies and submit further
recommendations as appropriate.
