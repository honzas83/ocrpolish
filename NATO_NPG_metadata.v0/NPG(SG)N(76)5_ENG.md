> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 12th January, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on January 12, 1976, at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting focused on the preparation of the Ministerial Agreed Minute, with the Chairman emphasizing the importance of Staff Group members being present during Ministerial sessions to facilitate the drafting process. The Chairman noted that some delegations had not allocated one of their five available seats to Staff Group members, complicating the subsequent preparation of the Agreed Minute under the Assistant Secretary General for Defence Plans and Policy. The group also discussed ministerial interventions, recalling that members were invited to inform colleagues of points to be raised by Ministers, particularly concerning Item III (Nuclear Force Posture Issues). Additionally, the Chairman suggested that Ministers should be briefed on the omnibus presentation concept for illustrative studies if it was deemed useful. Finally, the the group agreed to cancel the meeting on January 19 and scheduled a debriefing for representatives of Canada, Greece, the Netherlands, and Norway on January 26, followed by a general meeting of the Staff Group on the same afternoon.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels,
on Monday, 12th January, 1976

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared for
the security classification of the matters to be
discussed.

PREPARATION OF THE MINISTERIAL AGREED MINUTE

2. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a :statement by the Chairman who
recalled that it was customary that only five seats
per delegation were available during the
NPG Ministerial Sessions; he had observed that some
delegations did not give a seat to their Staff
Group member; as a result the subsequent
preparation of the Agreed Minute by Staff Group
members under the Chairmanship of the Assistant
Secretary General for Defence Plans and Policy was
made more difficult because some of the represent-
atives had not been personally present during the
Ministerial discussion or during the ensuing
Chairman's Summary by the Secretary General;

(b) noted the further remarks of the Chairman who
recommended that if Staff Group members were unable
to be present during the Ministerial discussion,
they should ensure that the person taking their

MINISTERIAL INTERVENTIONS

3. The STAFF-GROUP:
(a) recalled that, at its previous meeting; the Chairman had invited members to
inform their colleagues before the Ministerial meeting of any particular points
which might be raised by their Ministers, especially with regard to Item III
(Nuclear Force Posture Issues);

(b) noted the further remarks by the Chairman who suggested that, if the omnibus
presentation concept for illustrative studies was considered useful, Ministers
should be briefed on this item.

FUTURE MEETINGS OF THE STAFF GROUP

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed not to hold a meeting on Monday, 19th January;
(b) noted an invitation by the Chairman to representatives of countries not
taking part (Canada, Greece, Netherlands, Norway) to attend a debriefing in his
office on Monday morning, 26th January, at 10.30 a.m.;

(c) agreed to meet on Monday afternoon, 26th January, 1976 at 3 p.m.
