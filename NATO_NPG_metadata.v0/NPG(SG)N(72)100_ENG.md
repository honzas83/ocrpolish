> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 6th November, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on November 6, 1972, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key administrative and strategic items. The group took note of the status of the Agreed Minute from the London meeting of NPG Ministers, noting that additional statements from the Ministers of Denmark and the United States were still being finalized. The group also reviewed several papers, including NPG(Staff Group)N(72)98 and NPG(Staff Group)N(72)96, and considered observations from the Netherlands regarding the tactical use of nuclear weapons. Discussions were held regarding the upcoming meeting of the Ministers of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC), with a proposed agenda focusing on an activities report and the update of NATO nuclear planning directives. The group also discussed follow-up actions from the London ministerial meeting, including forces transition, structure, and deployment, and the distribution of basic NPG documentation to Portugal. Additionally, the group addressed the issue of participation at future NPG Ministerial Meetings, specifically regarding a statement by the Danish Minister, and noted that this would be examined at the Permanent Representative level. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next Staff Group meeting on November 13, 1972.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels,
on Monday, 6th November, 1972
DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said
that the Agreed Minute of the meeting of NPG
Ministers held in London on 26th and 27th October
had not yet been circulated because at the end of
that meeting the Ministers of Denmark and
the United States had made further statements ; the
precise wordings of the two additional paragraphs
for inclusion in the Agreed Minute were still under
discussion by the representatives of the seven
participants; as soon as agreement had been reached
the Minute would be circulated with all speed; its
reference would be NiG/D(72)18;
(b) noted the further remarks of the Chairman who drew
attention to the following papers which had been
issued:
(i) NPG(Staff Group)N(72)98, dated 2nd November, 1972:
This paper consists of the full text of the
Agreed Minute for the London meeting, less the
insertions mentioned in sub-paragraph (a) above;
(ii) NPG(Staff Group)N(72)96, dated 3rd November, 1972:
This paper, prepared by the International Staff,
discusses the action required as a result of the
decisions taken by the Ministers at the London
meeting;
(iii) NPG/Study/34, dated 31st October : Netherlands
observations on the follow-on tactical use of
nuclear weapons.

MEETING OF THE MINISTERS OF THE NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS
COMMITTEE (NDAC)
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that there appeared to be
general agreement that a meeting of the NDAC in Ministerial Session should take
place, as was customary, immediately previous to the meeting of the Defence
Planning Committee in Ministerial Session scheduled for 6th December; the
meeting of the NDAC would therefore probably start at 9.30 a.m. that day and
last for about one hour;

(b) noted the further remarks of the Chairman who said that no objection had
been raised to the draft Agenda(1) proposed for this meeting; this consisted of:

(i) the consideration of a report on the activities of the Nuclear Plarming
Group during the last 12 months;

(ii) examination of the proposal to update the NATO nuclear planning directives;
(iii) any other business;
(c) invited the Chairman to submit the `proposed agenda to the Secretary General
for approval;

(d) invited the International Staff to prepare and circulate to the Staff Group
with all speed a draft activities report for discussion at the next meeting and
for subsequent submission to Capitals for approval by the Silence Procedure.
