> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday, 6th April, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on April 6, 1977, to discuss administrative and strategic planning. The group agreed on the agendas for three upcoming meetings of the NPG Permanent Representatives scheduled for April 21, May 5, and May 23, 1977. The agenda for the April 21 meeting focuses on the balance of nuclear forces, including the role of cruise missiles, and the New Technology Study. The May 5 meeting is set to cover the NATO Theatre Nuclear Posture, specifically NIKE-HERCULES and Dual-Capable aircraft, and the WINTEX-77 nuclear report. The May 23 meeting will address the Future Work Programme and the Permanent Representatives' report on the New Technology Study. Additionally, the the Staff Group reviewed a revised working paper regarding Exercise WINTEX 77, agreeing to further revisions and subsequent communication to capitals for final approval. The meeting concluded by scheduling the next Staff Group meeting for April 18, 1977.

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared to the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

MEETINGS OF NPC PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that the following dates had been arranged with the Private Office
for meetings of the NPG Permanent Representatives:

(i) Thursday, 21st April, 1977 at 3.30 p.m.,
(ii) Thursday, 5th May, 1977 at 10.30 a.m.,
(iii) Monday, 23rd May, 1977 at 3.30 p.m.;
(b) agreed that the agenda for the 21st April meeting should contain:
(i) a discussion on the balance of nuclear forces (including the rôle and
potential of cruise missiles) PO/77/45,

(ii) an initial discussion of the New Technology Study (NPG/STUDY/47);
