> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 7th January, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on January 7, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key strategic and technical issues. The group took note of several documents regarding NDAC Ministers' decisions, the downgrading of classified documents, and improvements to the nuclear consultation process. They discussed the political and military implications of current technological developments, noting that most NPG countries, with the possible exception of Denmark, Greece, and Norway, were interested in participating in a study on the matter. The United States Representative expressed hope for instructions regarding potential United States study leadership. The group also addressed the political aspects of early combined use of nuclear weapons, with the Italian Representative mentioning a forthcoming paper from his authorities. Furthermore, the group discussed the nuclear aspects of deterrence, deciding that the paper prepared by the phase II study team should be brought to the notice of Permanent Representatives. The Staff Group also agreed to undertake a study on communicating NATO's intentions and accepted a proposal for a draft paper from the International Staff. Finally, the group noted progress on a future Military Committee paper regarding its function in the nuclear consultation process, and scheduled their next meeting for January 14, 1974.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 7th
January, 1974

DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman, who drew
attention to the following papers which had recently
been distributed:
(a) NDAC/D(73)2, 17th December, 1973: the decision
sheet of the meeting of NDAC Ministers held on
7th December, 1973;
(b) NDAC/N(73)2, 12th December, 1973: down-grading of
NDAC classified documents;
(c) PO/73/156, 14th December, 1973: measures to improve
the nuclear consultation process (revised text).
IMPLICATIONS OF TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENT
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman, who recalled
the ministerial requirement for a study on the political
and military implications of current developments in
the technological field; he asked whether further
instructions had been received by members regarding
participation in the study;

(b) noted that, with the possible exception of Denmark, and the probable
exception of Greece and Norway, that all NPG countries were interested in taking
part, to a greater or lesser degree, in the study;

(c) noted, with appreciation, the remarks of the United States Representative,

(d) noted a further statement by the Chairman, who described in general terms
the possible scope of the study, and stressed that it would be complex in scope

political and psychological considerations; he suggested that some countries
might wish to be involved only in certain parts of the work;

(e) concurred that the necessary first step was to decide upon participation and
leadership, and agreed to settle this matter, to the extent possible, at the
next meeting, and then to discuss the scope and organization of the study.

POLITICAL ASPECTS OF EARLY COMBINED USE
3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Italian Representative, who said that he hoped,
in due course, to receive a paper from his Authorities on the political aspects
of early combined use which he would communicate to members.
