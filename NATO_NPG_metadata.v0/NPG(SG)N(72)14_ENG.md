> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 7th February, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on February 7, 1972. The group discussed several documents, including the agreed dates for NPG Permanent Representatives meetings and the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Battlefield Use. A significant portion of the meeting was dedicated to the initiation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Programme, where the group reviewed a revised paper and discussed the timing, composition of the ad hoc team, and terms of reference. The group also noted preparations for an upcoming NPG ministerial meeting in Copenhagen, Denmark. Additionally, the United Kingdom representative raised questions regarding United States budgetary appropriations for next-generation submarine launch missiles and their impact on the US strategic programme. The United States representative agreed to inquire about providing more information to the Nuclear Planning Group. The meeting concluded by outlining the items for the next meeting on February 14, 1972, which will include continued discussion on Phase II, the SHAPE Illustrative Study of Battlefield Use, and the Turkish Study.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 7th
February, 1972

DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to a number of
recently issued papers:

(a) NPG(Staff Group)N(72)10 of 27th January
This notice sets out the agreed dates for the next four meetings of the NPG
Permanent Representatives;

(b) NPG/A(72)1, dated 28th January
This is the Agenda for the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives on Friday
11th February to hear the briefing on the Turkish Follow-on Use Study. The
Chairman hoped it would be possible for the meeting to start punctually at 3.30
p.m. for the convenience of all concerned and especially for that of the United
Kingdom Permanent Representative who has to travel that evening;

(c) NPG/Study/14, dated 1st February
This is the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Battle-field Use, soon to be addressed
by the Staff Group.

2. The STAFF' GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussion on the
initiation of Phase II which had taken place at the previous meeting of the
Staff Group held on 24th January, and introduced a revised paper(1) prepared by
the International Staff in the light of that discussion;

(b) reopened the discussion on the whole matter with particular reference to the
timing of Phase II, the composition of the proposed ad hoc team, the need for
inputs from all concerned and the terms of reference of the team, and took note
of the views expressed;

(c) noted a growing consensus on all points except the question as to the
relationship between the ad hoc team and the NPG Permanent Representatives;

(d) invited the International Staff, in the light of the views expressed, and of
agreements reached on certain parts of the text of the revised working paper, to
prepare a further revised paper for discussion at the next meeting.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN DENMARK

3. The STAFF GROUP:
noted with appreciation a statement by the Danish representative who said that
preparations for the NPG ministerial meeting in Copenhagen were in hand and
that, if Delegations required any information or advice regarding arrangements,
he or Major Bjoerke would be very glad to help.

NEXT GENERATION SUBMARINE LAUNCH MISSILES

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the United Kingdom Representative who referred
to the United States budgetary appropriations for the next generation of
submarine launch missiles and said that it would be most interesting to know
what effect this would have on the US strategic programme;
