> # Operational Concepts and Doctrine
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated August 1, 1973, regarding NATO operational concepts and doctrine for the employment of nuclear weapons for tactical purposes. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, notes that during discussions on the SHAPE Report, the member from Denmark raised a question about the need for self-defence against attack by enemy aircraft. To clarify this point, a proposed addition to paragraph 12 of the draft Permanent Representatives' report is included as an Annex. The proposed addition specifies that the nuclear capability of the NIKE/HERCULES system in an air defence role is envisaged under circumstances such as preventing a development in the air battle decidedly disadvantageous to NATO, protecting the NIKE/HERCULES system itself, or demonstrating NATO resolve without causing collateral damage to NATO territory. The Staff Group is scheduled to consider this proposed addition at its meeting on August 6, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
OPERATIONAL CONCEPTS AND DOCTRINE
Note by the Chairman
Staff Group members will recall that during their consideration of NATO
operational concepts and doctrine currently governing employment of nuclear
weapons for tactical purposes there has been considerable discussion of that
part(1) of the SHAPE Report dealing with conditions for selective employment,
and that a new paragraph was inserted in the most recent revision(2) of the
draft Permanent Representatives' report as a partial reflection of this
discussion. During the consideration of the draft report at the last meeting of
the Staff Group, the member from Denmark raised a question about the meaning of
one of the conditions for selective employment mentioned in the SHAPE Report,
namely, the need for self defence against attack by enemy aircraft(3). In the
light of the discussion at the meeting and subsequent informal conversations
with the SHAPE representative and me, the Staff Group member from Denmark
suggests that it would be useful to include some additional clarifying comment
on this point in the draft Permanent Representatives' report. Attached at Annex
is a proposed addition to paragraph 12 of the draft report which could meet this
requirement.

2. It is planned that the Staff Group consider the proposed addition at its
meeting on Monday, 6th August, 1973.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM.

NPG(Staff Group)N(73)68
PROPOSED ADDITION TO DRAFT PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES' REPORT
ON OPERATIONAL CONCEPTS AND DOCTRINE
NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)8(2nd Revision), 19th July, 1973.
Page 11, paragraph 12, add the following:
"One of the situations mentioned in this part of the SHAPE Report is the need
for self defence against attack by enemy aircraft(1). In amplifying the
conditions under which requests might be initiated for the release of nuclear
weapons for this purpose, SHAPE has indicated that use of the nuclear capability
of the NIKE/HERCULES system in the air defence rôle is envisaged, inter alia,
under circumstances such as those outlined in the Provisional Political
Guidelines for the Initial Defensive Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons by NATO,
that is, to prevent a development in the air battle decidedly disadvantageous to
NATO; in cases where the continued effectiveness of the NIKE/HERCULES system is
threatened; if the aggressor were initiating conventional attacks against the
nuclear strike potential of Allied Command Europe; or if NATO resolve to employ
nuclear weapons could be demonstrated without causing collateral damage to NATO
territory(2)."

(1) SHAPE Report, paragraph 27.e.1.
(2) DPC/D(69)58(Revised), paragraph 60 "
