> # Report on the Function of the Military Committee in the Nuclear Consultation Process
> 
> On May 22, 1974, the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting in Brussels at NATO Headquarters. The primary focus of the meeting was to review and a report on the function of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation process. The Staff Group took note of a revised version of paragraph 14 of the Annex to Working Paper NPG/WP(74)4, which had been prepared in cooperation with the International Military Staff. This revised text removed specific words such as 'possibly' and 'precise' at the request of the Netherlands Ambassador. The International Military Staff representative confirmed that the latest version of paragraph 14 was acceptable to the Chairman of the Military Committee. The group expressed a general feeling that the revised wording would be satisfactory to all capitals. The members agreed to obtain instructions from their respective governments and communicate them to the Chairman. Furthermore, they agreed to withhold the circulation of the final document, NPG/D(74)5, until a consensus had been reached. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next meeting on Monday, May 27, 1974.

REPORT ON THE FUNCTION OF THE MILITARY COMMITTEE IN THE NUCLEAR CONSULTATION
PROCESS

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussion held at
the last meeting on paragraph 14 of the Annex to Working Paper(1) on the
Military Committee Function; some delegations had difficulty with the revised
wording(2), and therefore a new version had been prepared in cooperation with
the International Military Staff; this latest text consisted of the original
paragraph 14 less the words "and p.c.sibly" and "precise"(3), and with a new
text for the last sentence; (this new sentence was handed out to members);

(b) took note of a statement by the representative of the International Military
Staff who confirmed that this latest version of paragraph 14 was acceptable to
the Chairman of the Military Committee;

(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and of a general
feeling that this version would prove satisfactory to all capitals;
