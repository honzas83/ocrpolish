> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 22nd November, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on November 22, 1971, to address several key agenda items. The group endorsed the welcome of Major J. Bjoerke from the Danish Delegation. They discussed the approval of working papers NPG/WP(71)13 and NPG/WP(71)14, which were to be issued as NDAC and DPC documents for an upcoming Ministerial Meeting on December 8. Regarding United States naval forces, the group reviewed the modernization of U.S. Fleet ballistic missile submarines, specifically the replacement of POLARIS missiles with the POSEIDON weapon system, and agreed upon revised wordings for a draft report. The group also planned a visit to the Situation Centre on November 29 and postponed further discussion on Illustrative Studies. Additionally, the United States offered to conduct a preliminary assessment of the methodology and resource requirements for a study on the air battle in the Central Region, including the need for dynamic war-gaming and new computer models. The group agreed to continue discussions on Phase II of the studies on follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. Finally, the group proposed May 16 and 17, 1972, for the next meeting of NPG Ministers in Denmark, and scheduled their next Staff Group meetings for November 29 and December 6, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 22nd November, 1971
WELCOME
1. The STAFF GROUP:
warmly endorsed the welcome extended by the Chairman to Major J. Bjoerke, of the
Danish Delegation, who was attending the Staff Group meeting for the first time.

APPROVAL OF WORKING PAPERS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that if no comments or objections were received by 22nd November to
NPG/WP(71)13 (NPG Report to NDAC) and to NPG/WP(71)14 (Nuclear aspects of AD 70
Follow-up Action), these papers would be considered as approved and would be
issued as NDAC and/or DPC documents for the Ministerial Meeting to be held on
8th December;

(b) noted that there were no comments or objections to the Working Papers; noted
however that the United Kingdom Representative was still awaiting confirmation
from his Authorities;

(c) invited the International Staff, as soon as confirmation had been received
from the United Kingdom Authorities, to issue these papers in the following
manner:

(1) NPG/WP(71)13 (NPG Report to NDAC) to become NDAC/D(71)1,
(11) NPG/WP(71)14 (Nuclear Aspects of AD 70 Follow-up Action) to become
NDAC/D(71)2 and DPC/D(71)31.

UNITED STATES NAVAL FORCES IMPROVEMENTS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussion which
had taken place at the last meeting of the Staff Group on the question of the
modernisation of the U.S. Fleet ballistic missile submarines by replacing
POLARIS missiles with the POSEIDON weapon system; he recalled briefly the
wording of the draft report by the Defence Planning Committee in Permanent
Session proposed in PO/71/653, which had been issued subsequent to the last
meeting of the Staff Group;

(b) took note of the views of the United States Representative, who suggested
that the action proposed in the draft report was too extensive, and said that it
should be limited to a review of the most relevant paper - the Concept for the
Rôle of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE(1); he proposed two changes to the
text of the draft report;

(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and agreed upon
revised wordings of the changes proposed by the United States; invited the
United States Representative to seek the agreement of his Authorities upon these
changes;

(d) invited the Chairman, in the event that the United States Authorities were
able to accept the revised wording, to report to the Secretary General who might
wish to issue an amendment to the draft report in time for discussion at the
Meeting of Permanent Representatives to be held on Friday, 26th November.

FOLLOW-UP ACTION TO THE BRUSSELS MINISTERIAL MEETING(1)

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES

4.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that the Council Operations Directorate, in liaison with the
International Military Staff, was organising a visit for the Staff Group to the
Situation Centre on 29th November, and that this visit would include a briefing
on crisis management;

(b) agreed to postpone further discussion on Illustrative Studies until after
the visit.

STUDIES ON FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS

5.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled the wish of the NPG Ministers, expressed at their meeting in
Brussels(2), that arrangements should be made for a specific study of the air
battle in the Central Region;

(b) noted with appreciation a statement by the United States Representative in
which he said that a preliminary review by his Authorities had shown that the
Air Study would be an undertaking of considerable magnitude, complexity and
difficulty; dynamic war-gaming would be necessary; new models and computers
would have to be provided and fitted to each other; his Authorities offered the
services of the United States in conducting the preliminary assessment of the
methodology and resource requirements and would report to Permanent
Representatives as soon as this was completed; he handed out a text of his
statement;

(c) noted the further remarks of the United States Representative, who made the
following points:

(1) it would be most useful if interested capitals would contribute their views
during the period of preliminary assessment, tasking working-level

FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS – PHASE II
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman on the subject of initiating Phase
II of the studies on follow-on tactical use; it should be started in time to
provide material for Ministerial meetings as soon as Phase I was completed;
problems included participation, assignment of responsibilities, Terms of
Reference, and timing;

(b) took note of the views expressed and the suggestions made during the
discussion which followed, and agreed to discuss the matter further at a future
meeting.

MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS IN SPRING 1972

7. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) noted with appreciation a statement by the Danish Representative, who said
that his Authorities suggested that the best dates for the meeting of NPG
Ministers in Denmark were in the weeks beginning 8th or 15th May, and that in
particular Tuesday and Wednesday, 16th and 17th May, seemed most suitable;

(b) agreed, after having taken note of other NATO activities scheduled for April
and May, that 16th and 17th May appeared most suitable, and invited
Representatives to obtain the views of their capitals on these proposed dates.

NEXT MEETINGS OF THE STAFF GROUP

8. The STAFF GROUP:

agreed to hold its next meetings as follows:

(a) Monday, 29th November, 1971, at 3 p.m. (Situation Centre visit)

(b) Monday, 6th December, 1971, at 3 p.m.
