> # Note by the Chairman Regarding WINTEX 73
> 
> This document is a note from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group dated 21st February 1973, regarding the WINTEX 73 exercise. The Chairman suggests that the Staff Group draft a preliminary report containing observations on the exercise to contribute to the upcoming NPG Ministers' meeting in Ankara. Due to the expected delay in formal reports from Major NATO Commanders and the Military Committee, the Chairman advises Staff Group members to observe as much of the exercise activity as possible and seek assistance from delegation colleagues and the International Staff. The note outlines five general topics of interest to the NPG, including the timely provision of information on the impending use of nuclear weapons, the classification of selective release messages, and the continuity of nuclear consultation. It also references previous reports on consultation procedures and technical facilities for political consultation in time of war. Additionally, it mentions the specific arrangements for WINTEX 73, including the participation of the Defence Planning Committee (DPC) for a brief period and the requirements for special passes for entry into the Situation Center and Presentation Room.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
WINTEX 73
Note by the Chairman
During recent Staff Group discussions, it was concluded that the NPG Ministers
were likely to make reference in one way or another to WINTEX 73 during their
forthcoming meeting in Ankara, since all or most of them would have been
personally involved in the active exercise play just a relatively short time
earlier. The suggestion was therefore made that the Staff Group might usefully
consider drafting an NPG Permanent Representatives' report containing some
preliminary observations on the exercise, as a contribution to any Ministerial
discussion that takes place at Ankara. The Staff Group agreed to explore this
possibility immediately after the conclusion of WINTEX 73.

2. It is very likely that the Staff Group will have to depend mainly on its own
resources to gather material that might go into a possible Permanent
Representatives' report. If past experience may be taken as a guide, the first
written reports from the Major NATO Commanders may not be available until two
months or so after completion of the exercise. It could be a year or more before
the formal report of the Military Committee is approved. Consequently, it is
suggested that Staff Group members should plan to observe as much of the
exercise activity as possible, whether or not they have an active role in the
participation of their respective delegations. Members may also wish to seek
advice and assistance from their delegation colleagues who may be in a position
to contribute. Finally, it is expected that the Staff Group will be able to draw
informally on WINTEX 73 participants from the International Staff and
International Military Staff for information and preliminary comments.

3. In preparing to observe the exercise, it may be useful to recall five general
topics of interest to the NPG that were identified in the last NPG Permanent
Representatives' report on consultation procedures and facilities(1).

(a) provision of timely information on possible impending use of nuclear
weapons;

(b) classification and precedence of selective release messages addressed 'to
political authorities';

(c) arrangements for continuity of nuclear consultation;
(d) maintenance of effective communications between governments and their
Permanent Representatives to facilitate nuclear consultation in the Council/DPC;

(e) procedures for developing and disseminating assessments and evaluations
pertinent to nuclear consultation.

It may also be helpful to review the NPG Permanent Representatives' report
containing a description of the general procedures that are expected to be used
within the Alliance for political consultation in time of war on questions of
using nuclear weapons and the technical facilities that are available for
carrying out this consultation process(2).

4. With respect to the specific arrangements for WINTEX 73, Staff Group members
will recall the briefing on WINTEX 73 given to the Group by the Director,
Council Operations on 11th December, 1972, and the points that were brought out
in the ensuing discussion. Members may also wish to refer to the Council
Operations and Exercise Committee documents(3) setting forth the arrangements
for the participation of the Defence Planning Committee in WINTEX 73. In this
regard, it will be noted that the DPC will participate actively for only a brief
period of 24-36 hours(4); the other periods in WINTEX 73 (which runs from 7th
February to 16th March) should not be ignored, since that could be of value in
preparing a possible NPG Permanent Representatives' report. Finally, Staff Group
members are reminded that special passes will be required
