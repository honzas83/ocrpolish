> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Thursday, 5th May, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on May 5, 1977. The meeting's primary focus was to address several administrative and strategic items. First, the group discussed observations on WINTEX 77, noting that document NPG/I/P(77)3 would be circulated as an NPC document following editorial changes and the inclusion of remarks from the United States and Canada, as well as statements from Canadian and German representatives. Second, the group coordinated travel arrangements for an upcoming trip to Ottawa, including the arrival of a German aircraft and requests for seat confirmations and hotel reservations. Third, the group discussed the New Technology Study, agreeing that a revised draft would be sent to Capitals to obtain instructions before reopening the discussion on May 13. Finally, the group confirmed the next meetings as scheduled in Notice NPG(Staff Group)N(77)41.

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared
to the security classification of the matters to
be discussed.

OBSERVATIONS ON WINTEX 77

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who
referred to the discussion on the Observations
on WINTEX 77 which had taken place at the
meeting of Permanent Representatives that
morning; NPG/I/P(77)3 could now, subject to some
editorial changes, be circulated as an NPC
document(1);

(b) took note of the further remarks of the Chairman
who said that some minor changes proposed by,
the United States and Canada would be "included" in
the document, and that the statements by the
Canadian and German representatives would be
circulated.

(1) now circulated as NPG/D(77)3, 6th May, 1977

ARRANGEMENTS FOR OTTAWA

3. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note that the German aircraft was scheduled to land at Ottawa at 2
p.m., local time, on Tuesday, 7th June, 1977; this would probably imply a
departure from kёln/Bom airport at 9 a.m., local time, on the Tuesday morning;

(b) noted the request by the German representative that the International Staff
and delegations should now confirm the number of seats they required, taking
into account the restrictions on wives;

(c) took note of the request of the Canadian representative that delegations
should inform him soonest of their revised hotel reservation needs.

NEW TECHNOLOGY STUDY

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) noted the remarks of the Chairman who proposed that the Staff Group should
continue its discussion of the draft on the New Technology Study with a view to
giving the International Staff sufficient guidance for the preparation of a
revised text;

(b) agreed that the revised draft(1) should be sent to Capitals with a view to
obtain instructions in time to re-open the discussion on this paper on Friday,
13th May.

NEXT MEETINGS

5. The STAFF GROUP:

agreed to hold its next meetings as set out in Notice NPG(Staff Group)N(77)41,
5th May, 1977.

NATO, 1110 Brussels.

(1) circulated as NPG(Staff Group)WP(77)5(Revised) on 6th May, 1977.
