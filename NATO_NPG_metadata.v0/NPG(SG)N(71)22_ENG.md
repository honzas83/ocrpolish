> [!abstract] 
> # National Participation in NATO Military Nuclear Planning
> 
> This document consists of a cover note from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) and a draft note requesting national authorities of Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) countries to report on their experience with the arrangements for national participation in NATO military nuclear planning decided by the Defence Planning Committee (DPC) in May 1968. The Chairman, H. B. Seim, proposes that countries report by June 15, 1971, whether the current arrangements have been working to their satisfaction. An annex provides a detailed history of the following actions: the DPC Ministerial meeting of May 10, 1968, which approved recommendations for increased national participation through direct cooperation between Ministries of Defence and Major NATO Commands, the use of National Military Representatives, and the composition of planning staffs. It also references the Military Committee memorandum MCM-34-68 and SACEUR's instructions (SHAPE 7450/20) regarding the policy for national participation in Allied Command Europe (ACE) nuclear planning, emphasizing the following: national intelligence contributions, the development of operational plans by NATO military commanders, and the strict control of the dissemination of operational plans based on a 'need to know' basis.

Note by the Chairman

Attached is the first draft of a note on the subject of national participation
in NATO military nuclear planning. As suggested in our meeting on Monday, this
draft, or a suitable revision, might provide a basis for obtaining approval in
principle from capitals that countries should report their experience with the

NDAC countries.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP
NATIONAL PARTICIPATION IN NATO MILITARY NUCLEAR PLANNING
Draft note by the Chairman
The subject of national participation in NATO nuclear planning has been a major
item of interest for the Nuclear Planning Group since its inception; its various
aspects have been discussed at several Ministerial meetings.

2. In the light of recommendations flowing from NPG/NDAC consideration of this
subject, the DPC approved, at the Ministerial meeting on 10th May 1968, certain
arrangements designed to achieve increased national participation in NATO
military nuclear planning(1). For ease of reference, the Ministerial decision as
well as the subsequent follow-up actions by the Military Committee and SACEUR
are set out at annex.

3. Almost three years have passed since the DPC decision was taken and it is
therefore reasonable to review the application of the arrangements in the light
of practical experience. Accordingly, the national authorities are requested to
report by 15th June,1971 whether in practice the current arrangements have been
working to their satisfaction.

(1) DPC/D(68)18, paragraph 7, 13th May, 1968

ANNEX to NPG(Staff Group)N(71)22

I. DPC MINISTERIAL MEETING, 10th MAY 1968(1)
....7. Ministers approved the recommendations of the Military Committee,
endorsed by the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee and the Nuclear Planning
Group:

(a) that, following the provisions of MC 57/2 and the conclusions of MCM-25-67,
the Military Committee should continue to develop its capabilities to perform
its rôle in NATO military nuclear planning by, amongst other things,
accommodating national views;

(b) that increased national participation in NATO military nuclear planning
should be achieved by some or all of the following:

at Major NATO Command level by
(1) Ministries of Defence taking greater advantage of the existing channels
between Major NATO Commands and nations by direct co-operation;

(ii) increasing the use of National Military Representatives, keeping National
Military Representatives periodically informed on the subject of nuclear plans;

(iii) making available within NATO Headquarters the relevant portions of nuclear
plans to affected Ministries of Defence for analysis;

(iv) holding such conferences with national authorities as necessary;
at Major Subordinate Command level by
(v) holding regular conferences with national authorities at relevant stages of
planning;

(c) that the nuclear planning staffs at Major NATO Commands'/Major Subordinate
Commands' Headquarters should be composed of an appropriate cross-section of the
nations concerned.

(1) DPC/D(68)18, para 7, 13th May, 1968

II. MILITARY COMMITTEE MEMORANDUM TO THE MAJOR NATO COMMANDERS, MCM-34-68, 21st
MAY, 1968

2. The Military Committee invited the Major NATO Commanders to take follow-up
action to the Ministerial decision.

III. SACEUR's INSTRUCTIONS TO SUBORDINATE COMMANDS AND SHAPE 7450/20, 20th MAY,
1969

3. In response to the DPC decision and MCM-34-68, SACEUR issued the following
instructions to ACE Commands:

".... 6. Policy for National Participation in ACE Nuclear Planning

(a) National viewpoints affecting overall policy and strategy are provided at
the Military Committee and Defence Planning Committee level. The guidelines
which emanate from these Committees serve as a basis for operational planning by
SACEUR and his subordinate military Commanders.

(b) NATO military Commanders are responsible for the preparation of operational
plans. National participation in the preparation of these plans will continue to
be as follows:

(1) National intelligence contributes to the planning function by providing to
NATO Commanders information such as threat analysis and the identification of
targets.

(2) After making an analysis of enemy capabilities NATO military Commanders
develop specific operational plans using the forces and weapons which nations
undertake to provide. As stated in paragraphs 3 and 4 above, these plans must
consider a broad spectrum of possibilities and provide for the use of both
conventional and nuclear weapons, as may be appropriate to the situation.

(3) A significant and vital national effort is required to support operational
planning. This effort must be directed toward adequate personnel manning,
logistic support, training and force readiness. It will continue to be closely
coordinated between the MODs and the NATO military Commanders in the regions
concerned.

(4) Operational planning staffs at SHAPE, MSC headquarters and SACEUR's
representation at the US Joint Strategic Target Planning Staff will continue to
be composed of an appropriate cross-section of officers who have been assigned
as part of those internationally integrated military staffs.

(c) Based upon authorization and direction from appropriate higher authority,
the execution and implementation of approved operational plans will be the
responsibility of the NATO military commander concerned.

(d) It is in the vital security interests of NATO to closely control the
dissemination of operational plans and their contents. Information regarding
relevant and specific portions of operational plans may be furnished by SHAPE
and subordinate ACE headquarters to national authorities on a case-by-case basis
only when a "need to know" has been definitely established.

(e) Conferences and meetings with national authorities concerning operational
planning will be conducted at SHAPE and MSC headquarters as needed to develop
and complete operational plans.

(f) Subject to the installation of approved secure communications, capable of
handling COSMIC TOP SECRET ATOMAL information, NMRs at SHAPE may be used to
provide a secondary channel for the exchange of information between SHAPE and
MODs relative to nuclear planning and associated operational matters."
