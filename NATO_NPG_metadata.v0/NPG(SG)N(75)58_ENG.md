> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 28th July, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on July 28, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group discussed the timing and location of the Autumn Ministerial Meeting, suggesting it may take place in Brussels or Hamburg. The meeting covered the documentation of the Monterey Agreed Minute and other ministerial meeting participation. The Staff Group noted a planned visit to Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE) on September 9, 1975. There were discussions on nuclear matter courses at Oberammergau, and the work of a small working group under Mr. Beaumont of the United Kingdom to study communication and warning of NATO's intentions. Communication and Nuclear Release Requests from member countries were the topics of further deliberation. The group also considered the future of the Air Defence Illustrative Study and the modernization of the Theatre Nuclear Force Posture in Europe. The United States representative provided updates on Phase III guidance and scope papers. The meeting concluded with the decision to hold the next meeting on August 18, 1975.

FUTURE WORK PROGRESS(1)(2)
Communication and Warning of NATO's Intentions
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that, at the moment, there
was a paper on CNI but no corresponding procedure paper, and that no paper and
no procedures had, as yet, been produced on the subject of Warning; he recalled
that, at its meeting on 9th July, 1975, the Staff Group had discussed the
possibility of forming a small working group to study the questions referred to
in paragraph 10 of the Agreed Minute concerning warning of NATO's intentions;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the subsequent discussion and agreed
upon the following points:

(i) the small working group, under the direction of Mr. Beaumont, United Kingdom
representative, should work in close co-ordination with the International Staff
Nuclear Planning Directorate;

(ii) the work of the Beaumont group was still at exploratory stage and would not
necessarily be ready for use at Permanent Representative level in time for the
Autumn Ministerial Meeting;

(iii) if possible, the thrust should be toward a single paper combining the
Warning messge considerations and the related procedures;

(c) invited the Beaumont group to make an oral report upon the evolution of its
ideas at the next meeting of the Staff Group.

Country requests:
8. The NATO GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to paragraph 4
of the Agreed Minute, concerning Nuclear Release Requests from NATO member
countries;

(b) took note of the views expressed and invited members to forward comments and

(c) took note of the remarks of the SHAPE representative who drew attention to

political and military considerations were closely linked in such requests;

Illustrative Studies
9. The STAFF GROUP:

taken;

(b) took note of the suggestions made in the ensuing discussion and of differing
views upon the desirability of including the Air Defence Study in the agenda for
the Autumn meeting;

(c) noted in particular a statement by the United States representative who
considered that the Air Defence Study had been addressed at Permanent
Representative level; he felt that Ministers preferred to discuss policy matters
rather than procedures;

Modernisation of Theatre Nuclear Force Posture in Europe
10. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman who said that paragraph 14 of the
Agreed Minute clearly charged the NPG with the task of facilitating consultation
between the United States and its NATO Allies on the more sensitive aspects of
further studies arising from the modernisation of the Theatre Nuclear Force
Posture.

Phase III -- Guidance
11. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the United States representative who welcomed
the suggestion that countries and NMAs should submit papers on an informal basis
outlining their views on what Phase III should accomplish; the United States
authorities were presently working on a scope paper and hoped to have something
to show in August;

(b) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who said that he hoped to receive
comments in time for the next meeting or at any rate before the end of August;
contributions were, of course, entirely voluntary.
