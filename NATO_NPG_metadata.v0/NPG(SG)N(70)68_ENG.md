> # Notes of a Meeting Held on 16th September, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on 16th September 1970 to address two primary items. First, the group discussed and agreed upon amendments to document NPG(Staff Group)WP(70)5 regarding special political guidelines for the possible use of atomic demolition munitions, with a final report to be submitted to Permanent Representatives on 29th September 1970. Second, the group reviewed the draft agenda for the Ottawa Nuclear Planning Group Ministerial meeting, including a briefing by the United States Secretary of Defence on the 'Balance of Strategic Forces' and 'Follow-on Studies'. The Chairman was invited to prepare a revised agenda and request agreement via the 'silence procedure' by 2nd October 1970. Additionally, the group scheduled its next meeting for 21st September 1970 at the NATO Situation Centre in Brussels to discuss documents NPG/D(70)6, NPG/D(70)7, and NPG/D(70)8.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on 16th September, 1970.

I. SPECIAL POLITICAL GUIDELINES FOR THE POSSIBLE USE OF
ATOMIC DEMOLITION MUNITIONS
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) having discussed document NPG(Staff Group)WP(70)5(4th revise) and
agreed a number of amendments proposed by members of delegations and the NATO
Military

Authorities, invited the Chairman to prepare and issue a revised version of that
document(1);

(b) noted that the final report of the Staff Group
mentioned at (a) above would be submitted to
Permanent Representatives for discussion at
their meeting on 29th September, 1970.

II. DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE OTTAWA NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP
MINISTERIAL MEETING
2. The STAFF GROUP:
having discussed document NPG(Staff Group)WP(70)4
and agreed proposals for the inclusion of further
items, namely a briefing by the United States
Secretary of Defence on the "Balance of
Strategic Forces" and "Follow-on Studies"(2),
invited the Chairman to prepare and issue a
further draft of the proposed Agenda for the
Ottawa NPC Ministerial meeting, requesting
agreement under the "silence procedure" by
Friday, 2nd October, 1970.

(1) Subsequently published as NPG(Staff Group)WP(70)3(5th revise)
(2) To be issued as NPG(Staff Group)WP(70)4(Revised)
