> # Military Committee Function in the Nuclear Consultation Process
> 
> This document, a third revision of a working paper from the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, provides a revised draft report on the Military Committee's function within the nuclear consultation process. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, notes that previous drafts did not clearly illustrate the approach for 'Alternative ending TWO', and this version aims to rectify that deficiency. The report details that NPG Ministers in Ankara in May 1973 identified uncertainties regarding how the Military Committee in Permanent Session would be involved in the process, specifically concerning requests or proposals for the use of nuclear weapons. The document recommends that the Nuclear Planning Group submit a draft report to the Defence Planning Committee (DPC) and that the Ministers agree that the conceptual approach is consistent with the General Guidelines for Consultation Procedures on the Use of Nuclear Weapons. It further suggests that the Military Committee's functions in support of the nuclear consultation process should conform to this conceptual approach, subject to endorsement by the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee. The report emphasizes the importance of ensuring that the nuclear consultation process can be carried out rapidly and effectively.

Draft Report by the Permanent Representatives

At their meeting in Ankara in May, 1973, the NPG Ministers considered a
report(1) by the Permanent Representatives which provided some preliminary
observations on the nuclear consultation aspects of WINTEX 73. During their
discussion, the Ministers noted some uncertainties concerning the ways in which
the Military Committee in Permanent Session would be involved in the process,
particularly with respect to dealing with specific requests or proposals for use
of nuclear weapons. They invited(2) the Permanent Representatives to review the
function of the Military Committee in nuclear consultation.

2. The views of the Military Committee on this subject(3), provided at the
request of the Permanent Representatives, were a very useful contribution for
making the review.
