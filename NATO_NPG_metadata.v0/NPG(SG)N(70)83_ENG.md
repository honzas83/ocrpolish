> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Tuesday 20th October, 1970: Arrangements for the Ottawa Meeting
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on October 20, 1970, to finalize arrangements for an upcoming meeting in Ottawa. The meeting's agenda included logistical details such as invitations to a dinner at the Chateau Laurier and visits to Calgary and North Bay. The Staff Group noted statements from the United States and other representatives regarding attendance for briefings on the Balance of Strategic Forces and various follow-on studies, including those by Lord Carrington and ministers from Norway, Canada, and Italy. The group also discussed the timing of the Spring meeting in 1971 and modifications to the Provisional Political Guidelines for AIMS. The Chairman noted that a draft advance press release would be released in Brussels on October 26, 1970. Additionally, the document emphasizes the need for increased precautions when transporting classified documents between NATO countries. The Staff Group reviewed the work to be done leading up to the NDAC, DPC, and Council Meetings in December and the remainder of 1971. Future work includes the Report on Alliance Defence Problems for the 1970s and various illustrative studies and sample analyses. The next meeting of the Staff Group is scheduled for November 9, 1970, in Brussels, with Portugal invited to attend.

22nd October. 1970
NOTICE
NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Tuesday 20th October, 1970
ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE OTTAWA MEETING
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Canadian Representative in which he mentioned
the following points:

(1) everyone would be invited to the dinner at the Chateau Laurier on Thursday,
29th October, 1970; black ties and evening dresses would be needed;

(11) everyone would be invited to take part in the visit to Calgary; warm
informal clothes would be needed;

(111) most delegates, but not wives, would be able to go on the visit to North
Bay.

AGENDA FOR THE OTTAWA MEETING
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the United States Representative who said that
attendance for Item I. (Briefing on the Balance of Strategic Forces) would be
the same as for the other items (5 per delegation);

(b) with regard to Item V (Follow-on Studies(1)) noted that short statements
would be made by Lord Carrington (NORTHAG Study), Mr. Schmidt (CEN TAG Study),
the Norwegian and Canadian Ministers (North-Region Study), the Italian Minister
(Analysis of Tactical Nuclear Maritime Warfare, and the Transition Study), and
probably by the Greek Minister (Analysis of Battle Situation – Southern Region);

(1) NPG/D(70)1, dated 24th February, 1970.

DRAFT ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE

3. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled that no objections having been
received to the Draft Advance Press Release(5), it would be released in Brussels
on Monday, 26th October, 1970, at 3 p.m., with optional simultaneous release in
other capitals.

SECURITY OF DOCUMENTS

4. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew the attention of all concerned
to the need for increased precautions when transporting classified documents
between NATO countries, and invited Delegations to take advantage of the
arrangements made by the International Staff and the Canadian Authorities for
the carriage of documents to and from Ottawa(4)

(b) noted that the Report on Alliance Defence Problems for the 1970s currently
being prepared for the Ministerial Meeting in December would probably involve

(c) noted that the first group of Follow-on Studies were scheduled to be

NEXT MEETING

6. The STAFF GROUP:

(1) questions arising and work resulting from the Ottawa meeting
