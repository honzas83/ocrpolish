> [!abstract] 
> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 21st February, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on February 21, 1972. The meeting covered several key agenda items. First, the group took note of a briefing on NATO Alert Procedures scheduled for February 29, 1972, for the Council Operations and Exercise Committee. The group then discussed the initiation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Programme, reviewing organizational and procedural aspects and considering alternative endings for the International Staff's paper. There was a continuing divergence of views on whether the Study Group's report should be submitted directly to ministers or through NPG Permanent Representatives. The group agreed to conduct informal contacts to reach an agreement on the text for submission to capitals. Additionally, the group reviewed the first draft of a report on the Turkish Follow-on Study, with a focus on escalation and a request for the Canadian representative to prepare a new draft paragraph on this topic. Finally, the group noted the progress of the Mediterranean Maritime Study, which had been received from Rome, and discussed the feasibility of a briefing for Permanent Representatives on March 9. The next meeting was scheduled for February 26, 1972, to continue discussions on Phase II and the Turkish Study.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 21st
February, 1972

BRIEFING ON NATO ALERT PROCEDURES
1. The STAFF GROUP: took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that a
briefing on NATO Alert Procedures would be given as Item I of a meeting of the
Council Operations and Exercise Committee to be held on Tuesday, 29th February,
1972, at 2.30 p.m. ; he had been informed that members of that Committee were
encouraged to bring with them any members of their Delegations who might find
the briefing useful, and suggested that the Staff Group might wish to take
advantage of this invitation.

INITIATION OF PHASE II OF THE FOLLOW-ON USE PROGRAMME
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who, with a view to placing the
discussion of Phase II in a proper context, reviewed briefly the organisational
and procedural aspects that distinguish the NPG from other NATO activities,
referring to the documents mentioned in NPG(Staff Group)N(72)18 of 15th
February, 1972;

(b) took note of further remarks by the Chairman who introduced the third
revision of the International Staff’s paper(1), drew attention to the two
alternative endings (paragraphs 8-11) proposed, and invited comments;

(c) `took note of the statements made by the representatives of Italy and the

(d) `agreed upon the need to develop a suitable paper in time to be sent to

DRAFT REPORT ON THE TURKISH FOLLOW-ON USE STUDY(1)
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) `took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the first draft(2)
of a report on the Turkish Follow-on Study, and invited comments;`

(b) `took note of the suggestions made in the ensuing discussion and in
particular of the views expressed on the subject of escalation;`

(c) `invited the Canadian representative to prepare a new draft paragraph on
escalation for insertion in Part III of the paper;`

(d) `agreed to a number of changes in the text of paragraphs 1-8;`
(e) `agreed to continue the discussion at the next meeting.`

MEDITERRANEAN MARITIME STUDY
4. THE STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that the major part of the text of the Mediterranean Maritime Study
had now been received from Rome, and that every effort was being made to prepare
and print it as soon as possible; noted that Ministerial approval of the Study
had not yet been received;

(b) agreed, in view of the short time now remaining for examination of this
study, to consider at the next meeting of the Staff Group whether the briefing
on the Study planned for Permanent Representatives on 9th March was still a
feasible arrangement.

NEXT MEETING
5. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 26th February, 1972, at 3 p.m.; the
principal subjects for discussion would be the Initiation of Phase II, and the
report on the Turkish Study.
