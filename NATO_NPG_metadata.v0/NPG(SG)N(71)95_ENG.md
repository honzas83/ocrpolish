> # Unresolved Problems Related to Tactical Nuclear Defence
> 
> This document, issued by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), provides a list of suggested changes to the revised draft report NPG(Staff Group)N(71)80(Revised). The primary focus is to align the investigation of unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence, specifically those mentioned in PO/71/226, with the ongoing follow-on use work programme. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, notes that while some steps can be taken immediately to establish an initial framework, other aspects of the own follow-on use studies must assume that deterrence has failed, and thus cannot fully address the deterrent qualities of nuclear weapons or initial use questions. The document specifies detailed textual substitutions and deletions across multiple pages of the draft report, aiming to reach an agreement on a Working Paper to be sent to Capitals in early September 1971, in anticipation of a Permanent Representatives' meeting. The document originates from NATO headquarters in Brussels, Belgium.
>

>

> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Nuclear-Deterrence — The document explicitly mentions the need to examine the 'deterrent qualities of nuclear weapons' and notes that follow-on use studies assume deterrence has failed, distinguishing this from the examination of deterrence itself.

NPG(Staff Group)N(71)95
Suggested Changes to NPG(Staff Group)N(71)80(Revised)
a. Pages 2 and 3, Paragraph 2, delete entire paragraph and substitute the
following:

"2. The individual problems mentioned in PO/71/226 are closely related to one
another, to the various nuclear problems cited in the AD 70 report and to other
NPG efforts, particularly the follow-on use work programme. In addition, work
that has been done or is being carried out by countries or by the NATO Military
Authorities could have a direct bearing on the PO/71/226 questions.
Consequently, the first step in addressing these problems might be oriented
mainly toward finding out where matters now stand."

b. Page 4, Paragraph 3, delete entire paragraph and substitute the following:
"3. In developing proposals and ordering priorities for the investigation of
PO/71/226 problems, particular attention must be paid to their close
relationship with the follow-on use work programme. The latter can be expected
to define more clearly the status of many of the unresolved PO/71/226 problems,
but it is still in its early stages and the full impact is yet to be felt. Care
will therefore have to be taken to ensure that any new efforts based on
PO/71/226 complement or build on the expected contributions of the follow-on use
work programme and do not preempt, duplicate or ignore these valuable
contributions."

c. Pages 4 and 5, Paragraph 4, delete entire paragraph and substitute the
following:

"4. This is not to say, however, that all PO/71/226 work must or should be
deferred until the follow-on use study effort is completed. There are clearly
some steps that can be taken now, particularly in establishing the initial
framework within which PO/71/226 problems should be examined. Moreover, the
follow-on use work programme does not cover all aspects of the questions raised
in PO/71/226. For example, because all follow-on use studies must assume that
deterrence has failed, they cannot do full justice to the examination of the
deterrent qualities of nuclear weapons. Also, by definition, they do not address
initial use questions. Finally, the terms of reference for the follow-on use
work programme require the studies to be based on current nuclear capabilities
and present concepts for their employment whereas PO/71/226 does not rule out
the possibility of changes in NATO's existing nuclear capabilities and related
employment concepts. Therefore, it may be desirable in due course to initiate
some additional PO/71/226 work that would proceed in parallel with the follow-on
use work programme."
