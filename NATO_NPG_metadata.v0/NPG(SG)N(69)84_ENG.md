> # Tentative Political Guidelines for the Possible Initial Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons by NATO
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on Thursday, November 6, 1969, to focus on the political guidelines for the potential initial tactical use of nuclear weapons within the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO). The meeting's agenda included a review of common statements from the United Kingdom and Germany regarding revisions to the document, as well as a discussion of proposed changes by the United Kingdom. Additionally, the Danish Representative requested the deletion of a sentence concerning studies on the 'use at sea'. The group also touched upon the future development of the strategic use of nuclear weapons and discussed suggestions for handling changes proposed by Permanent Representatives during an Ambassadorial meeting on October 13. It was noted that the paper specifically addresses the employment of nuclear weapons in the NATO area and not outside of it. The meeting concluded with administrative remarks from the United States Representative regarding Airlie House facilities and the agreement to hold the next meeting on November 17, 1969, in Brussels, Belgium.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (ST/FF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Thursday, 6th November, 1969
TENTATIVE POLITICAL GUIDELINES FOR THE POSSIBLE INITIAL TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR
WEAPONS BY NATO

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted with appreciation the common statement made by the United Kingdom and
Germany that a revision of concerned parts of the document was prepared in order
to take into account various national suggestions; these amendments will not be
included in the document now;

(b) discussed in some details the proposed changes by the United Kingdom;
(c) noted the Danish Representative's request to delete the sentence in
paragraph 5, page 6 of the document "Further studies ... dealing with 'use at
sea'."

STRATEGIC USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
noted the views of some representatives on the future development of the
subject.

CONSULTATION ON THE USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) discussed some suggestions on how to deal with the changes proposed by
Permanent Representatives during the Ambassadorial meeting on 13th October;

(b) noted the agreed proposal that the Belgian Minister recalled in the
introductory remarks that the paper deals only with the employment of nuclear
weapons in the NATO area and not outside.
