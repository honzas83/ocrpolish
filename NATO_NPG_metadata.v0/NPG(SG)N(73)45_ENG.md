> [!abstract] 
> # Future NPG Activities
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, summarizes potential subjects for future Ministerial agendas over the next one to two years. It details several key areas of focus, including a U.S. briefing on the balance of strategic forces, the progress of the Follow-on Use Work Programme, and unresolved problems related to Tactical Nuclear Defence. The note also addresses the illustrative studies requested by Ministers, the investigation of consultation procedures and facilities, and the implementation of Special ADM Guidelines. Additionally, it discusses the role of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation process and the initiation of studies on communicating NATO's intentions and the Warsaw Pact's politico-military strategy and military doctrine. Finally, it suggests that the Staff Group continue discussions on the conceptual analysis of the nuclear aspects of deterrence. The document serves as a basis for further discussion at the Staff Group meeting on June 4, 1973, and provides a planning timetable for the Autumn 1973 Ministerial meeting.
>

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
FUTURE NGP ACTIVITIES
Note by the Chairman
In the light of actions taken at the recent NPG Ministerial meeting in Ankara on
15th and 16th May(1) and discussions in the Staff Group, the shape of possible
future tasks to be accomplished by the NPG during the next one or two years has
become more discernible. The following paragraphs summarize the potential
subjects, and possible timing of their appearance on Ministerial agendas, in
order to provide a basis for further discussion at the Staff Group meeting on
Monday, 4th June. A planning time-table for preparations for the next NPG
Ministerial meeting in the Autumn of 1973 is set forth at annex.

POTENTIAL SUBJECTS
2. U.S. Briefing on Balance of Strategic Forces. At Ministerial meetings during
the past several years, the United States has offered to brief the Ministers on
the balance of strategic forces, and the Ministers have expressed the hope that
the practice would continue. This subject does not normally require preparatory
work on the part of the Permanent Representatives or the Staff Group.

Ministerial discussion of the basic paper(1) and a subsequent Permanent

stages of preparation and should be available to the "PG by mid-July at which

start. It appears, therefore, that these two remaining topics could be
considered by the Ministers at their Autumn 1973 meeting. Insofar as later
meetings are concerned, it is possible that other subjects mentioned in PO 226
could be addressed by the Ministers but no arrangements have as yet been made
for preparatory steps to be taken by countries or the NMAs. It is also possible
that requirements for further investigations could emerge from Ministerial
discussion of reports that will go before them this Autumn.

6. Consultation Procedures and Facilities. A number of aspects of this subject
are under active investigation in the NPG. For example, the Ministers at London
in October 1972 invited(1) the Permanent Representatives to continue their
examination of the problem of classification and precedence of selective release
messages addressed to political authorities and to report in due course. At the
recent Ankara meeting, the Permanent Representatives were asked(2) to look at
the question of identification and handling of messages related to nuclear
consultation and of the content of selective release messages. It will also be
recalled that the Ministers agreed at London that the NPG should keep itself
informed of progress being made in other bodies, such as COEC, on matters that
are relevant to the nuclear consultation process. Accordingly, it is believed
that one or more of the aspects mentioned above could form the basis for a
Permanent Representatives' report for Ministerial consideration at the Autumn
1973 meeting. Other aspects of the general subject of consultation procedures
and facilities could also be placed before the Ministers during 1974,
particularly in the light of their invitation(3) to the Permanent
Representatives to review the WINTEX 73 results when final reports and analyses
are available.

8. Function of the Military Committee in the Nuclear Consultation Process. At
their last meeting, the Ministers invited(1) the Permanent Representatives to
review the rôle of the Military Committee in this regard. As a first step, it
has been proposed that the Permanent Representatives ask for the views of the
Military Committee. If the Permanent Representatives concur, a request will be
sent by the Secretary General to the Chairman of the Military Committee in mid-
June. Since the subject is likely to be controversial, no specific deadline for
a Military Committee response will be given. It is considered improbable,
however, that a reply would be received in time to allow preparation of a
Permanent Representatives' report for Ministerial consideration in the Autumn of
1973. It is likely, therefore, that this subject would be placed before the
Ministers in 1974.

9. Communicating NATO's Intentions. This is another facet of the nuclear
consultation process that was addressed by the Ministers at Ankara. At the
conclusion of their discussion, they invited(2) the Permanent Representatives to
initiate a study on the implications of communicating NATO's intentions to an
enemy, to other countries and to the public and on the related procedural
matters. As a first step, it is suggested that the Staff Group may wish to
explore the question of whether one or more countries are interested in
preparing the study or whether the Permanent Representatives should do it. Since
this step may take some time and since the study itself will also require a
considerable effort, it seems unlikely that the final product would be available
for Ministerial consideration before the Autumn of 1974.

10. Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy and Military Doctrine. At Ankara the
Ministers invited(1) the Permanent Representatives to arrange for a study of
this matter. In this case also, the Staff Group may wish to investigate whether
one or more countries will assume the responsibility for conducting the study or
whether it should be undertaken by the Permanent Representatives. In this
regard, it will be recalled that the Netherlands Authorities have expressed a
willingness to act as Discussion Leader for this study, provided there are one
or more other participants. In view of the existing uncertainties, it would be
unrealistic to expect that the Ministers would be able to address the study
before the Autumn of 1974.

11. Conceptual Analysis of the Nuclear Aspects of Deterrence. At their last
meeting, the Ministers invited(2) the Permanent Representatives to consider the
value of a conceptual analysis of the nuclear aspects of deterrence for
Ministerial consideration at a future meeting. This wording seems to require
that a judgement first be reached by the Permanent Representatives on whether
such an analysis has potential value. If so, steps should then be taken to
initiate the work. Accordingly, it is suggested that the Staff Group continue
its discussions on the question of whether the analysis should be made, perhaps
attempting to get a clearer idea of what the analysis would cover and how it
might contribute to the work of the NPG. In view of the circumstances, no
estimates can be made concerning when, or indeed whether, such an analysis might
appear on a Ministerial agenda; it is certainly not a contender for the Autumn
1973 meeting.

PREPARATIONS FOR THE AUTUMN 1973 MEETING
16. On the assumption that the subjects mentioned in paragraph 12 above should

proposed that the timetable at annex be adopted for planning purposes, in order

and 18th October.
