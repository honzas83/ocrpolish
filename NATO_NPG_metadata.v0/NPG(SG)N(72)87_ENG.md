> [!abstract] 
> # Downgrading or Destruction of Selected NDAC Documents and Related Working Papers
> 
> This document is a notice from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated 5th October 1972, instructing members to review and downgrade security classifications for specific documents from 1967 and 1968. The notice explains that a review by the International Staff determined that original security classifications no longer applied to certain Nuclear Planning Group Advisory Committee (NDAC) documents, and the subsequent agreement of NDAC countries has led to this action. Annex A lists eight NDAC documents, including reports and decision sheets for ministerial meetings, being downgraded from NATO SECRET to either NATO CONFIDENTIAL or NATO RESTRICTED. Annex B lists related working papers, including draft reports and cover notes, that are also authorized for downgrading. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, encourages Staff Group members to verify that their copies of these documents have been appropriately downgraded and suggests the destruction of copies that are no longer needed. The document originates from Brussels, Belgium, and is part of the archival record of the Nuclear Planning Group.

NOTICE
NFG(Staff Group)N(72)87

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

DOWNGRADING OR DESTRUCTION OF SELECTED
NDAC DOCUMENTS AND RELATED WORKING PAPERS

Note by the Chairman

Several months ago, a review of NDAC documents issued in
1967 and 1968 was conducted by the International Staff to determine
whether the original security classifications assigned to the
documents still applied. As a result of the review, it was proposed(1)
that a number of documents be downgraded, to which the NDAC countries
subsequently agreed. Holders of the documents were then notified(2)
of the new classifications by the Head of the NATO Registry. For
the convenience of Staff Group members, the NDAC documents that were
affected by this action are listed in Annex A.

2. As a related matter, a review of the various drafts and
working papers circulated in connection with the preparation of the
NDAC documents listed in Annex A has been undertaken to identify
additional papers that may also be downgraded. These documents
are listed in Annex B, together with the revised classifications
that have been authorised by the originators. It is expected that
the head of the NATO Registry will also notify holders of these
documents of this downgrading action.

3. Staff Group members may wish to verify that copies of the
documents listed in Annexes A and B which may be in their possession
have been appropriately downgraded. It is also suggested that
Staff Group members may wish to destroy copies of these documents
for which they have no further need.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM.

1110 Brussels.
