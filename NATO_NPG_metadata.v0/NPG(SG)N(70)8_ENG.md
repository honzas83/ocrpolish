> # Topics for Staff Group Exploration
> 
> This document, issued by the Acting Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines a series of proposed topics for exploration by the Staff Group. The goal is to illuminate general questions of control and constraints in tactical nuclear warfare. The proposed topics are divided into five main areas: Command and Control, Postures, Targeting, Collateral Damage, and the contents of Selective Release Requests. Specifically, the Command and Control area covers ACE communications, command structure, selective release procedures, and situation reporting. The Postures area focuses on alert measures, deployment and disposal of nuclear-capable systems, logistics, and the transition from conventional to nuclear postures. The Targeting area includes the division slice model, target acquisition, weapons selection criteria, and the measurement of casualties and effects. The Collateral Damage area addresses calculation models, enemy potential for causing collateral damage, and civilian evacuation. Finally, the document suggests an examination of FAILLEX-68 selective release requests. The Acting Chairman, R. Briband, invites delegations and representatives from IMS and SHAPE to comment on the đúng a list of topics and suggest informed persons for briefings.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
TOPICS FOR STAFF GROUP EXPLORATION
Note by the Chairman

At the meeting of the Staff Group held on 13th February, 1970 it was agreed to
initiate within the Staff Group a series of discussions and briefings on topics
which might illuminate the general questions of control and constraints in
tactical nuclear warfare. Attached is tentative list of topics which have been
proposed by some Delegations, and around which these discussions and briefings
might be organized. Delegations may wish to add to this list. IMS and SHAPE
representatives may wish to comment on which subjects lend themselves to
briefings and whether informed persons are available who could discuss these
topics in substance with the Staff Group.

2. Delegations will note that the topics listed lend themselves first to
descriptions of systems and procedures, and second to an examination of problems
associated with their actual operations.

(Signed) R. BRIBAND
Acting Chairman

TOPICS FOR STAFF GROUP EXPLORATION
I. Command and Control area
1. ACE communications : fixed and field (e.g., see annex to ADM Plan for
Turkey).

2. Command structure and responsibilities for nuclear delivery systems and
warheads.

3. Selective release procedures :
(a) weapon-by-weapon release
(b) aggregated release
4. Situation reporting.
II. Postures Area
1. Alert measures relevant to nuclear-capable systems and warheads.
2. Deployment/disposal of nuclear-capable systems.
3. Nuclear weapons logistics and custodial arrangements.
4. Transition from conventional to nuclear postures.
III. Targeting Area
1. The division slice model : numbers, types, and disposition of targets.
2. Target acquisition techniques.
3. Criteria for weapons selection.
4. Measurement of casualties and other measurable effects.
5. Ways of judging reduction of effectiveness and effects on enemy tactics.
IV. Collateral Damage area
1. Models for calculation.
2. Enemy potential for causing collateral damage.
3. Warning and evacuating the civilian population.
V. Contents of Selective Release Requests
1. An examination of FAILLEX-68 selective release requests.
