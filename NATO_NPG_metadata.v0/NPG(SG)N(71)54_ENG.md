> # Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE
> 
> This document provides commentary on the role and employment of dual capable aircraft within the Allied Command Europe (ACE) theatre nuclear strike forces to ensure a balance between conventional operations and nuclear deterrence. It addresses clarifications requested by the NATO Military Committee regarding the potential contradictions in the concept paper for the role of theatre nuclear strike forces. The text emphasizes that dual capable aircraft are critical for NATO's strategy of flexibility in response, allowing for a range of options from conventional to general nuclear response. It discusses the deterrent role of these aircraft, noting that while some are maintained in a Quick Reaction Alert (QRA) posture, others contribute to the deterrent posture even when employed in conventional roles. The document argues that the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) should have the flexibility to use dual capable aircraft for conventional operations, provided that such use does not deplete the forces to a point where NATO can no longer effectively execute its nuclear options. It also highlights the political importance of involving non-nuclear nations in strike programs to demonstrate Alliance solidarity and enhance credibility. The final summary concludes that SACEUR should employ dual capable aircraft in conventional operations to the fullest extent consistent with the retention of a nuclear capability that satisfies essential deterrent and political requirements.

CONCEPT FOR THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES IN ACE
I. BACKGROUND
In approving the Concept for the R6le of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in
ACE(1), the Defence Planning Committee invited(2) the NATO Military Authorities:

(a) to continue to adjust their plans to conform with the approved concept;
(b) to report progress to the Nuclear Planning Group by 31st March, 1971; and
(c) to make suggestions, as appropriate, for further clarification or refinement
of the concept.

2. The Military Committee has confirmed(3) that plans are in accord with the
concept and that any future planning will also be in accord. In addition, the
Military Committee has called attention to statements in certain paragraphs of
the concept paper, i.e., paragraphs 60, 61 and 87, that might be construed as
contradictory; they suggest that further clarification would be most desirable.
In this context, the NATO Military Authorities hold the view that "the intent of
paragraphs 60 and 61 of the concept document(1) is to provide SACEUR with the
flexibility to use, if appropriate, all dual capable aircraft for conventional
operations but caution that SACEUR should exercise prudent military judgement to
prevent attrition of ACE strike forces to the point where NATO could no longer
effectively execute its options for selective or general nuclear response." The
Military Committee concluded that if the NATO Military Authorities' view is
correct, the concept document could be clarified by modifying the last sentence
of paragraph 61 to read in a less restrictive manner. If the NATO Military
Authorities' view is not a correct understanding of the intent, then presumably
a more extensive revision of the concept document would be necessary to avoid
ambiguity and possible misinterpretation(4).

II. COMMENTARY
3. In evaluating the interpretation given by the NATO Military Authorities of
the paragraphs in question, several important points in the concept document
pertaining to dual capable aircraft must be kept in mind.

Basic Significance of Dual Capable Aircraft
4. The concept paper indicates that a principal function of the theatre nuclear
strike forces as a whole is to contribute to the Alliance strategy of
flexibility in response, as part of the overall NATO capability to execute a
range of options from conventional operations, through tactical nuclear
operations, to a general nuclear response. This function has both political and
military implications, and requires that planning for the disposition of theatre
nuclear strike forces must provide for the militarily effective use of available
resources for various contingencies, and at the same time maintain a readily
visible deterrent posture. Dual capable aircraft are particularly significant in
the context of this principal function, because they are capable of being
employed effectively across the full range of options and also because they
comprise the larger part of the theatre nuclear strike forces(1).

Deterrent Role of Dual Capable Aircraft
5. The concept document notes that, in order to provide a readily visible and
credible deterrent posture, the Alliance maintains a minimum number of theatre
nuclear delivery systems in a Quick Reaction Alert (QRA) posture in peacetime.
In time of tension or in time of actual conflict short of general nuclear war,
the number of theatre nuclear delivery systems maintained in a QRA status for
deterrence purposes would probably be increased, but the level would depend on
the existing circumstances. Both aircraft and missiles would be used in the QRA
rôle. In so far as possible, QRA requirements would be met by delivery systems
that are limited to operating in a nuclear rôle only, such as Polaris, Pershing
and strike-only aircraft(2).

6. The precise level and mix of delivery systems to be maintained on QRA depends
on variable, unpredictable factors, such as the size, nature and disposition of
the enemy threat (particularly the nuclear capable components), the size, nature
and disposition of NATO's remaining nuclear assets, the relative balance between
NATO and the enemy in general terms in the light of the existing and foreseeable
situations, and the consequent estimate of what sort of a deterrent posture
would appear credible in the enemy's view.

7. Although the precise level and mix of QRA forces is unpredictable, it is
reasonable to assume that some dual capable aircraft would be involved in most
situations that might arise today. Such participation becomes more likely as the
policy of supplanting strike-only aircraft with dual capable aircraft is
implemented(1); it becomes a certainty when all theatre strike aircraft are dual
capable.

8. Finally, it should be appreciated that dual-capable aircraft, because of
their inherent capability for nuclear operations, contribute indirectly but
significantly to the overall Alliance deterrent posture even though they may be
employed at the moment in a conventional role rather than on QRA status. A
prudent enemy has to take account of this potential.

Operational Role of Dual Capable Aircraft
9. The concept document points out that the militarily effective use of
available resources for various contingencies requires flexibility in planning
as well as in the allocation of weapons systems to different tasks during actual
operations. Dual capable aircraft can operate in the conventional phase, in the
tactical nuclear phase or as part of the general nuclear response. Increased
importance is attached to the provision of dual capable aircraft for commitment
to the conventional rôle. Plans should be made for the maximum number of dual
capable aircraft to be available, if required, for conventional operations;
there should be no inflexible reservation of dual capable aircraft for the
general nuclear response. On the other hand, it is recognized that losses of
dual capable aircraft during conventional operations could reduce the extent to
which SACUR could contribute forces to the general nuclear response(2).

10. The use of dual capable aircraft in a conventional rôle does not necessarily
mean they will be used up. In other words, employment is not synonymous with
loss although some attrition as a result of operations can be expected. On the
other hand, some attrition (loss) of dual capable aircraft withheld from the
conventional rôle for QRA can also be expected as a result of enemy attacks on
airfields.

Political Role of Dual Capable Aircraft
11. The concept paper stresses that there are important political reasons for
involving strike elements of the non-nuclear nations in the strike programmes
and plans both for the tactical nuclear phase and for the general nuclear
response. This not only demonstrates the solidarity of the Alliance but also
enhances the credibility of the overall NATO deterrent(1).

12. In cases where all of a particular non-nuclear nation's strike elements
consist of dual capable aircraft, the foregoing clearly implies that some of
these aircraft would have to be safeguarded to insure that they were available
for tactical nuclear operations or the general nuclear response. Although it is
possible that all of the nation's dual capable aircraft could be employed in the
conventional rôle in the early phases of conflict, they should not continue to
be so employed to the point where there is a substantive risk that they could
all be destroyed during conventional operations.

Summary
13. The interpretation by the NATO Military Authorities(2) cited in paragraph 2
above appears to be compatible with the points covered in the foregoing
commentary, particularly if the expression "use" is construed to mean "employ"
and not to mean "expend". Furthermore, although all dual capable aircraft are
available, if necessary, it is unlikely in practical terms that all dual capable
aircraft would be employed in a conventional rôle at any given moment, in view
of the important deterrent and political aspects of such aircraft in their
nuclear configuration. In other words, SA CEUR should employ his dual capable
aircraft in conventional operations to the fullest extent consistent with the
retention of a nuclear capability that satisfies essential deterrent and
political requirements, but he should be prepared to warn political authority
and propose a transition to nuclear warfare at a point in time before attrition
of ACE strike forces, and particularly dual capable aircraft, has deprived him
of the capability to wage it.
