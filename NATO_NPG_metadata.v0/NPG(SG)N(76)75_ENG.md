> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 30th August, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on August 30, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key administrative and strategic items. The group approved a revised text for the Nike Hercules study terms of reference, agreeing to circulate it as an NPG Working Paper. They also discussed the compilation of nuclear consultation messages, noting that changes would take effect on October 1, 1976, and agreed to re-examine the paper on compilation in light of remarks from the United Kingdom. The meeting also covered preparations for the Autumn NPG Ministerial Meeting, including the scheduling of meetings for NPG Permanent Representatives and the coordination of dates for the Ministerial meeting, noting conflicts for the United States. Additionally, the group outlined forthcoming activities for the week of September 27, involving the Phase 3 Study Team, the Technology Panel, and the Political Implications Team. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next Staff Group meeting on September 6, 1976.

TERMS OF REFERENCE FOR THE NIK E HERCULES STUDY
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the Staff
Group paper(1); he recalled that the views of Germany and the United States
relative to the text of paragraph 12 had been opposed; discussions between the
representatives of these two countries and the International Staff had resulted
in a compromise solution which was contained in the German letter dated 26th
August, 1976;

COMPILATION
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks by the Chairman concerning
the Staff Group paper on compilation(1) and
proposed that if the Staff Group agreed to
the paper in its present form it should be circulated
for approval under the silence procedure effective
30th September; he recalled that changes to
nuclear consultation messages would come into
effect on 1st October, 1976;
(b) took note of a statement by the United Kingdom
representative who said that when this working
paper had been completed in 1975 it had been
generally accepted that the Staff Group should
take a further look at it before circulating it
as an NPG Working Paper; he felt unable, without
guidance from his capital, to agree to issue this
paper without further study within the Staff Group;
(c) agreed, after discussion, to re-examine the paper
in the light of the remarks by the UK representative;
(d) agreed upon the necessity to brief NPG Permanent
Representatives, at their next meeting, on the
changes decided upon by the Military Authorities.

PREPARATIONS FOR THE AUTUMN NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING
Meetings of NPG Permanent Representatives
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who said that, after consultation
with the Private Office, the following dates for NPG Permanent Representatives'
meetings had been agreed in principle: Wednesday, 29th September, at 4 p.m.
Friday, 22nd October, at 10.30 a.m. Thursday, 28th October, at 3.30 p.m.

(b) took note of the further remarks of the Chairman who said that Dr. Gaffney
would probably attend the first two of the meetings mentioned above.

Dates of Autumn NPG Ministerial Meeting
6. The STAFF GROUP (less the representatives of the countries not attending the
Autumn meeting):

(a) discussed the problem of the dates of the Autumn meeting noting in
particular that the United States were unable to be present on 22nd and 23rd
November;

(b) took note of the preferences of the various countries concerned;
(c) took note of the statement by the UK representative who said that he would
transmit to his Authorities the views expressed and hoped for a proposal, or
possibly a decision, from his Minister;

(d) took note of the statement by the US representative who expressed his regret
at the inconvenience caused to the UK Authorities and to the other participants
by the decision of his Authorities.

7. The STAFF GROUP:

the Phase 3 Study Team, the Technology Panel and the Political Implications
Team; he hoped that Dr. Gaffney would be able to make a status report on Phase 3
to the NPG Permanent Representatives on 29th September;
