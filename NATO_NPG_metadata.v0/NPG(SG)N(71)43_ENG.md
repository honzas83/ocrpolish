> [!abstract] 
> # Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated March 31, 1971, concerning the role of theatre nuclear strike forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE) area. It addresses a report by the Military Committee suggesting a clarification of a contradiction between paragraphs 60, 61, and 87 of a Concept paper. The Chairman outlines several options for the Ministers to resolve the issue, including agreeing on a textual change in paragraph 61, accepting the Military Committee's interpretation without changing the text, referring the matter to Permanent Representatives, or taking no action. Specifically, the Chairman suggests that replacing the words 'not permit' with 'seek to avoid' in the last sentence of paragraph 61 would resolve the Military Committee's point. He proposes that delegations seek views from their national authorities on these options and that the matter be discussed again at the Staff Group and Permanent Representatives levels in late April. The document is signed by H. B. Seim.

Note by the Chairman

Delegations have received a Report(1) by the Military Committee on the Concept
for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE. The document will be
redistributed in the near future in English and French under cover of
NPG/D(71)1.

2. The Staff Group will have noticed that the Military Committee suggests a
clarification of an apparent contradiction between paragraphs 60, 61 and 87 of
the Concept paper(2). The Military Committee offers its interpretation of these
paragraphs and recommends modification of the last sentence of paragraph 61 to
read in a less restrictive manner without, however, proposing precise language
for the desired change.

3. It may be useful for the Staff Group to discuss what action the Ministers
might take on the Military Committee's suggestion and what action, if any, the
Permanent Representatives could take to facilitate the Ministerial
consideration.

4. The Ministers might decide that there is merit in the Military Committee's
point. In this case, they could

(a) agree on a textual change in paragraph 61 and recommend that change to the
NDAG/DFC for approval;

(b) agree with the Military Committee's interpretation, reflect this in the
Agree Minute, but decide that no change of the Concept paper is necessary.

5. The Ministers could also refer the matter to the Permanent Representatives
for a submission of a report for the fall meeting or for a settlement at
Permanent Representatives level in the light of Ministerial guidance and further
national instructions.

6. Finally, the Ministers could conclude that no action is required on their
part.

7. The issue before the Ministers is a narrow one and well defined in the
Military Committee's report, so that there does not seem to be a need for
further clarification through an additional Permanent Representatives report. It
would appear that the Ministers will be in a position to take a decision in the
sense of paragraphs 4(b), 5 and 6 above without further multilateral
preparation. If, however, a decision along the lines of paragraph 4(a) above is
to be taken, i.e. if all national authorities concerned agree with the Military
Committee's proposal for a change of language in the Concept paper, it would
appear desirable for the Permanent Representatives to prepare in advance precise
language that might be adopted by the Ministers. The Military Committee's point
will probably be met if, in the last sentence of paragraph 61, the words "not
permit" were replaced by "seek to avoid", so that the sentence would begin as
follows: "Therefore, SACEUR should seek to avoid attrition of his strike forces
…".

8. Against this background I would propose that:
(a) there is no need for a report by the Permanent Representatives;
(b) nonetheless, delegations seek views from their national authorities as to
what decision might be taken by the Ministers and, if the decision is along the
lines of paragraph 4(a) above, whether the modification set out in paragraph 7
above is acceptable to them;

(c) the matter be brought up again for discussion at Staff Group and Permanent
Representatives levels at meetings late in April after national views are known.

(Signed) H. B. SEIM
