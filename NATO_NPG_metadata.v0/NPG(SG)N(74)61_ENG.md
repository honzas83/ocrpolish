> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 27th May, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on May 27, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The group noted the circulation of the 'Future Work Programme' (PO/74/50(Revised)), which was scheduled for the agenda of the NPG Ministerial meeting in June. The meeting also addressed the report on the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process, confirming a consensus on a revised version of paragraph 14 of the Annex to the report. This consensus included agreement from Belgium and Portugal, despite their absence from the meeting. The Staff Group agreed that the Permanent Representatives would be sought for endorsement of the final version of paragraph 14, and the report would be circulated as an NPC Document once approved. The group also discussed preparations for the meeting of NPG Ministers in Bergen, with the Chairman stating he would circulate a draft Final Communiqué and a draft framework for the Agreed Minute. Finally, the group agreed to hold its next meeting on Wednesday, June 5, 1974, in Brussels.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 27th
May, 1974

DOCUMENTATION

1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that PO/74/50(Revised), dated 22nd May, 1974, had been circulated; this
paper, entitled Future Work Programme, was on the Agenda for the NPG Ministerial
meeting in June.

REPORT ON THE MILITARY COMMITTEE FUNCTION IN THE NUCLEAR CONSULTATION PROCESS

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that, at the last
meeting, a new version of paragraph 14 of the Annex to the report(1) on the
Military Committee’s Function in the nuclear consultation process, had been
proposed; this new version consisted of the original paragraph 14 less the words
"and possibly" and "precise", and with a new text for the last sentence; members
had agreed to obtain instructions from their authorities;

(b) noted that all members now had instructions and that there was a consensus
in favour of the latest version of paragraph 14; noted also that the
representatives of Belgium and Portugal, although not present, had informed the
Chairman of their agreement;
