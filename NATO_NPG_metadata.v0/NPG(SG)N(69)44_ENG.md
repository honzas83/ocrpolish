> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters on Monday, 19th May, 1969
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met at NATO Headquarters on May 19, 1969, to address several key items. First, the group discussed and amended a proposed draft advance press release for an upcoming London meeting, instructing the International Staff to circulate the amended text. Second, the group coordinated logistical arrangements for the London meeting, including dinner at Hampton Court and the scheduling of drafting sessions. They also noted arrival times provided by the protocol office in London and a request from the United Kingdom Representative for aircraft arrival times. Third, the group discussed the method and timing for further work on the Anglo-German guidelines paper regarding the tentative political guidelines for the possible initial tactical use of tactical weapons by NATO. This discussion highlighted the need for combined political and military basis for studies, the timing of the next NPG Ministerial meeting relative to the North Atlantic Council meeting, and the overall programme of work leading up to submission to the Defence Planning Committee and the Ministers of Defence.

III. ANGLO-GERMAN GUIDELINES PAPER

Reference : NPG/D(69)2

3. With a view to preparing a decision on the date of the next NPG Ministerial
Session, the STAFF GROUP discussed briefly the method and timing of further work
on the Anglo-German paper on tentative political guidelines for the possible
initial tactical use of tactical weapons by NATO, and noted in particular that
consideration must be given to the following problems :

(a) the need to give further studies a combined political and military basis,
for example by inviting experts nominated by the Major NATO Commanders to attend
meetings of the Staff Group;

(b) the date of the next NPG Ministerial meeting (which if fixed too late in the
year would be too near the NDAC meeting, and if fixed too soon would leave
insufficient time for the studies);

(c) the programme of work (comments, re-write, review by capitals, presentation
to Permanent Representatives) and allocation of time for the different phases
leading up to submission of the document to the Defence Planning Committee and
then to the Ministers of Defence.
