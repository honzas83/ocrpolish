> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 22nd November, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on November 22, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to review the outcomes of a recent meeting in London. The group discussed the effectiveness of the simultaneous preparation of the Agreed Minute and Final Communiqué by Ministers and Permanent Representatives. The Chairman noted that the Phase III Follow-on Use Study, Theatre Nuclear Forces, and Technology Studies are closely linked and may require the involvement of other agencies. Regarding the Spring Meeting of NPG Ministers, the group noted that the proposed dates of June 8th and 9th, 1977, suggested by the Canadian Authorities, are acceptable but remain provisional. Additionally, the group examined the need to improve how NPG policy documents receive approval at the Defence Policy Committee (DPC) level, emphasizing the role of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC). The Staff Group requested the International Staff to prepare a paper on future work priorities and tasking based on the Agreed Minute of the London meeting. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next session on November 29, 1976.

SPRING MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
noted the remarks of the Chairman who said that the new dates of 8th and 9th
June, 1977 proposed by the Canadian Authorities appeared to be very acceptable;
nevertheless, these dates remained provisional until general agreement had been
reached; he hoped that such agreement would be reached shortly and he requested
the Canadian representative to try to obtain early information on the programme
proposed by his Authorities because this was most helpful to Ministers when
preparing their work schedules.

PROCESSING NPG DOCUMENTS
4. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the need to
examine how best NPG policy documents could receive full approval at DPC level;
the position of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) should not be
overlooked.

AGREED MINUTE(1) AID FUTURE WORK
5.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of initial comments on the Agreed Minute of the London meeting and
of a request by the Chairman that members should submit to him their lists of
future work set out in the priority they considered appropriate together with
their views on tasking;

(b) invited the International Staff to prepare a paper setting out, in the light
of the suggestions made, what work should be done by whom and when.

NEXT MEETING
6.  The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 29th November, 1976 at 3 p.m.
