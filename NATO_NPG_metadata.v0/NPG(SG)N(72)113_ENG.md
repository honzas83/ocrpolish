> [!abstract] 
> # Possible Staff Group Visit to the United States
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) regarding a potential visit to the United States in 1973. The Chairman refers to a meeting on 13th November 1972, where the Staff Group considered a visit during the period from 21st January to 18th February. The group had previously agreed to investigate official air transportation for transatlantic and internal U.S. travel. However, it appears unlikely that official airlift could be made available during the January-February timeframe. Consequently, the Chairman suggests that the Staff Group consider at their meeting on 8th January whether to use commercial air travel for a visit in late January or early February, or to postpone the visit to a later date in 1973 to utilize official airlift.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

POSSIBLE STAFF GROUP VISIT TO THE UNITED STATES

Note by the Chairman

You will recall that, at our meeting on 13th November, 1972, the Staff Group
reopened its consideration of a possible Staff Group visit to the United States
in 1973, perhaps sometime during the four-week period from 21st January to 18th
February(1). The Staff Group agreed that the official air transportation for the
transatlantic legs, and for internal movements within the U.S., should be
investigated, and invited members from those countries that may be in a position
to provide such transportation to make enquiries with their national
authorities.

2. Although delegations concerned have not, in all cases, received advice from
capitals, it appears unlikely that official airlift could be made available for
a Staff Group trip during the January–February timeframe. Therefore, the Staff
Group may wish to consider at our meeting on 8th January whether to try to
schedule a visit in late January or early February, using commercial air travel,
or to try to work out visit arrangements at a later date in 1973 using official
airlift.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM

1110 Brussels

(1) NPG(Staff Group)N(72)103, 13th November, 1972
