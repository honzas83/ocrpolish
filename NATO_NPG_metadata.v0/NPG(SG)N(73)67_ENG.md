> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on 30th July, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on 30th July 1973 at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The group welcomed new arrivals from the Canadian and United Kingdom delegations. Discussions focused on the revision of a Working Paper on consultation procedures and facilities, with the group agreeing that a shorter status report would be more suitable for Ministers while the detailed report would serve as background for Permanent Representatives. The group also examined the second revision of the Working Paper on Operational Concepts and Doctrine, agreeing to final changes and planning for its circulation to capitals. Additionally, the group noted thes distribution of reports from SHAPE and the Military Committee regarding the early combined use of conventional and nuclear capabilities. A representative from the United States discussed tactical nuclear doctrine and the contributions of the Nuclear Planning Group to the Follow-on Use Work Programme. Finally, the group accepted a proposal from the Netherlands representative to reschedule the first meeting of the Study Team on Warsaw Pact strategy and doctrine to 4th September 1973.

WELCOME
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the presence of the following new arrivals who would be
participating from time to time at future Staff Group meetings:

- Colonel F.N. Sheffield, Canadian Delegation,
- Mr. R.C. Harford, United Kingdom Delegation.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the latest
version(1) of the Working Paper on consultation procedures and facilities,
revised in the light of previous discussions; he indicated the various changes
and additions and suggested that, despite the absence of representatives of
Belgium, Italy and Turkey, the Staff Group might wish to continue to discuss the
form and content of the paper with the aim of reaching agreement during the next
two or three weeks upon a text in the form of an NP Working Paper for
distribution to capitals; he noted with appreciation the presence of members of
the Council Operations and Communications Directorate;

(b) took note of the opinions expressed in the ensuing discussion and in
particular of general support for the view that the revised report might be too
detailed, technical and lengthy for use by Ministers;

(c) agreed that a short substantive status report would be more suitable for

usefully be attached as annex to the proposed status report to Ministers;

of handling messages, with special reference to re-programming of computers;

(g) invited the Chairman to continue his discussions with elements of the
International Staff, the International Military Staff and Major NATO Commands
with a view to facilitating positive action regarding improvements to
consultation procedures and facilities;

(h) noted with appreciation the remarks of the Chairman who said that he hoped
to be able to prepare a draft status report, and further revision of the Working
Paper during the next ten days.

EARLY COMBINED USE OF CONVENTIONAL AND NUCLEAR CAPABILITIES

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that the SHAPE and Military Committee reports on early combined use of
our conventional and nuclear capabilities would be distributed during the next
24 hours(1);

(b) noted with appreciation that the Chairman intended shortly to circulate a
note on these reports to serve as a basis for a discussion of the matter at the
next meeting.

FOLLOW-UP TO THE DEP MINISTERIAL MEETING

5. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the United States representative who referred to a
discussion that had taken place in the Defence Planning Committee on 12th July
concerning basic issues of NATO defence planning that had raised at the Spring
DPC Ministerial meeting; with respect to developing tactical nuclear doctrine,
he wished to make it clear that United States reference to the Phase II Study
Team of the Follow-on Use Work Programme was intended only to illustrate how the
on-going activity of the NPG would contribute and was not intended to imply that
the whole task should be reserved for the Phase II Team; his authorities
recognised that other aspects of current NPG activity would also make useful
contributions to the overall objective.

STUDY OF WARSAW PACT STRATEGY AND DOCTRINE
6. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Netherlands representative who said that an
initial paper had now been prepared by his authorities who proposed that the
first meeting of the Study Team should take place in the League on Tuesday, 4th
September, 1973, not on 6th September as originally suggested; this change of
date was accepted.

NEXT MEETING
7. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 6th August, 1973, at 3 p.m.
1110 Brussels.
