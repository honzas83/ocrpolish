> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 22nd April, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on April 22, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group discussed documentation regarding a press release for the Spring 1974 Ministerial Meeting, arrangements for a Technology Study, and the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process. They agreed in principle to the proposed dates of November 7th and 8th for the NPG Ministerial Meeting in Rome. The group also coordinated briefings at SHAPE on April 23rd. A significant portion of the meeting was part of a detailed review of a draft report on Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy and military doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear weapons, incorporating intelligence assessments and amendments from the United Kingdom and Canada. The group discussed the future work programme and the opportunity for NPG countries to indicate specific selective use options for future studies. Regarding the Spring Ministerial Meeting, they clarified that a policy statement by Norway on nuclear and base policy would not set a precedent for other host countries. Finally, the group addressed requests from Turkey and Canada to discuss the shift in United States nuclear deterrent policy and targeting policy for strategic forces at the upcoming May 16th meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives.

SPRING MINISTERIAL MEETING
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) in answer to a query by the United Kingdom representative, confirmed that
the inclusion of an item on the agenda of the Spring Ministerial Meeting dealing
with Norwegian nuclear and base policy did not set a precedent requiring host
countries at future meetings to make similar policy statements;

(b) noted that the Norwegian representative expected to receive from his
authorities in the near future an outline programme for the information of the
participating countries.

U.S. NUCLEAR DETERRENT POLICY
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Turkish representative expressing the strong desire
of his Ambassador that the subject of US nuclear deterrent policy, particularly
the shift in targeting policy for strategic forces, be discussed in a forum in
which the Turkish Minister of Defence (who would not be present at Bergen) could
participate, that is, either the June DPC Ministerial meeting or a special
Ministerial meeting of the NDAC to be scheduled in June for that purpose. His
Ambassador hoped the NPG Permanent Representatives could address this question
at their meeting on 16th May;

(b) noted a comment by the Canadian representative who said his Ambassador had
expressed an interest in discussing the substance of the targeting policy shift
at the 16th May meeting.

DATE OF NEXT MEETING
8. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 29th April, 1974 at 3 p.m.
NATO, 1110 Brussels.
