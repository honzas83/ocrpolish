> # Initiation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme
> 
> This document, a working paper from the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines proposals for the initiation and implementation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme. The Follow-on Use Work Programme is designed to explore the implications and interactions of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons across various contingencies. The programme is divided into three phases: Phase I involves the preparation of basic data inputs and studies; Phase II focuses on the comparative analysis of these inputs, synthesis of findings, and identification of policy questions; and Phase III involves the formulation of political guidelines for approval. The document suggests that Phase II should be initiated at the Spring 1972 meeting of the Ministers to ensure a substantive report can be presented by the Spring 1973 meeting. It discusses timing, objectives, and organizational arrangements, noting that the bulk of Phase I work, including regional studies, will be completed by the end of 1972. The author, H.B. Seim, suggests that the primary focus of Phase II should be on policy aspects rather than exhaustive technical analysis. The document also mentions the current status of various regional studies, including those for Turkey and the Mediterranean Maritime, as well as the Danish and ACLANT studies.

Purpose
This report submits for Ministerial consideration certain proposals for the
initiation and implementation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme,
in response to the request made by the Ministers at their meeting in Brussels on
26th and 27th October, 1971(1).

Background
2. The Follow-on Use Work Programme, approved by the Ministers in November 1969,
is designed to explore and spell out the implications and interactions of
follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons over the range of possible
contingencies and options. As one of its objectives, the Programme is expected
to assist the Ministers in evaluating and, where necessary, refining existing
guidance and elaborating further guidance for the NATO Military Authorities(2).

3. In this context, it has been agreed that the work Programme will consist of
three major parts:

Phase I : preparation of basic data inputs in the form of studies, and related
comments from the NDAC countries and the NATO Military Authorities;

Each of these phases covers a part of the process that is distinctly different

4. With respect to the general objective to be sought in Phase II, the NFG has
agreed that the first step would be a careful comparative analysis of all of the
completed regional and other studies, together with any comments that have been
submitted. At the same time, the NFG noted certain considerations that would
have to be taken into account during this analytical effort, such as the need to
apply great caution in drawing generally applicable conclusions. The end product
of the process of comparative analysis would be a synthesis of findings, the
formulation of policy questions of a general nature and the definition of
possible policy choices(2).

5. Insofar as organisational arrangements for Phase II are concerned, the NFG
has already noted that analysis and synthesis could be undertaken initially
either by the NFG collectively or, on its behalf, by a small number of NPG
countries, perhaps with collaboration of the NATO Military Authorities. The NFG

questions and the possible policy choices(1).

Discussion
6. In addressing the question of starting Phase II, it may be convenient to
examine the aspects of timing, objective and organisation separately, although
it should be recognised that these aspects are all closely related to each
other.

emphasis during 1971 has been placed on Phase I, the preparation of basic data
inputs. The Ministers have already discussed four of the eight regional studies
included in the initial work, and it is anticipated that the remaining four will
be placed on Ministerial agendas during 1972, i.e., the Turkish and
Mediterranean Maritime Studies in the Spring

11. Objective. The question might be raised whether it is necessary or desirable
to define the objective of the Phase II effort more precisely than has been
stated in already approved documents(1). While in theory there may be some
benefit to be gained in providing more specific terms of reference, there is
also a possibility that too much detail might unduly restrict the flexibility
that should be granted to those who will undertake the task. Furthermore, it can
be expected that all who participate in the Phase II work will be well apprised
of the overall objectives of the Work Programme and of the role of Phase II in
that Programme, and that they will be able to shape their efforts to be
responsive to those objectives. On balance, it would seem that no additional
instructions in this regard are needed, although it could perhaps be agreed that
the primary focus should be on the policy aspects and that an exhaustive
technical analysis is not intended.

12. Organisation. The decision on the organisational pattern to be used in
carrying out Phase II must take into account a number of considerations, some of
which are conflicting. Among these are the requirement to keep the analytical
group from becoming so large and unwieldy that it becomes inefficient; the
desire to involve participants from national capitals; the need to provide a
full opportunity to all interested NPG countries to make national inputs to the
analytical work, to contribute to the final report before it is placed on the
Ministerial agenda and, for those countries not participating at Ministerial
level, to make known any particular national views that they feel should be
taken into account during the Ministerial discussion; and the desire to
facilitate a meaningful input to the process from the NATO Military Authorities.

13. Although some of these considerations could be satisfied by assigning to the
Permanent Representatives the task of conducting Phase II, others would not
necessarily be met, particularly if primary reliance were placed on the Staff
Group to do the initial analytical work. Furthermore, not all of the eleven NPG
countries have the capability and desire to become actively involved in the
comparative analysis step. In these circumstances, it is suggested that the
approach employed in Phase I of the Work Programme might be adapted for use in
Phase II. The NPG might commission a small ad hoc study team to carry out the
comparative analysis and to prepare a report containing the general findings and
identifying the policy questions, much in the same way that similar study teams
prepared the various

Recommendations
15. In light of the foregoing, it is recommended that the Ministers:
a. agree that Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme should be initiated
immediately after the Spring 1972 Ministerial meeting;

b. agree that an ad hoc study team composed of participants from ----, ----,
---- and ---- (with ---- acting as discussion leader), assisted by
representatives of NATO military commands as appropriate, should be charged with
the task of conducting the comparative analysis and preparing the Phase II
report, for consideration by the Ministers at their meeting in the Spring of
1973 provided that Ministerial consideration of the eight regional studies in
Phase I will have been completed by the end of the Autumn 1972 Ministerial
meeting;

c. establish a target date of 1st February, 1973 for circulating the Phase II
report to NPG countries, in order to provide an opportunity for multilateral
consideration at Permanent Representatives' level;

d. urge the NATO Military Authorities and those countries who plan to submit
comments on Phase I studies to do so as expeditiously as possible, in order to
facilitate the work of the study team.
