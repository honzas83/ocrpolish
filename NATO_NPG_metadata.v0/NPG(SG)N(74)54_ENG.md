> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 13th May, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on May 13, 1974, in Brussels to coordinate preparations for the upcoming NPG Ministerial meeting in Norway. The group discussed the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process and agreed to submit a report to Permanent Representatives on May 16 without changes. They also addressed a proposed change to a report on the Warsaw Pact's politico-military strategy and military doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear weapons. Regarding future work, the United States representative suggested delaying new illustrative studies until after the Autumn meeting. The group also reviewed the progress of an ad hoc group studying the political and military implications of technological developments concerning tactical nuclear weapons. Additionally, the group discussed the revisions needed for the Future Work Programme to convert it from a paper for Permanent Representatives to a paper for Ministers. The meeting also touched upon the agenda for the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives on May 16, including U.S. nuclear deterrent policy and the opportunity for non-attending countries to exchange views. The Italian member mentioned the intention of the Italian Minister to invite the group to hold its Autumn 1974 Ministerial meeting in Rome. Finally, the group established deadlines for the circulation of documentation for the Spring Meeting, specifically mentioning documents NPG/WP(74)4 and NPG/WP(74)5, and scheduled their next meeting for May 20, 1974.

DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that the agenda for the meeting of NPG Ministers in Norway had recently
been circulated under reference NPG/A(74)3 dated 9th May, 1974.

MILITARY COMMITTEE FUNCTION IN THE NUCLEAR CONSULTATION PROCESS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to the draft report(1)
on the Military Committee Function in the Nuclear Consultation Process and to
the recently received comments(2) by the Chairman, Military Committee, who had
recommended some changes to paragraph 14 of the annex to the report;

(b) noted, during the ensuing discussion, that most members had received the
agreement of their authorities to the original text of the report, and agreed
that it should be submitted to the Permanent Representatives on 16th May without
change.

STUDY ON WARSAW PACT POLITICO-MILITARY STRATEGY AND MILITARY DOCTRINE FOR THE
TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the proposed
change(1) to paragraph 3 of the Annex to the draft report by Permanent
Representatives on the Warsaw Pact Study;

(b) agreed to the proposed change and invited the International Staff to
circulate a corrigendum in time for the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives
on 16th May.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME(2)

Illustrative Studies

4. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the United States representative who suggested that
the NPG should not commission a new illustrative study until after the Autumn
meeting; this would still give enough time for it to be ready for the Autumn
meeting of the following year.

Implications of Technological Developments

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement made by the United States representative in reply
to a question by the Canadian representative regarding the progress of the ad
hoc group for the Study of the political and military implications of
technological developments concerning the tactical use of nuclear weapons;

(b) noted that the United States hoped to circulate a paper on the organisation
and conduct of the study before the Ministerial meeting in Bergen;

(c) discussed whether the wording of paragraph 2 (Implications of Technological
Developments) in the Summary of possible NPG Future Work(1) should be modified
to explain the apparent lack of progress; agreed to await possible Permanent
Representatives'comments.

Revision of the Future Work Programme

6. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that a revised version of the
Future Work Programme would have to be made, not only to take into account
possible Permanent Representatives comments, but also because a number of
editorial changes were necessary to convert it from being a PO for Permanent
Representatives to becoming a paper for Ministers; it was important that this
revised version be ready and circulated not later than 24th May.

MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES ON THURSDAY,
16TH MAY, 1974

7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman concerning Items IV, V and VI on
the agenda for the meeting of Permanent Representatives to be held on 16th May;
he made the following points:

(1) Item IV (U.S. Nuclear Deterrent Policy): the exchange of messages between
SACEUR and the U.S. Secretary of Defence(2) was relevant,

(2) Item V (Exchange of Views): this would give an opportunity for countries not
attending the forthcoming NPG Ministerial Meeting to make known their views on
matters on the ministerial agenda;

(b) took note of the statement by the Italian member that his Ambassador would,
under Any Other Business, mention the intention of his Minister to invite the
NPG to hold its Autumn 1974 Ministerial meeting in Rome on 7th and 8th November.

DOCUMENTATION FOR THE MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS IN JUNE
8. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who reminded the Staff Group that all
documentation for the Spring Meeting should be circulated not later than 24th
May; this applied particularly to the issue in document form of NPG/WP(74)4 and
NPG/WP(74)5; if these two papers were not finalised on Thursday 16th May it
might be necessary on Friday 17th May to hold a meeting of the Staff Group, or
to establish some informal contacts, in order to complete this work in good
time.

NEXT MEETING OF STAFF GROUP
9. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 20th May, 1974.
NATO, 1110 Brussels.
