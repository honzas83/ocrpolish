> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 14th June, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on June 14, 1971, to address several administrative and strategic items. The group welcomed new members Captain Ernest R. Barrett of the US Navy and Colonel Robert M. Krone of the US Air Force. The meeting included a discussion on the logistics for an upcoming visit to the Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE). Regarding regional studies, the group noted progress on a study concerning a limited battle area in Denmark, co-led by Denmark and the United Kingdom with participation from Germany, Norway, and NATO Military Authorities, which is expected to be completed by autumn 1972. Additionally, the group discussed the nuclear aspects of the Allied Defence (AD) 70 follow-up action, reviewing an International Staff paper and discussing the managerial arrangements for handling these questions. The Staff Group agreed to carry out the managerial functions outlined in the International Staff's paper and invited the International Staff to prepare a paper identifying unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence. Members were also asked to inform the International Staff of current work being undertaken in their respective capitals and headquarters. The meeting concluded with the agreement to continue discussions on June 28 and to hold the next meeting on June 21, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 14th June, 1971
WELCOME
1. The STAFF GROUP :
unanimously endorsed a statement made by the Chairman who welcomed Captain
Ernest R. Barrett, US Navy, who had joined the International Military Staff's
Nuclear Policy team and would be taking part in the work of the Staff Group, and
Colonel Robert M. Krone, US Air Force, of SHAPE, who was taking over from
Colonel Pustay.

STAFF GROUP VISIT TO SHAPE
2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the SHAPE representative who outlined the
programme for the visit;

(b) noted that the departure of the bus from NATO would be at 8 a.m. from the
Press Entrance on Wednesday, 16th June.

NUCLEAR ASPECTS OF AD 70 FOLLOW-UP ACTION

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who outlined the contents of the
International Staff's initial paper on the nuclear aspects of AD 70 Follow-up
Action(1) and recalled the proposal that the NPG Permanent Representatives
should meet sometime before the summer holidays to confirm the managerial
arrangements for handling these questions and to give guidance to the Staff
Group upon further work; noted also the Chairman's view that DPC Ministers would
require a report on this problem in December;

(b) agreed that the Permanent Representatives and the Staff Group should carry
out the managerial functions set out in the International Staff's paper;

(c) noted that some members considered that work being undertaken or to be
undertaken in the foreseeable future under the aegis of the Nuclear Planning
Group, by other agencies and by nations, probably covered most if not all of the
nuclear problems raised in Annex I to the AD 70s Report(2) and also to a certain
extent the unresolved problems described in PO/71/226;

(d) noted a general feeling that it was therefore unnecessary to submit a
revised version of the International Staff's paper referred to in paragraph 4(a)
above to the Permanent Representatives, but that the Staff Group should proceed
now to carry out the actions proposed in paragraph 9(b) of the International
Staff's paper(1);

(e) recalled however that Permanent Representatives had been charged by
Ministers at Mittelwald(1) with preparing suggestions for investigating
unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence to be considered by
Ministers at their next meeting; agreed that there was a clear link between

Authorities to inform the International Staff as soon as possible of present

(h) agreed that, in the light of the information obtained (see sub-paragraphs
(f) and (g) above), it should then be determined what further work should be
undertaken, with a view to submitting proposals to the NPG Permanent
Representative for endorsement;

(i) noted that several members appeared to be doubtful as to the need to prepare
a detailed report on nuclear matters for inclusion in the AD 70 follow-on report
to Ministers in December, 1971;

(j) agreed to continue the discussion at its meeting on Monday, 28th June.

NEXT MEETING

5. The STAFF GROUP:
