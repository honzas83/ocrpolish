> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters on Wednesday, 4th June, 1969
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) met at NATO Headquarters on June 4, 1969, to coordinate follow-on actions resulting from the London Ministerial meeting. The group discussed several key areas, including the desire of most delegations to hold an early ambassadorial meeting regarding a United States paper on Consultation, though the United States representative argued that such a meeting was not provided for in the Agreed Minute. Regarding Tactical Use and the Anglo-German Guidelines paper, the group noted requests for specific language changes and national comments, which the United States representative opposed. The group also agreed on a proposed timetable for the NFG autumn Ministerial meeting and discussed the terms of reference for longer-term studies and the Air Defence Missile (ADM) problem in certain selected areas of ACE. On the subject of Strategic Use, the the group agreed to a timetable for receipt of SACEUR's recommendations regarding the aircraft/missile mix and the dual-capability programme. Finally, the group decided that publishing an official Nuclear Planning Group document on follow-on actions was undesirable and instead opted to circulate a revised draft on an unofficial basis. The next meeting was scheduled for June 16, 1969.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held at NATO Headquarters
on Wednesday, 4th June, 1969.

ORGANISATION OF LONDON MEETING

1. The CHAIRMAN, on behalf of the STAFF GROUP, expressed his thanks to the
United Kingdom representative for the excellent arrangements made in London for
the recent NEP Ministerial meeting.

FOLLOW-ON ACTION AFTER NEP MINISTERIAL MEETING IN LONDON

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a first draft of a paper (1) outlining the follow-on action to
be taken as a result of the decisions taken at the Ministerial meeting held in
London;

(b) with regard to the subject of Consultation,
(i) noted that it was the desire of most of the delegations to hold an early
ambassadorial meeting in order to give them an opportunity to express their
views on the U.S. paper as stated by several Ministers in London. Due to the
late arrival of the paper, Ministers had not had sufficient time to study it and
were in no position to comment upon it;

(ii) noted a statement by the United States representative to the effect that
such an early meeting was not provided for in the Agreed Minute and should not
take place. The stipulation in the Agreed Minute that Consultation should remain
on the Group's agenda referred to Ministerial, not the Permanent Representative
level;
