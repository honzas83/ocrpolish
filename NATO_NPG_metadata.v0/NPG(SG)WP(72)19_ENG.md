> # Revision of the Status Report on the Follow-on Use Work Programme
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, dated 27th September 1972, regarding the revision of the status report on the Follow-on Use Work Programme. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, notes that the United States has communicated that an air battle study should not be undertaken at this time due to the complexities involved and the lack of available analytical tools to perform an in-depth examination of the tactical use of nuclear weapons by NATO air forces. Consequently, the Chairman proposes modifications to the status report to be considered by the NPG Permanent Representatives on 5th October, including the deletion of expectations for a preliminary assessment from the United States. The proposed modifications suggest that no further action be taken toward making arrangements for a specific study of the air battle in the Central Region, while the United States intends to continue investigating the possibility of developing improved techniques and methodologies for future analysis. The document includes references to NPG/WP(72)9 and NPG/D(72)10.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
REVISION OF THE STATUS REPORT ON THE FOLLOW-ON USE WORK PROGRAMME
Note by the Chairman
Staff Group members will recall that the draft Status Report on the Follow-on
Use Work Programme, to be considered by the NPG Permanent Representatives on 5th
October, states that a preliminary assessment of methodology and resource
requirements for the air battle study from the United States is expected in the
near future(1). A letter on this subject has now been received from the United
States(2), which in substance concludes that the air battle study should not be
undertaken at this time.

2. Since it would appear to be appropriate for the Permanent Representatives'
status report to reflect this development, I have attached some proposed
modifications for Staff Group consideration at our next meeting, with a view to
circulating a corrigendum or a revised Working Paper before the 5th October
Permanent Representatives' meeting.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM

PROPOSED MODIFICATIONS TO NPG/WP(72)9
1. Paragraph 5: delete the last sentence, and add:
"The response by the United States(1) indicates that the complexities involved
in an air study are greater than originally anticipated, and that the analytical
tools now available cannot accomplish the desired in-depth examination of the
tactical use of nuclear weapons by NATO air forces. The United States has
therefore concluded that it does not appear likely that at this stage air study
could be commissioned by the NPG which would hold reasonable promise of
producing any substantial amount of new and meaningful information which would
contribute to the formulation of follow-on use guidelines. Nevertheless,
convinced of the important benefits that would derive from a comprehensive and
credible analysis of the rôle of air forces in the tactical use of nuclear
weapons, the United States intends to continue to investigate the possibility of
developing improved techniques and methodologies for analysing the multifold
complexities involved in this problem and will report any progress to the NPG.
In view of the foregoing, the Permanent Representatives suggest that no further
action should be taken at this time toward making arrangements for a specific
study of the air battle in the Central Region."

2. Paragraph 8, change to read:
"8. The Ministers may wish to: (a) take note of this report; (b) agree that no
further action should be taken at this time toward making arrangements for a
specific study of the air battle in the Central Region(2)."
