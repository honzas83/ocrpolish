> # Senior Officers' Orientation Course
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) has issued a notice regarding the Senior Officers' Orientation Course (short title: I-31), conducted by the NATO School (SHAPE) in Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarize senior NATO officers and Colonel-rank officers and civilian officials of equivalent grade on a need-to-know basis with the major concepts of NATO weapons employment in Allied Command Europe. A COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance is required for attendance. The document provides a schedule of twelve courses scheduled for 1977, ranging from January 17th to December 2nd. Some courses are listed as having French translation available, and others are conducted in English only. The course lasts one week from Monday through Friday. Application processes differ based on the location of the personnel: officials in national delegations at NATO Headquarters in Brussels are to apply through the Nuclear Planning Directorate of the International Staff, while officials in capitals and national commands should apply through their Ministry of Defence. Due to limited space and high demand, early application is at most a month in advance, and a 'Joining Report' must be sent to the NATO School (SHAPE) at least four weeks prior to the course. For further information, personnel may contact Mr. Serio or Miss Winters at the Nuclear Planning Directorate.

Note by the Chairman

The subject course (short title: I-31) is conducted by the NATO School (SHAPE)
(NS(S)), Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarise
senior NATO officers and key personnel in staff appointments with the major
concepts of NATO weapons employment in Allied Command Europe. Officers of the
rank of Colonel and above and civilian officials of equivalent grade are
eligible to attend on a need-to-know basis. A COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance is
required. The annotation F in the last column below shows courses for which
French translation will be available; other courses will be conducted in English
only. The course lasts one week – Monday through Friday. The following courses
are scheduled for 1977:

2. The course provides a general orientation and background that is considered
useful for those involved in NFG work; it may also be of interest to other
senior officials in delegations and in capitals. Officials in national
delegations located at NATO Headquarters in Brussels, who are interested in
attending one of the courses listed above, should apply through the Nuclear
Flaming Directorate of the International Staff which acts as the point of
contact with SHAPE and the NS(S) at Oberammergau for obtaining spaces for
personnel in delegations and on the International Staff. Officials in capitals
and national commands should apply through their Ministry of Defence.

3. The number of spaces in each course is limited and the demand is usually
quite heavy. Only one or two spaces for each course are usually allocated to
NATO officials. Therefore, it is advisable to apply well in advance of the
desired course, i.e., at least a month before. This also allows time for
submission of the "Joining Report" which must be sent to NS(S) at least four
weeks prior to commencement of the course.

4. The Nuclear Planning Directorate would be pleased to provide further
information or to obtain quotas. Please contact Mr. Serio on extension 204E, or
Miss Winters on extension 2027.
