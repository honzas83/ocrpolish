> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 25th November, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on November 25, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to coordinate preparations for the upcoming December meeting of NPG Ministers. The group discussed and agreed upon the agenda for the Ministerial meeting, which includes a briefing on the Strategic balance by Mr. Schlesinger and discussions on the nuclear aspects of the Nunn amendment. Other agenda items include Selective Employment Planning and the Future Work Programme. The group also addressed security arrangements, requiring COSMIC TOP SECRET ATOMAL clearance for certain items. The Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) was noted as being unable to hold a meeting in December, but it was agreed that the yearly report on Nuclear Planning Group activities would be sent to theAC. The Additionally, the group noted that the Phase II Report on Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons was ready for circulation as a Working Paper. Discussions also touched upon the military assessment of the Nunn amendment and a technological study on the tactical use of nuclear weapons. Finally, the group scheduled its next meeting for December 2, 1974, and noted a farewell party for Colonel Rolf Thyse.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 25th
November, 1974

CURRENT WORK

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who reviewed the current NPG
situation in relation particularly to the forthcoming Ministerial meeting in
December, and to problems connected with NPG membership;

(b) noted that a revised version of the Future Work Programme(1) would be
circulated shortly.

DECEMBER MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS

Timing

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note that two dates had been suggested for a short meeting of the NPG
Ministers during the week beginning 9th December; these were

(i) Tuesday, 10th December, in the morning, before the start of the one and a
half-day DPC meeting,

(ii) Wednesday, 11th December, from approximately 3 p.m. to 5 p.m.

(b) noted that there was some difference of opinion as to which date would be

Agenda
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted with appreciation the remarks of the United States representative who
said that Mr. Schlesinger maintained his offer to give a briefing on the
Strategic balance; his authorities would also be prepared to discuss the nuclear
aspects of the Nump amendment; agreed that these two matters would be items I
and II on the NPG Ministerial Agenda;

(b) agreed that Selective Employment Planning should become item III but would
be addressed only if time permitted;

(c) agreed that the rest of the agenda should be limited to the Future Work
Programme, Any Other Business and the Communiqué;

(d) invited the Chairman to circulate the agenda immediately so that Permanent
Representatives, at the DPC meeting next day, would know what had been decided
concerning the content of the NPG Agenda.

Agreed Minute and Communicé
4. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed with the proposals of the Chairman concerning the method to be adopted
for the preparation of the communiqué and the Agreed Minute;

NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of views expressed concerning the feasibility of holding a meeting
of the NDAC in December and agreed that, although it was desirable to
acknowledge the continuing existence of the NDAC, a meeting of this body did not
appear practicable in the short time available to Ministers;

(b) agreed that the yearly report on Nuclear Planning Group Activities should be
prepared by the NPG and sent to the NDAC during the course of December; it was
agreed that the NDAC should be invited to approve this report by the silence
procedure.

PHASE II OF THE STUDY ON FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS BY NATO
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted with appreciat on a statement by the United States representative who
said that the Phase II Report on Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons was
now ready and would be given this day to the International Staff for
reproduction and circulation;

(b) agreed that this report should be circulated as a Working Paper for comments
by Capitals; the report would subsequently be amended by the three-country Study
Team in the light of the comments and would then be the subject of a report by
Permanent Representatives;

(c) expressed the hope that it would be possible to include the matter in the
agenda for the Spring meeting of NPG Ministers.

STUDY OF THE POLITICAL AND MILITARY IMPLICATIONS OF TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENTS
