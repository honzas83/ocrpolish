> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Friday, 11th March, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on March 11, 1977, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels, Belgium. The meeting's primary focus was the logistical arrangements for the upcoming NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting in Canada, including travel, hotel accommodations, and planned visits to the Canadian Forces Air Base at Cold Lake and Whitehorse, Yukon territory. The group also noted a statement by the German representative regarding available seats on military flights from Cologne/Bonn. Documentation matters were included, where the Chairman recommended that a draft report to the North Atlantic Council (NDAC) on NPG activities be filed without further processing. The group also scheduled a field trip for May 9-10, 1977. Additionally, the meeting addressed WINTEX 77 and the Manual of Crisis Management, and finalized revisions to the Maritime Nuclear Warfare Study, agreeing to prepare a Draft Report in the form of a two-language NPC Working Paper for immediate circulation.

WINTEX 77
5. The STAFF GROUP:
noted the remarks of the Chairman who drew attention to Chapter V of the Manual
of Crisis Management and recommended that the attention of Deputy Permanent
Representatives should be drawn to this publication.

MARITIME NUCLEAR WARFARE STUDY
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that at the last
meeting the US member had interrupted the silence procedure to provide his
Authorities with additional time and that the Staff Group had invited the
International Staff to circulate a further revision of the draft Permanent
Representatives' Report(1) which would give all Capitals an opportunity to
evaluate the US representative's alternative language proposal in a full and
proper context;

(b) examined the revised text with particular reference to the text proposed by
the US for the last sentences of paragraph 3 of the draft;

(c) agreed to certain changes and invited the International Staff to prepare the
Draft Report in the form of a two-language NPC Working Paper for immediate
circulation(2);

(d) agreed that if no changes had been proposed by 25th March, 1977, the
'Working Paper would be circulated in the form of an NPC Document.

NEXT MEETING
7. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to leave the date of its next meeting open.
