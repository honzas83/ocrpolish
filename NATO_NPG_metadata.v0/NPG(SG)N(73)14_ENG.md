> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 19th February, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on 19 February 1973 at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to coordinate preparations for the next meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives and the upcoming NPG Ministerial meeting in Ankara. The group discussed the distribution of documentation, including the ACLANT Follow-on Study and reports on operational concepts and early combined use of nuclear and conventional weapons. The meeting covered logistical arrangements for the Ministerial meeting, such as the possibility of charter flights to Ankara and the transport of classified documents. The Staff Group also reviewed the progress of agenda items for the Spring 1973 Ministerial Meeting, including a briefing on the balance of strategic forces by the United States Secretary of Defense. The Additionally, the group considered potential agenda items for the Autumn 1973 meeting, such as the monitoring of progress on the ADM question and consultation procedures. The meeting concluded by scheduling the next Staff Group meeting for 26 February 1973, noting that the meeting on 12 March would be cancelled due to WINTEX 73 activities.

NEXT MEETINGS OF NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

1. The Staff Group:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled
that the next meeting of the NPG Permanent
Representatives would take place in the Presentation
Room on Thursday, 22nd February, 1973 at 3.30 p.m.;
a presentation of the ACLANT Area Follow-on Study
would be given by the joint Netherlands/United
Kingdom Study Team followed by a questions and
discussion period;
(b) noted that it was unlikely that any matters would
be raised under "Any Other Business" at this
meeting;
(c) took note of the further remarks of the Chairman
who recalled that a meeting of Permanent Represent-
atives would be necessary in mid-April so that
final action could be taken on, and approval given
to the various reports and documents required for the
meeting of NPG Ministers on 15th and 16th May, 1973;
the date of the Permanent Representatives' meeting
should not be so early in April that Capitals would
not have enough time to study the papers, nor so
late in April that the final versions of the papers
could not be sent to all concerned in good time;
furthermore this meeting should not clash with the
dates of the meeting of the Phase II Study Team
(10th-12th April); he therefore suggested Monday,
16th April as a possible date for the next Permanent
Representatives' meeting; the International Staff
would investigate the suitability of this date and
report back to the Staff Group at the next meeting;

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN ANKARA

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled
that the Staff Group had invited the International
Staff to investigate, in co-operation with the
Turkish representative, the possibilities of
organising a charter flight to Ankara and back; noted
that in view of the limited staffs attending NPG
Ministerial meetings, and the use made of aircraft
provided for Ministers, that it appeared most
unlikely that the number of persons who would use a
charter flight would be sufficient to make the
operation economically worthwhile; agreed therefore
not to pursue this option;
(b) noted with appreciation the offer by the Netherlands
representative to investigate the possibility of
transporting the NATO Classified Documents courier
in the aircraft of the Netherlands Minister.

REVIEW OF PREPARATIONS FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING

briefing;
(iii) the Follow-up on PO/71/226: it had been hoped
that one or both of the two reports(2) by the
Military Authorities might have been available
but this was now highly unlikely; work on a Military

PREPARATIONS FOR THE AUTUMN 1973 NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who, looking forward to the Autumn
1973 NPG meeting, suggested that the work programme and agenda should be based
on the following considerations:

(i) no more follow-on studies were foreseen,
(ii) although Phase II would be well advanced, it was unlikely that a final
report could be available so soon,

(iii) the problem of Consultation procedures and facilities would still be
continuing, particularly with regard to timely warning and the provision of
information,

(iv) the monitoring of progress on the ADM question required further attention,
(v) the Military Authorities report on early combined use would be available,
(vi) an illustrative study might be required,
(vii) a briefing on the strategic balance would, hopefully, be given;
(b) took note of the views expressed in the subsequent discussion and agreed to
continue the discussion at a future meeting.

NEXT AND FUTURE MEETINGS OF THE STAFF GROUP

7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 26th February, 1973 at 3 p.m.;
(b) agreed to continue to meet on Mondays with the exception of Monday, 12th
March, which would be taken up by WINTEX 73 activities.
