> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on 25th April, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on 25 April 1975 at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group discussed the nuclear aspects of the Nunn amendment, noting that the unclassified summary version of the Nunn report was acceptable to Permanent Representatives. Regarding the NPG Ministerial meeting in Honiton, the group reviewed arrangements for air transportation from Travis AFB to Andrews AFB and transport and accommodation details. The group also finalized the agenda for the NPG Ministerial meeting, agreeing to maintain the content as set out in NPG(Staff Group):N(75)2, while excluding the Air Defence Illustrative Study. They expressed hope that the United States would provide a progress report on the Nunn amendment and that SACMR and SACLANT would be able to answer questions on Selective Employment Planning. The group further reviewed the 3rd revision of the draft report on exercise VINTEX 75, incorporating changes from the Canadian representative and other members. Finally, the group scheduled the next meetings of the Permanent Representatives for 12 May and 3 June 1975, and the next Staff Group meetings for 2 May and 5 May 1975.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on 25th April,
1975

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared for the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

NUCLEAR ASPECTS OF THE NUNN AMENDMENT

2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that, at a recent informal
meeting, the Permanent Representatives had indicated to the United States
Ambassador that the unclassified summary version of the Nunn report was
acceptable; the Chairman understood that this report was on its way to
Washington.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN HONITERY

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the United States representative who gave some
details regarding arrangements for the lion'ery meeting;

(b) took note that replies should be sent as soon as possible to the United
States delegation concerning:

AGENDA FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING
4. THE STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman concerning the content of the NPG
Ministerial Agenda and the order in which items should be taken;

(b) took note of the various views expressed and agreed to maintain the content
of the agenda as set out in NPG(Staff Group):N(75)2 of 17th January, 1975;

(c) noted general agreement that the Air Defence Illustrative Study should not
be included in this agenda;

(d) expressed the hope that the United States Authorities would be able to
circulate, in time for the meeting, a progress report on the Nunn amendment;

(e) took note of the remarks of the United Kingdom representative who hoped that
SACMR and SACLANT might be prepared to reply to questions concerning Selective
Employment Planning if this matter was raised by Ministers;

(f) invited the International Staff to prepare and circulate the agenda in the
form of an NPG Working Paper for consideration in Capitals and subsequent
approval by Permanent Representatives at their meeting on 12th May.
