> # Implications of Technological Developments
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated 29th November 1973. It references a Phase II Study Team status report which indicated that current technological developments, specifically in the field of new guidance systems, could significantly impact the tactical use of nuclear weapons. Following recommendations from Permanent Representatives and the Ministers, the Chairman suggests that the Staff Group discuss the organization of a separate study effort at their next meeting on 3rd December. The proposed approach involves forming an ad hoc study team composed of interested countries, including those willing to assume leadership. Additionally, the Chairman encourages Staff Group members to exchange views on the specific meaning of 'current developments' and the 'whole complex of questions surrounding the tactical use of nuclear weapons' to better define the scope of the study.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
IMPLICATIONS OF TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENTS
Note by the Chairman

Staff Group members will recall that the recent Phase II Study Team status
report stated(1) that " ... it has become apparent to the Study Team that
current developments, especially in the field of new guidance systems, could
have a potentially important impact on the whole complex of questions
surrounding the tactical use of nuclear weapons. Since a full examination of
this subject would be beyond its terms of reference and in any event should be
considered in a wider forum, the Study Team suggests that the NPG should
consider the advisability of a separate study of the political and military
implications of these developments." With respect to this suggestion, the
Permanent Representatives commented(2) that "we have no doubt that this subject
deserves further examination." In view of the nature of the subject which
implies that advice from military and technical sources would also be needed,
the work could perhaps best be undertaken by an ad hoc study team composed of
those countries interested in participating." In the light of the Permanent
Representatives' recommendation that the Ministers discuss the Study Team's
suggestion and indicate what further action should be undertaken, the Ministers
invited(3) the Permanent Representatives to "arrange for the preparation of a
study of the political and military implications of those new developments for
Ministerial consideration at a future meeting."

this context, it should be noted that the Permanent Representatives' comments
expressed a preference for an ad hoc study group; the Phase II Study Team's
reference to a "wider forum" possibly suggests that such a group might include
