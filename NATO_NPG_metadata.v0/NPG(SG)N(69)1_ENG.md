> [!abstract] 
> # Follow-On to SHAPE Presentation to NPG Ambassadors
> 
> Following a SHAPE presentation on the role of theatre nuclear strike forces on December 3, 1968, the Ambassadors of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) decided that further questions needed to be addressed. The NPG Staff Group, assisted by Military Authorities, is tasked with collecting and preparing these questions for a subsequent meeting with the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR). A meeting of the Staff Group is scheduled for Tuesday, January 21, 1969, to examine these points. The Secretary, M.H.G. Wilson, requests that members of the Staff Group inform the Director of Nuclear Planning or himself of any specific questions or points for further information by Friday, January 10, 1969. These submissions will be collated and distributed to all Staff Group members and SHAPE before the scheduled meeting.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP(STAFF GROUP)
FOLLOW-ON TO SHAPE PRESENTATION TO NPG AMBASSADORS

Note by the Secretary

The Staff Group will recall that at the end of the SHAPE presentation of the
rôle of theatre nuclear strike forces which took place on 3rd December, 1968, it
was agreed by the Ambassadors that a number of questions remain to be asked. The
Ambassadors had therefore decided :

(a) that the NPG Staff Group, with the help of the Military Authorities, should
collect, discuss and prepare these questions;

(b) that a further meeting of the NPG Ambassadors should be held to which SACEUR
would be invited, and at which the questions would be further examined.

2. It was agreed that a meeting of the Staff Group on this matter should be held
in the near future, and that the date chosen should be communicated to SHAPE as
soon as possible; this meeting is now scheduled for Tuesday, 21st January.

3. It would be much appreciated if members of the Staff Group would inform the
Director of Nuclear Planning or myself of any questions which they wish to ask,
and of any other points on which they wish for further information.

4. These further questions, which should if possible be sent in not later than
Friday evening, 10th January, will be collated and made available to all members
of the Staff Group and to SHAPE before the meeting on 21st January. Additional
questions may of course be raised at the time of the meeting.

(Signed) M.H.G. WILSON
