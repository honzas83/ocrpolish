> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 11th February, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on February 11, 1974. The group endorsed the farewell of Colonel R. Krone and welcomed Colonel Carl R. Smith as his replacement. The Chairman noted several documents regarding the NDAC documents for downgrading, the conceptual analysis of nuclear aspects of deterrence, and the Military Committee's role in the nuclear consultation process. The group discussed arrangements for studying the political and military implications of technological developments, incorporating changes proposed by the United States representative to a paper by the International Staff. The International Staff was tasked with preparing a revised version of this paper for the next meeting. Additionally, the group noted that most countries participating in the NPG 1974 Spring Meeting had accepted the dates of June 11th and 12th, pending a reply from Greece. The meeting also touched upon the Study Group on Warsaw Pact Strategy and Doctrine, with the Netherlands representative requested to report on its progress. The next meeting was scheduled for February 18, 1974.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 11th
February, 1974

STAFF GROUP MEMBERSHIP

1.   THE STAFF GROUP:
(a) warmly endorsed the words of appreciation and farewell expressed by the
Chairman to Colonel R. Krone, SHAPE representative on the Staff Group since
1971;

(b) noted with appreciation the reply by Colonel Krone;
(c) welcomed Colonel Carl R. Smith who is replacing Colonel Krone.

DOCUMENTATION

2.   THE STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the following
papers which had been or were about to be circulated:

(a) NPG(Staff Group)N(74)14, 4th February, 1974: a list of NDAC documents for
downgrading action;

(b) NPG/WP(74)1, 6th February, 1974: Conceptual Analysis of Nuclear Aspects of
Deterrence;

(c) NPG/D(74)1, 12th February, 1974: the Military Committee's rôle in the
Nuclear Consultation Process.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE STUDY OF POLITICAL AND MILITARY
IMPLICATIONS OF TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENTS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who
invited members to resume the discussion held at
the last meeting and asked if the United States
representative had now received the views of his
authorities;
(b) noted the remarks of the United States repres-
entative who said that his authorities were in general
agreement with the International Staff's paper(1);
they felt however that if the paper were to form
the basis for a Permanent Representatives' report,
the text should be shortened; he distributed a
paper containing a number of changes to this
effect;
(c) discussed the paper in the light of the United
States proposals, and of suggestions by other
members, and agreed to a number of changes;
(d) invited the International Staff, in the light of
the discussion, to prepare and circulate a revised
version, for discussion at the next meeting;
agreed also to discuss at that meeting how
the paper should be presented for the Permanent
Representatives.
NPG 1974 SPRING MEETING
4. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who said that
with the exception of Greece, all countries
participating in the NPG 1974 Spring Meeting had
accepted the dates of 11th and 12th June; he hoped
that an early reply could be obtained from Athens.

STUDY OF WARSAW PACT STRATEGY AND DOCTRINE
5. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Study Group on
Warsaw Pact Strategy and Doctrine was meeting in NATO Headquarters on 12th, 13th

NATO, 1110 Brussels.
