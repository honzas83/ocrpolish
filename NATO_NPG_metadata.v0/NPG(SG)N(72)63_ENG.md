> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, on Monday, 21st August, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met at NATO Headquarters on August 21, 1972, to address several key nuclear planning issues. The meeting began with the welcome of Commander W.M. Beckett as the new Canadian representative. The group reviewed documentation regarding the Transition Study, the implementation of special Air Deliverable Munitions (ADM) guidelines, and the Follow-on Use Study for the Denmark Area. A significant point of discussion was the security classification of the Denmark Study, which was upgraded from NATO SECRET to ATOMAL SECRET due to advice from Washington, causing concern for the Danish representative. The group also examined consultation procedures and facilities, suggesting that these be tested during the WINTEX 73 exercise. Discussions on the Transition Study were deferred until further input from NATO Military Authorities was available. Regarding the implementation of special ADM guidelines, the group debated whether the matter should be handled by Permanent Representatives or included on the Ministerial agenda. Finally, the group discussed proposed United States revisions to the concept for the role of theatre nuclear strike forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE), specifically in relation to the POSLEDON weapon system, and requested the International Staff to prepare a paper on these amendments for the Defence Planning Committee (DPC).

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the Ministerial
request to Permanent Representatives at the Copenhagen Meeting "to examine
further ... the procedures and facilities ... for consultation"(1); he suggested
that the aim of the Staff Group should be to prepare a draft report by Permanent
Representatives for NPG Ministers; this draft report should be agreed at Staff
Group level during the next 10 days with a view to its circulation and despatch
to Capitals in the first days of September, and its subsequent examination by
Permanent Representatives at their meeting scheduled for Friday, 29th September,
1972; he further suggested that the International Staff revised draft(2),
amended in the light of comments already received, and of what might be agreed
at the present meeting, could form the basis of the draft report; he invited
views;

(b) took note of a statement by the SHAPE Representative who recalled SACEUR's
views on timely information on nuclear use and his proposal that procedures
should be exercised in WINTEX 73;

(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and of a general
consensus (on a personal basis in the case of some representatives) that the
procedures and the provision of information to political authorities should
await testing in WINTEX 73; further advice by the NPG Ministers would depend
upon the results of this exercise;

IMPLEMENTATION OF SPECIAL ADM GUIDELINES

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the progress
report by the Military Committee on the implementation of the ADM Guidelines,
and to the International Staff's initial draft(1) of a possible report or action
document by the Permanent Representatives; he reminded the Staff Group that in
view of the probable length of the Ministerial Agenda it would be worth
considering whether Permanent Representatives should not themselves take action
on this matter and thereby shorten the Ministerial agenda.

(b) noted that whilst the United Kingdom and United States Representatives would
prefer the matter to be dealt with by Permanent Representatives, the Greek
Representative considered, on a personal basis, that his authorities would like
to see it on the Ministerial agenda;

(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and of some
suggestions regarding changes to the text of the International Staff's initial
draft;

(d) agreed to continue the discussion at the next meeting of the Staff Group,
and invited the International Staff to prepare in the meantime revised wording
for paragraphs 6 and 7.
