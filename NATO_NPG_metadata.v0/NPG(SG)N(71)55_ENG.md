> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 3rd May, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on May 3, 1971, to discuss several key administrative and strategic documents. The group noted a status report on regional studies (NPG/D(71)2) and the agenda for the Mittenwald meeting (NPG/A(71)5). Regarding the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons, the group discussed Working Paper NPG/WP(71)3 and agreed to changes in the text to be circulated as a corrigendum. They also reviewed a report by the Military Committee on the concept for the role of theatre nuclear strike forces, noting a general consensus that no changes were needed to the Concept paper or the Military Committee's Report. A key point of discussion was the interpretation of 'ACE Strike Forces,' which the group emphasized must categorically include dual-capable aircraft. They invited the Chairman to inform the Secretary General of these conclusions and requested a categorical statement from the NATO Military Authorities to ensure agreement among Permanent Representatives on May 6, 1971. The group agreed to meet again on May 10, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 3rd May, 1971
I. CURRENT PAPERS AND DOCUMENTS
1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to two documents:
(a) NPG/D(71)2, dated 4th May, 1971 The non-controversial status report on
regional studies would be distributed on 4th May. This document is on the
Ministerial Agenda.

(b) NPG/A(71)5, dated 29th April, '971 This is the Agenda for the Mittenwald
meeting, and has already been circulated.

II. STUDIES OF FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that Working Paper
NPG/WP(71)3 would be discussed by Permanent Representatives on Thursday, 6th
May, and would subsequently become an NPG document(1) on the Agenda for the
Mittenwald meeting; he invited the Staff Group to propose and discuss any
changes which might usefully be made at this stage;

(b) took note of suggestions made, and in particular of proposals by the
Canadian, German and Greek representatives;

(c) agreed to a number of changes in the text, and invited the International
Staff to circulate a corrigendum in time for the meeting of Permanent
Representatives on 6th May.

(1) NPG/D(71)3 - to be circulated

REPORT BY THE MILITARY COMMITTEE ON THE CONCEPT FOR THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR
STRIKE FORCES

3. The Staff Group:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Permanent
Representatives, at their meeting on 29th April, 1971, had discussed the action
to be taken on the report by the Military Committee, and had agreed to continue
their discussion on Thursday, 6th May, inviting the Staff Group, in the
meantime, to give further attention to the matter;

(b) took note of a paper(1) prepared by the International Staff describing the
background and commenting upon the problem;

(c) discussed the problem in detail, taking particular note of the German and
Italian views.

(d) noted a general consensus on the following points:
(i) no change was needed, or proposed, to the Concept paper(2);
(ii) no change was needed to the Military Committee's Report, nor would it be
proper to propose one;

(iii) the wording used in the third sentence of paragraph 4 of the Military
Committee's Report was generally acceptable as a basis for a possible paragraph
in the Mittenwald Agreed Minute;

(iv) the interpretation of the expression "ACE Strike Forces" was the key to the
problem; it must be made clear, and confirmed categorically by the NATO Military
Authorities, that the expression covered and included under all circumstances,
dual-capable aircraft.

(e) invited the Chairman to inform the Secretary General of the discussion held
by the Staff Group; expressed the hope that an opening statement by the Chairman
of the Nuclear Planning Group, based on the position reached by the Staff Group,
followed by a categorical statement by the Chairman of the Military Committee,
or a representative of SACEUR, would enable the Permanent Representatives, at
their meeting on Thursday, 6th May, to reach an early agreement on the matter.

(1) NPG(Staff Group)N(71)54, 3rd May, 1971
