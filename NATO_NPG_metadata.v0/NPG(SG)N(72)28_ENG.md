> # Illustrative Study on Battlefield Use
> 
> This document, issued by the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), presents a revised Part II regarding an illustrative study on the battlefield use of nuclear weapons. The study was prepared by the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) at the request of the Ministers to confront political authorities with complex consultation and decision-making problems. The primary objective of the Ministerial discussion is to familiarize political authorities with the implications of recommended courses of action and the information available for making decisions regarding the selective use of nuclear weapons during wartime. It is explicitly stated that the discussion is not intended to reach collective advance judgments or force countries to take positions on specific release requests. Furthermore, the scenario is designed to set the stage for considering the tactical use of nuclear weapons and is not a forecast of real crises or a preference of NATO. The document also notes that some alternatives, such as the use of Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMS), were not covered in the study. The revised text was prepared by the International Staff to be discussed by the Staff Group on March 13, 1972.

REVISED PART II

II. BACKGROUND

2. The Ministers will recall that at their last meeting, after reaffirming the
value of the continuing dialogue between the political authorities and the NATO
Military Authorities, they invited the Chairman of the Military Committee to
arrange for SACEUR to prepare "a further illustrative study involving
battlefield use that would confront political authorities with more complex
consultation and decision-making problems." The SHAPE Study deals with the
military aspects of the scenario and the associated procedures used in ACE.

3. The Ministerial discussion of illustrative studies is designed to provide a
basis for political authorities to become aware of the implications of
recommended courses of action and to permit them to become acquainted with the
kind of information available on which decisions might have to be based in time
of war concerning initial or follow-on selective use of nuclear weapons, the
consequences of such decisions, and the environment in which decisions may have
to be made. The discussion also provides an opportunity for the Ministers, in
their dialogue with the NATO Military Authorities present, to explore ways to
ensure that the data to be provided by the NATO Military Authorities in time of
war will be as responsive as possible to the needs of the political authorities.
It is not the objective of the Ministerial discussion to reach collective
advance judgements as to the decisions that might be taken in this particular
illustrative situation were it actually to occur, nor should countries be
obliged to take a position on the merits of the particular release requests used
as examples in this study. Moreover, views expressed in the discussions should
not be interpreted as restricting or prejudging positions that countries might
take in actual crises.

4. The scenario in this study has been deliberately designed to set the stegge
for Ministerial consideration of a situation in which the tactical use of
nuclear weapons on the battlefield might be proposed. As such, it is neither a
forecast of what would occur in a real crisis, nor an indication of what migh't
be preferred by NATO. By the same token, some alternatives, such as the use of
ADMS, were not covered. Under the circumstances, the Ministers may wish to
confine any discussion of the scenario aspects to general comments that may
indicate the kinds of situations to be covered in possible future illustrative
studies.

5. Against this background, several possible areas for Ministerial discussion
are identified below. This is not intended to be exhaustive or restrictive, or
to preclude the Ministers from raising other points they may wish to discuss.
