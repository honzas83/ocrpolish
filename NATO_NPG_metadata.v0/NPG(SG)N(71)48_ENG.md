> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 19th April, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on April 19, 1971, to address several key items. The group approved a statement welcoming Mr. H.C.G. Carsten, who replaced Dr. W. van Eekelen as the Netherlands member. They reviewed background material for the NORTHAG/TWOATAF area study and reviewed a notice from the Executive Secretary of NATO regarding 'Special Limitations Information' and the need for access lists. The group discussed a Military Committee report on the concept for the role of theatre nuclear strike forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE) region, requesting the International Staff to provide suitable wording for a proposal. the group confirmed the draft agenda for the Mittenwald Meeting and scheduled upcoming meetings of NPG Permanent Representatives for April 20, April 29, and May 6. Additionally, they reviewed the status report on work in progress on follow-on use of nuclear weapons and the draft report on illustrative studies and examples of release requests, incorporating amendments from the Belgium representative. Finally, the group discussed a paper on issues emerging from the studies of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons, agreeing to its reproduction as an NPG Working Paper and its dispatch to capitals for further review.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 19th April, 1971
WELCOME
1. The STAFF GROUP :
approved a statement by the Chairman welcoming Mr. H.C.G. Carsten who will in
due course replace Dr. W. van Eekelen, the Netherlands member of the Staff
Group.

BACKGROUND MATERIAL TO NORTHAG/TOOATAF AREA STUDY
2. The STAFF GROUP :
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that some background
information to NPG/Study(71)3 in the form of addenda and maps was available for
consultation in his office. There was also a small amount of ATOMAL data
regarding this study which could be consulted in the Nuclear Planning
Directorate also.

SPECIAL LIMITATIONS INFORMATION
3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who drew attention to a notice (1)
circulated by the Executive Secretary of NATO on the subject of a special
category of ATOMAL information known as "Special Limitations Information";

(b) noted that it was recommended that lists of persons authorised to see such
information should be established by Delegations now, so as to avoid a last-
minute rush to make access lists if such information was received at short
notice.

REPORT BY THE MILITARY COMMITTEE ON THE CONCEPT FOR THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR
STRIKE FORCES IN ACE (1)

4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) recalled its discussion at the meeting held on 5th April and its decision to
give further consideration at a future meeting to action to be taken on the
Military Committee Report;

(b) invited the International Staff to circulate suitable wording for a
proposal, based on the report by the Military Committee;

(c) agreed to discuss this matter at the next meeting of the Staff Group with a
view to bringing the subject to the attention of Permanent Representatives at
their meeting on 29th April.

DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE MITTENWALD MEETING
5. The STAFF GROUP :
noted that no objections having been received to the draft agenda (2) for the
Mittenwald Meeting, it could now be considered as firm, subject to confirmation
by the Permanent Representatives.

FUTURE MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
6. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) recalled that a meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives would take place
next day and that the Agenda comprised a briefing on the NORTHIAG/TWOATAF Study,
the Status Report on Follow-on Studies, and the report on Illustrative Studies;

(b) recalled that the subsequent meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives
would take place on Thursday, 29th April, with a view to hearing a briefing on
the Macedonia and Western Thrace Area Study, to considering the action to be
taken regarding the Report (3) by the Military Committee on the Concept for the
Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE (see paragraph 4(c) above), and to
confirming the form and content of the Agenda for the Mittenwald Ministerial
Meeting, if necessary;

FOLLOW-ON ISSUES PAPER
9. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman concerning the paper (1) produced
by the International Staff on issues emerging from the Studies of Follow-on
Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons;

(b) agreed that after detailed discussion by the Staff Group, the programme for
this paper should be as follows :

(i) reproduction in amended form as an NPG Working Paper,
(ii) despatch to Capitals for views and instructions,
(iii) discussion by Permanent Representatives on 6th May (see paragraph 6(c)
above);

(c) discussed the paper in detail, agreed to a number of changes, and invited
the International Staff to prepare and issue a revised version in the form of an
NPG Working Paper (2).
