> # SHAPE Preliminary Review of CENTAG Area Follow-on Use Study
> 
> This document consists of a notice from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) and an annexed draft report by the Permanent Representatives. The notice, dated 13th October 1971, proposes a meeting of the Staff Group to discuss a preliminary review by Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE) of the CENTAG Area Follow-on Use Study. The draft report identifies key areas for Ministerial discussion, specifically focusing on targeting concepts ALFA and BRAVO. Concept BRAVO, which involves discrete target selection to minimize collateral damage and civilian casualties, is noted as reflecting current NATO military doctrine but is acknowledged as potentially difficult to implement in practice due to factors like target acquisition and delivery system accuracy. The report also addresses the 'Pause for Political Negotiations,' noting that SHAPE considers a pause during follow-on use to be militarily unrealistic and potentially disadvantageous to NATO. The Ministers are encouraged to discuss how delays imposed by nuclear follow-on use on enemy advances might be exploited for political and military purposes, as well as the concept of a self-imposed pause. Additionally, the report mentions the importance of command and control factors and suggests that Ministers indicate if further studies are needed. The recommendations suggest that Ministers take note of the SHAPE preliminary review and use the provided analysis to guide their discussions and determine if further action is required.

Draft Report by the Permanent Representatives
I. PURPOSE
When the CENTAG Follow-on Use Study(1) was distributed, the Nuclear Planning
Group invited(2) the NATO Military Authorities to submit comments. This report
identifies some possible areas for Ministerial discussion, derived from the
SHAPE preliminary review(3) of the CENTAG Study submitted in response to this
invitation, to augment the possible discussion topics set forth in an earlier
report by the Permanent Representatives(4).

II. POSSIBLE AREAS FOR MINISTERIAL DISCUSSION
Concepts ALFA and BRAVO
2. The SHAPE preliminary review states that Concept BRAVO – discrete target
selection considering collateral damage and civilian casualty estimates –
reflects current NATO military doctrine, but recognises that the results
postulated for this Concept might not, in practice, be fully achievable. The
SHAPE review cites a number of factors that could affect the real-life
implementation of Concept BRAVO, such as uncertainties about the location of the
civilian population and their protection, delivery system accuracy, availability
of sub-kiloton weapons and problems of target acquisition(5).

3. In the light of these and other comments by SHAPE on Concept ALFA and BRAVO,
the Ministers may wish to discuss whether, in their opinion, the targeting
approach reflected in Concept BRAVO should continue to be considered as the
objective on which doctrine should continue to be based, and, if so, what
further steps might be taken to ensure that this Concept would be applied in
future nuclear planning activities. They may also wish to explore the
implications of additional measures suggested by SHAPE to enhance the
applicability of this Concept, such as improved target acquisition, more
accurate delivery means, and sufficient numbers of low yield weapons. In this
connection, attention might also be given to the complexity of the data required
to implement Concept BRAVO and the consequent increased emphasis on the use of
computers in tactical nuclear warfare(1).

Pause for Political Negotiations

4. The SHAPE preliminary review notes that a pause during the follow-on use
might well be militarily unrealistic and could be disadvantageous to NATO, and
suggests that, in the development of any nuclear policy for NATO, consideration
of pauses, as reflected in the CENTAG Study, should be handled with special
caution(2). It may be noted that, on this point, the study does not address the
question of a self-imposed cessation of NATO nuclear or conventional operations
for the purpose of creating a pause for political negotiations. Rather, the
study assesses the effectiveness of each NATO option in terms of its ability to
(a) halt the enemy attack; (b) cause him to cease his aggression or withdraw;
(c) provide time for political consultations and possible warnings to, and
negotiations with, the Warsaw Pact; (d) provide an opportunity for NATO
conventional exploitation, and (e) minimise collateral damage(5).

5. In light of the foregoing, the Ministers may wish to discuss ways in which
delays imposed by NATO nuclear follow-on use on the enemy's advance might be
best exploited for political and military purposes. It might also be of value
for the Ministers to address the concept of a self-imposed pause with particular
reference to the political and military advantages and disadvantages.

General
6. The SHAPE preliminary review identifies several other important factors
limiting the validity and applicability of the CEN-TAG Study(1) which may
require further attention, as recorded, in some instances, in the Study itself.
Of particular interest are the SHAPE views on the question of command and
control, on which some further elaboration would be of value. The Ministers may
wish to indicate whether there are any specific subject areas in which further
studies may be needed at this stage.

III. RECOMMENDATIONS
7. The Ministers may wish to:
(a) take note of the SHAPE preliminary review(2);
(b) draw, inter alia, on the foregoing paragraphs in Part II for their
discussions;

(c) indicate, in the light of their discussion, whether there are any particular
aspects on which further study or action should be undertaken.
