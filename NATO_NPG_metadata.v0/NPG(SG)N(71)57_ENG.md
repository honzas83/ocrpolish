> # Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE
> 
> This document is a draft report by the Permanent Representatives of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) concerning the role of theatre nuclear strike forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE). It addresses a report from the Military Committee suggesting that certain paragraphs in the concept document (paragraphs 60 and 61) might be contradictory and could benefit from clarification or modification to avoid ambiguity. The Permanent Representatives argue that the concept paper is the result of a careful policy formulation process and that any revision, even for clarification, could upset the delicate balance of the document and reduce the flexibility of the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) to employ strike forces. They suggest that the intent of paragraphs 60 and 61 is to allow SACEUR the flexibility to use dual-capable aircraft for conventional operations while exercising prudent military judgment to prevent attrition that would compromise nuclear response options. Ultimately, the Permanent Representatives recommend that the Ministers take note of the Military Committee's confirmation that current plans are in accord with the guidance, but agree that a revision of the concept paper is neither necessary nor desirable, suggesting instead that any necessary clarifications be handled through discussions among the Ministers and NATO Military Authorities at Mittenwald.

I. BACKGROUND

1. In approving the Concept for the Rôle of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE
(1), the Defence Planning Committee invited (2) the NATO Military Authorities :

(a) to continue to adjust their plans to conform
with the approved concept;
(b) to report progress to the Nuclear Planning
Group by 31st March, 1971; and
(c) to make suggestions, as appropriate, for further
clarification or refinement of the concept.

2. The Military Committee has confirmed (3) that plans
are now in accord with the concept and that any future planning
will also be in accord. In addition, the Military Committee has
called attention to paragraphs 60, 61 and 87 and has pointed out
that statements in paragraphs 60 and 61 might be construed as
contradictory; they suggest that further clarification would be
most desirable. In this context, the NATO Military Authorities
hold the view that "the intent of paragraphs 60 and 61
of the concept document (4) is to provide SACEUR the
flexibility to use, if necessary, all dual capable aircraft for
conventional operations but caution that SACEUR should exercise
prudent military judgement to prevent attrition of ACE strike

II.    DISCUSSION

3.    In considering the Military Committee's comments on
the apparent contradiction in paragraphs 60 and 61, the
Permanent Representatives recalled that the concept paper is
the product of a careful and deliberate policy formulation
process, during which a range of views of countries and military
authorities were accommodated. Moreover, the document now embodies
a large measure of flexibility that enables SACEUR to employ his
theatre nuclear strike forces effectively. In light of the
foregoing, it is the consensus of the Permanent Representatives
that revision of the concept paper should be avoided, in
particular to preserve the flexibility SACEUR now has.

4.    The Permanent Representatives share the view that the
concept paper must be considered in its entirety and that an
interpretation of one or two paragraphs cannot be made without
reference to other relevant parts of the paper. Such an
interpretation promises to be complex and lengthy, and its
formulation in addition to, and separate from, the concept paper
itself could prove to be more confusing than enlightening. There
would appear to be great risk of upsetting the delicate balance
achieved in the concept paper, and of reducing SACEUR's flexibility.
