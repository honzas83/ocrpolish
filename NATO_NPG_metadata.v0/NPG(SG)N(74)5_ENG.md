> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 14th January, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on January 14, 1974, in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group endorsed the addition of Dr. R. Huber of the German Delegation to the Staff Group membership. Regarding technological developments, the group noted that most North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) countries, except Greece and Norway, wished to participate in a study on the political and military implications of current technological developments, with the United States accepting the leadership of this study. The group also reviewed a revised paper on the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process and discussed an illustrative study on the tactical use of nuclear air defence weapons in a land environment. Additionally, the the group noted plans for a paper on the analysis of the nuclear aspects of deterrence and a draft paper on communicating NATO's intentions. The Chairman mentioned the intention to prepare a compilation of nuclear consultation procedures. To assist new members, the group concurred with a suggestion to arrange a series of briefings and visits. Finally, the Norwegian representative indicated that the upcoming NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting would likely be held in Bergen or Oslo, and the next Staff Group meeting was scheduled for January 21, 1974.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 14th
January, 1974

CHANGE IN STAFF GROUP MEMBERSHIP

1. The STAFF GROUP:
endorsed the warm welcome extended by the Chairman to Dr. R. Huber of the German
Delegation, who will be attending future meetings of the Staff Group.

IMPLICATIONS OF TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENTS

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman, who recalled the discussion held
at the previous meeting, and said that he had been informed by the
representative of Portugal (who was unable to be present) that Portugal was
interested in taking part in the study on the political and military
implications of current developments in the technological field;

(b) noted the remarks of the Danish representative, who said that his country,
although unable to make a technical contribution, wished to take part in the
more general aspects of the work;

(c) noted, in consequence, that all the NP countries, with the exception of
Greece and Norway, wished to take part in the study;

(d) noted, with appreciation, a statement by the United States representative,
who said that his Authorities had instructed him to say that the United States
accepted the leadership of the study; furthermore, they wished to know if any
country desired to undertake co-leadership;

(e) took note of a general feeling that co-leadership was not appropriate, but,
at the request of one country, agreed to wait until the end of the month before
taking a decision;

(f) noted, with appreciation, a further statement by the United States
representative, who said that, notwithstanding the deferred decision on
leadership, his Authorities in Washington would proceed with the preparation of
a paper on organising the study, which would be circulated as soon as possible
to participating countries;

(g) agreed to continue discussion of study arrangements in light of future
developments.

REPORT BY THE MILITARY COMMITTEE ON ITS FUNCTION IN THE NUCLEAR CONSULTATION
PROCESS

3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Military Committee representative, who said that
a revised paper(1) on the Military Committee's function in the nuclear
consultation process had been distributed; the deadline for comments was 30th
January.

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDY FOR SPRING 1974 MEETING

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that the Chairman of the Military Committee had been requested to
prepare an illustrative study dealing with tactical use of nuclear air defence
weapons in a land environment, to be made available to the Secretary General by
1st February, 1974;

(c) noted that the International Staff would prepare a first draft of a

ANALYSIS OF THE NUCLEAR ASPECTS OF DETERRENCE

5. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman, who said that he intended to circulate
a paper concerning the Phase II study team's report(1) on Deterrence, in time
for discussion at the next meeting of the Staff Group.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTIONS

6. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman, who said that he hoped to circulate a
draft paper on Communicating NATO's Intentions before the end of the month; this
paper would stress the close connection of this matter with Crisis Management
work.

COMPILATION OF CONSULTATION PROCEDURES

7. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman, who said that he hoped, in due course,
to prepare a compilation of nuclear consultation procedures reflecting decisions
taken in the NPG; evidently this would have to be very carefully aligned with
the Crisis Management work of the Council Operations and Exercise Committee.

STAFF GROUP BRIEFINGS AND VISITS

8. The STAFF GROUP: concurred with the suggestion by the Chairman, who said that
in view of the large proportion of new members in the Staff Group, it would be
valuable to arrange for a series of briefings and visits for the education of
members in nuclear and associated matters.

NPG SPRING MINISTERIAL MEETING

9. The STAFF GROUP: took note of a statement by the Norwegian representative who
said that he had been informed by his Authorities that the site of the NPG
Spring Ministerial Meeting would probably be Bergen or, possibly, Oslo.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP

10. The STAFF GROUP: agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 21st January,
1974, at 3.0 p.m.

NATO, 1110 Brussels.
