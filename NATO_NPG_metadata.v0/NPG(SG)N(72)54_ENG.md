> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 12th June, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on June 12, 1972, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting's primary focus was the development of a report for Ministers regarding Consultation Procedures and Facilities. The group discussed five key points for the report: arrangements for continuity of nuclear consultation, the provision of timely information on the impending use of nuclear weapons, the maintenance of effective communications between governments and permanent representatives, procedures for developing and disseminating assessments and evaluations, and the classification and precedence of selective release messages. The group also noted the communication of certain NPG documents and studies to the Portuguese delegation. The United Kingdom representative highlighted the work of the Council Operations and Exercise Committee (COEC) and the NATO Integrated Communications System Management Agency (NICSMA) regarding survivability and crisis management. The Canadian representative suggested that a list of relevant current working papers from COEC and NICSMA be prepared. The group agreed that the five points identified by the International Staff were suitable for inclusion in the the report and invited the International Staff to prepare an initial draft. Additionally, the meeting concluded with a notice of a farewell luncheon party for Mr. Quade, Mr. Stephansen, Mr. Morgan, and Colonel Montoya, and the scheduling of the next meeting for June 19, 1972.
