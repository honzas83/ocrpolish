> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 6th September, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on September 6, 1971, to address several key administrative and strategic planning issues. The group took note of recently issued papers regarding the CENTAG Area Study, SACLANT's comments on the North Norway Area Study, and maritime use in the Mediterranean. They discussed the request for military comments on the CENTAG and Turkish studies, and the process for submitting reports on the nuclear aspects of AD 70 follow-up action to the Defence Policy Committee (DPC) Ministers. There was a lack of consensus on the final agenda for the October Ministerial Meeting, leading the Chairman to request the Secretary General to decide on a draft agenda. Additionally, the group scheduled future meetings for NPG Permanent Representatives to discuss the CENTAG Area Study briefing and other working papers. The meeting also touched upon administrative requirements for the October meeting as raised by the Belgian Representative, and the group agreed to meet again on September 10, 1971, in Brussels.

DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE MINISTERIAL MEETING IN OCTOBER
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that, despite further detailed discussion a consensus regarding the
content of the Ministerial Agenda had not been achieved; noted however a growing
agreement to include the Status Report on Follow-on Studies and to exclude the
report on the nuclear aspects of the AD 70 Follow-up action;

(b) concurred with a statement by the Chairman who said that, in view of the
lack of consensus, he would request the Secretary General himself to decide upon
a draft agenda; this would be issued as an NPG Working Paper for early
examination in capitals and for subsequent decision, if possible by the silence
procedure, with a view to circulating a final agreed agenda well before the end
of September.

REGIONAL FOLLOW-ON STUDIES – CENTAG AREA STUDY
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the International
Staff's paper(1) on the CENTAG Area Study issues would be circulated next day
and that he strongly recommended that the Staff Group should meet again this
week to discuss it; he hoped that it would be possible to complete Staff Group
action on this paper by Monday evening 13th September;

(b) further noted the Chairman's view that the time available to prepare the
CENTAG issues paper was very limited, and that if Permanent Representatives were
not in a position to take final action on it at the end of September they would
have to meet very early in October for this purpose;

(c) took note of a statement by the United States Representative who, recalling
his remarks at the last meeting, confirmed that the most suitable moment for the
CENTAG Study Team to give a briefing on the Study to NPG Permanent
Representatives would be on Monday, 27th September, 1971
