> # Advance Press Release for the Meeting of NPG Ministers
> 
> This document consists of a notice from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group and an attached advance press release. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, informs the group that the text of the press release has been agreed upon by the Staff Group and national authorities, including the United States, following a reservation. The press release is scheduled for distribution on Friday, November 7th, 1959, at 3 p.m. Brussels local time, with optional simultaneous release in NPG capitals. The press release itself specifies that the Nuclear Planning Group of NATO, comprising the Defence Ministers of Belgium, Denmark, Germany, Italy, Turkey, the United Kingdom, and the United States, will hold its sixth meeting on November 11th and 12th, 1959, near Washington, D.C., under the chairmanship of NATO Secretary General Manlio Brosio.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE FOR THE MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS
Note by the Chairman
at its meeting on 20th October, 1959, the Staff Group agreed, in the light of
instructions from national authorities, to the text of an advance Press release
concerning the meeting of Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group to be held
near Washington on 11th and 12th November, 1959, subject to a reservation by
United States. I have now been informed by the United States delegation that the
text is acceptable to them.

Attached is the agreed text of the press release, which will be made on Friday,
7th November, 1959, at 3 p.m. Brussels local time, with optional simultaneous
release in NPG capitals.

(signed) H.B. SEIM
OTAN/NATO, Brussels, 39.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP
advance Press Release

As decided at its last ministerial meeting, the Nuclear Planning Group of NATO,
which at present consists of the Defence Ministers of Belgium, Denmark, Germany,
Italy, Turkey, the United Kingdom, and the United States, will hold its sixth
meeting on 11th and 12th November near Washington, D.C., under the chairmanship
of Mr. Manlio Brosio, Secretary General of NATO.
