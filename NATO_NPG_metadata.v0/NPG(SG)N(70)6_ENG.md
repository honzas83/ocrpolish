> # Notice of a Meeting Held on Friday, 13th February, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on February 13, 1970, to record the results of their discussions. The group noted and supported a statement by the Chairman regarding NATO nuclear briefings for the Military Committee, expressing appreciation for a briefing held on February 10 and requesting that the representative of the Military Committee convey their thanks to the Military Committee. The group also noted that two further briefings would be scheduled for March and April on a 'need to know' basis, and expressed hope to attend these future sessions. Additionally, the Staff Group discussed the role and functions of NPG Permanent Representatives and the Staff Group, taking note of a conversation between the United States ambassador and the Secretary General. This conversation highlighted the importance of personal involvement of Defence Ministers and their immediate staffs to maintain the vitality of the NPG, while cautioning against an undue reliance on the staff group that might weaken the direct participation of senior officials. The meeting also addressed the requirement for political control in tactical nuclear warfare and the need for expert briefings to provide background for regional and other studies. Finally, the group expressed hope for clarity regarding the attendance and dates of the NPG Ministerial Meeting scheduled for early June 1970 in Italy, and agreed to hold their next meeting on February 23, 1970.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notice of a meeting held on Friday, 13th February, 1970
NATO NUCLEAR BRIEFINGS FOR THE MILITARY COMMITTEE
1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted, and unanimously supported, a statement by the Chairman in which he
expressed the appreciation of the Staff Group for the opportunity of attending
the IMS nuclear briefing held on 10th February, and requested the representative
of the Military Committee to convey the thanks of the Staff Group to the
Military Committee;

(b) noted a statement by the representative of the Military Committee who said
that two more briefings, following on from that of 10th February, would be
given, one in March and one in April. Attendance would be on a "need to know"
basis;

(c) requested the Military Committee representative to convey to the Military
Committee the Staff Group’s hope that it might be able to attend these future
briefings.

ROLE OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES/STAFF GROUP
2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of an introductory statement by the Chairman who referred to
remarks made at the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives on 2nd February
concerning the rôle and various functions of the NPG Permanent Representatives
and the NPG Staff Group, and to a subsequent conversation between the United
States ambassador and the Secretary General;

POLITICAL CONTROL IN TACTICAL NUCLEAR WARFARE
3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement made by the Chairman on the requirement for
political control and on ways to assure it;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the subsequent informal discussion;
(c) agreed upon the need for briefings by, and discussions with, experts, to
give the Staff Group and national staffs in capitals the best possible
background to their approach to the regional studies and other studies which
would follow;

(d) invited the Chairman to look into the possibility of organising Staff Group
briefings.
