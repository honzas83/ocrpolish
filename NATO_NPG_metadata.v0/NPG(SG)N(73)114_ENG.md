> # Senior Officers' Orientation Course
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) issued a notice on 9 November 1973 announcing the Senior Officers' Orientation Course (short title: 10-31) conducted by the NATO Weapons Systems School (NWSS) in Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarise senior NATO officers and key personnel in staff appointments with the major concepts of NATO weapons employment in Allied Command Europe. Eligibility is limited to officers of the rank of Colonel and above, and civilian officials of equivalent grade, on a need-to-know basis, requiring a COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance. Instruction is provided in English or French, and each course lasts one week from Monday to Friday. A schedule of ten courses is listed for 1974, ranging from January to December. Officials in national delegations at NATO Headquarters in Brussels are instructed to apply through the Nuclear Planning Directorate of the International Staff, while officials in capitals and national commands should apply through their respective Ministries of Defence. The number of spaces per course is limited to approximately 30, with only one or two spaces typically allocated to NATO officials. Applicants are advised to apply at least a month in advance and submit a 'Joining Report' to the NWSS at least three weeks prior to the course commencement. Further information and quota requests can be directed to Mr. Serio or Miss Hyde at the Nuclear Planning Directorate.

2. The course provides a general orientation and background that is considered
useful for those involved in NPG work; it may also be of interest to other
senior officials in delegations and in capitals. Officials in national
delegations located at NATO Headquarters in Brussels who are interested in
attending one of the courses listed above should apply through the Nuclear
Planning Directorate of the International Staff which acts as the point of
contact with SHAPE and the NWSS at Oberammergau for obtaining spaces for
personnel in delegations and the International Staff. Officials in capitals and
national commands should apply through their own Ministry of Defence.

3. The number of spaces in each course is limited to about 30 and the demand is
usually quite heavy. Only one or two spaces for each course are usually
allocated to NATO officials. Therefore, it is advisable to apply well in advance
of the desired course, i.e., at least a month before. This also allows time for
submission of the "Joining Report" which must be sent to NWSS at least three
weeks prior to commencement of the course.

4. The Nuclear Planning Directorate would be pleased to provide further
information or to obtain quotas. Please contact Mr. Serio on extension 2048, or
Miss Hyde on extension 2027.
