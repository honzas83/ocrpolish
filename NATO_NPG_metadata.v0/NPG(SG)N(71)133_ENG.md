> # Possible Approaches to the Preparation of Illustrative Studies
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Staff Group, outlines proposed approaches for the preparation of illustrative studies to be presented at Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministerial meetings. It highlights the need for studies that combine both military and political aspects to confront political authorities with complex consultation and decision-making problems. The Chairman discusses the requirement for a battlefield use study to be prepared by SACEUR by February 1, 1972, and reviews previous studies on maritime use in the Mediterranean and demonstrative use in North Norway. The document proposes a new pattern for preparation where Major NATO Commanders provide military scenarios, which the Secretary General then supplements with a political scenario counterpart. This integrated approach is intended to ensure that the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) and SHAPE are not tasked with political analysis, and that the Permanent Representatives can focus on identifying major points for Ministerial discussion. The Chairman also addresses the potential for deferring the battlefield use study to the Autumn 1972 meeting if sufficient time is not available to develop a worthwhile politico-military presentation for the Spring 1972 meeting. Finally, the document emphasizes the role of the Permanent Representatives in selecting studies and preparing reports for Ministers.

POSSIBLE APPROACHES TO THE PREPARATION OF
ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES
Note by the Chairman
At its meeting on Friday, 3rd December, the Staff Group will continue its
consideration of the problem of preparing illustrative studies for Ministerial
meetings. The following paragraphs set out some of the factors that the Group
may wish to bear in mind during its further discussions.

BACKGROUND
2. The NPG Ministers have reaffirmed the value of illustrative studies and have
requested that SACEUR prepare one involving battle-field use for presentation to
Ministers in the Spring of 1972. The study is to confront political authorities
with more complex consultation and decision-making problems(1). As a follow-up
to the Ministerial decision, the Secretary General has requested the Chairman of
the Military Committee to arrange for the SACEUR study document to be made
available for distribution to the NPG by 1st February, 1972.

3. Reflecting their strong interest in the political aspects of dealing with
release requests, the NPG Ministers at their last meeting also invited the
Permanent Representatives, with the assistance of the NATO Military Authorities,
to prepare studies that would illustrate the evolution of the situation and the
procedures that would be involved and the types of information that would be
required, both at the Council/DPC level and in national capitals, for
consultation and decision-making by political authorities on requests for use of
nuclear weapons(2).

4. Two illustrative studies prepared by SACEUR have already been circulated to
the NPG; 'one on maritime use in the Mediterranean and the other on
demonstrative use in North Norway(1). Ministers have considered the maritime use
scenario at the past two meetings. The Norwegian authorities have requested that
Ministerial consideration of the North Norway scenario be deferred until a
meeting at which the Norwegian Defence Minister will be present. Under current
rotation arrangements, the next Ministerial meeting at which the Norwegian
Minister will participate will be in the Spring of 1973.

5. A specific date for the Spring 1972 Ministerial meeting has not yet been
chosen but it appears likely that it will take place during the second half of
May.

6. The following points have emerged during earlier Staff Group discussion:
(a) In the light of the Ministerial comments at recent meetings, it is clear
that any illustrative scenario placed on their agenda in the future should cover
both political and military aspects. This would apply to the battlefield study
now being prepared.

(b) SHAPE should not be expected to include the political aspects in its
battlefield use study; the political counterpart should be developed under the
aegis of the Permanent Representatives on the basis of SHAPE's military input.

(c) The question remains open whether there would be enough time after receipt
of the SHAPE battlefield use study (about 1st February) to permit development of
a political counterpart and the preparation of a politico-military presentation
in time for the Spring 1972 meeting. If there is not a good possibility that a
worthwhile overall study and associated presentation can be produced by then, it
would be appropriate and advisable to suggest deferring Ministerial
consideration of a battlefield use illustrative study until a later meeting,
e.g. the Autumn 1972 meeting.

(d) If the battlefield use study is deferred, consideration could be given to
the following alternatives for the Spring 1972 meeting:

(1) Expand the existing maritime use study to include political aspects and
present it to Ministers. Since the same basic scenario has been on the last two

(11) Expand the currently available North Norway demonstrative use study to

DISCUSSION

7. There appear to remain at least three important questions on which the Staff
Group may wish to focus its attention:

(a) What arrangements should be made for preparation of the political scenario
of the illustrative study?

(b) Should deferral of the battlefield use study be suggested?
(c) What additional work, if any, should the Permanent Representatives initiate
with respect to the political aspects of dealing with release requests?

8. Political scenario. By virtue of their general responsibility to oversee
preparations for Ministerial sessions and in the light of the specific tasks
given to them by Ministers in paragraph 7 of the Brussels Minute, the Permanent
Representatives should spell out the specific approach to be used for
preparation of the political scenarios of the illustrative studies. As noted
above, it has already been recognised that it would be inappropriate to task
SHAPE with this responsibility. The Permanent Representatives could, of course,
charge themselves with this task but this runs the risk of raising a number of
complications, e.g., it would undoubtedly

be quite time-consuming, particularly if unanimous agreement on a detailed
scenario had to be reached on the basis of national instructions; it could lead
to a requirement that there be national agreement on the military aspects of the
scenario as well. Alternatively, the Permanent Representatives might request the
Secretary General to take the responsibility for developing a political scenario
to supplement the SHAPE military scenario input and for circulating a single
illustrative study document covering both aspects. This would be consistent with
the approach currently used, wherein a Major NATO Commander rather than a group
of national representatives, e.g., the Military Committee, prepares the
illustrative study. In practice, it can be expected that the Secretary General
would work in close but informal coordination with delegations and the NATO
Military Authorities. Furthermore, this approach would enable the Permanent
Representatives to concentrate their attention on the preparation of a report to
Ministers identifying major points for possible Ministerial discussion.

9. To summarise, the pattern for preparation of illustrative studies might be
along the following lines:

(a) The Secretary General would be charged with an overall responsibility for
the preparation of a number of suitable illustrative studies from which the
Permanent Representatives could select one or more for use as a basis for
Ministerial discussion at a particular meeting. In discharging this
responsibility, the Secretary General would seek to develop studies that are
responsive to the needs and interests of Ministers as reflected in their
comments at meetings and in national instructions received by the Permanent
Representatives;

(b) The Major NATO Commanders would prepare individual studies as they do now,
setting forth a military scenario leading up to the submission of release
requests. These studies would be forwarded to the Secretary General;

(c) Upon receipt of an MNC study, the Secretary General would develop a
political scenario counterpart that would supplement the military scenario and
also illustrate the evolution of the situation for an additional period of time
while political consultation was in progress.

The supplementary material would also illustrate the procedures to be used
during this process and the types of information required by political
authorities. The Secretary General would work in close, informal coordination
with delegations and the NATO Military Authorities in developing the political
counterpart;

(d) Upon completion of this step, the Secretary General would circulate an
illustrative study document in the NPG/Study series that would combine in a
single paper both the military and political aspects of the illustrative
scenario.

(e) At least two months prior to a particular Ministerial meeting, the Permanent
Representatives would select the illustrative study or studies to be presented
to Ministers and would invite the Secretary General to prepare, in collaboration
with the Major NATO Commander concerned, an appropriate presentation;

(f) The Permanent Representatives would prepare a report to Ministers setting
forth suggested matters for Ministerial discussion;

(g) Several weeks before the Ministerial meeting, the Permanent Representatives
would make a preliminary review of the presentation to be given to Ministers.

10. Deferment of the battlefield use study. The answer to this question could be
affected in a significant way by the decisions that are made regarding
development of the political aspects of the scenario. As noted earlier, the
answer could also depend on what other items might be on the agenda and what
other illustrative studies, if any, might be substituted if it were decided that
the next Ministerial agenda must include an illustrative study. There appears to
be no justification now for proposing deferment and no reason to change the
suggested target date of 1st February, 1972 for receipt of SHAPE’s military
scenario. The Staff Group may want, however, to keep this question under review.

11. Additional work concerning the consultation process. At their last meeting,
Ministers not only confirmed a need for expanding the illustrative study
presentations to cover political aspects but also expressed interest in
receiving, possibly a year from now, some studies setting out in general terms
the types of information that would be required at the political level and the
procedures that would be used in the consultation process. This requirement is
reflected in paragraph 7 of the Brussels Minute. The Staff Group

12. In light of the foregoing, the Staff Group may wish, at the meeting on 3rd

development and presentation of illustrative studies, perhaps along the lines of
paragraph 9 above. A Working Paper reflecting these modified arrangements would
then be circulated to delegations with a request that instructions be sought
from capitals in anticipation of a Permanent Representatives' meeting during the
second half of January 1972. If time permits, the questions of additional work
and deferment could be discussed in a preliminary way.
