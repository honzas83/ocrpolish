> # Future Work Programme
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, outlines a proposed future work programme to facilitate discussion among the Staff Group and assist in the preparation of a Paper of Outline (PO) for the upcoming Ministerial meeting. The programme covers several key areas: the Follow-on Use Work Programme, specifically Phase II, involving a report from an ad hoc Study Team comprising the United States, United Kingdom, and Federal Republic of Germany. It also addresses the political and military implications of technological developments concerning the tactical use of nuclear weapons, led by the United States. Additionally, the document discusses consultation procedures and facilities, including the ATOMAL classification and the compilation of nuclear consultation procedures. It mentions the evaluation of the WINTEX.75 exercise to refine consultation procedures for nuclear weapons use. The Chairman suggests the preparation of additional illustrative studies to promote dialogue between political and military authorities, and the the monitoring of the implementation of Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs). Finally, it proposes three areas of interest for further work on Communicating NATO's Intention (CNI) to use nuclear weapons, including analysis of warning during conflict and the use of illustrative studies for CNI problems.

To facilitate further discussion by the Staff Group of the possible future
activities of the NPC and, in particular, to assist me in the preparation of the
PO on this subject which will be circulated prior to the Ministerial meeting, I
have attached a list of tasks already initiated by the NPG Ministers on which
work is currently underway, tasks that might flow from Ministerial actions at
their next meeting, and subjects on which the Permanent Representatives have a
general remit to propose additional work.

2. In reviewing the future work programme, it is important to keep in mind that
a lead time of a year or more is usually needed before a subject has reached the
stage where it is ready for Ministerial consideration. It follows from this that
work has to be initiated well in advance of the time that it is expected to be
on a Ministerial agenda. Furthermore, it is difficult in many cases to predict
precisely the particular meeting for which a report or study will be available.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME

1. Follow-on Use Work Programme. In May 1972, the NPG Ministers had approved(1)
the initiation of Phase II of the Work Programme. In accordance with the
approach adopted(2), an ad hoc Phase II Study Team (U.S., U.K., F.R. of Germany)
was set up to prepare a report. The preliminary draft of which will be discussed
with the NPG Permanent Representatives. In its status report(3), circulated
before the last Ministerial meeting, the Phase II Study Team expressed the hope
that it would be possible to submit a draft of the preliminary report for
consideration by the other NPG nations some time in the summer of 1974. It is
understood, through informal information, that a draft of the preliminary report
has been completed and circulated to countries participating in the Phase II
effort. Its distribution to other NPG countries can therefore be expected soon,
probably during September, 1974. A meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives with
the Study Team to discuss the draft report might then be envisaged towards the
end of October, for example on the 31st. In any case, a minimum of 4 weeks time
should be foreseen for capitals to formulate their views and circulate them in
writing before the Permanent Representatives' meeting. In the light of the
Permanent Representatives' exchange of views on the preliminary draft, the Study
Team is supposed to proceed with the completion of its Phase II report. Since it
is planned that the Permanent Representatives will prepare their own report on
the Phase II report for concurrent Ministerial consideration, the final report
should be submitted by the ad hoc study team no later than 1st February 1975 if
it is intended to include the item in the Ministerial Agenda for the Spring NPG
Ministerial meeting. Thus, about three months time would be available for the
preparation of the Permanent Representatives' report. At the November
Ministerial meeting, the status of work of Follow-on Work Programme can be
covered in the Secretary General's PO on future NPG work.

2. Implications of Technological Developments. At their meeting in The Hague on
6th and 7th November, 1973 the Ministers invited the Permanent Representatives
to arrange for the preparation of a study on the political and military
implications of technological developments concerning the tactical use of
nuclear weapons(1). The Permanent Representatives have commissioned an ad hoc
group, composed of all countries presently involved in NPG activities and under
the leadership of the United States, to carry out the study effort(2). A first
outline(3) concerning the organisation and conduct of the study has recently
been circulated by the United States. Submission of a report to the NPG
Ministers for their autumn 1975 meeting is therein suggested.

3. Consultation Procedures and Facilities. In this area, NPG future efforts will
be required in connection with the following topics.

(a) ATOMAL Classification. The NPG has previously indicated a need to explore
the implications flowing from the requirement to use the ATOMAL classification
for certain nuclear consultation traffic(4), but has deferred further
examination of the matter until revised classification guidelines have been
promulgated(5). The process of developing revised guidelines is still in
progress and no firm forecasts can be made concerning when it may be completed.

(b) Compilation of Nuclear Consultation Procedures. At their meeting at The
Hague on 6th and 7th November, 1973, the Ministers invited the Permanent
Representatives to develop a compilation of nuclear consultation procedures(6).
Work is underway in International Staff to prepare a basis for Staff Group
development of a draft compilation for Permanent Representatives' consideration.
Completion of the work might be foreseen for early 1975. In this connection, it
should be noted that the compilation would not necessarily be placed on a
Ministerial agenda.

(c) WINTEX.75. Lessons learned through WINTEX.75
Exercise led to a number of actions aimed at improving procedures for
consultation on nuclear weapons use, but showed that further experience from
future NATO exercises would help in refining these procedures or in highlighting
new areas requiring corrective actions(1). Such experience might also be useful
to confirm the effectiveness of the actions taken. It would appear therefore
that the NPG might wish to examine the results of WINTEX.75 exercise as far as
nuclear consultation aspects are concerned. If a decision is taken to have a
nuclear portion in WINTEX.75, the Permanent Representatives might wish to
prepare a report on preliminary observations on WINTEX as in 1973. Since
WINTEX.75 is planned to take place early in 1975, it might be possible to
prepare such a preliminary report for the Spring 1975 Ministerial meeting.

4. Illustrative Studies. With a view toward promoting the useful dialogue
between political authorities and the NATO Military Authorities, the Ministers
at their meeting in Ankara in May 1973 invited the Chairman of the Military
Committee to arrange for the Major. NATO Commanders to prepare additional
illustrative studies for Ministerial consideration at future meetings(2). Apart
from the Air Defence Illustrative Study which will be on the Rome Agenda this
autumn, no other studies have so far been requested for future Ministerial
discussion. The NPG Permanent Representatives recommended(3), and the Ministers
agreed at their meeting in Bergen, that a request for the preparation of another
illustrative study might be initiated after Ministerial consideration of the Air
Defence Illustrative Study in Rome, in the light of which the Ministers may wish
to indicate their preferences, if any, for the kinds of use to be illustrated in
subsequent studies. In consideration of the lead time involved in the
preparation, it is understood that Military Authorities, in case of
confirmation, be requested to prepare such a study for Ministerial consideration
at the Autumn 1975 Ministerial meeting. (For reference purposes, a list of NPG
documentation related to Illustrative Studies is attached).

5. Implementation of Special ADM Guidelines.

In fulfillment of their continuing task(1) of monitoring the progress being made
in implementing the Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of Atomic
Demolition Munitions (ADMs)(2), the NPG Permanent Representatives prepared
status reports in 1971, 1972 and 1973. The most recent of these reports
indicated that further progress was still required and the Ministers, at their
meeting at The Hague, urged countries concerned and the NATO Military
Authorities to take that action necessary for further implementation of the
Special ADM Guidelines(3). It is therefore suggested that the Permanent
Representatives consider now whether Ministers should be approached at the
forthcoming meeting to initiate another round for the NATO Military Authorities
to report on this issue in 1975.

6. Future Work on CNI. Three possible areas of interest for further work on the
matter of Communicating NATO's Intention to Use Nuclear Weapons(4) might be
considered by the NPG Permanent Representatives:

- a more detailed analysis of "Warning during Conflict" and its potential
implications with regard to other audiences, such as third countries and own
public;

- the suitability of illustrative studies for presenting CNI problems in
addition to examples of release requests;

- more definite descriptions of procedures to be followed and measures to be
taken in implementing CNI actions as part of the nuclear consultation process.
