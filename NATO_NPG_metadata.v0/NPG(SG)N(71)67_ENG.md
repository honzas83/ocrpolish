> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 21st June, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on June 21, 1971, to discuss several administrative and strategic items. The group endorsed a statement of thanks to the Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE) for a recent visit on June 16, 1971, and specifically thanked Major-General S.C. Waters and Major-General L.S. Lightner. The meeting also addressed technical and safety aspects of Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs). The group scheduled a farewell luncheon for departing members, including Dr. W.F. van Eekelen and Lt.Col. W.R. Rhodehamel. Additionally, the Italian Representative suggested a visit to the United States Strategic Air Command Headquarters in Omaha, Nebraska, with potential transportation provided by German authorities. The group discussed the timing for the next NPG Ministerial Meeting in Belgium, considering dates in October or November 1971, while noting Greek national holidays and the opening of the new Permanent Headquarters for COMIBERLIANT in Portugal. Procedural arrangements for the subsequent stages of the Follow-on use work programme were discussed, referencing an International Staff paper. The group also considered the possibility of a meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives on July 19 or 20, 1971, to discuss illustrative studies and nuclear problems arising from the AD 70s Study. The finally, the group noted the need for a meeting of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) in December 1971 to examine a report on the status of work. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for June 28, 1971.

Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 21st June, 1971
VISIT OF THE STAFF GROUP TO SHAPF
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) warmly endorsed a statement by the Chairman who expressed his thanks to the
SHAPE Representative for the most interesting and agreable visit which the Staff
Group had made to SHAPE on 16th June, 1971;

(b) requested the SHAPE Representative to convey the appreciation of the Staff
Group to Major-General S.C. Waters, ACOS P & P and to Major-General L.S.
Lightner, ACOS OP8;

(c) took note of a statement by the SHAPE Representative who gave further
information regarding answers to questions on Atomic Demolition Munitions which
had been asked during the visit;

(d) took note of the views expressed in the subsequent discussion of technical
and safety aspects of ADMS.

FAREWELL LUNCH FOR DEPARTING MEMBERS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the intention of the
Staff Group to give a farewell luncheon party to:

Dr. W.F. van Eekelen
Lt.Col. W.R. Rhodehamel
Col. G. Trakakis
Capt. E.H. Schorz
Capt. J.F. Barlow
(b) noted that this luncheon party would now take place on Tuesday, 29th June,
1971 and that further details would be circulated in good time.

VISIT BY THE STAFF GROUP TO HEADQUARTERS, STRATEGIC AIR COMMAND

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a suggestion by the Italian Representative that it would be a
valuable experience for the Staff Group if were able to visit the United States
Strategic Air Command Headquarters at Omaha; he suggested that the Staff Group
might take advantage of the fact that the Defence Review Committee intended to
go to Monterey in September 1971 or in April 1972 and that the German
Authorities had offered to provide free air transportation;

(b) noted general interest in this suggestion.

NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN AUTUMN 1971

4. (a) took note of a statement by the Belgian Representative who explained the
various possible dates for the next NPG Ministerial Meeting in Belgium and said
that it appeared to him that the best moment for the meeting would be on
27th/28th October, or alternatively 9th/10th November, 1971;

(b) noted that the 28th October was a Greek national holiday and the 26th
October was the date fixed for the opening of the new Permanent Headquarters for
COMIBERLIANT in Portugal;

(c) after detailed discussion agreed that the choice lay between 26th/27th
October or 9th/10th November, 1971;

(d) agreed to obtain reactions from Capitals as soon as possible and to discuss
the matter further at the next meeting with a view to informing the Belgian
Authorities without delay.

PROCEDURAL ARRANGEMENTS FOR SUBSEQUENT STAGES OF FOLLOW-ON STUDIES

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that Ministers at
Mittelwald had invited the Permanent Representatives to develop procedural
arrangements for handling the subsequent stages of the Follow-on use work
programme;

(b) he referred to the International Staff paper(1) and made the following

(1) the timing and participation in later phases of the work programme had not

stage;

(ii) the Mittelwald 'creed Minute did not specify that future procedural

the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group, should be circulated as a NPG
working paper with a view to its study and approval by Capitals; it would then
be circulated as a NPG document;

(f) agreed to continue discussion at the next meeting of the Staff Group.

NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE MEETING IN DECEMBER

7. The STAFF GROUP:
