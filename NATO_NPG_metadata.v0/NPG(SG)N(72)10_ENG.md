> # Future Meetings of the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent Representatives
> 
> Following a meeting of the Staff Group on 24th January 1972, it was agreed that four meetings of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Permanent Representatives would be held prior to the NPG Ministers' meeting in Denmark on 18th and 19th May 1972. The first meeting, scheduled for 11th February, will focus on the presentation of the Turkish Follow-on Use Study. The second meeting on 9th March will cover the Mediterranean Maritime Follow-on Use Study. The third meeting on 13th April is dedicated to the examination of draft reports required for the Spring NPG Ministerial Meeting. The final meeting on 2nd May will feature a presentation by SHAPE on the Battlefield Use Illustrative Study and a final exchange of views on the Ministerial Agenda. This last meeting was moved forward from 4th May to accommodate a three-day visit to Germany by the Council from 3rd to 5th May.

NPG(Staff Group)N(72)10

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
FUTURE MEETINGS OF THE NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP
PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
Note by the Secretary

At the meeting of the Staff Group held on Monday, 24th January, 1972, it was
agreed that four meetings of the NPG Permanent Representatives should take place
during the period leading up to the meeting of NPG Ministers to be held in
Denmark on 18th and 19th May, 1972. These meetings are as follows:

(a) Friday, 11th February (early afternoon – time to be notified later)
Presentation of the Turkish Follow-on Use Study
(b) Thursday, 9th March, at 3.30 p.m.
Presentation of the Mediterranean Maritime Follow-on Use Study (This meeting was
originally tentatively planned for 2/th February)

(c) Thursday, 13th April, at 3.30 p.m.
Examination by Permanent Representatives of the drafts of the various reports
required for the Spring NPG Ministerial Meeting.

(d) Tuesday, 2nd May, at 3.30 p.m.
Presentation by SHAPE of the Battlefield Use Illustrative Study, and final
exchange of views on matters on the Ministerial Agenda. (This meeting was
originally planned for 4th May but has been brought forward because of the 3-day
visit to Germany now scheduled for the Council on 3rd-5th May).

(Signed) M.H.G. WILSON
