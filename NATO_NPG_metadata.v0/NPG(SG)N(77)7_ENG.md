> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 24th January, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on January 24, 1977, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels, Belgium. The meeting's primary focus was included the confirmation of the Spring NPC ministerial meeting scheduled for June 8th and 9th, 1977, in Ottawa, Canada, with a discussion on air transportation and the venue at the Government Conference Centre. The group also noted progress on a draft Theatre Information and Engagement Systems (TIES) status report. Additionally, the Staff Group requested members to consider the participation of the Nuclear Planning Group in WINTEX 77, occurring from March 8th to 15th, 1977. Finally, the group discussed the revised Status of Work report, specifically regarding deadlines for the New Technology Study and Phase III, incorporating suggestions from the United Kingdom representative. The International Staff was invited to circulate the new version of the report as an NPC Working Paper in two languages.

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 24th

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared for the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

SPRING MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that it was now confirmed
that the Spring NPC ministerial meeting would take place in Ottawa on 8th and
9th June, 1977; he requested that participating countries should consider the
problem of air transportation as soon as possible and said that the
International Staff would be grateful for any offer of help;

(b) noted the remarks of the Canadian representative who said that the meeting
would take place in the Government Conference Centre next to the Château Laurier
hotel in Ottawa; he had hopes that a visit would be arranged for participants
after the Conference.

THEATRE INFORMATION AND ENGAGEMENT SYSTEMS (TIES)

3. The STAFF GROUP:

took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that satisfactory progress on

NPG PARTICIPATION IN WINTEX 77

4. The STAFF GROUP:

took note of a request by the Chairman that members should begin to consider the
problem of NPG participation in WINTEX 77 (active period 8th to 15th March
inclusive); he requested their views at the next meeting.

STATUS OF WORK REPORT

5. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the revised
version of the Staff Group Working Paper(1) and said that he hoped that, after
the present discussion, this paper could be circulated as an NPG working Paper
in two languages;

(b) took note of the further remarks of the Chairman concerning the desirability
of fixing deadlines for the completion of the New Technology Study and Phase
III;

(c) took note of the remarks of the UK representative who suggested that a date
for the New Technology Study might be useful but had doubts about the usefulness
of a firm date for Phase III;

(d) noted a measure of agreement with the UK suggestions;
