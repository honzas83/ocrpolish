> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters on Monday, 24th January, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened at NATO Headquarters on January 24, 1972, to address several administrative and strategic items. The group welcomed Group Captain R.J. Davenport of the United Kingdom Military Representative's staff. The meeting covered the firm dates for the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent Representatives' meetings in February, April, and May 1972, including briefings on the Turkish Follow-on Use Study and the Battlefield Use Illustrative Study. Discussions were held regarding a possible visit to the United States in June 1972, with representatives from Canada, Germany, and the United States discussing travel arrangements and potential visits to NORAD Headquarters at North Bay. The group also discussed the initiation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme, recommending that it begin in Spring 1972, while noting divergences in views regarding the composition of the ad hoc group to manage the work. Finally, the International Military Staff confirmed that the Battlefield Use Illustrative Study would be forwarded to the Secretary General by January 31, 1972. The next meeting was scheduled for February 7, 1972.

Representatives required for the Spring NPG Ministerial Meeting (Afternoon).

(iii) Thursday, 4th May, 1972 (2)
Presentation of the Battlefield Use Illustrative Study, and final exchange of
views on matters on the Ministerial Agenda (Afternoon).

(1) Later scheduled for the afternoon it may be changed to another day.
(2) In view of IFC visit to Germany, will probably be changed to Tuesday

POSSIBLY: STAFF GROUP VISIT TO THE UNITED STATES
3. THE STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the Staff
Group Notice NPG(Staff Group)N(72)8 of 20th January, 1972, amended to which were
copies of his letter to the United States Delegation concerning a possible visit
by the Staff Group to the United States, and the reply which he had received
from the United States representative;

(b) noted also the suggestion by the Chairman that the start of the visit might
be brought forward to the week beginning 5th June, now that it was known that
the Spring Ministerial meetings were all planned to take place in May;

(c) noted with appreciation the remarks of the Canadian and German
representatives who said that, in the event of the invitation being confirmed,
they would approach their authorities in the hope of obtaining free air
transport across the Atlantic for the Staff Group; noted also that the Canadian
representative advised that this would be easier to arrange early in June,
rather than later when troop rotation was at its height;

(d) noted the remarks of the United States representative who warned that free
military air transport in the United States should not be counted upon, and in
any case, if available, would not be known until the last moment; he said that
any reduction in the size of the Staff Group party would increase the chances of
obtaining military transportation within the United States;

(e) noted with appreciation the suggestion by the Canadian representative who
offered, if the Staff Group wished, to try to arrange for a visit to the NORAD
Headquarters at North Bay;
