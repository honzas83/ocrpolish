> # Allied Command Europe Senior Officers' Orientation Course
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) provides details regarding the Allied Command Europe Senior Officers' Orientation Course (I-31), conducted by the NATO School in Oberammergau, Germany. The course is designed to familiarise senior NATO officers and key personnel in staff appointments with the major concepts of NATO weapons' employment in Allied Command Europe. Eligibility is limited to officers of the rank of Colonel and above, and civilian officials of equivalent grade, requiring a COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance. The course lasts one week, with a tuition cost of 98 United States dollars per student. A schedule of eight courses is listed for 1980, with French translation available for all listed dates. The application process requires officials in Brussels to apply through the Nuclear Planning Directorate of the International Staff, while those in capitals and national commands should apply through their Ministry of Defence. Bids for vacancies must be submitted to SHAPE by 5th November 1979, with delegation members applying to Mr. R. Serio or Miss K. Winters by 31st October. Additionally, the document notes that billeting availability in Oberammergau will be severely limited between 25th May and 28th September 1980.

Note by the Chairman

The Allied Command Europe Senior Officers' Orientation Course (short title:
I-31) is conducted by the NATO School (SHAPE) in Oberammergau, Germany. The
purpose of the course is to familiarise senior NATO officers and key personnel
in staff appointments with the major concepts of NATO weapons' employment in
Allied Command Europe. Officers of the rank of Colonel and above and civilian
officials of equivalent grade are eligible to attend on a need-to-know basis. A
COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance is required. The course lasts one week – Monday
through Friday. Tuition costs are 98 United States dollars per student, which
for personnel of NATO Delegations must be paid by their national authorities.
The course is offered on the following dates in 1980. The annotation "F" in the
last column below shows courses for which French translation will be available.
