> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 110 Brussels on Friday, 17th March, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on March 17, 1972, at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The group took note of several papers, including a draft agenda for the NPG Spring Ministerial meeting and a draft advance press release. The meeting focused on the need to complete three working papers regarding the Mediterranean Maritime Study, the Report on the Illustrative Study of Battlefield Use, and Consultation Facilities and Procedures. The Staff Group discussed Consultation Facilities and Procedures and the Illustrative Study of Battlefield Use, agreeing that these two papers should be carefully related and revised. The International Staff was tasked with preparing revised versions of both documents and maintaining contact with the Council Operations and Exercise Committee and the NATO Situation Centre. Additionally, the group noted that an issues paper on the Mediterranean Maritime Study would be circulated soon. Finally, the group addressed arrangements for the NPG Ministerial meeting in Copenhagen, noting that the official dinner on May 15, 1972, would be formal dress. The next meeting was scheduled for March 27, 1972.

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the International
Staff's paper(1) on Consultation Facilities and Procedures and said that in view
of the relationship between the report on the Illustrative Battlefield Study and
the paper on Consultation Facilities and Procedures, it had been agreed at the

(b) took note of the views expressed and the suggestions made during the ensuing
discussion, and agreed to a number of changes to the text;

(c) invited the International Staff, in the light of the discussion, to prepare
a revised version of the paper in time for it to be re-examined at the next
meeting; invited the International Staff at the same time to maintain its
contacts with the Council Operations and Exercise Committee and with the NATO
Situation Centre with a view to verifying statements made in the paper and in
its Annex A;

(d) took up again briefly the discussion held at the last meeting on the report
on the Illustrative Study of Battlefield use, and took note of the views
expressed, in particular those of the German representative; agreed that this
paper, and that on Consultation Facilities, should be carefully related one to
the other;

(e) invited the International Staff, in the light of the views expressed, to
prepare a revised version of the report;

(f) agreed to discuss both papers at the next meeting.
MEDITERRANEAN MARITIME STUDY

took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he hoped to be able to
