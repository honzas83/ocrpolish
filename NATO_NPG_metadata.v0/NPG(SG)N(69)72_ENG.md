> # Notes of a Meeting Hold on 29th September, 1969
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on 29 September 1969 to coordinate administrative and substantive content for the upcoming November Ministerial Meeting. The group discussed the schedule for meetings of NPG Permanent Representatives, noting that the Danish Representative's Ambassador could not attend on 17 October and that the Secretary General's absence would prevent meetings during the week of 20-24 October. Regarding the November Ministerial Meeting, the group identified four principal papers: an Anglo-German report on Guidelines for Initial Tactical Use, a paper on Further Studies on Tactical Use, and a US-German report on theatre strike forces, and a Belgian document on Consultation. The group also discussed the agenda for the Ministerial Meeting, specifically the Italian proposal to move Consultation to Item I, which was opposed by the German Representative. The Staff Group invited the International Staff to prepare a new version of the paper on the Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons based on recent discussions. Additionally, the United States Delegation agreed to assist with obtaining visas for members of delegations attending the November meeting. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for 6 October 1969.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP) Notes of a meeting hold on 29th September,
1969

FUTURE MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled that meetings of the NPG
Permanent Representatives were scheduled for : Thursday, 2nd October Friday,
17th October Monday, 27th October

(b) noted the remarks of the Danish Representative who said that his Ambassador
could not be present on 17th October and that it would be a matter of regret for
him if another date could not be chosen;

(c) noted that it would not be possible to hold a meeting of Permanent
Representatives during the week 20th-24th October because the Secretary General
would be absent.

SITUATION REGARDING DOCUMENTS FOR THE NOVEMBER MINISTERIAL MEETING
2. The STAFF GROUP :
(i) noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the four principal
papers required for the NPG Ministerial Meeting in November were : - The Anglo-
German report on Guidelines for Initial Tactical Use, - Further Studies on
Tactical Use, - The US-German report on theatre strike forces, - The Belgian
document on Consultation

(b) noted that the Anglo-German report could be circulated as soon as Hilster
Schrader had signed his agreement; this was expected in the next few days;

(c) noted that the preparation of the paper on Further Studies was the present
preoccupation of the Staff Group, whose aim was to complete its work by 10th
October so that the paper might be sent to Capitala before its discussion at
Permanent Representative level;

(d) noted that the US-German report required final approval by Minister Schröder

available as soon as possible;

(e) noted that the Belgian paper on Consultation was already circulated.
AGENDA FOR THE MINISTERIAL MEETING IN NOVEMBER
3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled that any suggestions
concerning the agenda (1) for the Ministerial meeting, and any comments on the
Italian proposal to put Consultation as Item I, should be sent to him before
10th October, date on which the Agenda would be finalised;

(b) noted the view of the German Representative who recommended that
Consultation should remain as Item III;

(c) noted that the Italian Representative could accept this view if it was the
wish of the other Delegations.

FURTHER STUDIES ON THE TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) discussed the most recent International Staff paper on Further Studies on
the Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons (2), including an addendum handed out on a
limited basis;

(b) invited the International Staff to prepare, in the light of the comments and
suggestions made in the discussion, a new version of the paper as soon as
possible;

(c) agreed to discuss the new version of the paper at the next meeting.
