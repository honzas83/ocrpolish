> # Follow-Up Action to the Ministerial Meeting in Brussels on 26th and 27th October, 1971
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, details the actions required to implement decisions from the NPG Ministerial meeting in Brussels on October 26-27, 1971. It addresses strategic questions regarding the exchange of views on US briefings on the balance of strategic forces. It outlines the requirements for a battlefield use illustrative study to be prepared by SACEUR for the Spring 1972 meeting, with a target distribution date of February 1, 1972. The document also discusses the need for studies on the evolution of consultation and decision-making procedures for the use of nuclear weapons. It mentions a specific study of the air battle in the Central Region and the initiation of Phase II of the follow-on work programme. It addresses unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence and the planning for the next Ministerial meeting in Denmark. Additionally, it specifies the documents to be distributed to Portugal, including the Agreed Minute and reports on the Mediterranean Maritime Use and CENTAG Study. The note concludes by suggesting discussions on the requirements for Permanent Representatives' meetings in late 1971 and the general outline of the Spring 1972 Ministerial meeting.
>

BRUSSELS ON 26th AND 27th OCTOBER, 1971
Note by the Chairman
The following paragraphs comment on actions that might be required to implement
the decisions taken by the NFG Ministers in Brussels on 26th and 27th October,
as reflected in the Agreed Minute(1). It is proposed that we discuss these
actions at our meeting on Monday, 8th November.

I. STRATEGIC QUESTIONS
2. The Ministers agreed to develop further the practice of conducting a free
exchange of views against the background of US briefings on the balance of
strategic forces (paragraph 2 of NPG/D(71)15). Although no specific measures
were mentioned by the Ministers, the Staff Group may wish to discuss at an
appropriate occasion the question of what steps, if any, might be taken in
advance of Ministerial meetings to facilitate the desired free exchange of
views.

SACEUR to prepare, for Ministerial consideration in the Spring of 1972, a
further illustrative study involving battlefield use (paragraph 6 of

letter. It will be recalled that SHAPE had already indicated that it is
proceeding with the development of a battlefield use study and would be
prepared, if requested, to make a presentation at the Spring 1972 meeting. In
this connection, it is noted that the illustrative study document should be
circulated at least two months prior to the date of the Ministerial meeting(1).
Since the meeting will probably take place in April or May 1972, a target date
of 1st February, 1972 for the distribution of the study document to the NEF
would appear to be appropriate. It is also anticipated that SHAPE would proceed
with the preparation of a presentation, similar to the Maritime Use presentation
at the last meeting, with a view to giving a preliminary review to the Permanent
Representatives as early as mid-March 1972. It is also expected that the Staff
Group would begin drafting a Permanent Representatives' report on the
Battlefield Use Study shortly after its receipt from SHAPE.

4. The Ministers invited the Permanent Representatives, with the assistance of
the NATO Military Authorities, to prepare studies for Ministerial consideration
which would, on the basis of the Mediterranean maritime use study and the
forthcoming battlefield use study, illustrate the evolution of the situation and
the procedures that would be involved and the types of information that would be
required, both at the Council/DPC level and in national capitals, for
consultation and decision-making by political authorities on requests for use of
nuclear weapons (paragraph 7 of NPG/D(71)15). A number of questions will need to
be examined by the Staff Group in dealing with this requirement, e.g. would it
be feasible and appropriate to prepare a study for Ministerial consideration at
the next meeting? If so, should the study cover only one of the use situations,
or should an attempt be made to cover both situations? It is suggested that the
Staff Group have an initial exchange of views on this matter at its next meeting
so that consensus on an approach can be reached as soon as possible.

III. STUDIES ON FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS

5. The Ministers invited the Permanent Representatives to make arrangements for
a specific study of the air battle in the Central Region (paragraph 12 of
NPG/D(71)15). As a first step, the Staff Group may wish to discuss at the next
meeting the possible alternatives for handling this question, including such
matters as potential participants, terms of reference and timing. In due course,
it will probably be desirable to prepare a paper for Permanent Representative
approval setting out the proposed arrangements.

6. The Ministers indicated that written comments on the CENTAG study from the
countries of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee would be of value in later
phases of the follow-on work programme and would be welcomed (paragraph 13 of
NPG/D(71)15). Since all NDAC countries have received the Brussels Agreed Minute,
no further action seems to be required.

7. The Ministers invited the Permanent Representatives to develop proposals for
the initiation of Phase II of the follow-on work programme to be considered at
their next meeting (paragraph 15 of NPG/D(71)15). The Staff Group should have an
initial discussion of this problem at its next meeting so that the drafting of a
proposed Permanent Representatives' report can begin in the near future.

IV. UNRESOLVED PROBLEMS RELATED TO TACTICAL NUCLEAR DEFENCE
8. With regard to paragraph 18(a), (b) and (c) of NPG/D(71)15, the invitation to
the NATO Military Authorities to prepare reports for the Nuclear Planning Group
is being confirmed by letter from the Secretary General to the Chairman of the
Military Committee. At an appropriate time in the future, after the NATO
Military Authorities have had a chance to work out their arrangements and timing
for developing the requested reports, the Staff Group will wish to examine the
question of how and when the various subjects will be placed before the
Ministers at future meetings.

9. No further action is required with regard to the invitation made by the
Ministers to the Nuclear Planning Group countries in paragraph 19 of
NPG/D(71)15, since all these countries have received the Brussels Agreed Minute.
However, they should probably be kept informed concerning plans to include this
question on the Ministerial agenda in the future.

10. With regard to the proposals for possible future work mentioned in paragraph
20 of NPG/D(71)15, the importance of which is underlined also in paragraph 11 of
the same document in the context of follow-on studies, the Staff Group will want
to give some thought to whether any suggestions for further work should be
developed for Permanent Representatives' consideration with a view to placing
them on the next Ministerial agenda. This matter might be looked at during a
Staff Group meeting in late December or early January.

V. TIME OF NEXT MINISTERIAL MEETING
11. The Ministers accepted the invitation by the Danish Government to hold the
next Ministerial meeting in Denmark (paragraph 21 of NPG/D(71)15). It is
suggested that the Staff Group might have a preliminary exchange of views on our
next meeting on the possible dates, taking into account the time needed to
prepare for the Ministerial meeting, the schedule of holidays, and the need to
coordinate with other Ministerial meetings scheduled for next Spring.

VI. DISTRIBUTION OF BASIC DOCUMENTATION TO PORTUGAL
12. In accordance with established procedure, copies of the Brussels Ministerial
meeting's basic documents not yet in possession of Portugal should be sent to
that country. It is suggested that these should be:

(a) Agreed Minute – NPG/D(71)15.
(b) Report by the Permanent Representatives on SACEUR's Illustrative Study –
Mediterranean Maritime Use – NPG/D(71)10.

(c) Report by the Permanent Representatives on the CENTAG Study – NPG/D(71)11.
(d) Study of Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons – CENTAG Area –
NPG/Study(71)6 – Vol. I

(e) Report by the Permanent Representatives on the Status of Work in Progress –
NPG/D(71)12.

(f) Report by the Permanent Representatives on Unresolved Problems related to
Tactical Nuclear Defence – NPG/D(71)13.

In connection with the above list, it should be noted that Portugal has already
received SACEUR's illustrative study (NPG/Study(71)4) and PO/71/226. It should
also be noted that it is proposed to distribute only Volume I of the CENTAG
Study which is classified NATO SECRET. Portugal would be informed that, because
of their classification (CTS ATMAL), Volumes II, III and IV of the CENTAG Study
were not being provided as part of the basic documentation but that copies could
be examined on a need basis in the ATMAL Registry of the International
Secretariat.

VII. PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES' MEETINGS
14. The Staff Group may wish to discuss what requirements, if any, there may be

the general outline of the next Ministerial meeting and the nature and timing of
the steps to be taken in preparing for it.
