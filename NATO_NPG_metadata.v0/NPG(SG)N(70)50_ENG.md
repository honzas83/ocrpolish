> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 25th May, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on May 25, 1970, to coordinate preparations for the Venice meeting. The group took note of a briefing by Colonel H.R. Panell of the United States Delegation on atomic security, as well as requirements agreed upon by the United States and Italy. The meeting also addressed the request from the Italian Representative for notification of atomic couriers' arrival details. It was noted that an advance press release for the Venice meeting would be issued in Brussels on June 5, 1970. Regarding the status report on follow-on studies, the group noted that a Turkish Annex would be added as an addendum. The agenda for the Venice meeting was adjusted to accommodate the schedule of the United Kingdom's Defence Minister Healey, moving Item II to be taken first. Additionally, the group discussed seating arrangements, specifically a request from Norway for an extra seat at the conference table. Finally, the group considered future work, including the draft concept for the role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces and a revision of the provisional Tactical Guidelines, and agreed to hold its next meeting on either June 12 or 15, 1970.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 25th May, 1970

ATOMIC SECURITY ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE VENICE MEETING

1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a briefing given by Colonel H.R. Panell, Security Advisor to
the United States Delegation, on the subject of ATOMIC Security as applicable to
the Venice meeting;

(b) took note of a paper (1) distributed by the International Staff setting out
the principal requirements and arrangements for ATOMIC Security as agreed by the
United States Security Advisor and General Spalla of the Italian Delegation;

(c) noted the request of the Italian Representative that the names of ATOMIC
couriers and their date, time and place of arrival in Venice should be notified
to his Delegation as soon as possible.

ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE - VENICE MEETING

2. The STAFF GROUP :
noted a statement by the Chairman who said that all Delegations had now approved
the Advance Press Release, which would be made in Brussels at 3 p.m. on Friday,
5th June, with simultaneous release in capitals at the option of the country
concerned.

ST/ATUS REPORT ON FOLLOW-ON STUDIES

3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who said that the Turkish Authorities had
requested that a Turkish Annex be added to the Status Report (1); this would be
issued shortly in the form of an addendum to the document;

(b) noted that the Agenda (2) for the Ministerial Meeting would not need to be
changed because the Turkish Minister would still make the oral status report
mentioned under II.2(a).

AGENDA FOR VENICE MEETING

4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted, with reference to Defence Minister Healey's inability to be present
during all of the Venice Meeting, that Item II on the Agenda (2) would be taken
first, Item I being reserved for the afternoon of 6th June, when Minister Healey
would be present;

(b) noted that this change did not necessitate a change to the form or text of
the Agenda.

SEATING ARRANGEMENTS AT THE VENICE MEETING

5. The STAFF GROUP :
noted a statement by the Italian Representative, who recalled the Norwegian
request for an extra Seat at the Conference table, and requested that he might
be informed as soon as possible of the wishes of the Group.

FUTURE WORK

6. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who invited the Staff Group to consider,
in the context of the Ministerial agenda, what would be the work flowing from
the Meeting of Ministers in Venice, how this should be addressed, and on what
time schedule;
