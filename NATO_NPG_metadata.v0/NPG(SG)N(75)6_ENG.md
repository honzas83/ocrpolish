> # Next Meetings of the NPC Staff Group
> 
> This document is a notice from the Secretary of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, dated 16th January 1975, outlining the schedule for upcoming meetings. The first meeting is scheduled for Monday, 20th January 1975, to discuss the Phase II Study in the presence of experts and Study Team leaders. A second meeting, organized by the United States authorities with the United States Technology Study Team, is scheduled for Tuesday, 21st and Wednesday, 22nd January 1975. A third meeting on Monday, 27th January 1975, is intended to be a normal Staff Group meeting to consider the implications of the Nunn Amendment and the forthcoming report by SACEUR, agree on dates for NPG Permanent Representatives meetings, and prepare for the NPG Spring Ministerial meeting, including the possibility of including Selective Employment Planning (SEP) in the agenda. Additionally, the Secretary reminds members that ATOMAL material may be discussed during the meetings on the 21st and 22nd of January and requests certificates of ATOMAL clearance for all attending staff.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
NEXT MEETINGS
Note by the Secretary
The next meetings of the NPC Staff Group will be as follows:
(a) Monday, 20th January, 1975, at 2.30 p.m.
Discussion of the Phase II Study(1) in the presence of experts from capitals and
the Study Team leaders;

(b) Tuesday, 21st and Wednesday, 22nd January, 1975, at 2.30 a.m.
Attendance at a two-day meeting organised by the United States authorities with
the presence of the United States Technology Study Team;

(c) Monday, 27th January, 1975, at 3 p.m.
A normal Staff Group meeting whose principal objects will be:
(i) Further consideration of the implications of the Nunn Amendment in the light
of the forthcoming report by SACEUR,

(ii) to agree upon the dates of the next NGP Permanent Representatives meetings,
(iii) to continue preparations for the NGP Spring Ministerial meeting and, inter
alia, to consider the possibility of including Selective Employment Planning
(SEP) in the ministerial agenda.
