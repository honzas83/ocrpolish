> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 20th December, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on December 20, 1971, to address several strategic and administrative matters. The group warmly endorsed farewell words for Mr. R. Braband, who left to become Director of Force Planning. Regarding the air battle in the Central Region, the group formally accepted an offer from the United States Authorities to conduct a preliminary assessment of methodology and resource requirements for a specific study. They also discussed the necessity of updating NATO nuclear planning directives, specifically the Concept for the Role of Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE, due to the introduction of the POSEIDON weapon system. While acknowledging the system's increased striking power and deterrent potential, some representatives expressed concerns regarding its effects on consultation, control, and targeting. Additionally, the group agreed that military comments should be requested for the upcoming Mediterranean Maritime Study and discussed the possibility of a briefing by the Study Team in February. Finally, the group noted the the departure of the Italian Representative, Mr. E. Da Rin, and arranged for a lunch party to mark the occasion.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 20th December, 1971

FAREWELL

I. The STAFF GROUP:
warmly endorsed the words of farewell addressed by the Chairman to Mr. R.
Braband who, after having been a member and vice-chairman of the Staff Group
since its inception, has now left to become Director of Force Planning.

THE AIR STUDY – FOLLOW-ON USE STUDIES

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussion held in
the Staff Group on 22nd November concerning the wish of Ministers(1) that
arrangements be made for a specific study of the air battle in the Central
Region; this would be an additional study in Phase I of the follow-on study
programme; he reminded the Staff Group of the proposal made by the United States
Authorities who had offered to make a preliminary assessment of the methodology
and resource requirements for the study; he suggested that the Staff Group might
now wish, after further discussion if necessary, to accept formally the helpful
offer of the United States Authorities;

(b) took note, in the ensuing discussion, of the unanimous and appreciative

(c) invited the United States Representative to convey this acceptance to his

(d) noted that a number of countries would be prepared to help in this study;

UNITED STATES NAVAL FORCES IMPROVEMENTS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Defence
Ministers, at their meeting on 8th December, 1971, had noted(1) that the Nuclear
Planning Group might wish to consider whether an updating of the relevant NATO
nuclear planning directives, in particular the Concept for the Role of Nuclear
Strike Forces in ACE(2), might be appropriate as a consequence of the
introduction of the POSEIDON weapon system; he invited views on this matter;

(b) agreed that some updating of the relevant directives, and in particular of
the Concept paper(2), would undoubtedly be appropriate;

(c) noted that it would be necessary to decide whether modifications to these
directives should be sent through normal channels (NPG – NDAC – DPC) for
approval at the Ministerial meetings at the end of 1972, or whether the date of
the implementation of the force improvements (bringing into service of the
POSEIDON weapon system on 1st July, 1972) would require modifications to be in
force by that date (and therefore perhaps handled outside Ministerial channels);

(d) whilst noting that the POSEIDON weapon system would provide NATO with an
advanced weapon system in terms of increased target flexibility, greater
striking power and significantly improved deterrent potential, some
representatives felt that there was a need to look more closely at the possible
effects upon consultation, control and targeting which the use of these new
multiple warhead weapons might have;

(e) agreed to continue the discussion at the first meeting of the Staff Group in
January.

MEDITERRANEAN MARITIME STUDY – FOLLOW-ON STUDIES

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the Mediterranean
Maritime Study was expected to be received within the next fortnight and would
be reproduced and circulated probably about mid-January; he suggested that time
might be saved if the Staff Group was prepared to agree now that the NATO
Military Authorities should be invited to submit comments upon this Study;

(b) agreed that military comments should be requested, and invited the
representatives of the Military Committee and SHAPE to convey the substance of
this decision to their authorities in advance of the formal written requests
which would follow;

(c) agreed to discuss at a future meeting whether the Mediterranean Maritime
Study should be placed on the agenda of the 1972 Spring or the Autumn NPG
Ministerial meetings;

(d) noted a statement by the Italian Representative who said that he believed it
might be possible to organize a briefing by the Study Team in February; agreed
to discuss the date of the briefing at a future meeting.

DEPARTURE

5. The STAFF GROUP:
noted with regret that Mr. E.Da Rin, the Italian Representative and doyen of the
Staff Group, would be leaving NATO at the end of January; agreed that this
occasion should be marked by a lunch party, and invited the International Staff
to take the necessary action.
