> # Atomic Demolition Munitions
> 
> This document consists of a cover note from H. B. Seim, Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), and a draft letter from the Secretary General to the Chairman of the Military Committee. The cover note, dated 4th December 1970, informs the staff group that a draft letter is attached regarding the implementation of Defence Planning Committee (DPC) decisions concerning Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADM). The draft letter specifies that on 2nd December 1970, the Ministers of the Defence Planning Committee approved Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of Atomic Demolition Munitions and directed that these be issued as policy guidance for national authorities and a directive to the NATO Military Authorities. The Secretary General requests the Chairman of the Military Committee to ensure that appropriate steps are taken by the NATO Military Authorities to implement this Ministerial decision. Additionally, the draft letter recalls that the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) invited their Permanent Representatives to monitor progress, and requests a status report to be submitted by 1st August 1971.

ANNEX to NPG(Staff Group)N(70)92
DRAFT LETTER FROM SECRETARY GENERAL TO CHAIRMAN, MILITARY COMMITTEE
ATOMIC DEMOLITION MUNITIONS
At their meeting on 2nd December 1970, the Ministers of the Defence Planning
Committee:

(a) approved the Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of Atomic
Demolition Munitions(1);

(b) directed that it be issued as a DPC document as policy guidance for national
authorities and as a directive to the NATO Military Authorities.

2. The guidelines will be issued as a document in the DPC/D series in the near
future. Yourself and the Major NATO Commanders will receive copies.

3. I should like to request you to ensure that appropriate steps are taken by
the NATO Military Authorities to implement the Ministerial decision.

4. In this context I should like to recall to your attention that at their
recent meeting in Ottawa, the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group invited
their Permanent Representatives to monitor the progress being made by the NATO
Military Authorities in implementing the Special ADM Guidelines(2). In execution
of this responsibility, the NPG Permanent Representatives request that a status
report be submitted to me for circulation to the NPG delegations by 1st August
1971. I would appreciate if you could take the necessary action.

(1) NPG/D(70)9, 9th October, 1970
(2) NPG/D(70)12, paragraph 21, 4th November, 1970
