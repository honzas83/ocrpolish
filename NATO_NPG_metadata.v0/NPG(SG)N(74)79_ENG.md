> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Thursday 18th and Friday 19th July, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting in Brussels on July 18th and 19th, 1974, to address several key strategic and military issues. The group took note of documents regarding Norway's nuclear and base policy, the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process, and a study on Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy and military doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear weapons. Additionally, the group discussed progress made by an ad hoc group consisting of Germany, the United Kingdom, and the United States on the implications of technological developments concerning the tactical use of nuclear weapons. The meeting also focused on the communication of NATO's intention to use nuclear weapons, agreeing to a two-stage approach for a study and a subsequent report by Permanent Representatives. The group further agreed to consider a draft report on the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Air Defence Use at their next meeting and requested a draft agenda for the upcoming Autumn Meeting of NPG Ministers in November 1974. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next Staff Group meeting on July 23rd, 1974.

DOCUMENTATION

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that the following documents had been or were about to be circulated:
(a) NPG/D(74)8, 8th July, 1974: Norway's nuclear and base policy;
(b) DPC/D(74)17, 24th July, 1974: Military Committee Function in the Nuclear
Consultation Process;

(c) DPC/D(74)18, not yet dated: Study on Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy
and Military Doctrine for the Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons.

STUDY OF THE POLITICAL AND MILITARY IMPLICATIONS OF
TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENTS CONCERNING THE TACTICAL USE OF
NUCLEAR WEAPONS

2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the German representative who said that good
progress had been made at the meeting the previous week in Washington of the ad
hoc group (Germany; UK, US) for the study on the implications of Technological
Developments; a draft preliminary report was nearly ready and, after examination
in the three capitals, would be circulated to the Staff Group at the end of
August or early September.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTION TO USE NUCLEAR WEAPONS

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the revised draft
working paper(1) and pointed out the changes which had been made; he recalled
that the Staff Group had been moving towards a decision to treat the problem in
two stages - a study prepared by the Staff Group, followed by a report by
Permanent Representatives to Ministers which could bring in further points,
recommendations and follow-up actions; he proposed that the aim should be to
agree not later than mid-September upon an NPG Working Paper for circulation to
Capitals and subsequent approval by Permanent Representatives in mid-October;

(b) agreed to the two-stage approach, noting however that some overlap could be
accepted;

(c) discussed a number of points concerning the origin and aim of the Study, its
presentation and content and the possible need for a summary; agreed to a number
of changes and additions;

(d) examined the revised working paper paragraph by paragraph and agreed to a
number of modifications to the text;

(e) invited the Chairman to prepare, in the light of the discussion, a further
revision of the Working Paper for circulation if possible not later than Friday,
25th July; requested that a draft summary be attached to the new version;

(f) agreed to await preliminary reactions from Capitals before beginning work on
a draft report by Permanent Representatives.
