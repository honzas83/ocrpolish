> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 18th June, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on June 18, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key strategic and operational issues. The group discussed the preparation of an illustrative study on nuclear air defence in a land environment for the Spring 1974 NPG Ministers meeting. They also coordinated the dates for the Autumn 1973 NPG Ministerial meeting, specifically addressing scheduling conflicts for Italy and the United States. The meeting covered operational concepts and doctrine, referencing reports from SHAPE and the Military Committee, and requested a draft paper for further discussion. There was a discussion on Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy and military doctrine, noting the need for more countries to assist the Netherlands in study leadership. The group also addressed the communication of NATO's intentions to enemies, the public, and other countries, and the communication of the same. The German representative expressed the position of the German government regarding the impact of SALT II and the MBFR on NATO defence. the group noted the Chairman's intention to circulate a note on consultation procedures and facilities. Finally, the meeting concluded with a reminder about a farewell lunch for Colonel J.B. Spiegelenberg and the date for the next meeting on June 25, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 18th
June, 1973

FUTURE NPG ACTIVITIES - ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman, who said that in accordance with the
wishes of the Staff Group, he had submitted to the Secretary General a draft of
a letter to the Chairman of the Military Committee inviting him to ask SACEUR to
prepare an illustrative study dealing with nuclear air defence, in a land
environment, for study by NPG Ministers at their meeting in the Spring 1974;
copies of this letter would be sent to Delegations under a PO reference.

2. The STAFF GROUP:
DATES OF THE AUTUMN MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS
(a) took note of the latest positions regarding the choice of dates for the NPG
Autumn Ministerial meeting, and observed that, subject to confirmation by the
United States Authorities, the dates of 6th and 7th November or thereabouts
would suit all countries except Italy; requested the Italian representative to
ask his Authorities whether 8th and 9th November would be acceptable to them;

(b) expressed the hope that early replies could be received from the Italian and
United States authorities, so that a firm decision could be taken in the near
future; this would be helpful to the Netherlands authorities.

OPERATIONAL CONCEPTS AND DOCTRINE
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to the report by
SHAPE(1) on Operational Concepts and Doctrine, and to the Military Committee's
comments(2) on the SHAPE report; he recalled that these documents had been
initiated as a result of former Secretary General Brosio's paper(3) on
unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence; he invited
representatives to discuss and comment upon these two NPG documents with a view
to high-lighting points suitable for inclusion in a report by Permanent
Representatives to Ministers at the Autumn 1973 meeting; he also said that a
corrigendum, covering six non-substantive points in the SHAPE report, would be
circulated shortly;

(b) took note of the suggestions made during the ensuing discussion;
(c) invited the International Staff, in the light of the views expressed, to
prepare a draft paper as a basis for further discussion in the Staff Group.

WARSAW PACT POLITICO-MILITARY STRATEGY AND MILITARY DOCTRINE
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that at the last
meeting of the Staff Group the hope had been expressed that more countries, in
addition to Germany and the United States (on a limited basis) would be prepared
to help the Study Leaders (the Netherlands) in their work on Warsaw Pact
strategy and doctrine;

(b) noted that instructions were still awaited by certain interested countries
and agreed to raise the matter again as soon as possible.

COMMUNICATION OF NATO'S INTENTIONS

5. The STAFF GROUP:
recalled its discussion at the last meeting on the formation of a study team to
examine the communication of NATO's intentions to the enemy, to other countries
and to the public, and noted that no instructions had yet been received by
representatives of countries who might be interested in joining Norway and the
United States in this work.

NUCLEAR ASPECTS OF SALT II AND MBFR

6. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the German Representative who referred to paragraph
3 of the Ankara Agreed Minute(1) concerning the means of assessing the impact on
NATO defence of SALT II and the possible nuclear implications of MBFR; he said
that his Government's position was that, although it might be necessary to
strengthen present methods, it did not appear necessary at this time to create
any new machinery.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES

7. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he intended to circulate
a note on consultation procedures and facilities in time for it to serve as a
basis for discussion at the next meeting of the Staff Group.

FAREWELL LUNCH

8. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman who reminded representatives that the
farewell lunch party for Colonel J.B. Spiegelenberg would take place at 12.30
for 1 p.m. next Monday, 25th June, 1973.
