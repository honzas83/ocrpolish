> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday and Tuesday, 30th and 31st August, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on August 30 and 31, 1971, to address several key nuclear strategy and planning issues. The meeting began with a tribute to Major J. Aagaard of the Danish Delegation. The group discussed an Illustrative Study on maritime use in the Mediterranean and agreed to prepare a revised version as a Nuclear Planning Group working paper for Permanent Representatives. They also addressed unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence and Special Atomic Demolition Munition (ADM) guidelines, requesting revised versions of these papers for further review. Additionally, the group reviewed the status of work on follow-on use studies and AD 70 follow-up action. Regarding regional studies, the group noted the status of the Turkish Study and the CENTAG Area Study, emphasizing the need for military comments. The meeting also involved discussions on the draft agenda for the October Ministerial Meeting, revealing a divergence of views between the United States and several European nations regarding the length of the comprehensive agenda. Finally, the group planned future meetings for Permanent Representatives and scheduled their next Staff Group meeting for September 6, 1971, in Brussels.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday and Tuesday, 30th and 31st August, 1971

TRIBUTE TO MAJOR J. AAGAARD
1. The STAFF GROUP:
endorsed a statement by the Chairman who expressed on behalf of the Staff Group
his sorrow for the untimely death of Major J. Aagaard to the Danish
Representative and requested him to convey the deepest sympathy of all present
to the members of the Danish Delegation and to the bereaved family.

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDY – MARITIME USE IN THE MEDITERRANEAN
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Staff Group
had already examined the first five paragraphs of the International Staff paper
on the Illustrative Study(1) at the last meeting, and invited members to
continue their discussion;

(b) took note of the views expressed and suggestions made, and agreed to a
number of changes in the text;

(c) invited the International Staff, in the light of the discussion, and after
consultation with the Italian Representative who was unable to be present, to
prepare a revised version of the document, in the form of a NPG working paper;

(d) agreed that this working paper should be sent to Capitals without delay,
with a view to its subsequent discussion by NPG Permanent Representatives at a
meeting to be held at the end of September.

UNRESOLVED PROBLEMS RELATED TO TACTICAL NUCLEAR DEFENCE

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussion of the
International Staff's paper(1) on Unresolved Problems which had taken place the
meeting held on 23rd August, and drew attention to the changes proposed at that

be held in late September.

STUDIES ON FOLLOW-ON USE – STATUS OF WORK IN PROGRESS

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussion on the Status
of Work report(1) held in the Staff Group on 17th August, and invited further

a revised text for issue as a NPG working paper for consideration in Capitals,
and for subsequent discussion at a meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives to
be held at the end of September.

AD 70 FOLLOW-UP ACTION – NUCLEAR ASPECTS

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note that a draft paper(2) on AD 70 Follow-up Action had been issued by
the International Staff;

(b) agreed to discuss this paper at the next meeting and if possible to agree to
a version which might immediately be issued in the form of a NPG working paper
for study in Capitals and subsequent discussion by NPG Permanent Representatives
at the end of September.

REGIONAL FOLLOW-ON STUDIES

7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted with regard to the Turkish Study(1) that the Turkish Representative
had not yet received instructions from his Authorities as to whether comments
should be invited from the Military Authorities;

(b) noted that approval of the CENTAG Area Study(2) had not yet been received;
(c) reaffirmed their view that military comments should normally be obtained,
subject to the agreement of the concerned country, from the Major NATO
Commanders concerned, and from the Chairman of the Military Committee on a
personal basis;

(d) noted a statement by the Chairman who said that Permanent Representatives
would doubtless wish to complete their report on the CENTAG Area Study by the
end of September; he expressed the hope that authorisation for the issue of the
CENTAG Area Study would be forthcoming very soon(3);

DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE NEG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN OCTOBER

8. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussions which
had already taken place in the Staff Group on the subject of the Ministerial
Agenda, and made the following points:

(1) there appeared to be general acceptance for not adding the interim report on
the implementation of ADM Guidelines to the Agenda;

(ii) a number of representatives wished to remove from the Agenda the Item V (AD
70 Follow-up) and Item VI (Status of Work on Study Programme);

(1) NPG/Study(71)7, 15th July, 1971
(2) NPG/Study(71)6, not yet circulated

September, and the CENTAG Area Study is now issued.
