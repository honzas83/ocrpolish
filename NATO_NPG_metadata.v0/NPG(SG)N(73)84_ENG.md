> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Friday, 31st August, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on August 31, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group discussed a draft Ministerial Agenda, noting the status of briefings from the United States and the possibility of including background information on the NPG's mission in the Secretary General's introductory remarks. They also addressed the follow-up to the Defense and Politics Committee (DPC) Ministerial and the status of the Follow-on Use Work Programme. Regarding early combined use of conventional and nuclear capabilities, the group reviewed a draft report by Permanent Representatives and discussed the relationship between early combined use and initial and follow-on use. Additionally, the group agreed to a proposal by SHAPE to amend consultation procedures and facilities. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next meeting on September 4, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Friday, 31st
August, 1973

DRAFT MINISTERIAL AGENDA
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who introduced a draft Ministerial
Agenda(1) based on previous discussions in the Staff Group, and drew attention
to two items which had not been included on the Agenda:

(i) follow-up to points raised in the previous DPC Ministerial;
(ii) status of Follow-on Use Work Programme;
(b) noted that the United States member was not yet in a position to confirm the
briefing scheduled under Item I but had no indication that it would not be
agreed by his authorities;

(c) in response to a proposal by the Netherlands Representative, invited the
Chairman to explore the possibility of including some background information on
the NPG's mission in the Secretary General's introductory remarks at the
Ministerial meeting;

(d) in this connection, noted the suggestion by the Chairman that the Staff
Group could usefully devote some time to a review of NPG's activities before the
next Ministerial meeting;

(e) with respect to the paper that the Secretary General would circulate for
item VI (Future Work Programme), invited the Chairman to discuss its possible
content with the Staff Group and agreed that it would also be useful for the
Secretary General to discuss the subject with the Permanent Representatives at
their meeting on 4th October;

(f) on the subject of follow-up to the DPC Ministerial, agreed to consider after
The Hague meeting whether an NPG response to the DPC Ministers in December was
needed and, if so, whether it should be a separate report or an NPG input to the
report by DPC Permanent Representatives;

(g) with respect to the Follow-on Use Work Programme, invited the Phase II Study
Team to develop a status report as soon as possible after the planned meeting
during the week of 10th September and agreed that, unless the status report
contained matters of substance, it should be handled at Permanent
Representatives level and not appear on the Ministerial agenda;

(h) agreed that the draft Ministerial agenda should be circulated as an NPG
Working Paper for consideration by Permanent Representatives at their meeting of
4th October, noting that if that meeting were postponed the final agenda would
have to be circulated without an opportunity for discussion by Permanent
Representatives in order not to interfere with preparations in capitals for the
Ministerial meeting.

possible report by Permanent Representatives on early combined use of
conventional and nuclear capabilities(1);
