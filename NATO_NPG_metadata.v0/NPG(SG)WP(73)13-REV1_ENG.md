> # Follow-On Use Work Programme Status Report
> 
> This document consists of a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, H.B. Seim, and a draft status report on the Follow-On Use Work Programme as of October 1, 1973. The report outlines the progress of the programme, which is divided into three phases: Phase I (basic data inputs), Phase II (comparative analysis and synthesis), and Phase III (formulation of political guidelines). Phase I was concluded in May 1973, and an ad hoc study team comprising representatives from the United States, the Federal Republic of Germany, and the United Kingdom is currently developing the Phase II report. The study team's report suggests that new developments in guidance systems could significantly impact tactical nuclear weapon use and recommends a separate study on these implications. The document also seeks guidance from the Ministers on whether the status report should be included on the Ministerial Agenda for a meeting in The Hague, and how to handle the Study Team's proposal for a new study. The study team has also produced working papers on collateral damage, deterrence, and weaponry, and is preparing a paper on political control.
>

STATUS OF WORK IN PROGRESS AS OF 1st OCTOBER, 1973
I. PURPOSE
This report summarises the status of work in progress in the programme for the
study of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons(1) as of 1st October, 1973.

II. BACKGROUND
2. At their meeting in November, 1969, the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning
Group approved the Follow-on Use Work Programme and invited the Permanent
Representatives to make arrangements for the fulfilment of the initial work, to
monitor the work programme on a continuing basis and to propose further work as
appropriate(2). In discharging their monitoring function, the Permanent
Representatives have made several brief progress reports in the past(3).

3. As one of its primary objectives, the Work Programme seeks to provide,
through its exploration of the implications and interactions of follow-on
tactical use of nuclear weapons, a basis for the Ministers to develop policy
proposals, as appropriate, for submission to the Defence Planning Committee. In
this context, the NFG has agreed that the Work Programme would consist of three
major components: Phase I (preparation of basic data inputs in the form of
studies and related comments from countries belonging

III. STATUS
4. Phase I of the Work Programme came to a conclusion in May 1973 with
Ministerial consideration of the last of the nine studies envisaged for that
part of the programme. Following a decision(2) by the Ministers in May 1972 to
initiate Phase II(3), an ad hoc study team, composed of participants from the
United States, the Federal Republic of Germany and the United Kingdom, has been
engaged for the past year in developing a Phase II report. A brief summary of
the status of this work as of 15th September, 1973, prepared by the study team,
is attached at Annex.

5. We note that paragraph 5 of the Phase II Study Team status report points out
that current developments, especially in the field of new guidance systems,
could have a potentially important impact on the whole complex of questions
surrounding the tactical use of nuclear weapons but observes that a full
examination of the subject would be beyond its terms of reference. The Study
Team therefore suggests that the NPG should consider the advisability of a
separate study of the political and military implications of these new
developments. We see considerable merit in the Study Team's proposal.

PHASE II STUDY TEAM REPORT

STATUS AS OF 15th SEPTEMBER, 1973

1. Since submitting its first status report last April, the FRG, UK, US Ad Hoc
Study Team has continued its work toward preparation of a provisional Phase II
report. Development and review of papers dealing with subjects relevant to the
Phase I studies have continued, and the Study Team met again in Washington on
12th-14th september

2. At the Washington meeting working papers on Collateral Damage, Aspects of
Deterrence, and Weaponry were tentatively approved by the Team. New drafts on
Enemy Response, the Function of Conventional Forces in Follow-on Use, Use in
Extended Geographical Areas, and the Relationship of Follow-on Use to Initial
Use were considered and revised. As noted in their previous status report,
because of the clear interrelationship between the various subjects being
studied, all papers are being kept under review as the work of the Team
proceeds.

3. Preparation of a new paper on the subject of Political Control was discussed
by the Study Team at the Washington meeting. A working paper will be prepared
for initial review by the Team at the next meeting in London.

4. The Study Team has noted that NPQ Ministers, at their last meeting in Ankara
in May of 1973, invited the Permanent Representatives to consider the value of a
conceptual analysis of the nuclear aspects of deterrence. In view of the work
which the Team itself has done in this area, it has decided to circulate, as a
contribution to consideration of the question, the working paper which it has
prepared on "Aspects of Deterrence." The Study Team emphasises that the paper
does not represent national positions, but only the views of the members of the
Team.

5. In the course of its work, the Study Team has considered the points made by
former Secretary General Brosio in his PO/71/226 of 10th May, 1971 about the
possible impact of new weapons technology. Although, as the US Representative
noted at the Ministerial Meeting at Ankara, there are no current plans to
produce or deploy a new family of weapons, it has become apparent to the Study
Team that current developments, especially in the field of new guidance systems,
