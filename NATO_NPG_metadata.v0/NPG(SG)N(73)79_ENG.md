> # Consultation Procedures and Facilities
> 
> This document is a note from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated August 22, 1973, regarding consultation procedures and facilities. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, proposes a suggested change to the draft report NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)7(3rd Revision) to expand the language pertaining to message substance. The proposed amendment aims to assist Major NATO Commanders in being responsive to political consultation and decision-making processes while clarifying the limitations of their capability to provide certain information, such as predictions of Warsaw Pact reactions to NATO's use of nuclear weapons or precise timing of weapon employment in fluid tactical situations. The Staff Group is invited to discuss this proposal at their meeting on August 27, 1973, with the goal of reaching an agreement on a version to be circulated as an NP Working Paper for the Nuclear Planning Permanent Representatives' meeting on October 4, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES

Note by the Chairman

During its consideration of the draft report(1) on consultation procedures and
facilities at the last meeting, the Staff Group agreed that the language in the
paragraphs pertaining to message substance should be expanded and invited the
Chairman to circulate some proposals in this regard. In response to this
request, I attach a suggested change to the draft, which the Staff Group may
wish to discuss at the next meeting on Monday, 27th August, as it continues its
examination of the draft with a view to reaching agreement on a version that may
be circulated as an NP Working Paper for consideration by the NP Permanent
Representatives at their meeting scheduled for 4th October.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM.

1110 Brussels.

SUGGESTED CHANGE TO NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)7(3rd Revision)
Page 4, paragraph 4, and Page A-3, paragraph 3:
DELETE last sentence and INSERT the following:
"This dialogue should, among other things, assist the Major NATO Commanders in
their efforts to be as responsive as possible to the needs of the political
consultation and decision-making process. At the same time, it should help to
give political authorities a clearer understanding of the factors that
unavoidably limit the capability of the Major NATO Commanders to provide some of
the desired information, such as predictions of the Warsaw Pact reaction to
NATO's use of the weapons whose release is being requested, or forecasts about
the exact location and precise timing of weapon employment in fluid tactical
situations."
