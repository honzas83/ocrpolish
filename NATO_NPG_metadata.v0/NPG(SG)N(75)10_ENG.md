> # Agreed Minute of the NPG Technology Study Group Meeting
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Technology Study Group held its first meeting of the Full Study Panel on January 21-22, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting was attended by representatives from Belgium, Canada, Denmark, Germany, Greece, Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Turkey, United Kingdom, and the United States. The Panel received briefings from the United States Delegation on nuclear weapons technology and related conventional weapons technology. The Panel agreed on the purpose, scope, assumptions, and methodology for the study, as well as the general technological, military, and political factors to be considered. To facilitate the study, two study teams were formed: the Military Implications Team and the Political Implications Team, with a Technology Advisory Panel providing technical data and advice. The United States serves as the chairman of the NPG Technology Study Group, with the Federal Republic of Germany chairing the Political Implications Team and the United Kingdom chairing the Military Implications Team. The target date for the final report's ministerial consideration is Spring 1976, with progress reports to be provided at the 1975 Spring and Fall Ministerial meetings of the NPG. The Panel may meet again in late 1975 and March 1976 for review and approval of the final report.

Meeting of the Full Study Panel of the NPG Technology Study Group – January
21-22, 1975

Agreed Minute

1. The first meeting of the Full Study Panel of the NPG Technology Study Group
took place on January 21-22, 1975 at NATO Headquarters, Brussels. The meeting
was attended by representatives from Belgium, Canada, Denmark, Germany, Greece,
Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Turkey, United Kingdom and the United States.
Representatives from the International Staff, International Military Staff,
SHAPE and SACLANT also attended.

2. The Panel received briefings by the United States Delegation on nuclear
weapons technology and related conventional weapons technology, and engaged in
discussion of the material presented.

3. The Panel agreed on the purpose, scope and assumptions and methodology to be
used in accomplishing the tasks of the NPG Technology Study Group. The Panel
agreed in general terms on technological, military and political factors to be
considered in the study.

4. The Panel agreed to the formation of two study teams: the Military
Implications Team, and the Political Implications Team. Belgium, Denmark, FRG,
Italy, Netherlands, Norway, Turkey, the United Kingdom and the United States
agreed tentatively to put representatives on both teams. Canada and Greece
agreed to put representatives on the Military Implications Team. The Panel also
agreed to the formation of a Technology Advisory Panel, which will be under the
direction of the Military Implications Team, whose function will be to provide
data and advice on technical points to both teams. The United States, United
Kingdom, FRG, Italy, Netherlands and Norway agreed to contribute to the
Technology Advisory Panel. The Major NATO Commanders, International Military
Staff, and International Staff agreed to participate in the study in
advisor/observer status.

5. The United States, as chairman of the NPG Technology Study Group, will be
responsible for overall coordination. The Federal Republic of Germany will chair
the Political Implications Team and the United Kingdom will chair the Military
Implications Team.

6. The Panel set the Spring, 1976 as the target date
