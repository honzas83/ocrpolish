> # Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated 22nd April 1971. It addresses the need for clarification of the intent of several paragraphs in the Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in Allied Command Europe (ACE). The Chairman suggests that action by Ministers at the Mittenwald meeting may be reflected in the Agreed Minute of that meeting. He notes that the NATO Military Authorities' interpretation is that paragraphs 60 and 61 of the concept document provide the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) the flexibility to use dual-capable aircraft for conventional operations, provided that prudent military judgement is exercised to prevent attrition of strike forces to a level where NATO could no longer effectively execute its options for selective or general nuclear response. The matter is scheduled for further discussion at a meeting on 26th April 1971, allowing the eleven interested countries to provide their views before a decision is taken at Mittenwald.
>

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
CONCEPT FOR THE ROLE OF
THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES IN ACE
Note by the Chairman
At our meeting on 5th April, it was concluded that some action by the Ministers
was desirable in respect of that part of the Military Committee’s report(1) on
the Concept for the Rôle of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE(2) suggesting
the need for clarification of the intent of several paragraphs in the Concept
paper. The Staff Group also believed that this action might be reflected in an
entry in the Agreed Minute of the meeting in Mittenwald.

2. Without prejudice to the particular action the Ministers at Mittenwald might
decide to take in this instance or to the eventual language of the Minute, there
would appear to be some merit in having a Staff Group discussion (and later, a
similar discussion at Permanent Representatives level) on the specific statement
of the Military Committee’s interpretation of the Concept paper paragraphs in
question. The NATO Military Authorities hold the view that "the intent of
paragraphs 60 and 61 / Of the concept document/is to provide SACEUR with the
flexibility to use, if necessary, all dual-capable aircraft for conventional
operations but caution that SACEUR should exercise prudent military judgement to
prevent attrition of ACE strike forces to the point where NATO could no longer
effectively execute its options for selective or general nuclear response(3)."

3. This matter will again be discussed at our meeting on Monday, 26th April,
with a view to giving opportunity to all eleven interested countries to make
their views known before a decision is taken at Mittenwald.

(Signed) H. B. SEIM
