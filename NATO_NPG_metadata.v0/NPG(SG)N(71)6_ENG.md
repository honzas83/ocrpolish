> # Arrangements for a Note-Taker for Military Observers Attending NPG and NDAC Ministerial Meetings
> 
> This document, issued by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), details the proposed arrangements for military observers attending Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) and Nuclear Defence Advisory Committee (NDAC) ministerial meetings. The Chairman, H. B. Seim, notes that these points represent a consensus developing from a Staff Group meeting on 11th January 1971. The proposed rules state that military observers are permitted to have one note-taker in the conference room during sessions, with the possibility of rotating officers in this role. Note-takers must be specifically identified, designated in advance, and certified as having proper security clearances. Furthermore, the notes taken during these meetings are to be appropriately classified and treated as privileged, with access carefully limited. The document specifies that a note-taker is defined as an officer of colonel or lieutenant colonel rank rather than a stenographer, and may provide additional staff support to the observers. The final arrangements are expected to be transmitted to NATO Military Authorities via a letter from the Secretary General.

ANNEX to NPG(Staff Group)N(71)6

ARRANGEMENTS FOR A NOTE-TAKER FOR MILITARY OBSERVERS
ATTENDING NPG AND NDAC MINISTERIAL MEETINGS

1. Military Observers attending an NPG or NDAC Ministerial meeting would be
permitted to have a note-taker(1) in the conference room during the sessions.

2. It would be permissible to rotate officers in this function but only one
note-taker could be present in the conference room at any given time.

3. Any officers performing this function would be specifically identified and
designated in advance of the meeting and should be certified as having the
proper clearances.

4. Notes taken during the meeting would be appropriately classified and would be
considered to be of a privileged character; accordingly, access to them would be
carefully limited.

5. These arrangements would apply to NPG and NDAC Ministerial meetings.

(1) For the purposes of these arrangements, a "note-taker" is interpreted to be
an officer of colonel/lieutenant colonel rank rather than a stenographer. In
addition to taking notes, the officer could provide other staff support for the
military observers, as appropriate.
