> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NAFC Headquarters
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NAFC headquarters on January 15 and 16, 1970. The meeting covered several key topics, including a visit to Germany by the Staff Group, expressing gratitude to the United States representatives and authorities for visits to EUCO and USAREUR. The group also discussed the possibility of a similar visit to the NORTHAG region, as suggested by the United Kingdom representative. Regarding tactical use studies, the group agreed on a modified version of a working paper and requested the International Staff to circulate it as a report to the Nuclear Planning Group. They also expressed hope that Permanent Representatives would meet in early February to approve the report. Additionally, the group requested the Chairman to explore the possibility of holding the next meeting of the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent Representatives on February 2, 1970. The Italian representative noted that the most suitable dates for the NPG ministerial meeting in Venice were June 8 and 9, 1970. The group also noted a scheduled nuclear briefing by the International Military Staff on February 10, 1970. Finally, the group expressed concern over a potential security leak mentioned by the United Kingdom representative regarding a civilian publication, which is currently being investigated by the International Staff's Security and Nuclear Planning Directorates.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held at NAFC headquarters on:
Thursday, 15th January and continued on Friday 16th January, 1970

I. STAFF GROUP VISIT TO GERMANY
1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) warmly approved statements by the Chairman and the Doyen who expressed their
gratitude and appreciation to the US Representatives and the United States
Authorities for the visit to EUCO and USAREUR;

(b) noted and welcomed a statement by the United Kingdom representative who said
that a similar visit should be made to the NORTHAG region, and that he would try
to arrange for this.

II. FURTHER STUDIES OF FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE
2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) continued its discussion (1) of the working paper (2);
(b) agreed upon a modified version of the working paper, and invited the
International Staff to circulate the new version in the form of a report to the
Nuclear Planning Group (3);

(c) expressed the hope that it would be possible for the Nuclear Planning Group
Permanent Representatives to meet during the week beginning 2nd February with a
view to approving the report.

(1) NPG(Staff Group)N(70)3, 12th January, 1970
(2) NPG(Staff Group)WP(69)11 of 16th December, 1969 and
(3) NPG/WF(70)1, 19th January, 1970

III. NEXT MEETING OF NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
3. The STAFF GROUP :
requested the Chairman to explore the possibility of holding the next meeting of
the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent Representatives on Monday, 2nd February,
1970 at 2.50 p.m.

IV. NEXT MEETING OF THE NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP IN MINISTERIAL SESSION
4. The STAFF GROUP :
noted a statement by the Italian Representative who said that the most suitable
– and in fact the only available – dates for the NPG ministerial meeting in
Venice were the 8th and 9th June, 1970.

V. NUCLEAR BRIEFING - INTERNATIONAL MILITARY STAFF
5. The STAFF GROUP :
noted that the International Military Staff was organising a nuclear briefing to
be held on the morning and afternoon (at 9.30 a.m. and 2 p.m.) of Tuesday 10th
February, to which the Staff Group would be invited.

VI. SECURITY
6. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted the remarks of the United Kingdom Representative who drew the
attention of the Staff Group to an article in a certain civilian publication;

(b) expressed concern about the implied existence of a serious leak;
(c) noted a statement by the Chairman that the matter was already being
investigated by the International Staff (Security Directorate and Nuclear
Planning Directorate);

(d) noted the Chairman's comment that any information available to delegations
that could assist in the investigation would be appreciated.

VII. NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
7. The STAFF GROUP :
agreed to leave open the date of its next meeting.
