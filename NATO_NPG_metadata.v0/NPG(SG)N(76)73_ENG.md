> # Nuclear Consultation Procedures Compilation
> 
> This document consists of a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) and an attached letter from the Canadian Delegation to NATO. The Chairman, R.E. Shearer, notes that the approval of the draft Compilation on Nuclear Consultation Procedures was delayed due to requests from Belgium and the United States for more time and written comments from Canada. The attached letter from G. Olson, on behalf of D.L.B. Hamlin, outlines specific Canadian concerns regarding the notification requirements for the withdrawal or cancellation of release authorizations for nuclear weapons. Canada argues that notification of changes in decisions to use nuclear weapons should be of the same importance as the original authorization. Additionally, Canada requests a delay in the circulation of the white working paper until they have reviewed the revised format for SACEUR's Romeo Message and suggests including a description of the Blue Dot Seven message in Part III of the paper. The document also includes several editorial observations regarding the use of asterisks, references to ATOMAL information handling, and typographical errors in Annex B.

By NPG(Staff Group)WP(75)7(2nd Revise), dated 23rd July, 1976, it was proposed
that the draft of the Compilation be approved by the Staff Group under the
silence procedure, with a time limit of 6th August, 1976.

2. Subsequently, the Belgian and United States representatives requested a
week's delay in the silence procedure and written comments were received from
the Canadian Representative. The Canadian comments, which are attached, are such
that a new time limit for the silence procedure cannot now be determined.

3. You are requested to make known your views on the Canadian comments, together
with any other observations you may wish to make, so that a suitable procedure
for the approval of the draft can be set.

Dear Dick,

Re. NPG(Staff Group)WP(75)7(2nd Revise)

The reference document has been reviewed by my authorities and has been found to
be generally satisfactory. However, we have a number of comments to offer on the
paper.

First, in Part II para 24, we are concerned to find that the nuclear power has
apparently been given discretion as to whether or not it would notify MNCs,
Council/DPC and capitals if for any reason it decided that a release
authorization should be withdrawn or cancelled. Similar discretion is also
permitted the MNC on the matter of informing Council/DPC and capitals in the
event he is unable to use a weapon that has been released to him. In our view
the requirement to be informed of any change in decision to use nuclear weapons
would be of the same order of importance as the original decision to authorize
release. This would be particularly true for a decision related to initial use.
Therefore, in order to remove any doubt as to whether or not capitals, etc.,
would be kept fully informed of such developments, we would prefer that phrases
"it is expected that" in lines 4 and 5 of para 24, and "it is likely that" in
line 10 be deleted. The expression "similarly" should also replace the term
"conversely" in line 8.

Second, as you know, the revised format for SACEUR's Romeo Message was not
issued until August 4th. Therefore, I would prefer delaying circulation of the
white working paper for approval by Permanent Representatives until my
authorities have seen the revised format.

message in Part III of the paper. The relationship of the NWRP message which
provides information on holding or non-execution of an approved release, to the
