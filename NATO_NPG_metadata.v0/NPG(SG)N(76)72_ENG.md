> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Friday, 6th August, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on August 6, 1976, to address several administrative and security matters. The group took note of security clearances for all attendees and welcomed Mr. Banguoglu representing Turkey. Regarding the compilation of a working paper, the group agreed to extend the deadline for comments to August 13, 1976, with the possibility of further extensions for Canada and Major NATO Commanders. The meeting also focused on the terms of reference for the Nike Hercules Various Levels Study, where the German representative expressed concerns that military aspects should take precedence over cost and manpower information, while the United States representative emphasized the necessity of resource consideration. Due to these diverging views, the group decided to invite representatives from Germany and the United States to seek guidance from their respective authorities. The group also discussed the arrangements for the Autumn NPG Ministerial meeting, with the United States preferring November 18-19, the United Kingdom proposing November 22-23 in Edinburgh, and Canada preferring the week of November 15. The meeting concluded with the decision to leave the date of the next Staff Group meeting open.

TERMS OF REFERENCE FOR THE NIKE HERCULES STUDY
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the
International Staff paper entitled Draft Terms of Reference for NIKE HERCULES
Various Levels Study(1) and to suggested changes to paragraphs 4, 12, 13 and 15
circulated at the table;

(b) examined in detail the text of the draft and of the suggestions and agreed
to a number of changes to their form and content;

(c) took note of a statement by the German representative who said that his
authorities had problems with paragraph 12; they considered that the military
aspect of NIKE HERCULES was of outstanding importance and that the question of
cost and in particular the information required in paragraph 12 was of a
secondary nature;

(d) took note of the remarks of the United States representative who said that
his authorities felt strongly that questions of money and manpower must be
considered so that the best use could be made of available resources; he felt
sure that his Secretary of Defense and Ministers would be sure to ask for
information concerning costs and manpower;
