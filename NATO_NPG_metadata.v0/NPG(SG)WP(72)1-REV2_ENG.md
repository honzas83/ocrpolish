> # Initiation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme
> 
> This document, a second revision of a report by the Permanent Representatives to the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministers, outlines proposals for the initiation and implementation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme. The Work Programme is divided into three phases: Phase I involves the preparation of basic data inputs through studies and comments from NATO military authorities and NDAC countries; Phase II focuses on the comparative analysis of these inputs, synthesis of findings, and identification of policy questions; and Phase III involves the formulation of proposed political guidelines for follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. The report notes that while most Phase I studies are expected to be completed by the end of 1972, a new study on the air battle in the Central Region is still pending. To maintain momentum, it is recommended that Ministers initiate Phase II in the Spring of 1972, potentially creating a provisional report based on available data. The proposed organizational structure for Phase II involves an ad hoc study team composed of participants from interested countries to conduct the analytical work and a process for multilateral participation through the Permanent Representatives and NATO Military Authorities to ensure political aspects are the addressed. The document emphasizes the need for flexibility and the desire to be efficient while allowing for national inputs from all interested NPG countries.

Purpose
This report submits for Ministerial consideration certain proposals for the
initiation and implementation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme,
in response to the request made by the Ministers at their meeting in Brussels on
26th and 27th October, 1971(1).

Discussion
2. One of the primary objectives of the Work Programme is to provide, through
its exploration of the implications and interactions of follow-on tactical use
of nuclear weapons, a basis for the Ministers to develop policy proposals, as
appropriate, for submission to the Defence Planning Committee. In this context,
the NPG has agreed that the Work Programme will consist of three major parts,
namely, Phase I (preparation of basic data inputs in the form of studies and
related comments from the NDAC countries and the NATO military authorities);
Phase II (comparative analysis of the basic data inputs, synthesis of findings
and identification of the policy questions); and Phase III (formulation, as
appropriate, of proposed political guidelines for follow-on tactical use of
nuclear weapons for NDAC and DFC approval)(2).

6. It is, however, important to initiate the Phase II work as early as possible
both to enable Ministerial discussion of the provisional Phase II report in the
course of 1973 and to sustain the momentum of the programme as a whole. It is
therefore recommended that a decision to initiate the Phase II work be taken by
the Ministers at the Spring 1972 meeting. This would enable steps to be taken
during the Summer of 1972 to work out the details of the study effort and a
start to be made on comparative analysis. However, it should be appreciated that
completion of the provisional Phase II report could not occur until after
Ministerial consideration of the Phase I studies on their Autumn 1972 agenda. By
the same token, no interim report on the Phase II effort should be expected for
the Autumn 1972 meeting. To facilitate the preparation of a provisional Phase II
report, all concerned should make every effort to ensure the timely introduction
of Phase I basic data by, for example, submitting comments on Phase I studies as
soon as possible after they have been considered at Ministerial level.

7. Organisation for Phase II. The arrangements to be followed in carrying out
Phase II must take into account a number of considerations, some of which are
conflicting. Among these are the need to provide a full opportunity to all
interested NFG countries to make national inputs to the Phase II work, to
contribute to the final report before it is placed on the Ministerial agenda
and, for those countries not participating at Ministerial level, to make known
any particular national views that they feel should be taken into account during
the Ministerial discussion; the requirement to keep the analytical group from
becoming so large and unwieldy that it becomes inefficient; the desire to
involve participants from national capitals; and the wish to facilitate a
meaningful input to the process from the NATO Military Authorities. It should
also be
