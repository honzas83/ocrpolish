> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 15th November, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on November 15, 1971, to review several key documents, including draft reports to the Nuclear Defence Advisory Committee (NDAC) and revised reports on AD 70 follow-up action. The group discussed the practice of conducting free exchanges of views based on briefings on the balance of strategic forces provided by the United States Secretary of Defense. There was a deliberation on whether advance copies of these briefings should be distributed to facilitate Ministerial discussions, though some members felt this might reduce spontaneity. The group also addressed the plan to consider an illustrative study involving battlefield use and political decision-making problems for the 1972 Spring meeting, noting that a dual presentation might require more time than available. Additionally, the Italian representative raised concerns regarding the United States' intention to replace POLARIS missiles with the POSEIDON weapon system in fleet ballistic missile submarines, and the group agreed that the Nuclear Planning Group should study the implications of this modernization on nuclear policy and consultation procedures. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next meeting on November 22, 1971, in Brussels.

DOCUMENTATION

from the Secretary General to the Chairman of the Military Committee recalling
the requests made to the NATO Military Authorities by NPG Ministers at their
meeting in Brussels and in particular their wish to consider a battlefield study
at their 1972 Spring meeting;

FOLLOW-UP ACTION TO THE BRUSSELS MINISTERIAL MEETING (1)
STRATEGIC QUESTIONS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled the agreement by Ministers, during their meeting in Brussels (2),
to develop further the practice of conducting a free exchange of views against
the background of the briefings on the balance of strategic forces given by the
United States Secretary of Defense at NPG Ministerial meetings;

(b) noted a statement by the United States representative who said that the form
of future briefings will be changed by his authorities in Washington in the
light of the comments made at the Brussels meeting;

(c) took note of a suggestion by the Greek representative that the Ministerial
discussion would be facilitated if advance copies of the briefing could be
distributed before the meeting;

(d) noted, in the ensuing discussion, that there was a general feeling that the
issue of advance copies of the briefing might cause it to be less spontaneous;
noted on the other hand a general feeling that a discussion of the briefing at
Staff Group level after the meeting was most desirable, and that for such
discussions the availability of some notes of the briefings, omitting sensitive
material where appropriate, would be useful.

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES
3. The STAFF GROUP;
(a) recalled the wish of the NGP Ministers, expressed at their meeting in
Brussels (1), to consider at the Spring meeting of 1972, an illustrative study
involving battlefield use and confronting political authorities with complex
consultation and decision-making problems;

(b) noted that the preparation and integration of both a battlefield and a
political scenario might require, if it were to be done well, more time than was
actually available before the Spring meeting; Ministers would not be satisfied
with a partial study and therefore if a dual presentation could not be prepared
in time for the next Ministerial meeting it should be held over until the
Autumn;

(c) took note of the views expressed during the ensuing discussion, and of
suggestions regarding collaboration with the Military Committee and with the
Council Operations Directorate, and to the relevance of the studies to the
participation of military and civil authorities in HILEX.

(d) noted with appreciation that the Council Operations Directorate, in liaison
with the International Military Staff, had offered to organise a visit for the
Staff Group to the Situation Centre on 29th November; this visit would include a
briefing on crisis management;

(e) invited members to reflect upon the problems involved in preparing a dual
presentation, and agreed to continue the discussion at the next meeting.

US NAVAL FORCES IMPROVEMENTS
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Italian representative who drew attention to
the United States' intention, announced at a recent briefing, to modernise the
US fleet ballistic missile submarines by replacing the originally installed
POLARIS missiles with the POSEIDON weapon system, and to modify the United
States commitments to NATO in submarines to reflect the modernisation programme;
he asked whether this was
