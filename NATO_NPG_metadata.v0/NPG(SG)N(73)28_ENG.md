> # Future NPG Activity
> 
> The Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) has issued a note to explore possibilities for future work to ensure timely planning. The document outlines three general areas of interest for discussion: actions resulting from the upcoming May Ministerial meeting in Ankara, work related to ongoing projects such as the Follow-on Use Work Programme and tactical nuclear defence, and new areas for future investigation. Additionally, the note addresses practical questions regarding the coordination of interrelated aspects of NPG work, the assignment of tasks, and the ensuring of orderly progression and timely completion of projects. The Chairman suggests that the Staff Group hold a general discussion on these matters at its next meeting on Monday, 2nd April.

NOTICE NPG(Staff Group)N(73)28
NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
FUTURE NPG ACTIVITY
Note by the Chairman
For purposes of timely planning, it may be useful to look beyond the preparation
for the forthcoming May Ministerial meeting in Ankara and to explore the
possibilities for future NPG work.

2. In this context, three general areas of interest might be discussed in order
to identify these possibilities:

(a) actions that might flow from the forthcoming Ankara meeting;
(b) work in connection with on-going projects (Follow-on Use Work Programme,
unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence, implementation of
political guidance, etc.);

(c) further areas for future investigation by the NPG (new topics, subjects
related to on-going work or recent observations).

Closely linked to the discussion of potential subjects will be practical
questions as: Who might be asked to do what and when? What arrangements might be
needed for coordinating interrelated aspects of NPG work? What steps should be
taken to ensure the orderly progression of activity and the timely completion of
the various projects that are undertaken?

3. It is suggested that the Staff Group have a general discussion of this matter
at its next meeting on Monday, 2nd April.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM.
NATO, 1110 Brussels.
