> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, on Monday, 25th September, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened at NATO Headquarters on September 25, 1972, to address several key agenda items. The meeting began with a farewell to Colonel Hiernaux of Belgium. The group reviewed several recently circulated papers, including reports on the Denmark Follow-on Use Study, the updating of NATO Nuclear Planning Directives, and forces transition, structure, and deployment. Discussions focused on the draft agenda for the October Ministerial meeting, including the balance of strategic forces and adjustments to the schedule to accommodate the Danish Minister. The Staff Group approved amendments to the report on the Denmark Follow-on Use Study and deferred consideration of the status report on work in progress for follow-on use studies. They also discussed consultation procedures and facilities, specifically the handling of nuclear release traffic by communications personnel. The group reviewed modifications to the SACLANT Illustrative Study report and invited the International Staff to issue a revision. The United States Representative provided an oral status report on Phase II of the follow-on use work programme, noting that the Ad Hoc Study Team had met in Washington and is pursuing a subject-oriented approach. Finally, the group discussed possible dates for the 1973 Spring NPG Ministerial in Ankara, Turkey, and scheduled their next meeting for September 28, 1972.

ORAL STATUS REPORT ON PHASE II OF FOLLOW-ON USE WORK PROGRAMME BY THE UNITED
STATES (DISCUSSION LEADER)

8. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a status report and replies to members' questions, given by the
United States Representative, which contained the following information:

(i) The Ad Hoc Study Team for Phase II had met the previous week in Washington.
Agreement had been reached on the pursuance of a methodological approach to the
work based upon a subject oriented approach, the viability of which had already
been tested in some initial work, to be further examined by the Study Team at
their next meeting in December.

(ii) The basic drafting was being undertaken in the capitals for consideration
at the joint meetings. The establishment of a time-frame for the work was as yet
premature, but further information on this would be contained in a more
substantive oral report to be given to Staff Group members after the meeting of
the Study Team in December.

1973 SPRING NGP MINISTERIAL
9. The STAFF GROUP:
