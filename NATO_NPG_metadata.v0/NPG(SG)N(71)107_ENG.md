> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 20th September, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on September 20, 1971, to coordinate preparations for the NPG Ministerial Meeting scheduled for October 26-27, 1971. The group endorsed the welcome of Colonel R. Thyes as the new German Representative. The meeting focused on the primary goal of agenda setting for the upcoming ministerial level meeting. Additionally, the group discussed the comments from military authorities on Regional Follow-On Studies, specifically addressing the timing and handling of the CENTAG Area Study. The several working papers were reviewed and agreed upon, including the Illustrative Study (NPG/WP(71)6), Unresolved Problems (NPG/WP(71)7), Implementation of Special ADM Guidelines (NPG/WP(71)8), and the Status of Work Report on Follow-on use studies (NPG/WP(71)9). The group also addressed the nuclear aspects of AD 70 Follow-Up Action (NPG/WP(71)10) and the draft report on the CENTAG Area Study (NPG/WP(71)12). Finally, the Turkish Representative provided a briefing schedule for the Turkish Thrace and North-Western Turkey Area Study, with a briefing for Permanent Representatives expected in late January 1972. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for September 24, 1971.

WELCOME
1. The STAFF GROUP:
endorsed a statement by the Chairman who warmly welcomed Colonel R. Thyes, who
is replacing Colonel F. Körner as German Representative on the Staff Group.

AGENDA FOR THE MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS ON 26th-27th OCTOBER
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that a draft agenda for the NPG Ministerial Meeting to be held at
the end of October had been circulated as a Working Paper(1) and that if no
desired changes had been proposed by 20th September, it would be considered as
approved;

(b) noted that no objections were raised;
(c) invited the International Staff to issue the Agenda in final form as soon as
possible.
