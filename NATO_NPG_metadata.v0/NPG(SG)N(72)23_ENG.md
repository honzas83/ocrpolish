> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 28th February, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on February 28, 1972, to address several key agenda items. The meeting began with the welcome of Mr. J. Mentzel to the International Staff, Nuclear Planning Directorate. The group discussed documentation including the Mediterranean Maritime Study and a Private Office Memorandum regarding military comments. The group approved invitations for SACEUR and SACLAN to attend the NPG Ministerial Meeting in Copenhagen on May 18-19, 1972, and noted administrative requirements for the Copenhagen meeting. The group also addressed the initiation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Programme, noting that the United States representative confirmed an acceptable text had been sent to Washington for instructions. Discussion on the Turkish Follow-on Use Study was held, and the group agreed to prepare a revised draft report in the form of an NPG Working Paper. The group deferred discussion on the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Battlefield Use to the next meeting. Additionally, the group agreed to examine comments from capitals on Working Papers before submission to Permanent Representatives. Finally, the group coordinated the scheduling of future meetings for both the NPG Permanent Representatives and the NPG Staff Group, accounting for HILEX-5, SHAPEX, and the Easter holiday.

Invitation to SACEUR and SACLAN
3. The STAFF GROUP:
approved a statement by the Chairman who said that, subject to no objection

SACEUR and SACLAN to attend the meeting of NPG Ministers to be held in

Administrative Arrangements
4. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Danish representative who requested that
Delegations should give him the following information as soon as possible:

(a) their needs regarding office space, equipment and furniture during the
Ministerial meeting;

(b) the probable size and composition of their Delegation during the meeting;
(c) whether hotel accommodation should be reserved for them, or if their
Embassies in Copenhagen would deal direct with hotels.

INITIATION OF PHASE II OF THE FOLLOW-ON USE PROGRAMME
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussions on the
initiation of Phase II held during recent meetings and the consensus at the last
meeting that informal contacts and discussions should take place in the hope of
reaching agreement on a suitable text or texts for submission to Capitals and
subsequently to Permanent Representatives ; he underlined the need to send an
agreed working paper to capitals well before the meeting of Permanent
Representatives ; he invited comments;

(b) took note of a statement by the United States representative who confirmed
that contacts had

DRAFT REPORT ON THE TURKISH FOLLOW-ON USE STUDY

6. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Staff Group,
at its last meeting, had discussed the first draft(1) of a possible report by
Permanent Representatives on the Study of Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear
Weapons in the Turkish Thrace and Northwestern Turkey area, and had agreed to a
number of changes and had invited the Canadian member to draft a new paragraph
on escalation; the new paragraph and related revisions of original paragraphs 6,
7 and 8 had been issued in a Staff Group Notice(2); he invited comment;

(b) took note of the suggestions made and the views expressed, and agreed to a
number of changes to the text;

(c) invited the International Staff, in the light of the discussion, to prepare
a revised version of the draft report in the form of an NPG Working Paper, and
invited representatives to send this working paper to their capitals for
comment.

SHAPE ILLUSTRATIVE STUDY ON BATTLEFIELD USE(1)
7. The STAFF GROUP:
noting that the hour was late, agreed to discuss the SHAPE Illustrative Study at
its next meeting, and invited the International Staff, in collaboration with the
representatives of staffs and Delegations, who were requested to give their
views, to prepare and distribute a first draft paper on this matter.

EXAMINATION OF WORKING PAPERS IN THE LIGHT OF COMMENTS FROM CAPITALS
8. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed that the Staff Group should examine the comments of capitals on the
Working Papers before their submission to Permanent Representatives, with a view
to reaching agreement on minor amendments and leaving only matters of importance
to be discussed by Permanent Representatives.

FUTURE MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
9. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that the next meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives would
take place on Thursday, 9th March, 1972, at 3.50 p.m. to receive a briefing on
the Mediterranean Maritime Study;

(b) recalled that a meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives had been planned
for Thursday, 13th April, 1972, with a view to approving the drafts of the
various reports by Permanent Representatives required for the Spring Ministerial
Meeting ; noted that the day was not convenient to Belgium, and invited the
Chairman to investigate the possibility of holding the meeting on 12th April,
and to raise the matter again at the next meeting.
