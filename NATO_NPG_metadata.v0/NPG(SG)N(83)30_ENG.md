> # Allied Command Europe Senior Officers' Orientation Course
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) has issued a notice regarding the Allied Command Europe Senior Officers' Orientation Course, also known as I-31, conducted by the NATO School in Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarize senior NATO officers and key personnel in staff appointments with the major concepts of NATO weapons' employment in Allied Command Europe. Eligibility is limited to officers of the rank of Colonel and above, as well as civilian officials of equivalent grade, on a need-to-know basis, requiring a COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance. The course lasts one week from Monday through Friday, with a tuition cost of 165 United States dollars per student. The document lists eight course dates for 1984, ranging from February to November, noting that French translation will be available for two of these sessions. Application procedures vary by location: officials in Brussels at NATO Headquarters are to apply through the Nuclear Planning Directorate of the International Staff, while officials in capitals and national commands should apply through their Ministry of Defence. Members of Delegations are instructed to send applications to Mr. R. Serio or Miss K. Winters of the Nuclear Planning Directorate by December 2, 1983.

Note by the Chairman
The Allied Command Europe Senior Officers' Orientation Course (short title:
I-31) is conducted by the NATO School (SHAPE) in Oberammergau, Germany. The
purpose of the course is to familiarize senior NATO officers and key personnel
in staff appointments with the major concepts of NATO weapons' employment in
Allied Command Europe. Officers of the rank of Colonel and above and civilian
officials of equivalent grade are eligible to attend on a need-to-know basis. A
COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance is required. The course lasts one week – Monday
through Friday. Tuition costs are 165 United States dollars per student, which
for personnel of NATO Delegations must be paid by their national authorities.
The course is offered on the following dates in 1984. The annotation "F" in the
last column below shows courses for which French translation will be available.
