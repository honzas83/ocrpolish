> # Ministerial Meeting in Venice on 8th and 9th June, 1970: Air Transport
> 
> This document, issued by the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), proposes the chartering of a civilian aircraft to transport members of delegations and International Staff from Brussels to Venice for a ministerial meeting scheduled for June 8th and 9th, 1970. The Secretary notes that scheduled civilian flights are limited, and a chartered flight would ensure seat availability, flexible departure times, and potentially lower costs per passenger. The proposal suggests a departure from Brussels on Saturday, June 6th, and a return trip on Wednesday, June 10th. Depending on the number of interested participants (approximately 40 for a propeller-driven aircraft or 80 for a jet), the type of aircraft would be determined. The Secretary, N.H.G. Wilson, requests that delegations and staff provide information regarding their preferred arrival and return times in Venice and Brussels, as well as the total number of people, including spouses, who wish to travel.

NOTICE
NEG(Staff Group)N(70)20
NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
MINISTERIAL MEETING IN VENICE ON 8TH AND 9TH JUNE, 1970
AIR TRANSPORT
Note by the Secretary
It has been suggested that a civilian aircraft be chartered in order to take
members of the delegations and of the International Staff to Venice for the
Ministerial meeting in June. There is only one suitable scheduled civilian
flight to Venice (via Milan) per day and therefore a chartered flight would have
the following advantages:

(a) passengers would be sure of getting seats;
(b) the time of departure could be arranged to suit the convenience of the
group;

(c) cost per passenger might be less.
The object of this note is to find out whether Delegations/Staffs would be
interested in a chartered flight leaving Brussels on Saturday, 6th June at a
time to be decided later and returning from Venice to Brussels on Wednesday
morning, 10th June. If the number of interested people was approximately 40, the
chartered aircraft would probably be propeller driven. If, on the other hand
about 80 people were interested, it might be possible to obtain a jet.

If you are interested, please let me know the answer to the following questions
as soon as possible:

(a) at approximately what time would you wish to arrive in Venice;
(b) at approximately what time would you wish to arrive in Brussels on the
return trip;

(c) how many people from your Delegation/Staff, including wives, would wish to
take part.

(Signed) N.H.G. WILSON
1110 Brussels.
