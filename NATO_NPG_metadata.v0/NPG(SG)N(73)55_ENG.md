> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 25th June, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on June 25, 1972, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group warmly endorsed the welcome of Group Captain L.E.H. Scotchmer, RAF, as the replacement for Group Captain V. Cramer. The group took note of a request from the Secretary General to SACEUR to prepare an illustrative study on nuclear air defence in a land environment for the Spring 1974 NPC Ministers meeting. They discussed the function of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation process and the availability of reports on the early combined use of conventional and nuclear armaments. The meeting also finalized the dates for the Autumn Meeting of NPG Ministers on November 6th and 7th, 1973, to be hosted by the Netherlands. The Norwegian representative proposed hosting the Spring 1974 Ministerial Meeting in Norway during the first half of May, which was the group agreed upon. The United Kingdom representative raised concerns regarding the paucity of information provided to the NATO Council on nuclear weapons inventories of NATO and the Warsaw Pact. The group discussed future NPG activities, including studies on Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy and Military Doctrine, communication of NATO's intentions, and the conceptual analysis of nuclear deterrence. Finally, the group addressed consultation procedures and facilities, noting the need to improve the content and handling of messages and to inform Ministers of progress. The next meeting was scheduled for July 2, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 25th
June, 1972

WELCOME
1. The STAFF GROUP:
warmly endorsed the welcome extended by the Chairman on behalf of the Staff
Group to Group Captain L.E.H. Scotchmer, RAF, the replacement for Group Captain
V. Cramer, who is leaving SHAPE on retirement.

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the Secretary General had
written to the Chairman of the Military Committee inviting him to ask SACEUR to
prepare an illustrative study dealing with nuclear air defence in a land
environment for study by NPC Ministers at their meeting in the Spring 1974; a
copy of this letter would shortly be circulated to Delegations under reference
PO/73/89.

FUNCTION OF THE MILITARY COMMITTEE IN NUCLEAR CONSULTATION
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the letter from
the Secretary General to the Chairman of the Military Committee requesting its
views on the function of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation
process had been held back at the request of the Canadian Ambassador who had
wanted an exploratory discussion of the matter;

SPRING 1974 MEETING OF NPC MINISTERS
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Norwegian representative who said that his
Authorities intended to issue a formal invitation to the NPC to hold its Spring
1974 Ministerial Meeting in Norway; sometime during the first half of May was
suggested;

(b) noted with appreciation the Norwegian proposal and agreed that the period
suggested would be suitable.

(c) noted with appreciation the remarks of the Italian representative who said
that if Norway for any reason was unable to host this meeting, Italy would take

INFORMATION ON NUCLEAR MATTERS PROVIDED TO THE NATO COUNCIL
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the United Kingdom representative who referred
to the briefing given to the NATO Council on 20th June, 1973, on the inventory
of nuclear weapons available to NATO Forces and to the forces of the Warsaw
Pact; he had been surprised at the paucity of information provided, and at the
apparent unwillingness of the Military Authorities to give figures; he felt that
if NATO Headquarters was to become involved in MBEF, there would be a need for
better information;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion during which the
following points were made:

(i) the briefing fitted a request made some months ago and which may not have
been sufficiently specific for present circumstances;

(ii) some countries in the Council do not normally receive information on
nuclear matters above a certain security level;

(iii) there were specific limitations on information

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES

9. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the International
Staff's first draft of a possible report by NPG Permanent Representatives on
some selected aspects of consultation procedures and facilities; he invited
comments;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the discussion and in particular of the
following points:

(i) the need to initiate action by the Military Authorities to remedy certain
shortcomings in the procedures and facilities, especially with regard to the
content and handling of messages;

(ii) the need to inform Ministers at their next meeting of the progress
achieved;

(iii) the desirability of close co-operation with the Council Operations and
Communications Directorate;

(iv) the need to take into account the new guide to security classifications
which was being prepared for issue;

(c) agreed that the questions in subparagraph(i) above might be examined
informally by the Chairman and some interested Staff Group members with the
assistance of representatives of the Military Authorities and other NATO
agencies concerned;

(d) agreed to continue the discussion at the next meeting.
