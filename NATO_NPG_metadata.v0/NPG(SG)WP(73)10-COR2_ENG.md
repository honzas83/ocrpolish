> # Corrigendum 2 to NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)10: Early Combined Use
> 
> This document is Corrigendum 2 to NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)10, issued by the North Atlantic Council, providing specific amendments to a draft report concerning the early combined use of nuclear weapons. It modifies paragraph 12 to emphasize the deterrent value and war-fighting aspects of the tactical employment of nuclear weapons. It introduces a new section on 'Escalation', defining it as a significant increase in the intensity or magnitude of combat action, noting that the first use of nuclear weapons automatically constitutes escalation under the SHAPE definition. The text discusses the risk of escalation and the factors influencing enemy perceptions and motivations, suggesting that further exploration of the concept of escalation may be necessary. It also addresses thees uncertainties in predicting enemy responses to early combined use versus later use, noting that later use could be more disadvantageous for NATO forces. Finally, it provides a summary of the military and political implications of early combined use, emphasizing that a complete assessment can only be made in the light of the particular situation at the time, and stresses the importance of political consultation machinery and the readiness of conventional and nuclear forces.

"17. In commenting on the difficulties in predicting the enemy response to early

apply to later use. However, the consequences to NATO of an enemy reaction,
particularly by nuclear means, could be more disadvantageous for NATO forces

"(1) SHAPE Report, paragraph 46."
"Summary"
"18. From the analysis in the SHAPE Report and the related Military Committee
comments, we gain the impression that there are likely to be military advantages
from early combined use that would not be realised under later use
circumstances, although there would also be military disadvantages to be taken
into account in both cases. As noted earlier, a complete assessment of the
military advantages and disadvantages, and the resulting balance, can only be
made in the light of the particular situation that obtains at the time that
early combined use is under active consideration. The same reservation applies
to the assessment of the political implications. From the political point of
view, the evaluation of advantages and disadvantages is complicated further by
the fact that the various factors to be considered cannot be defined and
analysed in advance to the degree of detail that was achieved in the examination
of military factors. Nevertheless, political authorities should make themselves
aware of the possible political and military implications of early combined use
so as to be ready to deal effectively with a real situation. This should also
help NATO to avoid losing by default the opportunity to implement the early
combined use option. The importance of effective political consultation
machinery is also self-evident."

"19. It is also clear that NATO's ability to exercise the early combined use
