> # Notes of a Meeting Held at NATO Headquarters, on Monday, 11th September, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters on September 11, 1972, to coordinate administrative and strategic planning. The group discussed documentation including a draft Status Report on follow-on use studies and a notice regarding ATOMAL arrangements for the Denmark Study. The United States Representative noted that ATOMAL classification may not be necessary for the Denmark Study if specific yields are avoided in discussions. The meeting also addressed scheduling changes, noting that the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives originally planned for September 29 was rescheduled to October 5, 1972. Furthermore, the group reviewed a revised version of the International Staff paper on the Transition Study, incorporating views from August 21 and the Military Committee's report on the Structure and Deployment of NATO Forces for Action in a Nuclear Environment. The discussion covered the form of the report, recommendations, and the potential political implications of adopting a nuclear posture. The Staff Group agreed to further revise the Working Paper and scheduled its next meeting for September 18, 1972.

Notes of a meeting held at NATO Headquarters, on Monday, 11th September, 1972

DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) NPG/WP(72)9, dated 8th September, 1972. This is the draft Status Report on
work in progress on the follow-on use studies, for examination in Capitals and
subsequent consideration by NPG Permanent Representatives on 5th October;

(b) MFG/N(72)1, dated 6th September, 1972. This is a notice regarding ATOMAL
arrangements for the presentation of the Denmark Study to Permanent
Representatives on Thursday, 14th September.

NEXT MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the United States Representative who referred to
the ATOMAL classification of the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives to be
held on Thursday, 14th September, 1972; he said that he had received a
preliminary indication from Washington that probably the only ATOMAL aspects of
the Denmark Study were the mention of specific yields, and that if the
Presentation Team, and the Permanent Representatives in their discussion,
avoided such specific data, there would be no need for ATOMAL treatment of
notes, cables and other material arising from the meeting;

FORCES TRANSITION, STRUCTURE AND DEPLOYMENT
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the revised
version(1) of the International Staff paper on the Transition Study(2); he
recalled that the new version took into account the views expressed during the
Staff Group's discussion of the subject on 21st August, and reflected also the
Military Committee's report(3) on the Structure and Deployment of NATO Forces
for Action in a Nuclear Environment; he drew attention to the links between the
Transition Study, the Military Committee's report, the AD70s work and the
unresolved problems raised in PO/226; he suggested that the Staff Group should
first discuss the draft recommendations (paragraph 18 of the revised draft) with
a view to deciding whether they should remain non-committal, or whether a
specific initiative regarding future work should be included; he invited views;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion which covered,
inter alia, the form of the report, the recommendations, the extent of
involvement of the NATO Military Authorities, the adoption of a nuclear posture
by NATO forces and whether this might have political implications;
