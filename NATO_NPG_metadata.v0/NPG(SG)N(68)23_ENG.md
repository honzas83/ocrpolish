> # Notes of an NPG Staff Group Meeting held on Monday, 2nd December, 1968
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on December 2, 1968, to address several key agenda items. The first item concerned NATO's response to the initial use of nuclear weapons by the Warsaw Pact, noting that no country had yet submitted a paper on contingencies. The group agreed to hold a further meeting on this subject on January 7, 1969, and invited the International Staff to prepare an agenda and bibliography for a meeting with Soviet experts. The group also noted that the next NPG Ministerial meeting was scheduled for May 29-30, 1969, at Lancaster House in the United Kingdom. Regarding consultation, the group recalled a decision from October 24, 1968, and noted a report from the Netherlands representative regarding the collation of replies. A meeting of NPG Ambassadors on December 17, 1968, was scheduled to be the first item on the agenda for the convenience of Canada. A final discussion on ADM time factors, based on a Working Paper from the United States Delegation, was conducted, and the group invited the International Staff to prepare a revised version of the paper. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for December 9, 1968.

Notes of an NPG Staff Group Meeting held on

NATO's RESPONSE TO INITIAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS BY
WARSAW PACT

Documents :
NPG/D(68)10, paragraph 7
NPG/D(68)11, paragraph 6

1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted that so far no country had been able to
submit a paper on the kinds of contingencies
involving Soviet initial use of nuclear weapons.
(b) noted that some countries preferred to have a
general discussion with Soviet experts before
preparing a paper, while others preferred first
to prepare a framework and then to discuss.
(c) after taking note of the points raised in
discussion, invited the International Staff to
prepare in cooperation with Delegations and the
Military Authorities, an agenda together with
bibliography of relevant books and documents,
to serve as a framework for the meeting with the
Soviet experts.
(d) agreed to hold a meeting on this subject on
7th January, 1969.

NEXT MINISTERIAL MEETING OF NPG

Document : NPG/D(68)10, paragraph 10

2. The STAFF GROUP :

(a) took note of a statement by the United Kingdom representative who said that
Lancaster House had been reserved for the NPG. Ministerial meeting on 29th and
30th May, 1969.

(b) noted that it was advisable to reserve hotel accommodation well in advance,
and preferably this month.

CONSULTATION

Reference : Meeting of NPG Ambassadors on 24th October, 1968

3. The STAFF GROUP :

(a) recalled the Permanent Representatives discussion on 24th October at which
it was decided that answers to the document on Consultation (1) should be
circulated by 15th November, and that the Netherlands should collate them.

(b) took note of a situation report by the Netherlands representative who said
that although three replies had not yet been received, he hoped to be able to
send a draft of his paper to all members early next week.

(c) noted a general wish that this matter should be discussed at Permanent
Representative level as soon as possible, and agreed to hold a meeting of NPO
Ambassadors on 19th December (a), subject to the Netherlands paper being
available in time for prior discussion by the Staff Group.

(d) agreed that for the convenience of Canada, the subject of Consultation would
be the first item on the Agenda for that day.

(1) NPG/D(68)3

(2) Subsequently fixed for Tuesday afternoon, 17th December, at the request of
the Secretary General.

ADM TIME FACTORS

4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted with appreciation the Working Paper prepared by the United States
Delegation.

the Italian representative.

discussion at the next meeting of the Staff Group.
