> # Preparations for the Autumn 1974 NPG Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This document is a notice from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, H.B. Seim, detailing the preparations for the NPG Ministerial meeting to be held in Rome on November 7th and 8th, 1974. The Chairman identifies potential agenda items for the Ministers, including a briefing on the balance of strategic forces, the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Initial Air Defence Use of Nuclear Weapons, and a study on communicating NATO's intentions to use nuclear weapons. Other mentioned topics include employment planning options, the Follow-on Use Work Programme, the Technology Study, and nuclear aspects of the WINTEX 73 reports. The document notes that some items, such as the Implementation of ADM Guidelines and Atomal classification, have been deferred. It also includes a planning timetable in the annex, which schedules weekly Staff Group meetings throughout the summer and deadlines for reports and final reviews by Permanent Representatives before the Ministerial meeting.

PREPARATIONS FOR THE AUTUMN 1974 NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING

Note by the Chairman

The following paragraphs discuss briefly the principal tasks that, in my view,
the Staff Group may have to undertake in preparing for the next NPG Ministerial
meeting to be held in Rome on 7th and 8th November, 1974. Some timing
considerations are also mentioned. It is suggested that the Staff Group discuss
these matters at its next meeting on Monday, 24th June.

Ministerial Agenda Topics

2. Although it would be premature to decide at this time on the specific items
to be listed on the agenda for the Autumn 1974 NPG Ministerial meeting, it would
be useful, in terms of framing the Staff Group's work, to review the
possibilities. Since no action was taken at the Bergen meeting that would
identify specific additional agenda items for the next Ministerial session,
consideration is essentially limited to items resulting from earlier
initiatives. On the basis of current information, the following subjects appear
to be reasonable possibilities:

(a) briefing on the balance of strategic forces followed by a general
discussion;

(b) Illustrative Study: Air Defence Use;
(c) Communicating NATO's Intentions to Use Nuclear Weapons.

Ministerial meeting include information provided by the Major NATO Commanders

4. Substantive reports resulting from the two on-going study efforts (Phase II
and Technology) are not likely to be available for Ministerial consideration at
the Autumn 1974 meeting.

5. Briefing on the Balance of Strategic Forces. This briefing, which has become
a regular feature of NPG Ministerial meetings, sets the stage for a review among
Ministers of recent past developments and future prospects in the strategic
nuclear field as well as for general discussion of other nuclear-defence issues
of common interest. Normally, this topic does not require any preparatory work
on the part of the NPG Permanent Representatives or the Staff Group.

6. Illustrative Study – Air Defence Use. It appears certain that the SHAPE
Illustrative Study on Initial Air Defence Use of Nuclear Weapons(2) will be on
the next Ministerial agenda. Accordingly, the Staff Group has begun the
preparation of a draft Permanent Representatives' report suggesting topics which
the Ministers may wish to discuss during their consideration of the Study. An
initial draft, based on earlier Staff Group discussions, should be available in
the near future.

7. Communicating NATO's Intention to Use Nuclear Weapons. At their meeting in
Ankara in May, 1973 the NPG Ministers invited the Permanent Representatives to
initiate a study on the implications of communicating NATO's intentions to an
enemy, to other countries and to the public and on the related procedural

Timing Considerations

8. At annex is a planning timetable for preparing for the next Ministerial
meeting. Although the timetable indicates that the Staff Group would meet on a
weekly basis throughout the summer, the schedule is obviously subject to
adjustment in the light of the workload, the anticipated turnover of personnel
(particularly the Chairman) and the availability of Staff Group members. The
timetable also does not reflect the impact of other activities and meetings,
particularly in September and October, which can affect the Staff Group
preparatory work.
