> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Tuesday, 10th August, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on August 10, 1971, to address several administrative and administrative and strategic items. The group welcomed the new SACIANT Representative, Captain J. Orrill of the United States Navy. The meeting covered a following list of documents, including procedural arrangements for work programmes, illustrative studies for the Autumn Ministerial meeting, and a progress report on the implementation of Special Political Guidelines for Atomic Demolition Munitions. Additionally, the group discussed unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence, noting a paper by the International Staff and an alternative text from the United Kingdom. The Staff Group aimed to reach agreement on a text for a Working Paper to be sent to Capitals by September 6, 1971. The Military Committee Representative was invited to examine nuclear weapon systems requirements of the Military Authorities. The Chairman outlined the plan to prepare drafts for illustrative studies, a status report on follow-on work, an agenda for the October NPG Ministerial meeting, and issues emerging from the CENTAG Area Study. The group agreed to hold subsequent meetings on August 12 and 17, 1971, in Brussels, Belgium.

UNRESOLVED PROBLEMS RELATED TO TACTICAL NUCLEAR DEFENCE
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who described the approach taken by
the International Staff in preparing their paper(1) on the subject of unresolved
problems;

(b) noted the Chairman's recommendations that it should be the aim of the Staff
Group to reach agreement on a text during the coming weeks so that a document,
in the form of a NPG Working Paper, could be sent to Capitals not later than 6th
September, 1971;

(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion, and of an
alternative text circulated by the United Kingdom Representative; noted in
particular a variety of views on the extent to which a study of unresolved
problems could usefully be undertaken before the results of the follow-on
studies had been analysed;

STAFF GROUP WORK IN AUGUST

4. The STAFF GROUP

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he intended to
prepare and circulate the following papers for attention by the Staff Group
during August:

(i) the first draft of a paper on illustrative studies issues;
(ii) the first draft of a status report on follow-on work(1);
(iii) the first draft of an Agenda for the NPG Ministerial meeting in October;

final look at its various papers should take place during the week beginning
30th August, 1971.
