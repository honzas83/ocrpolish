> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Thursday, 9th April, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on April 9, 1970, to address several key administrative and public relations issues. The group discussed the handling of information on nuclear planning appearing in public newsheets and reviewed a letter from the Secretary General regarding relations with the press. The group also considered modifications to the International Staff working paper on Allied Deployment Models (A.D.M.s), including proposals from the United States representative to include summaries and comparisons of SACCUR studies. It was suggested that a short briefing on these studies be provided to Permanent Representatives and Ministers, with the SHPE representative tasked to investigate the possibilities. Additionally, the German representative noted that a separate national study was pending ministerial approval. The group agreed that a meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives in mid-April would be premature and decided that the first item for the next ministerial meeting in June would be the agenda. Finally, the United Kingdom representative requested up-to-date information on the tactical stockpile, a request which the United States representative agreed to transmit to his authorities. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for April 16, 1970.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Thursday, 9th April, 1970

INFORMATION TO THE PRESS

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who
recalled discussions in the Staff Group at previous
meetings on the subject of information on nuclear
planning appearing in a public newsheet, and
drew the attention of the Staff Group to a letter(1)
on the subject of relations with the Press sent
by the Secretary General to all members of the
International Staff.

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman regarding
possible modifications to the composition and
thrust of the International Staff working paper(2)
on A.D.M.s, and of the list of specific changes(3)
which had been prepared as a result of the discussion
held on Monday, 6th April;
(b) noted the general approval of the working paper
expressed by members of the Staff Group who had
not been able to attend the meeting held on
6th April;

(c) took note of the views and suggestions expressed in the subsequent
discussion, and in particular the proposals by the United States representative
regarding the inclusion of sections summarising and comparing SACCUR'S studies;

(d) noted a suggestion that a short briefing on the studies might be useful both
to Permanent Representatives and to Ministers, and agreed to get views on this
proposal from their authorities;

(e) took note of various suggestions as to when and how the briefing might be
made, and invited the SHPE representative to investigate the possibilities;

(f) noted that the SHPE representative had no specific information about the
fourth ADH study (FORTTHAG) that was promised for late April;

(g) noted a statement by the German representative that a separate German
national study was now before his Minister; since it was not known whether or
when he might approve it, present work should in no way be held up;

(h) invited the International Staff, in the light of the views expressed, to
prepare a revised version of the working paper in time for the next meeting of
the Staff Group.

NEXT MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed that a meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives during the period
13th to 24th April would be premature;

(b) agreed that selection of an appropriate date would be the subject of further
discussion.

AGENDA FOR MINISTERIAL MEETING IN JUNE

4. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed that the first matter to be discussed at the next meeting should be the
Agenda for the meeting of NPG Ministers.
