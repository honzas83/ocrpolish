> # Review of Final WINTEX 73 Reports
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated 23rd July, 1974. It addresses the review of final reports from Exercise WINTEX 73, following a request from Ministers at the Ankara meeting. The Chairman notes that the International Military Staff and the Council Operations and Exercise Committee have circulated summaries of the final reports prepared by NATO Major Commanders. After reviewing these papers, the Chairman concludes that no new information of interest to the NPG Ministers has been brought to their attention that was not already known. Consequently, he suggests that the Staff Group agree that an additional report by the Permanent Representatives is not required. If confirmed, this conclusion will be part of the Secretary General's documentation for the Autumn 1974 NPG Ministerial meeting's agenda item on the Future Work Programme.

CONSEIL DE L'ATLANTIQUE NORD
NORTH ATLANTIC COUNCIL

23rd July, 1974.
NOTICE
NPG(Staff Group)N(74)80
NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
REVIEW OF FINAL WINTEX 73 REPORTS
Note by the Chairman
After discussing a report by the Permanent Representatives containing some
preliminary observations on WINTEX 73(1), the Ministers at the Ankara meeting
last year asked(2) the Permanent Representatives to review the final WINTEX 73
reports and to comment, if appropriate, on nuclear consultation aspects. The
International Military Staff and the Council Operations and Exercise Committee
recently circulated two papers(3) containing a summary of the final reports on
Exercise WINTEX 73 prepared by the NATO Major Commanders. A review of these
papers leads me to the conclusion that none of the final WINTEX 73 reports
contains comments or observations on matters of interest to the NPG Ministers
that have not already been brought to their attention. Accordingly, no further
report by the Permanent Representatives would appear to be warranted.

2. If, after discussing this subject at an early meeting, the Staff Group agrees
that an additional Permanent Representatives' report on WINTEX 73 is not
necessary, I suggest that this conclusion be placed before the Permanent
Representatives during their review of future NPG work in preparing for the
Autumn 1974 NPG Ministerial meeting. If confirmed by the Permanent
Representatives, the conclusion would then be reflected in the PO prepared by
the Secretary General to provide a basis for Ministerial discussion of the
agenda item on Future Work Programme.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM
NATO, 1110 Brussels.
