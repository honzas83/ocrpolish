> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on 6th August, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on 6th August, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with the recording of personnel changes, specifically the unexpected departure of Lt. Colonel G.K. Venetsanos of Greece and the farewell of Colonel J. Spiegelenberg. Regarding documentation, the group took note of the Military Committee report on the Implementation of the Special ADM Guidelines. The group discussed operational concepts and doctrine, agreeing to circulate a Working Paper on the subject, including a clarifying comment requested by Denmark. The consultation procedures and facilities report was reviewed, with the United States recommending a less peremptory tone, and it was decided that the paper should be recast as a detailed report for Permanent Representatives. The group also addressed the early combined use of conventional and nuclear capabilities, noting reports from SHAPE and the Military Committee, and agreed to continue discussions on the political aspects of this issue. Finally, the group concurred with a suggestion from the Germany representative for a briefing at SHAPE Headquarters on Nuclear Strike Planning and associated subjects, and invited the SHAPE representative to arrange such a briefing for late September. The next meeting was scheduled for 13th August, 1973.

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels,
on 6th August, 1975

DEPARTURES

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who announced with regret the
unexpected departure of It. Colonel G.K. Venetsanos, the Greek representative,
who was returning to his country on promotion to a post of Command;

(b) noted with appreciation the farewell observations of It. Colonel Venetsanos;
(c) warmly endorsed the farewell remarks expressed by the Chairman to Colonel J.
Spiegelenberg who was attending his last meeting of the Staff Group.

DOCUMENTATION

2. The STAFF GROUP: took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the
Military Committee report on the Implementation of the Special ADM Guidelines
had been received and would be circulated immediately to all concerned under
reference NPG/D(73)9, dated 2nd August, 1973.

(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled that the Staff Group
had discussed the latest version of the Working Paper(1) on Operational Concepts
and Doctrine at its last meeting and had agreed, subject (1) NPG(Staff

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the latest
version(2) of the draft report on Consultation Procedures and Facilities; he
explained the various changes made in the light of the discussion of the
previous version, and invited comments on the form and content of the new paper;
he underlined that a number of statements concerning the views of, or action by
Capitals and/or the NATO Military Commanders remained to be substantiated;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion with particular
regard to the form and content of the report to Ministers, and to the remarks of
the United States representative who recommended that the tone of the paper
should be less peremptory;

(c) agreed that the paper should be recast in the form of a detailed report to
be approved at Permanent Representatives level, which would contain the draft of
an additional brief report to be forwarded to the Ministers by the Permanent

EARLY COMBINED USE OF CONVENTIONAL AND NUCLEAR CAPABILITIES

5. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled that the SHAPE report
on Early Combined Use, and the related Military Committee comments had now been
circulated(1); he reminded the Staff Group of the original paper(2) by Mr.
Brosio on unresolved problems related to nuclear defence, and suggested that
Permanent Representatives would probably wish to make a report to Ministers on
the SHAPE and Military Committee contributions; the Permanent Representatives'
report should include in particular their views on the political aspects of
Early Combined Use, since the SHAPE report naturally confined itself to the
military side of the problem; he proposed that the Staff Group should hold an
initial discussion of the matter;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the subsequent discussion and agreed to
continue it at the next meeting.

BRIEFING OF STAFF GROUP IN SHAPE HEADQUARTERS ON NUCLEAR STRIKE PLANNING AND
ASSOCIATED SUBJECTS

6. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) concurred with the remarks of the German representative who suggested that
the Staff Group would benefit from a briefing in SHAPE Headquarters on Nuclear
Strike Planning and associated subjects;

(b) invited the SHAPE representative to examine with his Authorities the
possibility of arranging such a briefing towards the end of September.

NEXT MEETING

7. The STAFF GROUP:

agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 13th August, 1973, at 3.00 p.m.

NATO, 110 Brussels.
