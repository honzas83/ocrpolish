> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Friday, 24th September, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on September 24, 1971, to review and approve various working papers and administrative matters. The meeting began with a farewell to Colonel F. Körner and a welcome to Mr. V. Villadsen from the Danish Ministry of Foreign Affairs. The group discussed and approved changes to several working papers, including the Illustrative Study (NPG/WP(71)6), Unresolved Problems (NPG/WP(71)7), and the Status of Work (NPG/WP(71)9), while noting that the Implementation of Special ADM Guidelines (NPG/WP(71)8) required no changes. They also addressed the AD 70 Follow-up Action (NPG/WP(71)10) and the CENTAG Area Study (NPG/WP(71)12), discussing the transmittal procedure for reports to Ministers. The group further discussed the military comments on regional follow-on studies, noting support for the Canadian view that such comments should be reviewed at the Staff Group or Permanent Representative level before being forwarded to Ministers. Finally, the group established the schedule for the next meetings of the NPG Permanent Representatives and the Staff Group, with the next Staff Group meeting scheduled for October 4, 1971.

AD 70 Follow-up Action – NPG/WP(71)10

8. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that the text of the Working Paper on the nuclear aspects of AD 70
Follow-up Action had already been agreed by the Staff Group, and that the matter
was not on the Agenda for Ministers;

(b) recalled that the main problem with this paper was the choice of procedure
with regard to its transmittal to Ministers; noted in this respect that there
were two courses of action:

(1) to submit a report from the NPG to the DPC Ministers via the NDAC, the
report to be approved by the Permanent Representatives on the basis of National
instructions;

or (11) to provide an input for the DPC Permanent Representatives to use in
their report to Ministers; this report could be handled at NPG Permanent
Representative or Staff Group level;

(c) noted that, although opinions were divided, there appeared to be a growing
tendency in favour of the first course of action;

(d) agreed to discuss the matter again at a future meeting of the Staff Group.

COMMENTS BY THE MILITARY AUTHORITIES ON REGIONAL FOLLOW-ON STUDIES
10. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled the discussion on the subject of military comments which had taken
place at the last meeting of the Staff Group;

(b) noted statements by the Representatives of Denmark, The Netherlands, Norway
and Turkey in support of the Canadian view that military comments should be
reviewed at Staff Group/Permanent Representative level before being forwarded to
Ministers;

(c) recalled that in the particular case of the CENTAG Study shortage of time
might prevent the desired review; expressed however the hope that the
preparation of comments might be expected.

VIEWS OF NON-PARTICIPATING COUNTRIES
11. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed that, in accordance with usual practice, an opportunity shall be
given to those NPG countries not participating in the forthcoming Ministerial
Meeting (Canada, The Netherlands, Norway and Turkey) to make known their views
on matters on the Agenda;

(b) noted that there would be an opportunity for an exchange of views at the
meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives to be held on 14th October, 1971;
noted, however, in view of the problem on timing mentioned in paragraph
10(c).above, that it might be necessary to arrange for another meeting to
discuss the military comments on the CENTAG Area Study, depending on when these
comments were received.
