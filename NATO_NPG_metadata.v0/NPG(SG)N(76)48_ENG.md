> # Nuclear Consultation – CNI Procedures
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group dated 26th May 1976. It addresses the handling of Consultation on Nuclear Installations (CNI) procedures among iFG countries. The Turkish Delegation requested a change to the paragraph in the Annex to NPG/WF(76)1 to clarify the role of the Secretary General in ensuring the Council/DPC considers the desirability and feasibility of common action. Following discussions on 24th May 1976, the Staff Group agreed to a revised version of the text. The Chairman, R.E. Shearer, suggests that this amendment be handled via the silence procedure, with a deadline for comments by 31st May 1976. If no objections are raised, the final document will be circulated with the amended language included.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
NUCLEAR CONSULTATION – CNI PROCEDURES
Note by the Chairman
In the cover note to NPG/WF(76)1, 28th April, 1976, it was mentioned that iFG
countries through their Staff Group members had agreed to handle CNI procedures
at Permanent Representative level and under the silence procedure with a
deadline set for close of business, Friday, 21st May, 1976.

2. The Turkish Delegation has informed(1) the Secretary General of their desire
to change paragraph 3, first sentence, in the Annex to NPG/WF(76)1, to read as
follows:

"The Secretary General will ensure that the Council/ DPC, as the nuclear
consultation develops, consider the desirability and feasibility of common
action and/or of communicating to the nuclear power(s) national views regarding
an appropriate CNI action."

3. The Staff Group, having discussed the matter in more detail on Monday, 24th
May, 1976, agreed to seek national authorities' approval to the following
amended version:

"The Secretary General will ensure that the Council/ DPC, as the nuclear
consultation process develops, consider the desirability and feasibility of
common action, and will communicate to the nuclear power(s) national views
regarding an appropriate CNI action."

4. It is suggested this amendment be dealt with by the silence procedure;
therefore, if no comments are received by close of business on Monday, 31st May,
1976, the final document will be circulated with the amended language included.

(Signed) R.E. SHeAReR
This document consists of one page.
