> # Corrigendum to NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)8(Revised)
> 
> This document is a corrigendum to the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) working paper NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)8(Revised), dated 11th July, 1973. It requests holders of the document to delete paragraph 4, page 4, and replace it with a new version. The updated paragraph states that the Military Committee endorses the view of the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) that the concepts and doctrine in the SHAPE Report provide NATO with the necessary flexibility to respond to enemy aggression and enable military planning to counter threats to Allied Command Europe. Additionally, it notes that the Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic (SACLANT) has stated that the operational concepts and doctrine for tactical nuclear weapons employment in his command are essentially the same as those in the SHAPE Report, while referencing previous documents prepared for the NPG Ministers regarding the maritime environment.
> 
> ## Categories/Topics
> 
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Flexible-Response — The document discusses the 'concepts and doctrine' outlined in the SHAPE Report, which provided NATO with the 'flexibility to respond to potential enemy aggression,' a core tenet of the Flexible Response strategy (MC 14/3).
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Extended-Deterrence-and-Tactical-Nuclear-Sharing — The text explicitly mentions the 'employment of nuclear weapons for tactical purposes' in both the European and maritime environments, which falls under the scope of tactical nuclear sharing and extended deterrence.
> #Internal-Alliance-Dynamics/Specific-Commands-and-Infrastructure — The document focuses on the views and reports of specific NATO commands, namely SACEUR (Supreme Allied Commander Europe) and SACLANT (Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic).

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Corrigendum to NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)8(Revised), 11th July, 1973
Holders of the above-mentioned document are requested to delete paragraph 4,
page 4, and insert the following:

"4. The Military Committee endorses(1) SACEUR's view that the concepts and
doctrine outlined in the SHAPE Report provide NATO with the necessary
flexibility to respond to potential enemy aggression, and also provide SACEUR
and his subordinate commanders with the means to carry out military planning to
counter the threat to Allied Command Europe. Similarly, the Supreme Allied
Commander Atlantic (SACLANT) has stated that the operational concepts and
doctrine currently governing the employment of nuclear weapons for tactical
purposes in his command are essentially the same as those set forth in the SHAPE
Report. In this context, we note that SACLANT has prepared several documents(2)
for the NPG Ministers in the past that have discussed the operational concepts
and doctrine pertaining to the employment of nuclear weapons for tactical
purposes in the maritime environment in considerable detail."
