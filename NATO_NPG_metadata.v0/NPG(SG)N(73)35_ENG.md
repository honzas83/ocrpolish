> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Thursday, 26th April, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on April 26, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to coordinate preparations for the upcoming NPG Ministerial meeting in Ankara. The group reviewed several documents related to the Ministerial agenda, the ACLANT Area Study, the Follow-on work programme, nuclear consultation in WINTEX 73, and the SHAPE Illustrative Study. Additionally, the group approved a draft advance press release to be issued simultaneously in Ottawa, Bonn, Ankara, and Washington. Discussions also covered the Danish intervention regarding Ministerial participation and the preparation of a draft agreed minute and final communiqué. The Turkish authorities' proposed programme of work and entertainment, including a visit to the Hittite Museum, was noted. The group also discussed future NPG activity, including Warsaw Pact nuclear doctrine, the concept of deterrence, and escalation, while prioritizing Phase II of the Follow-on studies. Finally, the group scheduled its next meetings for May 7 and May 21, 1973, and noted that the location for the Autumn NPG Ministerial meeting had not yet been fixed.

THE ANKARA MEETING - MISCELLANEOUS MATTERS
Any other Business
4.  The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the Danish
intervention (1) at the last NPG Ministerial meeting regarding Ministerial
participation at NPG Meetings; he recalled that this question had been examined
by the NPG Permanent Representatives who had agreed that no change should be
made to present arrangements; this matter would be referred to by the Secretary
General under "Any other business".

Initial Draft Agreed Minute and Communiqué
5.  The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he intended to
prepare the outline of a draft agreed minute and of a final communiqué for the
use of the International Staff, and would also circulate copies to members in
about ten days time; he emphasized that these drafts were simply for convenience
and should not be considered in any way as prejudging the results of the
meeting;

(b) took note of a suggestion that the final communiqué should contain more
substance than in the past.

Arrangements by the Turkish Authorities
6.  The STAFF GROUP :
took note of the remarks of the Turkish representative who outlined the
programme of work and entertainment proposed by this Authorities; with regard to
the Ladies programme final arrangements would be made as soon as further
information was available as to the number of ladies and their names; there
would also be a visit to the Hittite Museum during the afternoon of Wednesday,
16th May, for those who wished to take part..

FUTURE NPG ACTIVITY

7. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Staff Group,
at its meeting on 2nd April, 1973, had discussed future NPG activity, along the
lines set out in a Staff Group notice (1); planning of NPG activity should be on
a longer term basis than simply the next NPG Ministerial meeting; he suggested
that the Staff Group might wish to examine, inter alia, the following matters :

(i) Warsaw Pact nuclear doctrine,
(ii) the concept of deterrence,
(iii) escalation,
(iv) further illustrative studies, possibly
by individual countries,
(v) the implementation of ADM Guidelines;
(b) took note of the views expressed and the suggestions made during the ensuing
discussion; noted in particular a general agreement that the work on Phase II of
the Follow-on studies should continue to be considered

as a priority matter;
(c) agreed to continue the discussion of future NPG activity after the Ankara
meeting and in the light of the Ministerial decisions.

PLACE OF THE AUTUMN NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING

8. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the place of the
Autumn NPG Ministerial meeting had not yet been fixed;

(b) expressed the hope that this problem could be solved in the near future.
