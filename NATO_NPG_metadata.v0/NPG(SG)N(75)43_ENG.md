> # Amendment to Permanent Representatives' Report on Observations on VINTEX 75
> 
> This document, issued by the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) on May 16, 1975, outlines proposed amendments to the Permanent Representatives' report on observations on VINTEX 75. Following a meeting on May 12, 1975, it was agreed to insert additional language regarding the timing and relative characteristics of initial and follow-on nuclear weapon use. Specifically, the amendments target Paragraph 8, to clarify the timing of nuclear weapon requests and the relationship between initial and follow-on use, and Paragraph 19(c), to ensure these issues are addressed in Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme. The note, signed by R.E. Melar, specifies that if no further comments are received by May 20, 1975, the final Nuclear Planning Group document will be distributed.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

AMENDMENT TO PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES' REPORT ON OBSERVATIONS ON VINTEX 75

Note by the Chairman

At the Permanent Representatives' meeting on 12th May, 1975 it was generally
agreed to insert additional language into the NFG Permanent Representatives'
report on VINTEX 75(1).

2. Staff Group members on May 15th agreed to the following amendments:

- Paragraph 8 to continue after last sentence with the following:

"In discussing these requests for the use of nuclear weapons, the DFC noted that
confusion could develop with regard to their relative timing, especially taking
into account the unique characteristics of initial use and its relationship to
follow-on use. This relationship is also under consideration in the Follow-on
Use Work Programme now underway."

- Paragraph 19(c), change to read as follows:

"Agree that the questions discussed in this report, especially those concerning
the relationship of initial use, should be addressed further in Phase III of the
Follow-on Use Work Programme."

3. If no further comments are received by close of business on 20th May, 1975
the final NFG document will be distributed.

(Signed) R.E. MELAR.

NATO, 1110 Brussels.
