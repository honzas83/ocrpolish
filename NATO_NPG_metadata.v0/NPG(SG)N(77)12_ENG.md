> # Downgrading or Destruction of Selected NPG Documents
> 
> This document, issued by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), provides a notice regarding the downgrading or destruction of selected documents from 1972 and 1973. Following a review of NPG documents, a number of papers have been authorized for downgrading to lower classification levels, such as from NATO SECRET to NATO CONFIDENTIAL or NATO CONFIDENTIAL to NATO RESTRICTED. The notice informs Staff Group members that the Head of the NATO Registry will notify holders of these documents of the downgrading action. It further suggests that members verify that their copies of the documents listed in the annexes are appropriately downgraded and destroy copies of documents for which they have no further need. The annexes provide a comprehensive list of 66 documents, including working papers and notices, related to the Nuclear Planning Group and its Staff Group, covering topics such as follow-on use, ministerial meetings, and consultation procedures. These documents are listed by document number, date, and description, and specify the authorized downgrading for each item.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
DOWNGRADING OR DESTRUCTION OF SELECTED NPG DOCUMENTS
Note by the Chairman
As a result of a recent review of NPG documents issued in 1972 and 1973, it was
proposed(1) that a number of documents be downgraded, to which NPG countries
subsequently agreed. Holders of the documents were then notified(2) of the
authorised downgrading by the Head of the NATO Registry.

2. As a related matter, a review of the various drafts and working papers
circulated in connection with the preparation of those NPG documents, for which
a downgrading action was authorised, has been undertaken to identify additional
papers that may also be downgraded. These documents are listed at Annex,
together with the revised classifications that have been authorised by the
originators. It is expected that the Head of the NATO Registry will also notify
holders of these documents of this downgrading action.

3. Staff Group members may wish to verify that copies of the documents listed at
Annexes to NPG/N(76)1 and to this Notice, which may be in their possession, have
been appropriately downgraded. It is also suggested that Staff Group members may
wish to destroy copies of these documents for which they have no further need.

(Signed) R.E. SHEARER
1110 Brussels.
(1) NPG/N(76)1, 12th August, 1976
(2) DN(77)1, 12th January, 1977
