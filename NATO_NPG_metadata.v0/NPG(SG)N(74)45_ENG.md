> # Phase II Study Team Status Report
> 
> This document is a status report from the Phase II Study Team of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), reflecting the status of its work as of 24th April, 1974. The report is intended to be appended to the Secretary General's PO on future Nuclear Planning Group work. The Phase II Study Team has met twice since its last report in September 1973, specifically in London and Bonn. During these meetings, the team completed working papers on various critical topics including timing and scale of use, aspects of deterrence, enemy response, military effectiveness,effectiveness of weaponry, function of conventional forces, collateral damage, and political control. It was decided that these working papers would be incorporated into the Study Team's Preliminary Report. A first draft of the Preliminary Report was considered in London and revised in Bonn. The team hopes to submit a draft for consideration by other Nuclear Planning Group nations in the summer of 1974.

PHASE II STUDY TEAM REPORT
STATUS AS OF 24th APRIL, 1974
1. Since its last status report of 15th September 1973, the Ad Hoc Study Team
has met twice, in London on 22nd-24th January and in Bonn on 2nd-4th April. At
these meetings the Study Team completed its preparation of working papers on
timing and scale of use, aspects of deterrence, enemy response, military
effectiveness, weaponry, function of conventional forces, collateral damage, use
in an extended geographical area, relationship of follow-on use to initial use,
and political control. It was decided that the working papers need not be
subject to final revision and agreement, but that their main conclusions should
be incorporated in the Study Team's Preliminary Report.

2. The Study Team also considered at the London meeting a first draft of the
Preliminary Report which was subsequently revised and reconsidered at the Bonn
meeting. This draft is now being subjected to further revision, and in view of
the progress which has been made it is now hoped that it will be possible to
submit a draft for consideration by the other NPG nations some time in the
summer of 1974.
