> # Procedural Handling of US Paper on Improving the Effectiveness of NATO's Theatre Nuclear Force Posture
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, dated 12th March 1976, regarding the procedural handling of a United States paper titled 'Improving the Effectiveness of NATO's Theatre Nuclear Force Posture'. The Chairman intends to raise this matter in the Staff Group on 5th April to address the handling of military assessments, preparations for Permanent Representatives' discussions, and procedures for the Spring 1976 Ministerial meeting. The document provides a summary of comments by US Secretary of Defense Donald Rumsfeld, who noted that the Nunn Amendment led to a comprehensive survey of improving NATO's theatre nuclear force posture. Mr. Rumsfeld emphasized that the collective analysis should be coordinated and rational, seeking broad consensus on major discussion areas rather than considering individual items separately. It was agreed that the process would involve assessment by NATO Military Authorities and consideration by NPG Permanent Representatives based on national views, followed by a discussion by Ministers at their next meeting.

PROCEDURAL HANDLING OF US PAPER ON IMPROVING THE EFFECTIVENESS OF NATO'S THEATRE
NUCLEAR FORCE POSTURE

Note by the Chairman

1. In response to several informal inquiries from NPG member nations, I intend
to raise, in the Staff Group on 5th April, the matter of procedural handling of
the US paper(1) on "Improving the Effectiveness of NATO's Theatre Nuclear Force
Posture" which was distributed during the Hamburg meeting. Consideration of this
issue should include: the handling of the military assessment expected during
April; preparations for the related Permanent Representatives' discussion; and
procedures for handling this matter at the Ministerial meeting in the Spring of
1976.

2. To facilitate our discussion, and to aid those not present at Hamburg as well
as those who will not be included in the Spring Ministerial meeting, a summary
of the comments by the US Secretary of Defense as he presented the US report to
NPG Ministers is provided below:

Mr. Rumsfeld recalled that the Nunn Amendment had led to a comprehensive survey
of the possibility of improving the effectiveness of NATO's theatre nuclear
force posture and that the paper just distributed(2) contained proposals on how
this question might be the subject of a collective analysis. He stated that this
collective analysis should be undertaken in a co-ordinated and rational way

considering also the need for further improvements. The Secretary then
highlighted a number of substantive points raised in the U.S. Study (these,
among others, covered areas which are also under consideration in the Technology
Study and in Phase III). Mr. Rumsfeld went on to say that individual items
should not be considered on a separate basis. Broad consensus should be sought
on the framework of major discussion areas. In this context he underlined that
views of other should be known while shaping own views. His suggestions that the
paper, as a preliminary to NPG discussion, be assessed by the NATO-Military
Authorities and then be considered by the NPG Permanent Representatives on the
basis of national views and contributions were accepted(1) and it was agreed(2)
that Ministers themselves discuss at their next meeting the issues raised in the
U.S. paper, together with any preliminary views expressed by the NATO Military
Authorities.
