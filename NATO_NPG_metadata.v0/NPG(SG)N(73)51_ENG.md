> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday, 13th June, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on June 13, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key administrative and strategic items. The group endorsed the welcome of Lieut. Colonel J.P.H. van Valderen and planned a farewell luncheon for Colonel J.B. Spiegelenberg. The meeting covered documentation updates, including requests for reports on special ADM Guidelines and the function of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation process. The group discussed the possibility of providing basic documents to Portugal and discussed potential dates for the Autumn NPG Ministerial meeting, specifically favoring November 6th and 7th. Future NPG activities were introduced, including a United States briefing on the balance of strategic forces and a follow-on use work programme. The group also agreed that the Secretary General should request SACEUR to produce an illustrative study on nuclear air defence in a land environment. Other topics included consultation procedures, the implementation of ADM Guidelines, and the Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy and Military Doctrine, noting that the Netherlands would cease to be a full member of the NPG on July 1, 1974. The United Kingdom representative provided updates on the conceptual analysis of the nuclear aspects of deterrence. Finally, the group agreed on a few proposed dates for meetings of Permanent Representatives and scheduled their next meeting for June 18, 1973.

Illustrative Studies (Paragraph 5)
11. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that, at Ankara,
Ministers had invited the Chairman of the Military Committee to arrange for the
Major NATO Commanders to prepare additional illustrative studies;

(b) took note of the views expressed and the suggestions made by the members of
the Staff Group;

(c) agreed that the Secretary General should be advised to write to the Chairman
of the Military Committee inviting him to ask: SACEUR to produce an illustrative
Study dealing with nuclear air defence, in a land environment, in time for its
inclusion in the agenda for the Spring 1974 NPG meeting.

Consultation Procedures and Facilities (Paragraph 6)
12. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the various views expressed concerning the transmission,
identification and handling of messages related to nuclear consultation;
discussed also the clarity of messages and the possibility of lifting ATOMAL
limitations in time of war,

(b) accepted with appreciation an offer by the Chairman to prepare a note
setting out the present situation, describing the factors affecting the problem
and making a number of draft proposals, for discussion at a future meeting.
