> # Notes of the Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 8th April, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on 8th April 1974 at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address a range of strategic and administrative issues. The meeting included a note on membership changes, specifically the arrival of Capt. S.P. Gallup replacing Capt. E. Barrett as the IMS Representative. The group discussed several documents, including a conceptual analysis of nuclear deterrence and a SACEUR illustrative study on initial air defence use. Plans were coordinated for the NPG Ministerial Meeting in Bergen, Norway, and a future meeting in Italy. The group also addressed the status of a Technology Study and the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process. Discussions touched upon United States targeting policy and the status of Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs). The group agreed to request a new illustrative study, possibly on the land battle environment, to be submitted by January 1975. Additionally, the meeting covered the political implications of early combined nuclear use and the status of the Phase II Team's work. Finally, the STAFF GROUP reviewed a draft report on Warsaw Pact strategy and doctrine, agreeing to further discussion at their next meeting scheduled for 22nd April 1974.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of the meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday,
8th April, 1974

STAFF GROUP MEMBERSHIP CHANGE
1. The STAFF GROUP: noted the arrival of Capt. S.P. Gallup, replacing Capt. E.
Barrett as the IMS Representative.

DOCUMENTATION
2. The STAFF GROUP: took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention
to and commented upon the following papers which had recently been circulated:

(a) NPG/D(74)2, 29th March, 1974: The Report by the Permanent Representatives on
a Conceptual Analysis of Nuclear Aspects of Deterrence. This matter was given
final approval at Permanent Representative level and therefore it will not
feature on the agenda for the Ministerial meeting;

(b) Corrigendum to NPG/Study/44, 3rd April, 1974: a letter from SHAPE requesting
that certain changes be made to NPG/Study/44, SACEUR Illustrative Study on
Initial Air Defence Use;

(c) NPG/WP(74)3, 5th April, 1974: The Draft Agenda for the NPG Ministerial
Meeting in Bergen, Norway on 11th and 12th June, 1974. If no comments or
objections are received from participating countries by close of business on
Friday, 3rd May, 1974, it will be presumed that the draft is acceptable and the
final Ministerial Agenda will be issued.

TECHNOLOGY STUDY
3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who informed the Group that, since no
comments or objections were received by 5th April on NPG/WP(74)2, arrangements
for a Technology Study, Draft Report by the Permanent Representatives, such
document will be shortly circulated as NPG/D(74)3.

MILITARY COMMITTEE FUNCTION IN THE NUCLEAR CONSULTATION PROCESS
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that the three countries which at the last Staff Group meeting were
not in a position to agree to distribution of the draft report as an NPG Working
Paper had now accepted it;

(b) invited the Chairman to take the necessary steps to circulate an NPG Working
Paper on the subject for consideration by the Permanent Representatives at their
meeting on 16th May.

U.S. TARGETING POLICY
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman concerning the interest shown by a number
of Permanent Representatives in having a further exchange of view on the matter
at a future NPG Permanent Representatives meeting;

(b) examined various alternatives for providing for such an exchange and noted
that it might be conveniently handled at the NPG Permanent Representatives
meeting scheduled for 16th May;

(c) agreed to continue discussion at 'the next Staff Group meeting.
TRANSPORTATION TO SHAPE
6. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman informing the Staff Group members that
bus transportation to SHAPE in occasion of the visit scheduled for 23rd April
was not available and consequently members should make personal arrangements.

DRAFT ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE

7. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed on a text of an advance press release for the Spring 1974 Ministerial
meeting(1) to be forwarded to capitals of the participating countries for
approval.

ADMs

8. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Permanent
Representatives have a continuing responsibility to monitor the progress being
made in implementing the Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of
Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs), and suggested that, if the Permanent
Representatives wished to make a review of the status in the Autumn of 1974, a
request should be made now for an input from the NATO Military Authorities;

(b) after a discussion of various factors which might have a bearing on a
decision, agreed that the review should be made some time in 1975. This should
be reflected in the PO on NPG future work, which will be circulated to the
Ministers by the Secretary General.

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES

9. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the general request
for further illustrative studies made by the Ministers in Ankara(2) and noted
that the only illustrative study not yet considered by the Ministers(3) would
probably be on their Autumn 1974 agenda;

(b) in view of the considerable time needed by the NATO Military Authorities to
prepare such studies, agreed with the intention of the Chairman to recommend
that the Secretary General send a letter to the Chairman, Military Committee,
requesting that an illustrative study, possibly in the land battle environment,
be submitted to the Secretary General by 31st January, 1975 for possible use by
the NPG Ministers at one of their meetings in 1975;

(c) agreed that a specific decision to place the requested study on the agenda

MINISTERIAL MEETING IN NORWAY
10. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a request by the Norwegian Representative that any particular

DATE AND PLACE OF NEXT NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING
11. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Italian member who informed the other members that
the Italian Government intended to extend an invitation to hold the next NPG
Ministerial meeting in Italy on 7th and 8th November, if these dates were
convenient to other participants;

(b) agreed that members would seek to get instructions concerning the proposed
dates from their capitals in time for the Permanent Representatives' meeting on
16th May.

EARLY COMBINED USE
12. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Italian member who informed the Group that his
authorities now agreed with the view expressed by the other countries that a
detailed study of the political implications of an early combined use required
no further effort at the moment but should be given attention early in a
developing crisis well in advance of the outbreak of hostilities if possible; a
statement recognising this agreed position should be included in the PO on NPG
future work.

PHASE II
13. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the US member who informed the Group that the Phase
II Team at its last meeting in Bomm in early April had decided to undertake the
preparation of a preliminary draft for discussion with the Permanent
Representatives and that the Team will soon make available to the Chairman a
written report on the status of its work for possible attachment to the PO on
NPG future work.
