> [!abstract] 
> # Preparations for the Autumn Ministerial Meeting
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on July 26, 1971, to discuss the agenda and a detailed timetable for the Autumn Ministerial Meeting scheduled for October 26-27, 1971, in Brussels. The meeting's focus was on defining the tasks required for the preparatory work, including follow-on use studies for CENTAG and Turkey, and potentially Italy. The group discussed the preparation of a status report on the follow-on use work programme, an illustrative study presentation, and suggestions for unresolved problems mentioned in PO/71/226. Additionally, the group addressed the implementation of ADM guidelines and contributions to the AD 70 report. The document includes a detailed timetable from July to October 1971, outlining the meetings of the Staff Group and Permanent Representatives to finalize the agenda and reports for the Ministers. The meeting concluded with agreement on the next meeting date of August 2, 1971, in Brussels.
>

>

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

PREPARATIONS FOR THE AUTUMN MINISTERIAL MEETING
1. The STAFF GROUP:
discussed a possible Agenda for the Autumn Ministerial Meeting which had been
tabbed by the Chairman and the tasks which would have to be undertaken by the
Staff Group under each Item. (The general points on each Item of the possible
Agenda are given below).

A. Follow-on Use Studies
Selection of Studies which would include the CENTAG Study, and possibly that of
Turkey, bearing in mind the Turkish preference for deferment until the Spring of
1972; other studies such as those of Italy could included if available in time.
The question of requesting a prior review by the NMAs would be discussed in the
SG on a case-by-case basis. Preparation of a report to NPG Ambassadors on the
selected studies. Presentation of the CENTAG Study to NPG Ambassadors between
4th and 15th October.

B. Status Report on Follow-on Use Work Programme
Preparation of a Status Report for NPG Ambassadors to submit to Ministers. Study
teams would have to produce inputs upon the status of active studies as of 15th
September. These would be annexed to the report. The Staff Group may also wish
to consider whether the report should contain proposals for additional Phase I
work and for the initiation of work on later phases.

C. Illustrative Study Presentation
Preparation of a report to be submitted to Ministers by the NPG Ambassadors on
procedural and substantive questions involved in the presentation study, for
approval by Ambassadors in late September or early October. Preparation for a
preliminary review of the presentation by the Ambassadors during the week
beginning 11th October.

D. Suggestions on PO/71/226 – Unresolved Problems
The Mittenwald Minute invited the Permanent Representatives to make suggestions
to the Ministers at the next meeting for investigation of unresolved problems
mentioned in PO/71/226. Based on earlier discussions at Staff Group and
Permanent Representatives meetings, the International Staff will prepare an
initial draft for consideration at an early Staff Group meeting.

E. ADM Guideline Implementation
The action to be taken would be decided when the Military Committee's report
became available (1st August was the target date). The matter might not have to
be formally addressed at a Ministerial Meeting.

F. Contribution to AD 70 Report
It appeared that a report should be submitted to Ministers since the subject
matter would have to be approved or noted by the NDAO and subsequently by DPO
Ministers at their December meeting. In any case, a draft report would be
circulated during August for discussion in the Staff Group.

PROPOSED TIMETABLE UP TO THE AUTUMN MINISTERIAL MEETING

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a timetable for work up to the Autumn Ministerial Meeting which had
been tabled by the Chairman (attached at Annex);

(b) noted that certain delegations could not be represented at all the meetings
in August; and that meetings would therefore tend to assume an informal
character during that period.
