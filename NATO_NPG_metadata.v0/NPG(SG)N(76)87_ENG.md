> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 4th October, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on October 4, 1976, to address several key items. The group discussed the approval of a paper on Compilation and its subsequent inclusion in the Crisis Management Manual. They identified the need to update the COSMIC TOP SECRET document on the Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Forces in ACE. The meeting also covered briefings to the North Atlantic Council Permanent Representatives, specifically regarding Theatre Nuclear Force Capabilities. The group reviewed a briefing on New and Refined Procedures, suggesting improvements and scheduling its presentation to SACEUR and the Military Committee. Additionally, they addressed the classification of nuclear consultation messages as ATOMAL and the preparation of the draft Ministerial Agenda and Press Release for the upcoming NPG Ministerial Meeting. Finally, the Chairman suggested improvements to the method of preparing NPG(Staff Group) papers, emphasizing the need for broader member input and the inclusion of the descriptive precis of documents. The next meeting was scheduled for October 11, 1976.

PREPARATION OF NPG(Staff Group) PAPERS

9. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who suggested that some changes in
the method of preparing NPG(Staff Group) papers might usefully be considered;
for example, when invited to produce a draft paper on some new subject the
Nuclear Planning Directorate had often, in the past, been invited to make a
first draft without help from the members of the Group; he felt that the first
step should be to obtain the initial views of as many members as possible; such
help would enable the Nuclear Planning staff to produce a first draft which
already contained a broad view of the problem in question;

(b) noted the further remarks of the Chairman who asked for members' views on
the inclusion, as the first page of future NPG documents, a precis which would
highlight the principal points and substance of the paper;

(c) noted a further point raised by the Chairman who recalled that part of the
work of the NPG Staff Group was educational in the sense that the Group had a
responsibility towards Permanent Representatives, Ministers and their staffs in
making them aware of nuclear problems in general and in particular of nuclear
consultation in time of crisis or war.
