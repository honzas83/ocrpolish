> # Notes of a Meeting Held on 15th July, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on 15th July 1970 to address several key military and strategic planning items. The group noted that the fall ministerial meeting in Canada would likely take place on 29th and 30th October, as the 10th and 11th November dates were not feasible due to a conflicting Atlantic Assembly meeting. The group also noted that the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) had been invited to brief the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent Representatives on revised nuclear strike plans and selective release procedures during the week of 4th September. Additionally, the group invited the Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic (SACIANT) representative to arrange a briefing on the nuclear role of Allied Command Atlantic (ACLANT) forces. The Staff Group also requested the International Staff to arrange a visit to Northern Army Group (NORTHAG) during the autumn months. Finally, the group discussed revised draft special Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADM) guidelines and requested further revisions before finalizing the draft for distribution to capitals before the end of the month. The next meeting was scheduled for 22nd July 1970 in Brussels.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GRUP)
Notes of a meeting held on 15th July, 1970

FALL MINISTERIAL MEETING IN CANADA
1. The STAFF GROUP:
with respect to an inquiry by the Canadian Delegation, noted that the dates of
10th and 11th November were not feasible because of a meeting of the Atlantic
Assembly during that period and that 29th and 30th October remained the probable
dates for the meeting.

BRIEFING BY SACEUR
2. The STAFF GRUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman that the Secretary General had invited SACEUR
to give a briefing to the NEP Permanent Representatives on his revised nuclear
strike plans and on his new selective release procedures, the briefing to take
place during the week of '4th September.

BRIEFING BY SACIANT
3. The STAFF GRUOP:
(a) agreeing with a suggestion by the Netherlands representative, invited the
SACIANT representative to approach his authorities with a view to having a
briefing given to the Staff Group on the nuclear rôle of ACLANT forces;

(b) noted a statement by the SACIANT representative that he would transmit the
request to his authorities of whom he expected a favourable reply.

ATOMIC DECLIPITION NUNITIONS (ADMs)
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) discussed the revised draft of special ADI guidelines(1);
