> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, on Monday, 15th September, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened at NATO Headquarters on September 15, 1972, to review several working papers and expected reports on the SACLANT Illustrative Study and the Denmark Study. The group discussed the agenda for the upcoming NPG Ministerial Meeting, noting a request from the Danish Representative to potentially postpone the Denmark Study item due to the Ministerial arrival time. The meeting also addressed the transition, structure, and deployment of forces, agreeing to issue a revised Working Paper on Transition as an NPG Working Paper for examination by Permanent Representatives on October 5, 1972. Additionally, the group agreed to the text of a draft Advance Press Release and established a process for obtaining approval from capitals by October 10, 1972. Administrative arrangements for the NPG Ministerial Meeting in London were discussed, with the United Kingdom Representative requesting attendee lists and spouse accompaniment information. The meeting concluded with a welcome to Lt. Colonel J.C. Gill and the scheduling of the next Staff Group meeting for September 25, 1972, to continue discussions on the Ministerial Agenda and review comments from capitals.

DOCUMENTATION

1. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the following
papers which had recently been circulated:

(i) NPG/WP(72)8, dated 8th September, 1972: Implementation of Special ADM
Guidelines;

(ii) NPG/WP(72)12, dated 14th September, 1972: Consultation procedures and
Facilities;

(iii) NPG/WP(72)13, dated 14th September, 1972: Draft Agenda for the Ministerial
Meeting;

(iv) NPG(Staff Group)WP(72)17, dated 14th September, 1972: Advance Press
Release;

(b) noted that the following papers were expected to be circulated very shortly:

(i) NPG/WP(72)10: Report on the SACLANT Illustrative Study;

(ii) NPG/WP(72)11: Report on the Denmark Study;

(iii) NPG/WP(72)14: Updating of Concept – Poseidon;

(iv) NPG/D(72)9: Military Committee paper on Transition.

AGENDA FOR THE MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Danish Representative who said that there was a
possibility that his Minister would not arrive at the NPG Ministerial Meeting
until the afternoon of the first day (26th October); bearing in mind his special
interest in the Denmark Study (Item II on the Agenda) it might therefore be
necessary to postpone this item until the afternoon Session.

TRANSITION, STRUCTURE AND DEPLOYMENT OF FORCES
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the International
Staff's latest revision(1) of its Working Paper on Transition, and said that
this version reflected the suggestions made at the last meeting of the Staff
Group and took into account the views of the Military Authorities expressed in
the Military Committee paper(2); he invited comments;

(b) took note of the points made in the ensuing discussion, and agreed to a
number of changes to the text;

(c) invited the International Staff, in the light of the discussion and of the
agreed changes, to issue the paper as an NPG Working Paper, to be sent
immediately by Capitals and subsequently to be examined by NPG Permanent
Representatives on 5th October, 1972.

DRAFT ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE
4. The STAFF GROUP:

Release(5);

circulated as a Staff Group paper with cover letter requesting Delegations,

NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN LONDON – ADMINISTRATIVE ARRANGEMENTS
5. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the United Kingdom Representative who urged members
to inform him as soon as possible of the names of those people from their
Capitals and Delegations who would be attending the meeting in London, and
whether they would be accompanied by their wives; he also said that he would be
circulating a draft programme.

WELCOME
6. The STAFF GROUP:
endorsd the welcome extended by the Chairman on behalf of the Staff Group to Lt.
Colonel J.C. Gill from the Instructing Staff, Oberammergau, who attended the
meeting.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
7. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 25th September, 1972, with a view to:
(a) continuing the discussion on the Ministerial Agenda;
(b) examining, to the extent of their availability, any comments which may have
been received from Capitals on Working Papers.
