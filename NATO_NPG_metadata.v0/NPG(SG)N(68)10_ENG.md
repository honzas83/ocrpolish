> # Clarification of Statements made at the NPG PERMREP Meeting, 16 September 1968
> 
> This document consists of a note by the Secretary and a letter from the Deputy Chief of Staff, Plans and Policy Division, SHAPE, to the Chairman of the NPG Staff Group. The letter clarifies the SHAPE position on force-oriented matters regarding aircraft in Allied Command Europe (ACE), specifically addressing the interpretation of the '72 ONWARDS' study. SHAPE explains that the reduction of aircraft assigned to the General Strike Plan (GSP) to 234 aircraft was intended to make more aircraft available for conventional operations, while requiring that these aircraft be dual-capable to augment nuclear strike capabilities if necessary. Additionally, the proposal called for 21 additional fighter-bomber squadrons for close support and air superiority, and 782 dual-capable aircraft for the Military Committee's nuclear On-Call Plan. SHAPE urges Belgium, Canada, Germany, Italy, Greece, and the Netherlands to accelerate the conventional capability of their strike squadrons through aircraft modification and increased training. The letter also mentions ongoing studies regarding the relative capabilities of NATO and Warsaw Pact forces, as well as the survivability of SAECUR's nuclear strike and conventional attack capabilities. Finally, SHAPE emphasizes that force planning issues should be addressed through the formal NATO force planning process involving the MODE and Major and Subordinate Commanders.

SUBJECT: Clarification of Statements made at the NPG PERMREP Meeting, 16
September 1968.

TO: The Chairman
NPG Staff Group
1. Some of the questions raised during the PERMREP meeting on the Canadian
fighter-bomber paper ranged into a discussion of important issues concerning
forces and the role of fighter-bombers in ACE. The purpose of this letter is to
clarify the SHAPE position on these issues which, as you know, were the subject
of intensive examination last year at SHAPE. The SHAPE Special Study, "72
ONWARDS", presented SHAPE'S views on most of the force-oriented and related
matters regarding aircraft in ACE.

2. The Canadian paper interpreted that, in "72 ONWARDS", SHAPE suggested a
reduction in aircraft assigned exclusively for nuclear strike missions. The
SHAPE proposal did in fact reduce to 234, the aircraft assigned specifically to
participate in the General Strike Plan (GSP). The trends of the discussion by
PERMREP, though correct in their interpretation regarding the reduction of
aircraft assigned an exclusive nuclear role, failed to take into account other
accompanying, qualifying and equally important proposals in this regard:

First, the reduction to 234 aircraft for the GSP was for the purpose of making
more aircraft available initially for conventional operations. Aircraft made
available by this proposal were estimated to be on the order of 800 - 1000. The
proposal also included the requirement that aircraft be dual capable to augment
the 234 all-weather GSP nuclear strike aircraft, in the event the battle
escalates to a nuclear phase.

Secondly, "72 ONWARDS" proposed a requirement for 21 additional fighter-bomber
squadrons. The additional squadrons are essential if the dual missions of close
support and air superiority are to be attained. In this regard, the Study
recognized, that with the inefficiency of ground forces, tactical air forces,
because of their inherent flexibility, provided the best means of providing
quick and heavy firepower to support the land battle.

Third, in addition to the 234 all-weather aircraft sarmarked for the GSP role,
there is the requirement, also spelled out in "72 ONWARDS", for 782 dual-capable
aircraft for the MSC's nuclear On-Call Plan.

3. Among other relevant issues discussed in "72 ONWARDS", and by the PERMREPS,
is the important matter of dual capable aircraft. Of major significance are
certain measures which SHAPE has recommended to nations and to the Military
Committee. In a recent memorandum (SHAPE 2110/20) to the Military Committee on
19 September, SHAPE strongly urged Belgium, Canada, Germany, Italy, Greece and
the Netherlands to accelerate achievement of the conventional capability of
strike squadrons. SHAPE stated that this capability could be achieved by
expediting aircraft modification, increasing conventional flying hours and
procuring training and war reserve ordnance.

4. Currently, there are significant "post 72 ONWARDS" studies in preparation,
which deal with relevant issues on force posture and capabilities. It may be
useful for the NPG to review existing studies and await the outcome of the
studies under preparation so that requirements are not added which have already
been covered. In the majority of completed studies the Military Commanders’
views have been fully expressed, and are well-known.

5. Of potential impact on the assessment of mix of tactical air forces is the
current study under the guidance of the DFC concerning the "Relative
Capabilities of NATO and Warsaw Pact Forces". As you are also no doubt aware
SHAPE has under preparation a Study for the Military Committee concerning the
survivability of SAECUR'S nuclear strike and conventional attack capability.
Important sub-studies from nations are under preparation in support of this
study. These latter studies should also bear heavily on the subject at hand.

6. In the formal NATO force planning process involving the MODE and the Major
and Subordinate Commanders, issues concerning forces are addressed and decided
in forums other than the NPG. SHAPE believes this formal system offers the best
means for addressing such issues.

7. As practicable, and to prevent possible misunderstanding, SAECUR'S stated
views may be helpful to the various delegations in the NPG. No significant
changes have occurred since the SHAPE Special Study was distributed which would
change the general proposals included therein, although the comprehensive
studies referred to above will undoubtedly be helpful in the further
clarification of the complex issues at hand.

SHAPE P & P Division (Signed) H. L. HARTY
