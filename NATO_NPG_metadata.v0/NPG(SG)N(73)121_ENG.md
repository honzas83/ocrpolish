> # Preparations for the Spring 1974 NPG Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This document, a notice from the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, details the preparations for the Spring 1974 NPG Ministerial meeting tentatively scheduled for June 4th and 5th in Norway. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, proposes several potential agenda topics for the Staff Group to discuss, including briefings on the balance of strategic forces and US research and development regarding tactical nuclear weapons. Other proposed subjects include an illustrative study on the tactical use of nuclear air defence weapons in a land environment, Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy and military doctrine, and the function of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation process. The document notes that some of these topics are contingent on external inputs and suggests alternative agenda items such as the general political aspects of early combined use and communicating NATO's intentions. Additionally, the document includes a detailed planning timetable from November 1973 to April 1974, outlining weekly Staff Group meetings, the NDAC meeting, a DRC workshop, and the HILEX 74 exercise, alongside deadlines for the distribution of various reports and studies.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

PREPARATIONS FOR THE SPRING 1974 NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING

Note by the Chairman

To facilitate further Staff Group discussion of the steps that may be undertaken
in preparing for the next NPG Ministerial meeting in Norway, now tentatively
scheduled for 4th and 5th June, 1974, the following paragraphs comment on
possible agenda subjects and some timing considerations. It is suggested that
the Staff Group discuss these matters at its next meeting on Monday, 26th
November.

Ministerial Agenda Topics

2. Although it would be premature to attempt to make firm decisions at this time
on the subjects to be included on the agenda for the next NPG Ministerial
meeting, it would be useful, for the purposes of organising the Staff Group
work, to review the prospects. On the basis of current information, the
following subjects appear to be reasonable possibilities:

(a) briefing on the balance of strategic forces;
(b) briefing on US research and development with respect to tactical nuclear
weapons;

(c) illustrative study;
(d) Warsaw Pact Politico-military strategy and military doctrine;
(e) function of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation process.

This document consists of 3 pages, and an Annex of 2 pages.

3. Briefing on Balance of Strategic Forces. This briefing, which has become a
regular feature of Ministerial meetings, not only provides information for the
Ministers on developments and future prospects in the strategic nuclear field
but also serves as a background for the discussion of other basic nuclear
defence issues of common concern. For these reasons, it is assumed that the NPG
would welcome a US offer to brief the Ministers on this topic at the next
meeting. This topic does not normally require any preparatory work on the part
of the NPG Permanent Representatives or Staff Group.

4. Briefing on US Research and Development with respect to Tactical Nuclear
Weapons. At their last meeting, the Ministers accepted a US offer to provide a
briefing on this subject(1). Although the offering of weapon improvement has
been mentioned in various NPG papers in the past(2), the topic has not been
actively considered. The Ministers, in contrast to the situation that prevails
with respect to the strategic briefing, have not developed a frame of reference
within which a briefing on tactical nuclear weapons can be understood and
discussed. The Staff Group may wish to explore ways in which this problem might
be alleviated.

5. Illustrative Study. The Chairman of the Military Committee has been
requested(3) to arrange for the preparation of an illustrative study dealing
with tactical use of nuclear air defence weapons in a land environment, to be
made available to the Secretary General by 1st February, 1974 for distribution
to NPG countries. It is anticipated that soon after the distribution of the
study the Staff Group would begin drafting a Permanent Representatives' report
setting forth some specific aspects for possible Ministerial discussion.

6. Warsaw Pact Politico-military Strategy and Military Doctrine. The ad hoc
study group has indicated that it hopes to complete at least a preliminary
report in time for Ministerial consideration at the Spring 1974 meeting. When
the report is received, the Staff Group would have to consider such questions as
whether it should be placed on the Ministerial agenda and whether an
accompanying Permanent Representatives' report should be prepared. At the
moment, there is no clear forecast of when

7. **Function of the Military Committee in the Nuclear Consultation Process.**
This topic is a potential agenda item for the next Ministerial meeting, provided
the Permanent Representatives' report can be prepared in time. Discussions of
its substance could begin soon in the Staff Group, but drafting of the report
itself probably cannot be effectively undertaken until the Military Committee
report on this subject is received, possibly in January.

8. **Potential Alternatives.** The agenda possibilities mentioned in paragraphs
5, 6 and 7 above are contingent on inputs from other sources. Two of them have
no specific availability dates and, for the moment, have to be viewed as
questionable candidates. Furthermore, each of the agenda possibilities mentioned
in paragraphs 3, 4 and 5 is quite likely to include some type of presentation
which might argue against including all of them on a single agenda. In view of
the foregoing, the Staff Group may wish to discuss some other potential agenda
topics whose preparation could be started as insurance. In this regard, the
Staff Group might give consideration to developing reports on the general
political aspects of early combined use, and on communicating NATO's intentions,
either or both of which might be completed for the next Ministerial meeting if
they are prepared as Permanent Representatives' reports.

**Timing Considerations**

9. At annex is a planning timetable for preparing for the next Ministerial
meeting, based on the assumption that the tentative dates of 4th and 5th June,
1974 will be confirmed. If these dates are changed, the planned timing will need
to be reviewed. The timetable attempts to take into account the processing and
delivery times that would be required to transmit documents to capitals and,
where appropriate, to permit national authorities to review them and send
instructions to delegations. To the extent possible, the timetable also reflects
other activities that will affect meeting preparations. The possible impact of
the Spring Council and DPC meetings has not been shown, since the dates for
those meetings have not yet been firmly established.
