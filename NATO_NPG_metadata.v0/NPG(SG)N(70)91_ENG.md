> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Tuesday, 24th November, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on November 24, 1970, to address several key items. The group took note of draft communiqué paragraphs for the upcoming Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) meeting on December 2, 1970, and agreed on amendments to be forwarded to the ASG Defence Plans and Policy. The group also reviewed the agenda for the NDAC meeting and confirmed that all necessary documents had been issued. Additionally, the group discussed amendments to the concept of the role of theatre nuclear strike forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE), and noted a statement regarding the provision of note-takers for military observers at ministerial meetings. The United Kingdom Representative and the International Military Staff Representative suggested that the Staff Group participate informally in the nuclear aspects of Exercise WINTEX 1971, which was scheduled for January 27 to February 4, 1971. The group agreed that such participation would be valuable for studying sample requests and invited the International Military Staff to prepare a paper on the matter. Finally, the group noted arrangements for a farewell luncheon for Mr. Gordon Smith, the Canadian Representative, and scheduled its next meeting for December 7, 1970, in Brussels.

AMENDMENTS TO THE CONCEPT OF THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR
STRIKE FORCES IN ACE(1)

4. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled that if no objections or
suggestions had been received by the close of business next day (25th November)
the changes to the concept paper(1) proposed in PO/70/542 of 18th November,
1970, would be considered as approved and would be circulated.

PROVISION OF NOTE-TAKERS FOR MILITARY OBSERVERS ATTENDING
NPG AND NDAC MINISTERIAL MEETINGS

5. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who drew the attention of the Staff Group to
the paper(2) on the subject of note-takers, and expressed the hope that this
matter could be examined early in the New Year.

STAFF GROUP PARTICIPATION IN EXERCISE WINTEX 1971

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a suggestion by the United Kingdom Representative, supported by the
International Military Staff Representative, that the Staff Group might wish to
take part in an informal manner, in the nuclear aspects of EXERCISE WINTEX 1971,
taking place 27th January – 4th February;

(b) noted that such participation would not imply nor require any reflection of
national views or reference to national authorities;

(c) agreed that participation in such an exercise could be of value to the Staff
Group, particularly with regard to the study of sample requests;

(d) invited the International Military Staff to prepare a paper on the matter in
time for discussion by the Staff Group at its next meeting.
