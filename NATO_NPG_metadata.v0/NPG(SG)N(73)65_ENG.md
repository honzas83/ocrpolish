> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 16th July, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on July 16, 1973, to address several key strategic and administrative items. The group took note of a statement regarding a US-USSR Agreement on the prevention of nuclear war, including a White House Fact Sheet and remarks by Dr. Kissinger. The meeting also focused on scheduling future meetings for NPG Permanent Representatives in preparation for the Autumn NPG Ministerial meeting, confirming a meeting for October 4th. Discussions were held on a revised draft report concerning current NATO operational concepts and doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear weapons, with a need for further advice from SACLAN and CINCHAN. The group also considered the potential need to re-examine NATO concepts and doctrine for tactical nuclear weapons in the maritime environment due to Soviet posture changes. Additionally, the group discussed consultation procedures and facilities, noting the connection between a report on the participation of NATO Headquarters in WINTEX 73 and a draft report on consultation procedures. The meeting touched upon follow-up issues from the Spring Defence Planning Committee (DPC) Ministerial meeting and reports on Early Combined Use from the Military Committee. Finally, the group noted the progress of the report on the implementation of ADM Guidelines and scheduled its next meeting for July 30, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 16th
July, 1973

DOCUMENTATION

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to an NPG Staff
Group Notice(1) concerning the recent US-USSR Agreement on prevention of nuclear
war; the Notice contained three annexes:

Annex A: text of the Agreement;
Annex B: a Fact Sheet issued by the White House; and
Annex C: the transcript of Dr. Kissinger's remarks to the press.

FUTURE MEETINGS OF THE NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to an NPG Staff Group
Notice(2) regarding dates for NPG Permanent Representatives meetings in
preparation for the Autumn NPG Ministerial meeting, and made the following
points:

(i) Thursday, 4th October appeared to be a suitable and acceptable date on which
NPG Permanent Representatives could examine and take final action on their
reports to Ministers;

(ii) after the Notice was circulated, information had been received that other
commitments made Thursday, 25th October unsuitable for the Secretary General and
possibly some Permanent Representatives as a date for final exchange of views on
Ministerial Agenda items;

OPERATIONAL CONCEPTS AND DOCTRINE
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced a new version(1),
revised in the light of the Staff Group's discussion at its last meeting, of the
draft report on current NATO operational concepts and doctrine concerning the
tactical use of nuclear weapons, and a revised paragraph 4(2); he drew attention
to the various changes made by the Staff Group, and said that the scope of the
report should not be limited to ACE only; in this respect he stressed that the
wording of paragraph 4 was, as yet, incomplete and that further advice from
SACLAN and CINCHAN was required;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the subsequent discussion and agreed to
a number of changes to the text;

(c) invited the International Staff, in the light of the discussion, to prepare
a further revised version and to circulate it in time for discussion on 30th
July;

(d) expressed the hope that the representative of SACLAN would be able to obtain
advice in time for it to be incorporated into the new draft;

(1) NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)8(Revised), 11th July, 1973
(2) NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)8(Revised), Corrigendum, 13th July, 1973

(e) expressed the hope that after the next discussion it would be possible to
circulate the draft report in the form of an NPG Working Paper, with a view to
its examination in Capitals prior to final action by Permanent Representatives
on 4th October;

(f) took note of a further statement by the Chairman who said that the Assistant

it advisable to re-examine NATO concepts and doctrine pertaining to tactical

wish to give some thought to this question and to continue its discussion at a
future meeting.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the revised version
of the Staff Group Working Paper(1) containing a draft of a possible Permanent
Representatives report on some selected aspects of consultation procedures and
facilities would be circulated in the near future, with a view to its discussion
at the Staff Group meeting on 30th July;

(b) expressed the hope that it would be possible to finalise this report, as far
as its nature permitted, at the next meeting;

(c) noted that the DPC, at its meeting on 12th July, had examined the report(2)
by the Council Operations and Exercise Committee on participation of the NATO
Headquarters in WINTEX 73 and had approved the recommendations contained
therein; noted the connection between this report and the draft report presently
being prepared by the Staff Group on Consultation Procedures and Facilities;
agreed that certain points in the COEC report should be reflected in the NPG
report.

FOLLOW-UP TO SPRING DPC MINISTERIAL MEETING
5. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Defence Planning
Committee Permanent Representatives, at their meeting on 12th July, had examined
the PO(1) entitled "Follow-up to the meeting of the DPC in Ministerial Session
held on 7th June, 1973"; the Permanent Representatives would again be addressing
basic follow-up issues in the light of a paper awaited from the United States,
and in due course the NPG would receive guidance on its tasks; he felt there was
a possibility that the DPC might require a paper from the NPC in time for the
December 1973 Ministerial Meeting.

SHAPE AND MILITARY COMMITTEE REPORTS ON EARLY COMBINED USE
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the representative of the International Military Staff
who said that the reports on Early Combined Use would be examined by the
Military Committee next week, and would then be made available to all concerned;

(b) expressed the hope that it would be possible for the Staff Group to hold an
initial discussion on these reports at its meeting on 30th July.

IMPLEMENTATION OF ADM GUIDELINES
7. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the IMS representative who said that, hopefully, a
final version of the report on the implementation of ADM Guidelines would be
available for circulation by 1st August.
