> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 31st May, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on May 31, 1976, to address several administrative and strategic items. The group took note of security clearances for all attendees and reviewed documentation, including a draft of the MIT report and a revised Working Paper. Discussions focused on Nuclear Consultation - CNI Procedures, where the group agreed to an amendment proposed by Turkish authorities and confirmed that these procedures do not amend the Athens guidelines. The United Kingdom representative suggested testing these procedures in Exercise WINTEX 77. Additionally, the group reviewed the NPG Future Work Programme and its annexes, noting that the United States representative provided updates on Phase III Progress Reports and the Technological Study. The meeting concluded with the decision that a meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives was not necessary for June 2, 1976, and the group scheduled its next meeting for June 8, 1976.

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared to the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

DOCUMENTATION
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to papers which had been
or were about to be circulated; in particular he mentioned:

(a) the latest draft of Volume I of the MIT report which had been distributed by
the United Kingdom Delegation;

(b) the revised version of the Working Paper(1) which would be circulated next
day;

(c) NPG/D(76)4 (Transmission of Preliminary National Views on Improving the
Effectiveness of NATO's Theatre Nuclear Forces) would be circulated as soon as
national views became available;

NUCLEAR CONSULTATION - CNI PROCEDURES
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that the Turkish authorities had proposed an amendment(1) to
NPG/WP(76)1, 28th April, 1976;

(b) agreed to this amendment and invited the International Staff to circulate
the draft report on Nuclear Consultation - CNI Procedures in the form of an NPG
document;

(c) agreed that the CNI Consultation paper in no way amended the Athens
guidelines;

(d) further noted that although this paper was not addressed to Ministers, it
would doubtless reach the Defence Planning Committee through the paper on
Compilation;

(e) noted the views of the United Kingdom representative who hoped that CNI
Procedures could be tested in Exercise WINTEX 77 and that some Permanent
Representatives would participate in that part of the Exercise where these
procedures would be tried.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman concerning the NPG Future Work
Programme(1) and to the Annexes to be attached to it;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and agreed to a
number of editorial changes;
