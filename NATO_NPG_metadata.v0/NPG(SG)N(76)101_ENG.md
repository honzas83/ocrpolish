> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Tuesday, 2nd November, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on November 2, 1976, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group discussed a revised presentation on New and Refined Procedures in the Field of Nuclear Consultation, including feedback from the Assistant Secretary General for Defence Planning and Policy and a statement of disappointment from the Turkish representative. The meeting also touched upon the possible revision of the Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE), noting a general consensus that no modifications should be made until further progress is made with Phase III. Preparations for the upcoming NPG Ministers meeting in London on November 17th and 18th were reviewed, including the agreement on the Advance Press Release, the Conference Handbook, and the initial drafts of the Agreed Minute and Final Communiqué. Additionally, the group discussed potential dates for the NPC Spring Ministerial Meeting in Canada, with representatives from the Netherlands and Italy expressing suitability issues. Finally, the group invited representatives from the United Kingdom and the United States to explore the possibility of visits to nuclear installations, and agreed to hold no further meetings until after the London Ministerial Meeting.

SECURITY

Nuclear Consultation.

NUCLEAR CONSULTATION

(a) took note of a presentation given by Mr. J. Mentzel,
Nuclear Planning Directorate, and Cdr. R. Devies
from SHAPE; this presentation was a shortened
version of that given to Permanent Representatives
on 22nd October, 1976;

(b) took note, at the end of the briefing, of the comments by the Assistant
Secretary General for DP&F who recalled that 'the NPC Permanent Representatives
had invited the staffs to shorten the briefing and at the same time to eliminate
those parts of the scenario which might tend to sidetrack the Ministerial

(c) took note of the further remarks of the ASC DP&F who advised that the
opening remarks by the Secretary General in his brief should emphasise that the
scenario should be regarded simply as a synthetic framework upon which to base
the presentation;

(d) noted the further remarks by the ASC DP&F who underlined the need to prepare
Permanent Representatives, and through them their Ministers, and advised that
use should be made of the folder of Message Examples(1); however, he hoped to
avoid another meeting of Permanent Representatives before the Ministerial
Meeting;

(e) took note of a statement by the Turkish representative who expressed his
disappointment in the new version of the presentation; he considered that the
original presentation had been excellent and that so much had been cut out that
the revised version was of little value;

(f) took note, during the ensuing discussion, of the suggestions made,
particularly with regard to:

(i) the need to mention the VINTEX 77 scenario;

(ii) the need to emphasise that the scenario was not a real life situation;

(iii) the desirability of making available the Message Examples;

(iv) the need to prepare Permanent Representatives most carefully for such
presentations;

POSSIBLE REVISION OF THE CONCEPT FOR THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES
IN ACE

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who asked if any representative had
received instructions on the question of amending the Concept;

(b) noted that there was a general feeling that amendments of facts and figures
might be made but that no action should be taken on modifying the Concept until
further progress had been made with Phase III;

(c) agreed to re-open the discussion at some later date when more members had
received instructions.

MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS IN LONDON ON 17TH AND 16TH NOVEMBER 1976
Advance Press Release
5. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that the content of the Advance Press Release had been agreed by all
concerned and that the time and date for its release (1 p.m. Brussels time on
12th November) would be observed in Capitals of most of the countries taking
part.

Conference handbook
5. The STAFF GROUP:
noted the remarks of the UK representative who said that he hoped to issue a
final version of the Conference Handbook during the week.

Initial Tentative Drafts of the Agreed Minute and the Final Communiqué.

7. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that initial drafts would be available on or about 10th November.

Revised:PO-on Work Programme

8. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that the new PO on the Future Work Programme would be circulated during
the next day or two.

DATES OF THE NPC SPRING MINISTERIAL MEETING

9. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a request by the Canadian representative who asked for views on
the 25th May as a possible date for the Spring Meeting in Canada;

(b) noted that 25th May was unsuitable for the Netherlands, as was the period
immediately prior to 2nd June for the Italians;

(c) took note of a number of suggestions and agreed to inform the Canadian
representative of possible dates, with a view to re-opening the discussion on
this matter.

VISITS BY THE STAFF GROUP TO NUCLEAR INSTALLATIONS

10. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a suggestion by the Danish representative that the Staff Group
should visit US and UK nuclear installations;

(b) invited the representatives of the UK and the US to check with their
Authorities whether such visits could be arranged.

NEXT MEETING

11. The STAFF GROUP: agreed to hold no further meetings until after the
Ministerial Meeting in London on 17th and 18th November.

NATO, 1110 Brussels.
