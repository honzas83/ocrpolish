> # Follow-On Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons
> 
> This working paper, authored by Chairman R.G. Shreffler, outlines the need for a draft report to Ministers concerning the terms of reference for exploring the implications and interactions of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons in the defense of NATO. The document specifies that the draft should address the scope of the project, including general objectives and assumptions, as well as an overall outline of substantive work and proposed arrangements for initial studies. An attached annex provides draft terms of reference, which include objectives such as exploring military and non-military implications, developing insights on tactical nuclear engagement, and determining the extent to which guidelines should be elaborated. The assumptions underlying these studies are based on existing and planned forces of both NATO and the Warsaw Pact, the occurrence of initial nuclear use, and the adherence of NATO operations to approved guidelines and strategy. The Chairman invites suggestions for the substantive work and first series of studies to be discussed at a meeting on September 15, 1969.

WORKING PAPER
NPG(Staff Group)II(69)64

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
Note by the Chairman

1. A pressing task now facing the Staff group is to put together a draft report
to the Ministers on terms of reference: for exploring in more detail the
implications and interactions of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons in
defence of NATO, as well as NATO responses to Warsaw Pact initial use (1).

2. This draft might address the following points :
(a) A discussion of the scope of the project : its general objectives and
assumptions;

(b) An overall outline of substantive work; specific studies will be drawn from
this outline;

(c) Proposed arrangements for the first studies : specific studies,
responsibilities, and schedule.

3. Suggested objectives and assumptions (subpara 2(n) above) are outlined at
Annex. It is proposed to have an initial discussion of the general approach as
well as of the objectives and assumptions at our meeting on sunday, 15th
September.

4. Meanwhile, suggestions for the overall outline of substantive work and for
the first series of studies (subparas 2(b) and (c) above) are invited. A first
draft will be circulated in due course.

(Signed) R.G. SHREFFLER

(1) NPG/D(69)5, para 10.

ANNEX to NPG(Staff Group)N(69)64
DRAFT TER'S OF REFERENCE FOR EXPLORING
THE FOLLOW-ON USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
I. OBJECTIVES
1. To explore and spell out the military and non-military implications and
interactions of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons;

2. To develop clearer insights on how the tactical nuclear engagement should be
carried out, and to make recommendations, if appropriate;

3. To determine whether and to what extent guidelines might be elaborated;
4. To develop a better capability within NATO countries to analyse complex
nuclear problems in greater depth.

II. ASSUMPTIONS
1. Existing and planned forces (both NATO and Warsaw Pact);
2. Initial use has occurred (either or both sides);
3. NATO operations will be governed by approved guidelines and strategy.
