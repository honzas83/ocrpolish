> # Preparations for the Autumn 1972 Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) detailing the preparatory work required for the Ministerial meeting scheduled for October 26th and 27th, 1972, in the United Kingdom. The Chairman identifies potential agenda topics, including the ACLANT Illustrative Study, Follow-on Use Studies (specifically the Denmark and Transition Studies), and a briefing on the balance of strategic forces. Other possible topics include updating approved nuclear policy documents regarding the POSEIDON missile system, the status of implementation of the Special ADM Guidelines, consultation procedures and facilities, and unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence. The note emphasizes the importance of timing, specifying that all agenda documents should reach capitals by October 16th, and that the Permanent Representatives should approve final reports during the late September to early October period. It further outlines a Staff Group schedule for weekly meetings through early July and recommencing in August to finalize Working Papers and the developed Ministerial agenda. The document also mentions the coordination of input from the NATO Military Authorities and the Military Committee, the role of the United States in providing briefings on strategic forces and policy updates, and the specific request from Italy for a presentation on the Transition Study.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
PREPARATIONS FOR THE AUTUMN 1972
MINISTERIAL MEETING
Note by the Chairman
The following paragraphs discuss briefly the principal tasks that, in my view,
the Staff Group may have to undertake in preparing for the Ministerial meeting
to be held in the United Kingdom on 26th and 27th October, 1972. Some timing
considerations are also mentioned. It is intended that the Staff Group discuss
these matters at its next meeting.

Ministerial Agenda Topics
2. Although it is no doubt too soon to make any specific judgments about what
items will be on the Ministerial agenda for the next meeting, it would be
useful, in terms of organizing the Staff Group approach to the meeting
preparation task, to review the possibilities. Topics with a relatively high
probability of being on the agenda would appear to include:

(a) the ACLANT Illustrative Study;
(b) one or two Follow-on Use Studies;
(c) briefing on the balance of strategic forces.
3. Certain other topics might also have to be given consideration in developing
the Ministerial agenda, such as:

(b) status of implementation of the Special ADM Guidelines;

4. The decision on whether a particular topic should be included on the next
Ministerial agenda would be governed by such factors as the importance of the
topic from a Minister’s point of view, its relative priority or urgency, its
relationship to the rest of the agenda, and the timely availability of the
relevant input studies or reports.

5. ACLANT Illustrative Study. The Ministers at Copenhagen requested that this
Study be prepared by SACANT for their consideration at the next meeting. It is
assumed that a Permanent Representatives’ report identifying possible areas for
discussion will accompany the Study. It is also possible that comments on the
Study will be received from the Military Committee. The Study and any related
Military Committee comments should be available by 25th August and Staff Group
preparation of a draft Permanent Representatives’ report should begin
immediately after receipt, that is, in late August and early September.

6. It is also anticipated that, in conformity with the approach used at recent
Ministerial meetings, a brief presentation will be used to set the stage for
Ministerial discussion of the Study. Development of this presentation cannot be
started until the Study itself becomes available. It may be useful, however, for
the Staff Group to discuss such points as the general scope and length of the
presentation, including the coverage to be given to the political consultation
aspect, in order to provide guidance to those who will be directly involved in
developing the presentation.

7. Follow-on Use Studies. Three studies contained in the original programme are
still outstanding. The Ministers have agreed to consider the ACLANT Study at
their Spring 1973 meeting. The last status report indicated that the other two –
Denmark Study and Transiction Study – would be available for consideration at
the Autumn 1972 Ministerial meeting. If past practice is followed, it can be
expected that the NATO Military Authorities would be invited to comment on each
of these studies, and that a Permanent Representatives’ report identifying
possible areas for Ministerial discussion would

8. Balance of Strategic Forces. This topic has been placed on past agendas at
the initiative of the United States, whose Secretary of Defence normally gives a
briefing to set the stage for Ministerial discussion. It is expected that the
United States will, in good time, advise the NPG of its intentions with respect
to the next meeting. In any event, no preparatory work by the Staff Group is
foreseen.

9. Updating of Policy Documents. The United States has offered to make
suggestions for updating the concept paper for use by the Staff Group in
preparing a draft NPG report containing recommended changes to be approved by
the DPC. The question of whether this report should be placed on the Ministerial
agenda can be addressed later in the context of the factors mentioned in
paragraph 3 above.

10. Special ADM Guidelines. A report from the Military Committee is expected by
1st August. The Staff Group would then be in a position to prepare a draft NPG
report which would either be considered by the Ministers or be handled at
Permanent Representatives' level as the circumstances at the time may suggest.

11. Consultation Procedures and Facilities. The mandate given to the Permanent
Representatives does not go into detail concerning the type of response to the
Ministers or its timing. It is suggested, however, that the Staff Group should
continue its examination of this subject in order to identify the major points
of concern and to develop an approach for dealing with these points. This would
appear to be an essential first step before any decision can be taken on whether
a report should be submitted to the Ministers at their next meeting.

12. Unresolved Problems related to Tactical Nuclear Defence. The Staff Group was
informed by the IMS representatives at its

last meeting that there was some possibility that a report on one
of the three subjects, i.e., the structuring and deployment of
forces might be submitted in the next few months. Depending

Timing Considerations

13. Although it is not possible to work out a firm time schedule at the moment,
there are certain critical time-related

steps that can be identified. For example, in order to permit
capitals to prepare for the Ministerial meeting effectively,
all documents listed on the agenda should be in their hands
at least a week or ten days before the meeting, say 16th October.
When the time needed for document reproduction and the time
required for delivery of documents from Brussels to capitals are
taken into consideration, it is apparent that Permanent
Representatives should give their approval to the final versions
of their reports during the week of 25th September, or the week
of 2nd October at the latest. The Working Papers that would be
the basis for this final Permanent Representatives' action
would, in turn, need to be circulated to capitals in time for
them to prepare instructions for their Ambassadors. Such
Working Papers, prepared by the Staff Group, should reach
capitals around the 15th of September. This would require
the Staff Group to complete its drafting action during the
week of 4th September if at all possible, and certainly no
later than the week of 11th September. From the foregoing,
it is also clear that the input material needed for the Staff
Group drafting action, such as reports, studies and comments,
should be available by mid-August and preferably sooner.

14. For planning purposes, several meetings of the
Permanent Representatives should be foreseen, probably during
the last two weeks of September and first week of October.
Purposes would be to receive presentations on the Follow-on
Use Studies (Denmark, Transition), have a preliminary review of
the ACLANT ILLustrative Study, take action on various draft
Permanent Representatives' reports whose number and nature
cannot be accurately predicted at the moment, and to have
a final exchange of views on agenda items. Obviously, some of
these matters could be handled at the same meeting.

15. As far as Staff Group activity is concerned, it is intended to continue to
hold weekly meetings through

the week of 3rd July (5th, 12th, 19th, 26th June, 3rd July) in
order to accomplish as much as possible on matters that do not
depend on reports, studies and comments from other sources.
These matters might include, for example, consultation procedures
and facilities and possibly
