> [!abstract] 
> # Consultation Procedures - Country Request
> 
> This working paper, prepared by the Permanent Representatives, provides detailed procedures for political consultation within the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO) in the event a member government proposes the use of nuclear weapons. The document responds to a Ministerial request from a meeting in Monterey. It outlines two primary ways a member government may raise the issue of nuclear use: through a politico-military assessment of the actual situation or to redress an unfavourable military situation or achieve political purposes. The procedures emphasize the need for similarity between country requests and the same actions available to Major NATO Commanders (MNCs) to facilitate rapid handling. Politico-military assessments should be sent via AIG 6014 with FLASH precedence and a classification determined by the originator. For specific requests for use, the requesting country should address the message to both political authorities and MNCs simultaneously. Because only MNCs have the authority to implement weapon release decisions, the country request must be integrated into the MNCs Nuclear Weapons Release Procedures (NWRP). The process culminates in a decision by the nuclear powers. The document recommends that the Ministers take note of the the report and approve the suggested procedures for handling country requests for the possible use of nuclear weapons.

I. PURPOSE
1. This document provides procedures for political
consultation within the Alliance in the event a member
government proposes the use of nuclear weapons.
II. BACKGROUND
2. During their discussion of WINTEX 75 at Monterey in
June 1975, NPG Ministers invited(1) the Permanent Representatives
to examine the need for establishing more detailed procedures
for formulating and processing nuclear release requests from
NATO member governments. The possibility that member
governments may recognise a need to forward requests for the
use of nuclear weapons is included in the General Guidelines
for Consultation Procedures(2), in an earlier Permanent
Representatives' report(3), and is implied in the Provisional
Political Guidelines(4). The procedural aspects concerning
such requests are treated only in very general terms in these
documents. Experience gained during WINTEX 75 points to the
need for clearer procedures for country request as an aid to
expediting the consultation process. Furthermore, it appears
desirable to complete the compilation of nuclear consultation
procedures(5) by the insertion of a section dealing with country

III. PROCEDURES

3. A NATO member government may raise the issue of the possibility of using;
nuclear weapons in at least two ways: first, in connection with a politico-
military assessment of the actual situation; second, to redress an unfavourable
NATO military situation or to achieve certain political purposes.

There are certain similarities between these two actions and those available in
the same context to the Major NATO Commanders. Therefore, it appears appropriate
to strive for similarity in the procedures concerning such actions so as to
reduce their volume and facilitate common understanding and rapid handling of
the various related messages.

Assessment

4. Under certain conditions, politico-military assessments prepared by member
governments in time of hostilities could properly contain national views on the
requirement for using nuclear weapons in defence of the Alliance.

These assessments cannot be bound to any rigid format, but they should contain
sufficient information to allow the initiation of specific political
consultation and help the Major NATO Commanders concerned in preparing their
assessment of the national request. The address should be the same as for
similar messages originated by MNCs, that is AIG 6014, which includes all
political and military authorities concerned with the consultation process. The
precedence should be FLASH, while the classification would be determined by the
originator. As in the case of assessments originated by MNCs, the consultation
process should start as soon as a country assessment dealing with the use of
nuclear weapons is received at NATO Headquarters. To assist political
consultation, the MNC(s) concerned should provide, without delay, a military
assessment of the country request to the Council/DFC and capitals.

Request for Use

5. There may be many reasons why a NATO country may wish to activate the
consultation process to deal with a nuclear use request. Two of the more obvious
reasons are: first, the desire to achieve certain political objectives
independently from the on-going military operations; and second, the perceived
national necessity to redress an unfavourable military situation affecting the
territory of the country. In addition to other action the country may take to
inform NATO capitals of its views on such issues, it should send a message to

6. Whatever the purpose which gives rise to the country request (purely
political objectives and/or perceived military necessity), this will in effect
require a decision with regard to the use of nuclear weapons and therefore the
related nuclear consultation process should be put in motion and should
culminate in a decision by the nuclear power(s).

7. According to the NATO organisation and current policy, only MNC(s) have
authority to implement the weapon release decisions and to direct nuclear weapon
use. This is the principal reason for requiring the country submitting the
request to address it simultaneously to the political authorities and to the
MNC(s). The use of AIG 6014 would satisfy such necessity.

5. Since, as stated above, the implementation of the use decision is the
responsibility of the MNCs, it appears advisable to devise a method for
inserting country requests into the machinery of the MNCs Nuclear Weapons
Release Procedures (NWRP) and into the political consultation process without
disrupting the in-being proven arrangements. In our opinion the following
suggestions meet this requirement.

these messages will be in accordance with present procedures for nuclear
consultation messages. The procedures to be implemented after the country
proposal has been materialised in an MNC request are those now in use for the
nuclear consultation process(1).
