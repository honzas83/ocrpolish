> # Visit by the Staff Group to Headquarters Strategic Air Command
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on Monday, June 28, 1971, in Brussels. The group discussed a potential visit to the United States Strategic Air Command Headquarters in Omaha, noting that while the visit would be of great value, it was not currently convenient for the delegations or United States authorities. The group requested the United States Representative to explore the possibility of an invitation at a later date. Additionally, the group addressed the scheduling of the next NPG Ministerial Meeting in Autumn 1971, noting that previous dates in October and November were unsuitable for Germany and Greece. They proposed November 17-18, 1971, as the next possible dates and requested representatives to confirm feasibility with their respective capitals. The meeting also covered procedural arrangements for subsequent stages of follow-on studies, inviting the International Staff to prepare a working paper for circulation by mid-July. Finally, the group noted a statement by the United States Representative regarding the progress of the CENTAG Area Study, which was expected to be submitted by August 1, 1971. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for July 1, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 28th June, 1971

VISIT BY THE STAFF GROUP TO HEADQUARTERS STRATEGIC AIR COMMAND

1. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) recalled the suggestion made at its last meeting that the Staff Group might
accompany the Defence Review Committee on its visit to Monterey and at the same
time might be able to visit United States Strategic Air Command Headquarters at
Omaha;

(b) noted that, after due reflection by those concerned, it did not appear that
this visit by the Staff Group would be convenient either to Delegations or to
the United States Authorities;

(c) agreed, however, that such a visit by the Staff Group would be of great
value and requested the United States Representative to examine the possibility
of an invitation for the Staff Group at a later date;

(d) noted with appreciation, a statement by the German Representative who
indicated that there was a possibility that free air transportation might be
provided by the German Air Force.

DATES OF THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN AUTUMN 1971

2. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) recalled the discussion which had taken place at its last meeting at which
it had been proposed that the next NPG Ministerial Meeting should take place
either on 26th/27th October or on 9th/10th November, 1971;

PROCEDURAL ARRANGEMENTS FOR SUBSEQUENT STAGES OF FOLLOW-ON STUDIES
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussion which
had taken place in the Staff Group at its last meeting on procedural
arrangements and invited the Staff Group to continue its discussion of the
International Staff's paper(1);

(b) took note of the views expressed and of the suggestions made in particular
with reference to the question whether analysis and synthesis should be
undertaken by the Nuclear Planning Group collectively or by a smaller group;

(c) invited the International Staff in the light of the views expressed, to
prepare a NPG working paper with a cover letter signed by the Secretary General,
for immediate circulation, with a view to obtaining instructions from capitals
by the middle of July;

(d) agreed that the working paper should then be re-examined in the Staff Group,
if necessary brought to the attention of the Permanent Representatives, and then
circulated as a NPG document.
