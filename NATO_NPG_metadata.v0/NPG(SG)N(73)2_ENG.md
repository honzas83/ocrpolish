> [!abstract] 
> # Nuclear Documents - Limitation of Distribution
> 
> This document, issued by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) on 8th January 1973, addresses the limitation of distribution for classified nuclear papers. Due to the sensitive nature of these documents, especially during the discussion stage, the Chairman emphasizes the need to keep the number of printed copies distributed to Delegations and Staffs to a reasonable minimum. He urges registries in both NATO and national capitals to avoid requesting more copies than are strictly necessary. The document includes an annex containing a proforma for recipients to indicate their revised minimum requirements for various series of nuclear papers, including NDAC Ministerial, NDAC non-Ministerial, NPG Ministerial, NPG non-Ministerial, NPG/Study, and NPG(Staff Group) papers in both English and French. Recipients are requested to return the completed proforma to the Secretary by 29th January 1973.

NPG(Staff Group)N(73)2

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
NUCLEAR DOCUMENTS - LIMITATION OF DISTRIBUTION

Note by the Chairman

Classified papers on nuclear matters, especially at the discussion stage, are of
a sensitive nature and for this reason it is desirable to keep to a reasonable
minimum the number of copies printed and distributed to Delegations and Staffs.
It is therefore appropriate to review periodically the requirements with regard
to NDAC, NPG and NPG(Staff Group) papers and wherever possible to reduce the
number of copies distributed to addressees. It is important to resist the
temptation for registries, both in NATO and in Capitals, to ask for more copies
than are strictly necessary.

2. Attached at Annex is a proforma showing the present number of copies of
nuclear papers received by you. Please complete the proforma, by indicating your
minimum requirements, and return it to the Secretary not later than 29th
January, 1973.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM
