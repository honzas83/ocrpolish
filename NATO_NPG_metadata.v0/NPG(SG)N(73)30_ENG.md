> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 2nd April, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on April 2, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to review several documents and plan for future activities. The group took note of various working papers and notes regarding illustrative studies on demonstrative use, WINTEX 73, and future NPG activity. Regarding Exercise WINTEX 73, the group agreed to a revised wording proposed by the German representative and requested a corrigendum to NPG/WP(73)5. The Chairman suggested that forward planning for NPG work should extend beyond a six-month cycle to better prepare for Ministerial meetings scheduled for Autumn 1973, Spring 1974, and Autumn 1974. Potential sources for future activities were identified, including unexplored areas of interest from past Ministerial meetings, actions from the forthcoming Ankara meeting, and ongoing projects such as the Follow-on Use Work Programme. The group also noted a general interest in Warsaw Pact nuclear doctrine. Discussions regarding the agenda for the Ministerial meeting in Ankara revealed that a military paper on operational concepts and doctrine would not be ready in time, leading to a consensus in favor of including the Illustrative Study on Demonstrative Use. The SHAPE representative noted that while a formal presentation was precluded by workload, SACEUR might provide a short briefing. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next Staff Group meeting on April 9, 1973.

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 2nd

DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the following
documents which had been, or were about to be circulated:

(a) NPG/WP(73)4, 29th March, 1973: Illustrative Study – Demonstrative Use;
(b) NPG/WP(73)5, 30th March, 1973: Preliminary observations on WINTEX 73;
(c) NPG(Staff Group)WP(73)5(Revised), 30th March, 1973: Draft advance press
release circulated to the eight participating countries with a request that
comments and approval be received by 26th April, 1973;

(d) NPG(Staff Group)N(73)28, 29th March, 1973: Future NPG activity;
(e) NPG(Staff Group)N(73)29, 30th March, 1973: Dates of future meetings of NPG
Permanent Representatives and of the NPG Staff Group.

FUTURE NPG ACTIVITY

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman, who referred to his note(2) on
future NPG activity and suggested that the Staff Group might wish to discuss
what steps should be taken to ensure the orderly development of NPG work in
relation to the forthcoming NPG Ministerial meetings to be held in the Autumn
1973, the Spring 1974, and the Autumn 1974; he suggested that forward planning
should not be limited to a six-month cycle but should look forward over a much
longer period; he said that future NPG activities would come from the following
sources:

(i) areas of interest indicated by Ministers at past meetings, but as yet not
explored;

(ii) actions that might flow from the forthcoming Ankara meeting;
(iii) work in connection with on-going projects (Follow-on Use Work Programme,
unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence, implementation of
political guidance, etc.);

(iv) further areas for future investigation by the NPG (new topics, subjects
related to on-going work or recent observations);

the Chairman suggested a number of matters for possible action, and asked for
comments;

AGENDA FOR MINISTERIAL MEETING IN ANKARA
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that it was now apparent that the military paper on operational
concepts and doctrine would not be ready in time to be included in the agenda
for Ankara; noted a growing consensus in favour of including the subject of the
Illustrative Study on Demonstrative Use;

(b) took note of the remarks of the SHAPE representative who said that the
workload in SHAPE precluded a formal presentation, but that SACEUR might be able
to give a short briefing without graphics;

(c) agreed to decide finally upon the matter at the next meeting of the Staff
Group.

NEXT MEETINGS OF THE STAFF GROUP
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 9th April, 1973, at 11 a.m.
(b) agreed that it might be necessary to hold another meeting of the Staff Group
later next week.
