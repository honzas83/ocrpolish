> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 11th October, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on October 11, 1976, at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels, Belgium. The meeting covered several key points, including the security clearance of all present members and a welcome to Major P.A. Lauritz and Captain A. Hansen from Denmark. The Chairman summarized the current work situation, noting that the International Staff Nuclear Planning Directorate had briefed SHAPE on new and refined procedures. Other items discussed included the draft PO on Future Work, the classification of Nuclear-Consultation Messages, and a paper on Compilation. The group also noted a proposal for a one-day course on Crisis Management. The Chairman recalled the Nuclear Planning Committee Permanent Representatives Work Programme for October and November, including aられる a Phase III Progress report and a Ministerial meeting in London on November 17-18. The group noted that the progress of the Ministerial agenda was temporarily delayed due to a lack of instructions from Washington. The meeting also addressed the advance press release and administrative arrangements for the London meeting, with the UK representative requesting delegation lists. Finally, the group agreed not to hold its next meeting on October 18, and to meet again as required.

SECURITY

WELCOME

Captain A. Hansen (Danish Defence Intelligence Service), both on temporary duty
at SHAPE.

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who summarised the current NPG work
situation in the NATO Headquarters, drawing attention to the following points:

(i) members of the International Staff Nuclear Planning Directorate had been to
SHAPE on 6th October to run through the briefing for the Presentation of new and
refined Procedures;
