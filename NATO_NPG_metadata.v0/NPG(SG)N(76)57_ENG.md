> # Follow-Up Action to the Ministerial Meeting at NATO Headquarters, Brussels on 14th and 15th June 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO headquarters in Brussels on Monday, June 28, 1976. The primary focus was the follow-up action to the Ministerial Meeting held on June 14 and 15, 1976. The group noted a statement by the Chairman regarding the basic paper NPG(Staff Group)N(76)5, which outlined objectives and priorities for follow-up work, including studies on NIKE HERCULES sites and dual-capable aircraft. The Staff Group examined and amended the basic paper, requesting the International Staff to publish the agreed text as a Nuclear Planning Group Working Paper for consideration by Permanent Representatives on July 7 or 14. Additionally, the group agreed to consider the Addendum to the basic paper at the earliest opportunity and noted that the NPG Military Representatives (MILREPs) could accept paragraph 2 of both Annexes to the Addendum. The meeting also noted that the Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE) was prepared to provide an informational briefing on proposed changes to nuclear weapons release procedures, provisionally scheduled for July 12, 1976. Finally, the the Staff Group agreed to schedule its next meeting for Tuesday, July 5, 1976.

FOLLOW-UP ACTION TO THE MINISTERIAL MEETING AT NATO HEADQUARTERS, BRUSSELS ON
14TH AND 15TH JUNE 1976

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman that the basic paper, NPG(Staff
Group)N(76)5, dated 23rd June, 1976, outlined the objectives and priorities for
follow-up work and that the broad terms of reference for the studies on NIKE
HERCULES sites and dual-capable aircraft were contained in Annexes A and B
respectively to the Addendum to the basic paper which had been circulated on
2/4th June, 1976;

(b) examined and amended the basic paper and requested the International Staff
to publish the agreed text as a NPG Working Paper(1) for consideration at a
meeting of Permanent Representatives to be held on either 7th or 14th July;

(c) agreed to consider the Addendum to the basic paper at the earliest possible
opportunity;

(d) noted a statement by the IMS Representative that the NPG MILREPs could
accept paragraph 2 of both Annexes to the Addendum.

(1) NPG/P(76)3, dated 29th June, 1976
