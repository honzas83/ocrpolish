> # Follow-Up Action to the Ministerial Meeting in Copenhagen on 18th and 19th May, 1972
> 
> This document outlines the necessary follow-up actions to be implemented following the decisions made by the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministers in Copenhagen on May 18-19, 1972. It details the progress of the Follow-on Use Work Programme, noting that Phase II is to be started and that the United States, Germany, and the United Kingdom are to organize the study team. It mentions the need for comments on Phase I studies and the preliminary assessments for an Air Battle Study. It also notes that the ACLANT Follow-on Use Study will be considered in the Spring of 1973. Furthermore, it requests the Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic (SACLANT) the preparation of an Illustrative Study on the initial use of nuclear weapons at sea in the ACLANT area. It addresses consultation procedures and facilities for the use of nuclear weapons, and lists the specific basic documentation to be distributed to Portugal. It concludes by stating that the intention is to circulate a Staff Group Notice regarding preparations for the Autumn 1972 Ministerial meeting.
>

These are core elements of tactical nuclear sharing and consultation on nuclear use in Europe.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
FOLLOW-UP ACTION TO THE MINISTERIAL MEETING IN COPENHAGEN ON 18TH AND 19TH MAY,
1972

Note by the Chairman
The following paragraphs comment on actions that might be required to implement
the decisions taken by the NPG Ministers in Copenhagen on 18th and 19th May, as
reflected in the Agreed Minute(1). It is proposed that the Staff Group review
these actions at its next meeting on Tuesday, 30th May.

1. FOLLOW-ON USE WORK PROGRAMME
2. The Ministers invited NDAC countries to contribute comments on the Turkish
and Mediterranean Maritime Follow-on Use Studies for use in later phases of the
work programme (paragraph 5 of NPG/D(72)7). Since all NDAC countries have
received the Copenhagen Agreed Minute, no further action is required.

3. The Ministers agreed that Phase II of the work programme should be started
now, and approved the approach and method for carrying out Phase II proposed by
the Permanent Representatives (paragraphs 6 and 7 of NPG/D(72)7). At the
meeting, Secretary Laird indicated that the United States, as leader of the ad
hoc study team, was prepared to meet in the near future with the two other
members, Germany and the United Kingdom, to organize the study team work. This
document consists of 4 pages.

4. In connection with the Phase II effort, the Ministers urged all concerned to
submit comments on Phase I studies and any material relevant to Phase II as
expediently as possible (paragraph 9 of NPG/D(72)7). In this regard, it might be
noted that no country has date circulated any comments on any of the Phase I
studies. The NATO Military Authorities were requested to submit comments on the
studies and have done so. It might be observed parenthetically that no official
records are available for study team use that would reflect the comments on the
studies made at various meetings by Ministers or Permanent Representatives.
Countries concerned may wish to consider circulating copies of part or all of
these comments, so that they are readily available to the study team as well as
other NPG members.

5. The Ministers also noted that the United States and other nations are making
preliminary assessments of the methodology and resource requirements for an Air
Battle Study and that the extent to which the study should be reflected in Phase
II will have to be determined at a later date (paragraph 8 of NPG/D(72)7).
Further action at Staff Group level awaits receipt of the preliminary
assessments.

6. The Ministers agreed that they would consider the ACLANT Follow-on Use Study
in the Spring of 1973 (paragraph 11 of NPG/D(72)7). No further action need be
taken on this matter, other than taking it into consideration in developing the
Ministerial meeting agendas.

II. ILLUSTRATIVE STUDY
7. The Ministers invited the Chairman of the Military Committee to arrange for
SACLANT to prepare an Illustrative Study dealing with the initial use of nuclear
weapons at sea in the ACLANT area (paragraph 14 of NPG/D(72)7). The Secretary
General is sending a letter to the Chairman of the Military Committee confirming
this request and also noting that SACLANT should be ready to participate in the
preparation and delivery of a presentation. The Staff Group will want to discuss
details of such a presentation in connection with its consideration of
preparations for the next Ministerial meeting.

III. CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES
8. The Ministers invited the Permanent Representatives to examine further, as a
matter of priority and in the light also of the General Guidelines for
Consultation Procedure on the Use of Nuclear Weapons, the procedures and
