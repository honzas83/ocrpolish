> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 18th August, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on August 18, 1975, in Brussels to address several key nuclear-related issues. The group welcomed new members from Greece and Turkey. The meeting covered the United States' nuclear employment policy and the circulation of a revised last page of their paper. The group agreed to a briefing on satellites and the use of nuclear weapons by Dr. Kane of the IAW corporation. A visit to SHAPE (Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe) was scheduled for September 9. The group discussed practical aids to decision-making and the Air Defence Illustrative Study, deciding not to include the Air Defence Illustrative Study in the autumn 1975 agenda for NPG Ministers. However, they agreed that a study on the use of nuclear weapons for air defence should be undertaken by the Staff Group, linked with the NIKE-HERCULES Study. and the CENTAC and NORTHAG Studies. The group also touched upon the future work programme, selective employment planning, the Nunn Amendment follow-up, and communicating NATO's intentions via the Jeasmont group. The meeting concluded with a request for members to obtain views from their capitals on country requests for the use of nuclear weapons, and the approval of the next meeting date for August 29, 1975.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 18th
August, 1975

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared for the level of security: of
the matters to be discussed.

NEW MEMBERS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
warmly endorsed the welcome extended by the Chairman to two new members of the
Staff Group: Lt.Col. D. Flevrakis, Greece, and Mr. Tugay Özceri, Turkey.

UNITED STATES POLICY FOR THE EXPLOITATION OF NUCLEAR ASPECTS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted the remarks of the United States representative who referred to the
United States paper on nuclear employment policy, and said that a revised last
page was being circulated;

(b) noted the statement by the Chairman who suggested that the United States
paper should, for ease of reference, be circulated in both languages under an
NPG document reference.

BRIEFING ON SATELLITES AND THE DEPLOYMENT OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that Dr. Kane of the IAW
corporation had offered to give a briefing to the Staff Group on satellites and
the use of nuclear weapons;

(b) agreed to accept this offer and invited the Chairman to make arrangements
for the briefing to take place on Monday, 8th September in the Presentation
Room;

(c) noted that members could invite other persons in their delegations or staffs
who might be interested.

STAFF GROUP VISIT TO SHAPE

5. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who confirmed that the Staff Group
visit to SHAPE would take place on Tuesday, 9th September; a NATO bus would
depart from the Press entrance, NATO Headquarters, at 10.30 a.m., and return to
NATO at about 17.30 the same day; members were requested to use this bus so that
the Staff Group would arrive as one party.

PRACTICAL AIDS TO DECISION-MAKING: ILLUSTRATE STUDIES

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted the remarks of the Chairman who drew attention to the International
Staff paper on decision-making(1); he proposed that the Staff Group should
discuss this paper towards the end of next month and invited members to discuss
the problems involved with their capitals;

(b) noted that the International-Staff intended to circulate a paper on
procedures for nuclear consultation and the compilation of guidelines towards
the end of the week.

AIR DEFENCE ILLUSTRATIVE STUDY

7. The Staff Group:

(a) took notes of the remarks of the Chairman who called for views on whether
the Air Defence Illustrative Study should be included in the agenda for NFG
Ministers in autumn 1975;

(b) took note of the views expressed and agreed that the Illustrative Study
should not be included in the Agenda;

(c) took note of a statement by the United Kingdom representative who said that
his authorities proposed that a study on the use of nuclear weapons for air
defence should be undertaken by the Staff Group;

(d) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion, and in
particular of the following points:

(i) such a study should be linked with the NIKE-HERCULES Study;

(ii) the on-going air battle study and the CENTAC(1) and NORTHAG(2) Studies
should be taken into account;

(iii) some recent questions by Mr. Schlesinger on air defence should also be
taken into account;

(e) invited the United Kingdom representative to co-operate with his authorities
to prepare a description of the task.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME

8. The Staff Group:

took note that papers on the initiation of phase III (follow-on use work
programme) were expected to arrive from London and Bonn during the next few
days.

SELECTIVE EMPLOYMENT PLANNING

9. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who underlined the importance of
informing NFG Ministers more fully on selective employment planning;

(b) noted that the subject would be discussed during the visit to SHARE on 9th
September.

NPG FOLLOW-UP WORK ON THE NUNN AMENDMENT

10. The STAFF GROUP:

noted a statement by the United States representative who said that he hoped to
receive information on the Nunn Amendment follow-up from his authorities.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTIONS

11. The STAFF GROUP:

took note of a statement by the United Kingdom representative who said that the
Jeasmont group was preparing a draft report which would be circulated probably
in early September; it would deal principally with warning messages and would
draw upon the NPG Study on CNI(1).

COUNTRY REQUESTS FOR THE USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS

12. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recommended that members should
obtain the views of their authorities on the question of country requests for
the use of nuclear weapons, and offered to prepare a first draft paper on this
matter as a basis for discussion;

(b) agreed to re-open the discussion in two weeks time in the light of views
from capitals.

NEXT MEETING

13. The STAFF GROUP:

approved to hold its next meeting on Monday, 29th August, 1970, at 3.p.m.
