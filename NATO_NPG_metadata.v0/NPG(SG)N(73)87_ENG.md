> [!abstract] 
> # Future Work Programme
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, details the future work programme of the NPG. It lists tasks already initiated by NPG Ministers, such as the Phase II Follow-on Use Work Programme, illustrative studies on air defence by SHAPE, and a review of the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process. It also addresses the study on Warsaw Pact Politico-military Strategy and Military Doctrine led by the Netherlands. Additionally, the note identifies tasks that may arise from upcoming Ministerial decisions, including the compilation of nuclear consultation procedures and special ADM guidelines. The Chairman emphasizes the lead time required for subjects to reach Ministerial consideration and the potential for the Defence Policy Committee (DPC) Ministers to require information on nuclear aspects of basic issues. Finally, it outlines subjects where Permanent Representatives have a general remit to propose additional work, such as air battle studies and unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence, including political and military value of improved weapons and flexibility in political control.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME
Note by the Chairman
To facilitate further discussion by the Staff Group of the possible future
activities of the NPG and, in particular, to assist me in the preparation of the
PO on this subject which will be circulated prior to the Ministerial meeting, I
have attached a list of tasks already initiated by the NPG Ministers on which
work is currently underway, tasks that might flow from Ministerial actions at
their next meeting, and subjects on which the Permanent Representatives have a
general remit to propose additional work.

2. In addition to the material contained in these lists, the possibility exists
that the NPG will wish to provide information to the DPC Ministers in December
on nuclear aspects of the basic issues raised by the DPC Ministers last spring.
The Staff Group will recall that it has agreed to examine this matter after the
Hague meeting to see whether a report is needed and, if so, whether it should be
a separate NPG report or an input to a DPC Permanent Representatives report.

3. In reviewing the future work programme, it is important to keep in mind that
a lead time of a year or more is usually needed before a subject has reached the
stage where it is ready for Ministerial consideration. It follows from this that
work has to be initiated well in advance of the time that it is expected to be
on a Ministerial agenda. Furthermore, it is difficult in many cases to predict
precisely the particular meeting for which a report or study will be available.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM
NATO, 1110 Brussels

Tasks previously initiated on which work is underway
1. Follow-on Use Work Programme. In May 1972, the Ministers approved(1) the
initiation of Phase II of the Work Programme. In accordance with the approach
adopted(2), an ad hoc Phase II study team (US, UK and Germany) is now preparing
a report, the preliminary draft of which will be discussed with the Permanent
Representatives. The study team is scheduled to meet in Washington during the
week of 10th September after which it is expected that the Staff Group will be
informed of the current status of the study team effort and possibly be given
some indication of when a preliminary draft might be circulated. No realistic
estimate can be made at the present time of when the matter might be ready for
Ministerial consideration.

2. Illustrative Studies. In response to the Ministers' request(3), SHAPE is
preparing an illustrative study dealing with air defence for Ministerial
consideration at the Spring 1974 meeting. The Staff Group may wish to discuss
the possible need for additional studies for later Ministerial meetings and
whether requests should be made now for any that are foreseen for the Autumn
1974 meeting.

3. Function of the Military Committee in the Nuclear Consultation Process. At
the last meeting, the Ministers invited(4) the Permanent Representatives to
review the function of the Military Committee in the nuclear consultation
process. As a first step in making this review, the Permanent Representatives
asked(5) for the views of the Military Committee on the subject, but did not
give any target date for a reply. Since preparation of the Permanent
Representatives response to the Ministers is dependent on the Military Committee
reply, the Staff Group may wish to query the IMS member about the status of this
project in the Military Committee.

4. Warsaw Pact Politico-military Strategy and Military Doctrine. At their last
meeting, the Ministers invited(6) the Permanent Representatives to arrange for a
study on this subject. The work is being done by a group of countries under the
leadership of the Netherlands who plan to complete a study in time for
Ministerial consideration at the Spring 1974 meeting.

5. Implications of Communicating NATO's Intentions. The Ministers at Ankara
invited(1) the Permanent Representatives to initiate a study on this subject. No
decisions have been taken yet on how this work is to be done or when it might be
undertaken.

6. Conceptual Analysis of Nuclear Aspects of Deterrence. At their last meeting,
the Ministers invited(2) the Permanent Representatives to consider the value of
a conceptual analysis of the nuclear aspects of deterrence. During subsequent
Staff Group discussion, mention was made of a UK paper prepared in the context
of the Phase II study team effort that might be useful as a point of departure
in assessing the value of a possible analysis. The Phase II study team is
expected to decide at its meeting in Washington to release the UK paper to the
Staff Group for this purpose.

7. Consultation Procedures and Facilities. At their last meeting, the Ministers
invited(3) the Permanent Representatives to review the final WINTEX 73 reports
and to report on nuclear consultation aspects of appropriate. Further work on
this score depends on the substance of these final reports which are not yet
available. In addition, the Ministers have requested(4) the Permanent
Representatives to investigate a number of other questions related to nuclear
consultation procedures which are not completely resolved in the report(5) now
being developed for the next Ministerial meeting, i.e., special operating signal
and ATOMAL classification.

Tasks which might flow from Ministerial decisions at the next meeting
8. Consultation Procedures and Facilities. The current draft of the report to
the Ministers on this subject recommends that they invite the Permanent
Representatives to prepare a compilation of nuclear consultation procedures(6).

9. Special ADM Guidelines. Although the current draft of the report to the
Ministers on this subject contains no specific proposals for further work, the
paper recommends that the Ministers may wish to indicate, in the light of their
discussion, whether there are any particular aspects on which further study or
action should be undertaken.

10. Early Combined Use. The present draft of this report, still under
consideration at Staff Group level, contains no specific proposals for future
work. In completing this report, the Staff Group may wish to consider whether
any particular suggestions in this respect should be included in the paper.
Regardless of the Staff Group decision, the Ministerial discussion in itself may
lead to requirements for further activity.

Subjects on which the Permanent Representatives have a general remit to propose
additional work

11. Follow-on Use Work Programme. When they approved the Work Programme in 1969,
the Ministers invited(1) the Permanent Representatives to propose, as
appropriate, further work within the context of the Programme. One possibility
in this regard that has not been foreclosed is an air battle study, the
initiation of which might be proposed by the Permanent Representatives if the
improved techniques and methodologies necessary for the analysis are developed
in the future(2).

12. Unresolved Problems Related to Tactical Nuclear Defence. When the Ministers
discussed at London the question of structure and deployment of NATO forces for
action in a nuclear environment in October 1972, they agreed(3) that the matter
should be kept under review as the investigation of other unresolved problems
continued. Furthermore, following a discussion at their meeting in October 1971
of a number of suggestions by the Permanent Representatives for investigating
these questions, the Ministers invited(4) the Permanent Representatives to
submit for Ministerial consideration proposals for future work as the need
arises, e.g. possible studies addressing such questions as the potential
political and military value of improved weapons, the constrained but continuing
combined follow-on use, and flexibility in political control.
