> [!abstract] 
> # Preparation and Reproduction of Studies on Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons
> 
> This document, issued by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines the requirements for the standardization of a series of studies on the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons commissioned by the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group in November 1969. It specifies that each study must be produced in 120 copies and provides options for reproduction by either the discussion leaders or the International Staff. To ensure consistency, the document details the sequence of text, including cover notes, cover letters, and table of contents, and provides specific typing and layout requirements such as page dimensions (21 x 29.7 cm) and line spacing. It also addresses page numbering and the possibility of producing long studies in multiple volumes. Furthermore, the Chairman requests that authors avoid over-classification of documents to facilitate circulation, suggesting that highly sensitive ATOMAL information be placed in separate annexes. The International Staff is available to provide further advice on security classification and reproduction services.

PREPARATION AND REPRODUCTION OF STUDIES ON FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR
WEAPONS

Note by the Chairman

A series of studies on Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons was
commissioned by the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group at their meeting at
Airlie House, in November, 1969(1). The first to be issued was the North Norway
Study (discussion leaders Norway and Canada) which was circulated early this
week under the reference NPG/Study(71)1. Other studies are expected shortly and
it would be useful if all the studies in this series adopted the same layout,
format and outer cover. The following remarks and suggestions are made with a
view to achieving this standardisation.

2. Reproduction

Each study must be produced in 120 copies. Some of the, discussion leaders may
be in a position to undertake the complete production and printing of their
study in the necessary number of copies and with the appropriate outer cover.
Other discussion leaders may prefer that the reproduction of their study be
undertaken by the International Staff. The object of these notes is to help
those who prefer that the International Staff should print their study.

Bearing in mind that studies will carry an NPG reference number it is preferable
that the International Staff should undertake the distribution. Discussion
Leaders who are able to do their own reproduction should therefore send 120
copies to NATO Headquarters, Brussels, for distribution.

4. Sequence of Text
The study should be laid out in the following manner:
(a) cover note by the Secretary of the Nuclear Planning Group (prepared by the
International Staff);

(b) cover letter by the discussion leader(s);
(c) table of contents;
(d) main text (please adopt consecutive paragraph numbering throughout the whole
of the text);

(e) annexes (if annexes are highly complicated, or require folding or are
printed in colour it is requested that the discussion leader should provide the
International Staff with 120 copies for incorporation in the document).

5. Layout and Typing
In preparing a master copy for offset reproduction by the International Staff
printing service, the following points should be noted:

(a) typing should allow for print: ag on paper who.e overall dimensions are 21 x
29.7 cm ; this implies that the text including the classification, the page
number, the reference number, footnotes etc. shall be contained within a
rectangle 17cm across and 27cm deep.

(b) spacing should be 1½ except in the case of very long documents when it may
be desirable to adopt single spacing so as to avoid a very bulky study;

(c) typing should be clean and black so that a good reproduction can be made on
the International Staff offset machine.

6. Page Numbering
Studies for reproduction by the International Staff should be page numbered in
the same way as the North Norway Study. International Staff will add the
reference number of the Study at the top of each page and space should be left
for this purpose.

7. Long Studies
If a study turns out to be particularly long it can be produced in two or more
volumes.

8. Discussion leaders are requested to avoid over-classification and where
possible to adapt their text and the detailed information required in it so that
as low a classification as possible may be applied to the whole paper. If it is
essential to the correct understanding of the Study that ATOMAL information be
included it is suggested that such information should be given in a separate
annex or paper which could be circulated through ATOMAL channels.

Conclusion
9. The International Staff will be glad to reproduce and to distribute studies
for discussion leaders in the necessary number of copies and is at their
disposition for any further advice which may be required, including questions
concerning security classification.

(Signed) H. B. SEIM
