> [!abstract] 
> # Follow-on Action to the Ministerial Meeting in London on 29th and 30th May, 1969
> 
> This document outlines a schedule of work for the NATO Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group following a Ministerial meeting in London in May 1969. It details the consultation on the use of nuclear weapons, noting that Belgium has accepted to lead the discussion. It addresses the tactical use of nuclear weapons by Allied Forces Central Europe (FACE), forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE) area, and the role of atomic demolition munitions.  The document specifies deadlines for comments on the anglo-German guideline document, and the timeline for the refinement of these guidelines by the United Kingdom and Germany. It also covers the strategic use of nuclear weapons, including the role of the three nuclear strike forces and recommendations on the aircraft/missile mix in ACE. The United States and Germany are tasked with collecting national replies and SACEUR's recommendations for review by Ministers in November 1969.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (S.A. GRU.)
Follow-on action to the Ministerial meeting in London on 29th and 30th May, 1969
1. The following paragraphs outline a schedule of work that appears to be
indicated as a result of the Agreed minute of the London Ministerial meeting
(1); the schedule reflects the Staff Group’s discussion on 4th June.

I. CONSULTATION ON THE USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS (paragraph 5 of Agreed Minute)
2. Belgium has accepted to continue as discussion leader and to prepare a new
document for submission to the next Ministerial meeting.

II. TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS (paragraphs 6-10, 14 of Agreed Minute)
A. Tentative political guidelines for the possible initial tactical use of
nuclear weapons by F.A.R.

3. The Secretary General, in consultation with the Chairman of the Military
Committee, has arranged for comments by SACEUR, SACLEMT, together with those of
the Chairman, on the anglo-German guideline document (2) to be made available to
the discussion leaders by 18th July, 1969.

4. The International Staff has distributed copies of NPG/D(69)2 to Norway and
Portugal; Canada, Greece and the Netherlands have received copies when the
document was first circulated in April last. Any national comments on the
document by the NDAC (including N-G) countries should be made available to the
discussion leaders by 16th July, 1969; where possible such comments should
include precise drafting changes to the appropriate sections of the current
anglo-German document.

(1) NPG/D(69)5, 2nd June, 1969.
(2) NPG/D(69)2, 25th April, 1969.

5. The United Kingdom and Germany will then refine their document and circulate
it by 15th September, 1969 for review by the Permanent Representatives around
11th October.

6. If this timetable can be adhered to, it should be possible for the discussion
leaders to submit their final document before the end of October and to hold the
next Ministerial meeting in November.

7. work on the development of terms of reference for exploring in more detail
the implications and interactions of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons,
as well as NATC responses to Warsaw Pact initial use will need to be undertaken
sufficiently in advance of the fall meeting to allow Ministers to discuss the
subject at that meeting.

B. Tentative guidelines covering the use of atomic demolition munitions.
(paragraphs 12 and 13 of Agreed Minute)

8. The Staff Group has already been instructed by the Permanent Representatives
to prepare terms of reference for analyses of the potential utility of aDs for
the defence of certain selected sectors of representative areas of ACE.

9. Draft terms of reference have recently been distributed by the International
Staff and discussions can start in the Staff Group immediately, in cooperation
with the representatives of the military authorities. Working out a time
schedule will be among the tasks of the Staff Group.

III. STRATEGIC USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
(paragraphs 7-9 of Agreed Minute)

10. The comments by the Staff Group on the preliminary report by the Staff Group
on the role of the three nuclear strike forces (1) and their replies to the
questions contained in that report should be submitted by 25th July.

11. The Secretary General will convey to SACEUR, through the Chairman of the
Military Committee, the Minister's invitation to submit specific recommendations
on the aircraft/misile mix in ACE and on a programme for achieving dual-
capability by 12th September.

12. The United States/German collection of the national replies and SACEUR's
recommendations should be submitted by mid-October for review by the Ministers
in November.

(Signed) R.G. GILBERT
OTAN/NATC Bruges, 39.
