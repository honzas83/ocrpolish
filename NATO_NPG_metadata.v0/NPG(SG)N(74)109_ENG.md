> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 21st October, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on October 21, 1974, at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting covered several administrative and strategic points. First, the group noted that all present were cleared for the classification level of the matters discussed. Under documentation, the group acknowledged the distribution of the Secretary General's Note on the Future Work Programme and noted delays in the preparation of the SHAPE paper on Selective Employment Planning. The Chairman extended the cut-off date for the approval of the Advance Press Release to October 25. A significant portion of the meeting was dedicated to procedures for consultation between the United States and its NATO Allies concerning reports stemming from the Nunn Amendment. The Canadian and United Kingdom representatives emphasized the sovereign nature of reports to the US Congress, while the United States representative expressed a desire to maintain high standards of consultation. The group agreed to submit necessary data to the Secretary General to be discussed further in Rome. Additionally, the group noted an invitation to a vin d'honneur for the departure of Colonel Rolf Thyse, Dean of the Staff Group. Finally, it was agreed that no further meetings would take place until after the upcoming Rome Meeting.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels,
on Monday, 21st October, 1974

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that all persons present were cleared for the
level of classification of the matters to be
discussed.

DOCUMENTATION
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that the Note by the Secretary General on the
Future Work Programme had been distributed the
previous week under reference PO/74/140 dated
16th October, 1974;
(b) noted that some difficulties had arisen in the
preparation of the SHAPE paper on Selective
Employment Planning but that it was hoped that it
would be circulated shortly.

ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE
3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said
that for convenience, he had decided to put back
the cut-off date for approval of the text and
timing of the Advance Press Release until
Friday, 25th October.

PROCEDURES FOR CONSULTATION WITH RESPECT TO THE NUNN AMENDMENT

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to the informal
meeting of the Staff Group held in his office on 17th October during which a
valuable exchange of views with two nuclear planning officers from Washington
had taken place; he invited further discussion on the question of procedures for
consultation between the United States and her NATO Allies with regard to
reports stemming from the Nunn Amendment;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion, and in
particular those of the Canadian and United Kingdom representatives who
underlined the sovereign nature of a report to Congress, and expressed
appreciation that, in such circumstances, the United States authorities should
nevertheless envisage consultation with the NPG;

(c) noted with appreciation a statement by the United States representative who
said that, although he had no instructions at this early stage, he knew that his
authorities were interested in taking into account the views of their Allies and
wished to maintain a high standard of consultation; he had good reason to hope
that Secretary of Defense Schlesinger would speak on this matter in Rome;

(d) concurred with the views of the Chairman who proposed to submit to the
Secretary General all necessary data on this matter so that it could profitably
be brought to the fore in Rome.

FAREWELL TO THE DEAN OF THE STAFF GROUP

5. The STAFF GROUP:

noted with appreciation an invitation by the Chairman and members of the Nuclear
Planning Directorate to a vin d'honneur on Tuesday 17th December at 5 p.m. to
mark the departure of Colonel Rolf Thyse, Dean of the Staff Group.
