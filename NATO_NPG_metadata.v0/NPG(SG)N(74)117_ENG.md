> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 2nd December, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on December 2, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to coordinate preparations for the upcoming NPG Ministerial Meeting on December 10. The group discussed the distribution of several key papers, including the agenda for the Ministerial meeting, a revised Future Work Programme, and a draft communiqué. The meeting addressed the logistics of obtaining ATOMAL passes for entry to the Presentation room for the Ministerial meeting, and the Chairman noted that the draft NPG Ministerial Communiqué might require revision based on potential United States statements. The group agreed to a framework for the Agreed Minute and decided that its finalization would depend on the situation on December 10. Additionally, the group reviewed a draft report to the Nuclear Delegations Advisory Committee (NDAC) on NPG activities in 1974, noting the importance of acknowledging the role of the NDAC. Additionally, the group discussed the Secretary General's revised Future Work Programme, with the Canadian representative expressing concerns regarding the influence of NATO's Phase II Follow-on Use Study and the Technological Study on the U.S. national study effort. The group ultimately agreed to no amendments to the revised Future Work Programme but urged the United States to provide its Washington report on technological studies with speed. The meeting concluded by scheduling the next meeting for December 16, 1974.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME

7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the Secretary
General's revised Future Work Programme(1) and drew attention to some changes
made since its distribution in non-revised form on 16th October;

(b) took note of an intervention by the Canadian representative who, referring
to paragraph 3 of the Future Work Programme, expressed grave doubts as to
whether NATO work on the Phase II Follow-on Use Study, and on the Study of the
Political and Military Implications of Technological Developments could, as
stated in paragraph 3, influence the U.S. national study effort, in particular
within the short time available before 1st April, 1975; he felt that the Phase
II report and the Technological Study were more likely to be affected by the
U.S. national report to Congress than the other way round;

(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and in particular
of the following points:

(i) work on the Phase II report had been proceeding for three years and the
ideas generated would certainly have been taken into account by the staffs
preparing the U.S. national report,

(ii) work on the Technological report was less advanced and therefore would, at
this stage, have less effect;
