> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 1st September, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on September 1, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key strategic and administrative matters. The group discussed the initiation of Phase III studies, noting that scope papers had been received from Germany and the United Kingdom, while awaiting a paper from Washington. The Staff Group aimed to produce an agreed paper on the scope of Phase III by early October. Additionally, the group proposed dates for the completion of current studies and suggested meetings for the Nuclear Planning Group Permanent Representatives on October 8 and 29. Regarding nuclear release requests from NATO member governments, the group agreed to seek advice from the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) and have the International Staff prepare a draft. The group also reviewed the first draft of the Compilation of Nuclear Consultation Procedures, which is intended for inclusion in the NATO Crisis Management Manual. The United Kingdom representative mentioned a proposed requirement for a study on the use of nuclear weapons for air defence, and the group agreed to avoid overlapping work. Finally, the group scheduled its next meeting for September 5, 1975, which will include a briefing by Dr. Kane on satellites and nuclear weapons.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 1st
September, 1975

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP: took note that all persons present were cleared for the
level of security of the matters to be discussed.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME - INITIATION OF PHASE III STUDIES
2. The STAFF GROUP: (a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that
scope papers had been received from the German and United Kingdom authorities;
he looked forward to receiving a paper from Washington and he urged other
members to obtain scope papers from their authorities if possible; (b) took note
of the statements by the representatives of Denmark, Germany, the Netherlands
and the United Kingdom and of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion; (c)
took note of the view of the Chairman that the Staff Group should aim at
producing an agreed paper on the scope of Phase III not later than early
October.

AUTUMN WORK SCHEDULE AND DATES OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES' MEETINGS

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who proposed dates for the
completion of papers on matters currently being studied by the Staff Group, and
suggested that the NPG Permanent Representatives should meet on or about 8th
October and again on or about 29th October;

(b) invited the Chairman to circulate, in time for the next meeting, a short
paper setting out the proposed schedule.

NUCLEAR RELEASE REQUESTS FROM NATO MEMBER GOVERNMENTS

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who suggested that the preparation
of a short paper on Country Requests required, in the first place, some advice
from SACEUR, followed by the preparation by the International Staff of a draft;

(b) agreed, after discussion, to proceed in this manner and proposed that, after
approval by Permanent Representatives, the agreed procedures should be included
in the current compilation of Nuclear Consultation Procedures.

COMPILATION OF NUCLEAR CONSULTATION PROCEDURES

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by Mr. Serio who introduced the International
Staff's first draft(1); he underlined that this paper was required in the
context of crisis management and would, in due course, be included in the NATO
Crisis Management Manual which was at present at the stage of being a working
paper(2); he recalled that the Compilation paper was required by Permanent
representatives not by Ministers;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the discussion and agreed that the
Compilation paper should be based strictly on existing policy and guidelines;

(c) invited members to give their comments, in writing, to the International
Staff who would prepare a revised version of the first draft in the light of the
discussion and of the written comments to be received.

NUCLEAR WEAPONS FOR AIR DEFENCE

6. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of the statement by the United Kingdom representative who said
that his authorities had prepared a short paper describing their proposed
requirement for a study on the use of nuclear weapons for air defence; this
paper would be distributed to Staff Group members during the next few days;

(b) took note that work was proceeding elsewhere on air defence problems and
agreed that care should be taken to avoid overlapping of work.

NEXT MEETING

7. The STAFF GROUP:

agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 5th September, 1975, at 3 p.m. and
noted that this meeting would begin with a briefing by Dr. Kane on satellites
and nuclear weapons.

NATO, 1110 Brussels.
