> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday, 5th September, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels on September 5, 1973. The meeting focused on two primary agendas: the implementation of special ADM guidelines and the early combined use of nuclear assets. Regarding the ADM guidelines, the Staff Group took note of a request from the United States Representative to delay the circulation of a draft report until amendments concerning the use of United States helicopters for SHAPE were confirmed. Additionally, the group noted that the Turkish Representative, Mr. Sungar, agreed to the circulation of the report as an NPG Working Paper while his authorities continued their review. On the matter of early combined use, the group resumed discussions on a possible report by NPG Permanent Representatives, focusing on definitions, escalation, and political implications. The group also considered incorporating the SHAPE report into crisis management documentation and agreed to certain text changes. The International Staff was tasked with preparing a revised version of the report for the meeting on September 17, 1973. The meeting concluded by scheduling the next session for September 10, 1973.

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday,

IMPLEMENTATION OF SPECIAL ADM GUIDELINES

1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a request by the United States Representative that the draft
report by NPG Permanent Representatives(1) should not be circulated as an NPG
Working Paper until he had confirmed the accuracy of his amendment to paragraph
23, concerning the proposal to SHAPE on the use of United States helicopters;

(b) took note of a statement by the Chairman that he had been in contact with
the new Turkish Representative, Mr. Sungar, who had agreed to the circulation of
the report as an NPG Working Paper, although his authorities were continuing
their review of it.

2. The STAFF GROUP:
