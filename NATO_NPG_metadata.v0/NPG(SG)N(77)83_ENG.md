> # Allied Command Europe Senior Officers' Orientation Course
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) has issued a notice regarding the Senior Officers' Orientation Course (short title: I-31) conducted by the NATO School (SHAPE) in Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarize senior NATO officers of the rank of Colonel and above, and civilian officials of equivalent grade, with the major concepts of NATO weapons employment in Allied Command Europe. A COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance is required for attendance. The document provides a schedule of seven courses scheduled for 1978, ranging from January to September, noting that French translation is available for all listed dates. Tuition costs are 85 United States dollars per student, which must be paid by national authorities for personnel of NATO Delegations. The course lasts one week from Monday to Friday. Officials in Brussels are instructed to apply through the Nuclear Planning Directorate of the International Staff, while those in capitals and national commands should apply through their Ministry of Defence. Due to limited vacancies and high demand, early application is at most a month in advance, and a 'Joining Report' must be sent to the NATO School (SHAPE) at least four weeks prior to the course. Further information can be obtained from Mr. R. Serio or Miss K. Winters of the Nuclear Planning Directorate.

SENIOR OFFICERS' ORIENTATION COURSE

Note by the Chairman

The subject course (short title: I-31) is conducted by the NATO School (SHAPE),
Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarise senior NATO
officers and key personnel in staff appointments with the major concepts of NATO
weapons employment in Allied Command Europe. Officers of the rank of Colonel and
above and civilian officials of equivalent grade are eligible to attend on a
need-to-know basis. A COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance is required. The annotation
"F" in the last column below shows courses for which French translation will be
available; other courses will be conducted in English only. The course lasts one
week – Monday through Friday. Tuition costs are 85 United States dollars per
student, which for personnel of NATO Delegations must be paid by their national
authorities. The following courses are scheduled for 1978:

2. The course provides a general orientation and background that is considered
useful for those involved in NPG work; it may also be of interest to other
senior officials in delegations and capitals. Officials of national delegations
located at NATO Headquarters in Brussels who are interested in attending one of
the courses listed above should apply through the Nuclear Planning Directorate
of the International Staff which acts as the point of contact with SHAPE and the
NATO School at Oberammergau-for obtaining vacancies for personnel in delegations
and on the International Staff. Officials in capitals and national commands
should apply through their Ministry of Defence.

3. The number of vacancies on each course is limited and the demand is usually
quite heavy. Since only a few vacancies are usually allocated to NATO officials,
it is therefore advisable to apply well in advance of the desired course, i.e.,
at least a month beforehand. This also allows time for submission of the
"Joining Report" which must be sent to the NATO School (SHAPE) at least four
weeks prior to commencement of the course.

4. The Nuclear Planning Directorate would be pleased to provide further
information or to obtain quotas. Please contact Mr. R. Serio or Miss K. Winters
on extensions 2048 and 2027 respectively.
