> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 17th April, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened on April 17, 1972, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several administrative and strategic items. The group discussed a possible visit to the United States, noting that while some preferred a 10-day visit, most were open to a late autumn or early winter trip due to transportation constraints and the busy schedule of Washington authorities. The meeting also reviewed the results of a meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives from April 15, where several working papers on battlefield studies, consultation procedures, and Turkish studies were approved or flagged for further action. Specific attention was given to the report on the Mediterranean Maritime Area Study, with the group agreeing to a number of changes and requesting immediate instructions from capitals. The group also touched upon low-yield tactical nuclear weapons, with the Canadian representative requesting information from the United States. Additionally, the group discussed the review of policy documents, specifically the Polaris-Poseidon conversion and its impact on the Concept paper. The meeting concluded with arrangements for SHAPE and International Staff briefings at the May Ministerial meeting and the scheduling of the Autumn NPG Ministerial meeting for October 26-27, as well as the the next meetings of the Permanent Representatives and the Staff Group.

POSSIBLE STAFF GROUP VISIT TO THE UNITED STATES
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that no official air
transport appeared to be available to the Staff Group either for transatlantic
transportation of for inter-state transportation in June; he asked whether, in
these circumstances, the Staff Group still wished to try to make the visit in
June or whether the Autumn or Winter might be preferable; he drew attention to
the possible use of charter flights, and asked for comments on the whole
problem;

(b) took note of the suggestions made in the ensuing discussion and in
particular of the following points:

(1) most representatives preferred a 10-day visit to a 15-day visit, despite the
financial advantages of the latter in respect of commercial air fares;

(11) although some representatives still hoped for a visit in June, most were
prepared to accept a late Autumn or early Winter visit, especially if free
transatlantic travel were available;

REPORT ON MEDITERRANEAN MARITIME AREA STUDY
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the amendments to
NPG/WP(72)7 agreed prior to and during the Permanent Representatives meeting,
and the wish of the Permanent Representatives that further examination of this
Working Paper should be made by the Staff Group with a view to reaching
agreement as soon as possible on a revised text, which, after reference to
Capitals, could be circulated on the "silence procedure";

(b) took note of the points raised and the suggestions made in the subsequent
discussion, and particularly those concerning timely release, and agreed to a
number of changes to the text;

(c) invited representatives to inform, by the fastest means, their Capitals of
the amendments to the text of NPG/WP(72)7 agreed by the Permanent
Representatives last Thursday and this day by the Staff Group, and to request
immediate instructions;

(d) agreed to inform the International Staff as soon as possible of the comments
and/or agreement of their capitals;

(e) invited the International Staff, provided all countries agreed to the
amended document, to issue it without delay as a final agenda document for the
Ministerial meeting;

(f) agreed that if any difficulties arose, a meeting of the Staff Group would be
convened to discuss them.

REVIEW OF POLICY DOCUMENTS
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Canadian representative who referred to the
Polaris-Posideon conversion due to be effective on 1st July, 1972 and asked what
should now be done regarding possible changes to NPG policy documents and in
particular to the revised concept paper(1);

(b) noted with appreciation the reply of the United States representative who
said that he would remind his authorities of this matter and suggest that work
on it should be started as soon as possible;

(c) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the only document
likely to be affected would be the Concept paper; the sequence of work would be

- the preparation of a report for Permanent Representatives in September,
setting out proposed modifications;

- the presentation of the agreed modifications to the NPC Ministers at their
Autumn meeting;

- agreement by Ministers of NDAC and then of the NPC in December.
