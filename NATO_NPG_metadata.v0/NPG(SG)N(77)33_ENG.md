> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 18th April, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on April 18, 1977, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting covered several key administrative and strategic items. First, the group confirmed that all attendees were cleared for the security classification of the discussed matters. Regarding a visit to military installations in Germany scheduled for May 9-10, 1977, the group noted the arrangements made by SHAPE and the German Representative. The International Staff was tasked with identifying participants and providing their identity cards and passports to SHAPE. Additionally, the group reviewed documentation, including a New Technology Study and a TIES Status Report. The meeting also addressed the second revision of the draft report on the WINTEX-77 exercise, agreeing to amend the text and convert it into an NPG Working Paper for approval by Permanent Representatives on May 5, 1977. Finally, the group viewed an audio-visual presentation on cruise missiles and scheduled their next meeting for April 20, 1977.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 18th
April, 1977

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:

took note that all persons present were cleared to the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

STAFF GROUP VISIT TO MILITARY INSTALLATIONS IN GERMANY

2. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) noted with appreciation the remarks of the SHAPE representative who outlined
the arrangements which had been made for the visit by the Staff Group to
military installations in Germany on 9th and 10th May, 1977;

(b) noted also with appreciation the arrangements proposed by the German
Representative for the evening of Sunday, 8th May;

(c) invited the International Staff to find out which members of the Staff Group
wished to take part and to inform SHAPE with all speed of the serial numbers of
their identity cards and passports;

(d) invited the International Staff to circulate a programme.

OBSERVATIONS ON WINTEX 77
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted the remarks of the Chairman who introduced the second revision of the
draft report on WINTEX-77;

(b) took note of the views expressed during the ensuing discussion, and agreed
to a number of changes in the text;

(c) agreed that the draft, amended in the light of the discussion could now
become an NPG Working Paper for examination in capitals and for approval by NPG
Permanent Representatives at their meeting to be held on 5th May, 1977;

(d) invited the International Staff to circulate this WP in both languages with
all speed.

CRUISE MISSILES
5. The STAFF GROUP:
attended the projection of an audio-visual tape film on cruise missiles.
