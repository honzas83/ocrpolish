> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday, 28th May, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on May 28, 1975, in Brussels to establish the agenda for the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives scheduled for June 3, 1975. The agenda items include a report on Phase II, the Future Work Programme, and any other business. The group discussed the Follow-On Use Work Programme - Phase II and agreed to minor editorial changes, noting that the revised Working Paper would become a document for the NPG Ministerial Meeting. Regarding the Future Work Programme, the group discussed the Secretary General's paper and the need for political consideration of further studies from NATO Military Authorities. They also addressed the need for illustrative studies, specifically mentioning the future of the existing Air Defence Study and the potential for future studies. Additionally, the group noted that the Mike Hercules warhead deployment study was being examined by the International Military Staff. It was confirmed that Germany would invite NPG Ministers to hold their Autumn Meeting 1975 in Germany. Finally, the group noted that Germany, Turkey, and the United States intended to publish an advance press release for the Monterey Meeting on June 9, 1975, and provisionally scheduled their next meeting for June 2, 1975.

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Wednesday,

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that all persons present were cleared to the security classification of
the matters to be discussed.

AGENDA FOR MEETING OF NPC PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES TO BE HELD
ON TUESDAY, 3RD JUNE, 1975 AT 10.30 A.M.

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed that the agenda for the meeting of NPC Permanent Representatives to
be held on Tuesday, 3rd June, should contain the following three items:

- The report on the Phase II report(1),
- the Future Work Programme(2),
- Any Other Business;
(b) agreed that if representatives received comments from their capitals
concerning the papers to be discussed during the next few days, they should
circulate them to the other members of the Staff Group.

FOLLOW-ON USE WORK PROGRAMME - PHASE II
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed to two minor editorial changes,
(b) noted that the Working Paper, revised as necessary in the light of comments
by the Permanent Representatives on 3rd June, would then become a document for
the NPG Ministerial Meeting.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the Secretary
General’s paper(1) would be circulated in a revised version in time for the
meeting; in Monterey, he invited comments on the present text;

(b) agreed that some changes to the wording of paragraphs 6 and 7 may be
advisable,

(c) concurred that any NPG country could ask for a study;
(d) agreed upon the need for IFG Permanent Representatives to consider from a
political standpoint the further studies which should be called for from the
NATO Military Authorities in connection with follow-up action to the
consultation in NATO on the 'un-amendment;

(e) agreed that it would be premature, at this stage, to ask for specific
studies;

(f) discussed the need for illustrative studies noting, in particular, the
following points:

(i) that SHAPE had asked for a decision on the future of the existing Air
Defence Study;

(ii) that it was probable that further studies on various matters, would be
required in the future;

DEPLOYMENT OF MIKE HERCULES WARHEADS IN EUROPE
5. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that the MIKE HERCULES Study was being examined by the International
Military Staff and noted that copies of this Study, together with the Military
Committee's comments will be made available to Delegations and to the
International Staff.

AUTUMN MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS
6. The STAFF GROUP:
noted with appreciation the remarks of the German representative who confirmed
that his Government wish to invite NPG Ministers to hold their Autumn Meeting
1975 in Germany.

ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE FOR MONTEREY MEETING
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that Germany, Turkey and the United States intended to publish the
Advance Press Release concerning the Monterey Meeting on 9th June;

(b) noted the remarks of the Chairman who requested replies from countries who
had not yet answered

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
8. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed provisionally to hold its next meeting on Monday, 2nd June, 1975 at 3
p.m., to be confirmed on Friday, 30th May.

1110 Brussels.
