> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday 26th November, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on November 26, 1973, in Brussels to coordinate activities and prepare for upcoming meetings. The group took note of the agenda for the Nuclear Planning Group Advisory Committee (NDAC) meeting scheduled for December 7, and confirmed that the NPG activities report would be circulated as an NDAC document. The meeting also focused on preparations for the NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting, discussing five potential agenda subjects including briefings on the balance of strategic forces, US research and development of tactical nuclear weapons, and an illustrative study on nuclear air defence. The group addressed the progress of a study on Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy and military doctrine, as well as the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process. Discussions also touched upon the political aspects of early combined use and the communication of NATO's intentions, with representatives from Italy, Norway, and the United States participating. The group agreed to the planning timetable for the Spring Meeting and discussed a conceptual analysis of deterrence and the potential for an illustrative study for the Autumn 1974 meeting. Finally, the group considered a possible study on the political and military implications of technological developments on tactical nuclear questions and scheduled their next meetings for December 3 and December 12, 1973.
