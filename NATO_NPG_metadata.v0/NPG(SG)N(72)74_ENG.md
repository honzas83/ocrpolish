> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters on Thursday, 7th September, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters on September 7, 1972, to address several key strategic studies. The group welcomed new members from Norway and the United States. Regarding the Denmark Follow-on Use Study, the group discussed a revised report and requested the International Staff to prepare a new version as an NPG Working Paper for dispatch to Capitals. For the SACLANT Illustrative Study of Initial Use at Sea, the group reviewed a first draft report and discussed timing and release authority, agreeing to a number of changes before it would be revised as an NPG Working Paper. The group also noted a statement on the Transition Study concerning forces transition, structure, and deployment, which was incorporated with views from the Military Committee. the group discussed the scheduling of the next meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives, with the United States representative expressing concerns over the tight schedule. It was agreed that drafts would be dispatched to Capitals immediately and the meeting of Permanent Representatives would be rescheduled to October 5, 1972. Finally, the Staff Group agreed to meet again on September 11, 1972.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters on Thursday, 7th September,
1972

WELCOME
1. The STAFF GROUP:
warnly endorsed the welcome extended by the Chairman on behalf of the Staff
Group to Mr. J. Leine, the new Norwegian member, and to Lt. Colonel G.R. Vavra,
of the United States Delegation who would be taking part in the work of the
Staff Group.

DENMARK FOLLOW-ON USE STUDY
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the International
Staff's revised version(1) of the report on the Denmark Study, and said that
this new version had been prepared in the light of the views expressed by the
Staff Group at their meeting on 30th August, and of the comments on the Study by
SACEUR(2) and SACLAND(3); he invited comments;

(b) discussed the revised paper in detail and agreed to a number of changes to
the text;

(c) invited the International Staff to prepare, in the light of the agreements
reached, a new version of the report in the form of an NPG Working Paper for
despatch to Capitals and subsequent examination by the Permanent Representatives
at the end of September or early October.

(1) NPG(Staff Group)WP(72)12(Revised), 4th September, 1972
(2) NPG/Study/29, 29th August, 1972
(3) NPG/Study/28, 3rd September, 1972

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDY – INITIAL USE AT SEA IN THE ACLANT AREA
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the International
Staff's first draft(1) of a possible report on the SACLAN T Illustrative Study
of Initial Use at Sea; he invited comments;

(b) took note of a statement by the International Military Staff representative
who said that the Military Committee had also received the Study and would be
circulating a note confirming their approval of it;

(c) examined the International Staff's draft in detail and in particular
discussed at length those parts of the paper concerning the timing and level of
release authority;

(d) took note of the views expressed, and agreed to a number of changes to the
text;

(e) invited the International Staff to prepare, in the light of the agreements
reached, a revised version of the report in the form of an NPC Working Paper for
despatch to Capitals and subsequent examination by the Permanent Representatives
at their meeting at the end of September or early October.

FORCES TRANSITION, STRUCTURE AND DEPLOYMENT
4. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the International Staff's
revised draft report(2) on the Transition Study, modified in the light of the
preliminary discussion held in the Staff Group on 21st August, and of the
Military Committee views as set out in their paper "Structure and Deployment of
NATO Forces for action in a Nuclear Environment"(3), would be circulated during
the next few days.

(1) NPG(Staff Group)WP(72)16, 4th September, 1972
(2) NPG(Staff Group)WP(72)10(Revised) – to be circulated
(3) MCM-63-72, 8th September, 1972

NEXT MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the United States representative who recalled
that the Staff Group had agreed that NPG Working Papers should be sent to
Capitals with a view to their subsequent review by the Staff Group on 25th
September, and their examination by NPG Permanent Representatives on 29th
September; he feared that, in the case of his own Authorities, this schedule
would be too tight;

(b) took note of a statement by the Chairman concerning the various factors
affecting the problem, and noted the views expressed in the subsequent
discussion;

(c) agreed that the International Staff's drafts should, as soon as a large
measure of agreement had been reached on them, be reproduced as NPG Working
Papers, for immediate despatch to Capitals; agreed that this procedure would not
prevent subsequent changes in the light of Capitals' views, and that if
necessary revised Working Papers could be printed and circulated for the meeting
of Permanent Representatives;

(d) agreed, subject to some doubts by the Italian representative, that the
meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives should if possible take place on
Thursday, 5th October, 1972, at 3.30 p.m., and not on 29th September as formerly
planned; requested the Chairman to take the necessary steps.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
6. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 11th September, 1972 at 3 p.m.
