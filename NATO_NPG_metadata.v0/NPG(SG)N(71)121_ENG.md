> # Draft Report by the Nuclear Planning Group
> 
> This document is a draft report summarizing the general activities of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) during 1971 for the information of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC). The report details the Ministerial meetings held in Mittenwald and Brussels, noting the participation of Belgium, Canada, Denmark, Germany, Greece, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, the United Kingdom, and the United States, with Turkey also participating at staff levels. It highlights briefings by the United States Secretary of Defense on strategic forces and briefings on regional studies regarding the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. The report describes the progress of a follow-on use work programme, specifically Phase I (preparation of basic data inputs) and the initiation of Phase II (comparative analysis and synthesis of findings). It mentions the examination of illustrative studies and sample release requests to help political authorities understand the implications of tactical nuclear weapon use. It also addresses unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence and the alignment of plans with the Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE. It further monitors the implementation of Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs). The report concludes by suggesting that the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee take note of the report and invite the NPG to continue its studies and policy proposals.

Note by the Chairman
Attached is an initial draft of a Report by the Nuclear Planning Group, to be
placed on the agenda for the meeting of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee
Ministers on Wednesday, 3rd December, 1971. It is proposed that the NPG Staff
Group discuss this draft at their meeting on Monday, 9th November, for the

that week. It is intended that approval of the Working Paper would be sought by
the silence procedure with a deadline date of Monday, 22nd November. If comments
or objections are received, we would try to resolve them first at the Staff
Group meeting on 22nd November or, failing this, at a meeting of the NPG
Permanent Representatives that could be scheduled for Wednesday afternoon, 24th
November. The final NDAC agenda document would be circulated during the week of
22nd November.

Draft Report by the Nuclear Planning Group
This report summarises the general activities of the Nuclear Planning Group
during 1971, for the information of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee.

2. The Nuclear Planning Group has met twice at Ministerial level: in Mittenwald
on 25th and 26th May, and in Brussels on 26th and 27th October. The next
Ministerial meeting is scheduled to take place in Denmark in the Spring of 1972.

3. Countries represented at the Ministerial meetings during 1971 were
Belgium(1), Canada(2), Denmark(1), Germany, Greece, Italy, the Netherlands(2),
Norway(2), the United Kingdom and United States. All countries mentioned above,
as well as Turkey, participated in Nuclear Planning activities at the Permanent
Representatives and staff levels.

4. The actions taken by the NPG Ministers at their meeting in Mittenwald and
Brussels have already been circulated(3)(4). In addition, all members of the
Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee now hold the basic documentation discussed by
the NPG Ministers at the two meetings.

5. At the meetings in Mittenwald and Brussels, the NPG Ministers received with

on the balance of strategic forces and engaged in a discussion of trends and
developments.

6. During 1971 several regional studies on follow-on tactical use of nuclear
weapons, undertaken as part of a follow-on use work programme adopted by the NPG
Ministers in November 1969, became available for Ministerial consideration. Of
the nine studies initiated to date within the context of the work programme,
three(1) were discussed by the Ministers at the Mittenwald meeting and one(2) at
the Brussels meeting. Of the remaining five, one(3) has been completed and the
others are expected to be finished during 1972. Emphasis during 1971 has been
placed on Phase I of the follow-on use work programme, namely, the preparation
of basic data inputs in the form of studies, and related comments from the NDAC
countries and the NATO Military Authorities. Phase I activity is expected to
continue well into 1972; however, the NPG Ministers have invited the NPC
Permanent Representatives to develop proposals for the initiation of Phase II of
the work programme (comparative analysis of the basic data inputs, synthesis of

7. At the two meetings in 1971, the Ministers devoted
attention to the examination of illustrative studies and related
sample release requests, intended to help political authorities to
become aware of the implications of the courses of action which
may be recommended when the tactical use of nuclear weapons is
required, the kind of decisions which might have to be taken, the
probable effects of these decisions, and the circumstances in which
decisions might have to be reached. It is expected that the NPG
Ministers will discuss other illustrative studies at future meetings,
with emphasis on those situations that would confront political
authorities with complex consultation and decision-making questions.
As a related matter, the NPG Ministers concluded, in the light of
their discussion of sample release requests at the 1971 meetings,
that the objective of a "request for consideration," mentioned
in the Provisional Political Guidelines(1), has been satisfied by
the provision for a warning message WHISKEY in the release procedures
of the Major NATO Commanders.

8. The NP3 Ministers also discussed a number of unresolved problems related to
tactical nuclear defence(1) raised by the Secretary General. While they
recognised that some of the work already in hand in the NPG may help to define
these unresolved problems more clearly and could contribute toward their
solution, the Ministers requested the preparation of a number of reports on
aspects of the unresolved problems that could be investigated profitably at this
time.

9. It will be recalled that the Defence Planning Committee, when approving the
Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE(2) in December,
1970, invited the NATO Military Authorities to report to the NPG by 31st March,
1971, on their progress toward adjusting their plans to conform with the
approved concept. At the Mitterwald meeting, the NPG Ministers discussed a
report from the Military Committee on this subject and noted the assurances by
the NATO Military Authorities that ACE-wide as well as regional plans are in
accord with the approved concept and that any future plans will also be in
accord with that guidance.
