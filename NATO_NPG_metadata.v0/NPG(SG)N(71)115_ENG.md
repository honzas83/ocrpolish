> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Wednesday, 13th October, 1971: SHAPE Preliminary Review of the CENTAG Area Follow-on Use Study
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on October 13, 1971, to discuss the SHAPE Preliminary Review of the CENTAG Area Follow-on Use Study. The Chairman noted that SHAPE had prioritized the preparation of this review, which was issued on October 12, and that the Permanent Representatives' Report on the CENTAG Study had been agreed upon prior to the review's publication. The group discussed whether to issue an addendum to the report or a new document based on a short paper produced by the International Staff. While the Canadian Representative supported the idea of a new document for approval by Permanent Representatives and subsequent inclusion in the NPG Ministers' agenda, the majority of the group felt that issuing another report was neither practical nor desirable at this stage. It was agreed that the matter would be further discussed at a meeting of the Staff Group after the Ministerial Meeting. Additionally, the group scheduled its next meeting for October 22, 1971, and cancelled its meeting on October 18, and set its first meeting after the NPG Ministerial Meeting for November 8, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Wednesday, 13th October, 1971
SHAPE PRELIMINARY REVIEW OF THE CENTAG AREA FOLLOW-ON USE STUDY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that SHAPE had given
high priority to the preparation of a preliminary review of the CENTAG Area
Study and that this review had been issued on 12th October by the International
Staff under reference NPG/Study(71)13; he recalled that the Permanent
Representatives' Report(1) on the CENTAG Study had been prepared and agreed
before the SHAPE preliminary review had been published; he suggested that
Permanent Representatives might wish, as a result of the SHAPE review, to take
some further action either in the form of an addendum to their report(1), or by
means of a new document; he said that with this in mind the International Staff
had produced a short paper(2) on which he invited comments;

(b) took note of a statement by the Canadian Representative who said that he was
in full agreement with the content of the International Staff's paper, and
proposed that it should form the basis for a new document for approval by the
Permanent Representatives the next day, and should subsequently be included in
the Agenda for the coming Meeting of NPG Ministers;
