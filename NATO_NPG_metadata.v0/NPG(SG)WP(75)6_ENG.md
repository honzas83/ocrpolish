> # Nuclear Aspects of the Nunn Amendment
> 
> This document is a notice from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) detailing the process of reviewing and unclassified versions of a report to the United States Congress regarding the nuclear aspects of the Nunn Amendment. US Public Law 93-365 directed the Secretary of Defense to study the tactical nuclear weapons in Europe and their relation to deterrence and conventional defense. Following a request from Secretary Schlesinger in November 1974, NATO's assessment of the political and military implications was sought. The document outlines the timeline of briefings by Mr. Donald R. Cotter and the distribution of the report 'The Theater Nuclear Force Posture in Europe' to the NPG Delegations and the Military Committee. It further describes the process of the US Ambassador to NATO coordinating the US Congress's request for an unclassified summary and full report, which were eventually accepted by the Secretary General and NPG Allies. The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) exchanged views on the unclassified version of the report on 30 April 1975, expressing appreciation for the opportunity to comment on the report prior to its public release.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
UNCLASSIFIED REPORT TO THE US CONGRESS ON THE
NUCLEAR ASPECTS OF THE NUNN AMENDMENT

US Public Law 93-365 directed the Secretary of Defense
to study the overall concept for use of tactical nuclear weapons
in Europe; how the use of such weapons relates to deterrence and
to a strong conventional defense; reduction in the number and
types of nuclear warheads which are not essential for the defense
structure for Western Europe; and the steps that can be taken to
develop a rational and coordinated nuclear posture by the NATO
Alliance that is consistent with proper emphasis on conventional
defense forces. The Secretary of Defense was requested to submit
a report on the results of this study to the United States Congress
on or before April 1, 1975.

2. On 5th November, 1974 Secretary Schlesinger, in response
to US Public Law 93-365, requested NATO's assessment on the
political and military implications of the nuclear aspects of the
NUNN Amendment(1). On 10th February, 1975 the Permanent
Representatives (NPG) met and discussed the SHAPE, SACEUR, Military
Committee military assessment called for by Secretary Schlesinger
and agreed to the preparation of a letter to provide the required
political evaluation of the military assessment.

3. PO/75/15(Revised) dated 25 February, 1975 advised the
Permanent Representatives (NPG) that the text of a letter to the
United States Secretary of Defense was approved by all participants
and would be forwarded, as required, by the Secretary General.

(1) USNATO(DPD)OUT/MS/74-142

4. On 18 March, 1975 Mr. Donald R. Cotter, representing the United States
Secretary of Defense, briefed the Permanent Representatives (NPG) on The Report-
on-NATO's Theater Nuclear Forces required by Public Law 93-365. On 25 March,
1975 Mr. Cotter returned to consult further with the Permanent Representatives
(NPG) and later that same day the US Ambassador-to-NATO-distributed copies of
the report to the NPG Delegations and to the Chairman, Military Committee.

5. On 18 April, 1975 the US Ambassador circulated an unclassified summary of the
report on "The Theater Nuclear Force Posture in Europe" and requested a
determination whether or not the Summary would be acceptable to the Secretary
General and the NPG Allies. At the same time, the US Ambassador advised the NPG
Allies that his authorities were preparing an unclassified version of the full
report to the Congress at the request of Senator Stennis. Both the unclassified
Summary and the unclassified version of the full report would form the basis for
statements by US officials in any Congressional hearings on this subject.

6. On Tuesday, 22 April, 1975 the US Ambassador ascertained that the
unclassified summary was acceptable to the Secretary General and the NPG Allies
and the United States Authorities were so advised.

7. On 26 April, 1975 the US Ambassador provided the Permanent Representatives on
the Nuclear Planning Group and the Chairman of the Military Committee copies of
the unclassified version of the full report on "The Theater Nuclear Force
Posture in Europe." At the same time, he indicated that because of the strong
interest in Congress in receiving this report, it would be forwarded to Senator
Stennis on Friday, May 2.

8. The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) on 30 April, 1975 exchanged views on
certain aspects of the unclassified version of the NUNN Amendment report and
relayed to the United States member appreciation for receipt of the report prior
to its public release and for the opportunity to comment on the report.
