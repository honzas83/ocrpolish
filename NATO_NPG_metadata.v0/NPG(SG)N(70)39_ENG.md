> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 4th May, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on May 4, 1970, to address several key agenda items. The group welcomed Lt.Col. J. Schils as the new Belgian deputy representative. They confirmed the dates for the next meetings of the NPG Permanent Representatives, scheduled for May 12 and May 20, 1970. The group noted the circulation of a revised draft report on Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs) and anticipated amendments from Germany and the United States. They also discussed a German-United States draft concept for the role of nuclear strike forces, noting that a final version would be prepared for consideration by the NPG, Nuclear Delegations and Arms Control (NDAC) and the Defence Committee (DFC) by the end of 1970. Additionally, the group reviewed a draft status report on the work programme for further studies on follow-on tactical use and urged study team leaders to submit their annexes. Finally, they discussed the draft agenda and advance press release for the upcoming NPG Ministers meeting in Venice, Italy, and scheduled their next meeting for May 11, 1970.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 4th May, 1970

NEW BELGIAN DEPUTY REPRESENTATIVE ON THE STAFF GROUP
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman who welcomed Lt.Col. J. Schils, who
replaced Col. van der Wal as deputy representative of Belgium on the Staff
Group.

NEXT MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) confirmed, after further discussion, the agreement reached at its previous
meeting that the next meeting of the NFG Permanent Representatives should take
place on Tuesday, 12th May, 1970, at 10.15 a.m., with the possibility of
continuing at 4 p.m.;

(b) agreed that the second meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives,
originally foreseen for 19th May, should now take place on Wednesday, 20th May,
1970, at 4 p.m.

DRAFT REPORT ON 'TOIC DEMOLITION MUNITIONS(1)
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that the revised draft report on ADMs(1) had been circulated on
Thursday, 30th April;

(b) noted that Germany and probably the US would have amendments to propose, and
agreed that these might usefully be discussed by the Staff Group at its next
meeting.

DRAFT CONCEPT FOR THE ROLE OF NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES(1)

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that the German-United States paper(1) on a draft concept for the rôle
of nuclear strike forces had been circulated on Thursday, 30th April;

(b) noted that this paper was still a draft concept, and that the next stage
should be the preparation, for consideration at NPG, NDAC and DFC levels at the
end of 1970, of a final version;

(c) noted that views on this paper could be given by the NPG Permanent
Representatives at their meeting to be held on 20th May.

STATUS REPORT BY PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES ON THE WORK PROGRAMME FOR FURTHER
STUDIES ON FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE(2)

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that the draft status report(2) had been approved by the Staff Group
on the silent procedure, but that the Annexes giving the situation of each study
as at 1st May, mentioned in paragraph 4 of this report, were not yet available;

(b) urged the study team leaders concerned to send their Annexes to the
International Staff not later than 6th May;

(c) invited the International Staff to circulate the complete draft status
report as an NPG Working Paper as soon as possible, so that it might be
discussed by the NPG Permanent Representatives at their meeting on 12th May.

DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS IN VENICE(3)

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) discussed the draft agenda for the Venice Meeting and took note of the views
expressed;

(b) invited the International Staff to circulate a revised version of the agenda
in the form of an NPG Working Paper for discussion by the Permanent
Representatives at their meeting on 12th May.
