> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 15th September, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on September 15, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting covered several key agenda items: the communication of NATO's intentions, specifically focusing on CNI warning messages and procedures. The group discussed consultation procedures regarding country requests and invited the International Staff to prepare revised texts and drafts. Regarding the Follow-on USA Work Programme - Phase III, the group reviewed scope papers from Germany, the United Kingdom, and the United States, and agreed to retain them as background reference material. The group also reviewed a draft ministerial agenda for the autumn meeting of Ministers, with suggestions for a briefing by the US Secretary of Defense and the inclusion of a separate item for the Phase III scope paper. Finally, the meeting addressed the future meetings of NPG Permanent Representatives and the next meetings of the NPG (Staff Group), noting a schedule change for the Turkish Ambassador's attendance. The next meeting was scheduled for September 19, 1975.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 15th
September, 1975

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that all persons present were cleared to the security classification of
the matters to be discussed.

COMMUNICATION OF NATO's INTENTIONS

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who proposed that the Staff Group
should discuss a paper(1) prepared by the informal working group, on CNI warning
messages and procedures;

(b) took note, in the ensuing discussion, of various suggestions and agreed to a
number of changes;

(c) invited the International Staff to prepare revised texts for paragraphs 4, 5
and 6 in time for further discussion of this paper at the next meeting.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES - COUNTRY REQUESTS

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(t) took note of a statement by the Chairman who
introduced the International Staff's draft
paper(i) on country requests;
(b) took note of the views expressed in the discussion
and agreed to a number of changes;
(c) invited the International Staff to prepare, in the
light of the views expressed, a revised draft of
the paper for discussion at the next meeting.

FOLLOW-ON USA WORK PROGRAMME - PHASE III

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks by the Chairman who
recalled that Ministers, at their meeting in
Monterey, had invited Permanent Representatives
to develop detailed proposals for initiation and
implementation of an approach to Phase III for
consideration by Ministers at their Autumn 1975
meeting; he said that scope papers from the German,
United Kingdom and United States authorities were
now available to members;
(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing
discussion and observed a similarity of views in
the German and United Kingdom papers and useful
details of a complementary nature in the United
States paper;
(c) agreed that all three papers would be retained
in their present form as background reference
material;
(d) invited the International Staff to prepare a first
draft of a possible paper for Ministers with all
spec.

DRAFT MINISTERIAL AGENDA

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) tool note of the 'draft agenda prepared by the International Staff(1) for
the autumn meeting of :1: Ministers;

(b) took note of the views of the Italian representative supported by other
members that the customary briefing by the US Secretary of Defense would be
appreciated; he suggested that this matter be included in the present item II;

(c) noted the view of the United Kingdom representative that the Phase III scope
paper should be a separate item on the agenda because Ministers at Monterey had
called for it specifically;

(d) concurred with the view of the Chairman who underlined the need for some
discussion of SFPs and took note of the remarks of the SHAPE representative who
doubted whether SICER would have much to say on this matter;

(e) invited the International Staff to prepare a revised draft of the agenda in
the light of the views expressed.

FUTURE MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the NPG Permanent
Representatives would meet on 9th October, 1975 at 4 p.m., but that in view of
the difficulty which the Turkish Ambassador would have in attending a meeting on
29th October, the date of the second meeting was now changed to Monday, 3rd
November, 1975 at 4 p.m.; he added that 29th October could be held in reserve;

(b) noted that a firm decision on whether and when to hold the second meeting
should be taken on 9th October.

(1) NPG(Staff Group)WF(75)9, 10th September, 1975

NEXT MEETINGS OF THE NPG(STAFF GROUP)
7.  The STAFF GROUP.
