> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 31st January, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on January 31, 1977, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels, Belgium. The meeting covered three primary agenda items. First, the group discussed the draft terms of reference for a study on Maritime Nuclear Warfare in the SACLAN area and the Mediterranean, noting that a CMC under preparation in the Military Committee would define the aim, scope, and military parameters of the study. Representatives from Germany, the UK, and the US provided input on the scope and requirements of the study. Second, the group addressed participation in VINTEX 77, agreeing to prepare a report on nuclear activities following the exercise and discussing the timeframe for consultation and nuclear play. Third, the group discussed arrangements for the NPG Spring Ministerial meeting in Ottawa, Canada, including accommodation at the Hotel Château Laurier and the possibility of a visit to western Canada. The meeting concluded with the the agreement to hold the next session on February 14, 1977.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 31st
January, 1977

SECURITY

1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared for the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

DRAFT TERMS OF REFERENCE FOR THE MARITIME NUCLEAR WARFARE STUDY

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted the remarks of the Chairman who recalled that the organisation of the
study on Maritime Nuclear Warfare in the SACLAN area and the Mediterranean and
the draft terms of reference for the NPG Study on this matter was the subject of
a CMC under preparation in the Military Committee;

(b) noted a statement by the IMS representative who said that he hoped that this
CMC would reach the Secretary General during the next 24 hours; it would contain
the aim-and scope of the Study and the military parameters for its conduct;

(c) took note of a statement by the German representative who hoped that certain
matters would be included in the CHIC;

PARTICIPATION IN VINTEX 77
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the IMG representative who outlined the
timeframe for VINTEX 77 with particular regard to consultation and nuclear play;

(b) took note of the suggestions made in the ensuing discussion particularly
with regard to the tasks set by Ministers(2);

(c) agreed that the Staff Group should begin to prepare a report on nuclear
activities in VINTEX 77 as soon as the exercise was finished;

(d) agreed that the personal participation of Permanent Representatives should
not be limited solely to 9th and 10th March.
