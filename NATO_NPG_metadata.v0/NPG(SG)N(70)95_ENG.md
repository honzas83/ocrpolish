> # WINTEX 71
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) regarding the participation of NATO Headquarters in the upcoming WINTEX 71 exercise, scheduled for January 27th to February 4th, 1971. Due to revisions in the Operation Order following a probable compromise and decisions by the Defence Policy Committee (DPC) and the Military Committee, participation at NATO Headquarters in Brussels is limited to two small cells: a group of International Military Staff (IMS) officers representing the Military Committee, the and a two-man International Staff (IS) International Staff (IS) cell representing the DPC. The Chairman notes that there will be no actual play at Brussels, no participation of the Situation Centre, and no formal post-exercise critique. However, he suggests that the Staff Group may seize the opportunity to observe the development of the exercise and aspects relevant to the use of nuclear weapons through periodic briefings provided by the IMS nuclear cell in the Situation Centre. The offer of these briefings is contingent upon the availability of sufficient exercise inputs, specifically whether release requests will contain full justification and essential data. Because the details of the WINTEX revision are still being finalized by NATO Military Authorities, a final decision on these arrangements is to be postponed until the meeting on January 11th, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
WINTEX 71
Note by the Chairman
As agreed at our meeting on Monday, 7th December, I have discussed possible
Staff Group activities in the context of WINTEX 71 (27th January – 4th February)
with representatives of the Council Operations Directorate and the IMS.

2. It appears that parts of the original WINTEX 71 Operation Order are being
rewritten and simplified after its probable compromise earlier this year. As a
result of this revision and the prior decision by the DPC and the Military
Committee not to participate in the exercise, it is foreseen that participation
at NATO Headquarters, Brussels, will be limited to two small cells,

- a limited group of IMS officers representing the Military Committee, and
- probably a two-man IS (Council Operations Directorate) cell representing the
DPC,

who will insert action by the Military Committee and the DPC respectively as and
when desired by the SHAPE Directing Staff. Apart from the activities of these
two cells and the Communications Centre, there will be no actual play at
Brussels, nor will the Situation Centre participate. Furthermore, there will be
no formal post-exercise critique or analysis of the WINTEX 71 participation
carried out at NATO Headquarters.

NATO, 110 Brussels.

3. These very limited activities would seem to make it pointless for the Staff
Group to observe exercise proceedings as originally envisaged during the
discussion at our last meeting.

4. Nevertheless, it may be beneficial to the Staff Group to seize the rare
opportunity of a NATO-wide large scale militia Command Post Exercise (CPX) to
make its own arrangements in parallel with the exercise activities which would
permit it to observe the development of the exercise and those facets and
aspects that are of particular relevance to the use of nuclear weapons and of
particular interest to the Group. In this context, our colleagues of the IMS
nuclear cell have indicated their readiness to investigate the possibility of
giving periodic briefings in the Situation Centre to the Staff Group on the
developing situation and in particular on incoming information relevant to
release requests as well as on these requests themselves. At the same time,
there would be some opportunity to become familiar with procedures and
facilities. To the extent it desired, the Staff Group could also engage in
informal discussion of the matters raised in the briefings.

5. The offer by the IMS is contingent upon the availability of sufficient
exercise inputs to make briefings and discussions meaningful. For instance, it
will be necessary to ascertain whether the release requests will contain the
full justification and other essential data in view of the fact that the DPC
will not play.

6. Since the details of the WINTEX revision are still being worked out by the
NATO Military Authorities, it is not practicable to decide now on the question
of the arrangements mentioned above. The IMS will continue to look into the
feasibility of these arrangements and hopes to be able to make some specific
suggestions early next year. I propose that we then resume our discussion at our
meeting on 11th January, 1971.

(Signed) H. B. SEIM
