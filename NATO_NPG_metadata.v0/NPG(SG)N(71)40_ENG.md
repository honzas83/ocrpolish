> # Illustrative Studies and Sample Release Requests
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held meetings on March 19 and 22, 1971, to address several key nuclear planning issues. The group discussed the approach, form, and content of a paper for Ministers regarding illustrative studies and sample release requests, requesting a revised version from the International Staff. They also reviewed a draft status report on work in progress and agreed on modifications to the draft agenda for the meeting of NPG Ministers in May 1971. Additionally, the group noted the expected arrival of the Macedonia and Western Thrace area study and the NORTHAG area study, and planned for a future working paper on follow-on use. They took note of the Military Committee's progress report on the implementation of the Concept of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces and discussed the potential for amendments to the Provisional Guidelines. The group also scheduled meetings for the Northern Planning Group Permanent Representatives and future Staff Group meetings in Brussels, meetings which would include briefings on the Greek study and the NORTHAG area study.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held on Friday, 19th March, and on Monday, 22nd March, 1971

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES AND SAMPLE RELEASE REQUESTS

1. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who described the contents of the
International Staff paper(1) and said that he hoped that the Staff Group would
come to agreement upon the approach, form and content of the paper for Ministers
by Monday, 22nd March, and that an agreed version could then be made available
for submission to capitals;

(b) discussed the paper and invited the International Staff to prepare a new
version in the light of the views expressed, in time for the meeting on Monday,
22nd March;

(c) took note of a further statement by the Chairman who introduced the revised
version(2) on the Monday, explained that it was in two parts (Background and
List of Questions) and suggested, inter alia, that it would be more timely to
introduce the question of pre-conditioned release at a later stage when the
ACLANT and Mediterranean studies had been received;

(d) discussed the new version in detail, taking note of a proposal by the United
States Representative regarding the presentation of questions, and of the
various other views expressed;

STATUS REPORT ON WORK IN PROGRESS
