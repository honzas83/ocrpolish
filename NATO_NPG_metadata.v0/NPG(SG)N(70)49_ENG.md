> [!abstract] 
> # Provisional Arrangements for Atomal Security and for the Transport of Classified Documents (Venice Meeting)
> 
> This document provides detailed provisional arrangements for the handling, transport, and storage of ATOMAL classified documents for the Nuclear Planning Group Ministers meeting in Venice on 8 and 9 June 1970. It specifies that a designated courier with a Courier Certificate must be physical custody of the documents at all times when outside a NATO ATOMAL Central Registry or Control Point. The guidelines include requirements for correct packaging and sealing, ensuring that the case containing the documents is marked with the destination address but without any classification markings. Transportation from Brussels to Venice is recommended via air, preferably using Member Country military aircraft or civilian airlines. Upon arrival in Venice, documents are to be handed over to authorized persons at Tessara Aerodrome or the railway station and transported to the ATOMAL Control Point located in Room 16 of the Fondazione Cini on San Giorgio island. The document also details the return journey arrangements, noting that delegations are responsible for their own return transport of documents back to Brussels.

PROVISIONAL ARRANGEMENTS FOR ATOMAL SECURITY AND
FOR THE TRANSPORT OF CLASSIFIED DOCUMENTS
(VENICE MEETING)

Note by the Secretary
1. It will be noted that the Agenda for the meeting of the Nuclear Planning
Group Ministers in Venice on 8 and 9 June, 1970 contains references not only to
COSMIC TOP SECRET but also to three ATOMAL documents. Delegations will probably
feel the need to take copies of these documents to Venice and it may therefore
be useful to recall the general regulations concerning the handling of ATOMAL
documents and also to describe the arrangements made by the Italian Authorities
in Venice for their handling and storage.

GENERAL REGULATIONS
2. Designated Courier
A member of the Delegation should be officially designated as a courier for
ATOMAL documents and should be provided with a Courier Certificate which permits
him to take the package through the Customs without it being opened.

3. Packaging
ATOMAL documents should be correctly packaged and sealed before departure. It is
recommended that this be accomplished by ATOMAL control personnel in the
National Delegations or in the NATO Central Registry for members of the
International Staff.

4. Identification
The case containing the documents should be clearly marked with the address of
its destination, but NO indication of its classification should appear on the
case.

5. Safekeeping
ATOMAL documents must be in the physical custody of the officially designated
courier at all times when outside a NATO ATOMAL Central Registry, Sub-registry
or Control Point.

6. Transportation
It is recommended that ATOMAL documents be transported from Brussels to Venice
by air wherever possible. The use of Member Country military aircraft is
preferable. However, transport via NATO Member Country civilian airlines is an
acceptable alternative. Other methods of transportation are not acceptable,
since safeguarding and storage requirements of NATO security regulations cannot
normally be met under such conditions.

7. Arrival
See Corr. following
On arrival at destination, the case containing the ATOMAL documents should
either:

(a) be handed over to an authorized person, against receipt, or.
(b) be taken to an ATOMAL Control Point.

8. Handling During Conference
ATOMAL documents may be withdrawn against receipt during working hours but must
be handed back to the ATOMAL Control Point when no longer required and, in any
case, for safe-keeping during the night.

9. Return Journey
The same arrangements apply in reverse.

SPECIFIC ARRANGEMENTS AT VENICE MEETING

(Note: The arrangements outlined below are provisional. Full details will be
notified by the Italian Delegation in a few days time.)

10. Reception
See Carr. following

10.1. Italian Authorities are arranging for a reception point for documents to
be opened at Tessara Aerodrome and if necessary at the railway station. An
authorized person will be available to meet ATOMAL couriers arriving from
Brussels and to take their packages from them provided that Delegations inform
the Italian Delegation of the name of their courier and the time and place of
his arrival. Packages will be taken by the Italian Authorities to the Control
Point on San Giorgio island.

11. ATOMAL Control Point
The Italian Authorities are setting up an ATOMAL Control Point in Room 16 in the
Fondazione Cini on San Giorgio island. All ATOMAL documents must be stored at
this point during non-working hours and at night.

12. Departure Arrangements
(a) Delegations are responsible for transporting their ATOMAL documents back to
Brussels in the same way in which they brought them.

(b) On the day of departure, Wednesday 10th June, it is hoped that the Italian
Authorities will be able to arrange to bring ATOMAL and other documents from the
island of San Giorgio to the airport.

(b) It does not appear practicable for the Italian Authorities to undertake the
transport of bulky packages from Venice to Brussels through diplomatic or
military channels. Delegations should therefore make their own arrangements for
the return transport of all their documents.
