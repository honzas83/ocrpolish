> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Friday, 17th October, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on October 17, 1975. The group discussed and noted the Chairman's statement regarding the agenda for the upcoming Hamburg meeting, which would be circulated via the silence procedure. They also reviewed the text of an advance press release scheduled for release on November 17, 1975. Additionally, the German representative requested that delegations provide group certificates of ATOMAL clearance. The meeting also focused on the arrangements for Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme, where the group discussed a revised draft Permanent Representatives' report. The United Kingdom and United States representatives proposed changes to the text, with the United States representative awaiting further instructions on part IV of the paper. The International Staff was invited to circulate a further revision of the report to be finalized as an NPG Working Paper for the Permanent Representatives' meeting on October 29. Finally, the group agreed to hold its next meeting on October 20, 1975.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE HAMBURG MEETING

ARRANGEMENTS FOR PHASE "III"

5. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who introduced the revised version
of the draft Permanent Representatives' report on arrangements for Phase III of
the Follow-on Use Work Programme; he underlined the importance of finalising
this paper without further delay. In order that it might be circulated in the
form of an NPG Working Paper for examination in Capitals and approval by
Permanent Representatives at their meeting to be held on 29th October ;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and agreed to a
number of changes to the text;

(c) took note of, and discussed, a number of changes handed out by the United
Kingdom representative;

(d) took note of the remarks of the German representative who said that he would
be obliged to obtain further guidance from his Authorities;

(e) took note of a number of changes proposed by the United States
representative and of his statement to the effect that he was awaiting further
instructions from his Authorities concerning part IV of the paper;

(f) invited the International Staff to circulate a Further revision in time for
discussion, as early as possible, next week;
