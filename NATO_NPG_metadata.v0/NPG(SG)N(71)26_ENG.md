> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 15th February, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on February 15, 1971, in Brussels, Belgium. The meeting began with the welcome of new members, including Mr. H.R. Morgan from Canada and Mr. Paul Ziluca from Washington. The group discussed a briefing by the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) on current release procedures, noting the need for punctuality and special passes. Administrative matters were addressed, including the distribution of Nuclear Planning Group documents and the number of copies required by delegations. The group also reviewed the status of the Exercise WINTEX 71 critique, with a report from SHAPE expected by mid-March. Discussions continued regarding national participation in NATO military nuclear planning, with representatives instructed to consult their capitals. The group further reviewed studies on the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons, specifically mentioning the NORTAG Study, the Mediterranean Study, and the North Norway Study. Additionally, the group examined illustrative studies and sample release requests from Annex B to NPG/N(70)5. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next session on February 26, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 15th February, 1971
WELCOMES
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted and approved a statement by the Chairman who welcomed Mr. H.R. Morgan,
Canada, who joined the Staff Group in replacement of Mr. G. Smith and would be
working jointly with Lt.Col. C.R. Baker for the time being;

(b) noted and approved the remarks of the Chairman who welcomed the presence at
the meeting of Mr. Paul Ziluca from Washington.

BRIEFING BY SACEUR ON CURRENT RELEASE PROCEDURES
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to a note from
the Executive Secretary(1) concerning the issue of special passes for SACEUR's
Briefing on Monday, 22nd February, 1971;

(b) noted the Chairman's request that those attending the briefing should be
punctual since SACEUR had appointments immediately after the briefing.

DISTRIBUTION OF NPG DOCUMENTS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of NPG(Staff Group)N(71)24 regarding the number of copies of various
nuclear documents required by delegations.

EXERCISE WINTEX 71 CRITIQUE

4. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Representative of the Military Committee who
said that an analysis of the Exercise was being undertaken and a report was
expected from SHAPE; by mid-March it should be possible, if desired, to give a
review of this Exercise at a Staff Group meeting.

NATIONAL PARTICIPATION IN MILITARY NUCLEAR PLANNING

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman with regard to the International Staff's
note(1) concerning national participation in NATO military nuclear planning;

(b) continued the discussion begun at a previous meeting and took note of the
views expressed;

(c) agreed that representatives, using the International Staff's paper, should
obtain instructions from their capitals as to whether the paper, either in its
present form or modified, should be sent out officially;

(d) agreed to re-open the question in mid-March.

STUDIES OF FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note that there was now a strong possibility that the NORTAG Study
would be ready by the end of February or the first week of March;

(b) noted that there was a possibility that the Mediterranean Study might be
ready by mid-March;

(c) continued its discussion on the North Norway Study and took note of views
expressed;

(d) agreed that in selecting the main issues for presentation to Ministers it
was necessary to obtain maximum help from capitale; agreed to continue the
discussion at a future meeting.
