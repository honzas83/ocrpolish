> # Draft Agenda for the May 1971 Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This document is a second revision of a draft agenda for the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministerial meeting to be held in Mittenwald on May 25th and 26th, 1971. The note by the Chairman, H. B. Seim, emphasizes that discussion leaders for agenda items have been omitted to avoid complications regarding sponsorship of studies and the preferences of certain member states. Instead, it is agreed that Ministers directly involved in specific items, such as sponsors of follow-on use studies, will be invited to make initial remarks. The draft agenda includes a US briefing on the balance of strategic forces, studies on the follow-on use of nuclear weapons in North Norway, Macedonia, and Western Thrace, and the North Atlantic Group (NORTHAG) area. The agenda also covers illustrative studies and examples of release requests, the concept for the role of theatre nuclear strike forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE), and other business including the future work programme and the final communique. The document references several reports by Permanent Representatives and the Military Committee, and a briefing on SACEUR's nuclear weapons release procedures.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

DRAFT AGENDA FOR THE MAY 1971 MINISTERIAL MEETING

Note by the Chairman

Attached is a second revision of a possible agenda for the NP Group Ministerial
meeting in May, prepared in the light of Staff Group discussions last week.
Delegations are requested to obtain final agenda views in time to permit the
Staff Group to develop a final agenda proposal at our meeting on 19th April.

2. It will be noted that, pursuant to the Staff Group's agreement, discussion
leaders have not been listed on this agenda. For most agenda items,
identification of discussion leader(s) is either obvious (agenda item I) or
irrelevant (agenda items II, I, IV-VIII). For agenda item II.2, concerning
follow-on use studies, designation of discussion leaders is complicated by the
fact that five Ministers have been directly involved as sponsors or co-sponsors
of the three studies under consideration. Moreover, one of the five countries
has already indicated that it does not want its Minister to be shown on the
agenda as a discussion leader. With respect to agenda item III on illustrative
studies, the Staff Group noted that no particular Minister or Ministers had been
specifically identified with this subject, that the topic has not been on
earlier Ministerial agendas, that none of the Ministers has shown a desire to be
the discussion leader for this subject, and that none of the documentation for
this item has been prepared by countries. It was considered inappropriate for
one or more of the military observers to be designated as discussion leader to
lead a Ministerial discussion. In view of the foregoing, the Staff Group
concluded that it would be preferable to omit the listing of discussion leaders
on this agenda. In this connection,
