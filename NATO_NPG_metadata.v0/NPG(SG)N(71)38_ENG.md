> # Studies on Follow-On Use: Status Report on Work in Progress
> 
> This document consists of a cover note by Chairman H. B. Seim and an annexed draft report by Permanent Representatives regarding the status of work in progress for the follow-on use studies programme. The Chairman proposes that the Staff Group discuss the draft to agree on a Working Paper for capitals, with the intention that Permanent Representatives consider it during the week of 19th or 26th April 1971, and that the final Ministerial agenda document be circulated shortly thereafter. The draft report notes that the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group accepted a work programme for the study of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons in November 1969. It details that three regional studies—North Norway, Macedonia and Western Thrace, and NORTHAG—have been submitted and placed on the Ministerial agenda for Mittenwald. Other studies that have been initiated but are not yet completed include those on Turkey, the Mediterranean area, CENTAG, the ACLANT area, and a study on the transition from conventional to nuclear posture. The report further notes that the study on Denmark has not yet been initiated and that the Permanent Representatives believe it is too early to propose further work. The document references several previous NPG documents and reports, including NPG/D(69)10 and NPG/D(70)1.

STUDIES ON FOLLOW-ON USE
STATUS REPORT ON WORK IN PROGRESS
Note by the Chairman

Attached is the first draft of a status report by Permanent Representatives on
work in progress in the follow-on use studies programme which is to be on the
May Ministerial agenda. I propose that the Staff Group discuss this draft at our
meeting on 19th or 22nd March for the purpose of agreeing on a Working Paper
that could be sent to capitals next week. It is intended that Permanent
Representatives would consider the Working Paper at a meeting during the week of
19th or 26th April and that the final Ministerial agenda document would be
circulated shortly thereafter.

(Signed) H. B. SEIM

NATO, 1110 Brussels.

STATUS OF WORK IN PROGRESS

Draft Report by the Permanent Representatives

At their meeting at Airlie House on 11th and
12th November, 1969(1), the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning
Group accepted a work programme for the study of follow-on
tactical use of nuclear weapons(2). At the same time, they
invited their Permanent Representatives to:

(a) make arrangements for the fulfilment of the
initial work;

(b) monitor on a continuing basis the work programme
and the specific studies undertaken therein;

(c) propose, as appropriate, further work within the
context of the work programme(3).

2. In February, 1970, the Permanent Representatives
approved arrangements for fulfilment of the initial work(4),
