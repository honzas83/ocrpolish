> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Thursday, 28th March, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on 28 March 1974 at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting's primary focus was to set the date for the next meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives for 16 May 1974. Additionally, the group discussed a revised draft report on the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process, noting that some members lacked instructions from their capitals. The group agreed that the circulation of the draft report as an NPG Working Paper for the May meeting would depend on further comments. If consensus on a particular approach was not reached by 3 April, the Chairman would circulate the draft containing two alternative approaches. The group also reviewed an initial draft report on a study of Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy and military doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear weapons, agreeing to changes in form and content. Finally, the agreement was set for the next Staff Group meeting to take place on 8 April 1974.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Thursday,
28th March, 1974

NEXT MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

1. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed that the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives would take place on
Thursday, 16 May, 1974.

MILITARY COMMITTEE FUNCTION IN THE NUCLEAR CONSULTATION PROCESS

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) discussed a revised text of the draft report on this subject(1) and agreed
to a number of changes to the text;

(b) noted that some Staff Group members were not in a position to subscribe to
either of the two alternative approaches suggested in the draft report because
of lack of instruction from their capitals;

(c) agreed that the form in which the draft report will be circulated as an NPG
Working Paper for consideration by Permanent Representatives at their meeting on
16th May, 1974, will be dependent on comments by these members;

(d) invited the Chairman to circulate the draft report as an NPG Working Paper
containing the two alternative approaches if by Wednesday, 3rd April, a
consensus on one particular approach has not been reached.

(1) NPG(Staff Group)WP(74)3(3rd Revision), 26th March, 1974 and Addendum,
circulated on 27th March, 1974.

WARSAW PACT STRATEGY AND DOCTRINE
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced an initial draft(1)
of a possible Permanent Representatives' report on a study on Warsaw Pact
politico-military strategy and military doctrine for the tactical use of nuclear
weapons;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and agreed to a
number of changes to the form and content of the draft;

(c) invited the Chairman, in the light of the discussion, to prepare a revised
version of the draft report, for discussion at the next meeting.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
4. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 8th April, 1974 at 3 p.m.
NATO, 1110, Brussels.
(1) NP(Staff Group)WP(74)5, 21st March, 1974.
