> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 7th December, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on December 7, 1970, in Brussels to address several key strategic and administrative matters. The group welcomed new and temporary members from Denmark, Canada, and Norway, as well as a distinguished visitor, Mr. John H. Morse from the United States Department of Defense. The meeting covered the following topics: the targeting of Soviet Intermediate-Range/Medium-Range Ballistic Missiles (IR/MBRs) on Europe, noting a communication from the United States regarding this issue. The group also discussed follow-up activities from a recent Ministerial meeting, including the distribution of political guidelines for the initial defensive tactical use of nuclear weapons, the role of theatre nuclear strike forces in the Allied Command Europe (ACE), and guidelines for the use of atomic demolition munitions. The group further addressed the administrative question of note-takers for military observers attending Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) and Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) ministerial meetings. The United Kingdom representative suggested that the usefulness of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) was diminishing, though general support for the committee was existed. The Finally, the group discussed the potential participation of the Staff Group in Exercise WINTEX 71 and requested a paper on the matter for the next meeting scheduled for the January 11, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 7th December, 1970

WELCOMF TO NEW OR TEMPORARY MEMBERS

1. The STAFF GROUP:
approved some words by the Chairman who welcomed Mr. B. Jacobsen from the Danish
Ministry of Defence, now with the Danish Delegation, Colonel J.S. Pustay from
SHAPE, Lt. Colonel C.R. Baker, temporarily representing Canada, and Mr. E.
Selmer temporarily representing Norway.

DISTINGUISED VISITOR

2. The STAFF GROUP:
approved a statement by the Chairman who warmly welcomed the presence of Mr.
John H. Morse, Deputy Assistant Secretary of Defense, from the office of NATO
and European affairs in Washington, and thanked him for the interest he took in
the work of the Staff Group.

TARGETING OF SOVIET IR/MBRs ON EUROPE

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who said that under cover of a note by the
Secretary General(1), a letter from Secretary Laird and a United States paper
regarding targeting of Soviet IR/MBRs on Europe had been sent to NDA Defence
Ministers; copies would also be sent to the Chairman of the Military Committee
and to the three Major NATO Commanders;

FOLLOW-UP ACTIVITY STEMMING FROM THE RECENT MINISTERIAL MEETING

(a) noted it was planned to distribute the following papers, approved at the
recent Ministerial meeting on Thursday 10th December;

(1) Provisional Political Guidelines for the Initial Defensive Tactical Use of
Nuclear Weapons by NATO – DPC/D(69)58(Revised);

DPC/D(70)59;

(iii) Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of Atomic Demolition
Munitions – DPC/D(70)60;

(b) noted that the Secretary General would be writing to the Chairman of the
Military Committee regarding the Concept paper with a request that the NATO
Military Authorities should confirm that they are taking action to conform with
the guidance given in the paper, and would report, with comments and
suggestions, by 31st March, 1971;

(c) took note of and discussed a draft of a letter to be sent by the Secretary
General to the Chairman of the Military Committee concerning Atomic Demolition
Munitions and invited the International Staff to submit a new draft, revised in
the light of the discussion, to the Secretary General;

(d) recalled that a draft decision sheet for the meeting of the NDAC held on 2nd
December, 1970 had been circulated(1), and that if no objections had been raised
by the close of business on Wednesday, 9th December, 1970 it would be issued as
a NDAC document.

NOTE-TAKERS FOR MILITARY OBSERVERS ATTENDING NPG AND NDAC
MINISTERIAL MEETINGS

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted the Chairman's remarks regarding the question of note-takers for the
Chairman of

the Military Committee, S.L.EUR and S.ACLANT when taking part in NPG and NDAC
meetings(1);

(b) noted that most representatives were without instructions on this matter,
and agreed to re-open the discussion at the next meeting.

THE ROLE OF THE NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the United Kingdom Representative who suggested
that the usefulness of the NDAC appeared to be diminishing;

(b) in the course of the discussion which ensued, noted general support for the
NDAC and some suggestions as to how best it might participate in nuclear affairs
in the future.

STAFF GROUP PARTICIPATION IN EXERCISE WINTEX 71

7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted an introductory statement by the Chairman, who outlined the form and
aims of Exercise WINTEX 71 and invited comments;

(b) noted that although there was general agreement on the desirability of
exposing the Staff Group to such an Exercise, there were widely differing views
on the manner and degree of participation;

(c) invited the International Staff, in conjunction with the International
Military Staff and the Council Operations Directorate, to prepare and issue a
paper on the matter for discussion at the next meeting of the Staff Group.

NEXT MEETING

8. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 11th January, 1971.
