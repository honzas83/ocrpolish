> # Access to Single Integrated Operational Plan (SIOP) Information and Documents
> 
> This document is a notice from the Secretary of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) regarding an upcoming meeting of NPG Ministers in Brussels. The meeting's agenda includes a briefing by the United States Secretary of Defense on the Single Integrated Operational Plan (SIOP). The notice emphasizes that authorization for ATOMAL information does not automatically grant authorization for SIOP information, and all attendees must be authorized for both ATOMAL and SIOP ATOMAL information. It references security regulations C-I(71)27 and A7(73)883 for the handling of SIOP information, and instructs military and civilian staff, as well as members of Delegations and Representatives from capitals, to ensure they are appropriately cleared for access to extremely sensitive information documents. This is to avoid difficulties in the issuance of passes for the Ministerial Meeting.

ACCESS TO SINGLE INTEGRATED OPERATIONAL PLAN (SIOP)
INFORMATION AND DOCUMENTS
Note by the Secretary
1. The Agenda for the Meeting of NPG Ministers in Brussels next month will
contain, under Item III EMPLOYMENT POLICY (COSMIC TOP SECRET – ATOMAL), a
briefing by the United States Secretary of Defense on the SINGLE INTEGRATED
OPERATIONAL PLAN (SIOP).

2. Persons who are authorised to receive ATOMAL information are not necessarily
also authorised to receive SIOP information. All persons attending the Meeting
of NPG Ministers next month will require to be authorised for access both to
ATOMAL and SIOP ATOMAL information.

3. C-I(71)27, Amox paragraph 3(d), and the Security Regulations for the
International Staff, A7(73)883, Section II, lay down special procedures for the
handling of SIOP information. In particular, these documents require that
COSMIC/ATOMAL control officers shall prepare lists of persons authorised for
access to SIOP extremely sensitive information documents.

4. Military and civilian staff who will be attending this meeting are therefore
requested to ensure that they are appropriately cleared as soon as possible so
as to avoid difficulties in the issue of passes for the Ministerial Meeting.

5. This requirement will also apply to members of Delegations and
Representatives from capitals participating in the meeting.

Brussels.
(Signed) M.H.G. WILSON
