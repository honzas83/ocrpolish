> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 26th February, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on February 26, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with a welcome to Mr. Allen L. Keiswetter from the United States Department of State. The group discussed the preparation of a report on Exercise WINTEX 73, specifically focusing on high-lighting points for Ministerial discussion regarding nuclear consultation procedures and facilities, while coordinating with the Council Operations and Exercise Committee (COEC). The group also reviewed the latest version of the draft report on the ACLANT Area Follow-on Study, incorporating changes from the Military Committee and awaiting further instructions for the United States representative. It was agreed that a meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives would be held on April 16, 1973, to approve working papers for the NPG Ministerial Agenda for May 15 and 16. The Chairman announced plans to distribute draft papers on the Follow-on Study Programme, the Ankara Meeting agenda, and the North Norway Illustrative Study. Finally, the group scheduled its next meeting for March 5, 1973, and decided not to meet on March 12 due to WINTEX 73.

WELCOME TO A VISITOR
1. The STAFF GROUP:
endorsed the welcome extended to Mr. Allen L. Keiswetter from the United States
Department of State, who attended the meeting.

EXERCISE WINTEX 73
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that it had been generally
agreed that the Staff Group might wish to draft an NPG Permanent
Representatives' Report containing some preliminary observations on WINTEX 73,
as a contribution to any Ministerial discussion which may take place in Ankara;
he referred to the International Staff's note(1) on this subject and drew
attention to the five topics of interest to the Nuclear planning Group which
were listed in paragraph 3 of the note; he suggested that the Staff Group would
have to depend mainly on its own resources to gather material that might go into
the proposed Permanent Representatives' report; he welcomed the presence of
Colonel Vincent, Director, Council Operations, and invited his comments;

(b) took note of the remarks of the Director, Council Operations who said that
the Council Operations and Exercise Committee was responsible for preparing, as
soon as possible after an exercise in which the NATO

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced

NEXT MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

approving the various working papers which, as NPG
documents, would be on the NPG Ministerial Agenda
for 15th and 16th May.

Programme;

(b) a draft agenda for the Ankara Meeting;
