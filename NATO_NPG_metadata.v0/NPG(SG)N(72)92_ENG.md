> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday 16th October, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting in Brussels on October 16, 1972, to address several key administrative and strategic items. The group took note of the documentation issued for the NPG Ministerial Agenda, including documents 11 through 15 and a document on the implementation of ADM Guidelines. the group discussed the necessity of and potential agenda items for the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) meeting, noting general agreement that such a meeting should be held in December as part of the pattern of Ministerial meetings. Regarding the agenda for the Spring meeting of NPG Ministers in May 1973, the group proposed the inclusion of the ACLANT Study and the continuation of Strategic Briefings. They also noted that Military Authorities were preparing papers on current operational concepts, doctrine, and the combined use of nuclear weapons. The group agreed that an Illustrative Study would not be necessary for the agenda. Finally, the group discussed logistical arrangements for the upcoming NPG Ministerial Meeting in London, including a reception at Admiralty House and a Ladies Programme. The next meeting of the Staff Group was scheduled for October 23, 1972.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday 16th
October, 1972

DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who confirmed that all the documents
referred to in the NPG Ministerial Agenda were now issued ; he referred in
particular to the NPG documents numbered 11 through 15 ; in addition, the
document number 16 on the implementation of ADM Guidelines (not on the
Ministerial Agenda) had also been issued.

MEETING OF THE NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE (NDAC)
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to the basic charter
for NATO Nuclear Affairs and recalled that for the last five years the NDAC had
normally met in December at the same time as the DPC and Council ; he asked for
comments on the need to hold a meeting this coming December and on the matters
which might be placed on the Agenda ;

(b) took note of the views – mostly given on a personal basis – expressed during
the ensuing discussion;

(c) noted that there appeared to be general agreement, subject to confirmation
from Capitals, on the following points:

(i) the NDAC meeting was a natural part of the pattern of Ministerial meetings
held each December;

(ii) the NDAC meeting brought together the Ministers of all those NATO countries

AGENDA FOR THE SPRING MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who proposed that the Staff Group
should give early consideration to the composition of the agenda of the NPG
Ministerial Meeting to be held in May 1973; he outlined the various possible
items, and invited comments;

(b) noted the firm hope of all members that the series of Strategic Briefings
begun by Secretary Laird would be continued;

(c) agreed that the last of the follow-on studies - the ACLANT Study which would
be circulated shortly - should feature on the agenda;

(d) recalled that the Military Authorities were preparing, in response to the PO
on Unresolved Problems(1), two papers(2) which if they were ready in time, would
provide a substantive addition to the agenda; noted the remarks of the
Representative of the International Military Staff who said that it was intended
to prepare Military Committee comments on the two papers in question and that if
these papers were received very early in January 1973, it should be possible to
send both the papers and the Military Committee comments to the Staff Group in
February;

(1) PO/71/226, 10th May, 1971
(2) Current operational concepts and doctrine; military advantages and
disadvantages of early combined use of nuclear weapons.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN LONDON
4. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the United Kingdom Representative who made the
following points regarding the arrangements for the NPG London Meeting:

(a) the reception at Admiralty House at 6-8 p.m. on Wednesday, 25th October,
(not for wives) would be in informal dress;

(b) the Ladies Programme on Thursday, 26th October, would consist of a visit to
West Wyckham Park, followed by a lunch at Lord Carrington's country home;

(c) the final programme and delegation lists would be circulated in the NATO
Headquarters during the next few days.

NEXT MEETING
5. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 23rd October, 1972, at 3 p.m.
