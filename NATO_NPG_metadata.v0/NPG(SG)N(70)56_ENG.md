> # Documents of Possible Interest to Follow-on Use Study Teams
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) has issued a notice to follow-on use study teams regarding two documents of interest mentioned in the 1969 Annual Progress Report of the SHAPE Technical Center. These publications focus on the employment and effectiveness of Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs) and the calculation of nuclear casualty rates. The first document, a December 1968 report by L.F. Jones et al., describes a war gaming study using computer-assisted techniques to investigate the effectiveness and military significance of ADMs. The second document, a March 1969 report by R. Gye and M.D.F. Boultom, defines and examines factors involved in calculating nuclear casualty rates for infantry battalions or armoured regiments based on weapon yield and protection levels. Team members interested in obtaining copies of these documents are advised to request them through their respective National Military Representatives (NMRs) at SHAPE. The notice was signed by H.B. Seim in Brussels, Belgium.

ABSTRACTS
A Study on the Employment and Effectiveness of Atomic Demolition Munitions (NU)
L.F. Jones, R. Goad, M. Karavelioglu, K.G. Müller
December 1968
ABSTRACT: This report describes a war gaming study that was conducted to
investigate the effectiveness of Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs) using a
computer-assisted technique developed recently at STC. Concepts for the use of
ADMs, their military significance and quantitative effects are discussed in
detail. An account of the assessment procedures used for determining the
outcomes of interactions between opposing forces is also included.

TM-215
The Calculation of Nuclear Casualty Rates (NU)
R. Gye, M.D.F. Boultom
March 1969
ABSTRACT: Factors involved in the calculation of nuclear casualty rates are
defined and examined. The casualties to be expected in an infantry battalion or
armoured regiment are calculated for weapons yield of one to one-hundred
kilotons. It is found that simple estimates of casualties can be made which
depend on the weapon yield and on the type of protection which the target troops
have.
