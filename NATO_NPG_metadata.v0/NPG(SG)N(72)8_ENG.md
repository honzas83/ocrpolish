> [!abstract] 
> # Possible Staff Group Visit to the United States
> 
> This document consists of a note by Chairman H.B. Seim and two annexes containing correspondence between the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group and the United States. In a letter dated 8 December 1971, H.B. Seim requested a visit to the United States for approximately 30-35 members of the Staff Group to familiarize them with elements of the U.S. strategic nuclear posture relating to NATO security. The proposed itinerary included briefings in Washington, Omaha at the Strategic Air Command (SAC) Headquarters, a Minuteman site, the North-American Air Defence Command (NORAD) Headquarters, a missile submarine port, and a SAC bomber base. In a reply dated 19 January 1972, David M. Clinard, Director of the Nuclear Planning & Special Projects Division of the U.S. Delegation to NATO, confirmed that the United States would be pleased to receive the visit, although they could not provide funds for expenses. The U.S. authorities suggested a modified itinerary including Washington, Omaha, and potentially Norfolk, Virginia, at the SACLANT Headquarters. The correspondence emphasizes the need for participants to arrange trans-Atlantic transportation and notes that a smaller group size would increase the likelihood of securing military transportation within the United States.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
POSSIBLE STAFF GROUP VISIT TO THE UNITED STATES
Note by the Chairman

As a result of Staff Group discussion at our meeting on 3rd December, 1971 of a
possible visit to the United States(1), I sent a query to the U.S. authorities

reopen our discussion of this subject at our next meeting. For easy reference,
the texts of my letter and Mr. Clinard's response are included at Annex.

Nuclear Planning & Special Projects Division,
U.S. Delegation to NATO
Dear Dave,
In the past, the Nuclear Planning Group Staff Group has made a number of visits
to various headquarters and field installations in Europe for orientation
purposes. Members have commented on the value of such visits and have expressed
the hope that additional ones could be arranged. In this context, the Staff
Group has, on several occasions, indicated a strong interest in visiting the
United States. As you know, I had the opportunity to discuss this matter
informally with Captain Hanks and Colonel Crandall while I was in Washington in
September and again during the recent NPG Ministerial Meeting in Brussels in
October. They indicated that they would be willing to explore the possibility of
such a visit but would like to have some specific idea of what we had in mind.

Therefore, I would be grateful if you could pass the following information to
your Washington colleagues for their use in seeing whether a visit can be
arranged:

(a) Purpose of the Visit. To familiarise Staff Group members with elements of
the U.S. strategic nuclear posture relating to NATO security; to provide an
opportunity for exchange of views between U.S. planners and Staff Group members.

(b) Scope of Visit. To Washington for briefing on command and control
arrangements and discussions with U.S. planners; to Omaha for briefing on U.S.
strategic forces at SAC Headquarters and a briefing by the SACEUR Representative
with JSTPS; to a Minuteman site; to North-American Air Defence Command
Headquarters (NORAD) for briefing; to a port to visit a missile submarine; to a
SAC bomber base.

(c) Duration of Visit. About 10 days.

(d) Timing of Visit. During the period 12th-30th June, but could extend into the
first two weeks in July.

(e) Participation. About 30-35, which would allow about two persons per
Delegation, IMS, SHAPE, SACLANT and IS. No wives.

(f) Transportation. By scheduled official airlift (German, Canadian or U.S.)
between Europe and North America and by U.S. official airlift within the U.S.,
if possible.

(g) Accommodations. As arranged by the U.S.
(h) Security Clearance. All participants cleared for CTS ATOMAL.
The foregoing points are offered as suggestions only, and are not intended to
limit the flexibility of the U.S. authorities in working out details of a
projected visit.

Since the Staff Group may be able to avail itself of German or Canadian official
airlift for the trans-Atlantic legs, I am forwarding copies of this letter to
Howard Morgan and Rolf Thyes for their information, recognising that any further
exploration of these airlift possibilities would depend on whether the U.S.
authorities are able to invite us for a visit.

Sincerely,
H.D. SEIM.

19th January, 1972.
Mr. H.B. Seim,
International Staff,
North Atlantic Treaty Organisation,
1110 Brussels, Belgium.
Dear Harv:
This is in reply to your letter of 8 December 1971,
concerning a possible visit to the United States by the NPG
Staff Group.
I have conveyed the text of your letter to my authorities
in washington, who have now authorised us to reply as follows:
1. The United States would be pleased to receive a visit
from members of the Staff Group; we regret that we
cannot, however, provide funds to defer expenses
connected with the visit.
2. My authorities will attempt to arrange military
transportation within the United States. It should
be understood, however, that if such transportation
is not available, individual Staff Group members, or
their governments, must be prepared to pay for
3. The suggested itinerary for the group includes one or
more days in Washington for briefings, plus time for
discussions with their own embassy staffs, and two
days each at Headquarters SAC in Omaha, Nebraska, and
(subject to course to the agreement of that Headquarters)
at Headquarters SACLANT in Norfolk, Virginia, for
briefings and orientation.
4. Any reduction in the size of the group would increase
the chances of arranging military transportation
within the United States.
Finally, my authorities have suggested that, if the Staff
Group wishes to pursue the matter, it should proceed to arrange trans-
Atlantic transportation so that consideration can be given to a detailed
itinerary.

I am sending copies of this letter to our colleagues on the
Staff Group for their information.
Best regards.
Sincerely,
David M. Clinard,
Director, Nuclear Planning and Special Projects Division
