> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 9th April, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on April 9, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to coordinate preparations for a Ministerial Meeting in Ankara. The group discussed and reviewed several working papers, including the draft agenda for Ankara (NPG/WP(73)3), the report on the ACLANT Follow-on Use Study (NPG/WP(73)1), and the report on the Follow-on Use Programme Status Report (NPG/WP(73)2). They agreed to drop Item V from the draft agenda due to the lack of a military paper on operational concepts and doctrine, and to include the Illustrative Study on Demonstrative Use. The group also noted that the SACEUR Demonstrative Use Study would be included on the Ankara agenda, but SACEUR would provide an informal presentation rather than a formal one. Additionally, they addressed the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives scheduled for April 16, 1973, and agreed to cancel the May 3rd meeting. The group reviewed reports on WINTEX 73 (NPG/WP(73)5) and the Demonstrative Use Illustrative Study (NPG/WP(73)4), requesting corrigenda for these documents. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next Staff Group meeting on April 26, 1973.

reports required for the Ministerial Meeting;
(b) recalled that a further meeting of Permanent Representatives had been
scheduled for Thursday,

3rd May with a view to holding a final exchange of views before the Ankara
meeting and to hearing a

preliminary run-through of a presentation by SACEUR on the Illustrative Study;

(c) took note of a statement by the SHAPE representative who said that a review
of SHAPE's commitments through the busy months of April and May had shown that
it would be extremely difficult to develop a complete formal presentation of the
SHAPE Demonstrative Use Study for the NPG in the short time remaining; further,
it would not be possible to have such a presentation ready for a preview by NPG
Permanent Representatives on 3rd May; however, since it was now apparent that
the SACEUR Demonstrative Use Study would be included on the Ankara NPG Agenda,
SACEUR would make an informal presentation with introductory comments designed
to explain the Study and to set the stage for Ministerial discussion;

(d) noted that the Secretary General would be unable to chair a meeting on the
afternoon of 3rd May; noted also the difficulty of finding another suitable time
and date in early May;

(e) agreed that a final exchange of views might take place on 16th April, and
that, subject to the agreement of the Permanent Representatives of those NPG
countries not attending the Ankara Meeting, it should be possible to cancel the
3rd May meeting.

REVIEW OF WORKING PAPERS
DRAFT AGENDA FOR ANKARA – NPG/WP(73)3
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed, in the light of the non-availability of the military paper on
operational concepts and doctrine, to drop Item V from the draft agenda;

(b) agreed to include the Illustrative Study on Demonstrative Use, taking note
of the remarks of the SHAPE representative mentioned in paragraph 2(c) above;

(c) agreed that, the content of the agenda now being agreed, it would not be
necessary to burden the Permanent Representatives' agenda for 16th April with
this matter;
