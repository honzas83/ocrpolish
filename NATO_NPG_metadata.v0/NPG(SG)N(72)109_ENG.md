> # Notes of a Meeting Held in NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 27th November, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on November 27, 1972, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The group took note of several papers regarding the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) agenda, nuclear planning activities in 1972, and the evolution of NPG membership. Discussions focused on the exercise of nuclear consultation procedures during the WINTEX-73 exercise, with the United Kingdom representative noting that their authorities wished to test newly-amended nuclear procedures. The group also addressed the preparations for the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) Ministerial Meeting scheduled for December 6, 1972, including invitations for SACEUR, SACLANT, and CINCHAN. Additionally, the group discussed the ACLANT Area Phase I Follow-on Study, noting a movement in favor of a presentation to Ministers and requesting the International Staff to prepare a draft report. The meeting concluded with the probable dates for the Spring NPG Ministerial in Turkey (May 15-16, 1973) and the scheduling of the next Staff Group meetings on December 11, 1972, and January 8, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 27th
November, 1972

DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to a number of
papers issued in connection with the work of the NPG and NDAC:

(a) NDAC/A(72)1, dated 22nd November, 1972: the agenda for the meeting of the
NDAC in Ministerial Session to be held on 6th December, 1972;

(b) NDAC/D(72)1, dated 28th November, 1972: the report by the NPG to the NDAC on
nuclear planning activities in 1972;

(c) NDAC/D(72)2, dated 17th November, 1972: Updating of nuclear planning
directives (for NDAC agenda);

(d) DFC/D(72)34, dated 17th November, 1972: Updating of nuclear planning
directives (for DFC agenda);

(e) NPG/D(72)18, dated 23rd November, 1972: the Agreed Minute of the London
Meeting, (less paragraph 31 — Remarks by the United States Secretary of
Defense);

(f) NPG(Staff Group)N(72)104, dated 14th November, 1972: Senior Officers courses
at Oberammergau, Germany;

(g) NPG(Staff Group)N(72)108, dated 22nd November, 1972: Evolution of NPG
Membership.

EXERCISE OF CONSULTATION PROCEDURES IN WINTEX-73
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he was very hopeful
that the Director, Council Operations would give a talk at the next meeting of
the Staff Group(1), on the exercise of nuclear consultation procedures in WINTEX
73;

(b) noted a statement by the United Kingdom Representative who referred to the
wish of his authorities to test their newly-amended nuclear procedures during
WINTEX 73, and said that such testing would involve no deviation from existing
procedures.

MEETING OF THE NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE (NDAC) ON 6TH DECEMBER
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who said that letters of invitation
to the NDAC Ministerial Meeting on 6th December for SACEUR, SACLANT and CINCHAN
had been prepared for signature by the Secretary General; the Chairman of the
Military Committee had a standing invitation;

(b) concurred with the view of the Chairman who said that he expected that, as
in the past, a draft decision sheet would be circulated on the Silence Procedure
after the NDAC meeting, and would be issued in final form after a deadline date.

ACLANT AREA PHASE I FOLLOW-ON STUDY
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussion held in
the Staff Group at its last meeting on the question of whether a presentation
should be given to NPG Ministers at the Spring Meeting prior to the discussion
of the ACLANT Study; he said that it would be helpful to the Study Team if they
could be told if a presentation was required; he invited further comments;

(b) noted, in the ensuing exchange of views, a movement in favour of a
presentation to Ministers; agreed to seek instructions and to decide upon the
matter at the next meeting;

(c) noted that the ACLANT Study would be distributed in the very near future,
and invited the International Staff to prepare a first draft of a report upon

DATES OF THE SPRING NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING

5. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who said that it now appeared probable that
the dates for the Spring NPG Ministerial in Turkey would be Tuesday and
Wednesday, 15th and 16th May, 1973.

NEXT MEETINGS OF THE STAFF GROUP

6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) decided not to meet on Monday 4th December, in view of the Ministerial
Meetings taking place that week;

(b) agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 11th December, 1972, at 3 p.m.;
(c) agreed that, subject to no unexpected problems arising in the meantime, the
following meeting of the Staff Group would take place on Monday, 8th January,
1973.
