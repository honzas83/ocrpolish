> # Initiation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme
> 
> This document is a draft report by the Permanent Representatives to the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministers regarding the initiation and implementation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme. The Work Programme is divided into three phases: Phase I involves the preparation of basic data inputs and studies; Phase II focuses on the comparative analysis of these inputs, synthesis of findings, and identification of policy questions; and Phase III involves the formulation of proposed political guidelines for follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. The report notes that while Phase I studies are expected to be completed by the end of 1972, a new study on the air battle in the Central Region is still pending. To maintain momentum, it is recommended that Phase II be initiated in Spring 1972, leading to a provisional report in 1973. The document outlines a proposed organizational structure for Phase II, suggesting the formation of an ad hoc study team composed of participants from the United States, the United Kingdom, and Germany, with the United States acting as the discussion leader. This team would prepare a provisional report while allowing other countries and NATO Military Authorities to submit views and comments on the draft. The Permanent Representatives would then have the opportunity to exchange views on the report before it is presented for Ministerial consideration.

Purpose
This report submits for Ministerial consideration certain proposals for the
initiation and implementation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme,
in response to the request made by the Ministers at their meeting in Brussels on
26th and 27th October, 1971(1).

Discussion
2. One of the primary objectives of the Work Programme is to provide, through
its exploration of the implications and interactions of follow-on tactical use
of nuclear weapons, a basic for the Ministers to develop policy proposals, as
appropriate, for submission to the Defence Planning Committee. In this context,
the NPG has agreed that the Work Programme will consist of three major parts,
namely, Phase I (preparation of basic data inputs in the form of studies and
related comments from the MDAC countries and the NATO Military authorities);
Phase II (comparative analysis of the basic data inputs, synthesis of findings
and identification of the policy questions); and Phase III (formulation, as
appropriate, of proposed political guidelines for follow-on tactical use of
nuclear weapons for MEAC and DPC approval)(2).

6. It is, however, important to initiate the Phase II work as early as possible
both to enable Ministerial discussion of the provisional Phase II report in the
course of 1973 and to sustain the momentum of the programme as a whole. It is
therefore recommended that a decision to initiate the Phase II work be taken by
the Ministers at the Spring 1972 meeting. This would enable steps to be taken
during; the Summer of 1972 to work out the details of the study effort and a
start to be made on comparative analysis. However, it should be appreciated that
completion of the provisional Phase II report could not, in any event, occur
until after Ministerial consideration of the Phase I studies on their Autumn
1972 agenda. By the same token, no interim report on the Phase II effort should
be expected for the Autumn 1972 meeting. To facilitate the preparation of a
provisional Phase II report, all concerned should make every effort to ensure
the timely introduction of Phase I basic data by, for example, submitting
comments on Phase I studies as soon as possible after they have been considered
at Ministerial level.

7. Organisation for Phase II. The arrangements to be followed in carrying out
Phase II must take into account a number of considerations, some of which are
conflicting. An important factor is the need to provide full opportunity to all
countries who wish to do so to make national inputs to the Phase II work and to
contribute to the final report before it is placed on the Ministerial agenda.
Those countries not participating at Ministerial level should also be given a
chance to make known any particular national views that they feel should be
taken into account during the Ministerial discussion. In this regard, it will be
important to ensure that the Permanent Representatives are involved fully at all
stages. Another consideration is the requirement to avoid the creation of an
analytical group that is so large and unwieldy that it becomes inefficient.
Furthermore, it is desirable to involve participants from national capitals and
to facilitate a meaningful

11. Finally, the question might be raised whether it is necessary to define the
objective of the Phase II effort more precisely than has been done in previously
approved documents(1). While it might be argued that the provision of more
specific terms of reference for the study team might be helpful, there is a
danger that too much detail might unduly restrict the flexibility that should be
granted to those who will undertake the task. Furthermore, it can be expected
that all who participate in the Phase II work will be well apprised of the
overall objectives of the Work Programme, and that they will seek to shape their
efforts to be responsive to those objectives. On balance, it would seem that no
additional instructions are needed at this time. Nevertheless, in light of the
significance which all countries attach to the Phase II work, it should be noted
that the means already exist for additional guidance to be given to the study
team by the Permanent Representatives at a later date by virtue of their general
responsibility to monitor on a continuing basis the Work Programme and the
specific studies undertaken therein(2).

from the United States, the United Kingdom and Germany, with the United States
