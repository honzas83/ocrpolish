> # Implementation of Special Adm Guidelines
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated 29th March 1974. It addresses the implementation of Special Political Guidelines for the possible use of Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs). The Chairman notes that NPG Ministers in 1970 assigned the Permanent Representatives a continuing task of monitoring progress in implementing these guidelines. Reports were prepared in 1971, 1972, and 1973, with the last report indicating that further progress could be made. Consequently, the Chairman suggests that the Permanent Representatives should conduct a review of the implementation status toward the end of 1974. He further suggests that if this review is to proceed, the Secretary General should alert the Chairman of the Military Committee to ensure that input from NATO Military Authorities would be required by 1st September 1974. The matter is scheduled for discussion by the Staff Group at its meeting on Monday, 8th April.

NOTICE
NPG(Staff Group)N(74)35
NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
IMPLEMENTATION OF SPECIAL ADM GUIDELINES
Note by the Chairman
Staff Group members will recall that the NPG Ministers in 1970 assigned(1) to
the Permanent Representatives a continuing task of monitoring the progress being
made in implementing the Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of
Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs) (2). In fulfillment of this responsibility,
the Permanent Representatives prepared reports in 1971(3), 1972(4) and 1973(5),
the last one of which indicated that further progress could be made in the
implementation process. This raises the question of whether the Permanent
Representatives should make a review of the status of implementation toward the
end of 1974. If this possibility cannot be ruled out, it would be appropriate
for the Secretary General to alert the Chairman of the Military Committee now
that an input from the NATO Military Authorities would be required later this
year, say, by 1st September.

2. It is intended that the Staff Group discuss this matter at its meeting on
Monday, 8th April.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM.
NATO, 1110 Brussels.
