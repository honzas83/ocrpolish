> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 19th July, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on July 19, 1971, to address several key agenda items. The group agreed on amendments to documents NPG/WP(71)4 and NPG/WP(71)5, requesting the International Staff to issue corrigenda for the NPG Ambassadors' consideration. The meeting also discussed the scheduling of the NPG Ministerial Meeting in autumn 1971, noting that the United States Representative was awaiting confirmation of the dates October 26th and 27th, and that IBERIANT ceremonies would be rescheduled to avoid conflicts. the group noted a statement from the Turkish Representative regarding the distribution of a Turkish study and a preference to delay its presentation to Ministers until Spring 1972. Additionally, the group established that meetings would be held as necessary in August, with weekly meetings resuming on September 6, 1971, and scheduled the next meeting for July 26, 1971. The session concluded with farewells to Dr. W.F. van Eekelen of the Netherlands and Lt.Col. C.R. Baker of Canada, and a welcome to the new Greek Representative, Lt.Col. Venetanos.

STUDIES OF FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS – PROCEDURAL ARRANGEMENTS

1. The STAFF GROUP:
having discussed, and agreed a number of amendments to, document NPG/WP(71)4,

by the NPG Ambassadors on 22nd July, 1971.

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES AND EXAMPLES OF RELEASE REQUESTS – PRESENTATION AT THE

2. The STAFF GROUP:
having discussed, and agreed a number of amendments to, document NPG/WP(71)5,
invited the International Staff to issue a Corrigendum in time for consideration
by the NPG Ambassadors on 22nd July, 1971.

DATES OF THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN AUTUMN 1971
3. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that:
(a) the United States Representative was still awaiting confirmation from his
Authorities that 26th and 27th October, 1971, would be suitable dates;

(b) the IBERIANT Ceremonies would be rescheduled so as not to clash with the NPG
Ministerial meeting.

REGIONAL STUDIES - TURKISH STUDY

4. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Turkish Representative- that:
(a) the Turkish study would be distributed by the end of the following week;
(b) the Turkish Authorities would prefer to delay its presentation to Ministers
until the Spring of 1972. when the Turkish Minister would be present; and to
give the presentation to the NPG Ambassadors during the second half of January
or the first half of February 1972.

NEXT MEETINGS

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed that meetings would be held as necessary during August, the normal
routine of weekly meetings being resumed from 6th September, 1971;

(b) agreed to hold the next meeting on Monday, 26th July, 1971, at 3 p.m.

VALEDICTORY

6. The STAFF GROUP:
bid farewell to Dr. W.F. van Eekelen (Netherlands) and to Lt.Col. C.R. Baker
(Canada).

WELCOME

7. The STAFF GROUP:
welcomed the new Greek Representative, Lt.Col. Venetanos.
