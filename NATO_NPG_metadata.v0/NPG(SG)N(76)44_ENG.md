> # Downgrading or Destruction of Selected NPG Documents
> 
> This document is a notice from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) regarding the authorized downgrading of several NATO documents. Following a review of documents issued between 1966 and 1971, the NPG countries agreed to downgrade a number of papers. The Head of the NATO Registry has already notified holders of the authorized downgrading. The notice further identifies additional drafts and working papers that may also be downgraded, which are listed in the attached annexes. Staff Group members are encouraged to verify that their copies of these documents have been appropriately downgraded and are suggested to destroy copies of documents they no longer need. The annexes provide a detailed list of documents, including their original classification levels and their new, lower classifications, such as moving from NATO SECRET to NATO CONFIDENTIAL or NATO UNCLASSIFIED. Mentioned documents include those related to follow-on use studies, ministerial agendas, and reports to the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) and the North Atlantic Council (NDAC).
> 
> ## Categories/Topics
> 
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Extended-Deterrence-and-Tactical-Nuclear-Sharing — The document lists numerous papers related to 'Follow-on Use Studies' and 'Follow-on Tactical Use' (e.g., NPG/WP(69)1, NPG/WP(70)3, NPG(SG)N(71)2), which are central to the mechanisms of tactical nuclear sharing and the deployment of nuclear weapons in Europe.
> #Internal-Alliance-Dynamics/Command-and-Control-Authority — The document references a specific paper on 'Nuclear Release Procedures' (NPG(SG)N(71)35), which directly pertains to the command and control mechanisms for authorizing the use of nuclear weapons.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
DOWNGRADING OR DESTRUCTION OF SELECTED NPG DOCUMENTS
Note by the Chairman
As a result of a recent review of NPG documents issued in 1966, 1969, 1970 and
1971, it was proposed(1) that a number of documents be downgraded, to which the
NPG countries subsequently agreed. Holders of the documents were then
notified(2) of the authorised downgrading by the Head of the NATO Registry.

2. As a related matter, a review of the various drafts and working papers
circulated in connection with the preparation of the NPG documents, for which a
downgrading action was authorised, has been undertaken to identify additional
papers that may also be downgraded. These documents are listed at Annex,
together with the revised classifications that have been authorised by the
originators. It is expected that the Head of the NATO Registry will also notify
holders of these documents of this downgrading action.

3. Staff Group members may wish to verify that copies of the documents listed in
Annexes to NPG/N(75)1 and to this Notice, which may be in their possession, have
been appropriately downgraded. It is also suggested that Staff Group members may
wish to destroy copies of these documents for which they have no further need.

(Signed) R.E. SHEARER
110 Brussels.
(1) NPG/N(75)1, 17th February, 1975
(2) DN(76)10, 3rd March, 1976
