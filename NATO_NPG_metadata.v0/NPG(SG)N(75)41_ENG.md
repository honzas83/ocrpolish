> # Future Work Programme
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), details a future work programme intended for circulation prior to the Nuclear Theatre Committee (NTC) ministerial meeting in Monterey, California, in June 1975. The programme includes the initiation of Phase III of the Follow-on Use Work Programme to consolidate NATO's concept for the use of nuclear weapons. It addresses the implications of technological developments in guidance systems and the impact of tactical nuclear weapons research and development by the United States. It further discusses the following-up to consultations on United States Public Law requirements, specifically the Military Procurement authorisation act of 1975, and the necessary theatre nuclear force posture improvements. The document also suggests the use of illustrative studies to promote dialogue between political and military authorities on the initial and follow-on use of nuclear weapons. Additionally, it mentions the need to explore the implications of NATO classification for nuclear consultation traffic and the development of a compilation of nuclear consultation procedures. Finally, it mentions the monitoring of the implementation of Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of Atomic Detonation Munitions.

As in the past, the Secretary General intends to circulate, prior to the NTC
ministerial meeting, in Monterey, California, in June 1975, a list dealing with
future NTC activities. To facilitate discussion of this matter in the Staff
Group, and to assist in the preparation of the PO, there is attached a list of
subjects related to work which is currently underway or might flow from
ministerial action in Monterey, or which has been approved by ministers but is
still to be initiated.

2. This list of subjects is not intended to cover only the Autumn 1975
ministerial meeting. Obviously, a longer lealtime is required before a subject
has reached the stage where it is ready for ministerial consideration, and it is
very difficult to predict precisely the particular meeting for which a report or
study will be available. Staff Group members are invited to comment and, if they
deem necessary, to add to the list of topics at our meeting in the week of 20th
May, 1975.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME

1. Follow-on Use Work Programme. With Phases I and II of the work in this
programme nearing completion, the draft Permanent Representatives' report on
Phase II(1) recommends the initiation of Phase III through a study programme
which would compile all basic NFC policy documents along with other on-going NPG
work(2) into one consolidated statement of NATO's concept for the use of nuclear
weapons. It is not intended that the Phase III Study would supersede the
Provisional Political Guidelines, however it might show where existing guidance
should be expanded to take full account of follow-on use. More definite
proposals by the Permanent Representatives for the initiation and implementation
of Phase III should be considered by ministers during their autumn 1975 meeting
if this general approach is acceptable to them. Assessments with respect to the
magnitude of this work, or the time-frame needed, can only be given after the
Permanent Representatives' further examination, but at least one year will be
required.

5. Illustrative Studies. Illustrative study presentations at NPG Ministerial
meetings have, in the past, been useful in promoting a dialogue between
political authorities and the NATO military authorities on the crucial matter of
initial use of nuclear weapons. Current developments in the Follow-on Use Work
Programme (and the course of the VENIX 75 exercise as well) have identified the
relative value of initial use, as long as the implications and consequences of
follow-on use are not likewise considered. The illustrative study approach may
therefore be useful to highlight the difficulties incumbent in the relationship
between initial and follow-on use so that the implicit dilemmas can be exposed.

6. Such an illustrative study effort could be particularly valuable in
