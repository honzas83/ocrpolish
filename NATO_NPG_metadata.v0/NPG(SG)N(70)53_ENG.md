> # Notes of a Meeting Held on 12th June, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on June 12, 1970, to address several key items. The group unanimously approved a statement of thanks to the Italian Representative for the organization of the Ministerial meeting at Venice. Regarding documentation, the group agreed that basic documentation should be provided to Portugal as a member of the Nuclear Defence Advisory Committee (NDAC) and that specific NPG documents from the 1970 series should be distributed to Portugal in a custody manner. Additionally, Portugal was granted access to SHAPE studies and associated ATOMAL documents. The group noted that the Secretary General would request comments on the draft concept from SACEUR and SACLANT, and that the Military Committee would be invited to examine questions regarding helicopters and communications for atomic demolition munitions. The group also discussed the future work program, agreeing that guidelines for atomic demolition munitions should be concise and prepared as an Annex to provisional political guidelines, with a final paper expected by late September or early October. Furthermore, the group recommended that comments on the Draft Concept be distributed to all members of the Group. It was noted that SACEUR would brief the Military Committee on the revision of his Nuclear Strike Plans on June 24, and a briefing for Permanent Representatives on the General Strike Plan (GSP) and release procedures would be scheduled after the summer holidays. The group also acknowledged the possibility of providing assistance to the Executive Working Group on alliance defence problems for the 1970s. Finally, the group noted upcoming departures of representatives and scheduled the next meeting for June 29, 1970.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (ST. FF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on 12th June, 1970

VENICE MEETING

1. The STAFF GROUP:
unanimously approved a statement by the Chairman in which he thanked the Italian
Representative for the excellent organization and generous hospitality which had
characterised the Ministerial meeting at Venice, and requested the Italian
Representative to convey the appreciation of the Staff Group to his Authorities.

MATTERS RISING FROM THE VENICE AGREED MINUTE (1)

A. DOCUMENTATION (Agreed Minute, paragraph 6)

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that basic documentation should be given to Portugal as a member of
the NDAC;

(b) agreed that the NPG documents, numbers 1 to 5 in the 1970 series, should be
distributed in the custody manner to Portugal;

(c) agreed that Portugal could have access, if desired, to the SHAPE studies and
to the associated ATOMAL documents, retained in the custody of the International
Staff.

B. COMMENTS ON THE DRAFT CONCEPT (Agreed Minute, paragraph 7)
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that the Secretary General would be requesting, through the Chairman
of the Military Committee, comments from SACEUR and SACLANT;

(b) noted a statement by the representative of the Military Committee who said
that action had already been taken on this matter.

C. ATOMIC DEMOLITION MUNITIONS (Agreed Minute, paragraph 14)
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that the Secretary General would be requesting the Chairman of the
Military Committee to invite the NATO Military Authorities to examine the
question of helicopters and of communications as set out in sub-paragraphs 14(a)
and 14(b).

D. S'CEUR'S COMMENTS ON THE DRAFT CONCEPT AND THE ADM REPORT
5. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that comments made by SACEUR at Venice would be distributed to the Group
on Monday, 15th June.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME
A. ADMs
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed that the ADM guidelines should be as concise as possible, and should
be prepared in the form of an Annex to the provisional political guidelines;

(b) agreed that the first draft should be available for discussion at the end of
June, that a revised draft should be available for capitals by the end of July,
and that the subject should be ready for discussion by Permanent Representatives
early in September;

B. DRAFT CONCEPT
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recommended that comments on the Draft Concept should be sent not only to
the originators of the study, but also to all members of the Group;

(b) noted that the International Staff would be prepared to arrange for
reproduction and distribution of comments whenever this would be useful.

C. FOLLOW-ON STUDIES
8. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that for the moment no specific Staff Group action was required.

D. BRIEFING BY SACEUR
9. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that S:CEUR would be briefing the Military Committee on the revision
of his Nuclear Strike Plans on 24th June;

(b) agreed that it would be useful for Permanent Representatives to have a
briefing, in the context of their study of the Draft Concept, after the summer
holidays;

(c) agreed that this briefing should address the General Strike Plan (GSP) and
appropriate details on release procedures.

POSSIBLE ASSOCIATION OF NPG WORK WITH THE AD7O STUDY
10. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that it might be required, in due course, to provide assistance to the
Executive Working Group on alliance defence problems for the 1970s.

DEPARTURES

11. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) noted that at least nine representatives who regularly attended meetings of
the Staff Group would be leaving during the course of the summer;

(b) recalled that a cocktail party would be held in their honour on 25th June;
(c) agreed that a lunch party should bc held sometime during the week beginning
6th July at which there would be a further opportunity to say farewell.

NEXT MEETING

12. The STAFF GROUP:
