> # NPG Permanent Representatives' Meetings to Prepare for the Autumn 1972 NPG Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, outlining the schedule for preparatory meetings of the NPG Permanent Representatives in September and October 1972. These meetings are intended to prepare for the NPG Ministerial meeting to be held in the United Kingdom on October 26th and 27th, 1972. The scheduled dates include September 14th for a presentation by the Denmark Study Team, September 28th for the consideration and approval of draft reports to the Ministers, and October 12th for a preliminary review of the ACLANT Illustrative Study and a final exchange of views on Ministerial agenda topics. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, notes that these dates and topics are subject to modification based on future developments and that specific agendas for each meeting will be issued later.

NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES' MEETINGS

NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING
Note by the Chairman
At a recent meeting, the Staff Group agreed that it would probably be necessary
to schedule several meetings of the NPG Permanent Representatives in September
and early October to prepare for the NPG Ministerial meeting to be held in the
United Kingdom on 26th and 27th October, 1972. For planning purposes, the
following dates were earmarked:

Date        Purpose
Thursday, 14th September 3:30 pm    Presentation by the Denmark Study Team
Thursday, 28th September 3:30 pm    Consideration and approval of draft reports
to the Ministers

Thursday, 12th October 3:30 pm      Preliminary review of the ACLANT
Illustrative Study; final exchange of views on Ministerial agenda topics

2. Staff Group members may wish to note the foregoing dates on the activities
calendars of their respective Permanent Representatives, recognizing that the
dates and topics to be covered at the individual meetings are subject to
modification in the light of future developments. Specific agendas for each
meeting will be issued later.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM
NATO, 1110 Brussels.
