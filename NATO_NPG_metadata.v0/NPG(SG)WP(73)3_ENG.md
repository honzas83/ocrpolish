> # Draft Status Report on the Follow-on Use Work Programme
> 
> This document is a draft status report by the Permanent Representatives to the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministers on the progress of the Follow-on Use Work Programme. It outlines that Phase I of the programme, which involved studies on the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons in various regions including the Allied Command Atlantic (ACLANT) area, North Norway, Macedonia, Western Thrace, Central Europe (NORTHAG and CENTAG), Turkish Thrace, Northwestern Turkey, the Mediterranean Maritime area, and Denmark, has been completed. The report notes that 40 documents, including 9 studies and 19 comment papers from NATO Military Authorities, have been generated during Phase I. It further details that Phase II of the programme, involving a study team composed of representatives from the United States, the United Kingdom, and the Federal Republic of Germany, is currently underway, focusing on comparative analysis of basic data inputs and the synthesis of findings. The report concludes by recommending that the Ministers take note of the report and mentions that a status report from the Phase II Study Team as of 1st April 1973 is attached as an annex.

I. PURPOSE
This report summarises the present status of work in progress in the programme
for the study of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons(1).

II. BACKGROUND
2. At their meeting in November 1969, the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning
Group approved the Follow-on Use Work Programme and invited the Permanent
Representatives to make arrangements for the fulfilment of the initial work, to
monitor the work programme on a continuing basis and to propose further work as
appropriate(2).

In fulfilment of their monitoring function, the Permanent Representatives have
submitted a number of brief progress reports(3).

III. STATUS
3. By the end of 1972, all Studies envisaged for the initial phase of the Work
Programme(1) had been completed and circulated. The last of these Studies, which
examines implications of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons in the ACLANT
(Allied Command Atlantic) area(2), has been included on the Agenda for the
present meeting. Studies discussed at earlier Ministerial meetings covered
follow-on operations in different areas of Allied Command Europe(ACE): North
Norway(3); Macedonia and Western Thrace(4); NORTHAG(5) and CENTAG(6) areas of
Central European Command; Turkish Thrace and Northwestern Turkey(7); the
Mediterranean Maritime area(8); and Denmark(9). Another Study dealing with the
question of transition of NATO forces from a conventional posture to a nuclear
posture(10) was also addressed by the Ministers at an earlier meeting.

4. Comments on each of the nine Studies have been received from the NATO
Military Authorities. In addition, comments have been submitted by a number of
countries in response to the NPC Ministers' invitations(1) for contributions. A
total of 40 documents have been generated to date in Phase I of the Work
Programme: 9 Studies; 7 Reports by the Permanent Representatives on these
Studies; 5 additional national documents on Phase I; and 19 comment papers by
the NATO Military Authorities.

5. In implementation of the decision(2) by the Ministers last year to initiate
Phase II of the Follow-on Use Work Programme(3), an ad hoc study team, composed
of participants from the United States, the United Kingdom and the Federal
Republic of Germany, has been engaged during the past several months in carrying
out Phase II activity (comparative analysis of the basic data inputs, the
synthesis of findings and the identification of policy questions). A brief
report on the status of this work as of 1st April, 1973 has been received from
the Phase II Study Team and is attached at Annex.

6. The Permanent Representatives have no proposals to make at this time for
additional work within the context of the Follow-on Use Work Programme.

IV. RECOMMENDATION
7. The Ministers may wish to take note of this status report.
