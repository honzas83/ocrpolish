> # NATO Senior Officers Advanced Weapons Orientation Course
> 
> This document provides notice of the 1971 schedule for the NATO Senior Officers Advanced Weapons Orientation Course (IO-31) conducted by the NATO Weapons Systems Department, US Army School in Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarize senior NATO officers and key personnel in staff appointments with the major concepts of NATO weapons employment in Allied Command Europe. Eligibility is limited to officers of the rank of colonel and above, and civilian officials of equivalent grade, on a need-to-know basis, requiring a COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance. Instruction is provided in English or French. The course lasts one week from Monday through Friday. The document lists ten scheduled courses from February to November 1971. For those in national delegations interested in attending, quotas are obtained through the Nuclear Planning Directorate of the International Staff. Due to the same small class size of approximately 30 people and high demand, early application is advised. Further information or quota requests can be directed to Miss van Bellingham or Mr. Serio at the Nuclear Planning Directorate.

Note by the Chairman
The subject course (short title IO-31) is conducted by the NATO Weapons Systems
Department, US Army School, Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is
to familiarize senior NATO officers and key personnel in staff appointments with
the major concepts of NATO weapons employment in Allied Command Europe. Officers
of the rank of colonel and above and civilian officials of equivalent grade are
eligible to attend on a need-to-know basis. A COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance is
required. Instruction is in English or French. The course lasts one week –
Monday through Friday. The following courses are scheduled for 1971:

2. The course provides a general orientation and background that is considered
useful for those involved in NPG work; it may also be of interest to other
senior officials in national delegations. For those in national delegations
interested in attending, quotas for the courses are obtained through the Nuclear
Planning Directorate of the International Staff. The number of spaces in each
course is quite small (a total of about 30) and the demand is usually quite
heavy (from NATO commands, ministries in capitals and national delegations in
Brussels). It is advisable, therefore, to apply well in advance of the desired
course.

3. The Nuclear Planning Directorate would be glad to furnish further information
or to obtain quotas. Contact Miss van Bellingham, ext. 2028, or Mr. Serio, ext.
2048.
