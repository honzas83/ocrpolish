> # Atom. 3 Demolition Munitions – Changes to 'Working Paper(1)'
> 
> This document, issued by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, outlines proposed changes to a working paper on Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADMs). The amendments focus on refining the language regarding the distribution of analyses and the utility of ADMs in the defense of NATO territory. It suggests that Ministers should reach general conclusions on the use of ADMs and modify guidelines based on previous discussions in Venice and reports from Italy. The document further details the role of ADMs in fixed barriers in forward positions and their potential use in rear positions, emphasizing the limitation of aggressor freedom of action in suitable terrain. It also proposes improvements to selective release procedures for ADMs used as engineering demolition devices in pre-planned barriers, while maintaining normal procedures for other situations. Finally, it suggests the creation of a special ADM annex to the provisional guidelines paper to reflect Ministerial conclusions from SACEUR's studies and the Italian draft document.

2. Page 2, delete present paragraph 3 and substitute new paragraphs 3 and 4 as
follows:

"3. The foregoing analyses, together with the considerable number of documents
on ADMS produced earlier under NPG auspices(3), provide a solid point of
departure for a broad Ministerial discussion of basic ADM issues. In turn, as a
result of such discussion, Ministers may be in a position to reach general
conclusions concerning the utility of ADMS in the defence of N.TO territory and
to initiate measures to improve N.TO's defensive posture with respect to ADMS."

"4. Ministers may also recall that the definitive formulation of ADM guidelines
was deferred, pending the conclusion of these analyses, when the Italian
report(1) was discussed at London and when the provisional political guidelines
for the initial defensive tactical use of nuclear weapons by N.TO(2) were
approved at Tirle House. Ministers may now be in a position, in the light of
their discussion at Venice, to indicate to what extent the guidelines paper
insofar as ADMs are concerned should be modified, amplified or otherwise
augmented."
