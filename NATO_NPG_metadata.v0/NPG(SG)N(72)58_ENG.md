> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 19th June, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on June 19, 1972, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key administrative and strategic items. Regarding documentation, the group took note of a request for the Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic (SACLANT) to prepare an Illustrative Study for the next Ministerial meeting and noted a change in the presentation date for the Denmark Follow-on Use Study. The group also discussed the development of a report on Consultation Procedures and Facilities, aiming to complete a full draft by early July for capital reactions and a Working Paper by late August for Permanent Representatives. They agreed that the final report for Ministers should be concise, with detailed discussion texts placed in annexes, and that the report should be responsive to Ministerial concerns without infringing on the other groups' responsibilities. The group considered whether to wait for the VITTEX-73 exercise to gain experience with nuclear consultation before finalizing the report, but decided to proceed with drafting to meet the October Ministerial meeting deadline. They emphasized the importance of the views of Military Authorities and the collaboration of military representatives. Additionally, the group reviewed tentative plans for the NI Ministerial meeting in London on October 26-27, 1972, including the location at Lancaster House and social arrangements. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next meeting on June 26, 1972, which was later rescheduled for July 3.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 19th
June, 1972

DOCUMENTATION

1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to PO/72/345,
dated 12th June, 1972 in which the Secretary General requested for the Chairman,
Military Committee to arrange for SACLANT to prepare an Illustrative Study for
the next Ministerial meeting, and to NPG(Staff Group)N(72)56, dated 14th June,
1972 which recorded the dates that had been earmarked for Permanent
Representatives meetings in preparation for the Ministerial meeting ;

(b) took note of a statement by the Chairman that he had been informed by the
Danish Delegation that the Danish Authorities would prefer to give the
presentation on the Denmark Follow-on Use Study on 21st September vice 14th
September as indicated in the Notice.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES

2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the first draft(1)
of a report on Consultation Procedures and Facilities, parts of which were
incomplete ; he hoped that the Staff Group could complete a full draft by early
July which could be sent to capitals for initial reactions ; the Staff Group
could then prepare a Working Paper in late August for consideration by the
Permanent Representatives in the second half of September ;

(b) agreed that the final report for the Ministers should be short and that it
might be advisable to put any lengthy discussion texts at Annex ;

(c) agreed that, although several of the listed topics fell within the purview
of other groups, it was necessary for the Permanent Representatives' report to
cover them in order to be responsive to expressed Ministerial concerns ; care
should be taken, however, to avoid infringing on the responsibilities of other
groups ;

(d) noted that the Permanent Representatives' report might be a means for
generating Ministerial appreciation of, and support for, work on consultation
procedures and facilities now in progress under the aegis of other groups ;

(e) discussed whether it would be preferable to wait for the VITTEX-73 exercise
which will give some play and experience with nuclear consultation before
reviewing the Consultation Procedures and Facilities, but agreed to continue
discussion and drafting now with a view to completing a report for the Permanent
Representatives to submit to the Ministers for consideration at the next
Ministerial meeting in October ;

(f) agreed that it was very important to have the views of the Military
Authorities since agreed military command operating procedures might be affected
; expressed the hope that the military representatives participating in the
Staff Group activity would collaborate to the greatest possible extent in the
drafting stage ;

(g) invited the International Staff to circulate the remaining parts of the
draft and agreed to examine the full draft in detail at the next meeting.
