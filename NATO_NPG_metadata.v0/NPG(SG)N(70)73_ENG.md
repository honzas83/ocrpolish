> # Notes of a Meeting Held on 23rd September, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on 23rd September 1970 to address several key agenda items. Regarding the draft concept for the role of theatre nuclear strike forces in Allied Commands Europe (ACE), the Staff Group invited German and United States representatives to provide additional amendments to document NPG/D(70)6. The International Staff planned to circulate a revised text of this document by approximately 8th October 1970. Additionally, the group discussed provisional political guidelines, considering changes proposed by Denmark and a report from the Military Committee. The International Staff was tasked with drafting a working paper to serve as the basis for a report with recommendations for Ministers, incorporating Danish suggestions and the Military Committee's findings. The meeting also noted that a briefing on the nuclear role of STRIKEFORLAN would be place on 2nd October 1970. Finally, the group agreed to schedule its next meetings for 28th and 29th September 1970 in Brussels.

NOTICE

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on 23rd September, 1970
I. DRAFT CONCEPT FOR THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES IN ACE
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) invited the German and United States Representatives to convey to the
Discussion Leaders three possible additional amendments to document NPG/D(70)6
arising from points raised at the meeting of the NPG Permanent Representatives
the previous day;

(b) noted that the International Staff planned to circulate the revised text of
NPG/D(70)6, once it had been received from the Discussion Leaders, on or about
8th October, 1970.

II. PROVISIONAL POLITICAL GUIDELINES
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) at the conclusion of its discussion on the changes proposed by Denmark
(NPG/D(70)8) and the report from the Military Committee (NPG/D(70)7), invited
the International Staff to draft a working Paper, as a basis for a report with
recommendations from NPG Permanent Representatives to Ministers, reflecting the
Danish suggestions, the main issues emerging from the Military Committee report
and the staff Group’s discussion of them;

(b) agreed to consider the working Paper at its next meeting.
