> # Allied Command Europe (ACE) Orientation Course for Senior Officers
> 
> This document, issued by the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), outlines the details of the Allied Command Europe (ACE) Orientation Course (short title: IO-31) conducted by the NATO Weapons Systems Department at the USAREUR Combat Support Training Center in Oberammergau, Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarize senior NATO officers of the rank of Colonel and above, and civilian officials of equivalent grade, with the major concepts of NATO weapons employment in Allied Command Europe. A COSMIC TOP SECRET clearance is required for attendance, and instruction is provided in English or French. The course lasts one week and involves a tuition fee of approximately $90 per student. The document lists the scheduled courses for 1973, ranging from January to November. It further specifies that the course is useful for those involved in Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) work and other senior officials in delegations and capitals. Application processes differ based on location: officials in Brussels are to apply through the Nuclear Planning Directorate of the International Staff, while those in capitals and national commands should apply through their own Ministry of Defence. Due to limited space (approximately 30 per course), early application is at least a month in advance is recommended, and a 'Joining Report' must be submitted two weeks prior to commencement. Contact information for Mr. Serio and Miss Hydc is provided for further information or quotas.

Note by the Chairman
The subject course (short title: IO-31) is conducted by the NATO Weapons Systems
Department (IWSD), USAREUR Combat Support Training Center, Oberammergau,
Germany. The purpose of the course is to familiarise senior NATO officers and
key personnel in staff appointments with the major concepts of NATO weapons
employment in Allied Command Europe. Officers of the rank of Colonel and above
and civilian officials of equivalent grade are eligible to attend on a need-to-
know basis. A COSMIC TOF SECRET clearance is required. Instruction is in English
or French. The course lasts one week – Monday through Friday. A tuition fee of
approximately $90 per student is charged. The following courses are scheduled
for 1973:

2. The course provides a general orientation and background that is considered
useful for those involved in NPG work; it may also be of interest to other
senior officials in delegations and in capitals. Officials in national
delegations located at NATO Headquarters in Brussels who are interested in
attending one of the courses listed above should apply through the Nuclear
Planning Directorate of the International Staff which acts as the point of
contact with SHAPE and the NWS at Oberammergau for obtaining spaces for
personnel in delegations and the International Staff. Officials in capitals and
national commands should apply through their own Ministry of Defence.

3. The number of spaces in each course is limited to about 30 and the demand is
usually quite heavy. Only one or two spaces for each course are usually
allocated to NATO officials. Therefore, it is advisable to apply well in advance
of the desired course, i.e. at least a month before. This also allows time for
submission of the "Joining Report" which must be sent to NWS at least two weeks
prior to commencement of the course.

4. The Nuclear Planning Directorate would be glad to provide further information
or to obtain quotas. Contact Mr. Serio, ext. 2048 or Miss Hydc, ext. 2029.
