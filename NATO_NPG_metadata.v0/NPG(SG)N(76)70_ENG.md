> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 2nd August, 1976
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) convened at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on August 2, 1976. The meeting covered several key administrative and security clearances for members, including temporary representatives from Canada and Turkey. The group discussed the scheduling of the NPG Ministers' meeting in Edinburgh, with the United Kingdom representative suggesting November 23rd and 24th, 1976, and noting limitations on simultaneous interpretation cabins. The group also reviewed the status of the Compilation paper, noting a statement from the Chairman and a SHAPE letter, and addressed Belgian concerns regarding the extension of the silence procedure period. The meeting further addressed the release procedure messages undertaken by Military Authorities and the suggestion that Military Authorities hold preliminary discussions with the Staff Group on amendments to release messages. The Finally, the group discussed and agreed upon changes to the draft Terms of Reference for the NPG Study of levels of Nike Hercules missiles, incorporating input from the United States and the Military Committee. The International Staff was tasked with preparing a new draft based on these discussions. The date for the next meeting was left open.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 2nd
August, 1976.

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note that all persons present were cleared to the security classification
of the matters to be discussed.

WELCOME TO TEMPORARY MEMBERS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
concurred with the welcome extended to Colonel G. Olson, temporarily
representing Canada and Mr. A. Demirbag, temporarily representing Turkey.

AUTUMN MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the UK representative who said that the week
beginning 22nd November, 1976 was the only suitable period to hold the NPG
Ministers’ meeting in Edinburgh; his Authorities had a strong preference for
Tuesday 23rd and Wednesday, 24th November; he requested countries which had not
yet announced their preferences to do so as soon as possible;

(b) took note of the further remarks of the UK

representative who said that the size of the available conference room in
Edinburgh limited simultaneous interpretation possibilities to three cabins

COMPILATION

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that, at the last
meeting, it had discussed the latest version of the Compilation paper(1) and
that, if no comments had been received by close of business on 6th August, 1976,
the paper would be circulated as an NPG Working Paper for approval by Permanent
Representatives by the silence procedure on 20th August, 1976;

(b) took note of SHAPE letter 1450/20-2-3/76 dated 30th July, 1976 and agreed
with its content;

(c) took note of the views of the Belgian representative and of his indication
that the Belgian Authorities might wish to prolong the silence procedure period
for the Compilation paper;

(d) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who expressed his understanding of
the Belgian position and confirmed, with regard to the changes to release
procedure messages undertaken by the Military Authorities, that such action on
their part was consistent with the Guidelines; the NATO Military Authorities had
the authority to publish changes in the same way as countries had the right to
discuss such changes;

**TERMS OF REFERENCE FOR THE NIKE HERCULES STUDY**

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman and of an initial draft of Terms of
Reference for the NPG Study of levels of NIKE HERCULES; he recalled the initial
discussion on this subject held at the last meeting;

(b) discussed the form and content of the draft taking into account the United
States letter dated 15th July, 1976 and the Military Committee paper CMCM-78-76
dated 29th July, 1976 and its enclosed Programme of Work;

(c) agreed to a number of changes to the text and layout of the Draft Terms of
Reference and invited the International Staff, in the light of the views
expressed, to prepare and circulate with all speed a new draft based on the
discussion(1).

**NEXT MEETING**

6. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to leave open the date of its next meeting.

NATO, 1110 Brussels.
