> # Meeting of the NPC Standing Group
> 
> The NPC Standing Group convened to address several key agenda items. First, they noted a scheduled talk by Dr. R. P. D. Tiller on nuclear matters for Friday, 27th May 1977. They also discussed the upcoming meeting of NATO Nuclear Planning Committee (NPC) Permanent Representatives on 23rd May, noting that this was the last opportunity for non-participating countries to provide input on agenda items and highlighting the military importance of the New Technology Study. Additionally, the group addressed Theatre Nuclear Force Improvements, Theatre Nuclear Force Improvements, noting a draft of IPC/7(77)6 and agreeing to a procedure for its circulation as an NPC Document for Ministers, despite a lack of complete consensus. The meeting concluded by scheduling the next meeting for Tuesday, 24th May 1977, at 10:30 a.m. in Brussels, Belgium.

LECTURE BY DR. R. P. D. TILLER
3. The STAN. GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman who promised representatives that Dr.
Tiller would give a talk on nuclear matters on Friday, 27th May, 1977; he hoped
that members, and particularly those who were also members of the COEIC, would
be able to attend and would bring interested persons with a need-to-know.

MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES TO BE HELD ON
23RD MAY
4. The STAN. GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman concerning the meeting o NPC
Ambassadors on the following Monday and recalled that this was the last chance
for non-participating countries to make known their views on agenda items;

(b) took note of the views of the IIS representative who underlined the
importance from a military point of view of the New Technology Study.

THEATRU NUCLEAR FORCE IMPROVEMENTS
5. The STAN. GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to a draft of
IPC/7(77)6 circulated to members and expressed the hope that this paper, after
further discussion, could be issued with a cover letter explaining that one
country was unable to complete the consensus; he further hoped that Permanent
Representatives would be able to agree to the circulation of this Working Paper
in the form of an NPC Document for Ministers;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and agreed to a
number of changes;

(c) agreed to the procedure proposed by the Chairman and invited the
International Staff to take appropriate action.
