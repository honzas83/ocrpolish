> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday and Wednesday, 16th and 18th September, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held meetings on September 16 and 18, 1974, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The primary focus of the meetings was the urgent need to finalize several key documents, including the draft Study on Communication of NATO's Intention to use nuclear weapons (CNI), the draft report by Permanent Representatives on the CNI Study, and the draft report on the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Air Defence Use. The group noted that the United States representative was without instructions regarding the Illustrative Study. The Staff Group agreed to continue discussions on the form and content of the third revision of the Working Paper on the CNI Study and incorporated various suggestions and changes to the text. Consequently, the International Staff was tasked with preparing revised versions of the Main Text, Summary, and Annex on Procedural Matters for final consideration at the next meeting. The group concluded by scheduling their next meeting for Thursday, September 19, 1974.

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that all persons present were cleared to the level of the Security
classification of the matters to be discussed.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTION TO USE NUCLEAR WEAPONS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that it was becoming
increasingly urgent to finalize the following papers:

(i) the draft Study on CNI;
(ii) the draft report by Permanent Representatives on the CNI Study;
(iii) the draft report on the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Air Defence Use;
he proposed that the Staff Group should discuss the first two papers at this
meeting and the Illustrative Study at the next meeting;

(b) noted that the United States representative was without instructions on the
Illustrative Study;
