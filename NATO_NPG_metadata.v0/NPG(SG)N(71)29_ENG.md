> # US Proposal for Handling of Studies on Follow On Use
> 
> This document outlines a United States proposal for the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) to handle studies on the follow-on use of nuclear weapons. It suggests that preparing nations should present their own studies directly to Ministers at NPG meetings, accompanied by a paper highlighting the key issues and points for discussion. These papers would replace any issues or discussion papers prepared by the NPG Staff Group. The proposal further suggests that NPG countries may submit written comments on the studies after their presentation. Finally, it proposes the appointment of a special multi-national committee to evaluate and compare the results of various studies once all currently commissioned follow-on studies have been presented, which would serve as the basis for the eventual preparation of NPG doctrine or guidelines on follow-on use of nuclear weapons.

ANNEX to NPG(Staff Group)N(71)29

US PROPOSAL FOR HANDLING OF
STUDIES ON FOLLOW ON USE

1. Preparing nations should present their own studies to Ministers at NPG
meetings..

2. Such presentation should include the preparation and circulation, in advance
of the Ministerial meetings, of a paper highlighting the issues and points which
preparing nations feel are most important with respect to each study, and which
they believe are particularly deserving of discussion at Ministerial level. Such
papers would be in place of any issues or discussion papers prepared by the NPG
Staff Group with respect to the follow-on studies.

3. After individual studies are presented at Ministerial level, NPG countries
could, if they wished, submit written comments on the studies.

4. After all currently commissioned follow-on studies have been presented, the
NPG might appoint a special multi-national committee to evaluate and compare the
results of the various studies. The work of this committee could form the basis
for eventual preparation of NPG doctrine or guidelines on follow-on use of
nuclear weapons.
