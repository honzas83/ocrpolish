> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 28th January, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on January 28, 1974. The meeting's primary focus was the organization of the NPG Spring Ministerial Meeting, with Norway suggesting dates in June 1974 in either Bergen or Oslo. The Staff Group approved a revised draft report on the conceptual analysis of nuclear aspects of deterrence, subject to minor amendments, and requested the International Secretariat to circulate it as a Working Paper. Additionally, the United States assumed leadership of a study on the political and military implications of current technological developments. The group also noted updates on the Military Committee's report on nuclear consultation and the possibility of arranging briefings for Staff Group members on nuclear matters. Finally, the group agreed not to meet during the week of March 18, 1974, due to Exercise HILEX-6, and scheduled their next meeting for February 4, 1974. The document also includes a notice for the next meeting, NPG(Staff Group)N(74)13, which outlines the objectives for the February 4 meeting.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 28th
January, 1974

STAFF GROUP MEMBERSHIP

1. The STAFF GROUP:
welcomed Colonel R. Taymans of the Belgian Delegation and Mr. A. Banguoglu of
the Turkish Delegation who were attending a meeting of the Staff Group for the
first time.

NPG SPRING MINISTERIAL MEETING

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) was informed that the Norwegian Authorities were unable to organize the NPG
Spring Ministerial Meeting on 4th and 5th June and suggested that the meeting
take place preferably on 11th and 12th June in Bergen, or alternatively on 25th
and 26th June in Oslo;

(b) requested delegations to report these dates to their Authorities and
ascertain their preferences before the next meeting.

CONCEPTUAL ANALYSIS OF NUCLEAR ASPECTS OF DETERRENCE

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) approved, subject to a minor amendment to paragraph 5, the revised draft
report circulated by the International Secretariat in NPG(Staff
Group)WF(74)1(Revised);

IMPLICATIONS OF TECHNOLOGICAL DEVELOPMENTS
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) welcomed the willingness of the United States to assume the leadership of
the study on the political and military implications of current developments in
the technological field and noted that there was no desire on the part of any
other participating nation to undertake co-leadership;

(b) noted that the International Secretariat would draw up, for consideration by
the Staff Group, a draft report which might be submitted to NPG Permanent
Representatives for approval by the silence procedure, outlining the
arrangements for the pursuit of the study in the Ad Hoc Study Group.

THE ROLE OF THE MILITARY COMMITTEE IN NUCLEAR CONSULTATION
5. The STAFF GROUP:
was informed by the Military Committee Representative that the draft Military
Committee report had now been agreed by five nations.

STAFF GROUP BRIEFINGS
6. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that the Chairman would consult the NATO Military Authorities on the
possibility of arranging briefings for Staff Group Members on nuclear and
associated matters.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
NEXT MEETING
The next meeting of the Staff Group will take place on Monday, 4th February,
1974, at 3.0 p.m. The principal objects of this meeting will be:

(a) to discuss an initial draft(1) of a possible Permanent Representative's
report on arrangements for the Study of the Political and Military Implications
of Technological Developments;

(b) to continue preparations for the Spring NGP Ministerial meeting, including
discussion of dates.

(Signed) N.L.C. PARK
