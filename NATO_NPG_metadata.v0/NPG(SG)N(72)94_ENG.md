> # Transport of Classified Documents to and from London
> 
> The Secretary of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) has issued a notice regarding the transport of classified documents for the NPG Ministerial Meeting. Mr. Bittner, the Head of the NATO Cosmic Control Section, is traveling from Brussels to London via a Belgian military aircraft. Delegations that lack secure means of transporting their ATOMAL and COSMIC TOP SECRET documents are invited to use Mr. Bittner's services for transportation. Documents must be packaged, sealed, and assigned an identification number by the Delegation, with the authorized persons for withdrawal clearly stated on the cover. Packages should be kept to a minimum weight and delivered to Mr. Bittner in the NATO Cosmic Registry by the evening of Tuesday, 24th October. Mr. Bittner is expected to arrive at Northolt airport at 1645 hours and proceed to Lancaster House, where packages will be stored in the Guard Room in the Main Hall. For the return journey, delegations are requested to hand in their packages to Mr. Bittner or the Guard Room immediately following the end of the Meeting to facilitate his quick departure.

NPG(Staff Group)N(72)94
NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
TRANSPORT OF CLASSIFIED DOCUMENTS TO ANDFROM LONDON
Note by the Secretary
On the occasion of the NPG Ministerial Meeting, Mr. Bittner, the Head of the
NATO Cosmic Control Section, has been invited to travel from Brussels to London,
and return, in a Belgian military aircraft. Delegations who have no secure means
of carrying their classified documents, and in particular their ATOMAL and
COSMIC TOP SECRET documents, are invited to give such documents to Mr. Bittner
for transportation.

2. Documents should be packaged, sealed and given an identification number by
the Delegation. The name or names of the persons authorised to withdraw the
package from safe-keeping should be clearly stated on the cover.

3. Packages, which should be kept to a minimum weight, should be given to Mr.
Bittner in the NATO Cosmic Registry not later than the evening of Tuesday, 24th
October. Mr. Bittner is due to arrive at Northolt airport at 1645 hours, and
will therefore probably arrive at Lancaster House about 1800 hours, where he
will put all packages into safe-keeping in the Guard Room in the Main Hall.
Delegations or individuals may collect their packages when they wish that
evening, or the next morning.

4. For the return journey, since the Belgian military aircraft will be departing
shortly after the end of the Meeting, Delegations are requested to hand in their
packages to Mr. Bittner or to the Guard Room, without delay, so that Mr. Bittner
can leave quickly.

(Signed) M.H.G. WILSON
