> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 28th February, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on February 28, 1977, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with a welcome to Mr. Luc Crollen, the new assistant representative for Belgium. The group discussed the status of NPG work, noting the need to identify agenda items for the June 1977 meeting. Updates were provided on the NIKE HERCULES report and a Dual Capable Aircraft study, both of which were expected to be circulated shortly. The group also reviewed Theatre Information and Target Systems, noting contributions from the United States, Turkey, and Germany. A visit to field installations approved by the US Authorities was discussed, with representatives from the US, Germany, and SHAPE meeting to arrange the details. The group agreed on a minimum of two meetings for NPG Permanent Representatives between April 21 and May 31. Additionally, the group reviewed the Maritime Nuclear Warfare Study, agreeing to changes and requesting a new version of the report. Finally, the group confirmed the dates for a Full Study Panel meeting on March 28 and 29, and scheduled their next meeting for March 2, 1977.

report on the status of NPG work;

(b) noted in particular the need to identify possible items for the agenda of
the June 1977 meeting

----(paragraph 9 of the document) and the further work required of the NPG
(paragraph 10 of the document).

DUAL CAPABLE AIRCRAFT STUDY

4.  The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a report by the IMS representative who said that he hoped to

THEATRE INFORMATION AND TARGET SYSTEMS

5.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a report by the IMS representative who said that he had
received papers from US, Turkey and Germany;

(b) noted the remarks of the SHAPE representative who said that he hoped to be
able to provide a paper in the near future.

STAFF GROUP VISIT TO FIELD INSTALLATIONS

6.  The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that he had been informed by
the US Authorities that a visit by the Staff Group to field installations had
been approved; he invited representatives from the UC delegation, the German
delegation and SHAPE to meet in his office at the end of the afternoon to
discuss arrangements.

FORTHCOMING MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

7.  The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the difficulty of scheduling NPG Permanent Representatives'
meetings during the period leading up to the Spring Council and DPC Ministerial
Meetings;

(b) agreed that a minimum of two NPG Permanent Representatives' meetings should
be held some time during the period 21st April - 31st May.

MARITIME NUCLEAR WARFARE STUDY

8. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to the International
Staff Working Paper(1) and said that he assumed that all representatives had
received instructions from Capitals;

(b) discussed the Working Paper in detail and agreed to a number of changes;

(c) invited the International Staff, in the light of the views expressed, to
prepare with all speed a new version of the report including a cover letter and
a revised version of the Terms of reference;

(d) agreed to discuss the new version in two days' time.

PIT/MIT FULL PANEL MEETING

9. The STAFF GROUP:

took note of a statement by the US representative who confirmed that the dates
of 28th and 29th March were suitable to the Panel Chairman for a meeting of the
Full Study Panel.

NEXT MEETING

10. The STAFF GROUP:

agreed to hold its next meeting on Wednesday, 2nd March, 1977 at 10.30 a.m.
