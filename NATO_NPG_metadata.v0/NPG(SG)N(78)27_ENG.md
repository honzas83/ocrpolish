> # Comprehensive Report by the Executive Working Group on the Long Term Defence Programme
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) providing a draft paragraph 39, titled 'Theatre Nuclear Modernization', for the Comprehensive Report on the Long Term Defence Programme to be submitted to the Defence Planning Committee. The draft paragraph explains that the modernization of NATO's theatre nuclear force has been a priority for the NPG for several years, and that the Defence Planning Committee has authorized the NPG, acting as Task Force 10, to manage the requirements for this modernization. It further notes that NPG Ministers, following a meeting in Denmark, have requested the Secretary General and NPG Permanent Representatives to provide necessary information for the Defence Planning Committee and the Summit, while considering the sensitivity of the subject, public policy implications, and ongoing arms control efforts. The NPG report on theatre nuclear force modernization is being provided separately to the Defence Planning Committee.

COMPREHENSIVE REPORT BY THE EXECUTIVE WORKING GROUP
ON THE LONG TERM DEFENCE PROGRAMME

Note by the Chairman

At our meeting today I noted that I would provide you a draft paragraph 39 for
the Comprehensive Report on the Long Term Defence Programme to the Defence
Planning Committee. My suggested paragraph 39 is as follows:

"THEATRE NUCLEAR MODERNIZATION"

39. Modernization of NATO's theatre nuclear force has been a matter of special
concern to the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) for the past several years. In
recognition of this, the Defence Planning Committee authorized the NPG, in the
capacity of Task Force 10, to continue to deal with the requirements for the
modernization of the theatre nuclear force in accordance with existing NPG
practice and procedures. NPG Ministers addressed this matter at their recent
meeting in Denmark and invited the Secretary General and NPG Permanent
Representatives to arrange for the provision of the information required for the
Defence Planning Committee and the Summit, taking into account the particular
sensitivity of the subject matter, the related public policy implications, and
on-going arms control steps. (1) In keeping with Ministers' instruction, and in
accordance with arrangements for the Long Term Defence Programme, the NPG report
on theatre nuclear force modernization is being provided separately to the
Defence Planning Committee.

(1) NFG/D(76)5, 20th April, 1978, paragraph 19"

(Signed) R.E. SHEARER

NATO, 1110 Brussels.
