> # Preparation of the December Ministerial Meetings of the NDAC and DPC
> 
> This document, a note by the Vice-Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, outlines the preparation for the December 1970 ministerial meetings of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee (NDAC) and the Defence Planning Committee (DPC). It specifies that three main proposals—the Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE, changes to the Provisional Political Guidelines, and the Special ADM Guidelines—will be served as separate agenda items. To avoid proliferation of documents and time constraints, the Vice-Chairman proposes that most documents be issued as 'Note by the Chairman' to bypass time-consuming capital approvals. The document includes several annexes: a draft agenda for the NDAC meeting, a report by the NPG to the NDAC, and draft notes by the Chairman for both the NDAC and DPC regarding the Concept, Provisional Guidelines, and Special ADM Guidelines. The report by the NPG summarizes activities in 1970, including ministerial meetings in Venice and Ottawa, and mentions the ongoing studies on the tactical use of nuclear weapons. The mentioned states involved in these activities include Canada, Germany, Greece, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, Turkey, the United Kingdom, and the United States, as well as Belgium and Denmark. The document is signed by R. Braband and Manlio Brosio.

recommendations on which the NDAC and DPC are requested to act.

distribution of the various documents. To alleviate this problem, it is proposed

NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
Report by the Nuclear Planning Group
This report summarises the general activities of the Nuclear Planning Group
during 1970, in order to provide a background to the discussions of the Nuclear
Defence Affairs Committee on 2nd December, 1970.

2. The Nuclear Planning Group has met twice at Ministerial level: in Venice on
8th and 9th June, and in Ottawa on 29th and 30th October. The next Ministerial
Meeting is scheduled to take place in Germany in the spring of 1971.

3. Countries represented at the Ministerial meetings during 1970 were Canada,
Germany, Greece(1), Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, Turkey(1), the United
Kingdom and the United States. All countries mentioned above, as well as Belgium
and Denmark participated in Nuclear Planning Group activities at the Permanent
Representatives and staff levels.

4. The decisions taken by the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group at their
meetings in Venice and Ottawa have already been circulated(2)(3). Basic
documentation discussed by the Ministers at these two meetings has also been
distributed separately.

(1) Turkey was present only in Venice and Greece only in Ottawa, in accordance
with a special bilateral agreement.

NUCLEAR DEFENCE AFFAIRS COMMITTEE
THE ROLE OF THEATRE NUCLEAR STRIKE FORCES IN ACE
Note by the Chairman
At their meeting in Ottawa, the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group
considered and approved a paper prepared by Germany and the United States
setting forth a "Draft Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in
ACE"(1).

2. The Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group recommend that the Nuclear
Defence Affairs Committee:

(a) endorse the "Concept for the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces in ACE";
(b) submit the document to the Defence Planning Committee for final approval as
policy guidance for national authorities and as a directive to the NATO Military
Authorities;

(c) recommend that the Defence Planning Committee, if it adopts the Concept,
invite the NATO Military Authorities:

(i) to continue to adjust their plans to conform with the approved Concept;
(ii) to report progress to the Nuclear Planning Group by 31st March, 1971;
(iii) to make suggestions, as appropriate, for further clarification or
refinement of the Concept.

(Signed) Manlio BROSIO
