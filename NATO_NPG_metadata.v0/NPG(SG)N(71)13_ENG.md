> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 25th January, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on January 25, 1971, to address several key agenda items. The group discussed briefings for Exercise WINTEX 71 to be provided by the IMS Nuclear Policy Team, noting that these were for study purposes rather than exercise input. They reviewed revised arrangements for staff officers serving the Chairman of the Military Committee and NATO Commanders at ministerial meetings. The group also planned briefings for the Defence Planning Committee (DPC) and NPG Permanent Representatives, including a session on selective release procedures and a joint Canadian/Norwegian study on North Norway. They addressed the layout and circulation of studies on the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons, emphasizing the need to avoid over-classification. Regarding the North Norway Study, the group agreed that regional studies are specialized and requested a paper identifying principal issues for the next meeting. Additionally, the group noted arrangements for a visit to SHAPE and discussed national participation in military nuclear planning following a statement by the Italian Representative. Finally, they considered the circulation of the text of SACEUR's General Strike Plan briefing and scheduled their next meeting for February 1, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 25th January, 1971
STAFF GROUP BRIEFINGS DURING WINTEX 71
1. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to Notice
NPG(Staff Group)N(71)12 dated 25th January in which were set out the times and
places for three briefings on Exercise WINTEX 71 to be given to the Staff Group
by the IMS Nuclear Policy Team on 28th January, 1st February and 2nd February;

(b) noted that these briefings were not designed to provide any exercise in-put
but should be viewed as part of the continuing Staff Group programme of study of
nuclear policy questions; participation should in principle be limited to
members of the Staff Group.

STAFF SUPPORT FOR MILITARY OBSERVERS AT MINISTERIAL MEETINGS
2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the revised
arrangements set out in NPG(Staff Group)N(71)9 for the attendance of a Staff
officer to serve jointly the Chairman of the Military Committee and the major
NATO Commanders who may be invited to attend NPG or NDAC ministerial meetings;

(b) confirmed the agreement of their Authorities to the arrangements set out in
the Annex to the above mentioned note;

(c) noted a statement by the Chairman who said that he would submit the draft of
a "PO" on this matter to the Secretary General for circulation to all concerned.

BRIEFINGS FOR THE DPC AND NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
3. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted that a briefing for the Defence Planning Committee Permanent
Representative on selective release procedures was being planned for Monday 22nd
February;

(b) noted that a letter would be sent by the Secretary General to the Chairman
of the Military Committee requesting him to make appropriate arrangements with
SACEUR for this briefing;

(c) noted that a briefing by a joint Canadian/Norwegian team for the Nuclear
Planning Group Permanent Representatives on the subject of the North Norway
Study was planned for Thursday, 4th March at 3.30 p.m.; a letter concerning the
detailed arrangements for this briefing would be circulated in due course.

STUDIES ON FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
A. General matters affecting all studies
4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to a notice of
the Staff Group to the notice NPG(Staff Group)N(71)11 concerning the layout,
reproduction and circulation of the studies in the Follow-on series;

(b) noted the advice of the Chairman regarding ATOMAL information, and to the
need avoid over-classification;

(c) noted that the studies would be circulated on a restricted distribution
list, similar, with the exception of some minor changes, to the list at annex
*to* NPG(Staff Group)N(71)2.

B. The North Norway Study
5. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that the time had come to
consider how individual studies, and the studies as a whole, should be
approached; he reminded the Staff Group that Ministers had charged NPG Permanent
Representatives with the fulfilment of the initial work, with monitoring the
work programme and specific studies, and with proposing further work as
appropriate; the Staff Group should identify in each study the principal points
for discussion by ministers, and
