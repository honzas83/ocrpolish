> [!abstract] 
> # Availability of Documents in the NPG/Study Series
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) has issued a notice regarding the availability of spare copies of documents from the NPG/Study series, specifically those associated with the Follow-on Use Work Programme. The NATO Registry is holding these documents and will distribute them to NPG participants on a first-come, first-served basis. Staff Group members are requested to notify the Chairman or the Secretary of their requirements for additional copies by Friday, 2nd February, 1973. Any remaining copies after this date will be authorized for destruction. The document includes a comprehensive list of 20 available documents, covering studies on North Norway, Macedonia, Western Thrace, the CEINTAG area, Turkish Thrace, northwestern Turkey, the Mediterranean Maritime area, the ACLANT area, Denmark, and various transition and casualty analysis papers from Canada and the Netherlands.
> 
> ## Categories/Topics
> 
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Nuclear-Strategy-and-Strike-Planning — The document lists numerous 'Follow-on Use' studies and 'Letter/yield code' documents, which are directly related to target selection, yield calculations, and the execution of nuclear strike planning.
> #Internal-Alliance-Dynamics/Civil-Emergency-Planning — The document references 'Canadian Study Paper 72-3: Scenario and Options' and specifically 'Canadian Study Paper 72-1: Analysis of Civilian Casualty Aspects', which falls under the resilience and impact assessment of civilian infrastructure and populations.
