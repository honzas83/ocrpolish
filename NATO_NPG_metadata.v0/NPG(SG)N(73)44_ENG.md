> [!abstract] 
> # Distribution of Basic Documentation to Portugal
> 
> This document, issued by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) on May 29, 1973, proposes the transmission of a series of basic documents to Portugal to comply with established procedures. The documents listed for transmission include the Agreed Minute NPG/D(73)7, studies on the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons in the ACLANT area, and various reports from Permanent Representatives, SHAPE, and SACLANT. Specifically, it includes the Permanent Representatives' Report on the ACLANT Study (NPG/D(73)1), the SHAPE Review of the ACLANT Study (NPG/Study/38), and the SACLANT Comments on the ACLANT Study (LPG/Study/39), as well as the Chairman of the Military Committee's comments. It also proposes sending Canadian contributions to Phase II work, including analyses of civilian casualty aspects of NORTHAG and CENTAG studies, observations on the time factor, and a note on scenario and options. The Staff Group is intended to consider this proposed distribution at its meeting on June 4, 1973, in Brussels.

Note by the Chairman
To comply with the established procedure, it is proposed that copies of the
following basic documents, discussed by the Nuclear Planning Group Ministers at
their meeting in ANKARA on 15th and 16th May, 1973, be transmitted to Portugal:

(a) Agreed Minute, NPG/D(73)7
(b) Studies of Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons – ACLANT Area,
NPG/Study/19

(c) Permanent Representatives' Report on the ACLANT Study, NPG/D(73)1
(d) SHAPE Review of the ACLANT Study, NPG/Study/38
(e) SACLANT Comments on the ACLANT Study, NPG/Study/39
(f) Chairman, Military Committee Comments on the ACLANT Study, NPG/Study/40
(g) Permanent Representatives' Status Report on the Follow-on Use Work
Programme, NPG/D(73)2

(h) Permanent Representatives' Report on Consultation Procedures and Facilities
– Preliminary Observations on WINTEX 73, NPG/D(73)3

(i) Permanent Representatives' Report on the SHAPE Illustrative Study,
NPG/D(73)4

(Note: The Study itself has already been distributed to Portugal.)
