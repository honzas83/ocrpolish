> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Thursday, 1st July, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on July 1, 1971, to address several key administrative and strategic planning matters. The group discussed the dates for the autumn 1971 NPG Ministerial Meeting, noting that October 26th and 27th were the only suitable dates for the German representative's Minister, and the meeting would be place in the Palais d'Émont in Brussels, Belgium. Additionally, the group reviewed a presentation on illustrative studies and examples of release requests for Ministers, agreeing that a final version of the paper should be prepared by the International Staff and that a revised version should be as a NPG working paper. The meeting also noted the nuclear aspects of AD 70 Follow-Up Action and unresolved problems as priority items for early discussion. Finally, the group reviewed the status of regional studies, noting that the CENTAG Area Study was expected by August 1, 1971, and that the Turkish Study had been received, while the Mediterranean and Transition Studies were well advanced. The next meeting was scheduled for July 5, 1971, though it was subsequently postponed to July 12, 1971.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held on Thursday, 1st July, 1971
DATES OF THE NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN AUTUMN 1971
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that, at its last meeting, the dates of 17th/18th November had been
proposed for the NPG Ministerial Meeting in the autumn;

(b) noted a statement by the German Representative who said that he had now
learned from his Authorities that the only suitable dates for his Minister were
26th/27th October, 1971;

(c) noted that these dates appeared to be convenient for all concerned, with the
exception of SAQLANT, and invited members to obtain confirmation from their
Authorities;

(d) took note of a statement by the Chairman who emphasised the need to reach an
early decision on this matter in order to help the Belgian Authorities;

(e) took note of a statement by the Belgian Representative who said that the
meeting would take place in the Palais d'Émont in Brussels.

ILLUSTRATIVE STUDIES AND EXAMPLES OF RELEASE REQUESTS - PRESENTATION TO
MINISTERS

2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that Ministers at the
Mittenwald Meeting had asked for a presentation on the subject of illustrative
studies and examples of release requests to be given to them at their meeting in
the autumn; he drew attention to the International Staff's paper(1) and invited
comments;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the subsequent discussion and noted a
consensus on the following points:

(1) the general lines of the International Staff paper were acceptable;
(ii) a final version of the paper should be agreed upon as soon as possible so
as to give SACEUR plenty of time to prepare his presentation;

(iii) as much flexibility as possible should be given to SACEUR in his choice of
scenario; however, an illustrative study on battlefield use would be preferred;

(iv) in preparing the Agenda for Ministers ample time should be allotted to the
presentation and subsequent discussion;

(v) there is no need to decide now whether there should be a discussion leader
for this item;

(vi) emphasis should be placed on the Major NATO Commanders' strategic
justification and upon the political implications of the illustrative study in
the context of the provisional guidelines;

(c) invited the International Staff in the light of the views expressed to
prepare a revised version of the paper in the form of a NPG working paper with a
cover letter by the Secretary General in his capacity as Chairman of the NPG;

(d) agreed that when reactions had been received from capitals on the working
paper the Staff Group should incorporate any required changes and decide whether
it was necessary for Permanent Representatives to meet or whether the paper
could be sent out as a NPG document without further discussion.
