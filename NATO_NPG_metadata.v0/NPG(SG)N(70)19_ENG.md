> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 16th March, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on March 16, 1970, to coordinate various nuclear planning activities. The group noted the upcoming NATO nuclear briefings for the Military Committee on March 23, 1970. They discussed the distribution of the first report by SACEUR on IDIS and the status of administrative studies. A work timetable was established to manage the busy period leading up to the Ministerial meeting, including scheduling meetings for Permanent Representatives in April and May. The group agreed that current nuclear planning activities did not require a meeting of the Nuclear Defence Affairs Committee or the Defence Planning Committee. The draft agenda for the NPG Ministers meeting scheduled for June 8-9, 1970, was reviewed and revised. Additionally, the group addressed selective release procedures and requested further discussion with experts from SHAPE. Finally, the group expressed grave concern over a leak of information regarding nuclear planning activities to a public newsheet and requested the International Staff to take action.

NATO NUCLEAR BRIEFINGS FOR THE MILITARY COMMITTEES
1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the Staff Group had been
invited to the next nuclear briefing for the Military Committee, which would
take place on Monday, 23rd March, 1970 at 9.30 a.m. and 2.30 p.m.

STATUS OF ADM STUDIES UNDER SACEUR SPONSORSHIP.
2. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who said that he had been informed that the
first report by SACEUR on IDIS would be distributed, on a basis of two copies
per Delegation, about 19th March and the remainder about 24th March.

WORK TIMETABLE
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman regarding the need for a work
timetable for the very busy period leading up to the Ministerial meeting;

(b) noted that Permanent Representatives would be heavily burdened during this
period, and especially during the latter half of May and early June;
