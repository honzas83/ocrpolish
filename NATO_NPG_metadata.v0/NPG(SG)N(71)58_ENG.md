> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 10th May, 1971
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on May 10, 1971, to review several key documents, including reports on regional studies for follow-on tactical use and the concept for theatre nuclear strike forces. The group discussed the agenda for the upcoming Mittenwald meeting and the Secretary General's views on unresolved nuclear field problems. They confirmed that an Advance Press Release would be issued in Brussels on May 19, 1971. Additionally, the group scheduled a visit to Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE) for June 16, 1971. Regarding the AD 70s Study, the group noted a draft report to Ministers by the Defence Planning Committee (DPC) and planned for preliminary work to be conducted by the Staff Group before a meeting of Permanent Representatives in June. The representatives of Italy and the Netherlands emphasized the role of the Staff Group in exchanging information and holding informal discussions on nuclear matters. Finally, the group coordinated logistics for the Mittenwald meeting, including the transport of classified documents via the NATO Cosmic Registry, and scheduled their next meeting for June 7, 1971.

CURRENT PAPERS AND DOCUMENTS
1. The STAFF GROUP :
noted a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the following documents
:

(a) NPG/D(71)3, dated 7th May, 1971. This was the Permanent Representatives
report to Ministers on the three regional studies so far received on follow-on
tactical use;

(b) NPG/D(71)5. This document, which is due to be circulated on 11th May, is the
Permanent Representatives report on the points raised in the Military
Committee's report (1) on the Concept for theatre nuclear strike forces (2);

(c) NPG/A(71)5(Revised), dated 7th May, 1971. This is the Agenda for the
Mittenwald meeting, revised to take into account the inclusion of the report by
Permanent Representatives referred to in (b) above;

(d) PO/71/226. This document, which is expected to be issued on 11th May, sets
out the Secretary General's views on various important but unresolved problems
in the nuclear field.

ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE BEFORE THE MITTENWALD MEETING
2. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that no objections having
been received to the proposed text and time of issue of the Advance Press
Release (1), it would go out in Brussels, as planned, on 19th May 1971, at 3
p.m., local time;

(b) noted a request by the Chairman that those countries who intended to make
simultaneous releases should inform him, or NATO Press Service;

NPG STAFF GROUP VISIT TO SHAPE
3. The STAFF GROUP : noted a statement by the Chairman who said that he had
received confirmation from SHAPE that Wednesday, 16th June, 1971 would be a
suitable day for the visit of the Staff Group, and that a flexible programme
would be prepared on the lines of that proposed at the Staff Group’s meeting
held on 28th April.

AD 70s STUDY – FOLLOW-ON WORK
4. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the draft report to
Ministers by the Defence Planning Committee (2) on the subject of follow-on work
in the AD 70s Study; noted that paragraph 55 of this draft report recalled that
fundamental questions needing attention in the nuclear field would fall within
the purview of the Nuclear Planning Group;

(b) recalled that the Secretary General, at the meeting of DPC Permanent
Representatives held on 24th March, had proposed that the Nuclear Planning Group
Permanent Representatives should meet to discuss the matter at a suitable moment
in June (probably towards the end of the month);

(c) recalled also that the Secretary General had suggested that the Staff Group
might do some preliminary work of this problem;

(d) noted that the International Staff would prepare and circulate a relevant
paper for discussion at the first meeting at the Staff Group to take place after
the return from Mittenwald.

INFORMAL EXCHANGES OF INFORMATION AND DISCUSSIONS IN THE STAFF GROUP
5. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) approved the remarks of the Italian and Netherlands representatives who
recalled that one of the r6les of the Staff Group was to exchange information on
a wide range of nuclear matters both practical and academic, and to hold
informal discussions in which ideas might be put forward without in any way
engaging the responsibility or requiring the approval of national authorities;

(b) noted a statement by the Chairman who said that as soon as the pressure of
current work had slackened it would be both possible and most desirable for the
Staff Group to discuss on an informal basis a number of matters not directly
connected with present studies; the visit to SHAPE for example would provide an
opportunity for such a discussion.

ARRANGEMENTS FOR THE MITTENWALD MEETING
6. The STAFF GROUP :
(a) noted a statement by the German Representative who said that a full list of
members of Delegations would be circulated at Mittenwald, and that he proposed
to send out a simplified advance list for the information of members of the
Staff Group during the course of the next few days;

(b) noted that transport for classified documents from Brussels to Mittenwald
and return was being arranged by the International Staff; documents for
transportation should be handed in to the NATO Cosmic Registry not later than 6
p.m. on Wednesday 19th May.

NEXT MEETING
7. The STAFF GROUP : agreed to hold its next meeting on Monday, 7th June, 1971,
at 3 p.m.
