> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels on Friday, 14th March, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on March 14, 1975, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The group discussed the nuclear aspects of Exercise WINTEX 75, including the timing of initial use at sea, communicating NATO's intentions, and the role of the Military Committee in the consultation process. The Turkish representative expressed regret over Turkey's inability to play a full part in the exercise and pledged future participation. The International Staff was tasked with preparing a first draft report on the lessons learned from WINTEX 75. Additionally, the group addressed the preparation of a Permanent Representatives' report on Phase II and the documentation for the upcoming Spring Ministerial Meeting, including the Ministerial Agenda and Advance Press Release. Logistics for the Spring Meeting in Monterey, United States, including air transport and accommodation requirements, were also discussed. The Belgian representative suggested increasing the number of persons per delegation seated at the NPG Ministerial meeting. Finally, the group noted the next meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives on March 18, 1975, which will include a briefing by Mr. Donald Cotter on the nuclear aspects of the Nunn Amendment, and scheduled their next Staff Group meeting for March 21, 1975.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels on Friday, 14th
March, 1975

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that all persons present were cleared to the security classification of
matters to be discussed.

EXERCISE VINTEX 75
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who said that, in company with the
Director of Council Operations, he will be attending SACEUR's post-exercise
discussion on 14th April; he hoped, when speaking at that meeting to be able to
give a brief summary of the report which he felt was necessary on the nuclear
aspects of WINTEX 75; he invited comments;

(b) took note of a statement by the Turkish representative who expressed his
regret that his authorities had been unable to play a full part in WINTEX 75;
they intended to participate fully in future exercises; he agreed to circulate
copies of his statement to members;

(c) noted, in the ensuing discussion, the views of members concerning the
exercise and the lessons to be drawn from it;

(d) noted particular interest in the following matters:

PHASE II REPORT – PREPARATION OF A PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES' REPORT

noted the remarks of the Chairman who said that a draft Permanent
Representatives' report on the Phase II report was ready for discussion and he
hoped that the Staff Group would provide guidance on this at its next meeting;
he said that it was particularly important to decide upon the relationship
between the Phase II report and future action regarding Phase III.

Arrangements for the Spring Meeting

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a request by the International Staff for information on

Seating Arrangements at NPG Ministerial Meeting

6. The STAFF GROUP:
tool note of the remarks of the 'belgian represent-ative who recalled his
suggestion at a previous meeting of the Staff Group concerning the possibility
of more than the customary five persons per delegation being seated at NPG
Ministerial meeting; agreed to discuss this at the next meeting.

NEXT MEETING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES

7. The STAFF GROUP:
noted that the next meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives would take place on
Tuesday, 18th March, 1975 at 11 a.m.; a briefing by Mr. Donald Cotter, Assistant
to the Secretary of Defence for Atomic Energy, on the nuclear aspects of the
Nunn Amendment, would be given.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP

8. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Friday, 21st March, 1975 at 10 a.m. with a
view to discussing the ministerial agenda, the Advance Press Release, the Future
Work Programme and the draft report on 'INTEX 75.'

NATO, 1110 Brussels.
