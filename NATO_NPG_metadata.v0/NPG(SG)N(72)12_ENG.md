> [!abstract] 
> # Destruction of Classified Papers
> 
> This document, issued by the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) on February 2, 1972, recommends the destruction of classified papers listed in the annex. In accordance with NATO Security Regulations, the recipients are advised to destroy any copies of these papers in their possession. These papers were used in the preparation of Nuclear Planning Group Advisory Committee (NDAC) documents in 1968, for which security downgrading action has already been approved. The annex lists several working papers for destruction, including DP/n(68)26, DP/n(68)27, DP/n(68)29, DP/n(68)27, DP/n(68)36, DP/n(68)29, DP/n(68)94, DP/n(68)95, and NDAC/WP(68)1. The notice was signed by M.H.G. Wilson.

DESTRUCTION OF CLASSIFIED PAPERS

In accordance with paragraph 88 and 89 of NATO Security Regulations, it is
recommended that you should destroy any copies which may happen to be in your
possession of the papers listed at Annex.
