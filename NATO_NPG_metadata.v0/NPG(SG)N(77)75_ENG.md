> # Follow-Up Action to Bari Ministerial Meeting
> 
> This document consists of a note from the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, R.E. Shearer, and an accompanying letter from the Italian Delegation. The Chairman's note, dated 25 October 1977, informs the Staff Group that a response from Italy has been received regarding a previous communication (DPP/NPD/77/49). The Italian Delegation, in a letter dated 24 October 1977, agrees in principle with a proposed letter to the Chairman of the Military Committee, but raises an objection regarding the satisfaction of the provisions of paragraph 13 of the Bari Agreed Minute. The Italian representative, A. Ciarrapico, suggests that Permanent Representatives should provide general guidelines or terms of reference for the high-level working group tasked with the long-term improvements study. The matter is scheduled for discussion at the next Staff Group meeting.

LETTER FROM THE ITALIAN DELEGATION
Brussels, 24th October, 1977

Dear ...,
I am referring to your DPF/NPD/77/49.
I can agree in principle on the content of the proposed letter to the Chairman
of the Military Committee.

As regards the substance of the last sentence in your cover letter, I have to do
one objection. We read on its paragraph 2: ".... the Secretary General will be
advised that the provisions of paragraph 13 of the Bari Agreed Minute have been
satisfied". On my judgement, the said letter does not satisfy all the provisions
of paragraph 13 of the Bari Agreed Minute but only paragraph 10, part of
paragraphs 11 and, of course, 13. I think that to meet Ministers directions
expressed in above mentioned paragraph 13; Permanent Representatives should
prepare for the high-level working group charged of the long-term improvements
study at least the general guidelines, if not just terms of reference.

Yours ...,
(Signed) A. CIARRAPICO
Mr. Dick SHEARER
Chairman NPG Staff Group
International Staff
NATO/BRUSSELS
