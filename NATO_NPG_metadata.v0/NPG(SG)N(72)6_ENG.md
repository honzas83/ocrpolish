> # Notes of a Meeting Held on Monday, 17th January, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on January 17, 1972, to address several key agenda items. The group welcomed new members, including Colonel W.B. Attenburg from SHAPE and Brigadier-General James E. Hill from the United States Air Force. The meeting included a presentation of the Turkish Study on February 11, 1972, and discussions on the initiation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Studies, with a target completion date of Spring 1973. The group also discussed the draft report on facilities available for consultation and the SHAPE Battlefield Use Illustrative Study. Additionally, the group agreed to update information on Warsaw Pact capabilities, doctrine, and intentions, and the Chairman noted an increase in the number of staff accompanying Ministers at NPG Ministerial Meetings. The meeting concluded with the agreement to hold the next meeting on January 24, 1972.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

Notes of a meeting held on Monday, 17th January, 1972

WELCOME

1. The STAFF GROUP:
endorse the warm welcome extended by the Chairman to Colonel W.B. Attenburg from
SHAPE who would be working on Phase II of the Follow-on Use Studies, and to
Brigadier-General James E. Hill, USAF, Deputy Assistant to the Secretary of
Defense (Atomic Energy) and to Lieutenant Colonel Paul Ziluca, OATSD (AE) Staff.

SYMPATHY

2. The STAFF GROUP:
endorsed the words of sympathy addressed by the Chairman to the Danish
representative concerning the sad loss recently suffered by the Danish nation.

FAREWELL LUNCHEON

3. the STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the farewell luncheon in
honour of Mr. Da Rin would take place in the Salon des Ambassadeurs at 12.45 for
1 p.m. on Wednesday, 19th January, 1972;

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Turkish representative who said that his
authorities had offered to give the presentation of the Turkish Study on Friday,
11th February, 1972;

INITIATION OF PHASE II OF THE FOLLOW-ON USE STUDIES

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who referred to the wishes
expressed by Ministers at their meeting at the Palais d'Égmont that Permanent
Representatives should develop proposals for the initiation of Phase II of the
follow-on use work programme, to be considered at the next Ministerial meeting;
(1)

(b) noted further remarks by the Chairman who proposed that consideration be
given to the following points:

(i) the time frame: he suggested that Phase II should be completed in the Spring
of 1973, so that the move forward into Phase III could then take place;

(ii) the task: he recalled that the scope of the task and its political features
had already been discussed in detail;

(iii) Organisation and participation: he outlined various options, and invited
comments on the problem as a whole.

(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion, and in
particular of the differing suggestions regarding the organisation and
participation required to undertake the task;

(d) noted that it was generally agreed that in order to maintain the momentum of
the study, the period Spring 1972 to Spring 1973 shall be allotted to Phase II;

(e) agreed that the task was already sufficiently well defined;

7. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the representative of the International Military

International Staff and Delegations before the target date of 1st February,
1972.

WARSAW FACT CAPABILITIES, DOCTRINE AND INTENTIONS

8. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Canadian representative and agreed with his
suggestion that information on Warsaw Pact capabilities, doctrine and intentions
should be brought up to date and made available to the Staff Group and other
interested bodies.
