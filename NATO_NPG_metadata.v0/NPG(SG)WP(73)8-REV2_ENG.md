> # NATO Operational Concepts and Doctrine Currently Governing Employment of Nuclear Weapons for Tactical Purposes
> 
> This document is a second revision of a draft report by the Permanent Representatives of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) regarding the operational concepts and doctrine governing the tactical employment of nuclear weapons within NATO. The report comments on a basic report prepared by the Supreme Headquarters Allied Powers Europe (SHAPE) and related comments from the Military Committee (MC). It discusses the operational concepts and doctrine within Allied Command Europe (ACE) and notes that these are consistent with political guidance from the Defence Planning Committee (DPC). The report highlights the flexibility of these concepts to respond to enemy aggression and confirms that the Supreme Allied Commander Atlantic (SACLANT) and Allied Commander-in-Chief, Channel Command (CINCHAN) utilize essentially the same concepts. It also addresses the tactical role of nuclear weapons in weakening the enemy's will to continue aggression, noting the significant political implications. The report aims to assist NPG Ministers in investigating unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence, including the combined use of conventional and nuclear capabilities and the reduction of collateral effects through lower yield weapons.
> 
> ## Categories/Topics
> 
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Flexible-Response — The document explicitly references MC 14/3 (Page 6, footnote 3), which is the foundational document for the Flexible Response strategy. It also discusses the 'early combined use of our conventional and nuclear capabilities' and the use of 'lower yield battlefield weapons' (Page 2), which are hallmarks of a tailored, escalating response rather than immediate total war.
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Nuclear-Strategy-and-Strike-Planning — The document is a report on 'operational concepts and doctrine currently governing employment of nuclear weapons for tactical purposes' (Page 2). It discusses the development of military planning, the role of SHAPE and the Military Committee in outlining these concepts, and the describes the 'tactical employment of nuclear weapons' (Page 1) and 'target selection' considerations like 'collateral effects' (Page 3).
> #Doctrine-and-Strategy/Nuclear-Deterrence — The document explicitly mentions that the operational concepts and doctrine offer a framework to support 'both the defence and deterrence aspects' (Page 7) of Alliance objectives in time of war.

I. PURPOSE
This report comments on NATO operational concepts and doctrine currently
governing employment of nuclear weapons for tactical purposes.

II. BACKGROUND
2. At their meeting in Mittenwald in May, 1971, the NPG Ministers discussed a
paper(1) on unresolved problems related to tactical nuclear defence which, among
other matters, raised the question of possible improvements in concepts and
tactics. In this context, the paper mentioned four factors that might be
examined: wider use of lower yield battlefield weapons and their combined use
with conventional weapons; the possibility of an early combined use of our
conventional and nuclear capabilities as soon as the attack is identified as a
major aggression and before our conventional forces have suffered substantial
losses, and a constrained but continuing combined use if the aggressor persists
in his attack; exploitation of the possible advantage of re-structured and
deployed defensive forces against the necessarily more concentrated attacker;
and possible reduction
