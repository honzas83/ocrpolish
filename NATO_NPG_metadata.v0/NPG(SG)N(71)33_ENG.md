> [!abstract] 
> # Draft Advance Press Release for the Mittenwald Meeting
> 
> This document consists of a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, H. B. Seim, and a draft advance press release. The note suggests that the press release be issued from NATO Headquarters in Brussels on May 19th, 1971, at 3 p.m., with simultaneous release in national capitals at the discretion of each country. The Staff Group is invited to discuss the draft text on March 15th to agree on a version that can be sent to national capitals for approval. The draft press release specifies that the Nuclear Planning Group, comprising the Defence Ministers of Canada, Germany, Greece, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, the United Kingdom, and the United States, will hold its ninth half-yearly meeting in Mittenwald, Germany, under the chairmanship of NATO Secretary General Manlio Brosio.

DRAFT ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE FOR THE MITTENWALD MEETING

Note by the Chairman

Attached is the draft of an advance press release concerning the Ministerial
meeting in Mittenwald on 25th and 26th May, 1971. The language closely follows
the pattern of the press releases made just prior to the Venice and the Ottawa
meetings.

2. It is suggested that the release might be made at NATO Headquarters on
Wednesday, 19th May at 3 p.m. Brussels time, with simultaneous release in
capitals at the discretion of the country concerned.

3. The Staff Group may wish to discuss this draft at our next meeting on 15th
March for the purpose of agreeing on a text that could be sent to capitals for
approval.

(Signed) H. B. SEIM

NATO, 1110 Brussels.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP

DRAFT ADVANCE PRESS RELEASE

As arranged at its previous Ministerial meeting in Ottawa, Canada, in October
1970, the Nuclear Planning Group of NATO, currently consisting of the Defence
Ministers of Canada, Germany, Greece, Italy, the Netherlands, Norway, the United
Kingdom and the United States, will hold its ninth half-yearly meeting on 25th
and 26th May in Mittenwald, Germany, under the chairmanship of Mr. Manlio
Brosio, Secretary General of NATO.
