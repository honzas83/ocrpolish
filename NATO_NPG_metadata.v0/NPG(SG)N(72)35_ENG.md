> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels on Monday and Tuesday, 27th and 28th March, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on March 27-28, 1972, to reach agreement on several key papers. The group focused on finalizing the report on the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Battlefield Use of Nuclear Weapons and the report on Consultation Procedures and Facilities. Additionally, they reviewed the report on the Mediterranean Maritime Study regarding the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. The Staff Group agreed that these modified texts should be submitted to Capitals and examined by Permanent Representatives on April 13, 1972. The meeting also addressed the logistics and timing of briefings for NPG Ministers in May 1972, including contributions from SHAPE and the International Staff. There was a discussion regarding a possible visit to the United States in June 1972, including transportation and expenses. The group noted the upcoming meeting of Permanent Representatives on April 13, which would examine various working papers including reports on Turkish studies and the initiation of Phase II. The Staff Group scheduled its next meeting for April 10, 1972, to prepare for the Permanent Representatives' discussion.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels. on Monday and
Tuesday, 27th and 28th March, 1972

IMMEDIATE TASK OF THE STAFF GROUP
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the task of the Staff
Group at this meeting was to reach agreement on the texts of two related papers
(the report on the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Battlefield Use of Nuclear
Weapons, and the report on Consultation Procedures and Facilities) and on the
text of the report on the Mediterranean Maritime Study; as soon as agreement had
been achieved and the approval of the Secretary General obtained, these three
papers would be issued as NPG Working Papers, for immediate dispatch to
Capitals, with a view to subsequent examination by Permanent Representative at
their meeting to be held on 15th April, 1972.

REPORT ON THE ILLUSTRATIVE STUDY ON BATTLEFIELD USE OF NUCLEAR WEAPONS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the previous
discussions on the report on the Illustrative Study on Battlefield use, and
introduced the latest version of the Staff Group Working Paper(1); he referred
particularly to the areas suggested for Ministerial discussion, and invited
comments;

(i) the paper was set out in the same form and sequence as previous reports on
studies of Follow-on Use in other areas;

(ii) this Study was included in Item III of the draft Ministerial Agenda, and
should remain there unless Permanent Representatives decided that it should be
considered by Ministers in the Autumn;

(iii) he hoped that comments upon this Study would be received shortly from
Capitals and from SACEUR, SACLANT and the Chairman of the Military Committee;
Permanent Representatives should be advised to consider what changes
