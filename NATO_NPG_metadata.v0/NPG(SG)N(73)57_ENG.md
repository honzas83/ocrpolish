> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 2nd July, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on July 2, 1973, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key strategic and administrative issues. The group reviewed documentation regarding an illustrative study and the Military Committee's role in nuclear consultation. A significant portion of the meeting focused on the policy for the use of tactical nuclear weapons, specifically addressing concerns raised by the United Kingdom representative regarding reports of United States' thinking on the matter. The group also discussed the re-orientation of the International Staff's working paper on consultation procedures and facilities. Regarding operational concepts and doctrine, the group agreed to changes in a draft paper and requested a revised version. The meeting also covered participation in studies, including Warsaw Pact politico-military strategy and military doctrine, with a study team comprising the Netherlands, Canada, Denmark, Germany, Norway, and the United States. Additionally, the group noted Norway's participation in a study on the communication of NATO's intentions and discussed the conceptual analysis of nuclear deterrence. Administrative arrangements for the upcoming NPG Ministers meeting in The Hague were addressed, and the group planned its activities for the remainder of July, including the discussion of a military paper on the early combined use of conventional and nuclear weapons. Finally, the group scheduled its next meetings for July 9, 16, and 30.

(a) took note of a statement by the United Kingdom representative who said that
recent reports had given the impression that Mr. Kissinger believed that no
rational policy existed for the use of tactical nuclear weapons; bearing in mind
that the NPG had been working on this problem for a number of years he wondered
whether Mr. Kissinger's views stemmed from ignorance of what had already been
done, or alternatively implied a new departure in United States' thinking on
this matter; whichever was the reason, he hoped that the United States
representative would obtain guidance from his authorities and enlighten the
Staff Group;

(b) took note of a statement by the Chairman who gave some information on the
general background to this question and observed that it might be necessary to
foresee a report on the question in time for the Autumn NPG Ministerial meeting;
he invited the United States representative to obtain further information;

(c) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion, and agreed to
re-open the matter when more ample information was available to the Staff Group.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES

3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that, at its last
meeting, the Staff Group had decided that the thrust of the first draft of the
International Staff’s working paper(1) should be re-oriented, with a view to
instigating action which could be reported to Ministers rather than to ask for
further guidance; he said that it was hoped to make progress in the next few
days when information would be received from the concerned sections of the
International Staff and from the Military authorities;

(b) agreed to re-open the examination of this matter as soon as further
information and data had been received.

OPERATIONAL CONCEPTS AND DOCTRINE

4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced the International
Staff's paper(1) and said that, in accordance with usual practice, it was set
out in four sections; he drew attention to the fact that duel capability had not
been mentioned; he also drew attention to a possible risk of misunderstanding
resulting from the use of the word concepts in the singular;

(b) took note of the general remarks and the detailed suggestions made during
the subsequent discussion, and agreed to a number of changes to the text;

(c) invited the International Staff to prepare a revised version of the draft
paper in the light of the views expressed and the decisions taken in the
discussion, and agreed to re-open the examination of this matter at a future
meeting.

PARTICIPATION IN STUDIES

Warsaw Pact Politico-Military Strategy and Military Doctrine

5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Netherlands representative who confirmed
that a basic paper on Warsaw Pact doctrine was being prepared in The Hague and
would be discussed by the Study Team (Netherlands, Canada, Denmark, Germany,
Norway, the United States) at a meeting to be held in The Hague in early
September;

(b) agreed that the meeting of the Study Team should be on 6th September so as
not to clash with the meeting of the Part II Study Team to be held in Washington
on 10th September.
