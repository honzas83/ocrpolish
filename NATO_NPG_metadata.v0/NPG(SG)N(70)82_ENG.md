> # Suggested Changes to Provisional Political Guidelines for the Initial Defensive Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons by NATO
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, outlines suggested modifications to the Provisional Political Guidelines for the initial defensive tactical use of nuclear weapons by NATO. The Chairman, H. B. Seim, outlines that following the Secretary General's remarks on October 6, 1970, it was necessary to modify the Provisional Political Guidelines to reflect the promulgation of the Special Atomic Demolition Munitions (ADM) Guidelines. Specifically, the document proposes substituting text in Paragraph 55 to ensure planning for the use of Atomic Demolition Munitions conforms to the Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of Atomic Demolition Munitions, and updating Paragraph 123 to ensure planning follows the guidelines in the paper and other specific policy guidance from the Defence Planning Committee (DPC). These suggested changes are intended to be presented to Ministers at the upcoming Ottawa meeting for consideration alongside the Special ADM Guidelines paper under agenda item IV. The document references previous guidelines from November 20, 1969, and anticipates a future reference number for the DPC policy document to be approved in December 1970.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

SUGGESTED CHANGES TO PROVISIONAL POLITICAL GUIDELINES
FOR THE INITIAL DEFENSIVE TACTICAL USE OF
NUCLEAR WEAPONS BY NATO(1)

Note by the Chairman

In his introductory remarks at the meeting of NPG Permanent Representatives on
6th October, the Secretary General pointed out that, if the Special ADM
Guidelines are approved in December, it would be appropriate for the DPC to
modify the Provisional Political Guidelines to reflect the promulgation of the
Special ADM Guidelines. Subsequently, the NPG Staff Group agreed to the
following suggested modifications to the Provisional Political Guidelines:

(a) Paragraph 55, page 17, substitute the following text:
Planning by the NATO Military Authorities for the use of ADs should conform to
the Special Political Guidelines for the Possible Use of Atomic Demolition
Munitions(*).

(b) Paragraph 123, page 34, change last sentence to read:
Planning should follow the guidelines in this paper and other specific policy
guidance promulgated by the Defence Planning Committee(*).

(*) DPC/D(70)... December, 1970 (This would be the reference number of the
policy document approved and promulgated by the DPC.7^

2. It is planned that a hand-out containing the above suggested language will be
circulated to the Ministers at the Ottawa meeting for their consideration
together with the Special ADM Guidelines paper under agenda item IV.

(Signed) H. B. SEIM

This document consists of 1 page.

(1) DPC/D(69)58, 20th November, 1969.
