> # Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) Ministerial Meeting Preparations
> 
> This document, a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group, details the preparatory work required for the upcoming Ministerial meetings in Autumn 1976 and the Spring of 1977. The Autumn 1976 meeting is expected to cover briefings on the balance of strategic forces, including the Euro-strategic balance and the role of cruise missiles, as well as progress reports on Nuclear Weapons Employment Policy Phase III and the Technology Study. It also includes presentations on newly developed nuclear consultation procedures and the TNF Improvement Programme. The Spring 1977 meeting is slated to cover the final report of the Technology Study, a draft report on Nuclear Weapons Employment Policy Phase III, and studies on dual-capable aircraft and NIKE HERCULES levels. The document emphasizes the need for close cooperation with NATO Military Authorities and the need for Permanent Representatives' reports. It further discusses the importance of early discussion on the impact of cruise missiles on NATO's defense and deterrent strategy, given their potential operationality by 1978. The note also addresses the importance of nuclear consultation and the process of nuclear decision-making in crisis conditions, including the participation of Ministers in the WINTEX 77 exercise. An annex provides a detailed schedule of work, including weekly Staff Group meetings and deadlines for the approval of compilation papers and terms of reference for the NIKE HERCULES study.

preparing for future NPG Ministerial meetings. Some timing considerations are

II. Discussion

6. Briefings on the Balance of Strategic Forces. At their last meeting NPG
Ministers noted a suggestion by the United States and the Federal Republic of
Germany that future briefings on the strategic balance should extend to the
nuclear balance in Europe(1) and a suggestion by Canada that the next briefing
should also cover the potential rôle and value of cruise missiles in NATO's
defence and deterrence strategy(2). During Ministerial discussion it was pointed
out that cruise missiles might be operational by 1978. This being the case, an
early discussion of the impact of cruise missiles on NATO's defense and
deterrent strategy appears to be a proper matter for Ministerial attention. With
respect to the Euro-strategic balance, it is expected that this subject, in the
light of the continuing improvements in armaments on the Warsaw Pact side, would
most likely become a regular feature of future NPG meetings.

7. Nuclear Employment Policy. The latest Phase III progress report(3) states
that the date for circulation of a first Phase III draft in the Working Group is
1st October, 1976. Permanent Representatives have been requested to monitor and
co-ordinate all ongoing NPG study work(4) and have confirmed in a document on
follow-up action(5) the requirement for their own involvement in Phase III work.
They have at their disposal a number of basic documents(6) which are useful in
this context. Further action by Permanent Representatives in connection with the
Phase III study will be determined subsequent to their meeting with the Study
Group Chairman which is now anticipated for September 1976.

8. Technology Study. The Technology Study is progressing and Ministers have
asked for a substantive progress report at their Autumn meeting. Permanent
Representatives should receive this report in time to add their comments, should
they so desire, prior to forwarding the report to Ministers.

9. Improvements to TNF Effectiveness. There are a number of TNF improvement
matters which will require Ministerial attention. Several nations have provided
their national views(1) and two studies, one on dual-capable aircraft and one on
NIKE HERCULES arc being initiated. In addition to these two studies, a number of
other TNF improvement projects will be active in the NPG arena in the months
ahead. Some of these projects are ground force nuclear support, the maritime
aspects of nuclear warfare, nuclear weapons storage site review, a review of ADM
requirements, and further improvement and better utilisation of Theatre
Information and Engagement Systems (TIES). The timing of these efforts should be
established well in advance and Permanent Representatives' reports on these
subjects may be expected.

10. Nuclear Consultation. The problem of providing Ministers and their advisors
additional opportunities to familiarize themselves with the problems of nuclear
decision-making in crisis conditions(2) will be under continuous review. In the
meantime, Ministers at their Autumn meeting will observe the presentation on
newly developed procedures in the field of nuclear consultation and they will
have an opportunity to participate personally in WINTEX 77. It is anticipated
that Permanent Representatives, immediately after the WINTEX exercise, will
follow established tradition and present a report on their preliminary
observations to NPG Ministers. This report might provide a suitable platform for
presenting to Ministers additional measures which they could endorse as proper
for further familiarization in nuclear decision-making in crisis conditions.

Final Remarks

11. Staff Group members are invited to consider these matters along with the
suggested schedule of work at Annex, at future meetings.
