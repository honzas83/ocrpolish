> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 17th January, 1977
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group held a meeting on January 17, 1977, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key nuclear planning issues. The group discussed the possibility of visiting nuclear installations, with the UK and US representatives tasked to ascertain feasibility from their respective authorities. Regarding Theatre Information and Engagement Systems (TIES), the group noted the proposal for a further meeting on January 20, 1977, and agreed that status reports should be available to Permanent Representatives and the Military Committee. The meeting also addressed the Nike Hercules study, noting that it should be completed by March 1, 1977, and discussed potential timeline adjustments to avoid delays in reporting to Ministers. The Staff Group reviewed the draft Status of Work Report and agreed to a number of changes, with the Nuclear Planning Directorate being invited to prepare a revised draft for consultation with Capitals. Additionally, the group noted the meeting of the Political Implications Team (PIT) scheduled for January 25-27, 1977, and scheduled their next meeting for January 24, 1977.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 17th
January, 1977

VISITS BY THE STAFF GROUP TO NUCLEAR INSTALLATIONS
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that, at its meeting
on 2nd November, 1976, the Staff Group had invited the UK and US representatives
to ascertain from their authorities whether it would be possible to arrange for
the Staff Group to visit nuclear installations;

(b) concurred with the further remarks of the Chairman who said that, in view of
the present workload, such visits, if feasible; should be undertaken in the near
future.

THEATRE INFORMATION AND ENGAGEMENT SYSTEMS (TIES)
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled that the TIES status
report had been discussed in his office on Thursday, 13th January, and he
proposed that another such meeting should take place on Thursday, 20th January;
he hoped that experts on this matter could be present;

(b) noted the Chairman's view that the TIES status [unreadable]

NPG STUDY OF LEVELS OF NIKE HERCULES

3. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that the NIKE HERCULES
Study should be completed and presented to NPG Permanent Representatives not
later than 1st March, 1977. If Permanent Representatives were to have sufficient
time to discuss the subject and to consider the preparation of a report to
accompany the study which should be submitted to Ministers in the Spring of
1977; he had hoped that SHAPE would be able to provide this report by 28th
January but he had reason to believe that SACEUR would ask for a 30 days
extension to the deadline;

(b) took note of a suggestion by the Chairman that these 30 days might be saved
by reducing consideration time in the Military Committee and for Permanent
Representatives by 15 days each.

NPG FUTURE WORK PRIORITIES AND STATUS OF WORK REPORT

4. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled that at its last
meeting the Staff Group had considered a paper (1) and had agreed, after further
discussion between the Nuclear Planning Directorate and the UK and US
representatives, to tackle the problem by making two papers instead of one; as a
result of this decision, a draft status of work report (2) had been circulated
to members; the question of future work priorities would be addressed in the
Spring;
