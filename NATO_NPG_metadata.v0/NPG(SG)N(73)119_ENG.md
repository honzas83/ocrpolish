> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 19th November, 1973
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on November 19, 1973, in Brussels to address several administrative and strategic items. The group welcomed the new active participation of Portugal and the appointment of Mr. J. Meira as its representative. The group also expressed appreciation for the Netherlands Authorities for their hospitality during the NPG Ministerial meeting in The Hague on November 6-7, 1973. The meeting's agenda included reviewing documentation regarding Portugal's participation, nuclear consultation traffic, and a Senior Officers' Orientation Course. Preparations were made for the upcoming Nuclear Delegation and Advisory Committee (NDAC) Ministers meeting on December 7, 1973, including the agreement on a draft agenda and a report on NPG activities for 1973. The group agreed to a text for insertion into the Final Communiqué of the December Ministerial meetings. Additionally, the group discussed the results of the Hague meeting, identifying key tasks such as studying the political aspects of Early Combined Use of nuclear weapons and compiling nuclear consultation procedures. The group also planned for a future study on the political and military implications of new technological developments. Tentative dates for the NPG Spring Ministerial meeting were set for June 4-5, 1974, and the next Staff Group meeting was scheduled for November 26, 1973.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 19th
November, 1973

CHANGE OF MEMBERSHIP
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that Portugal had elected
to become active in NPG affairs and warmly endorsed the welcome he extended to
Mr. J. Meira, representing Portugal on the Staff Group for the first time.

NPG MINISTERIAL MEETING IN THE HAGUE
2. The STAFF GROUP:
endorsed the remarks of the Chairman who expressed, on behalf of the Staff, his
appreciation for the excellent arrangements and generous hospitality provided by
the Netherlands Authorities during the meeting of NPG Ministers in the Hague on
6th and 7th November; he requested the Netherlands representative to convey his
thanks to the Netherlands Authorities.

DOCUMENTATION
3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to the following
documentation which had recently been distributed:

(a) PO/73/142, dated 6th November, 1973: Participation by Portugal in NPG
activities;

RESULTS OF THE MEETING OF NPG MINISTERS IN THE HAGUE

(a) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who commented upon the Agreed

(b) noted the following main tasks which would require early discussion at Staff

(i) with regard to Early Combined Use(3) to consider the initiation of a study
on how to consider the political aspects of Early Combined Use in advance of
hostilities;

(ii) with regard to Consultation Procedures and Facilities(4) to make a
