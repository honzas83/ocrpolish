> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday 29th July, 1974
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on July 29, 1974. The group took note of several papers regarding the Military Committee's function in the nuclear consultation process, NATO's intention to use nuclear weapons, and the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Air Defence Use. Additionally, the group discussed a draft agenda for the Autumn meeting of NPG Ministers, agreeing to reopen discussions in early September after receiving instructions from Capitals. Regarding the final WINTEX 73 reports, the group noted general agreement that no further report by the Permanent Representatives was necessary, pending confirmation in September. The meeting also served as the final session for the current Chairman, who announced his return to the United States in mid-August and introduced his successor, Mr. R. Shearer. The group expressed appreciation and farewells to the outgoing Chairman and the International Staff.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday 29th
July, 1974

DOCUMENTATION
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to two papers which
had recently been circulated and to one paper about to be distributed:

(a) DPC/D(74)17, 24th July, 1974: Military Committee Function in the Nuclear
Consultation Process (for DPC approval by the silence procedure - cut off date
22nd August, 1974);

(b) NPG(Staff Group)WP(74)7(2nd Revise), 25th July, 1974: Draft study on
Communicating NATO's Intention to Use Nuclear Weapons;

(c) NPG(Staff Group)WP(74)8(Revised), 30th July, 1974: Draft report by Permanent
Representatives on the SHAPE Illustrative Study on Air Defence Use.

DRAFT MINISTERIAL AGENDA FOR THE AUTUMN MEETING, 1974
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced a draft agenda(1)
for the Autumn meeting of NPG Ministers;

(b) noted favourable initial views on the content of the Agenda, and agreed to

REVIEW OF FINAL WINTEX 73 REPORTS
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) tool: note of a statement by the Chairman who drew attention to his note(1)
in which he recalled that Ministers had invited Permanent Representatives to
make further comment, if appropriate, on the nuclear consultation aspects of the
final WINTEX 73 reports;

(b) noted general agreement that no further report by the Permanent
Representatives appeared to be necessary, and that this conclusion should be
placed before the Permanent Representatives during their review of future NPG
work in preparing for the Autumn Ministerial Meeting; if confirmed by the
Permanent Representatives this conclusion would be reflected in the PO prepared
by the Secretary General to provide a basis for Ministerial discussion of the
agenda item on Future Work Programme;

(c) agreed to confirm the above decision in early September in the light of
advice from Capitals.

NEXT MEETING
4. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to hold its next meeting on Wednesday, 4th September, 1974, at 3 p.m.
