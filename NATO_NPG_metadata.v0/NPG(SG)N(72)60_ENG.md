> # Notes of a Meeting Held at the NATO Headquarters, on Monday, 3rd July, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met at NATO Headquarters on July 3, 1972, to welcome the new Assistant Secretary General for Defence Plans and Policy, Mr. D.C. Humphreys, and Rear-Admiral C.F. Ellis (Junior) of the United States. The meeting also included representatives from the Council Operations, SACLANT, and the US Air Force Academy. The group discussed the schedule for NPG Permanent Representatives meetings in preparation for the NPG Ministers meeting on October 26-27, 1972, proposing dates in September and October. They addressed consultation procedures and facilities, reviewing a second draft of a working paper on the topic. The SHAPE Representative suggested deleting sections regarding the provision of information to National Authorities on nuclear weapon use recommendations. The group agreed to send the current draft to capitals for initial examination and resume work in late August. Additionally, the group identified several expected studies and reports, including the Denmark Study and the ACLANT Illustrative Study, to be arrived by late August. The next meeting was scheduled for August 21, 1972.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held at the NATO Headquarters, on Monday, 3rd July, 1972
WELCOME TO ASSISTANT SECRETARY GENERAL
1. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) warmly endorsed the welcome extended by the Chairman to the newly arrived
Assistant Secretary General for Defence Plans and Policy, Mr. D.C. Humphreys, to
whom the Chairman introduced the members of the Staff Group;

(b) noted with appreciation a statement by the Assistant Secretary General who
expressed his keen interest in the work of the Staff Group.

NEW MEMBERS AND VISITORS
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) warmly endorsed a statement by the Chairman who welcomed Rear-Admiral C.F.
Ellis (Junior), Deputy Defense Advisor to the United States Delegation, who
would be representing the United States in the work of the Staff Group as often
as his duties permitted;

(b) noted with pleasure the presence at the meeting of Mr. T. Moran, Deputy
Director of Council Operations, of Captain H. Davies (USN) from SACLANT's Staff,
and of Mr. Stephen Rossetti from the US Air Force Academy, temporarily attached
to SHAPE.

FUTURE LEAVING OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
3. THE STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman with regard to the dates of three
meetings of the NPG Permanent Representatives in preparation for the meeting of
NPG Ministers on 26th and 27th October, 1972;

(b) noted that the following programme appeared feasible:
(1) Thursday, 14th September, 1972 at 3.30 p.m.
Presentation of the Denmark Follow-on Use Study by the Study Team.
(2) Friday, 29th September, 1972 at 10.15 a.m.
Consideration and approval of draft reports to Ministers.
(3) Thursday, 12th October, 1972 at 3.30 p.m.
Preliminary review of the ACLANT Illustrative Study; final exchange of views on
Ministerial Agenda topics.

(c) agreed to reserve the above dates in their Permanent Representatives work
programme.

CONSULTATION PROCEDURES AND FACILITIES
4. THE STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled that at its meeting on
5th June, 1972, the Staff Group had agreed that the question of Consultation
Procedures and Facilities should be included in the next NPG Ministerial Agenda
and should be dealt with as a matter of priority, in accordance with Ministerial
instructions(1); he also recalled that at its meeting on 12th June the Staff
Group had agreed upon five points whose discussion should form the basis of a
draft report; he also recalled that the International Staff had circulated a
first draft(2) which had been discussed in general terms

on 15th June, and a second more complete draft(1), which had been modified in

(c) took note of a statement by the SHAPE Representative who said that SACEUR

information to National Authorities concerning recommendations by Subordinate
NATO Military Commanders on the possible use of nuclear weapons was premature

(d) took note of the views expressed and the suggestions made in the ensuing
discussion;

(e) agreed to take note of some changes proposed by the representatives of the
International Military Staff and SHAPE;

(f) agreed that the paper, in its present form but taking into account the
textual changes proposed by the IMS and SHAPE, and the views expressed in the
discussion, would provide a suitable basis for an initial examination in
capitals, and a point of departure for the work of the Staff Group in late
August;

(g) agreed that the paper should be sent to capitals without delay, and
expressed the hope that the IMS, SHAPE and SACLANT representatives would also
study the paper and be prepared to participate in the work in late August.
