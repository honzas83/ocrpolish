> # Arrangements for a Staff Officer to Attend NDAC and NPG Ministerial Meetings with the Military Observers
> 
> This document, issued by the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group), details the revised arrangements for staff officer support for Military Observers attending North Atlantic Council (NDAC) and Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Ministerial meetings. The Chairman of the Military Committee and Major NATO Commanders invited as observers may be accompanied by a single staff officer of colonel or lieutenant colonel rank to provide joint support and take notes in the conference room. The guidelines specify that while officers may be rotated, only one officer may be present at any given time, and all such officers must be identified in advance and possess proper security clearances. Furthermore, the document emphasizes that discussions during these Ministerial meetings are privileged and that the dissemination of information to non-participants is strictly limited. The arrangements were prepared following a Staff Group discussion on January 18, 1971, and were intended for further deliberation on January 25, 1971.

NPG(Staff Group)N(71)9
ARRANGEMENTS FOR A STAFF OFFICER TO ATTEND NDAC AND NPG MINISTERIAL MEETINGS
WITH THE MILITARY OBSERVERS

1. The Chairman of the Military Committee and the Major NATO Commanders who may
be invited to attend an NPG or NDAC Ministerial meeting in a personal capacity
as observers will be permitted to be accompanied by a staff officer(1) to serve
them jointly in the conference room during the sessions.

2. It will be permissible to rotate officers in this function but only one can
be present in the conference room at any given time.

3. Any officers performing this function will be specifically identified and
designated in advance of the meeting and should be certified as having the
proper clearances.

4. In accordance with NPG and NDAC practice, any discussions that occur during
Ministerial meetings are considered to be of a privileged character intended
primarily for the personal benefit of the participants; accordingly,
dissemination of information pertaining to such discussions to non-participants
will be strictly limited.

(1) For the purposes of these arrangements, a "staff officer" is interpreted to
be an officer of colonel/lieutenant colonel rank. The officer can take notes and
provide other staff support for the military observers, as appropriate.
