> [!abstract] 
> # Consultation Procedures – Country Request
> 
> This document provides a detailed overview of the amplified procedures for formulating and processing nuclear release requests from NATO member governments. Following discussions during WINTEX 75 in June 1975, the Permanent Representatives were tasked with examining the need for more detailed procedures to expedite the consultation process. The document emphasizes that the ultimate decision on the use of nuclear weapons always remains with the nuclear power. It distinguishes between proposals for use originating from a nuclear power and requests from a member government or a Major NATO Commander (MNC). The procedures cover both politico-military assessments and formal requests for use, specifying that assessments should be sent via AIG 6014 with FLASH precedence. If a country decides to make a formal request, it must include politico-military objectives and the consequences of non-use, addressing it to the Council/DFC and Major NATO Commanders. Upon receipt of a request, the Major NATO Commanders are expected to provide a military assessment and recommended courses of action. The document concludes with recommendations for the Ministers to approve these procedures and test them during future NATO exercises.

I.
PURPOSE
1. This document amplifies procedures for formulating and processing nuclear
release requests from nato member governments within the framework of the
General Guidelines for Consultation Procedures(1).

II.
BACKGROUND
2. During their discussion of WINTEX 75 at Monterey in June 1975, NPG Ministers
invited(2) the Permanent Representatives to examine the need for establishing
more detailed procedures for formulating and processing nuclear release requests
from NATO member governments. The possibility that member governments may
discern the wish to forward requests for the use of nuclear weapons is
recognised in the General Guidelines for Consultation Procedures(3), and in an
earlier Permanent Representatives' report(4), and it is implied in the
Provisional Political Guidelines(5). But the procedural aspects concerning such
requests are treated only in general terms in these documents.

3. Experience gained during WINTEX 75 pointed to the need for amplified
procedures for country requests as an aid to expediting the consultation
process, and following the Ministers' enquiry the

Permanent Representatives have concluded that such procedures should be

In line with the General Guidelines on Consultation Procedures(1) they have been
kept as simple and as flexible as possible, to allow modification in the light
of actual circumstances. If agreed by Ministers, these amplified procedures will
be included in the compilation of nuclear consultation procedures that has been

4. In dealing with these procedural matters, it is necessary to bear in mind
that the General Guidelines for Consultation Procedure state(2) that the
ultimate decision on the use of a nuclear weapon will always lie with the
nuclear power. In other words, the nuclear consultation process, whether
activated by an MNC or a country request, does not culminate in a use decision
in the NAC/DPC. The Guidelines distinguish(3) between a proposal for the use of
nuclear weapons in defence of NATO originating from a nuclear power, and
requests from a member government or from a Major NATO Commander. Since, from a
strictly procedural point of view, in connection with nuclear consultation in
the Council/DFC, there are no differences between proposals and requests, the
following paragraphs cover both.

Assessment
6. Politico-military assessments prepared by member governments in time of
hostilities could properly contain national views on a possible need for the use
of nuclear weapons in defence of the Alliance. These assessments need not
conform to any rigid format, but they should contain sufficient information to
assist member governments and the Major NATO Commanders concerned in preparing
their views. The address on a national assessment of this kind should be the
same as for nuclear consultation messages originated by MNCs, that is AIG 6014,
which includes all political and military authorities concerned with the
consultation process. The precedence should be FLASH, while the classification
would be determined by the originator. As in the case of nuclear consultation
messages originated by MNCs, the consultation process should start as soon as a
country assessment dealing with the possible need for the use of nuclear weapons
is received at NATO Headquarters. To assist nuclear consultation, the MNC(s)
concerned should provide, as soon as possible, their views on the national
assessment to the Council/DFC and capitals.

Request for Use
7. If a NATO country decides to make a request for the use of nuclear weapons it
should send a message to the Council/DFC, capitals and MNCs making the request
in as much detail as possible, and including all information which may help in
carrying out the consultation process. This information should state, in
particular, the politico-military objectives of the requested use, including the
possible consequences of non-use.

8. Since the implementation of a nuclear release decision is the responsibility
of the MNCs alone, it is essential for country request messages to be addressed
to the MNCs at the same time as to the political authorities. The use of AIG
6014 would satisfy such necessity. The message prepared by the requesting
country need not be bound to any particular format provided the requirements for
rapid distribution (AG 6014 and FLASH precedence) and clear identification
(NUCLEAR CONSULTATION MESSAGE label) are fulfilled. The internal distribution
and handling of these messages will be in accordance with present procedures for
nuclear consultation messages.

9. Upon receipt of a country request the MNC(s) concerned should provide, as
soon as possible, to the Council/DFC and capitals a military assessment of the
request including, where appropriate, recommended courses of action to satisfy
the purposes of the requested use. The MNC should particularly seek to provide
those elements of information necessary to the process of consultation(1)
