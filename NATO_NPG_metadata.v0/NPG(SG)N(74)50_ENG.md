> # Possible Change to NPG/WP(74)5
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) regarding a possible change to the draft report NPG/WP(74)5. The Chairman suggests that the report to the Defence Policy Committee (DPC) should explicitly state that the WP Study employed an analytical approach that differed from other NATO analyses in terms of objective, point of departure, and rules of analysis. This addition is intended to expand the basis for NATO's political decision-making in actual conflict situations. The proposed text emphasizes that while the WP Study's approach was unique, it still drew heavily on the 1973 agreed NATO military intelligence base, including assessments of Western European military capabilities and the strategic military considerations affecting Soviet decision-making. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, suggests that the Staff Group discuss this proposed change at their next meeting on Monday, 6th May 1974. The document originates from NATO headquarters in Brussels, Belgium.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
POSSIBLE CHANGE TO NPG/WP(74)5
Note by the Chairman
A review of the final version of the draft report(1) on the WP Study suggests
that we may have inadvertently omitted a useful point in the proposed report by
the NPG Ministers to the DPC, namely, the observation that the WP Study employed
an approach that differed from other analyses prepared in NATO. This thought is
now expressed in the draft Permanent Representatives' report to the NPG
Ministers in the first sentence of paragraph 3 on page 4. It is suggested that
the Staff Group may wish to consider inserting the same thought in the draft NPG
report to the DPC. One way of doing this would be to add a new first sentence to
paragraph 3 on page 1 of the ANNEX and to revise slightly the existing two first
sentences, so that the beginning of the paragraph would read as follows:

"3. Since the primary purpose of the study effort was to expand the basis for
NATO's political decision-making in actual conflict situations, the approach
taken in the WP Study was necessarily different, in terms of objective, point of
departure and rules of analysis, from other approaches used within the Alliance.
Nevertheless, in developing its analysis, the WP Study drew(2) heavily on the
assessment of WP military capabilities and the summary of the strategic military
considerations affecting Soviet decision-making with regard to use of military
power contained in the 1973 agreed NATO military intelligence base(3).

This document consists of two pages.
