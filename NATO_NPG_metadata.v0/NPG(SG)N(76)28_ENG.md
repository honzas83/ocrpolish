> # Nuclear Consultation – CNI Procedures
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) dated 6 April 1976, concerning the procedures for Nuclear Consultation (CNI). Nuclear consultation procedures are being finalized through a silence procedure. The Chairman notes that since no comments were received regarding amendments suggested by Italy, those changes will be incorporated into the white NPG Working Paper. Additionally, the Chairman recommends that a clarifying amendment suggested by the Netherlands on 6 April 1976 be accepted via the silence procedure. If no further comments are received by 9 April 1976, the document will be forwarded for reproduction. The note is signed by R.S. Shelr and originates from NATO headquarters in Brussels, Belgium.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)

NUCLEAR CONSULTATION – CNI PROCEDURES

Note by the Chairman

Since no comments on the amendments suggested by Italy through my NPG(STAFF
GROUP)N(76)24, 25th March, 1976, have been received by Monday, 5th april, I will
circulate the white NPG Working Paper with the amendments requested by the
Italian authorities.

2. although the deadline on other than the Italian changes had ended on 25th
March, 1976, I do recommend to the Staff Group that the latest clarifying
amendment circulated on 6th april, 1976 by the Netherlands (1) be accepted by
the silence procedure. If no comments on the Netherlands' suggested language are
received by close of business on 9th april, I will forward the document for
reproduction, accordingly revised.

(Signed) R.S. SHELR.

(1) Netherlands letter N° 2109, 6th .april, 1976
