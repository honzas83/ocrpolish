> # Work Programme for Further Studies on the Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear Weapons
> 
> This document is a note by the Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) and a draft report by the Permanent Representatives regarding the work programme for further studies on the follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons. The Chairman, H.B. Seim, informs the Staff Group that a draft report has been prepared based on discussions from April 16th and is scheduled for consideration at a meeting on April 27th. The report outlines that the Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group had previously accepted a work programme in November 1969, tasking the Permanent Representatives to arrange for the fulfillment of initial work, monitor the programme, and propose further work. The Permanent Representatives have initiated five immediate studies and three additional studies to be started later, while also exploring ancillary problems through informal discussions. The document notes that study team leaders have provided brief reports on the status of these studies as of May 1st, 1970, which are to be annexed to the final report for the Ministers' information. The Permanent Representatives suggest that proposals for further work be made at a later date based on the experience gained from the initial studies. The final report, including the study team annexes, will be circulated for consideration by the Permanent Representatives on May 12th.

Draft Report by the Permanent Representatives
Note by the Chairman

Attached, for Staff Group consideration at the meeting on Monday, 27th April, is
a Draft Report by Permanent Representatives, prepared in the light of the Staff
Group discussion at the meeting on 16th April.

2. The Staff Group will recall that it was agreed at that meeting that each
study team should prepare a brief report outlining the status of its study as of
1st May and deliver it to the International Staff no later than 30th April.
These study team reports would be annexed to the Report by the Permanent
Representatives for information of the Ministers, but would not be subject to
review and approval by the Permanent Representatives or the Staff Group.
Accordingly, the Staff Group is in a position to complete its consideration of
the attached draft without waiting for the study team reports.

3. At a later date, as soon as the study team reports are received by the
International Staff, a Permanent Representatives Working Paper consisting of the
basic report agreed at Staff Group level and the study team reports as annexes
will be circulated for consideration by the permanent Representatives at their
meeting on 12th May.

(Signed) H.B. SEIM

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Work Programme for further studies on the follow-on tactical use of nuclear
weapons

Draft Report by the Permanent Representatives

1. At their meeting at Airlie House on 11th and 12th November, 1969(1) the
Ministers of the Nuclear Planning Group accepted a work programme for the Study
of follow-on tactical use of nuclear weapons(2).

2. In this context, the Ministers also gave three tasks to their Permanent
Representatives(5):

(a) make arrangements for the fulfilment of the initial work;
(b) monitor on a continuing basis the work programme and the specific studies
undertaken therein;

(c) propose, as appropriate, further work within the context of the work
programme.

3. In response to the first task, the Permanent Representatives have made
further arrangements for the fulfilment of the initial work(4), calling for the
immediate initiation of five studies and the later commencement of three
additional studies at times to be determined by the participants. The Permanent
Representatives also took note of the particular interest expressed b. several
delegations in a concurrent preliminary examination of some important ancillary
problems(5). The Staff Group has begun to explore these subjects by means of
informal discussions and briefings, but is not yet in a position to render a
report to the Permanent Representatives.
