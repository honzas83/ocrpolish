> # Notes of a Meeting Held on 16th April, 1970
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (NPG) Staff Group met on 16th April 1970 to coordinate preparations for the Ministerial Meeting in Venice scheduled for 6th to 9th June 1970. The group discussed and modified the draft agenda for the Venice meeting, including the removal of a German report due to lack of new material and the consideration of a short briefing for Ministers. The meeting also addressed the status of follow-on tactical use studies, requiring study teams to provide statements on their composition and task by 30th April 1970. Plans for future meetings of NPG Permanent Representatives were set for 12th May and late May or early June to discuss the A/D Report and a German/United States paper on the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces. Additionally, the United Kingdom representative emphasized the need for updated information on the tactical stockpile and requested a briefing from the Supreme Allied Commander Europe (SACEUR) at the Venice meeting. The group examined a revised draft report on ADms and agreed to a modified version to be prepared as a Working Paper. Finally, the meeting scheduled for 20th April was cancelled, and the next Staff Group meeting was set for 27th April 1970 in Brussels.

Notes of a meeting held on 16th April, 1970
INVITATIONS TO NPC MINISTERIAL MEETING IN VENICE
1. The STAFF GROUP:
noted a statement by the Chairman who said that the Secretary General had
extended formal invitations to S'CEUR and S'CLANT to attend the meeting of NPG
Ministers to be held in Venice on 6th and 9th June, 1970.

AGENDA FOR THE MINISTERIAL MEETING IN VENICE
2. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) discussed the draft agenda(1), and took note of the views expressed;
(b) noted a statement by the German representative who said that the Report by
Germany (Item II.5(b)) would not now be circulated since it did not contain any
significant new material; agreed therefore to remove this Item from the agenda;

(c) noted the views expressed regarding the Status Reports on Follow-on Studies,
(see paragraph 3 below) and agreed to modify the agenda accordingly;

(d) discussed at length the desirability of a short briefing for Ministers on
the 'DIN reports, and agreed that whatever decision might finally be taken,
there was no need specifically to mention such a briefing in the agenda;

STATUS REPORT OF FURTHER STUDIES OF FOLLOW-ON TACTICAL USE
3. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of an outline draft(1) of a status report proposed by the
International Staff and discussed the manner in which it should be developed;

(b) agreed that each study team should provide a statement outlining its
composition, task, working arrangements and status of studies as at 1st May,
1970; these statements should be received by the International Staff not later
than 30th April;

(c) invited the International Staff, in the light of the views expressed in the
discussion, to prepare a draft Status Report for consideration by Permanent
Representatives on 12th May, 1970.

NEXT MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed that the most suitable date for the next meeting of the NPG Permanent
Representatives would be Tuesday, 12th May, 1970;

(b) agreed that this meeting should address the A/D Report, the draft Status
Report and possibly the agenda for the Ministerial Meeting in June;

(c) agreed that it would be necessary to foresee a second meeting of Permanent
Representatives in late May or early June, at which the German/United States
paper on the Role of Theatre Nuclear Strike Forces would be discussed.

TACTICAL STOCKPILE
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that at its last meeting, the United Kingdom representative had
emphasized the need for up-to-date information on the tactical stockpile;

(b) noted a statement by the United Kingdom representative who said that his
Minister had recently written to Secretary Laird on this matter, expressing the
hope that SACEUR would be able to give a briefing on this subject at the Venice
meeting.

REPORT ON ADMS
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) examined the revised version of the draft report on ADms(1), and took note
of the views expressed;

(b) agreed to a modified version of the report and invited the International
Staff, in the light of what had been said, to prepare a Nuclear Flaming Group
Working Paper, for circulation not later than 24th April, which would be
discussed by Permanent Representatives at their next meeting.

NEXT MEETING OF THE STAFF GROUP
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) agreed to cancel its meeting scheduled for Monday, 20th April;
(b) agreed to meet again on Monday, 27th April, 1970 at 3 p.m.
NATO, 1110 Brussels.
