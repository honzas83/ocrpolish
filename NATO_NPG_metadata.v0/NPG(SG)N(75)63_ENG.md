> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 25th August, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on August 25, 1975, at the NATO Headquarters in Brussels. The meeting began with security clearances and the welcoming of Mr. Douglas Hamilton, the new Canadian representative, and Col. J.H. van Valderen as the new dean. The primary focus was the initiation of Phase III studies, with the group taking note of papers submitted by the United Kingdom and Germany, and awaiting a contribution from the United States. The United Kingdom representative suggested that a small group of three countries should collaborate on Phase III, treating it as a continuation of Phase II. The group agreed that the final product of these studies should not exceed the security classification of NATO Secret. Other matters discussed included the need for guidance from the United States on the circulation of a paper regarding the employment of nuclear weapons, the International Staff paper on decision-making, and a request for a paper from the United Kingdom on the use of nuclear weapons for air defence. The group also noted the interest in discussing with members of the United States team working on the Air Battle Study. Additionally, the Staff Group looked forward to a draft report from the Beaumont group on communicating NATO's intentions (CNI). The meeting concluded with the an agreement to discuss country requests for the use of nuclear weapons and nuclear consultation procedures at the next meeting scheduled for September 1, 1975.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters
1110 Brussels, on Monday, 25th August, 1975

SECURITY
1. The STAFF GROUP: took note that all persons present were cleared for the
level of security of the matters to be discussed.

NEW MEMBER
2. The STAFF GROUP: warmly endorsed the welcome extended by the Chairman to Mr.
Douglas Hamilton, the new Canadian representative on the Staff Group.

CHANGE OF DEAN
3. The STAFF GROUP: warmly endorsed the farewell remarks of the Chairman to Mr.
B. Rosenthal, the departing dean, and the welcome extended to the new dean, Col.
J.H. van Valderen.

FUTURE WORK PROGRAMME - INITIATION OF PHASE III STUDIES
4. The STAFF GROUP: (a) took note of the statement by the Chairman who said that
the United Kingdom paper on the initiation of Phase III had been received by
members; that the German paper on the same subject was in the process of
distribution, and that he hoped that the United States contribution would be
received shortly;

(i) took note of the remarks of the United Kingdom representative who introduced
their Phase III scope paper; he suggested that a small group of representatives
from (say) three countries should work on this matter in the closest co-
operation with the rest of the Staff group; he felt strongly that Phase III
should be treated as a continuation of Phase II and not as a separate study;

(c) took note of the statement by the German representative who introduced his
paper and said that it was similar in substance to the United Kingdom paper; he
suggested that the basis for future work should be existing papers and guidance;

(d) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing initial discussion and
agreed that where possible the final product should not exceed the security
classification of NATO Secret;

(e) took note of the remarks of the United States representative who said that
he hoped to receive a paper from his authorities within about a week;

(f) took note of the remarks of the Chairman who urged the other representatives
to make similar contributions and suggested that representatives could send
copies of these papers to their capitals.

VARIOUS MATTERS

5. The STAFF GROUP:

took note of the remarks of the Chairman who recalled:

(a) the need for guidance from the United States representative on the manner of
circulation of the United States paper on the employment of nuclear weapons;

(b) that the Staff Group should shortly discuss the International Staff paper on
decision-making(1);

(c) the need for a paper from the United Kingdom authorities describing their
proposed requirement for a study on the use of nuclear weapons for air defence;

(d) in this context, that if any members of the United States team working on
the Air Battle Study happened to come to Europe the Staff Group would welcome
the opportunity of a discussion with them;

(e) that on the question of communicating NATO's intentions (CNI) the Staff
Group looked forward to a draft report from the Beaumont group.

NEXT MEETING

6. The STAFF GROUP:

(a) agreed to discuss the following matters, inter alia, at their next meeting:

(i) country requests for the use of nuclear weapons;

(ii) nuclear consultation procedures - compilation(1);

(b) agreed to hold their next meeting on Monday, 1st September, 1975, at 3 p.m.
