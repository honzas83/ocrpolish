> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 6th March, 1972
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting on March 6, 1972, at NATO Headquarters in Brussels to address several key agenda items. The group took note of various documents, including a Military Committee study on the battlefield use of tactical nuclear weapons and a draft report on the Turkish Follow-on Study. They discussed the invitation of SACEUR and SACLANT to the Nuclear Planning Group Ministerial Meeting scheduled for May 18-19, 1972, in Copenhagen, and noted the social programme planned by the Danish representative. Regarding the initiation of Phase II of the Follow-on Use Programme, the group encountered a divergency of views on whether the report should be sent to Ministers or Permanent Representatives, leading to the agreement to submit the problem to Permanent Representatives. The group also reviewed a draft status report on the Follow-on Use Work Programme and a draft agenda for the Copenhagen Ministerial Meeting. Finally, the group scheduled the next meetings of the NPG Permanent Representatives for March 9 and April 13, and their own next meeting for March 8, 1972.

Administrative Arrangements
3. The STAFF GROUP:
noted with appreciation a statement by the Danish representative who outlined
the probable social programme planned for 18th and 19th May.

INITIATION OF PHASE II OF THE FOLLOW-ON USE PROGRAMME
4. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who recalled the discussions on the
initiation of Phase II which had taken place at several previous meetings, and
the informal contacts which had resulted in a compromise text which had been
sent by the United States representative to his authorities in Washington; he
invited the United States representative to inform the Staff Group of the
results;

(b) took note of a paper handed out by the United States representative who
explained that it was the original text agreed during the informal contacts, but
with three alterations to which his authorities attached considerable
importance;

(c) discussed in great detail the implications of these changes and took note of
the views expressed;

(d) noted that there still remained a clear divergency of views as to whether
the proposed Study team should send its report direct to Ministers or through
the Permanent Representatives;

(e) agreed that, in view of the impasse which had been reached, it would be
appropriate to submit the problem to Permanent Representatives;

(f) invited the International Staff to prepare an NPG Working Paper composed of
those agreed parts of the text of the most recent Staff Group Working Paper(1),
together with the text resulting from the informal contacts, clearly showing
both the original wording and the changes proposed by the United States
Authorities;

STATUS REPORT – FOLLOW-ON USE WORK PROGRAMME
5. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced a draft status
report on the Follow-on Use Work Programme, explaining that it was non-
controversial and was set out in the same form as previous Status Reports;
recalled that Delegations concerned with Studies still in hand were requested to
forward the appropriate annexes in good time and in the same format as for
previous reports;

(b) took note of the views expressed in the ensuing discussion and agreed to
some minor changes;

(c) invited the International Staff, in the light of the views expressed, to
prepare and circulate the Status Report in the form of an NPG Working Paper, for
comment by Capitals and consideration by Permanent Representatives at their
meeting on 13th April.

DRAFT MINISTERIAL AGENDA
6. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) took note of a statement by the Chairman who introduced a draft agenda for
the NPG Ministerial Meeting to be held in Copenhagen on 18th and 19th May, and
invited comments;

(b) took note of the views expressed and the suggestions made during the
subsequent discussion; agreed to a number of changes;

(c) invited the Chairman to prepare a revised version of the Agenda with a view
to it being circulated under a cover-note by the Secretary General inviting
comments by Capitals and for consideration on 13th April by NPG Permanent
Representatives.

NEXT MEETINGS OF NPG PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES
7. The STAFF GROUP:
(a) recalled that the next two meetings of the NPG Permanent Representatives
would take place as scheduled, i.e.:
