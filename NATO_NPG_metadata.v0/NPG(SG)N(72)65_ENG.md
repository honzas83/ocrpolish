> # Classification of the Denmark Follow-on Use Study
> 
> The Chairman of the Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) issued a notice to clarify the actions required by holders of the Denmark Follow-on Use Study. The study, originally distributed on August 1, 1972, was initially classified as NATO SECRET. However, on August 17, 1972, the US Delegation's Security Advisor informed the NATO Office of Security that US classification authorities had determined the study contains Formerly Restricted Data (FRD) regarding nuclear weapons organization, storage locations, and stockpile yields. Consequently, the NATO Registry's ATOMAL Information Control Officer requested that all recipients change the classification to NATO SECRET ATOMAL and apply ATOMAL control procedures. While the US Delegation has requested their national authorities to identify specific ATOMAL parts of the study to potentially allow for modifications that would eliminate the ATOMAL classification, holders are currently required to comply with the reclassification. Members of the Staff Group are advised to be guided by the US statement regarding the general terms of the ATOMAL content within the study.

CLASSIFICATION OF THE DENMARK FOLLOW-ON JSE STUDY
1. About 1st August, 1972, the Study of Follow-on Tactical Use of Nuclear
Weapons – Denmark Area(1) was distributed through the NATO Registry to the
normal addresses of such documents. The Study was classified NATO SECRET by the
originators.

2. On 17th August, 1972, the Security Advisor of the US Delegation informed the
Director, NATO Office of Security, that US classification authorities had
determined that "the study contains FRD /Formerly Restricted Data/, based on
identification of special weapons organization actually having nuclear weapons,
foreign storage location and yields of stockpile weapons." He requested that all
copies of the Study be marked NATO SECRET ATOMAL. The US Security Advisor is not
in a position at present to say what specific parts of the Study the US
classification authorities consider to be ATOMAL.

3. In compliance with instructions from the Director, NATO Office of Security,
the ATOML Information Control Officer in the NATO Registry requested(2) all
recipients of the Study to take all necessary immediate action to change the
classification of the document to NATO SECRET ATOMAL, and to bring it under
ATOMAL control procedures. The request did not indicate why the US authorities
consider the document to be ATOMAL.

4. The US Delegation has asked their national authorities to identify the
specific parts of the Study that are ATOMAL and to provide guidance, if
possible, on what modifications might be made in order to eliminate the need to
classify the document as ATOMAL. When this clarification is received, further
steps to facilitate handling of the Study and discussion of its contents can be
considered.

5. In the meantime, holders of the Study have no recourse other than to comply
with the directives to reclassify all parts of the document as ATOMAL and to
handle it accordingly. Insofar as Staff Group discussion of its contents is
concerned, members should be guided by the US statement in paragraph 2 above
which indicates in general terms those aspects of the Study that the US
classification authorities consider to be ATOMAL.

(2) A5(72)422, 18th August, 1972
