> # Notes of a Meeting Held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 24th November, 1975
> 
> The Nuclear Planning Group (Staff Group) held a meeting at NATO Headquarters in Brussels on November 24, 1975. The meeting's agenda included a report from the Chairman regarding his mission to Washington and the nuclear affairs information gathered. The Staff Group also noted the Chairman's announcement that a meeting of the Nuclear Planning Committee (NPC) Permanent Representatives would occur on November 25, 1975, following the Defense Policy Committee (DPC) meeting to discuss the postponed Autumn NPC Ministerial Meeting based on a letter from Ambassador Bruce. The Additionally, the Chairman indicated his intention to circulate a paper on Communicating NATO's Intentions (CNI) matters for future discussion. Finally, the group discussed a Technology Study, with the German representative requesting that reports and views be submitted to the Chairman of the Political Implications Team by December 5, 1975. The date for the next meeting was to be decided based on the outcome of the NPC Permanent Representatives' meeting.

NUCLEAR PLANNING GROUP (STAFF GROUP)
Notes of a meeting held in the NATO Headquarters, 1110 Brussels, on Monday, 24th
November, 1975

CHAIRMAN'S VISIT TO WASHINGTON
1. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of a statement by the Chairman who described his mission to Washington
and the information on nuclear affairs which he had been able to gather.

MEETING OF NPC PERMANENT REPRESENTATIVES ON 25TH NOVEMBER
2. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman who said that a meeting of the NPC
Permanent Representatives would take place in the afternoon of Tuesday, 25th
November, directly after the DPC meeting, to discuss, in the light of ambassador
Bruce's letter to the Secretary General dated 21st November, possible dates for
the postponed Autumn NPC Ministerial Meeting.

COMMUNICATING NATO'S INTENTION'S
3. The STAFF GROUP:
took note of the remarks of the Chairman who said that he intended to circulate,
within a few days, a paper on CNI matters as a basis for discussion within the
Staff Group.

TECHNOLOGY STUDY
4. The STAFF GROUP:
tool: note of a statement by the German represent-ative concerning the
Technology Study; he recalled that countries had been invited, if they so
wished, to submit reports and views to the Chairman of the Political
Implications Team; he requested that such reports should be sent in not later
than 5th December, 1975.

NEXT MEETING
5. The STAFF GROUP:
agreed to decide upon the date of its next meeting in the light of the decision
to be taken by the IPC Permanent Representatives on 25th November.
